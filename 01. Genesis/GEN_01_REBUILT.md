## CHAPTER SUMMARY

In the beginning, God created the heaven and the earth by His Word. The earth was without form and void (tohu wabohu); darkness was upon the face of the deep; the Spirit of God moved upon the waters. God spoke and brought forth creation in six literal days: Day 1 — light divided from darkness; Day 2 — firmament dividing waters above from waters below; Day 3 — dry land, seas, and vegetation; Day 4 — sun, moon, and stars set for signs, seasons, days, and years; Day 5 — sea creatures and fowl; Day 6 — land animals and man. God made man, male and female, in His own image and gave them dominion over all creation. Each day is marked by "the evening and the morning." God saw everything He had made and it was "very good." The seventh day is implied here and stated in Genesis 2:2-3 as the day of rest — establishing the Sabbath pattern.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "In the Beginning" — The Word as Creator

**Genesis 1:1** — "In the beginning God created the heaven and the earth."

- **John 1:1-3** — "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God. The same was in the beginning with God. All things were made by him; and without him was not any thing made that was made."
  - *Interpretation:* John deliberately opens his Gospel with "In the beginning" — the same phrase as Genesis 1:1 — to identify Jesus Christ as the Word who was present at and active in creation. This is not coincidence; it is John's intentional cross-reference.

- **Colossians 1:16-17** — "For by him were all things created, that are in heaven, and that are in earth, visible and invisible, whether they be thrones, or dominions, or principalities, or powers: all things were created by him, and for him: And he is before all things, and by him all things consist."
  - *Interpretation:* Christ created ALL things — visible and invisible — and holds them together. "By him all things consist" (sustain their existence) answers the question of what keeps the universe from collapsing.

- **Hebrews 1:2** — "Hath in these last days spoken unto us by his Son, whom he hath appointed heir of all things, by whom also he made the worlds"
  - *Interpretation:* The Son made the worlds. He is also appointed heir of all things — the Creator becomes the Inheritor.

- **Hebrews 11:3** — "Through faith we understand that the worlds were framed by the word of God, so that things which are seen were not made of things which do appear."
  - *Interpretation:* Creation ex nihilo — out of nothing visible. The seen came from the unseen. Faith accepts this because God says it.

### "The Spirit of God Moved" — The Holy Spirit in Creation

**Genesis 1:2** — "And the Spirit of God moved upon the face of the waters."

- **Job 26:13** — "By his spirit he hath garnished the heavens"
- **Job 33:4** — "The Spirit of God hath made me; and the breath of the Almighty hath given me life"
- **Psalm 104:30** — "Thou sendest forth thy spirit, they are created: and thou renewest the face of the earth"
  - *Interpretation:* The Spirit is the active agent of creation and life throughout Scripture. Genesis 1:2 establishes this from the very beginning — Father (v.1), Spirit (v.2), Word/Son ("God said," v.3) are all present and active in creation.

### "God Said" — Creation by the Spoken Word

**Genesis 1:3, 6, 9, 11, 14, 20, 24, 26** — "And God said..." (10 times in chapter 1)

- **Psalm 33:6, 9** — "By the word of the LORD were the heavens made; and all the host of them by the breath of his mouth... For he spake, and it was done; he commanded, and it stood fast."
  - *Interpretation:* God's Word is not a suggestion — it is creative command. When God speaks, matter obeys.

- **Isaiah 55:11** — "So shall my word be that goeth forth out of my mouth: it shall not return unto me void, but it shall accomplish that which I please"
  - *Interpretation:* The creative principle of Genesis 1 — God speaks and it happens — is stated as God's governing principle throughout all of history.

- **John 1:14** — "And the Word was made flesh, and dwelt among us"
  - *Interpretation:* The same Word that spoke creation into existence in Genesis 1 took on flesh in John 1. The Creator entered His creation.

- **2 Peter 3:5** — "For this they willingly are ignorant of, that by the word of God the heavens were of old, and the earth standing out of the water and in the water"
  - *Interpretation:* Peter directly cites Genesis 1 creation by God's Word as the precedent for God's future intervention in judgment (2 Peter 3:7). The same Word that created will judge.

### "Let Us Make Man" — Plurality in the Godhead

**Genesis 1:26** — "And God said, Let US make man in OUR image, after OUR likeness"

- **Genesis 3:22** — "Behold, the man is become as one of US"
- **Genesis 11:7** — "Go to, let US go down, and there confound their language"
- **Isaiah 6:8** — "Whom shall I send, and who will go for US?" — The plural in the context of the LORD speaking
- **John 1:1-3** — The Word was WITH God and WAS God in the beginning — plurality established
- **Genesis 1:2** — "The Spirit of God moved upon the face of the waters" — Spirit present and active
  - *Interpretation:* The plural pronouns ("us," "our") in Genesis 1:26 are not royal plural (Hebrew has distinct constructions for that) and are not referring to angels (angels do not create, and man is made in GOD'S image, not angels' image — Hebrews 1:5). The consistent Bible testimony is that Father, Son (the Word), and Holy Spirit were all present and active in creation. The Bible interprets its own plural by the testimony of John 1, Colossians 1, and Genesis 1:2.

### "Evening and Morning" — Literal Days

**Genesis 1:5, 8, 13, 19, 23, 31** — "And the evening and the morning were the [first through sixth] day"

- **Exodus 20:11** — "For in six days the LORD made heaven and earth, the sea, and all that in them is, and rested the seventh day: wherefore the LORD blessed the sabbath day, and hallowed it."
  - *Interpretation:* God Himself interprets the creation week as literal days and bases the Sabbath commandment on it. If the days were ages, the Sabbath command has no logic.

- **Exodus 31:17** — "It is a sign between me and the children of Israel for ever: for in six days the LORD made heaven and earth, and on the seventh day he rested, and was refreshed."
  - *Interpretation:* God was "refreshed" on the seventh day — language of rest after completed work, not the closing of a geological epoch.

- **Mark 10:6** — "But from the beginning of the creation God made them male and female" — Jesus places Adam and Eve at the beginning of creation, not at the end of long ages.

### "After His Kind" — Established Boundaries

**Genesis 1:11, 12, 21, 24, 25** — "after his kind" / "after their kind" (10 occurrences in chapter 1)

  - *Interpretation:* Paul's argument for resurrection bodies in 1 Corinthians 15 draws on the Genesis 1 principle — God assigns each creature its own body and kind. The boundaries of kind are built into creation.

- **Revelation 6:12-13** — Sun darkened, moon as blood, stars falling — during the sixth seal

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### BEGINNING (Hebrew: bereshit)

**Genesis 1:1** — "In the BEGINNING God created"

- First word of the Hebrew Bible — Bereshit
- Not "a beginning" (indefinite) but THE beginning — absolute commencement of time, space, and matter
- **John 1:1** — "In the beginning was the Word" — John's parallel is intentional; the Word existed BEFORE the beginning of creation, meaning the Word is eternal
- **Proverbs 8:22-23** — Wisdom (identified with Christ in 1 Corinthians 1:24, 30) was "set up from everlasting, from the beginning, or ever the earth was"
- *Context rule:* "Beginning" in 1 John 1:1 and 2:13-14 refers to the incarnation/ministry of Christ. "Beginning" in Genesis 1:1 and John 1:1 refers to the absolute origin of creation. Context distinguishes.

### WITHOUT FORM AND VOID (Hebrew: tohu wabohu)

**Genesis 1:2** — "And the earth was without form, and void"

- **Tohu** = formlessness, waste, desolation — used in Isaiah 45:18: "he created it not in vain (tohu), he formed it to be inhabited"
- **Bohu** = emptiness, void — only appears 3 times in the Hebrew Bible (Genesis 1:2; Isaiah 34:11; Jeremiah 4:23)
- **Isaiah 34:11** — tohu and bohu used together describing God's judgment on Edom — a land reduced to desolation
- **Jeremiah 4:23** — "I beheld the earth, and, lo, it was without form, and void (tohu wabohu)" — Jeremiah sees a judgment vision using creation language; the reversal of Genesis 1
- *Interpretation:* Tohu wabohu describes an uninhabitable, unstructured state. The six days of creation are God's act of bringing ORDER out of formlessness and FULLNESS out of emptiness. The same language appears in judgment contexts — sin and judgment reverse creation; redemption re-creates.

### DAY (Hebrew: yom)

**Genesis 1:5, 8, 13, 19, 23, 31** — "the evening and the morning were the [numbered] day"

- **In Genesis 1 context: LITERAL 24-hour day**
- **Biblical evidence for literal interpretation:**
  1. "Evening and morning" — bounded by actual twilight and dawn, indicating earth's rotation
  2. Numbered days — Hebrew usage: when yom is preceded by a number ("first day," "second day," etc.) it always means a literal day in the Hebrew Bible
  3. **Exodus 20:11** — God Himself equates the creation week with a literal work-week as the basis for the Sabbath commandment
  4. **Mark 10:6** — Jesus places man's creation at "the beginning of creation," not ages later

- **Other biblical uses of yom (context determines meaning):**
  - "Day of the LORD" = a specific prophetic event/season, not a 24-hour day (Isaiah 13:6; Joel 1:15)
  - "One day is as a thousand years" (2 Peter 3:8) = a prophetic timeline key IN CONTEXT of the Day of the LORD, not a general principle about all uses of "day" (see Key Terms in 2 Peter)
  - "In that day" = a prophetic era or event (Isaiah 2:11, 17, 20)
  - *Rule:* The same word "day" carries different meanings. Context — evening/morning markers, numbering, surrounding text — determines which meaning applies. Genesis 1 context = literal days. This is non-negotiable because God Himself interprets it as literal in Exodus 20:11.

### FIRMAMENT (Hebrew: raqia)

**Genesis 1:6-8** — "And God said, Let there be a firmament in the midst of the waters"

- **Raqia** = an expanse, a stretched-out space — from the root meaning to beat out or spread thin
- **Genesis 1:8** — "God called the firmament Heaven" — the firmament IS what God calls heaven in this context
- **Genesis 1:14-17** — The sun, moon, and stars are placed "in the firmament of the heaven" — therefore the firmament includes the visible sky and space
- **Psalm 19:1** — "The heavens declare the glory of God; and the firmament sheweth his handywork"
- **Psalm 150:1** — "Praise God in his sanctuary: praise him in the firmament of his power"
- **Daniel 12:3** — "And they that be wise shall shine as the brightness of the firmament" — the firmament as a symbol of glory
- *Interpretation:* The firmament is the expanse of sky and space. The "waters above the firmament" (v.7) are distinguished from the seas below. The exact nature of the waters above is defined by Genesis — they exist, they were divided by God, and the firmament was placed between them. Genesis 7:11 ("the windows of heaven were opened") and Genesis 8:2 suggest these upper waters were involved in the flood.

### IMAGE (Hebrew: tselem) / LIKENESS (Hebrew: demuth)

**Genesis 1:26-27** — "Let us make man in our IMAGE (tselem), after our LIKENESS (demuth)"

- **Tselem** = a carved or cut image, a representative likeness — used of idols (Numbers 33:52; Ezekiel 7:20) and of man (Genesis 1:26-27; 5:3; 9:6)
  - The same word used for idols is used for man — because man IS God's image/representative on earth; idolatry is the counterfeit of this truth
- **Demuth** = a resemblance, a pattern — from the root "to be like"
- **Genesis 5:3** — "Adam... begat a son in his own LIKENESS (demuth), after his IMAGE (tselem)" — the same two words used when Adam passes on his nature to Seth; image and likeness are transferable in the physical-spiritual lineage
- **Colossians 1:15** — Christ "is the IMAGE (eikon) of the invisible God" — the ultimate fulfillment of what man was created to be
- **Hebrews 1:3** — Christ is "the express IMAGE (charakter) of his person" — the exact imprint
- **Romans 8:29** — Believers predestined "to be conformed to the IMAGE of his Son"
- **1 Corinthians 15:49** — "And as we have borne the image of the earthy, we shall also bear the image of the heavenly"
- *Interpretation:* Man was created as God's image-bearer and representative on earth. The Fall damaged but did not destroy the image (Genesis 9:6 still bases capital punishment on man being made in God's image). Christ is the perfect image of God. Salvation is the process of being conformed to that image. The trajectory: created in God's image → fallen image → restored image in Christ → fully glorified image in resurrection.

### DOMINION (Hebrew: radah)

**Genesis 1:26, 28** — "let them have DOMINION... have DOMINION"

- **Radah** = to tread down, to rule, to have dominion — used of rulers over subjects (1 Kings 4:24; Psalm 72:8; Isaiah 14:6)
- **Genesis 1:28** — also "subdue it" (kabash = to bring into subjection, to tread down)
- **Psalm 8:4-8** — "What is man, that thou art mindful of him?... Thou madest him to have DOMINION over the works of thy hands; thou hast put all things under his feet"
- **Hebrews 2:6-9** — Quotes Psalm 8 and applies it to Christ: "But now we see not yet all things put under him. But we see Jesus, who was made a little lower than the angels for the suffering of death... that he by the grace of God should taste death for every man"
  - *Interpretation:* Man's dominion mandate was given in Genesis 1:26-28. Man lost that dominion through the Fall (Hebrews 2:8 — "we see not yet all things put under him"). Christ came as the Second Man/Last Adam to reclaim that dominion — first through death and resurrection, finally through the consummation of all things (1 Corinthians 15:25-28; Revelation 11:15).

### MALE AND FEMALE (Hebrew: zakar and neqebah)

**Genesis 1:27** — "male (zakar) and female (neqebah) created he them"

- **Zakar** = the male, he who remembers/is remembered — used consistently of biological maleness in humans and animals
- **Neqebah** = the female, she who is pierced/perforated — anatomical distinction built into the word
- **Matthew 19:4** — Jesus cites this verse: "Have ye not read, that he which made them AT THE BEGINNING made them male and female" — Christ confirms Genesis 1:27 as the permanent, creation-established definition of human sexual differentiation
- **Mark 10:6** — Same citation by Christ
- **Romans 1:26-27** — Paul's argument that same-sex behavior is "against nature" rests on the Genesis 1 creation order — male and female designed for each other
- *Interpretation:* Male and female are not cultural constructs but creation categories established by God before culture existed. Jesus quotes Genesis 1:27 as binding and permanent. This is the Bible's own interpretation of the verse.

### REPLENISH (Hebrew: male)

**Genesis 1:28** — "Be fruitful, and multiply, and REPLENISH the earth"

- **Male** = to fill, to be full — NOT "to refill" as if a prior filling existed
- The KJV "replenish" in 1611 usage meant "to fill" (see also Genesis 9:1 — same command to Noah, same word; Noah was not "refilling" a previously populated earth — he was starting fresh)
- The modern meaning of "replenish" (refill something that was previously full) is NOT the Hebrew meaning and NOT the 1611 KJV meaning
- **Misuse:** The Gap Theory uses "replenish" to argue that Genesis 1:28 implies a previous population that was destroyed — this imposes the modern English meaning onto a word that simply means "fill"
- **Genesis 9:1** — "And God blessed Noah and his sons, and said unto them, Be fruitful, and multiply, and REPLENISH (male) the earth" — same word, clearly means "fill" not "refill" in a cosmic sense

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### "Let There Be Light" — Type of the New Creation in Christ

**Genesis 1:3** — "And God said, Let there be light: and there was light"

- **John 1:1-5** — "In him was life; and the life was the light of men. And the light shineth in darkness; and the darkness comprehended it not."
- **John 8:12** — "I am the light of the world: he that followeth me shall not walk in darkness, but shall have the light of life."
- **2 Corinthians 4:6** — "For God, who commanded the light to shine out of darkness, hath shined in our hearts, to give the light of the knowledge of the glory of God in the face of Jesus Christ."
  - *Interpretation:* Paul directly draws the parallel: the same God who spoke "Let there be light" in Genesis 1 speaks light into the darkness of the human heart through Christ. Physical creation is the type; new creation (2 Corinthians 5:17) is the antitype. First creation began with light; new creation begins with the Light of the world entering the darkened soul.

### Man Made in God's Image — Type of the Second Adam

**Genesis 1:26-27** — Man created in the image of God

- **Romans 5:14** — Adam "is the figure (type) of him that was to come" — the Spirit of God explicitly calls Adam a TYPE of Christ
- **1 Corinthians 15:45-49** — "The first man Adam was made a living soul; the last Adam was made a quickening spirit... The first man is of the earth, earthy: the second man is the Lord from heaven."
- **Colossians 1:15** — Christ "is the image of the invisible God, the firstborn of every creature"
- **Hebrews 1:3** — Christ is "the express image of his person"
- **Romans 8:29** — Believers predestined "to be conformed to the image of his Son"
  - *Interpretation:* Adam was made in God's image — the first expression of that image in creation. He failed. Christ is the perfect, eternal image of God. The trajectory of redemptive history is: creation of the image (Genesis 1) → defacing of the image (Genesis 3) → restoration of the image in Christ (2 Corinthians 3:18) → glorification of the image in resurrection (1 John 3:2). The Bible tells one story about the image of God.

### The Six Days and the Seventh — Type of the Millennial Sabbath

**Genesis 1 — Six days of creation; Genesis 2:2-3 — seventh day of rest**

- **Hebrews 4:3-11** — "There remaineth therefore a rest (sabbatismos) to the people of God." The Sabbath rest of creation points to a coming rest for God's people.
- **2 Peter 3:8** — "But, beloved, be not ignorant of this one thing, that one day is with the Lord as a thousand years, and a thousand years as one day" — IN CONTEXT of the Day of the Lord, this is a prophetic timeline key
- **Psalm 90:4** — "For a thousand years in thy sight are but as yesterday when it is past" — Moses establishes the thousand-year / one-day equivalence in the context of God's eternal perspective
- **Revelation 20:1-6** — A literal thousand-year reign
  - *Interpretation:* The creation week establishes a pattern: six days of work (6,000 years of human history), then a seventh day of rest (the Millennial reign of Christ). This is not outside interpretation — it is the consistent pattern of the Bible's own use of the Sabbath principle, extending it from a weekly rest (Exodus 20) to a Sabbath year (Leviticus 25) to a Millennial rest (Revelation 20 / Hebrews 4). The numbers are the Bible's own.

### "Very Good" — The Original State That Will Be Restored

**Genesis 1:31** — "God saw every thing that he had made, and, behold, it was VERY GOOD"

- **Romans 5:12** — "Wherefore, as by one man sin entered into the world, and death by sin; and so death passed upon all men" — death did NOT exist before the Fall; "very good" = no death
- **Romans 8:20-22** — "For the creature was made subject to vanity, not willingly... For we know that the whole creation groaneth and travaileth in pain together until now" — the current state of creation is NOT very good; it is the result of the Fall
- **Revelation 21:4-5** — "And God shall wipe away all tears from their eyes; and there shall be no more death, neither sorrow, nor crying, neither shall there be any more pain: for the former things are passed away. And he that sat upon the throne said, Behold, I make all things NEW."
  - *Interpretation:* Genesis 1:31 is the standard against which everything else is measured. "Very good" means: no death, no suffering, no entropy, no pain. The Fall broke this. Redemption restores it. Revelation 21-22 is Genesis 1-2 restored. What was very good will be very good again.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED / MISAPPLIED PASSAGES

### "In the Beginning God Created" — Misused to Support the Gap Theory

**The Misuse:** Some teach a "Gap Theory" — that there was a prior creation between Genesis 1:1 and Genesis 1:2 that was destroyed (often connected to Satan's fall), and that Genesis 1:2 describes the ruins of that prior world. They also use "replenish" (v.28) to imply a refilling of something previously full.

**What Scripture says:**

- **Exodus 20:11** — "For in six days the LORD made heaven and earth, THE SEA, AND ALL THAT IN THEM IS" — no prior creation; ALL of it was made in six days
- **Exodus 31:17** — Same statement, repeated by God
- **John 1:3** — "All things were made by him; and without him was not any thing made that was made" — there is no "anything" that predates Genesis 1 creation except God Himself
- **Isaiah 45:18** — "For thus saith the LORD that created the heavens; God himself that formed the earth and made it; he hath established it, he created it NOT IN VAIN (tohu), he formed it to be INHABITED" — this verse is used to argue the Gap Theory (God didn't make it tohu, so if it's tohu in Genesis 1:2 it must have become that way by judgment). But Isaiah 45:18 is saying God's ultimate PURPOSE was not vain/desolation — His purpose was to inhabit it. Genesis 1 is the process of God fulfilling that purpose: moving it FROM tohu (unformed) TO inhabited.
- **Replenish (male) = FILL, not refill** — see Key Terms above. Genesis 9:1 gives Noah the same command — he was not "refilling" a previously populated earth.
- *Correction from Scripture:* The Gap Theory requires reading INTO the text what is not there. The text says God created in six days (Exodus 20:11 — God's own interpretation). The words tohu wabohu describe the STARTING STATE before God formed and filled the earth over six days — not the ruins of a prior creation.

---

### "Let There Be Light" Before the Sun — Misused to Deny Literal Days

**The Misuse:** If the sun wasn't created until Day 4 (v.16), how could there be "evening and morning" on Days 1-3? Therefore the days can't be literal 24-hour days.

**What Scripture says:**

- **Revelation 21:23** — "And the city had no need of the sun, neither of the moon, to shine in it: for the glory of God did lighten it, and the Lamb is the light thereof." — God Himself is a light source independent of the sun. He was the light source for Days 1-3.
- **Revelation 22:5** — "And there shall be no night there; and they need no candle, neither light of the sun; for the Lord God giveth them light" — the eternal state has no sun; God is the light
- *Correction from Scripture:* God did not need the sun to produce the light of Day 1, the rotation that produced evening and morning, or the cycle of Days 1-3. He is the light source (Revelation 21:23; 1 John 1:5 — "God is light"). The sun is God's appointed physical instrument for providing light to the earth — it is not the only source of light in the universe that God rules.

---

### "Male and Female" (v.27) — Misused or Ignored in Gender Debates

**The Misuse:** Some argue that Genesis 1:27 is culturally conditioned, that "male and female" is a spectrum rather than a binary, or that it does not settle modern gender questions.

**What Jesus says:**

- **Matthew 19:4** — "And he answered and said unto them, Have ye not read, that he which made them at the beginning made them MALE AND FEMALE" — Jesus cites Genesis 1:27 as the permanent, creation-established definition, quoting it in response to a question about marriage and divorce. He treats it as still binding and definitive.
- **Mark 10:6** — Same citation
- **Romans 1:26-27** — Paul's case that certain behaviors are "against nature" is grounded in the Genesis 1 creation order of male and female designed for each other
- *Correction from Scripture:* Jesus Himself quotes Genesis 1:27 as binding. The text does not require elaboration — it requires citation. When Jesus uses it to settle a legal question about marriage, He treats it as the final authority. The Bible interprets this verse by using it.

---

### "Let Us Make Man" (v.26) — Misused for Polytheism

**The Misuse:** Some groups (Mormons, others) use "Let US make man in OUR image" to argue that there are multiple gods who created together, or that man can become a god.

**What the rest of Scripture says:**

- **Isaiah 44:6** — "Thus saith the LORD the King of Israel, and his redeemer the LORD of hosts; I am the first, and I am the last; and beside me there is NO GOD."
- **Isaiah 45:5** — "I am the LORD, and there is none else, there is no God beside me"
- **Deuteronomy 6:4** — "Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God is one LORD" — the Shema; foundational declaration of monotheism
- **John 1:1** — "the Word was WITH God, and the Word WAS God" — the plurality is within ONE God, not multiple gods
- *Correction from Scripture:* The plural "us/our" is plurality within one God — the same God who says "I am the first and I am the last, and beside me there is no God" (Isaiah 44:6). The Bible defines its own plurals. John 1 and Colossians 1 explain who the "us" is: Father, Son (Word), and Spirit — one God, not a council of gods.

- [[49. Ephesians/EPHESIANS_05_REBUILT|Ephesians 5:8]] — "For ye were sometimes darkness, but now are ye light in the Lord" — believers described in creation terms

## FIRST MENTION PRINCIPLES

### First Mention of "Beginning" (Bereshit)

**Genesis 1:1** — "In the BEGINNING God created"
- Establishes that God PRECEDES all creation; He is not part of the created order
- **John 1:1** deliberately mirrors this: "In the beginning was the Word" — the Word was there BEFORE the beginning of creation
- The "beginning" of 1 John 1:1; 2:13-14 refers to the beginning of Christ's ministry — context distinguishes. Genesis 1:1's "beginning" is the absolute origin of the created universe.

### First Mention of "Created" (bara)

**Genesis 1:1** — "God CREATED (bara) the heaven and the earth"
- **Bara** = to create out of nothing — used exclusively of God's creative activity; no human ever "bara" in the Hebrew Bible
- Distinct from "asah" (to make/fashion from existing material) and "yatsar" (to form/mold)
- **Isaiah 45:18** — "God himself that formed (yatsar) the earth and made (asah) it; he hath established it, he CREATED (bara) it" — all three words used in one verse; bara is the unique creative act
- First use establishes: only God can create ex nihilo

### First Mention of Light (or)

**Genesis 1:3** — "And God said, Let there be LIGHT"
- Light is the first thing God creates/declares — before the sun exists
- Light is called "good" (v.4) — the first thing called good in Scripture
- Light is separated from darkness — God does the separating
- **John 1:4-9** — Christ is the true Light; this first mention establishes the type
- **1 John 1:5** — "God is light" — God's own nature is connected to the first created thing

### First Mention of "Good" (tov)

**Genesis 1:4** — "And God SAW the light, that it was GOOD"
- First use: God evaluates His creation and pronounces it good
- "Very good" at completion (v.31) — elevated standard for the finished work
- **Romans 5:12** — Death entered through sin, not through creation — "good" means no death was present in original creation
- The word "good" here sets the standard against which the Fall is measured and against which the new creation (Revelation 21-22) is the restoration

### First Mention of "Seed" (zera)

**Genesis 1:11-12** — "the herb yielding SEED... fruit tree yielding fruit after his kind, whose SEED is in itself"
- First occurrence of "seed" in Scripture — established in the botanical context
- **Genesis 3:15** — "I will put enmity between thee and the woman, and between thy SEED and her SEED" — the word immediately becomes loaded with redemptive significance
- **Galatians 3:16** — "He saith not, And to seeds, as of many; but as of one, And to thy seed, which is Christ" — Paul traces the singular "seed" from Genesis 3:15 to Christ
- The seed principle: life is contained within itself, it reproduces after its kind, it carries the full nature of what it came from. The Seed of the woman (Christ) carries the full nature of God in human form.

### First Mention of "Blessed" (barak)

**Genesis 1:22** — "And God BLESSED them, saying, Be fruitful, and multiply"
- First use of "blessed" — God blesses the living creatures He creates
- **Genesis 1:28** — God blesses man with the same command + dominion
- **Genesis 2:3** — God blesses the seventh day
- The first three recipients of blessing in Scripture: living creatures, man, and the Sabbath day
- The pattern of blessing runs from Genesis 1 through Abraham (Genesis 12:2-3) to the New Testament beatitudes and culminates in Revelation 22:14 — "Blessed are they that do his commandments"

### First Mention of "Image" (tselem)

**Genesis 1:26** — "Let us make man in our IMAGE"
- Only man among all creatures is made in God's image
- Establishes the unique dignity of human beings before any law, covenant, or nation exists
- **Genesis 9:6** — The image of God is the basis for prohibiting murder: "for in the image of God made he man" — this applies to ALL humanity after the Fall, not just the redeemed
- The image points forward to Christ, the perfect Image (Colossians 1:15), and to the ultimate conformity of believers to that image (Romans 8:29; 1 Corinthians 15:49)
