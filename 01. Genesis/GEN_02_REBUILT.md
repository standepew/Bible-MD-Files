## CHAPTER SUMMARY

God finished creation on the sixth day and rested on the seventh, blessing and sanctifying it — establishing the Sabbath pattern before any law was given. The LORD God (the first use of the covenant name YHWH combined with Elohim) formed man from the dust of the ground, breathed into his nostrils the breath of life, and man became a living soul. God planted a garden in Eden and placed man there to dress it and keep it. In the garden's midst stood the tree of life and the tree of knowledge of good and evil. God commanded man freely to eat of every tree but one, warning: "In the day that thou eatest thereof thou shalt surely die." God saw that it was not good for man to be alone and declared He would make a help meet for him. Man named all the creatures — none was a suitable companion. God caused a deep sleep to fall upon Adam, took one of his ribs, and from it built a woman and brought her to the man. Adam declared: "This is now bone of my bones and flesh of my flesh." The narrator records the marriage institution: a man shall leave his father and mother, cleave to his wife, and they shall be one flesh. They were naked and not ashamed.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### The Seventh Day Rest — The Sabbath Pattern Established Before Sinai

**Genesis 2:2-3** — "And on the seventh day God ended his work which he had made; and he rested on the seventh day from all his work which he had made. And God blessed the seventh day, and sanctified it: because that in it he had rested from all his work which God created and made."

- **Exodus 20:8-11** — "Remember the sabbath day, to keep it holy... For in six days the LORD made heaven and earth, the sea, and all that in them is, and rested the seventh day: wherefore the LORD blessed the sabbath day, and hallowed it."
  - *Interpretation:* The Sabbath commandment at Sinai is explicitly grounded in Genesis 2:2-3. God's reasoning is: "I did it — you do it." The Sabbath is not an invention of the Law; it is a creation ordinance. The Law restates and codifies what was already established at creation.

- **Exodus 31:17** — "It is a sign between me and the children of Israel for ever: for in six days the LORD made heaven and earth, and on the seventh day he rested, and was refreshed."
  - *Interpretation:* The Sabbath as a "sign" — a mark of covenant relationship. "Refreshed" (Hebrew: naphash — to breathe, to take breath) is connected to the same root as nephesh (soul/breath), echoing Genesis 2:7.

- **Hebrews 4:3-11** — "For we which have believed do enter into rest... There remaineth therefore a rest (sabbatismos) to the people of God. For he that is entered into his rest, he also hath ceased from his own works, as God did from his."
  - *Interpretation:* The seventh-day rest of Genesis 2 points forward to the spiritual rest believers enter through faith — ceasing from dead works (Hebrews 6:1) and resting in Christ's finished work. The Sabbath is a type; Christ's rest is the antitype.

- **Matthew 11:28-29** — "Come unto me, all ye that labour and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest. Take my yoke upon you... and ye shall find rest unto your souls."
  - *Interpretation:* Christ offers "rest unto your souls" — the same rest first modeled in Genesis 2:2-3. He IS the Sabbath rest.

- **Mark 2:27-28** — "The sabbath was made for man, and not man for the sabbath: Therefore the Son of man is Lord also of the sabbath."
  - *Interpretation:* Jesus affirms the Sabbath was created for man's benefit (Genesis 2:3 — "blessed and sanctified" for man). He then declares Himself Lord of it — the One the Sabbath points to.

### The Name Change — LORD God (YHWH Elohim) First Used Here

**Genesis 2:4** — "These are the generations of the heavens and of the earth when they were created, in the day that the LORD God made the earth and the heavens"

- In Genesis 1, God is called "Elohim" (the general word for God — power, might, the Creator God)
- Genesis 2:4 introduces "LORD God" — in Hebrew, YHWH Elohim. This is the FIRST use of the covenant name YHWH (rendered LORD in all-caps in the KJV) combined with Elohim
- **Exodus 3:13-15** — God reveals His name to Moses: "I AM THAT I AM... The LORD God of your fathers, the God of Abraham, the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob... this is my name for ever"
  - *Interpretation:* YHWH is the covenant, relational, self-existent name of God — the name He uses in His personal relationship with His people. Genesis 2 introduces this name precisely as the narrative shifts from the cosmic creation account to the personal, intimate account of man's creation, the garden, and the marriage. Elohim creates; YHWH Elohim forms, breathes, plants, commands, and provides. The shift in name marks the shift in relationship — from Creator to Covenant God.

### Man Formed from Dust — Mortal Without God's Breath

**Genesis 2:7** — "And the LORD God formed man of the dust of the ground, and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life; and man became a living soul."

- **Genesis 3:19** — "For dust thou art, and unto dust shalt thou return" — the verdict of the Fall; the dust-nature of man's body is now confirmed as his destiny apart from God's sustaining life
- **Ecclesiastes 12:7** — "Then shall the dust return to the earth as it was: and the spirit shall return unto God who gave it" — the two components of Genesis 2:7 separated at death: dust returns to earth (body), spirit (God's breath) returns to God
- **Job 33:4** — "The Spirit of God hath made me; and the breath of the Almighty hath given me life" — Job echoes Genesis 2:7
- **Job 10:9** — "Remember, I beseech thee, that thou hast made me as the clay; and wilt thou bring me into dust again?" — Job's lament draws on Genesis 2:7
- **Psalm 103:14** — "For he knoweth our frame; he remembereth that we are dust" — God's compassion grounded in Genesis 2:7
- **1 Corinthians 15:47-49** — "The first man is of the earth, earthy: the second man is the Lord from heaven... as we have borne the image of the earthy, we shall also bear the image of the heavenly"
  - *Interpretation:* The dust-origin of man's body is a permanent biblical fact. Man is not naturally immortal — he depends on God's breath for life. The resurrection is not the natural extension of human immortality; it is God's act of re-breathing life into what returns to dust. The same God who breathed life into dust in Genesis 2:7 will breathe resurrection life at the last day.

### The Tree of Life — Access Lost, Access Restored

**Genesis 2:9** — "The tree of life also in the midst of the garden"

- **Genesis 3:22-24** — Access to the tree of life blocked after sin: "lest he put forth his hand, and take also of the tree of life, and eat, and live for ever" — God mercifully prevents man from living forever in a state of sin
- **Proverbs 3:18** — "She (wisdom) is a tree of life to them that lay hold upon her" — the tree of life becomes a wisdom metaphor
- **Proverbs 11:30** — "The fruit of the righteous is a tree of life"
- **Revelation 2:7** — "To him that overcometh will I give to eat of the tree of life, which is in the midst of the paradise of God" — the same location: in the midst, in paradise
- **Revelation 22:2, 14** — "In the midst of the street of it, and on either side of the river, was there the tree of life, which bare twelve manner of fruits, and yielded her fruit every month: and the leaves of the tree were for the healing of the nations... Blessed are they that do his commandments, that they may have right to the tree of life"
  - *Interpretation:* The tree of life is a consistent, literal symbol throughout Scripture. In Genesis 2 it stands in the midst of the garden — accessible. In Genesis 3 it is blocked. In Revelation 22 it is restored, bearing twelve fruits, with healing leaves, accessible to the redeemed. What the cherubim's sword blocked, the cross of Christ reopens. The trajectory is unbroken from Genesis to Revelation.

### The Death Warning — "Thou Shalt Surely Die"

**Genesis 2:17** — "But of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil, thou shalt not eat of it: for in the day that thou eatest thereof thou shalt surely die."

- **Genesis 3:4** — Satan's counter: "Ye shall not surely die" — the first lie is the direct contradiction of God's warning in Genesis 2:17
- **Genesis 5:5** — "And all the days that Adam lived were nine hundred and thirty years: and he died" — Adam did die; God's word was true
- **Romans 5:12** — "Wherefore, as by one man sin entered into the world, and death by sin; and so death passed upon all men, for that all have sinned"
- **Romans 6:23** — "For the wages of sin is death; but the gift of God is eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord"
- **1 Corinthians 15:22** — "For as in Adam all die, even so in Christ shall all be made alive"
  - *Interpretation:* The death warning of Genesis 2:17 is the foundation of the entire biblical doctrine of sin and death. Death has two dimensions here: spiritual (separation from God — immediate, the day they ate) and physical (the body returning to dust — fulfilled in Genesis 5:5). Both entered on the day of disobedience. The entire redemption story is the answer to Genesis 2:17.

### One Flesh — Marriage Instituted by God

**Genesis 2:24** — "Therefore shall a man leave his father and his mother, and shall cleave unto his wife: and they shall be one flesh."

- **Matthew 19:4-6** — Jesus quotes Genesis 1:27 and 2:24 together: "Have ye not read, that he which made them at the beginning made them male and female... Wherefore they are no more twain, but one flesh. What therefore God hath joined together, let not man put asunder." — Christ uses Genesis 2:24 to define marriage and prohibit easy divorce
- **Mark 10:6-9** — Same citation and conclusion by Christ
- **1 Corinthians 6:16** — Paul quotes Genesis 2:24 to warn against sexual immorality: "Know ye not that he which is joined to an harlot is one body? for two, saith he, shall be one flesh." — the "one flesh" principle applies to any sexual union, not only marriage
- **Ephesians 5:28-32** — Paul quotes Genesis 2:24 and reveals its ultimate meaning: "This is a great mystery: but I speak concerning Christ and the church." — the marriage of man and woman is a type of Christ and the church
  - *Interpretation:* Genesis 2:24 is the Bible's foundational definition of marriage. It is quoted by Christ and by Paul. It establishes: (1) one man and one woman; (2) leaving previous family loyalty; (3) cleaving — permanent covenant bond; (4) one flesh — physical, legal, and spiritual union. Paul reveals the deeper mystery: this was always pointing to Christ and the church. Adam is the type; Christ is the antitype. Eve is the type; the church is the antitype.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### LORD GOD (Hebrew: YHWH Elohim)

**Genesis 2:4** — First use of the combined covenant name

- **Elohim** (Genesis 1) = the general word for God; used of the all-powerful Creator; plural in form, singular in usage; can refer to pagan gods in other contexts (context determines)
- **YHWH** (LORD in KJV all-caps) = the personal, covenant, self-existent name of God; derived from the Hebrew verb "to be" — "I AM THAT I AM" (Exodus 3:14); never used of pagan gods
- **YHWH Elohim** = the covenant God who is also the all-powerful Creator; the name used throughout the intimate personal narrative of Genesis 2-3
- **Exodus 3:13-15** — God declares this is His name "for ever... unto all generations"
- **Psalm 83:18** — "That men may know that thou, whose name alone is JEHOVAH (YHWH), art the most high over all the earth"
- *Context rule:* In English Bibles, "LORD" in small capitals = YHWH. "Lord" in mixed case = Adonai (master, lord). "God" = Elohim. "God" followed by a personal name or covenant reference = often YHWH Elohim. The distinction matters — YHWH is the relational, covenant name; Elohim is the power/authority name.

### BREATH OF LIFE (Hebrew: nishmat chayyim)

**Genesis 2:7** — "breathed into his nostrils the breath (nishmah) of life (chayyim)"

- **Nishmah** = breath, the spirit of God breathed directly into man — distinct from the general "breath" of living creatures
- **Genesis 7:22** — "All in whose nostrils was the breath (nishmah) of life" — used of humans and creatures in the flood; indicates the life-principle
- **Job 33:4** — "The breath (nishmah) of the Almighty hath given me life"
- **Job 32:8** — "But there is a spirit (ruach) in man: and the inspiration (nishmah) of the Almighty giveth them understanding"
- **Isaiah 2:22** — "Cease ye from man, whose breath (nishmah) is in his nostrils: for wherein is he to be accounted of?" — man's life is in his nostrils; remove God's breath and man is nothing
- *Interpretation:* The breath that God breathed into Adam is uniquely described as coming directly from God — not commanded into existence as the rest of creation was ("God said") but personally breathed. This is God's own life-principle imparted directly. It is this that makes man a living soul, capable of knowing God, capable of moral choice, capable of fellowship. Animals breathe but God did not personally breathe into them.

### LIVING SOUL (Hebrew: nephesh chayyah)

**Genesis 2:7** — "man became a living soul (nephesh chayyah)"

- **Nephesh** = soul, life, person, self, creature — the most common word for the inner life in the Hebrew Bible; used over 750 times
- **Genesis 1:20, 21, 24** — Animals also called "living creature" (nephesh chayyah) — the same phrase used of man in Genesis 2:7
- *Critical distinction:* What makes man unique is NOT having a nephesh (animals share this) but being made in GOD'S IMAGE (Genesis 1:26-27) and receiving God's direct breath (nishmah). Both distinctions matter for correct anthropology.
- **Different uses of nephesh in Scripture:**
  - Living person: Genesis 2:7 — man IS a living soul (not has a soul)
  - Life/blood: Leviticus 17:11 — "the life (nephesh) of the flesh is in the blood"
  - Person: Numbers 5:6 — "When a man or woman shall commit any sin... against a person (nephesh)"
  - Desire/appetite: Proverbs 13:25 — "The righteous eateth to the satisfying of his soul (nephesh)"
  - Dead body: Numbers 6:6 — "He shall come at no dead body (nephesh meth)" — a dead nephesh
- *Context rule:* Nephesh does not always mean the immortal soul in the way later theology uses the term. Context must determine whether it refers to a living person, a dead body, life-in-the-blood, or inner desire. Genesis 2:7 means man became a living person/being — the union of dust + God's breath = nephesh chayyah.

### SURELY DIE (Hebrew: moth tamuth)

**Genesis 2:17** — "thou shalt surely die" (moth tamuth — "dying thou shalt die")

- **Hebrew intensification:** The doubling of the verb (moth tamuth = "dying you shall die") is the Hebrew way of expressing absolute certainty — it is emphatic; it cannot be made "not happen"
- **Genesis 3:4** — Satan directly contradicts this: "Ye shall NOT surely die (lo moth tamuthun)" — he uses the same doubled construction in the negative. The first lie is a direct assault on the most emphatic statement God made.
- **Romans 6:23** — "The wages of sin is death" — Genesis 2:17 is the first statement of this law
- **Two dimensions of the death:**
  - **Spiritual death (immediate):** Separation from God — happens the day they eat (Genesis 3:8 — they hide from God; Genesis 3:23 — driven from the garden)
  - **Physical death (eventual):** The body returning to dust — "unto dust shalt thou return" (Genesis 3:19); fulfilled in Genesis 5:5 ("and he died")
- **Ephesians 2:1** — "And you hath he quickened, who were dead in trespasses and sins" — spiritual death described; they were physically alive but spiritually dead
- **John 11:25-26** — "I am the resurrection, and the life: he that believeth in me, though he were dead (physical), yet shall he live: And whosoever liveth and believeth in me shall never die (spiritual)" — Christ reverses both dimensions of the Genesis 2:17 death

### HELP MEET (Hebrew: ezer kenegdo)

**Genesis 2:18, 20** — "I will make him an help meet (ezer kenegdo) for him"

- **Ezer** = help, helper — used 21 times in the Hebrew Bible; of those 21 uses, 16 refer to GOD as man's helper
  - **Psalm 121:2** — "My help (ezer) cometh from the LORD" — same word used of God
  - **Psalm 115:9-11** — "O Israel, trust thou in the LORD: he is their help (ezer) and their shield"
  - **Deuteronomy 33:7** — "Be thou an help (ezer) to him from his enemies" — Moses' blessing of Judah
  - *Interpretation:* Ezer is not a word of weakness or inferiority — it is a word of essential, powerful, indispensable assistance. When it is used of God, it means the all-powerful God helping those who cannot help themselves. When applied to woman, it carries the same meaning: essential, powerful, indispensable.
- **Kenegdo** = corresponding to him, opposite to him, facing him — from the root "in front of," "over against"; denotes one who is his counterpart, his equal-and-complement, one who faces him
- *Combined meaning:* "A helper corresponding to him" — not a servant, not a subordinate, but a counterpart of equal dignity who supplies what he lacks and faces him as his complement.
- **1 Corinthians 11:9** — "Neither was the man created for the woman; but the woman for the man" — Paul draws on Genesis 2 for the order of creation; order ≠ inferiority (the same chapter states man's head is Christ, and Christ's head is God — 1 Corinthians 11:3 — yet Christ is not inferior to God, John 10:30)

### RIB (Hebrew: tsela)

**Genesis 2:21-22** — "he took one of his ribs (tsela), and closed up the flesh instead thereof; And the rib (tsela), which the LORD God had taken from man, made he a woman"

- **Tsela** = side, rib, chamber, board — used 41 times in the Hebrew Bible; in most uses outside Genesis 2, it means "side" (of the ark — Exodus 25:12; of a building — 1 Kings 6:5; of a hill — 2 Samuel 16:13)
- Some scholars note that tsela may mean "side" rather than a single rib — suggesting God took a portion of Adam's side (not just a rib bone) to build (Hebrew: banah — to build, construct) the woman
- **The key point confirmed by the text:** Woman is taken FROM man — she is bone of his bone, flesh of his flesh (v.23). She shares his substance, his nature, his material. They are one.
- **Typological significance:** Woman is built from the side of the sleeping/death-like Adam — type of the church built from the pierced side of the dying Christ (John 19:34; Ephesians 5:30)

### ONE FLESH (Hebrew: basar echad)

**Genesis 2:24** — "and they shall be one flesh (basar echad)"

- **Basar** = flesh, body, the physical person
- **Echad** = one — the same word used in Deuteronomy 6:4 ("The LORD our God is one LORD") — a unity that contains plurality; echad is a compound unity, not absolute singularity (yachid would be absolute singularity)
- **Matthew 19:5-6** — Jesus quotes this and says "they are no more twain, but one flesh" — two persons becoming one; a genuine, not merely symbolic, union
- **1 Corinthians 6:16-17** — "He which is joined to an harlot is one body? for two, saith he, shall be one flesh... But he that is joined unto the Lord is one spirit" — the one-flesh principle applies to sexual union; the spiritual parallel is union with Christ being "one spirit"
- **Ephesians 5:30-32** — "For we are members of his body, of his flesh, and of his bones. For this cause shall a man leave his father and mother... This is a great mystery: but I speak concerning Christ and the church" — Paul's explanation: Genesis 2:24 is the type; Christ and the church are the antitype. The one-flesh mystery was always pointing here.

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The Seventh Day — Type of the Millennial and Eternal Rest

**Genesis 2:2-3** — God rested on the seventh day, blessed it, sanctified it

- **Hebrews 4:3-11** — The "rest" of Genesis 2 points to the believer's spiritual rest now (entering by faith) AND a future rest that "remaineth for the people of God"
- **Psalm 95:11** — "Unto whom I sware in my wrath that they should not enter into my rest" — God's rest as a destination that can be entered or forfeited
- **Revelation 14:13** — "Blessed are the dead which die in the Lord from henceforth: Yea, saith the Spirit, that they may rest from their labours"
- **Revelation 20:4-6** — The millennial reign: a thousand-year rest following six thousand years of human labor — the pattern of six days + one day of rest projected onto history's full week
- *Interpretation:* The seventh-day rest of Genesis 2 is a prophetic pattern. Six days of creation work → seventh day of rest. Six thousand years of human history (under sin and labor) → a seventh thousand-year rest (the Millennium). The weekly Sabbath is the recurring memorial and sign of this larger pattern. Christ's "Come unto me and I will give you rest" (Matthew 11:28) is the personal dimension; the Millennium (Revelation 20) is the cosmic dimension. Both are grounded in Genesis 2:2-3.

---

### Adam's Deep Sleep — Type of Christ's Death and the Birth of the Church

**Genesis 2:21** — "And the LORD God caused a deep sleep (tardemah) to fall upon Adam"

- **Tardemah** = deep sleep — used in Genesis 15:12 of Abraham's deep sleep during the covenant ratification; used in 1 Samuel 26:12 of Saul's deep sleep; Isaiah 29:10 uses it of a spirit of spiritual stupor
- The woman is built from Adam's opened side while he is in this death-like sleep
- **John 19:34** — "But one of the soldiers with a spear pierced his side, and forthwith came there out blood and water" — Christ's side opened at His death on the cross
- **Ephesians 5:25-32** — Christ "loved the church, and gave himself for it... This is a great mystery: but I speak concerning Christ and the church"
- **Ephesians 5:30** — "For we are members of his body, of his flesh, and of his bones" — directly echoes Adam's declaration in Genesis 2:23 ("bone of my bones, and flesh of my flesh")
  - *Interpretation:* Adam's deep sleep → side opened → woman taken from his side → woman presented to Adam → Adam declares "bone of my bones." Christ's death → side opened (blood and water = redemption and cleansing) → the church built from His sacrifice → the church presented to Christ (Ephesians 5:27; Revelation 19:7-9) → the Bridegroom receives His Bride. The type in Genesis 2 maps exactly onto the antitype in the New Testament. This is not allegory — Paul explicitly says so in Ephesians 5:31-32.

---

### The Naming of the Animals — Adam's Dominion and the Search

**Genesis 2:19-20** — "Whatsoever Adam called every living creature, that was the name thereof... but for Adam there was not found an help meet for him."

- **Genesis 1:26-28** — Dominion mandate: the naming of creatures by Adam is an exercise of the dominion God gave him
- **Psalm 8:6-8** — "Thou madest him to have dominion over the works of thy hands; thou hast put all things under his feet"
- *The purposeful search:* The naming exercise is also God's way of making Adam aware of what he lacks. He sees pairs, kinds, creatures — and none corresponds to him. The awareness of his aloneness is cultivated before the solution is given.
- *Typological thread:* This search for a suitable counterpart foreshadows the entire redemptive story: Christ searching for a bride worthy of Him — and finding none among fallen humanity (Romans 3:10-12 — "There is none righteous, no, not one"). So He creates the bride Himself, at the cost of His own side (John 19:34; Ephesians 5:25-27). The only suitable bride is the one He makes.

---

### "Not Good That Man Should Be Alone" — The Only "Not Good" Before the Fall

**Genesis 2:18** — "And the LORD God said, It is not good that the man should be alone"

- Everything in Genesis 1 was "good" or "very good." This is the ONLY "not good" before sin enters.
- Man, made in the image of the triune God (who is Himself relational — Father, Son, Spirit in eternal fellowship), was made for fellowship. Aloneness is incompatible with the image of a relational God.
- **Ecclesiastes 4:9-10** — "Two are better than one... For if they fall, the one will lift up his fellow: but woe to him that is alone when he falleth"
- **John 17:11, 21-23** — Christ's prayer for believers to be one, "as we are one" — the divine fellowship is the model; the church is its expression
- **Hebrews 10:25** — "Not forsaking the assembling of ourselves together" — the command against willful isolation is grounded in Genesis 2:18: aloneness is not good by God's own declaration

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED / MISAPPLIED PASSAGES

### "In the Day That Thou Eatest Thereof Thou Shalt Surely Die" — Was God Wrong? (v.17)

**The Misuse:** Critics and skeptics (and often the same argument found in Genesis 3:4 — "Ye shall not surely die") note that Adam did not die immediately on the day he ate the fruit. Adam lived 930 more years (Genesis 5:5). Was God's warning false?

**What Scripture says:**

- **Ephesians 2:1** — "And you hath he quickened, who were dead in trespasses and sins" — Paul describes unsaved people as already dead spiritually while physically alive; spiritual death is real
- **Genesis 3:8** — The very day they ate, they hid from God — the relationship was broken immediately; this IS the death of their fellowship with God, the spiritual dimension
- **Genesis 3:23** — They were driven from the garden immediately — separation from the tree of life and from God's presence is the enacted spiritual death
- **Romans 5:12** — "Death passed upon all men" — this refers to the condition of ALL humanity from that point forward, not just Adam's eventual physical death
- **John 5:24** — "He that heareth my word, and believeth on him that sent me, hath everlasting life, and shall not come into condemnation; but is passed from death unto life" — Christ reverses the spiritual death of Genesis 2:17; you pass from death (spiritual, Genesis 2:17 condition) to life (John 5:24) through faith
- *Correction from Scripture:* "Surely die" (moth tamuth) covers both dimensions: spiritual death was immediate (separation from God, driven from the garden); physical death was set in motion (returned to dust — Genesis 3:19; fulfilled — Genesis 5:5). God was not wrong. The Bible defines death as more than physical cessation. Satan's "ye shall not surely die" was the lie — and every person born since has proven God right by dying.

---

### Genesis 2 "Contradicts" Genesis 1 — Two Different Creation Accounts?

**The Misuse:** Scholars using the Documentary Hypothesis claim Genesis 1 and Genesis 2 are contradictory creation accounts from different authors (the "J" and "P" sources). They point to apparent differences: order of creation, the name of God (Elohim vs. YHWH Elohim), vegetation created before man in Genesis 1 but apparently after in Genesis 2.

**What Scripture says:**

- **Genesis 2:4** — "These are the GENERATIONS (toledot) of the heavens and of the earth WHEN THEY WERE CREATED" — this is an explicit recap heading. Genesis uses "toledot" (generations/account of) throughout to introduce a closer look at what was previously summarized (see Genesis 5:1; 6:9; 10:1; etc.)
- **Genesis 2 is a zoom-in, not a contradiction:** Chapter 1 gives the panoramic account of all six days. Chapter 2 zooms in on Day 6 and the garden, providing detail on man's creation, environment, vocation, and marriage that chapter 1 only summarized.
- **The name change** (Elohim → YHWH Elohim) is not evidence of different authors but of a deliberate theological shift: chapter 1 introduces God as the cosmic Creator (Elohim); chapter 2 introduces Him in His covenant, relational character (YHWH) as He tends personally to man.
- **Matthew 19:4-5** — Jesus quotes BOTH Genesis 1:27 AND Genesis 2:24 in the same breath as one unified creation account, with no sense of contradiction. If there were a contradiction, the Son of God would have noticed.
- *Correction from Scripture:* The Bible treats Genesis 1 and 2 as one unified account. Jesus uses both chapters. The toledot structure of Genesis is its own explanation — chapter 2 is the detailed account (toledot) of what chapter 1 summarized.

---

### "Help Meet" — Misused to Diminish or to Over-Correct

**The Misuse (Diminishing):** Some use "help meet" to argue that woman is merely a servant or assistant — subordinate in nature and dignity, created to serve man's needs.

**The Misuse (Over-Correcting):** Others, reacting against the above, argue "help meet" means woman is man's equal in every role and that there is no God-ordained distinction between male and female function.

**What Scripture says:**

- **Ezer** (help) is used 16 out of 21 times in the Hebrew Bible to describe GOD as man's helper (Psalm 121:2; Psalm 115:9-11). If ezer implied inferiority, then God is inferior to man when He helps. The word carries no connotation of inferiority.
- **Kenegdo** (corresponding to him, facing him) establishes her as his counterpart — same substance (bone of his bone, flesh of his flesh, v.23), facing him, equal in nature
- **1 Corinthians 11:3, 8-9** — Paul draws on Genesis 2 for the creation order: man is the head of woman as Christ is the head of man, and God is the head of Christ. This is an order of FUNCTION, not of nature or dignity. Christ is not inferior to the Father in nature (John 10:30 — "I and my Father are one") but functions under the Father's headship (John 14:28). The same logic applies.
- *Correction from Scripture:* Woman is ezer — powerful, essential, and indispensable. She is kenegdo — his counterpart and equal in dignity. She is taken from his side — not from under his foot (to be trampled) or from his head (to rule him) but from beside him, to stand with him. The order of creation (man first, woman built from him) establishes function, not worth. Both are equally the image of God (Genesis 1:27).

---

### "They Were Naked and Not Ashamed" — Misused to Justify Nudism or Dismiss Modesty

**The Misuse:** Some use Genesis 2:25 to argue that nudity is inherently innocent and that clothing/modesty standards are artificial social constructs.

**What Scripture says:**

- **Genesis 2:25** — "not ashamed" is the condition of innocence BEFORE the Fall — no sin, no knowledge of evil, complete moral purity
- **Genesis 3:7** — "And the eyes of them both were opened, and they knew that they were naked; and they sewed fig leaves together" — the shame came with the knowledge of sin; it is the accurate perception of a fallen condition, not a cultural invention
- **Genesis 3:21** — God Himself clothed them: "Unto Adam also and to his wife did the LORD God make coats of skins, and clothed them" — God validates and provides the covering, not the nakedness
- **Isaiah 47:3; Nahum 3:5** — Nakedness in Scripture is consistently associated with shame, defeat, and judgment when it refers to the fallen state
- **Revelation 3:17-18** — "Thou art... naked" used as a picture of spiritual poverty; Christ offers "white raiment, that thou mayest be clothed, and that the shame of thy nakedness do not appear"
- *Correction from Scripture:* "Not ashamed" in Genesis 2:25 is the condition of perfect innocence — a condition that ended in Genesis 3:7. It cannot be used to justify behavior in the post-Fall world. The same God who created them naked provided clothing after the Fall. Modesty is God's answer to fallen nakedness (Genesis 3:21), not man's invention.

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### The Tree of Life — From Garden to New Jerusalem

**Genesis 2:9** — Tree of life in the midst of the garden

**Thread through all of Scripture:**
- **Genesis 3:22-24** — Access blocked by cherubim and flaming sword after the Fall
- **Proverbs 3:18** — Wisdom is "a tree of life to them that lay hold upon her" — the tree becomes a wisdom metaphor; Christ, who IS the wisdom of God (1 Corinthians 1:24), IS the tree of life
- **Proverbs 11:30** — "The fruit of the righteous is a tree of life" — righteousness (which is through Christ) produces tree-of-life fruit
- **Revelation 2:7** — "To him that overcometh will I give to eat of the tree of life, which is in the midst of the paradise of God" — same location: in the midst; restored to the overcomer
- **Revelation 22:2, 14** — Tree of life in the new Jerusalem, bearing twelve fruits, healing for the nations; access granted to those who have washed their robes
  - *Interpretation:* The trajectory of the tree of life is the trajectory of salvation history: given (Genesis 2) → forfeited through sin (Genesis 3) → promised to overcomers (Revelation 2:7) → fully restored in new creation (Revelation 22). Christ is the tree of life — He IS eternal life (John 14:6; 1 John 5:12). Access to Him is access to the tree. The cherubim's flaming sword blocked the way to life; Christ's cross opened it (Hebrews 10:19-20).

---

### Dust — The Humility of Human Origin and the Hope of Resurrection

**Genesis 2:7** — "formed man of the dust of the ground"  
**Genesis 3:19** — "for dust thou art, and unto dust shalt thou return"

**Thread through Scripture:**
- **Psalm 103:14** — "He knoweth our frame; he remembereth that we are dust" — God's compassion grounded in our dust-origin
- **Job 10:9** — "Remember, I beseech thee, that thou hast made me as the clay; and wilt thou bring me into dust again?"
- **Ecclesiastes 3:20** — "All go unto one place; all are of the dust, and all turn to dust again"
- **Isaiah 26:19** — "Thy dead men shall live, together with my dead body shall they arise. Awake and sing, ye that dwell in dust" — resurrection language drawn from the dust of Genesis 2:7
- **Daniel 12:2** — "And many of them that sleep in the dust of the earth shall awake" — the resurrection draws on the dust imagery of Genesis 2
- **John 5:28-29** — "The hour is coming, in the which all that are in the graves shall hear his voice, And shall come forth" — the same God who breathed life into dust will breathe resurrection life into the dust of graves
- **1 Corinthians 15:47-49** — "The first man is of the earth, earthy... as we have borne the image of the earthy, we shall also bear the image of the heavenly"
  - *Interpretation:* The dust of Genesis 2:7 is not a statement of worthlessness but of origin and dependence. Man is dust animated by God's breath. At death he returns to dust. At resurrection he rises from dust by God's power — the same power that first breathed life into dust. The resurrection is the final answer to Genesis 2:7.

---

### The Deep Sleep (Tardemah) — Surrender Before Transformation

**Genesis 2:21** — "the LORD God caused a deep sleep (tardemah) to fall upon Adam"

**Thread through Scripture:**
- **Genesis 15:12** — Tardemah falls on Abraham during the covenant ratification: "And when the sun was going down, a deep sleep fell upon Abram; and, lo, an horror of great darkness fell upon him" — Abraham sleeps while God alone passes through the covenant sacrifice; man is passive, God is active
- **1 Samuel 26:12** — Tardemah from the LORD causes Saul's camp to sleep deeply; God protects David
- **Isaiah 29:10** — "For the LORD hath poured out upon you the spirit of deep sleep (tardemah)" — here used of spiritual stupor in judgment
  - *Interpretation:* Tardemah in Genesis 2:21 is the deep sleep in which the man is passive and God is active — from this sleep comes the greatest gift: the woman. The pattern runs through Abraham's sleep (passive while God establishes the covenant) to the type of Christ's death (passive in death while the Father works the resurrection and the church is born from His side). The greatest works of God are accomplished while man sleeps — while man is passive and surrendered. Striving cannot produce what surrender receives.

---

### Naked and Not Ashamed → Clothed by God — The Arc of Covering

**Genesis 2:25** — "They were both naked, the man and his wife, and were not ashamed"  
**Genesis 3:7** — They sew fig leaves  
**Genesis 3:21** — God provides coats of skins

**Thread through Scripture:**
- **Isaiah 61:10** — "He hath clothed me with the garments of salvation, he hath covered me with the robe of righteousness" — spiritual clothing as the solution to Genesis 3 shame
- **Zechariah 3:3-5** — Joshua the high priest in "filthy garments" (shame/sin) before God; God commands: "Take away the filthy garments from him... I will clothe thee with change of raiment" — the same pattern: shame removed, clean clothing provided by God
- **Revelation 3:18** — Christ offers "white raiment, that thou mayest be clothed, and that the shame of thy nakedness do not appear"
- **Revelation 19:7-8** — The bride of Christ arrayed in "fine linen, clean and white: for the fine linen is the righteousness of saints"
- **Romans 13:14** — "Put ye on the Lord Jesus Christ" — being clothed with Christ as the answer to nakedness
  - *Interpretation:* The arc runs from Genesis 2:25 (innocent nakedness) → Genesis 3:7 (shameful nakedness, self-covered with fig leaves — man's inadequate attempt) → Genesis 3:21 (God covers them with skins — requiring death, a blood sacrifice) → Revelation 19:8 (the redeemed clothed in white, the righteousness of Christ). Man's coverings are always inadequate (fig leaves / self-righteousness). God's covering always involves blood (coats of skins / the cross). The arc of covering is the arc of redemption.

## FIRST MENTION PRINCIPLES

### First Mention of the Sabbath / Seventh Day Rest

**Genesis 2:2-3** — God rests, blesses, and sanctifies the seventh day
- The Sabbath exists BEFORE the Law, BEFORE Israel, BEFORE any covenant with man — it is a creation ordinance
- God BLESSED it (set it apart for blessing) and SANCTIFIED it (made it holy) — two actions establishing its sacred character
- **Exodus 20:11** — God's own explanation: "wherefore the LORD blessed the sabbath day, and hallowed it" — restating Genesis 2:3
- First mention establishes: the Sabbath belongs to God, is given to man for blessing, and is part of the creation order — not an invention of religion

### First Mention of YHWH (LORD) — The Covenant Name

**Genesis 2:4** — "the LORD God (YHWH Elohim) made the earth and the heavens"
- The personal, covenant name of God appears here for the first time
- It appears precisely when the narrative shifts to God's personal relationship with man
- **Exodus 3:14-15** — YHWH defined: "I AM THAT I AM... this is my name for ever"
- First mention establishes: God is not merely the distant Creator (Elohim of Genesis 1) but the personal, covenant, self-existent God who relates to His creatures

### First Mention of "Not Good"

**Genesis 2:18** — "It is not good that the man should be alone"
- The only "not good" before the Fall in all of Scripture's creation account
- First mention establishes: loneliness/aloneness is not God's design for man; fellowship is a creation need, not a weakness
- This "not good" is resolved by God, not by man — God provides the solution; man does not solve his own aloneness

### First Mention of Death (as Warning)

**Genesis 2:17** — "thou shalt surely die"
- First occurrence of death/dying in Scripture — as a warning, before it is a reality
- Death is introduced as the consequence of disobedience — not as a natural part of creation
- **Romans 6:23** — "the wages of sin is death" is the New Testament restatement of Genesis 2:17
- First mention establishes: death is NOT natural, NOT God's original design, NOT inevitable by nature — it is the consequence of a specific moral choice

### First Mention of Marriage

**Genesis 2:24** — "Therefore shall a man leave his father and his mother, and shall cleave unto his wife: and they shall be one flesh"
- The first institution ordained by God in Scripture — before worship, before sacrifice, before any religious rite
- Defined at creation: one man (ish), one woman (ishshah), permanent bond (cleave), physical/spiritual union (one flesh)
- **Matthew 19:4-6** — Jesus quotes this as the permanent, non-negotiable definition
- First mention establishes: marriage is a creation ordinance belonging to God's original design; it is not a cultural institution that can be redefined by culture

### First Mention of "Leave and Cleave"

**Genesis 2:24** — "shall a man leave his father and his mother, and shall cleave unto his wife"
- **Azab** (leave) = to leave, forsake, abandon — a strong word indicating that the marriage bond supersedes the family-of-origin bond
- **Dabaq** (cleave) = to cling, hold fast, be joined — the same word used of Israel "cleaving" to God (Deuteronomy 10:20; Joshua 23:8); of Ruth cleaving to Naomi (Ruth 1:14)
- First mention establishes: the marriage covenant creates a new primary family unit; it is a reordering of loyalties, not an addition to them
