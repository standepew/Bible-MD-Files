## CHAPTER SUMMARY

The serpent — identified by Scripture as Satan himself (Revelation 12:9; 20:2) — approaches the woman with a three-part strategy: question God's Word ("Hath God said?"), deny God's warning ("Ye shall not surely die"), and accuse God of withholding ("Ye shall be as gods"). The woman, seeing the tree was good for food, pleasant to the eyes, and desired to make one wise, eats and gives to her husband who eats with her. Their eyes are opened; they know they are naked, sew fig leaves for covering, and hide. God calls Adam: "Where art thou?" — the first question God asks fallen man. Adam blames the woman; the woman blames the serpent. God curses the serpent and delivers the proto-evangelium: enmity between the serpent's seed and the woman's seed — "it shall bruise thy head, and thou shalt bruise his heel." The woman receives sorrow in childbirth; the man receives a cursed ground, toil, and the verdict "dust thou art, and unto dust shalt thou return." Adam names his wife Eve (Chavvah — mother of all living). The LORD God makes coats of skins — requiring the first death, the first blood sacrifice — and clothes them. Man is driven from the garden; cherubim and a flaming sword guard the way to the tree of life.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### The Serpent — Identity Confirmed by Scripture

**Genesis 3:1** — "Now the serpent was more subtil than any beast of the field which the LORD God had made."

- **Revelation 12:9** — "And the great dragon was cast out, that old serpent, called the Devil, and Satan, which deceiveth the whole world: he was cast out into the earth, and his angels were cast out with him."
  - *Interpretation:* Scripture itself identifies the serpent. "That old serpent" is an explicit back-reference to Genesis 3. The identity is not guesswork — Revelation provides the caption.

- **Revelation 20:2** — "And he laid hold on the dragon, that old serpent, which is the Devil, and Satan, and bound him a thousand years."
  - *Interpretation:* Repeated identification. The serpent of Genesis 3 = the dragon of Revelation = the Devil = Satan. The Bible uses all four names together.

- **John 8:44** — Jesus says: "Ye are of your father the devil, and the lusts of your father ye will do. He was a murderer from the beginning, and abode not in the truth, because there is no truth in him. When he speaketh a lie, he speaketh of his own: for he is a liar, and the father of it."
  - *Interpretation:* "Murderer from the beginning" = Genesis 3 (where death entered). "Father of lies" = Genesis 3:4 ("Ye shall not surely die" — the first lie). Jesus traces Satan's essential character back to Genesis 3.

- **2 Corinthians 11:3** — "But I fear, lest by any means, as the serpent beguiled Eve through his subtilty, so your minds should be corrupted from the simplicity that is in Christ."
  - *Interpretation:* Paul refers to Genesis 3 as a real, historical event and uses it as a warning for the church. The same tactics — subtlety, corruption of God's word, promised wisdom — are still in operation.

- **2 Corinthians 11:14** — "And no marvel; for Satan himself is transformed into an angel of light."
  - *Interpretation:* The serpent appeared as a creature among creatures, not obviously as the enemy. His most effective deceptions always come from within — not from an obviously hostile outside.

### Satan's Three-Part Strategy — Consistent From Genesis to Revelation

**Genesis 3:1-5** — The progression of the attack:

1. **Question the Word:** "Yea, HATH GOD SAID?" (v.1) — casting doubt on whether God actually said it
2. **Deny the Word:** "Ye shall NOT SURELY DIE" (v.4) — direct contradiction of God's warning
3. **Slander God's character:** "For God DOTH KNOW... ye shall be as gods" (v.5) — accusing God of withholding good, of being a rival rather than a Father

**This three-part pattern appears throughout Scripture:**

- **Matthew 4:3-10** — Satan tempts Jesus with the same pattern: "IF thou be the Son of God" (questioning His identity/the Father's declaration at baptism) → quoting Scripture out of context → offering all kingdoms
- **Job 1:9-11** — Satan slanders God to God's face: "Doth Job fear God for nought?... put forth thine hand now, and touch all that he hath, and he will curse thee to thy face" — accusing God's relationship with His servant of being transactional
- **Revelation 12:10** — "The accuser of our brethren is cast down, which accused them before our God day and night" — Satan's primary activity is accusation and slander of God's character and of God's people
- *Interpretation:* The strategy has not changed in six thousand years. "Hath God said?" is still the primary attack on Scripture. "Ye shall not surely die" is still the primary denial of sin's consequences. "God is withholding good from you" is still the primary slander of God's character. The AI should recognize these three moves whenever they appear in current events, culture, or media.

### The Three-Fold Temptation — Matching John's Taxonomy

**Genesis 3:6** — "The woman saw that the tree was (1) good for food, and that it was (2) pleasant to the eyes, and (3) a tree to be desired to make one wise"

- **1 John 2:16** — "For all that is in the world, (1) the lust of the flesh, and (2) the lust of the eyes, and (3) the pride of life, is not of the Father, but is of the world."
  - *Interpretation:* John's taxonomy in 1 John 2:16 maps exactly onto Genesis 3:6. This is John defining the three channels of all temptation by reference to the original temptation. "Good for food" = lust of the flesh; "pleasant to the eyes" = lust of the eyes; "desired to make one wise" = pride of life (the desire to self-elevate through knowledge).

- **Matthew 4:1-11** — Jesus is tempted in all three areas and overcomes each with Scripture:
  - "Command these stones be made bread" (v.3) = lust of the flesh → "Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word of God"
  - "All these kingdoms will I give thee" (v.8-9) = lust of the eyes → "Thou shalt worship the Lord thy God, and him only shalt thou serve"
  - "Cast thyself down: for he shall give his angels charge concerning thee" (v.6) = pride of life (testing God to prove His care) → "Thou shalt not tempt the Lord thy God"
  - *Interpretation:* The Last Adam (Christ) is tempted in the same three channels as the first Adam — and overcomes. Where Adam failed using human reasoning, Christ overcame using Scripture. The answer to Genesis 3:6 is Matthew 4 — written Word wielded in faith.

- **Hebrews 4:15** — "For we have not an high priest which cannot be touched with the feeling of our infirmities; but was in all points tempted like as we are, yet without sin."
  - *Interpretation:* The temptations of Christ were real — not theater. He was genuinely tested in all three areas that Genesis 3:6 identifies, and He did not yield.

### Fig Leaves vs. Coats of Skins — Man's Covering vs. God's Covering

**Genesis 3:7** — "They sewed fig leaves together, and made themselves aprons."  
**Genesis 3:21** — "Unto Adam also and to his wife did the LORD God make coats of skins, and clothed them."

- **Isaiah 64:6** — "But we are all as an unclean thing, and all our righteousnesses are as filthy rags; and we all do fade as a leaf; and our iniquities, like the wind, have taken us away."
  - *Interpretation:* Man's self-generated covering (fig leaves / self-righteousness) is explicitly compared to a "fading leaf" — it withers, it is inadequate, it is rejected.

- **Isaiah 61:10** — "He hath clothed me with the garments of salvation, he hath covered me with the robe of righteousness, as a bridegroom decketh himself with ornaments"
  - *Interpretation:* The garment of salvation is God's provision — coats of skins / the robe of righteousness through Christ.

- **Zechariah 3:3-5** — Joshua the high priest standing before God in "filthy garments" — sin-covered. God commands: "Take away the filthy garments from him... I will clothe thee with change of raiment... and they set a fair mitre upon his head." God removes the filthy covering and replaces it with clean garments.
  - *Interpretation:* The same pattern as Genesis 3: man in filthy/inadequate covering → God removes it → God provides clean covering through His own act.

- **Romans 13:14** — "But put ye on the Lord Jesus Christ, and make not provision for the flesh"
  - *Interpretation:* Clothing with Christ is the New Testament counterpart to the coats of skins. God clothes man.

- **Revelation 19:7-8** — "The marriage of the Lamb is come, and his wife hath made herself ready. And to her was granted that she should be arrayed in fine linen, clean and white: for the fine linen is the righteousness of saints."
  - *Interpretation:* The final state of the redeemed is the fully realized version of the coats of skins — clothed in perfect righteousness, given by God, not self-generated.

### The Proto-Evangelium — First Gospel Promise

**Genesis 3:15** — "And I will put enmity between thee and the woman, and between thy seed and her seed; it shall bruise thy head, and thou shalt bruise his heel."

**THIS IS THE FIRST PROMISE OF A REDEEMER — THE ENTIRE GOSPEL IN SEED FORM**

- **Galatians 4:4** — "But when the fulness of the time was come, God sent forth his Son, made of a woman, made under the law" — the seed of the woman = Christ; born of a woman, not of a man's seed
- **Matthew 1:23** — "Behold, a virgin shall be with child, and shall bring forth a son" — the virgin birth fulfills "seed of the woman" (a woman provides the seed, not a man)
- **Luke 1:35** — "The Holy Ghost shall come upon thee, and the power of the Highest shall overshadow thee: therefore also that holy thing which shall be born of thee shall be called the Son of God"
- **Hebrews 2:14** — "Forasmuch then as the children are partakers of flesh and blood, he also himself likewise took part of the same; that through death he might destroy him that had the power of death, that is, the devil"
  - *Interpretation:* The "bruising of the head" = Christ destroying the devil's power through His death and resurrection. The "bruising of the heel" = the cross wound Christ, but the resurrection proved it was not fatal.
- **Colossians 2:14-15** — "Blotting out the handwriting of ordinances that was against us... And having spoiled principalities and powers, he made a shew of them openly, triumphing over them in it"
- **1 John 3:8** — "For this purpose the Son of God was manifested, that he might destroy the works of the devil"
- **Romans 16:20** — "And the God of peace shall bruise Satan under your feet shortly" — the complete fulfillment is coming; the head-bruising is in process and will be finalized
- **Revelation 20:10** — "And the devil that deceived them was cast into the lake of fire and brimstone... and shall be tormented day and night for ever and ever" — the final, complete bruising of the serpent's head
  - *Interpretation:* Genesis 3:15 contains the entire redemptive story in compressed form: the Redeemer will be born of a woman (virgin birth), will be wounded (the cross), and will deliver the fatal blow to the serpent (death, resurrection, and final judgment). Every major redemptive event from Genesis 4 to Revelation 20 is an unfolding of this one verse. This is why Genesis 3:15 is called the proto-evangelium — the first gospel.

### Thorns and Thistles — The Curse on Creation

**Genesis 3:17-18** — "Cursed is the ground for thy sake; in sorrow shalt thou eat of it all the days of thy life; Thorns also and thistles shall it bring forth to thee"

- **Matthew 27:29** — "And when they had platted a crown of thorns, they put it upon his head" — Christ wears the thorns of the curse on His head
- **Galatians 3:13** — "Christ hath redeemed us from the curse of the law, being made a curse for us: for it is written, Cursed is every one that hangeth on a tree"
  - *Interpretation:* The thorn crown is not accidental. Christ put the curse of Genesis 3:17-18 upon His own head. He was made the curse (Galatians 3:13) to redeem those under the curse.
- **Romans 8:20-22** — "For the creature was made subject to vanity, not willingly, but by reason of him who hath subjected the same in hope, Because the creature itself also shall be delivered from the bondage of corruption into the glorious liberty of the children of God. For we know that the whole creation groaneth and travaileth in pain together until now."
  - *Interpretation:* The curse on the ground (Genesis 3:17) subjected the whole creation to futility and pain. Creation groans, waiting for redemption. The restoration of creation is part of the full redemption — not just souls but the physical world.
- **Revelation 22:3** — "And there shall be no more curse" — the curse of Genesis 3:17-19 is completely removed in the new creation.

### Death Enters Through Adam — The New Testament Diagnosis

**Genesis 3:19** — "In the sweat of thy face shalt thou eat bread, till thou return unto the ground; for out of it wast thou taken: for dust thou art, and unto dust shalt thou return."

- **Romans 5:12** — "Wherefore, as by one man sin entered into the world, and death by sin; and so death passed upon all men, for that all have sinned"
- **Romans 5:17-19** — "For if by one man's offence death reigned by one; much more they which receive abundance of grace and of the gift of righteousness shall reign in life by one, Jesus Christ... For as by one man's disobedience many were made sinners, so by the obedience of one shall many be made righteous"
  - *Interpretation:* Paul draws a precise parallel: one man (Adam) → disobedience → death for all. One man (Christ) → obedience → righteousness and life for all who receive it. Genesis 3 and Romans 5 are the two poles of the same story.
- **1 Corinthians 15:21-22** — "For since by man came death, by man came also the resurrection of the dead. For as in Adam all die, even so in Christ shall all be made alive."

### "Where Art Thou?" — God Seeking the Fallen

**Genesis 3:9** — "And the LORD God called unto Adam, and said unto him, Where art thou?"

- **Luke 15:3-7** — The parable of the lost sheep: the shepherd leaves the ninety-nine to seek the one that is lost — God seeks the lost
- **Luke 19:10** — "For the Son of man is come to seek and to save that which was lost"
  - *Interpretation:* God's first words to fallen man are a question of seeking — "Where art thou?" God does not abandon the fallen; He seeks them. This is the pattern of all of Scripture: a seeking God pursuing fallen man. The parable of the prodigal (Luke 15:11-32) is Genesis 3 retold — the son hides in shame, the father runs to meet him.
- *Note:* God is omniscient — He knew where Adam was. The question is not informational; it is relational. "Where art thou?" is an invitation to come out of hiding, to face God, to confess. It is the first call to repentance.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### SERPENT (Hebrew: nachash)

**Genesis 3:1** — "Now the serpent (nachash) was more subtil than any beast"

- **Nachash** = serpent, snake — also carries connotations of shining, of bronze/copper (related root), and of divination
- **Not merely an animal:** The serpent speaks, reasons, and proposes a theological argument — capabilities no animal possesses
- **Revelation 12:9; 20:2** — "That old serpent, called the Devil, and Satan" — Scripture provides its own identification of the serpent; no external commentary needed
- **Isaiah 27:1** — "In that day the LORD with his sore and great and strong sword shall punish leviathan the piercing serpent... and he shall slay the dragon that is in the sea" — the serpent/dragon imagery is consistent from Genesis through the prophets to Revelation
- **Numbers 21:8-9** — The bronze serpent Moses lifts up in the wilderness — a serpent used as a type of Christ bearing sin (confirmed by Jesus in John 3:14-15: "And as Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, even so must the Son of man be lifted up")
- *Context rule:* "Serpent" in Scripture can mean (1) a literal snake, (2) Satan or demonic power, (3) a type of Christ bearing sin (Numbers 21/John 3:14-15 — the only redemptive use). Context always determines which. Genesis 3 = Satan. Revelation 12, 20 = Satan explicitly named.

### SUBTIL (Hebrew: arum)

**Genesis 3:1** — "the serpent was more SUBTIL (arum) than any beast"

- **Arum** = crafty, shrewd, cunning, prudent — the same root word used in Proverbs for the "prudent" man, where it is a positive attribute (Proverbs 12:16, 23; 13:16; 14:8, 15, 18; 22:3)
- **The irony:** The same word that describes the wisdom of the prudent man in Proverbs describes the craftiness of the serpent. This is intentional — Satan's subtlety is a perversion of true wisdom. He is not stupid; he is brilliantly deceptive.
- **2 Corinthians 11:3** — "the serpent beguiled Eve through his SUBTILTY (panourgia — craftiness)" — Paul uses this word deliberately; the enemy is subtle, not obvious
- **Matthew 10:16** — "Be ye therefore wise (phronimos) as serpents, and harmless as doves" — Jesus uses the serpent's craftiness as a model for believers' wisdom in navigating hostile environments; even Christ draws on the serpent's quality in a redeemed context
- *Interpretation:* The enemy of Genesis 3 is not a blunt force attacker — he is the most crafty of all creatures. His approach is always indirect, always questioning, always seemingly reasonable. The church is warned repeatedly that deception will come from within, subtly (Matthew 7:15 — wolves in sheep's clothing; Acts 20:29-30 — grievous wolves; 2 Peter 2:1 — false teachers who "privily bring in damnable heresies").

### BEGUILED (Hebrew: nasha; Greek: exapatao)

**Genesis 3:13** — "The serpent BEGUILED me, and I did eat"

- **Hebrew nasha** = to deceive, to lead astray, to cause to err — used specifically of the serpent's deception of Eve
- **2 Corinthians 11:3** — Paul uses Greek **exapatao** = to thoroughly deceive, to seduce mentally; stronger than simple apataō (basic deceive)
- **1 Timothy 2:14** — "And Adam was not deceived (apataō), but the woman being deceived (exapatao) was in the transgression" — Paul distinguishes: Eve was thoroughly deceived; Adam sinned with eyes more open
- *Critical distinction:* This does not excuse Adam — he is held MORE responsible throughout the New Testament (Romans 5:12-19 consistently says sin entered through ONE MAN, not through Eve). Adam's sin was not ignorance but willful disobedience. Eve's was deception. Both sinned; both are accountable; Paul's point in 1 Timothy 2:14 is about the nature of the deception, not the degree of culpability.
- **Revelation 12:9** — Satan "deceiveth the whole world" — the work begun in Genesis 3 continues globally

### BRUISE / CRUSH (Hebrew: shuwph)

**Genesis 3:15** — "it shall BRUISE (shuwph) thy head, and thou shalt BRUISE (shuwph) his heel"

- **Same Hebrew word (shuwph) for BOTH bruisings** — the distinction is not in the action but in the location
- **Head wound** = mortal, fatal, decisive — a crushed head ends the creature
- **Heel wound** = painful, wounding, but survivable — the wounded one continues
- **Romans 16:20** — "The God of peace shall BRUISE (Greek: suntribo — crush, shatter) Satan under your feet" — the New Testament uses a word that makes clear the head-bruising is a crushing/shattering, not merely a wounding
- **Hebrews 2:14** — Christ "destroy" (Greek: katargeo — render powerless, abolish) him who had the power of death — the head-bruising is a rendering powerless, a destruction of his power
- *Interpretation:* The same action (shuwph) produces very different results depending on where it lands. Satan's wound to Christ (the heel) = the cross — real, painful, apparently fatal, but temporary; Christ rose. Christ's wound to Satan (the head) = the cross and resurrection, with the final blow still coming (Revelation 20:10) — decisive, fatal, permanent. The two bruisings are not equal in outcome even though they share the same word.

### ENMITY (Hebrew: eybah)

**Genesis 3:15** — "And I will put ENMITY (eybah) between thee and the woman"

- **Eybah** = enmity, hostility, hatred — used only 5 times in the Hebrew Bible
- **Critical note:** "I will PUT enmity" — God Himself places this enmity. It is not natural or inevitable; God actively establishes the warfare. This means the enmity between the seed of the serpent and the seed of the woman is a divinely ordained cosmic conflict, not merely human conflict.
- **Numbers 35:21-22** — eybah used of premeditated hatred in legal contexts — it carries the weight of deliberate, sustained hostility
- **Ezekiel 25:15; 35:5** — eybah used of "perpetual hatred" — the hatred that is unrelenting and ancient
- *Interpretation:* God placing enmity between the serpent and the woman means He is separating what the Fall might otherwise have merged — humanity entirely under Satan's dominion. By placing this enmity, God guarantees that there will always be those who resist Satan, who hate his ways, who are drawn toward the seed of the woman. The enmity is the mechanism of redemption — it keeps the two seeds in conflict until the head-bruising is complete.

### SEED (Hebrew: zera)

**Genesis 3:15** — "between thy seed and HER seed"

- **Zera** = seed, offspring, descendants — can refer to biological descendants collectively OR to a single descendant
- **Two seeds in perpetual conflict:**
  - **Seed of the serpent** = those who follow Satan's way; those who do his works (John 8:44 — "Ye are of your father the devil, and the lusts of your father ye will do"); the lineage of deception and rebellion
  - **Seed of the woman** = ultimately Christ (singular); and collectively, those in Christ (Galatians 3:29 — "And if ye be Christ's, then are ye Abraham's seed")
- **Galatians 3:16** — "He saith not, And to seeds, as of many; but as of one, And to thy seed, which is Christ" — Paul shows that the singular "seed" carries messianic weight; it points to ONE, not many
- **Galatians 4:4** — "God sent forth his Son, made of a woman" — the seed of the woman = Christ born of Mary (no human father — uniquely the seed of the woman)
- **Revelation 12:17** — "The dragon was wroth with the woman, and went to make war with the remnant of HER SEED, which keep the commandments of God, and have the testimony of Jesus Christ" — the collective seed of the woman = believers who hold the testimony of Jesus; the warfare of Genesis 3:15 is still ongoing at the point of Revelation 12

### COATS OF SKINS (Hebrew: kotnoth or)

**Genesis 3:21** — "Unto Adam also and to his wife did the LORD God make coats (kotnoth) of skins (or), and clothed them"

- **Kotnoth** = tunics, coats, garments — a full covering, not a loincloth; covers more than the fig leaf aprons
- **Or** = skin (the word for skin/hide of an animal); not the word for leather goods made from skin — this is the raw animal skin
- **What this requires:**
  - An animal was killed — the first death in Scripture
  - Blood was shed — the first blood shed after the Fall
  - God Himself performed the sacrifice and the clothing — He was both the priest and the provider
- **Hebrews 9:22** — "Without shedding of blood is no remission" — the principle behind Genesis 3:21
- **Leviticus 17:11** — "For the life of the flesh is in the blood: and I have given it to you upon the altar to make an atonement for your souls: for it is the blood that maketh an atonement for the soul" — the principle established here was always true; Genesis 3:21 is its first enactment
- **John 1:29** — "Behold the Lamb of God, which taketh away the sin of the world" — every sacrifice from Genesis 3:21 forward pointed to this Lamb
- *Context and progression:* Genesis 3:21 (coats of skins) → Genesis 4:4 (Abel's blood offering) → Genesis 8:20 (Noah's sacrifice) → Genesis 22:13 (ram in the thicket) → Exodus 12 (Passover lamb) → Leviticus (entire sacrificial system) → John 1:29 (the Lamb of God) → Hebrews 9:12 (Christ's own blood) → Revelation 5:12 ("Worthy is the Lamb that was slain"). The entire sacrificial thread of Scripture begins in Genesis 3:21.

### EVE (Hebrew: Chavvah)

**Genesis 3:20** — "And Adam called his wife's name Eve (Chavvah); because she was the mother of all living"

- **Chavvah** = living, life-giver — from the root chavah, related to chay (life/living)
- **Timing is significant:** Adam names her AFTER the Fall and the curse. In the face of the death verdict ("unto dust shalt thou return"), Adam names his wife "life-giver" — this is an act of faith in the promise of Genesis 3:15. The one who will bear the seed that crushes the serpent's head is called the mother of all LIVING.
- **Acts 17:26** — "And hath made of one blood all nations of men for to dwell on all the face of the earth" — Eve as "mother of all living" is the biological foundation of human unity; all humans descend from one woman
- *Interpretation:* The name Eve/Chavvah given in the shadow of the death curse is one of the most quietly profound acts of faith in Genesis. Adam believes the promise. The mother of all living — including the Living One who will reverse the curse.

### CHERUBIM (Hebrew: keruvim)

**Genesis 3:24** — "he placed at the east of the garden of Eden Cherubims, and a flaming sword which turned every way, to keep the way of the tree of life"

- **Keruvim** = cherubim — the highest class of angelic beings associated with God's throne and presence
- **Exodus 25:18-22** — Two gold cherubim on the lid of the ark of the covenant (the mercy seat) — their wings covering the place where God meets man; they guard the holy place
- **Ezekiel 1:4-14; 10:1-22** — The four living creatures surrounding God's throne are identified as cherubim — they are the guardians of God's holiness and glory
- **Ezekiel 28:14-16** — Satan himself is described as "the anointed cherub that covereth" — before his fall he was a cherub, a guardian of God's glory; now he is the one who corrupts what he once guarded
- *Interpretation:* The cherubim placed at Eden's east gate are not merely bouncers. They are the same class of being that surrounds God's throne, guards the holy of holies, and is associated with God's most intense presence. Their placement at the tree of life means the way to eternal life is now guarded by the holiness of God — no fallen man can pass. The veil of the temple (which cherubim were embroidered on — Exodus 26:31) is the same barrier, translated into fabric. When Christ died and "the veil of the temple was rent in twain from the top to the bottom" (Matthew 27:51), the barrier of Genesis 3:24 was torn open. The way to the tree of life is now open again — through Christ.

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The First Blood Sacrifice — Type of Christ's Atoning Death

**Genesis 3:21** — "Unto Adam also and to his wife did the LORD God make coats of skins, and clothed them"

- **Something had to die:** An innocent creature was killed so that fallen, guilty humans could be clothed. This is the first picture of substitutionary atonement — the innocent dying so the guilty can be covered.
- **God did it Himself:** The LORD God made the coats. God was the first priest, performing the first sacrifice. He did not instruct Adam to provide his own covering — He provided it. This establishes grace: God provides what man cannot.
- **Hebrews 9:22** — "Without shedding of blood is no remission" — the principle that governs all subsequent sacrifices originates here
- **Progression of the sacrifice:** Genesis 3:21 → Abel (Genesis 4:4) → Noah (Genesis 8:20) → Abraham (Genesis 22:13) → Passover lamb (Exodus 12) → Tabernacle/Temple system (Leviticus) → the Lamb of God (John 1:29) → Christ's blood (Hebrews 9:12-14) → "Worthy is the Lamb that was slain" (Revelation 5:12)
- *Interpretation:* Every sacrifice in Scripture is a shadow of Genesis 3:21, and Genesis 3:21 is a shadow of the cross. The pattern is always: innocent dies, guilty is covered. The skin covered Adam and Eve's physical nakedness; Christ's righteousness covers the believer's spiritual nakedness.

---

### The Three-Part Temptation — Type and Antitype in Adam and Christ

**Genesis 3:6** — The first Adam fails the three-part temptation  
**Matthew 4:1-11** — The Last Adam overcomes the same three-part temptation

| Genesis 3:6 | 1 John 2:16 | Matthew 4 |
|---|---|---|
| Good for food | Lust of the flesh | "Command these stones be made bread" |
| Pleasant to the eyes | Lust of the eyes | "All these kingdoms will I give thee" |
| Desired to make wise | Pride of life | "Cast thyself down" (prove God cares) |

- **Romans 5:19** — "For as by one man's DISOBEDIENCE many were made sinners, so by the OBEDIENCE of one shall many be made righteous" — Adam's three failures in Genesis 3 are precisely reversed by Christ's three victories in Matthew 4
- **1 Corinthians 15:45** — "The first man Adam was made a living soul; the last Adam was made a quickening spirit" — the contrast is total: where one failed, the Other succeeded
- *Interpretation:* Matthew 4 is not just a report of Christ's temptation — it is the deliberate reversal of Genesis 3. Adam was in a garden, well-fed, in God's presence, with his wife, and failed. Christ was in a wilderness, fasting forty days, alone, at His weakest physically — and overcame. The conditions were worse for Christ in every visible way, and He still prevailed. This is the basis of the believer's confidence: Christ overcame in the exact conditions where humanity failed.

---

### The Curse on the Ground — Type of Creation's Redemption

**Genesis 3:17-19** — Ground cursed, thorns and thistles, toil and death

- **Romans 8:19-22** — Creation subjected to vanity "in hope" — the curse is not the final word
- **Isaiah 11:6-9** — In the Messianic kingdom: "The wolf also shall dwell with the lamb... and the lion shall eat straw like the ox... They shall not hurt nor destroy in all my holy mountain" — the undoing of the Fall's effects on the animal kingdom
- **Isaiah 35:1-2** — "The wilderness and the solitary place shall be glad for them; and the desert shall rejoice, and blossom as the rose" — the ground's curse reversed
- **Revelation 22:3** — "And there shall be no more curse" — the absolute, final removal of Genesis 3:17
- *Interpretation:* The curse on the ground in Genesis 3 is explicitly temporary — its removal is prophesied and guaranteed by Scripture. Christ's crown of thorns was not just symbolic; it was Him bearing the specific curse of Genesis 3:18. The thorn crown at the cross points to the removal of the thorn curse at the restoration. The cross bears the curse; the new creation removes it.

---

### The Cherubim at the East Gate — Type of the Temple Veil

**Genesis 3:24** — Cherubim guard the way to the tree of life at the EAST gate

- **Exodus 26:31** — The veil of the tabernacle/temple was embroidered with cherubim — the same angelic guardians of the holy place
- **The temple structure mirrors Eden's structure:** The holy of holies = the place where God's presence dwells (as in the garden); the veil embroidered with cherubim = the cherubim guarding Eden's entrance; the priest's entry to the holy of holies = an attempt to approach the tree of life
- **Matthew 27:51** — "And, behold, the veil of the temple was rent in twain from the top to the bottom; and the earth did quake, and the rocks rent" — when Christ died, the cherubim-embroidered barrier was torn from top to bottom — from God's direction downward
- **Hebrews 10:19-20** — "Having therefore, brethren, boldness to enter into the holiest by the blood of Jesus, By a new and living way, which he hath consecrated for us, through the veil, that is to say, his flesh"
  - *Interpretation:* The flaming sword and cherubim of Genesis 3:24 are the temple veil. Christ's death tears the veil. The way to the tree of life — blocked at the Fall — is reopened at the cross. "Through the veil, that is to say, his flesh" (Hebrews 10:20) means Christ's body IS the new path through the former barrier.

---

### "Where Art Thou?" — First Question, Last Invitation

**Genesis 3:9** — God's first words to fallen man: "Where art thou?"

- This is the first of God's questions in Scripture directed at man. God is omniscient — this is not informational; it is pastoral.
- **Luke 15:4** — "What man of you, having an hundred sheep, if he lose one of them, doth not leave the ninety and nine in the wilderness, and go after that which is lost, until he find it?" — God the seeking shepherd
- **Luke 19:10** — "For the Son of man is come to seek and to save that which was lost" — the entire mission of Christ is the answer to "Where art thou?" He comes to find what was lost in Genesis 3.
- **Revelation 3:20** — "Behold, I stand at the door, and knock: if any man hear my voice, and open the door, I will come in to him, and will sup with him, and he with me" — the last book of the Bible echoes the first: God is still calling to the hiding, the ashamed, the fallen — come out, I am here.
- *Interpretation:* "Where art thou?" in Genesis 3:9 opens the search that ends in Revelation 22:17 — "And the Spirit and the bride say, Come." From the first "Where art thou?" to the last "Come" — God is always calling the lost home.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED / MISAPPLIED PASSAGES

### "Ye Shall Not Surely Die" (v.4) — Satan's Direct Quote, Still in Use

**The Misuse:** Various teachers, universalists, annihilationists, and New Age systems use this verse (or its doctrine) to deny that sin leads to real, enduring death or judgment.

**What Scripture says:**

- **Genesis 2:17** — "Thou shalt SURELY die" — God's emphatic double-verb (moth tamuth); this is absolute
- **Genesis 5:5** — "And all the days that Adam lived were nine hundred and thirty years: and he died" — Adam died, exactly as God said
- **Romans 5:12** — "Death passed upon all men, for that all have sinned" — all humanity is under the Genesis 2:17 verdict
- **Revelation 21:8** — "The fearful, and unbelieving... shall have their part in the lake which burneth with fire and brimstone: which is the second death" — death is real, lasting, and has a second dimension beyond physical dying
- **John 8:44** — Jesus identifies "ye shall not surely die" as the statement of the one "who abode not in the truth, because there is no truth in him... a liar, and the father of it"
- *Correction from Scripture:* The first recorded lie in the Bible is the denial of death as a consequence of sin. Every theological system that minimizes, spiritualizes away, or eliminates the reality of judgment is repeating Satan's first recorded statement. Scripture never revises Genesis 2:17 — it confirms and deepens it (Romans 5:12; Revelation 20:14-15).

---

### "Ye Shall Be as Gods" (v.5) — Misused to Teach Human Divinity

**The Misuse:** New Age spirituality, Word-Faith movements, Mormon theology, and various mystical traditions use this verse to support the idea that man can become divine, that there is a "god within" waiting to be awakened, or that believers become gods.

**What Scripture says:**

- **Isaiah 44:6** — "Thus saith the LORD the King of Israel... I am the first, and I am the last; and beside me there is NO GOD"
- **Isaiah 45:5** — "I am the LORD, and there is none else, there is no God beside me"
- **Ezekiel 28:2, 9** — The prince of Tyre said "I am a God... yet thou art a man, and not God" — God directly rebukes the claim of human divinity as the spirit of the one who originated it (the "anointed cherub" of Ezekiel 28:14 = Satan)
- **John 10:34-35** — Jesus quotes Psalm 82:6 ("I said, Ye are gods") — this requires careful context: Psalm 82 is about human judges/rulers who held God-delegated authority and ABUSED it; God rebukes them. Jesus uses this to argue from the lesser to the greater — not to teach universal human divinity.
- *Correction from Scripture:* "Ye shall be as gods" is Satan's pitch. The result of accepting it was not divinity but death, shame, and exile. Ezekiel 28 shows this same claim — "I am God" — is the spirit of the adversary himself. The Bible consistently attributes this claim to the one who fell through pride (Isaiah 14:13-14 — "I will be like the most High"). Any teaching that promises man the status of god is repeating Genesis 3:5, not correcting it.

---

### "The Woman Whom Thou Gavest Me" (v.12) — Misread as Primarily About Gender

**The Misuse:** Some readers focus on Genesis 3:12 as primarily a statement about Adam blaming Eve, drawing conclusions about male-female dynamics, patriarchy, or women's culpability.

**What Scripture says:**

- **Romans 5:12-19** — Paul consistently and explicitly places the primary responsibility for the Fall on ADAM, not Eve: "by one MAN sin entered into the world" (v.12); "by one man's offence death reigned" (v.17); "by one man's disobedience many were made sinners" (v.19)
- **1 Timothy 2:14** — "And Adam was not deceived, but the woman being deceived was in the transgression" — Eve was deceived; Adam sinned knowingly
- **Adam's dual blame-shift in v.12:** "The woman WHOM THOU GAVEST TO BE WITH ME" — he blames both the woman AND God ("whom thou gavest me"). This is the first example of blaming God for one's circumstances. It has never stopped.
- *Correction from Scripture:* The blame-shifting in Genesis 3:12-13 is the beginning of all excuse-making — before God, before men, before judgment. God does not accept any of it. He holds each accountable: the serpent (v.14), the woman (v.16), the man (v.17-19). Each receives consequences proportionate to their role. The woman was deceived; the man sinned willfully; both are accountable; God is not to blame for either.

### The Childbirth Pains Curse (v.16) — A Prophetic Key, Not Just Biology

**Genesis 3:16** — "I will greatly multiply thy sorrow (itstsabon) and thy conception; in sorrow thou shalt bring forth children"

- **The prophetic use of childbirth pain throughout Scripture:**
  - **Isaiah 66:7-8** — "Before she travailed, she brought forth... shall a nation be born at once?" — the birth of Israel/the remnant described in childbirth terms
  - **Micah 5:3** — "Therefore will he give them up, until the time that she which travaileth hath brought forth"
  - **Matthew 24:8** — "All these are the beginning of SORROWS" — Greek: odin = birth pains; the same word used in the Septuagint for the Genesis 3:16 curse word
  - **1 Thessalonians 5:3** — "Then sudden destruction cometh upon them, as travail upon a woman with child; and they shall not escape"
  - **Revelation 12:1-2** — "A woman clothed with the sun, and the moon under her feet, and upon her head a crown of twelve stars: And she being with child cried, travailing in birth, and pained to be delivered"
- *Interpretation:* The childbirth pain of Genesis 3:16 is picked up by the prophets and by Christ Himself as a prophetic metaphor for the birth pains of the end times. Matthew 24:8 specifically calls the signs of the end "birth pains" (odin). This connects the original curse to the eschatological framework: just as birth pains intensify and accelerate as delivery approaches, the sorrows of the end times will intensify and accelerate. The curse is simultaneously a biological reality and a prophetic template.

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### The Serpent / Dragon — From Garden to Lake of Fire

**Genesis 3:1** — The serpent deceives in the garden  
**Revelation 12:9** — "That old serpent, called the Devil, and Satan, which deceiveth the whole world"  
**Revelation 20:2** — Bound a thousand years  
**Revelation 20:10** — Cast into the lake of fire

**Thread through Scripture:**
- **Isaiah 27:1** — "In that day the LORD with his sore and great and strong sword shall punish leviathan the piercing serpent, even leviathan that crooked serpent; and he shall slay the dragon that is in the sea"
- **Ezekiel 29:3; 32:2** — Pharaoh compared to a great dragon/serpent in the Nile — the enemy of God's people figured as the serpent
- **Psalm 74:13-14** — "Thou breakest the heads of the dragons in the waters. Thou brakest the heads of leviathan in pieces" — God crushing the serpent's power as a recurring prophetic image
- **Numbers 21:8-9 / John 3:14-15** — The one use in Scripture where a serpent is a redemptive type: Moses lifts up the bronze serpent; Jesus says "even so must the Son of man be lifted up." Christ was made sin (the serpent = sin/curse) on the cross so that looking to Him brings life. This is the one exception — the serpent as type of Christ bearing sin — which is entirely consistent with the serpent pattern: the cross is where Satan's strategy (bruise the heel) became his own defeat.
- *Interpretation:* The serpent of Genesis 3 is the same being identified as the dragon/Satan throughout Scripture. His appearance in the garden is his first recorded act; his casting into the lake of fire in Revelation 20 is his last. The trajectory of the serpent is from deception-of-one (Genesis 3) → deception-of-the-world (Revelation 12:9) → binding (Revelation 20:2) → eternal judgment (Revelation 20:10). Genesis 3:15 is the verdict; Revelation 20:10 is the execution.

### Fig Leaves → Coats of Skins → White Robes — The Arc of Covering

**Genesis 3:7** — Man's self-covering: fig leaves (inadequate, self-made)  
**Genesis 3:21** — God's covering: coats of skins (requires death, God-provided)  
**Revelation 19:8** — Final covering: fine linen, white and clean (righteousness of saints, given by God)

**Thread through Scripture:**
- **Isaiah 64:6** — "All our righteousnesses are as filthy rags" — fig leaf religion; self-generated, worthless before God
- **Zechariah 3:3-5** — Joshua in filthy garments before God; God provides clean raiment — the pattern repeated
- **Isaiah 61:10** — "He hath clothed me with the garments of salvation, he hath covered me with the robe of righteousness"
- **Matthew 22:11-12** — The man without a wedding garment is cast out — being in the right place without the right covering is not enough
- **Romans 13:14** — "Put ye on the Lord Jesus Christ"
- **Revelation 3:18** — White raiment offered to the Laodicean church: "that thou mayest be clothed, and that the shame of thy nakedness do not appear"
- *Interpretation:* The arc of covering is the arc of salvation. Man's covering is always inadequate (fig leaves / self-righteousness). God's covering always involves blood (coats of skins / the cross). The final state is white robes — perfect righteousness given by God, worn by the redeemed. What began in a garden with shame and fig leaves ends in a city with spotless linen.

### Birth Pains — Curse to Prophetic Key

**Genesis 3:16** — "in sorrow (itstsabon — birth pains) thou shalt bring forth children"

**Thread through Scripture:**
- **Isaiah 13:8; 21:3; 26:17-18** — Birth pain language for judgment and deliverance
- **Jeremiah 4:31; 6:24; 30:6** — "Birth pains" applied to national catastrophe
- **Micah 5:3** — "Until the time that she which travaileth hath brought forth" — the travailing woman as a prophetic marker
- **Matthew 24:8** — "All these are the beginning of sorrows (odin = birth pains)" — Jesus uses the Fall's curse as the prophetic template for end-times signs
- **1 Thessalonians 5:3** — "As travail upon a woman with child" — sudden, unavoidable, intensifying
- **Revelation 12:2** — "She being with child cried, travailing in birth, and pained to be delivered"
- *Interpretation:* God embedded a prophetic template into the curse itself. The pain of childbirth — intensifying, accelerating, unavoidable, leading to new life — becomes in the prophets and in Christ's own teaching the template for the end times. As birth pains accelerate as delivery approaches, end-times events accelerate. The curse of Genesis 3:16 contains the seed of the prophetic timeline.

### Hiding from God — The Pattern of the Fallen

**Genesis 3:8** — "Adam and his wife hid themselves from the presence of the LORD God"

**Thread through Scripture:**
- **Psalm 139:7-12** — "Whither shall I flee from thy presence?" — man cannot hide from God; the hiding is self-deception
- **Isaiah 28:15** — "We have made lies our refuge, and under falsehood have we hid ourselves" — collective hiding in deception
- **Amos 9:3** — "Though they hide themselves in the top of Carmel, I will search and take them out thence" — God's pursuit of the hiding
- **Jonah 1:3** — "But Jonah rose up to flee unto Tarshish from the presence of the LORD" — the most famous attempt to hide; it failed
- **Revelation 6:15-16** — "And the kings of the earth, and the great men... hid themselves in the dens and in the rocks of the mountains; And said to the mountains and rocks, Fall on us, and hide us from the face of him that sitteth on the throne" — at the final judgment, man still tries to hide; it still fails
- *Interpretation:* The hiding of Adam and Eve in Genesis 3:8 is the first instance of a pattern that runs to the last pages of Scripture. Fallen man hides from God — in religion, in busyness, in denial, in caves, in mountains. God always finds. What began with "they hid themselves among the trees" ends in "hide us from the face of him that sitteth on the throne." Same impulse, same futility.

## FIRST MENTION PRINCIPLES

### First Mention of the Lie

**Genesis 3:4** — "Ye shall not surely die" — the first recorded lie in Scripture
- It is a direct contradiction of God's word
- It comes from the creature that will be identified as "the father of lies" (John 8:44)
- First mention establishes: the lie is always a contradiction of God's word; always denies consequences; always promises something God has not promised
- The first lie targeted the most emphatic statement God had made (moth tamuth — surely die). The most important truths are always the most targeted.

### First Mention of Fear

**Genesis 3:10** — "I was afraid, because I was naked; and I hid myself"
- First occurrence of fear (yare) directed at God — and it comes after sin
- Before the Fall: no fear recorded; man walked with God
- After the Fall: fear is the first recorded emotion
- **1 John 4:18** — "There is no fear in love; but perfect love casteth out fear: because fear hath torment. He that feareth is not made perfect in love." — the love relationship of Genesis 2 → the fear response of Genesis 3:10 → the restoration of love casting out fear (1 John 4:18) = the arc of redemption in one verse

### First Mention of Shame

**Genesis 3:7** — "They knew that they were naked" — first occurrence of shame
- Shame enters with sin; it was absent before the Fall (Genesis 2:25 — "not ashamed")
- **Romans 10:11** — "Whosoever believeth on him shall not be ashamed" — Christ removes the shame of Genesis 3:7
- **Hebrews 12:2** — Christ "endured the cross, despising the shame" — He took the shame of Genesis 3:7 onto Himself
- First mention establishes: shame is the psychological signature of the Fall; it is not natural to man as God created him; it is the mark of sin's entry

### First Mention of Blame-Shifting

**Genesis 3:12-13** — Adam blames the woman (and implicitly God); the woman blames the serpent
- First recorded instance of refusing personal responsibility for sin
- Every party in the narrative deflects: Adam → Eve → God; Eve → serpent
- God holds each individually accountable despite the deflection
- **Proverbs 28:13** — "He that covereth his sins shall not prosper: but whoso confesseth and forsaketh them shall have mercy" — the answer to Genesis 3:12-13 is confession, not deflection

### First Mention of the Gospel (Proto-Evangelium)

**Genesis 3:15** — First promise of a Redeemer; first statement that Satan will be defeated; first prophecy of the virgin birth ("seed of the woman"); first prophecy of the cross ("bruise his heel"); first prophecy of Christ's victory ("bruise thy head")
- The entire gospel is contained in seed form in one verse
- Every subsequent prophecy of redemption is an unfolding of Genesis 3:15
- **Galatians 3:8** — "And the scripture, foreseeing that God would justify the heathen through faith, preached before the gospel unto Abraham" — the gospel was preached in Genesis; it began here

### First Mention of Blood Sacrifice

**Genesis 3:21** — Coats of skins require the death of an innocent creature
- First death in Scripture; first blood shed
- First enactment of the principle: "without shedding of blood is no remission" (Hebrews 9:22)
- First act of God as priest/provider of sacrifice
- Establishes the template for all subsequent sacrifices pointing to Christ

### First Mention of the Curse

**Genesis 3:14, 17** — The serpent is cursed; the ground is cursed
- First use of "cursed" (arur) in Scripture
- **Galatians 3:13** — "Christ hath redeemed us from the curse of the law, being made a curse for us" — the curse of Genesis 3 is what Christ redeems from
- **Revelation 22:3** — "And there shall be no more curse" — the last reference to the curse in Scripture is its permanent removal

### First Mention of an Angel Guarding Something

**Genesis 3:24** — Cherubim guard the way to the tree of life
- First recorded angelic assignment as guardian/barrier
- **Exodus 25:18-22** — Cherubim on the mercy seat guard the holy place — the same function
- First mention establishes: angels guard the holiness of God and the access to His presence; no fallen creature passes unchecked
