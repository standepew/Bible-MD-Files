## CHAPTER SUMMARY

As men multiplied on the face of the earth, the sons of God (bene ha-Elohim) took wives from the daughters of men. The Nephilim (giants) were in the earth in those days — mighty men, men of renown. God saw the wickedness of man was great; every imagination (yetzer) of the thoughts of man's heart was only evil continually. God was grieved and repented that He had made man; He determined to destroy all flesh from the earth. But Noah found grace (chen) in the eyes of the LORD. Noah was just, perfect (tamim) in his generations, and walked with God. God told Noah: the earth is filled with violence (chamas) and corruption (shachath) — He would destroy (shachath) all flesh with the earth. God commissioned the ark: gopher wood, pitched (kopher) within and without, 300 × 50 × 30 cubits, window above, one door in the side, three stories. God announced a flood to destroy all flesh wherein is the breath of life. He declared He would establish His covenant with Noah and his household. Noah was to bring two of every living creature — male and female — and food for all. Noah did according to all that God commanded him.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### Sons of God and Daughters of Men — The Identity Question Settled by Scripture

**Genesis 6:2, 4** — "The sons of God (bene ha-Elohim) saw the daughters of men that they were fair; and they took them wives of all which they chose... the sons of God came in unto the daughters of men"

The three main uses of bene ha-Elohim in the Old Testament:

- **Job 1:6** — "Now there was a day when the sons of God (bene ha-Elohim) came to present themselves before the LORD, and Satan came also among them" — the sons of God present themselves in the divine council; Satan is among them but distinct; this is clearly a heavenly assembly, not a human gathering
- **Job 2:1** — Same formula repeated — "the sons of God came to present themselves before the LORD"
- **Job 38:7** — "When the morning stars sang together, and all the sons of God shouted for joy?" — God addresses Job concerning the moment of creation; the sons of God were present at creation, before Adam existed; they cannot be humans

**Conclusion from Scripture's own usage:** The term bene ha-Elohim in the Old Testament refers consistently to supernatural beings — members of the heavenly assembly — not to human men. The Sethite interpretation (sons of God = godly men from Seth's line) requires reading a meaning into this specific phrase that the phrase does not carry elsewhere in the Old Testament.

**Confirming cross-references:**
- **Jude 6** — "And the angels which kept not their first estate, but left their own habitation, he hath reserved in everlasting chains under darkness unto the judgment of the great day"
  - *Interpretation:* Jude describes angels who abandoned their proper domain — their "first estate" (Greek: arche = beginning, position, domain) and their "own habitation" (Greek: oiketerion = dwelling place). The language of abandoning a proper domain for an improper one mirrors Genesis 6:2 — sons of God leaving their heavenly habitation to take earthly wives.
- **Jude 7** — "Even as Sodom and Gomorrha, and the cities about them in like manner, giving themselves over to fornication, and going after strange flesh" — Jude uses "in like manner" (Greek: homoios tropon) to connect the angels of v.6 to Sodom's going after "strange flesh." The angelic sin and Sodom's sin are compared — both involved crossing a boundary God established between kinds.
- **2 Peter 2:4-5** — "For if God spared not the angels that sinned, but cast them down to hell (Tartarus), and delivered them into chains of darkness, to be reserved unto judgment; And spared not the old world, but saved Noah the eighth person, a preacher of righteousness" — Peter places the angels that sinned and Noah's flood in direct sequence, connecting them to the same pre-flood period
- **1 Peter 3:19-20** — "By which also he went and preached unto the spirits in prison; Which sometime were disobedient, when once the longsuffering of God waited in the days of Noah" — the spirits in prison to whom Christ preached are connected to "the days of Noah"; they are imprisoned spirits (not deceased humans) associated with the pre-flood era

**The Nephilim connection:**
- **Numbers 13:33** — "And there we saw the giants (Nephilim), the sons of Anak, which come of the giants (Nephilim)" — the Nephilim appear again after the flood; Genesis 6:4 itself says "and also after that" — acknowledging post-flood Nephilim; this is why Canaan had to be cleared of giant clans (Anakim, Rephaim, Zamzummim, etc.)

### "Every Imagination of the Thoughts of His Heart Was Only Evil Continually" — The Doctrine of Total Depravity

**Genesis 6:5** — "And God saw that the wickedness of man was great in the earth, and that every imagination of the thoughts of his heart was only evil continually"

- **Five absolutes in this verse:** (1) EVERY imagination; (2) of the THOUGHTS; (3) of his HEART; (4) was ONLY evil; (5) CONTINUALLY — the language leaves no exception; no part of the inner life of man was unaffected
- **Genesis 8:21** — After the flood: "the imagination of man's heart is evil from his youth" — God makes the same assessment AFTER destroying the wicked world and starting fresh with Noah. The flood did not fix the problem; the problem is in human nature itself.
  - *Interpretation:* This is perhaps the most devastating back-to-back statement in Genesis. God destroys the world because of man's evil heart (Genesis 6:5). God sends the flood. The flood ends. God smells Noah's sacrifice. And God says — "the imagination of man's heart is evil from his youth" (Genesis 8:21). The flood cleansed the earth but not the heart. A new world with the same hearts produces the same problem. This is why a new birth — not a new environment — is the only solution.
- **Jeremiah 17:9** — "The heart is deceitful above all things, and desperately wicked: who can know it?" — Jeremiah declares what Genesis 6:5 demonstrates
- **Romans 3:10-18** — Paul's comprehensive statement of human depravity, citing multiple psalms: "There is none righteous, no, not one... There is none that doeth good, no, not one" — the New Testament's Genesis 6:5 in theological form
- **Romans 8:7** — "The carnal mind is enmity against God: for it is not subject to the law of God, neither indeed can be" — not just unwilling but unable; the carnal mind cannot submit to God
- **John 3:3-6** — "Except a man be born again, he cannot see the kingdom of God... that which is born of the flesh is flesh" — the only answer to Genesis 6:5's diagnosis is the new birth; a new heart, not a cleaned-up old one

### "It Repented the LORD" — God's Grief and the Question of Divine Immutability

**Genesis 6:6** — "And it repented the LORD that he had made man on the earth, and it grieved him at his heart"

- **Hebrew nacham** = to be sorry, to relent, to be comforted, to repent — a word of emotional response; here it speaks of God's sorrow
- **Hebrew atsab** = to grieve, to be pained, to be wounded — used of intense inner pain; the same root used in Genesis 3:16-17 of "sorrow" (itstsabon) in childbirth and in tilling the cursed ground
  - *Interpretation:* The pain God feels at man's wickedness in Genesis 6:6 uses the same root as the pain He pronounced on man in Genesis 3:16-17. Man's pain from the curse and God's pain at man's sin are linked by the same word. The one who pronounced the pain also bears it.
- **1 Samuel 15:11** — "It repenteth me that I have set up Saul to be king" — God expresses the same grief-response over Saul's disobedience
- **1 Samuel 15:29** — "And also the Strength of Israel will not lie nor repent: for he is not a man, that he should repent" — in the same chapter, the prophet affirms that God does NOT change His purposes; the "repenting" is not a change of character or of eternal decree but of relational posture
- **Numbers 23:19** — "God is not a man, that he should lie; neither the son of man, that he should repent: hath he said, and shall he not do it?" — God does not change His ultimate purposes
- **Malachi 3:6** — "For I am the LORD, I change not; therefore ye sons of Jacob are not consumed"
- **James 1:17** — "Every good gift and every perfect gift is from above, and cometh down from the Father of lights, with whom is no variableness, neither shadow of turning"
  - *Interpretation:* Scripture holds both truths simultaneously: God is genuinely grieved by sin (Genesis 6:6; 1 Samuel 15:11 — this is real, not merely anthropomorphic language) AND God does not change His ultimate nature, character, or eternal decrees (1 Samuel 15:29; Malachi 3:6). The "repenting" of God is His relational and responsive grief — the sorrow of a father watching a child self-destruct — not a change in His omniscience or His eternal plan.

### "Noah Found Grace" — First Mention of Grace

**Genesis 6:8** — "But Noah found grace (chen) in the eyes of the LORD"

- **First mention of "grace" (chen) in Scripture** — and it appears in the context of universal judgment; grace is always set against the backdrop of deserved condemnation
- The structure of the verse: all of Genesis 6:1-7 is judgment; then the single pivot verse: "BUT Noah found grace." The word "but" (Hebrew: ve) carries the full weight of the gospel in seed form — not because of what Noah did but because of what God gave
- **Hebrews 11:7** — "By faith Noah, being warned of God of things not seen as yet, moved with fear, prepared an ark to the saving of his house; by the which he condemned the world, and became heir of the righteousness which is by faith" — Hebrews does not say Noah was saved by his righteousness; he became "heir of the righteousness which is by FAITH"
- **Ephesians 2:8-9** — "For by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves: it is the gift of God: Not of works, lest any man should boast" — the New Testament definition of what Noah experienced
- **Romans 3:24** — "Being justified freely by his grace through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus" — "freely" = gratis, without cost on man's part; Noah "found" grace — he did not earn it, manufacture it, or deserve it
  - *Interpretation:* The first mention of grace (chen) in Scripture is the turning point of the most severe judgment narrative in Genesis. Grace appears not in paradise but in the shadow of global condemnation. This is the pattern of all grace: it is found exactly where judgment is deserved and where man cannot save himself. Noah "found" it — he did not produce it. God gave it — He was not obligated to.

### "I Will Establish My Covenant" — First Covenant with an Individual

**Genesis 6:18** — "But with thee will I establish my covenant; and thou shalt come into the ark"

- **First use of berith (covenant) applied to an individual** — the covenant concept has been implicit in God's dealings (the creation mandate, the protoevangelium), but this is the first explicit named covenant between God and a man
- **God initiates:** "I will establish" — not "let us negotiate" or "if you do your part." God announces a covenant He will establish; Noah's part is obedience (v.22), not contribution to the terms
- **Genesis 9:9-17** — The covenant is made explicit after the flood with the rainbow sign; God expands it to include all living creatures and all future generations
- **The covenant is made BEFORE the flood** — Noah enters the ark on the basis of God's declared covenant word, not on visible evidence; this is faith (Hebrews 11:7 — "things not seen as yet")
- **Hebrews 6:13-18** — "For when God made promise to Abraham, he sware by himself... That by two immutable things, in which it was impossible for God to lie, we might have a strong consolation" — God's covenant word is the immovable foundation; what God declares, He does
- **The covenant chain:** Noah's covenant (Genesis 6:18; 9:9-17) → Abraham's covenant (Genesis 15; 17) → Mosaic covenant (Exodus 19-24) → Davidic covenant (2 Samuel 7) → New covenant (Jeremiah 31:31-34; Luke 22:20; Hebrews 8:6-13) — each covenant builds on the previous; all find their fulfillment in Christ

### "The Earth Was Filled with Violence" — The Condition Then and the Sign for Now

**Genesis 6:11, 13** — "The earth also was corrupt before God, and the earth was filled with violence... the earth is filled with violence through them; and, behold, I will destroy them with the earth"

- **Matthew 24:37-38** — "But as the days of Noe were, so shall also the coming of the Son of man be. For as in the days that were before the flood they were eating and drinking, marrying and giving in marriage, until the day that Noe entered into the ark" — Jesus explicitly uses Genesis 6 as the prophetic type for the conditions preceding His return
- **Luke 17:26-27** — Same citation; "they... until the day that Noe entered into the ark, and the flood came, and destroyed them all"
- **2 Timothy 3:1-4** — "In the last days perilous times shall come. For men shall be lovers of their own selves, covetous, boasters, proud, blasphemers, disobedient to parents, unthankful, unholy, Without natural affection, trucebreakers, false accusers, incontinent, fierce, despisers of those that are good, Traitors, heady, highminded, lovers of pleasures more than lovers of God" — the last-days profile matches the Genesis 6:5, 11 profile
- *Interpretation:* Jesus chose the days of Noah — specifically the combination of (1) normalcy (eating, drinking, marrying) alongside (2) extreme wickedness and violence — as the prophetic template for the end times. The two conditions coexist: life appears normal on the surface while corruption saturates the foundations. The suddenness of the flood (they did not know "until the flood came and took them all away" — Matthew 24:39) is the template for the suddenness of Christ's coming.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### SONS OF GOD (Hebrew: bene ha-Elohim)

**Genesis 6:2, 4** — "the sons of God saw the daughters of men... the sons of God came in unto the daughters of men"

- **Bene ha-Elohim** = "sons of the God" — a specific Hebrew phrase with the definite article (ha-Elohim = THE God), distinguishing it from generic "sons of gods" language
- **Three Old Testament uses of this exact phrase:**
  1. Job 1:6 — heavenly assembly, Satan among them
  2. Job 2:1 — same heavenly assembly
  3. Job 38:7 — present at creation, before mankind
  - *All three uses refer to supernatural beings, not humans*
- **Psalm 29:1; 89:6** — "sons of the mighty" (bene elim / bene elohim) — same general category; supernatural beings in God's presence
- **Deuteronomy 32:8 (LXX and Dead Sea Scrolls)** — "He separated the sons of Adam; He set the bounds of the people according to the number of the sons of God (bene Elohim / angelon theou)" — the nations were divided according to the number of heavenly beings assigned to them
- **The Sethite view** (sons of God = godly men from Seth's line): this interpretation requires that bene ha-Elohim means something in Genesis 6 that it never means anywhere else in the Old Testament; it also raises the question of why godly Seth-line men would produce "mighty men" and "men of renown" of supernatural stature simply by marrying ungodly women — the text's own explanation of the Nephilim result requires something more than merely mixed marriages
- **Context determines:** The Bible interprets this term consistently as supernatural beings (Job 1:6; 2:1; 38:7). Jude 6 and 2 Peter 2:4 confirm the angelic sin interpretation. The Nephilim result (extraordinary beings) fits the supernatural parentage.

### IMAGINATION / INCLINATION (Hebrew: yetzer)

**Genesis 6:5** — "every IMAGINATION (yetzer) of the thoughts of his heart was only evil continually"

- **Yetzer** = formed thing, frame, inclination — from yatsar (to form, to shape; the same word used when God "formed" man in Genesis 2:7); the yetzer is what has been formed or shaped in the heart — the deep inclination, the bent of the inner life
- **Two yetzers in rabbinic thought:** Jewish tradition speaks of yetzer ha-tov (the good inclination) and yetzer ha-ra (the evil inclination) — this distinction arises from Scripture: Genesis 6:5 describes the yetzer as entirely evil; Genesis 8:21 repeats this after the flood; but Deuteronomy 6:5 commands loving God with "all your heart" — implying an inclination that can be directed toward God
- **Genesis 8:21** — "the imagination (yetzer) of man's heart is evil from his youth" — the same word; God repeats this assessment after the flood; the flood did not change the yetzer
- **Jeremiah 18:11-12** — "Return ye now every one from his evil way, and make your ways and your doings good. And they said, There is no hope: but we will walk after our own devices (yetzer), and we will every one do the imagination of his evil heart" — the yetzer as the stubborn inner inclination that resists God's call
- **Isaiah 26:3** — "Thou wilt keep him in perfect peace, whose mind (yetzer) is stayed on thee" — the redeemed yetzer: when the inclination is fixed on God, peace follows; the positive counterpart to Genesis 6:5
- **Romans 8:7** — "The carnal mind is enmity against God" — Paul's theological statement of what Genesis 6:5 shows narratively; the formed inclination of the unregenerate heart is hostility toward God
- *Interpretation:* The yetzer of Genesis 6:5 is not merely bad choices — it is the entire bent of the inner life, its deepest inclination, its formed direction. "Only evil continually" means the yetzer had no admixture of good — it was wholly directed away from God. This is the diagnosis that makes the new birth (John 3:3-7) and the new heart (Ezekiel 36:26) necessary — not reform of the old yetzer but a new one.

### GRACE (Hebrew: chen)

**Genesis 6:8** — "But Noah found GRACE (chen) in the eyes of the LORD"

- **First occurrence of chen in Scripture** — the word appears 69 times in the Hebrew Bible
- **Chen** = grace, favor, loveliness — from chanan (to be gracious, to show favor); it is always something given by the stronger to the weaker; it is never deserved or earned
- **"Found" grace** — Noah did not manufacture, produce, or earn grace; he found it; it was already there, a gift in God's disposition toward him
- **Context of the first mention:** grace appears immediately after the most comprehensive indictment of human wickedness in Scripture (v.5 — "only evil continually") and immediately before God's announcement of destruction. Grace is found in the worst possible context — total depravity, universal judgment. This is the pattern of grace throughout Scripture: it appears where it is least deserved and most needed.
- **Proverbs 3:34** — "He giveth grace (chen) unto the lowly" — grace is given to the humble; Noah's walk with God (v.9) implies the humility that makes grace findable
- **Zechariah 12:10** — "I will pour upon the house of David... the spirit of grace (chen) and of supplications" — grace poured out in the context of Israel's mourning for the One they pierced; again, grace in the context of guilt
- **John 1:17** — "For the law was given by Moses, but grace and truth came by Jesus Christ" — the New Testament fulfillment of chen; grace reaches its fullest expression in Christ
- **Ephesians 2:8** — "By grace are ye saved through faith" — the Noah pattern formalized into doctrine
- *Interpretation:* The first mention of grace in Scripture establishes its essential character: it is unilateral (God gives), unconditional (not Noah's merit), and it appears against the backdrop of deserved judgment. Every subsequent use of grace in Scripture is an amplification of Genesis 6:8.

### PERFECT / BLAMELESS (Hebrew: tamim)

**Genesis 6:9** — "Noah was a just man and PERFECT (tamim) in his generations"

- **Tamim** = complete, whole, without blemish, blameless — from tamam (to be complete, to be finished)
- **Not sinless:** The same word is used of:
  - Sacrificial animals "without blemish" (Leviticus 1:3, 10; 3:1) — physically whole, meeting the standard required, not morally perfect in a philosophical sense
  - Job: "perfect and upright" (Job 1:1) — yet Job offered sacrifices for potential sins of his children (Job 1:5) and later repented before God (Job 42:5-6); tamim = blameless in conduct, not sinless in nature
  - Deuteronomy 18:13 — "Thou shalt be PERFECT (tamim) with the LORD thy God" — complete in loyalty and integrity, not sinlessly flawless
- **"In his generations"** — this phrase has been read two ways: (1) "among his contemporaries" — Noah was blameless compared to the corrupt generation around him; (2) "in his genealogy/lineage" — his genetic line was uncorrupted; the second reading connects to the Nephilim corruption of human lines, suggesting Noah's lineage alone remained pure
- **Both readings are consistent with the text and with Scripture's pattern** — Noah was blameless in conduct (Hebrews 11:7 — "righteous... by faith") and he came from Seth's godly line (Genesis 5) which had not been corrupted as Cain's line was
- **Hebrews 11:7** — "by the which he condemned the world, and became heir of the righteousness which is by faith" — Noah's "perfection" was righteousness by faith, not self-generated moral excellence

### CORRUPT / DESTROY (Hebrew: shachath)

**Genesis 6:11-13** — "The earth also was CORRUPT (shachath) before God... for all flesh had CORRUPTED (shachath) his way... I will DESTROY (shachath) them with the earth"

- **Shachath** = to destroy, to corrupt, to ruin, to spoil — the same Hebrew word is used in this chapter for BOTH the earth's moral corruption (v.11-12) AND God's act of destruction (v.13, 17)
- **This is deliberate and theologically loaded:** The earth corrupted (shachath) itself → God will destroy (shachath) it. The judgment mirrors the sin in the very word chosen. What man did to the moral order, God will do to the physical world. The form of the judgment reflects the nature of the sin.
- **Leviticus 18:24-25** — "Defile not ye yourselves in any of these things: for in all these the nations are defiled which I cast out before you: And the land is defiled: therefore I do visit the iniquity thereof upon it, and the land itself vomiteth out her inhabitants" — the land is corrupted by sin and God's judgment cleanses it
- **Numbers 35:33** — "So ye shall not pollute the land wherein ye are: for blood it defileth the land: and the land cannot be cleansed of the blood that is shed therein, but by the blood of him that shed it" — innocent blood corrupts the land; judgment cleanses it
- **Isaiah 24:5** — "The earth also is defiled under the inhabitants thereof; because they have transgressed the laws, changed the ordinance, broken the everlasting covenant" — the prophet echoes Genesis 6:11-12 in describing end-times corruption
- *Interpretation:* The shachath word-play is the Bible's own commentary on the justice of the flood: mankind corrupted the earth; the same action — shachath — comes back upon them from God's hand. The punishment fits the crime in its very vocabulary. The flood is not arbitrary; it is the precise, proportionate reversal of what mankind did.

### VIOLENCE (Hebrew: chamas)

**Genesis 6:11, 13** — "the earth was filled with VIOLENCE (chamas)"

- **Chamas** = violence, wrong, injustice, cruelty — encompassing physical violence, oppression, judicial injustice, and moral outrage; a strong word for the violation of persons and of justice
- **Obadiah 10** — "For thy violence (chamas) against thy brother Jacob shame shall cover thee" — Edom's violence against Israel
- **Jonah 3:8** — "Let them turn every one from his evil way, and from the VIOLENCE (chamas) that is in their hands" — the Ninevites repent of chamas; God relents
- **Habakkuk 1:2-3** — "O LORD, how long shall I cry, and thou wilt not hear! even cry out unto thee of VIOLENCE (chamas), and thou wilt not save! Why dost thou shew me iniquity, and cause me to behold grievance? for spoiling and VIOLENCE (chamas) are before me" — Habakkuk's complaint about unpunished violence uses the same word as Genesis 6:11
- **Ezekiel 7:23** — "Make a chain: for the land is full of bloody crimes, and the city is full of VIOLENCE (chamas)" — Ezekiel's pre-judgment indictment echoes Genesis 6:11
- **Micah 6:12** — "For the rich men thereof are full of VIOLENCE (chamas), and the inhabitants thereof have spoken lies" — prophetic indictment using the flood-word
- **Matthew 24:37** — Jesus: "as the days of Noe were, so shall also the coming of the Son of man be" — the violence (chamas) of Genesis 6:11 is part of the prophetic template for the last days
- *Interpretation:* Chamas is specifically the word that triggers God's decision to destroy (v.13 — "the earth is filled with violence [chamas]"). It is the word that connects Genesis 6 to the prophets and to the last days. When the prophets use chamas, they are invoking the flood-standard of violence — the level of injustice and cruelty that calls down judgment.

### PITCH / COVERING (Hebrew: kopher)

**Genesis 6:14** — "shalt thou make in the ark, and shalt PITCH it within and without with PITCH (kopher)"

- **Kopher** = pitch, tar, bitumen — the waterproofing material applied to the ark; it is what kept the water of judgment out
- **The root connection:** The Hebrew word kopher (pitch) shares its root with kaphar (to cover, to atone, to make atonement) — the word behind the entire sacrificial atonement system of Leviticus; the "mercy seat" (kapporeth) is from the same root
- **The only other use of kopher as pitch in the Bible:** Exodus 2:3 — Moses' basket was "daubed with slime and with pitch (kopher)" — another vessel of salvation, pitched to keep the water out, carrying the one through whom Israel would be redeemed
- **Kaphar / atonement uses:**
  - Leviticus 17:11 — "The blood... maketh an atonement (kaphar) for the soul"
  - The Day of Atonement (Yom Kippur) — from kappar; the day when covering is made for sin
  - Numbers 35:33 — "the land cannot be cleansed (kaphar)" without blood
- *Interpretation:* The pitch that covered (kopher) the ark is the same root as the atonement (kaphar) that covers sin. The ark was kept safe from the waters of judgment by a covering — a kaphar/kopher. The believer is kept safe from the judgment of God by the covering of Christ's blood — the ultimate kaphar. This is not a coincidence of vocabulary; it is the Bible's built-in typology embedded in the very word choice. The ark's waterproofing is a picture of the atonement.

### GOPHER WOOD

**Genesis 6:14** — "Make thee an ark of GOPHER WOOD"

- **Gopher** = a type of wood identified only here in Scripture; the word appears nowhere else in the Hebrew Bible
- The exact species is unknown; suggestions include cypress, cedar, pine, or laminated wood
- **The theological point is not the species** — it is that God specifies the material; the ark is built to God's exact specifications, not Noah's preference; obedience is total (v.22 — "according to all that God commanded him, so did he")
- The ark is the type of Christ — and just as God specified the exact materials, dimensions, and design of the tabernacle (Exodus 25-30 — "according to the pattern," Hebrews 8:5), so He specifies the ark. Salvation is on God's terms, not man's.

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The Ark as Type of Salvation in Christ

**Genesis 6:14-18** — The ark: one vessel, specific dimensions, pitched inside and out, one door, one window above, three stories

**The type unpacked:**

- **One ark, one way of salvation:** There was no second ark. There was no partial safety near the ark. You were either inside or outside — and the flood killed everyone outside. **John 14:6** — "I am the way, the truth, and the life: no man cometh unto the Father, but by me." **Acts 4:12** — "Neither is there salvation in any other: for there is none other name under heaven given among men, whereby we must be saved."

- **The door in the SIDE:** Genesis 6:16 — "the door of the ark shalt thou set in the SIDE thereof." Genesis 7:16 — "And the LORD shut him in." The door was in the side of the ark; it was God who shut it. **John 19:34** — "But one of the soldiers with a spear pierced his SIDE, and forthwith came there out blood and water" — from Christ's pierced side flows the way into salvation; the door of salvation is through His opened side.

- **The window above:** Genesis 6:16 — "A window shalt thou make to the ark, and in a cubit shalt thou finish it ABOVE." The only light in the ark came from above — not from the sides, not from below, but from God's direction. **James 1:17** — "Every good gift and every perfect gift is from above, and cometh down from the Father of lights." The light of the believer's life comes from above alone.

- **The pitch (kopher) = atonement (kaphar):** The same root word; the covering that kept the water of judgment out of the ark is the covering of atonement that keeps the wrath of God from falling on the believer. **Romans 3:25** — Christ was set forth as a "propitiation" (Greek: hilasterion = covering, mercy seat) — the ultimate kopher/kaphar.

- **The dimensions:** 300 × 50 × 30 cubits. The ratio 300:50 (6:1) is recognized by naval architects as an optimal stability ratio for seakeeping in open water — not discovered in modern shipbuilding until thousands of years after Genesis was written. The ark was not a round vessel or a simple box — it was a barge-shaped hull optimized for riding out waves rather than navigating; it did not need to go anywhere, only to float and survive.

- **Three stories:** A three-level structure housing all of creation — anticipating the three-court structure of the tabernacle (outer court, holy place, holy of holies) and the three-heaven structure of Pauline cosmology (2 Corinthians 12:2).

- **1 Peter 3:20-21** — "Wherein few, that is, eight souls were saved by water. The like figure whereunto even baptism doth also now save us (not the putting away of the filth of the flesh, but the answer of a good conscience toward God,) by the resurrection of Jesus Christ" — Peter explicitly calls the ark a "figure" (Greek: antitupos = antitype, a figure corresponding to something else). The ark is the type; baptism (= union with Christ in His death and resurrection) is the antitype. The water of judgment surrounded the ark and those inside were saved; the water of baptism pictures death to the old life and resurrection to new.

**Genesis 6:8-9** — "But Noah found grace... Noah was a just man and perfect... and Noah walked with God"

- **Romans 5:17** — "They which receive abundance of grace and of the gift of righteousness shall reign in life by one, Jesus Christ" — the "one man" pattern: one righteous man, grace received, many saved
- **2 Peter 2:5** — "And spared not the old world, but saved Noah the eighth person, a preacher of righteousness" — Noah is specifically called a "preacher of righteousness"; he proclaimed coming judgment for 120 years (the construction period); like Christ, he preached and was rejected by all but the remnant
- **The eight souls:** Genesis 7:13; 1 Peter 3:20 — eight people were saved. Eight = the number of new beginning (the eighth day, circumcision on the eighth day — Leviticus 12:3; Christ's resurrection is on the first day = the eighth day of the previous week). The eight souls preserved through judgment begin a new creation — type of the new creation in Christ (2 Corinthians 5:17).

**Jesus' own use of the Genesis 6 type:**

**What "days of Noah" conditions look like per Scripture:**

- **The ark as the preached message:** Noah did not just preach with words — he preached with a massive structure; the ark itself was the visible, undeniable evidence that Noah believed God. For 120 years, the ark rose from the ground as a monument to coming judgment. Everyone who saw it had a choice.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED / MISAPPLIED PASSAGES

### "Sons of God" = Godly Men from Seth's Line (The Sethite View Examined)

**The Misuse:** A common interpretation holds that "sons of God" in Genesis 6:2 refers to men from Seth's godly line (Genesis 4:26 — "men began to call upon the name of the LORD") marrying women from Cain's ungodly line ("daughters of men"). This view avoids the supernatural interpretation.

**What Scripture says:**

- **The phrase bene ha-Elohim in Job 1:6; 2:1; 38:7** — never refers to humans; always to supernatural beings in the divine assembly; the phrase does not carry a human meaning anywhere else in the Old Testament
- **The result — Nephilim:** If Seth's men simply married Cain's women, why would the offspring be "mighty men... men of renown" of extraordinary stature (Numbers 13:33)? Normal intermarriage between human groups produces normal human offspring — not giants and legendary warriors. The Nephilim require a more than natural explanation.
- **"And also after that"** (Genesis 6:4) — the Nephilim appear both before and after the flood; Numbers 13:33; Deuteronomy 2:10-11; 3:11; 1 Samuel 17:4 (Goliath) all describe post-flood giant clans. This ongoing post-flood appearance is consistent with a second incursion of fallen angels after the flood.
- **Jude 6-7** — the angels who "left their own habitation" are placed in immediate sequence with sexual sin ("going after strange flesh") — the connection is deliberate; Jude presents the angelic transgression and Sodom's sexual transgression as analogous cases of crossing God-set boundaries
- **2 Peter 2:4-5** — the angels that sinned are placed in the same sentence as the flood and Noah; their sin is connected to the pre-flood period
- *Correction from Scripture:* The Bible's own use of bene ha-Elohim, combined with the Nephilim result, Jude 6-7, and 2 Peter 2:4-5, supports the supernatural interpretation. The Sethite view requires reading a human meaning into a phrase that Scripture never uses that way. This is one of the clearest cases where the Bible's own usage must interpret the text, even when the result is difficult.

---

### "An Hundred and Twenty Years" (v.3) — Misread as a Universal Lifespan Cap

**The Misuse:** Genesis 6:3 — "yet his days shall be an hundred and twenty years" — is frequently interpreted as God declaring that human lifespans would henceforth be capped at 120 years.

**What Scripture says:**

- **Genesis 11:10-32** — Post-flood lifespans remain in the hundreds: Shem 600 years, Arphaxad 438, Salah 433, Eber 464, Peleg 239, Reu 239, Serug 230, Nahor 148, Terah 205. If the 120-year cap were in effect immediately after Genesis 6:3, these numbers would be impossible.
- **Deuteronomy 34:7** — Moses died at exactly 120 — but this is a specific historical note about Moses, not a universal application; and Moses died after the 120-year principle had been in effect for long enough for some individuals to reach it naturally
- **Psalm 90:10** — "The days of our years are threescore years and ten" — written by Moses for his own generation; this is a general observation about the human lifespan in Moses' time, not a decree from Genesis 6:3
- **The context of Genesis 6:3:** God is speaking to Noah (or to the antediluvian world) about the time remaining before the flood. "His days" refers to humanity's remaining days before judgment — 120 more years, then the flood comes. This is a countdown to judgment, not a biological ceiling.
- *Correction from Scripture:* The post-flood genealogies of Genesis 11 make the "lifespan cap" reading impossible without contradicting the text itself. Genesis 6:3 is a countdown to the flood, not a biological decree. The gradual reduction of human lifespan through Genesis 11 to Psalm 90:10 is a separate phenomenon — the consequence of accumulated genetic deterioration after the flood and after God's stated limiting of man's days in general (Genesis 6:3 may be a general statement that God would not allow the pre-flood lifespans to continue indefinitely, but it does not function as an immediate cap).

---

### "Perfect in His Generations" (v.9) — Misread as Genetic Purity Alone

**The Misuse:** Some read "perfect in his generations" as exclusively referring to Noah's bloodline being genetically uncorrupted by Nephilim admixture — making the flood primarily a genetic cleansing rather than a moral judgment.

**What Scripture says:**

- **The phrase "tamim in his generations"** does have the possible reading of "without blemish in his lineage/descent" — and this reading is consistent with the context of Nephilim corruption of human lines
- **However, Hebrews 11:7** does not describe Noah's salvation in genetic terms: "By FAITH Noah, being warned of God of things not seen as yet, moved with fear, prepared an ark to the saving of his house; by the which he condemned the world, and became heir of the RIGHTEOUSNESS which is by FAITH" — faith and righteousness are the operative categories
- **Genesis 6:8-9** sequence: Noah found GRACE (v.8) → Noah was just, perfect, walked with God (v.9) — the moral and relational qualities are listed immediately after the statement of grace; they are descriptors of a man of faith, not merely a genetic profile
- **2 Peter 2:5** — "saved Noah the eighth person, a PREACHER OF RIGHTEOUSNESS" — a preacher of righteousness is a moral and theological category, not a genetic one
- *Correction from Scripture:* "Perfect in his generations" likely carries both meanings simultaneously — blameless in conduct (Hebrews 11:7; 2 Peter 2:5) and uncorrupted in lineage (consistent with the Nephilim corruption context). Both are true. The error is in reducing it to genetics only and thereby making the flood primarily a genetic event. The flood is primarily a moral judgment (v.5 — "every imagination only evil continually"; v.11 — "filled with violence") with Noah preserved by grace through faith.

---

### "It Repented the LORD" (v.6) — Misused to Argue God Changes His Mind / Is Not Omniscient

**The Misuse:** Genesis 6:6 ("it repented the LORD") is used to argue that God did not know in advance that man would become wicked — that He was surprised and had to change course — therefore God is not truly omniscient.

**What Scripture says:**

- **1 Samuel 15:29** — "And also the Strength of Israel will not lie nor repent: for he is not a man, that he should repent" — in the same chapter where God is said to "repent" of making Saul king (v.11), the prophet also states God does NOT repent as a man does; both are true simultaneously
- **Numbers 23:19** — "God is not a man, that he should lie; neither the son of man, that he should repent: hath he said, and shall he not do it?" — God's utterances do not require revision
- **Malachi 3:6** — "For I am the LORD, I change not"
- **Isaiah 46:9-10** — "I am God, and there is none like me, Declaring the end from the beginning, and from ancient times the things that are not yet done" — God knows the end from the beginning; the flood is not a surprise correction
- **Acts 2:23** — Christ delivered "by the determinate counsel and foreknowledge of God" — everything in redemptive history, including the flood and the cross, is within God's determinate counsel
- **The resolution:** The "repenting" of God is anthropopathic language — language that describes God's real emotional/relational response in terms a human being can understand. God genuinely grieves over sin (this is real, not merely rhetorical). God does not change His eternal character, decrees, or foreknowledge. Both truths are maintained by Scripture; neither cancels the other.
- *Correction from Scripture:* "It repented the LORD" describes God's genuine grief-response to human wickedness — a real emotional state, not a figure of speech about nothing. But it does not indicate a change of God's foreknowledge or eternal purpose. The same prophet can say God "repents" (expresses grief and relents in manner of dealing) and also say God "does not repent" (does not change His ultimate character or will) — because these refer to different aspects of God's relationship to time and to His creatures.

## FIRST MENTION PRINCIPLES

### First Mention of GRACE (chen)

**Genesis 6:8** — "But Noah found grace (chen) in the eyes of the LORD"
- First occurrence of the word grace in all of Scripture
- Context: appears immediately after the most comprehensive statement of human depravity (v.5) and in the shadow of announced global judgment (v.7)
- Pattern established: grace appears where it is least deserved and most needed; it is found, not earned; it is given by God, not generated by man
- The entire New Testament doctrine of grace (Ephesians 2:8-9; Romans 3:24; Titus 3:7) is the unfolding of this first word

### First Mention of COVENANT (berith) with an Individual

**Genesis 6:18** — "But with thee will I establish my covenant"
- First explicit named covenant in Scripture between God and a human being
- God initiates, defines, and establishes; Noah's role is obedience (v.22), not negotiation
- The covenant is given BEFORE the flood — Noah acts on God's word before seeing the rain; this is the faith principle (Hebrews 11:7) embedded in the covenant structure
- All subsequent biblical covenants follow this model: God initiates, declares, and keeps

### First Mention of VIOLENCE (chamas) as a Civilization-Ending Condition

**Genesis 6:11** — "the earth was filled with violence (chamas)"
- First use of chamas in Scripture as a diagnostic of civilization's moral state
- Establishes that violence — not merely spiritual corruption but physical injustice and cruelty — is the tipping point that calls for judgment
- Every subsequent prophetic use of chamas invokes the Genesis 6 standard

### First Mention of GOD'S GRIEF (atsab)

**Genesis 6:6** — "it grieved him at his heart"
- First time Scripture describes God as experiencing grief/pain in response to human sin
- Uses the same root word (atsab) as the sorrow/pain of Genesis 3:16-17 (the curse); God bears the pain He pronounced
- Establishes: God is not distant from human sin — it wounds Him; this is not weakness but the pain of a holy nature encountering its opposite

### First Mention of SPECIFIC BUILDING INSTRUCTIONS FROM GOD

**Genesis 6:14-16** — Ark dimensions, materials, pitch, window, door, three stories
- First time God gives precise building specifications in Scripture
- The pattern recurs: tabernacle (Exodus 25-30 — "according to the pattern"), temple (1 Chronicles 28:11-19 — "the pattern of all that he had by the spirit"), new Jerusalem (Revelation 21:15-17)
- Establishes: when God commissions a structure for redemptive purposes, He specifies it precisely; salvation is on God's terms, to God's design, not man's improvisation

### First Mention of TOTAL HUMAN DEPRAVITY AS AN OBSERVED FACT

**Genesis 6:5** — "every imagination of the thoughts of his heart was only evil continually"
- First comprehensive statement of human moral corruption in Scripture
- Five absolutes leave no exception: every, thoughts, heart, only, continually
- The same assessment is repeated in Genesis 8:21 after the flood, proving the condition is in human nature, not in environment
- This is the foundational text behind the New Testament doctrine of total depravity (Romans 3:10-18; Jeremiah 17:9; Ephesians 2:1-3)
