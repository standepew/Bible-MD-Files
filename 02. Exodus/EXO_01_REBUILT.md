## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Exodus opens where Genesis closed — with Israel in Egypt. The seventy souls who descended with Jacob have multiplied into a nation. A new Pharaoh, who did not know Joseph, arose and was threatened by Israel's growth. He enslaved them with hard bondage in mortar and brick, but the more they were afflicted, the more they multiplied. Pharaoh then commanded the Hebrew midwives, Shiphrah and Puah, to kill every male child at birth. The midwives feared God and disobeyed — and God rewarded them with houses. Pharaoh escalated his decree to all Egypt: every Hebrew son born must be cast into the river. The chapter establishes the setting for Moses' birth and the entire redemption narrative — a people under murderous oppression, crying out to a covenant God who has not forgotten.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Seventy Souls" — The Number That Entered Egypt

**Exodus 1:5** — "And all the souls that came out of the loins of Jacob were seventy souls"

- **Genesis 46:27** — "All the souls of the house of Jacob, which came into Egypt, were threescore and ten" — the number 70 established at entry
- **Deuteronomy 10:22** — "Thy fathers went down into Egypt with threescore and ten persons; and now the LORD thy God hath made thee as the stars of heaven for multitude" — Moses himself recalls this number and contrasts it with the multitude God produced
- **Genesis 15:13-14** — "Know of a surety that thy seed shall be a stranger in a land that is not theirs, and shall serve them; and they shall afflict them four hundred years... and afterward shall they come out with great substance" — God told Abraham this would happen; Exodus 1 is the beginning of that prophecy's fulfillment
- *Interpretation:* The number 70 is not incidental. It is the seed count — the starting point God uses to produce a nation. Deuteronomy 10:22 connects 70 souls to the stars-of-heaven promise of Genesis 15 and 22. The same principle applies to Daniel's 70 weeks — God counts in covenant numbers when He is working His redemptive purposes.

### "The More They Afflicted Them, the More They Multiplied" — Persecution Cannot Stop God's Purpose

**Exodus 1:12** — "But the more they afflicted them, the more they multiplied and grew"

- **Genesis 1:28** — "Be fruitful, and multiply, and replenish the earth" — the creation mandate; God's blessing cannot be reversed by human oppression
- **Genesis 12:2-3** — "I will make of thee a great nation... I will bless them that bless thee, and curse him that curseth thee" — Pharaoh is cursing the seed of Abraham; the Abrahamic covenant protects them
- **Acts 8:1-4** — persecution scatters the early church — and the scattered believers "went every where preaching the word" — the same pattern: affliction produces spread
- **Acts 12:24** — "But the word of God grew and multiplied" — New Testament language directly echoing Exodus 1
- **Philippians 1:12** — "The things which happened unto me have fallen out rather unto the furtherance of the gospel"
- **Isaiah 54:17** — "No weapon that is formed against thee shall prosper"
- *Interpretation:* The multiplication of Israel under affliction is not a historical accident — it is the Abrahamic covenant operating. Pharaoh's "wisdom" was worldly wisdom; God's wisdom uses the enemy's plan to advance His own. This pattern repeats: Exodus 1 is the historical prototype of what Acts 8 and every era of church history confirms.

### "A New King Which Knew Not Joseph" — Forgotten Deliverance

**Exodus 1:8** — "Now there arose up a new king over Egypt, which knew not Joseph"

- **Genesis 41:39-44** — Joseph saved Egypt from famine; Pharaoh honored him above all
- **Psalm 105:16-22** — "He sent a man before them, even Joseph who was sold for a servant... to bind his princes at his pleasure, and teach his senators wisdom"
- **Ecclesiastes 9:15** — "Yet no man remembered that same poor man" — human memory of deliverance is short; only God remembers faithfully
- **Judges 2:10** — "There arose another generation after them, which knew not the LORD, nor yet the works which he had done for Israel" — the same pattern: a generation forgets the works of God, and oppression follows
- **John 1:10-11** — "He was in the world, and the world was made by him, and the world knew him not. He came unto his own, and his own received him not" — the ultimate fulfillment: the world did not know the One through whom it was made and sustained
- *Interpretation:* "Knew not Joseph" is more than a historical note — it is a spiritual diagnosis. When a people forget what God did through His servant, persecution of God's people follows. The pattern recurs in Judges, in Israel's later history, and reaches its fullest expression in the rejection of Christ.

### The Midwives' Fear of God — Civil Disobedience Grounded in the Fear of God

**Exodus 1:17** — "But the midwives feared God, and did not as the king of Egypt commanded them"

- **Acts 5:29** — "We ought to obey God rather than men" — Peter states the exact principle the midwives applied
- **Daniel 3:18** — Shadrach, Meshach, Abednego: "We will not serve thy gods" — same principle under Babylonian authority
- **Daniel 6:10** — Daniel continued praying despite the royal decree
- **Hebrews 11:23** — "By faith Moses, when he was born, was hid three months of his parents, because they saw he was a proper child; and they were not afraid of the king's commandment"
- **Revelation 13:15-17** — the end-time decree: those who will not comply face death; the midwives' choice previews the ultimate end-time choice
- *Interpretation:* The midwives are the first recorded example in Scripture of civil disobedience rooted in the fear of God. Scripture does not present this as rebellion — it presents it as wisdom and faithfulness, rewarded by God. The principle is consistent: when human law requires violation of God's law, God's law takes priority. Exodus 1 sets the biblical precedent.

---

### "He Made Them Houses" — God Rewards Those Who Fear Him Above Kings

**Exodus 1:21** — "Because the midwives feared God, that he made them houses"

- **Proverbs 9:10** — "The fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom"
- **Psalm 128:1-3** — "Blessed is every one that feareth the LORD... thy wife shall be as a fruitful vine... thy children like olive plants" — the fear of the LORD produces household blessing
- **1 Samuel 2:35** — God builds a "sure house" for those faithful to Him
- **2 Samuel 7:11** — God tells David "the LORD will make thee an house" — covenant language: faithfulness receives a house/dynasty/legacy
- *Interpretation:* "He made them houses" is covenant language. Faithful obedience to God in the face of ungodly authority results in God building your household. The midwives risked everything — God gave them families. Those who fear God above men receive what men tried to destroy: a future and a legacy.

---

### Pharaoh's Infanticide Decree — The Serpent Attacking the Seed

**Exodus 1:16, 22** — Kill every Hebrew son

- **Genesis 3:15** — "I will put enmity between thee and the woman, and between thy seed and her seed" — the serpent's war against the seed announced; it runs throughout all of Scripture
- **Matthew 2:16** — Herod slaughters all male children under two in Bethlehem — identical pattern at the birth of Christ
- **Revelation 12:4** — "The dragon stood before the woman which was ready to be delivered, for to devour her child as soon as it was born" — the symbolic unveiling of the entire pattern
- **Jeremiah 31:15** — Rachel weeping for her children — quoted in Matthew 2:18 in the context of Herod's slaughter; the Exodus pattern repeats at Christ's birth
- *Interpretation:* Pharaoh's decree is not merely political — it is a manifestation of the Genesis 3:15 enmity. The serpent, working through human kingdoms, always targets the male seed because the Deliverer must come through the seed. Pattern: Pharaoh targets Hebrew males → Moses hidden → Israel delivered. Herod targets male children → Jesus hidden in Egypt → world redeemed. Exodus 1 is the historical outworking of Genesis 3:15; Revelation 12 is its apocalyptic unveiling.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### RIGOUR (Hebrew: perek)
- **Exodus 1:13-14** — "served with rigour" — perek = crushing severity, breaking harshness
- **Leviticus 25:43, 46, 53** — God forbids ruling over Israelite servants "with rigour" — the same word; the Law uses the Exodus experience as the standard of what is forbidden
- *Definition here:* Ruthless, crushing oppression designed to break the spirit — not merely hard labor but systematic degradation

### FEARED (Hebrew: yare)
- **Exodus 1:17** — "the midwives feared God" — yare = reverential awe that governs conduct
- Distinct from the political terror Pharaoh sought to instill
- **Proverbs 1:7** — "The fear of the LORD is the beginning of knowledge" — the midwives' yare overrode Pharaoh's threats
- *Definition here:* Fear of God = reverential awe producing obedience to God even at personal cost; the governing principle of the midwives' choice

### MULTIPLIED (Hebrew: rabah + sharat)
- **Exodus 1:7** — "increased abundantly" (sharat = swarmed/teemed as living creatures), "multiplied" (rabah = became great in number)
- The language echoes **Genesis 1:20-22** — the same words used for God's blessing on sea creatures and birds at creation
- *Definition here:* Israel's multiplication is the creation blessing of Genesis 1 operating through covenant promise — God's design cannot be frustrated by Pharaoh

### TASKMASTERS (Hebrew: sar mas)
- **Exodus 1:11** — "they did set over them taskmasters" — sar mas = officers of forced tribute/labor
- Same system appears in **1 Kings 5:13-14** — Solomon's forced labor levy; Israel under Solomon mirrors Israel in Egypt — a warning embedded in the text
- *Definition here:* Government-appointed overseers whose purpose is extraction of labor through compulsion and threat

**21** And it came to pass, because the midwives feared God, that he made them houses.
**22** And Pharaoh charged all his people, saying, Every son that is born ye shall cast into the river, and every daughter ye shall save alive.

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### Israel in Egypt as Type of the Church in the World

- Israel is God's distinct people surrounded by a hostile world system
- They are fruitful precisely because God's covenant blessing is on them — the world cannot stop it
- **John 15:19** — "If ye were of the world, the world would love his own: but because ye are not of the world, but I have chosen you out of the world, therefore the world hateth you"
- **1 Peter 2:11** — "Dearly beloved, I beseech you as strangers and pilgrims" — the New Testament believer occupies the same positional reality as Israel in Egypt: resident but not native

### Pharaoh as Type of the World System / the Dragon

- Pharaoh is never named in Exodus — he functions as a role/type, not merely a historical individual
- **Ezekiel 29:3** — God addresses Pharaoh directly as "the great dragon that lieth in the midst of his rivers" — the Bible itself identifies Pharaoh with the dragon
- **Revelation 12:3-4** — the dragon pursuing the woman and child; Pharaoh's decree against male children is the historical forerunner of the end-time dragon's war against the seed
- The pattern: Dragon (Pharaoh) attacks the male seed → Deliverer is hidden and preserved → Deliverer emerges → Redemption accomplished

### "Cast into the River" — Water as Death and Resurrection Type

- **Exodus 1:22** — every Hebrew son cast into the Nile (death decree)
- **Exodus 2:3** — Moses placed in the Nile and drawn out alive
- Moses' very name (drawn out of water) encodes the resurrection pattern
- **Romans 6:3-4** — baptism: buried with Christ, raised to newness of life
- **1 Corinthians 10:1-2** — Paul reads the water events of Exodus as baptism types: "all were baptized unto Moses in the cloud and in the sea"
- What Pharaoh intended for death became the setting for preservation and commissioning

### The Seventy Souls — Seed Number for a Nation

- 70 souls enter Egypt → a nation exits
- **Luke 10:1** — Jesus sends out 70 disciples — the harvest number; the new people of God going into all the world
- **Deuteronomy 32:8** — "He set the bounds of the people according to the number of the children of Israel" — 70 nations of Genesis 10, 70 of Jacob; God calibrates the nations around His covenant people

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### The River (Nile) — Judgment and Salvation

- **Exodus 1:22** — instrument of Pharaoh's death decree
- **Exodus 2:3** — instrument of Moses' preservation
- **Exodus 7:17-21** — turned to blood in the first plague
- **Exodus 14** — the sea (water motif continued) becomes Israel's salvation and Egypt's destruction
- *Thread:* What the enemy intends for destruction, God repeatedly uses for preservation, judgment, and new life

### Male Children Under Threat — The Seed War Through History

- **Genesis 3:15** — seed war announced
- **Exodus 1:16** — Hebrew males targeted by Pharaoh
- **Matthew 2:16** — male children of Bethlehem targeted by Herod
- **Revelation 12:4-5** — the dragon seeks to devour the man child
- *Thread:* Every time the Deliverer is about to emerge, the serpent attacks the male seed. This is a consistent prophetic pattern embedded in the structure of redemptive history — not coincidence.
