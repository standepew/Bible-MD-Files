## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Moses is tending Jethro's flock at Horeb, the mountain of God, when he sees a bush burning but not consumed. He turns aside to investigate. The Angel of the LORD appears from within the fire; God calls Moses by name and commands him to remove his sandals — he is standing on holy ground. God identifies Himself as the God of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob. He declares that He has seen Israel's affliction, heard their cry, and will bring them out to a land flowing with milk and honey. He commissions Moses to go to Pharaoh. Moses objects with "Who am I?" God answers: I will be with thee. Moses asks God's name. God answers: I AM THAT I AM — "I AM hath sent me unto you" and "The LORD God of your fathers... this is my name for ever." God details the plan: elders will believe, Pharaoh will resist until the mighty hand, and Israel will leave Egypt with spoil.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### The Angel of the LORD in the Bush — Pre-Incarnate Christ

**Exodus 3:2** — "And the angel of the LORD appeared unto him in a flame of fire out of the midst of a bush"

- **Exodus 3:4** — "God called unto him out of the midst of the bush" — the Angel of the LORD and God are identified as the same Person in the passage itself
- **Acts 7:30-32** — Stephen identifies this as "an angel of the Lord" appearing to Moses, then quotes God speaking from the bush — confirming the identification
- **Acts 7:38** — "This is he, that was in the church in the wilderness with the angel which spake to him in the mount Sinai" — the same Angel is present at Sinai
- **Genesis 16:7-13** — The Angel of the LORD speaks to Hagar, and she calls Him "Thou God seest me" — the Angel of the LORD receives worship and is identified as God
- **Genesis 22:11-12** — The Angel of the LORD calls from heaven to stop Abraham; speaks as God: "for now I know that thou fearest God"
- **Judges 13:21-22** — Manoah realizes he has seen "the Angel of the LORD" and says "We shall surely die, because we have seen God"
- **Malachi 3:1** — "The Lord, whom ye seek, shall suddenly come to his temple, even the messenger (angel) of the covenant" — the Angel of the covenant is the Lord
- **John 8:58** — Jesus: "Before Abraham was, I am" — Jesus identifies Himself with the I AM of Exodus 3; the audience understood and took up stones
- *Interpretation:* The Angel of the LORD throughout the Old Testament is the pre-incarnate Son. The pattern is consistent: the Angel of the LORD is identified as God, receives worship appropriate only to God, speaks as God, and is never merely a created angel. Exodus 3 is one of the clearest theophanies — John 8:58 confirms Jesus claimed to be this same I AM who spoke from the bush.

### "Put Off Thy Shoes" — Holy Ground

**Exodus 3:5** — "the place whereon thou standest is holy ground"

- **Joshua 5:15** — The Commander of the LORD's army says to Joshua: "Loose thy shoe from off thy foot; for the place whereon thou standest is holy" — same command at Jericho; the same divine Presence
- **Isaiah 6:2** — Seraphim cover their feet before the LORD — even angelic beings respond to God's holiness with covered extremities
- *Interpretation:* The removal of sandals signifies standing before holy ground — ground made holy by the presence of God Himself. The act is an acknowledgment that the creature comes before the Creator without covering, without protection, without the barrier of normal human activity. It is a posture of total submission to holiness. The same command given to Moses and Joshua points to the same divine Presence.

### "I Am the God of Thy Father" — The God of the Living

**Exodus 3:6** — "I am the God of thy father, the God of Abraham, the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob"

- **Matthew 22:31-32** — Jesus uses this verse directly to prove the resurrection: "Have ye not read that which was spoken unto you by God, saying, I am the God of Abraham, and the God of Isaac, and the God of Jacob? God is not the God of the dead, but of the living." — Jesus interprets Exodus 3:6 as proof of resurrection; if God is still their God in Moses' day (long after their deaths), they must still be alive
- **Luke 20:37-38** — Same argument: "that the dead are raised, even Moses shewed at the bush, when he calleth the Lord the God of Abraham"
- *Interpretation:* Jesus' use of Exodus 3:6 in Matthew 22 is the definitive interpretation of this verse. The present tense "I AM" (not "I WAS") the God of Abraham proves the patriarchs are still living. This passage is therefore a resurrection proof text — embedded in the burning bush account. The Bible interprets its own grammar as theological argument.

### "I Have Surely Seen... Heard... Know" — God's Awareness of Suffering

**Exodus 3:7** — "I have surely seen the affliction of my people... and have heard their cry... for I know their sorrows"

- **Genesis 16:11** — "The LORD hath heard thy affliction" — to Hagar; God sees the individual
- **Psalm 34:15** — "The eyes of the LORD are upon the righteous, and his ears are open unto their cry"
- **Psalm 56:8** — "Thou tellest my wanderings: put thou my tears into thy bottle: are they not in thy book?"
- **Revelation 6:9-10** — The souls under the altar crying "How long, O Lord?" — God hears the cry of the martyred
- **Luke 18:7** — "Shall not God avenge his own elect, which cry day and night unto him?"
- *Interpretation:* The three verbs — seen, heard, know — are the covenant God's full engagement with His people's suffering. He is not distant or indifferent. The accumulation of "surely seen" (emphatic Hebrew doubled verb) + "heard" + "know their sorrows" is God's declaration that no suffering of His people goes unregistered. The pattern from Genesis to Revelation: the cry of the afflicted reaches God, and He moves in appointed time.

### I AM THAT I AM — The Name of God

**Exodus 3:14** — "And God said unto Moses, I AM THAT I AM: and he said, Thus shalt thou say unto the children of Israel, I AM hath sent me unto you."

- Hebrew: EHYEH ASHER EHYEH — from the verb hayah (to be, to exist, to become)
- Rendered in the third person as YHWH (Yahweh / Jehovah) = He Is / He Who Is
- **Exodus 3:15** — "The LORD God of your fathers... this is my name for ever, and this is my memorial unto all generations" — God declares this name is eternal and memorial
- **John 8:58** — "Before Abraham was, I AM (ego eimi)" — Jesus uses the Greek equivalent of EHYEH; the crowd understood the claim to divine identity and sought to stone Him
- **John 6:35** — "I am the bread of life"; **John 8:12** — "I am the light of the world"; **John 10:11** — "I am the good shepherd"; **John 11:25** — "I am the resurrection and the life"; **John 14:6** — "I am the way, the truth, and the life"; **John 15:1** — "I am the true vine"; **Revelation 1:8** — "I am Alpha and Omega"
- *Interpretation:* The seven "I AM" declarations of Christ in John's Gospel are deliberate echoes of Exodus 3:14. Jesus is not claiming a title — He is claiming to be the same YHWH who spoke from the burning bush. The Jews in John 8 understood this perfectly — that is why they reached for stones. The full circle: the I AM of Exodus 3 is the same I AM of John 8 and Revelation 1.

### "This Is My Name For Ever" — The Memorial Name

**Exodus 3:15** — "this is my name for ever, and this is my memorial unto all generations"

- **Psalm 135:13** — "Thy name, O LORD, endureth for ever; and thy memorial, O LORD, throughout all generations"
- **Psalm 9:16** — "The LORD is known by the judgment which he executeth"
- **Malachi 3:6** — "For I am the LORD, I change not; therefore ye sons of Jacob are not consumed" — the unchanging I AM is the basis of covenant faithfulness
- *Interpretation:* God anchors the name YHWH as the permanent, generational covenant name. This is not a name for one era — it is the memorial name for all of human history. The connection to His covenant with the patriarchs (Abraham, Isaac, Jacob) anchors the name in promise. Every time Israel called on the name of the LORD, they were invoking the God of Exodus 3 — the God who had seen, heard, and remembered.

---

### "A Land Flowing with Milk and Honey" — The Promised Land Description

**Exodus 3:8** — "unto a good land and a large, unto a land flowing with milk and honey"

- **Numbers 13:27** — The spies confirm it: "It truly floweth with milk and honey; and this is the fruit of it"
- **Deuteronomy 8:7-9** — Moses describes the land's abundance in detail
- **Hebrews 11:16** — "They desire a better country, that is, an heavenly: wherefore God is not ashamed to be called their God: for he hath prepared for them a city" — the earthly promised land is the type; the heavenly country is the antitype
- **Revelation 21:1-4** — The new creation: the ultimate fulfillment of the promised land
- *Interpretation:* "Land flowing with milk and honey" is first mentioned here as God's description of what He is bringing Israel to. The phrase occurs 20 times in Scripture. The earthly Canaan is a type of the promised inheritance — Hebrews 4 and 11 make explicit that the rest of Canaan pointed forward to the true rest of God. Exodus 3 establishes the first use of this phrase.

---

### "I Will Stretch Out My Hand" and "Pakod Yifkod" — Surely Visit

**Exodus 3:16** — "I have surely visited you" — pakod yifkod (the doubled infinitive of absolute certainty; same language as Genesis 50:24-25)
**Exodus 3:20** — "I will stretch out my hand, and smite Egypt"

- **Genesis 50:24-25** — Joseph's dying words: "God will surely visit you (pakod yifkod), and bring you out of this land" — this is the fulfillment of Joseph's prophecy; Exodus 3:16 uses the exact same doubled verb
- **Exodus 13:19** — Moses takes Joseph's bones — fulfilling the oath
- **Acts 7:17** — "When the time of the promise drew nigh, which God had sworn to Abraham"
- *Interpretation:* The doubled verb pakod yifkod = "visiting, He will visit" is an emphatic guarantee. Joseph used it on his deathbed (Genesis 50:24). God uses it at the burning bush (Exodus 3:16). The exact same linguistic form appears in both places — the Bible connecting Joseph's dying prophecy to its exact fulfillment at the moment of commissioning. Nothing in Scripture is incidental.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### ANGEL OF THE LORD (Hebrew: Malak YHWH)
- **Exodus 3:2** — appears in the flame; identified as God in verse 4
- Throughout Scripture the Angel of the LORD functions as God, is worshipped as God, and speaks as God
- Distinct from created angels who refuse worship (Revelation 22:8-9)
- *Definition here:* The pre-incarnate Son of God; the Second Person of the Godhead appearing in physical form before the Incarnation

### I AM (Hebrew: EHYEH; Greek: EGO EIMI)
- **Exodus 3:14** — EHYEH ASHER EHYEH = I AM THAT I AM / I AM WHO I AM / I WILL BE WHAT I WILL BE
- The verb hayah = to be, exist, become — God's name is the statement of pure self-existence
- He does not derive existence from anything; He IS existence itself
- **John 8:58** — Jesus: ego eimi — "I am" (present tense, not "I was") — making the same claim
- *Definition here:* The name of absolute, self-existent, eternal being — the One who has life in Himself and from whom all existence derives

### HOLY (Hebrew: qadosh)
- **Exodus 3:5** — "holy ground" — qadosh = set apart, consecrated, of a wholly different nature
- First use in Exodus: ground is holy because of divine Presence, not because of anything inherent in the ground
- **Isaiah 6:3** — "Holy, holy, holy, is the LORD of hosts" — the triple holy (trisagion) defines the absolute otherness of God
- *Definition here:* Set apart to God; of a different nature/category; ground or objects do not become holy of themselves — they are made holy by proximity to or designation by God

### MEMORIAL (Hebrew: zeker)
- **Exodus 3:15** — "this is my memorial unto all generations"
- zeker = remembrance, something that causes to be remembered
- **Psalm 135:13** — "thy memorial, O LORD, throughout all generations" — directly echoes Exodus 3:15
- *Definition here:* The Name YHWH is God's appointed means of being remembered and invoked by every generation — it is not merely historical but perpetually active

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The Burning Bush — Unconsumed Israel

- The bush burns but is not consumed
- **Isaiah 43:2** — "When thou walkest through the fire, thou shalt not be burned; neither shall the flame kindle upon thee" — God's promise to Israel in the furnace of affliction
- **Daniel 3:24-25** — Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego in the furnace, not consumed; a fourth figure "like the Son of God" is present
- *Type:* The burning bush is Israel — afflicted, in the fire of Egypt, yet not consumed because the presence of God is in the midst of it. The fire of affliction that should have destroyed them instead became the very place where God appeared.

### The Mountain of God (Horeb/Sinai) — Meeting Place of God and Man

- **Exodus 3:1** — Moses at Horeb when God appears
- **Exodus 19-20** — the Law given at Sinai/Horeb
- **1 Kings 19:8** — Elijah flees to Horeb and encounters God
- **Galatians 4:24-25** — Paul calls Sinai/Horeb the covenant of bondage, contrasted with the heavenly Jerusalem
- **Hebrews 12:18-24** — Sinai (fire, darkness, tempest) contrasted with Mount Zion, the heavenly Jerusalem; Moses' mountain is the type, the heavenly reality is the antitype

### "I Am Come Down to Deliver" — The Incarnation Pattern

- **Exodus 3:8** — "I am come down to deliver them out of the hand of the Egyptians"
- **John 3:13** — "No man hath ascended up to heaven, but he that came down from heaven, even the Son of man"
- **John 6:38** — "For I came down from heaven, not to do mine own will, but the will of him that sent me"
- *Type:* God "coming down" at the burning bush to commission deliverance is the Old Testament pattern. The Incarnation is God truly coming down — not merely in theophany but in flesh. Exodus 3:8 is the historic preview; John 1:14 is the fulfillment.

### I AM — Seven Signs in John's Gospel

The seven "I AM" statements of Christ are the fulfillment of Exodus 3:14:
- John 6:35 — I am the bread of life (manna from heaven — Exodus 16 connection)
- John 8:12 — I am the light of the world (pillar of fire — Exodus 13 connection)
- John 10:9 — I am the door
- John 10:11 — I am the good shepherd
- John 11:25 — I am the resurrection and the life
- John 14:6 — I am the way, the truth, and the life
- John 15:1 — I am the true vine
Each "I AM" is a deliberate echo of the God who spoke from the burning bush.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Who Am I?" — Misread as Mere Humility

- **Exodus 3:11** — "Who am I, that I should go unto Pharaoh?"
- Often read as simple self-deprecation or false humility
- **BUT God's answer (v.12) is not "You are great enough"** — it is "I will be with thee"
- The answer to "Who am I?" is never a catalog of personal qualifications — it is always the character and presence of God
- **Judges 6:15-16** — Gideon: "Who am I?" / God: "Surely I will be with thee" — identical exchange
- **Jeremiah 1:6-8** — Jeremiah: "I am a child" / God: "Be not afraid... for I am with thee"
- *The full picture:* The question "Who am I?" is the correct question — but the answer Scripture always gives is not human qualification; it is divine accompaniment. The pattern is consistent from Moses to Gideon to Jeremiah to Paul (1 Corinthians 1:26-31).
