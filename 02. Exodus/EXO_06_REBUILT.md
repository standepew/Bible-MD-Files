## CHAPTER SUMMARY

God answers Moses' lament (Exodus 5:22-23) with the fullest covenant declaration in Exodus. He reveals that while He appeared to Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob as El Shaddai (God Almighty), He is now acting under His name JEHOVAH — the covenant name of redemption-fulfillment. He gives seven "I will" promises: bring out, rid, redeem, take, be their God, bring in, give the land. Moses delivers this to Israel, but they cannot hear for anguish of spirit and cruel bondage. God sends Moses back to Pharaoh; Moses objects again (uncircumcised lips). A Levitical genealogy is inserted, establishing the lineage of Moses and Aaron. The commission is confirmed: "I am the LORD."

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "By My Name JEHOVAH Was I Not Known to Them" — The Progressive Revelation of God's Name

**Exodus 6:3** — "I appeared unto Abraham, unto Isaac, and unto Jacob, by the name of God Almighty (El Shaddai), but by my name JEHOVAH was I not known to them"

- **Genesis 17:1** — "I am the Almighty God (El Shaddai)" — God's self-revelation to Abraham
- **Genesis 28:3** — "God Almighty bless thee" — Isaac blesses Jacob with the El Shaddai name
- **Genesis 35:11** — "I am God Almighty (El Shaddai): be fruitful and multiply"
- *Note:* The patriarchs DID use the name LORD (YHWH) — see Genesis 15:2, 22:14 — so Exodus 6:3 is not saying they never heard the word "LORD" but that they did not know God in the fullness of what JEHOVAH means: the God who fulfills covenant promises through redemptive action. The patriarchs knew God as the all-sufficient provider (El Shaddai); Israel will know Him as the covenant-keeper who acts in history to redeem His people (JEHOVAH).
- **Revelation 1:8** — "I am Alpha and Omega... which is, and which was, and which is to come" — YHWH encompasses past, present, and future covenant action
- *Interpretation:* God's names are not arbitrary titles — they reveal progressively deeper dimensions of His nature. El Shaddai = the God sufficient for every need. JEHOVAH/YHWH = the God who IS, who was, who is to come; the covenant-fulfilling, promise-keeping, redemption-working God. The Exodus is the event that reveals what YHWH means in fullness.

### The Seven "I Will" Covenant Promises — The Structure of Redemption

**Exodus 6:6-8** — Seven covenant pledges of God to Israel

1. **"I will bring you out"** — from under the burdens of Egypt → Salvation: deliverance from bondage
2. **"I will rid you out of their bondage"** — total liberation → Justification: breaking the legal claim of slavery
3. **"I will redeem you with a stretched out arm"** — with great judgments → Redemption: purchase at cost
4. **"I will take you to me for a people"** — covenant adoption → Election: "ye shall be my people"
5. **"I will be to you a God"** — covenant relationship → Covenant presence: "I will be your God"
6. **"I will bring you in unto the land"** — the inheritance → Inheritance: the promised land
7. **"I will give it you for an heritage"** — permanent possession → Security: given, not earned

- **Romans 8:30** — "Whom he predestinated, them he also called: and whom he called, them he also justified: and whom he justified, them he also glorified" — Paul's chain of salvation mirrors the Exodus 6 sequence
- **Ephesians 1:3-14** — The New Testament equivalent: chosen, predestinated, adopted, redeemed, forgiven, sealed, given an inheritance — the same covenant structure
- *Interpretation:* The seven "I will" promises of Exodus 6 are the covenant structure of salvation. They proceed from deliverance → liberation → redemption → election → relationship → inheritance → security. Paul's soteriology in Romans and Ephesians follows the same sequence. Exodus 6 is the Old Testament foundation of what the New Testament calls the ordo salutis (order of salvation).

---

### "I Will Redeem You with a Stretched Out Arm" — The First Use of Redeem in Exodus

**Exodus 6:6** — "I will redeem you (ga'al) with a stretched out arm, and with great judgments"

- Hebrew: ga'al = to redeem as a kinsman-redeemer; to act as goel (near kinsman who buys back)
- **Ruth 3:13; 4:1-10** — Boaz as the kinsman-redeemer (goel) for Ruth and Naomi — the full story of ga'al in action
- **Job 19:25** — "I know that my redeemer (goel) liveth, and that he shall stand at the latter day upon the earth"
- **Isaiah 41:14** — "Fear not, thou worm Jacob... thy redeemer (goel), the Holy One of Israel"
- **Isaiah 44:6** — "Thus saith the LORD the King of Israel, and his redeemer (goel) the LORD of hosts"
- **Galatians 4:4-5** — "God sent forth his Son, made of a woman, made under the law, To redeem (exagorazo) them that were under the law, that we might receive the adoption of sons" — Christ as the ultimate Kinsman-Redeemer; He became what we are (made under the law) to buy us out
- *Interpretation:* The word ga'al (redeem) establishes that God's relationship to Israel is that of a Kinsman-Redeemer. This requires nearness (He came down to deliver) and the payment of a price (the stretched-out arm, the great judgments). The pattern: God becomes near to His people → pays the redemption price → brings them into freedom. Christ is the fulfillment: He became near (Incarnation) → paid the price (cross) → brought freedom (resurrection and ascension).

---

### "I Will Take You to Me for a People" — Covenant Adoption

**Exodus 6:7** — "I will take you to me for a people, and I will be to you a God"

- **Deuteronomy 26:17-18** — "Thou hast avouched the LORD this day to be thy God... And the LORD hath avouched thee this day to be his peculiar people"
- **Jeremiah 31:33** — "I will be their God, and they shall be my people" — the new covenant form of the same declaration
- **Revelation 21:3** — "And I heard a great voice out of heaven saying, Behold, the tabernacle of God is with men, and he will dwell with them, and they shall be his people, and God himself shall be with them, and be their God" — the final fulfillment
- *Interpretation:* The covenant formula "I will be your God / you shall be my people" first appears in Exodus 6:7 in its full redemptive context. Jeremiah 31 uses it for the new covenant. Revelation 21 uses it for the eternal state. The same formula spans from Exodus to Revelation — establishing that the entire Bible is the story of God fulfilling this one covenant promise: a people who are His, and a God who is theirs.

---

### "They Hearkened Not... for Anguish of Spirit" — When Affliction Blocks the Word

**Exodus 6:9** — "But they hearkened not unto Moses for anguish of spirit, and for cruel bondage"

- **Mark 4:18-19** — "These are they which are sown among thorns; such as hear the word, and the cares of this world... choke the word, and it becometh unfruitful"
- **Luke 21:26** — "Men's hearts failing them for fear" — end-time parallel: pressure producing inability to receive
- **Psalm 69:20** — "Reproach hath broken my heart; and I am full of heaviness: and I looked for some to take pity, but there was none" — the broken spirit under crushing oppression
- *Interpretation:* Israel's inability to hear Moses' words is not rebellion here — it is the effect of crushing bondage on the human spirit. The Scripture is honest: there are conditions under which the human spirit is so broken that even the best news cannot be received. God does not condemn Israel for this; He continues to work. This is a caution against demanding immediate spiritual response from people whose spirits are crushed by oppression.

---

### The Levitical Genealogy — The Line of the Deliverers Established

**Exodus 6:14-27** — The genealogy traces from Reuben and Simeon briefly, then focuses on Levi → Kohath → Amram → Moses and Aaron

- **Numbers 26:59** — "The name of Amram's wife was Jochebed, the daughter of Levi, whom her mother bare to Levi in Egypt"
- **Hebrews 7:14** — Genealogy matters for priestly and redemptive office: "our Lord sprang out of Juda"
- *Interpretation:* The genealogy is not a detour — it is authentication. In a world where lineage determined legitimacy, God establishes Moses and Aaron's credentials. They are of Levi — the tribe God will choose for priestly service. The genealogy confirms that Moses and Aaron are exactly who God says they are. Nothing in Scripture is filler.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### JEHOVAH / LORD (Hebrew: YHWH)
- **Exodus 6:3** — the name revealed in its full redemptive weight
- From hayah = to be; YHWH = He Who Is / He Who Was / He Who Will Be
- The Tetragrammaton: four Hebrew letters Yod-Heh-Vav-Heh
- **Exodus 3:14** — "I AM THAT I AM" is the first-person form; YHWH is the third-person form
- *Definition here:* The personal covenant name of God; the God who acts in history to fulfill His covenant promises; the self-existent, eternal One whose very name is a declaration of being and faithfulness

### REDEEM (Hebrew: ga'al)
- **Exodus 6:6** — first use in Exodus in the context of the covenant
- ga'al = to act as kinsman-redeemer; to buy back; to vindicate
- Requires: kinship (nearness), willingness, and ability to pay the price
- **Ruth 4:1-10** — the definitive narrative illustration of ga'al
- *Definition here:* To purchase out of bondage by a near kinsman at personal cost; God's redemption of Israel is ga'al — He is their Kinsman (in covenant) who pays the price of judgment to bring them out

### ANGUISH OF SPIRIT (Hebrew: kotser ruach)
- **Exodus 6:9** — "for anguish of spirit" — kotser = shortness, cutting short; ruach = spirit/breath
- Literally: "shortness of spirit" — the spirit so crushed it cannot expand to receive
- *Definition here:* The condition of a spirit broken by long-term oppression; unable to receive even good news because the capacity for hope has been crushed — a pastoral reality God acknowledges without condemnation

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The Seven "I Will" Promises as the Gospel Pattern

The seven covenant pledges of Exodus 6:6-8 map directly onto the New Testament gospel:

| Exodus 6 | New Testament Fulfillment |
|---|---|
| I will bring you out | Romans 6:17-18 — freed from sin |
| I will rid you of bondage | John 8:36 — "the Son shall make you free" |
| I will redeem you | Galatians 3:13 — "Christ hath redeemed us" |
| I will take you for a people | 1 Peter 2:9-10 — "ye are a peculiar people" |
| I will be your God | 2 Corinthians 6:16 — "I will be their God" |
| I will bring you into the land | Hebrews 4:9 — "There remaineth a rest" |
| I will give it for a heritage | Romans 8:17 — "heirs of God, joint-heirs with Christ" |

### The Name JEHOVAH — Revealed Progressively, Fulfilled Completely in Christ

- El Shaddai (Genesis 17:1) = The All-Sufficient God; revealed to the patriarchs in the covenant of promise
- JEHOVAH (Exodus 6:3) = The Covenant-Keeping, Redemption-Working God; revealed at the Exodus
- Father, Son, Holy Spirit (Matthew 28:19) = The full Trinitarian name; revealed in the Incarnation and Pentecost
- *Thread:* The revelation of God's name is progressive throughout Scripture — each revelation is fuller, not contradicting but completing what came before.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### Exodus 6:3 — "By My Name JEHOVAH Was I Not Known" Used to Create a Contradiction

**Verse 3** — "And I appeared unto Abraham, unto Isaac, and unto Jacob, by the name of God Almighty, but by my name JEHOVAH was I not known to them."

**How it is misused:** Critics and some readers use this verse to argue that the Pentateuch is composed of contradictory sources, since the patriarchs clearly used the name YHWH (LORD) in Genesis (Genesis 15:2, 22:14, 26:22, etc.). The verse is cited as proof of textual contradiction. Some also use it to argue that the God of the patriarchs was a different deity than the God of the Exodus.

**What the text actually says:** The verse is not claiming the patriarchs never heard or used the word YHWH — it is making a distinction in the *experiential* knowledge of what that name means:
- **Genesis 17:1** — "I am the Almighty God (El Shaddai)" — God's primary self-revelation to Abraham was through the El Shaddai name, the all-sufficient provider
- **Genesis 15:2** — "And Abram said, Lord GOD (Adonai YHWH)..." — Abraham used YHWH in speech, confirming he knew the name; the issue is the dimension of the name's meaning he experienced
- **Exodus 6:6-7** — The seven "I will" promises define what YHWH means in its fullness: bring out, rid, redeem, take, be your God, bring in, give — these are the acts of YHWH that the patriarchs received as promises but never saw fulfilled in their lifetimes
- **Hebrews 11:13** — "These all died in faith, not having received the promises, but having seen them afar off" — the patriarchs knew the covenant; they did not see its execution in history

*Correction:* Exodus 6:3 does not record a textual contradiction or a different deity. It records a progression in the revealed meaning of God's covenant name. The patriarchs knew YHWH as a name; Israel in Egypt will know YHWH as the One who acts to fulfill what He promised. The name is not new; the fullness of experience of what the name means is new. Just as a man may know his father's name from birth but understand what the name means only through years of lived relationship.

---

### Exodus 6:9 — "They Hearkened Not" Used to Excuse Unresponsiveness to Truth

**Verse 9** — "But they hearkened not unto Moses for anguish of spirit, and for cruel bondage."

**How it is misused:** This verse is sometimes used to argue that emotional or circumstantial suffering excuses persistent unresponsiveness to God's word — that the conditions of oppression render people permanently unable to hear, and therefore spiritual accountability should be suspended for those in distress.

**What the text actually says:** The verse describes a real and pastoral condition, but the narrative does not make it a permanent state or a moral excuse:
- **Exodus 4:31** — "And the people believed: and when they heard that the LORD had visited the children of Israel, and that he had looked upon their affliction, then they bowed their heads and worshipped" — before the increased burden, the same people believed and worshipped; anguish of spirit is a condition that can change
- **Exodus 12:27-28** — After the Passover instruction, "the people bowed the head and worshipped. And the children of Israel went away, and did as the LORD had commanded" — the same people who could not hear in chapter 6 are able to obey completely in chapter 12
- **Mark 4:18-19** — "the cares of this world... choke the word, and it becometh unfruitful" — Jesus acknowledges the same dynamic; it is a warning, not a permanent excuse

*Correction:* Exodus 6:9 records a real pastoral reality: crushing oppression can temporarily close the spirit to receiving even the best news. The text does not condemn Israel for this condition — God continues to work. But the narrative shows this is a temporary state, not a permanent one. The same Israel who "hearkened not" in chapter 6 worships obediently in chapter 12. The anguish of spirit is a condition to be addressed and overcome by God's working, not a permanent category of unaccountability.

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### "Stretched Out Arm" — The Arm of the LORD

- **Exodus 6:6** — "I will redeem you with a stretched out arm"
- **Deuteronomy 4:34** — "by a stretched out arm" — Moses refers back to this promise as fulfilled
- **Isaiah 52:10** — "The LORD hath made bare his holy arm in the eyes of all the nations"
- **Isaiah 53:1** — "and to whom is the arm of the LORD revealed?" — the arm of the LORD revealed = the Servant who suffers and redeems
- **John 12:38** — John quotes Isaiah 53:1 as fulfilled in Jesus' ministry
- *Thread:* The "stretched out arm" of Exodus 6 becomes a shorthand for God's saving power throughout the Old Testament. Isaiah reveals that the ultimate stretching out of God's arm is the Suffering Servant — Christ with arms outstretched on the cross.
