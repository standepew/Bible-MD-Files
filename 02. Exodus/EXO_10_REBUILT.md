## CHAPTER SUMMARY

God tells Moses He hardened Pharaoh's heart specifically so these signs could be told to future generations. Pharaoh's own servants beg him to let Israel go — "Knowest thou not yet that Egypt is destroyed?" Pharaoh offers compromise 3: only the men go. Moses refuses — all go, including the little ones and the flocks. Plague 8: Locusts fill the land, eat everything the hail left; Pharaoh confesses again, Moses prays, a west wind carries the locusts to the Red Sea, not one remains. Pharaoh hardens. Plague 9: Three days of thick darkness throughout Egypt — so thick it "may be felt"; all the children of Israel had light in their dwellings. Pharaoh offers compromise 4: go with your little ones but leave your flocks. Moses refuses — "not a hoof shall be left behind." Final impasse: Pharaoh tells Moses he will die if he sees Pharaoh's face again. Moses agrees: "I will see thy face again no more." The stage is set for the final plague.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "That Thou Mayest Tell in the Ears of Thy Son" — The Plague Narrative as Generational Testimony

**Exodus 10:2** — "And that thou mayest tell in the ears of thy son, and of thy son's son, what things I have wrought in Egypt, and my signs which I have done among them; that ye may know how that I am the LORD"

- **Deuteronomy 4:9** — "Teach them thy sons, and thy sons' sons" — generational transmission of God's acts is commanded
- **Psalm 78:3-7** — "Which we have heard and known, and our fathers have told us. We will not hide them from their children, shewing to the generation to come the praises of the LORD, and his strength, and his wonderful works that he hath done... That the generation to come might know them... that they might set their hope in God, and not forget the works of God"
- **Exodus 12:26-27** — The Passover is instituted specifically with the question "What mean ye by this service?" answered for future generations
- **Joshua 4:6-7** — The twelve stones at Jordan: "When your children ask their fathers in time to come, saying, What mean ye by these stones? Then ye shall answer them"
- *Interpretation:* God explicitly states that part of the purpose of the plagues is generational testimony — the events are calibrated to be so extraordinary that they become the foundation of Israel's identity and their children's faith for all generations. The hardening of Pharaoh (which extended the plagues) served the purpose of multiplying the signs so the testimony would be unmistakable. This is God building the content of faith through history.

### "Egypt Is Destroyed" — Even the Enemy Sees the Reality

**Exodus 10:7** — "Pharaoh's servants said unto him, How long shall this man be a snare unto us? let the men go, that they may serve the LORD their God: knowest thou not yet that Egypt is destroyed?"

- **Proverbs 29:1** — "He, that being often reproved hardeneth his neck, shall suddenly be destroyed, and that without remedy"
- **Romans 2:5** — "But after thy hardness and impenitent heart treasurest up unto thyself wrath against the day of wrath and revelation of the righteous judgment of God"
- **Luke 19:41-44** — Jesus weeps over Jerusalem: "the days shall come upon thee, that thine enemies shall cast a trench about thee... because thou knewest not the time of thy visitation"
- *Interpretation:* Pharaoh's own servants see reality before Pharaoh does. The people around a stubborn leader often perceive the situation more clearly than the leader himself — and their warnings, unheeded, become part of the record of his inexcusable destruction. This is the pattern of every nation or ruler that persists in opposition to God past the point of mercy: those around them see the end coming while the leader continues to harden.

### Plague 8: The Locusts — Unprecedented Consumption

**Exodus 10:14-15** — "Before them there were no such locusts as they, neither after them shall be such"

- **Joel 1:4; 2:1-11** — Joel's locust plague: understood both as a literal agricultural disaster and as a prophetic pattern of end-time armies
- **Joel 2:25** — "I will restore to you the years that the locust hath eaten" — God promises reversal of the locust's destruction; He can restore what the judgment took
- **Revelation 9:3-11** — The locusts from the bottomless pit in the fifth trumpet — their description echoes and expands the Exodus locusts; they have a king over them (Abaddon/Apollyon), where the natural locust has no king (Proverbs 30:27); the spiritual locusts are the antitype of the natural ones
- **Proverbs 30:27** — "The locusts have no king, yet go they forth all of them by bands" — natural locusts; contrasted with the Revelation locusts who have a king
- *Interpretation:* The Exodus locust plague is the historical prototype of the Joel locust prophecy and the Revelation 9 spiritual locusts. The pattern: total consumption of what remains after previous judgments. The locusts eat "the residue of that which is escaped" from the hail — no escape from accumulated judgment. Joel's "I will restore" (Joel 2:25) shows that God can reverse the consumption — but only in response to genuine repentance.

### Plague 9: Darkness That Can Be Felt — The Anti-Creation Plague

**Exodus 10:21-23** — "thick darkness in all the land of Egypt three days... but all the children of Israel had light in their dwellings"

- **Genesis 1:2-3** — The earth was in darkness; God said "Let there be light" — Creation brought light out of darkness
- The ninth plague reverses this: God removes the light from Egypt, returning it to pre-creation darkness
- **Genesis 1:4** — "God divided the light from the darkness" — the ninth plague collapses this division for Egypt
- **John 1:5** — "And the light shineth in darkness; and the darkness comprehended it not"
- **John 3:19** — "Light is come into the world, and men loved darkness rather than light, because their deeds were evil"
- **Isaiah 60:2** — "For, behold, the darkness shall cover the earth, and gross darkness the people: but the LORD shall arise upon thee, and his glory shall be seen upon thee"
- **Matthew 27:45** — "Now from the sixth hour there was darkness over all the land unto the ninth hour" — at the crucifixion, the ninth plague is replicated; Egypt's darkness falls on Christ
- **Revelation 16:10** — "The fifth bowl: the kingdom of the beast became full of darkness; and they gnawed their tongues for pain"
- *Interpretation:* The ninth plague is the most theologically dense of the plagues. It reverses the first act of creation (Genesis 1:3) — God's light removed from Egypt. The simultaneous light in Goshen is a declaration: God's presence brings light; His absence brings darkness. This is not merely physical — it is spiritual. Christ's crucifixion darkness (Matthew 27:45) is the ninth plague applied to the cross: the Father's light withdrawn from the One bearing sin. The end-time darkness of Revelation 16:10 is the Exodus darkness scaled to the beast's kingdom.

### "Not a Hoof Shall Be Left Behind" — Total Obedience, No Negotiation

**Exodus 10:26** — "Our cattle also shall go with us; there shall not an hoof be left behind; for thereof must we take to serve the LORD our God; and we know not with what we must serve the LORD, until we come thither"

- **Luke 14:33** — "So likewise, whosoever he be of you that forsaketh not all that he hath, he cannot be my disciple"
- **Matthew 22:37** — "Thou shalt love the LORD thy God with all thy heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy mind" — no partial withholding
- **Hebrews 10:38-39** — "the just shall live by faith: but if any man draw back, my soul shall have no pleasure in him. But we are not of them who draw back unto perdition"
- *Interpretation:* Moses' statement "not a hoof shall be left behind" is the definitive answer to all four of Pharaoh's compromise offers. There is no version of obedience that leaves something behind in Egypt. The whole person, the whole household, all possessions — everything follows the command of God. "We know not with what we must serve the LORD, until we come thither" is the principle of total trust: we bring everything because we don't yet know what God will require. You cannot pre-determine which parts of your life are safe to leave behind.

### The Final Impasse — The End of Negotiation

**Exodus 10:28-29** — "Pharaoh said unto him, Get thee from me, take heed to thyself, see my face no more; for in that day thou seest my face thou shalt die. And Moses said, Thou hast spoken well, I will see thy face again no more."

- **Matthew 23:37-38** — "O Jerusalem, Jerusalem... how often would I have gathered thy children together... and ye would not! Behold, your house is left unto you desolate" — when the offer of mercy is finally and completely rejected, Christ declares the house desolate
- **Genesis 6:3** — "My spirit shall not always strive with man" — God's mercy has limits; the striving does not continue indefinitely
- **Proverbs 29:1** — "He, that being often reproved hardeneth his neck, shall suddenly be destroyed, and that without remedy"
- **Revelation 22:11** — "He that is unjust, let him be unjust still: and he which is filthy, let him be filthy still" — the final state of those who have rejected every offer of mercy
- *Interpretation:* Pharaoh's dismissal of Moses is the closing of the mercy door before the tenth plague. God has given nine opportunities (nine plagues with nine warnings and nine opportunities for repentance). Pharaoh's final word is a death threat against Moses. Moses agrees: the confrontations are over. The next move is God's alone — the tenth plague and the death of the firstborn. The mercy door has been closed from Pharaoh's side, not God's.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### LOCUSTS (Hebrew: arbeh)
- **Exodus 10:4** — arbeh = locusts; from rabah (to multiply) — the swarming, multiplying destroyer
- **Joel 1:4** — four stages of locust: gnawing locust, swarming locust, creeping locust, stripping locust — progressive stages of total consumption
- **Revelation 9:3** — pneumatic (spiritual) locusts from the abyss in end-time judgment
- *Definition here:* The arbeh is the instrument of total agricultural destruction; what the hail left, the locusts consume; the locust plague represents the removal of all material sustenance from those under judgment

### DARKNESS (Hebrew: choshek)
- **Exodus 10:21** — "darkness which may be felt" — choshek = darkness, obscurity; same word as Genesis 1:2
- **Isaiah 45:7** — "I form the light, and create darkness: I make peace, and create evil: I the LORD do all these things" — God is sovereign over both light and darkness
- **John 1:5** — "The light shineth in darkness; and the darkness comprehended it not" — spiritual darkness cannot contain or overcome God's light
- *Definition here:* The plague darkness is choshek — the same darkness as before creation; the reversal of God's first creative act; a judicial removal of light from those under His judgment

### SNARE (Hebrew: mokesh)
- **Exodus 10:7** — "How long shall this man be a snare unto us?" — mokesh = a bait-trap, a stumbling block
- **Psalm 91:3** — "He shall deliver thee from the snare of the fowler"
- **Romans 11:9** — Paul quotes Psalm 69:22: "Let their table be made a snare, and a trap"
- *Definition here:* Pharaoh's servants use snare (mokesh) to describe Moses' effect on Egypt — paradoxically the servants of the oppressor recognize that holding Israel is the trap, not releasing them

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The Three Days of Darkness — Death and Resurrection Pattern

- Three days of darkness over Egypt (Exodus 10:22-23)
- Israel has light; Egypt has none
- **Matthew 27:45** — Three hours of darkness at the crucifixion
- **Jonah 1:17** — "Jonah was in the belly of the fish three days and three nights" — Matthew 12:40 connects this to Christ's burial
- **Matthew 12:40** — "For as Jonas was three days and three nights in the whale's belly; so shall the Son of man be three days and three nights in the heart of the earth"
- *Type:* The three days of darkness in Exodus 10 is the prophetic shadow of three days of death. Egypt's darkness = the world under the power of death. Israel's light in Goshen = those who belong to God preserved through the darkness. The resurrection morning — when the light returns — is the fulfillment. Darkness three days; then the light.

### The Four Compromise Offers — Progressive Revelation of Satan's Strategy

Through Exodus 5-10, Pharaoh makes four increasingly accommodating offers, all of which Moses refuses:
1. "Sacrifice in the land" (8:25) — worship in bondage
2. "Not very far away" (8:28) — limited freedom
3. "Only the men go" (10:11) — partial obedience; families stay behind as hostages
4. "Leave your flocks" (10:24) — go but leave your livelihood

Each offer is more reasonable-sounding than the last. The final offer sounds almost generous. But Moses refuses all four because all four involve leaving something in Egypt's control. The pattern: the enemy never stops negotiating; he just changes the terms. Full obedience admits no negotiation.

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### Light in Goshen / Darkness in Egypt — The Separation of Light from Darkness

- **Genesis 1:4** — God divides light from darkness at creation
- **Exodus 10:23** — God divides light (Goshen) from darkness (Egypt) in judgment
- **John 1:5** — Light shines in darkness; darkness cannot overcome it
- **2 Corinthians 4:6** — "God, who commanded the light to shine out of darkness, hath shined in our hearts, to give the light of the knowledge of the glory of God in the face of Jesus Christ"
- **Revelation 21:23-25** — The new Jerusalem has no need of the sun; the Lamb is the light; no night there
- *Thread:* The Exodus 10 darkness/light separation is the covenant application of Genesis 1's light/darkness division. At the new creation (Revelation 21), the division is permanent: only light, no darkness, no night. The Exodus event is the middle chapter in the story that begins in Genesis 1:3 and ends in Revelation 22:5.
