## CHAPTER SUMMARY

God instructs Israel to camp at Pi-hahiroth by the sea, a position that looks like a trap — to entice Pharaoh. Pharaoh pursues with 600 chosen chariots and all Egypt's army. Israel, seeing the army, panics and cries to Moses — "Were there no graves in Egypt?" Moses commands: "Fear not, stand still, see the salvation of the LORD." God tells Moses to go forward and stretch his hand over the sea. The Angel of God and the pillar move from before Israel to behind Israel — darkness to Egypt, light to Israel all night. Moses stretches his hand; an east wind parts the sea; Israel walks through on dry ground with walls of water on both sides. Egypt pursues. In the morning watch, God looks down through the pillar and troubles Egypt's army — chariot wheels removed. Moses stretches his hand again; the sea returns; every single Egyptian is drowned. Israel sees the Egyptians dead. They fear the LORD and believe the LORD and His servant Moses.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Fear Not, Stand Still, See the Salvation of the LORD"

**Exodus 14:13** — "And Moses said unto the people, Fear ye not, stand still, and see the salvation of the LORD, which he will shew to you to day"

- **Isaiah 43:1-2** — "Fear not: for I have redeemed thee, I have called thee by thy name; thou art mine. When thou passest through the waters, I will be with thee"
- **2 Chronicles 20:15, 17** — Jehoshaphat: "Thus saith the LORD unto you, Be not afraid nor dismayed... Ye shall not need to fight in this battle: set yourselves, stand ye still, and see the salvation of the LORD"
- **Psalm 46:10** — "Be still, and know that I am God: I will be exalted among the heathen, I will be exalted in the earth"
- **Isaiah 40:31** — "They that wait upon the LORD shall renew their strength"
- *Interpretation:* "Stand still" is not passivity — it is the active posture of faith that lets God work. Israel's response to the army behind and the sea before must not be panic, plan, or retreat — it must be stillness and watching. This command recurs throughout Scripture whenever God is about to do something that human effort would only hinder. The pattern: God's greatest acts often require His people to stop trying and start trusting.

### "The LORD Shall Fight for You, and Ye Shall Hold Your Peace"

**Exodus 14:14**

- **Deuteronomy 3:22** — "Ye shall not fear them: for the LORD your God he shall fight for you"
- **Joshua 23:10** — "One man of you shall chase a thousand: for the LORD your God, he it is that fighteth for you, as he hath promised you"
- **Isaiah 31:4** — "So shall the LORD of hosts come down to fight for mount Zion"
- **Romans 8:31** — "If God be for us, who can be against us?"
- *Interpretation:* "The LORD shall fight for you" is the covenant warrior principle. Israel did not defeat Egypt — God did. This is the consistent pattern of Old Testament warfare when Israel is in covenant obedience: God fights; Israel watches or simply obeys. The moment Israel starts trusting in chariots and horses (Psalm 20:7), they lose this supernatural covering. The principle of Exodus 14:14 is the operational definition of faith in conflict.

### The Sea Parted — The Greatest Miracle of the Exodus

**Exodus 14:21-22** — "The LORD caused the sea to go back by a strong east wind all that night... and the children of Israel went into the midst of the sea upon the dry ground: and the waters were a wall unto them on their right hand, and on their left"

- **Psalm 77:14-20** — "Thou art the God that doest wonders... Thy way is in the sea, and thy path in the great waters, and thy footsteps are not known. Thou leddest thy people like a flock by the hand of Moses and Aaron"
- **Psalm 106:9** — "He rebuked the Red sea also, and it was dried up: so he led them through the depths, as through the wilderness"
- **Isaiah 51:10** — "Art thou not it which hath dried the sea, the waters of the great deep; that hath made the depths of the sea a way for the ransomed to pass over?"
- **Isaiah 43:16** — "Thus saith the LORD, which maketh a way in the sea, and a path in the mighty waters"
- **1 Corinthians 10:1-2** — "all our fathers were under the cloud, and all passed through the sea; And were all baptized unto Moses in the cloud and in the sea" — Paul calls the crossing a baptism
- **Romans 6:3-4** — baptism as death and resurrection
- *Interpretation:* Paul's identification of the Red Sea crossing as baptism (1 Corinthians 10:1-2) is the Spirit-breathed interpretation of Exodus 14. The Israelites passed through the sea (death waters) and came out the other side — the old life buried, the new life begun. Egypt (their old master) was destroyed in the same waters. This is the grammar of baptism: the believer passes through death with Christ; the old master (sin) is destroyed; the new life on the other side is the life of the redeemed.

### "There Remained Not So Much as One of Them" — Total Victory

**Exodus 14:28** — "there remained not so much as one of them"

- **Psalm 136:15** — "But overthrew Pharaoh and his host in the Red sea: for his mercy endureth for ever"
- **Hebrews 2:14** — "that through death he might destroy him that had the power of death, that is, the devil" — Christ's work destroys the enemy completely; "not one remaining" is the victory that Hebrews 2 describes at the cross
- **Colossians 2:15** — "And having spoiled principalities and powers, he made a shew of them openly, triumphing over them in it" — the cross as God's total triumph over the enemy
- *Interpretation:* "Not one of them remained" is the language of total, irreversible victory. This phrase appears in the context of complete destruction of the enemy force. Pharaoh's army represents sin's claim on the redeemed — and at the cross, that claim is completely destroyed. "Not one remained" is the New Testament truth of Romans 6:14: "sin shall not have dominion over you."

### "Israel Feared the LORD, and Believed" — The Proper Response to Divine Deliverance

**Exodus 14:31** — "And Israel saw that great work which the LORD did upon the Egyptians: and the people feared the LORD, and believed the LORD, and his servant Moses"

- **John 2:11** — "This beginning of miracles did Jesus in Cana of Galilee, and manifested forth his glory; and his disciples believed on him" — the miracle produces faith
- **Hebrews 11:29** — "By faith they passed through the Red sea as by dry land: which the Egyptians assaying to do were drowned" — Hebrews calls the crossing an act of faith
- **Romans 10:17** — "faith cometh by hearing, and hearing by the word of God" — Israel heard Moses' word and obeyed; the faith that moved them through the sea came from hearing and acting
- *Interpretation:* The chapter closes with the proper covenant response: fear and faith. Not just intellectual assent but the fear of the LORD — reverential awe that reorients behavior — and belief in both God and His appointed servant. This is the pattern of every genuine divine encounter in Scripture: the people who see what God does are changed; they fear Him differently; they trust Him and His word differently.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### SALVATION (Hebrew: yeshuah)
- **Exodus 14:13** — "see the salvation (yeshuah) of the LORD"
- yeshuah = deliverance, rescue, victory — also the root of the name YESHUA (Jesus)
- **Genesis 49:18** — Jacob's cry: "I have waited for thy salvation (yeshuah), O LORD" — first use of yeshuah in this form
- **Isaiah 12:2** — "Behold, God is my salvation (yeshuah); I will trust, and not be afraid: for the LORD JEHOVAH is my strength and my song; he also is become my salvation (yeshuah)"
- *Definition here:* At the Red Sea, yeshuah = the LORD's active, mighty rescue of His people from an impossible situation; this is the word embedded in the name of Jesus — He IS the salvation of the LORD made flesh

### DRY GROUND (Hebrew: charabah)
- **Exodus 14:21** — "made the sea dry land" / **14:22** — "upon the dry ground"
- charabah = dry land, desert; the same word used in Genesis 7:22 for the dry land that perished in the flood
- **Joshua 3:17** — Israel crosses the Jordan on dry ground — the pattern repeats at the entry to the promised land
- *Definition here:* The dry ground in the midst of the sea is the miracle path — what was water becomes the road of salvation; the very element of judgment becomes the pathway of life

### WALL (Hebrew: chomah)
- **Exodus 14:22** — "the waters were a wall unto them on their right hand, and on their left"
- chomah = a wall, as of a city; a standing fortification
- The water walls are not sprayed aside or merely pushed back — they stand upright as city walls, vertical, holding position while Israel passes
- *Definition here:* The walls of water are the protective barriers God places on either side of His people's path through judgment; they are simultaneously the instrument of Egypt's destruction and the protection of Israel

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The Red Sea Crossing as Baptism

- **1 Corinthians 10:1-2** — Paul explicitly: "all were baptized unto Moses in the cloud and in the sea"
- The pattern: pass through death water → old life/master destroyed behind → new life/identity on the other side
- Israel in Egypt = humanity under sin; the sea = death; the crossing = death and resurrection with Christ; Egypt drowned = sin's dominion broken
- **Romans 6:6** — "our old man is crucified with him, that the body of sin might be destroyed, that henceforth we should not serve sin"
- The Red Sea crossing is the Old Testament baptism; Christian baptism is the New Testament Red Sea crossing

### The Apparent Trap — God Designs the Impossible Situation

- Pi-hahiroth, Migdol, the sea — the geography creates a box; Pharaoh sees a trapped enemy
- God designed the position: "I will be honoured upon Pharaoh" (v.4) — the impossible situation is the setting for the greatest demonstration
- **2 Corinthians 12:9** — "My strength is made perfect in weakness"
- **John 11:4** — "This sickness is not unto death, but for the glory of God"
- *Type:* The Red Sea situation is the pattern for every apparent dead end in the believer's life. The situation that looks like a trap from the enemy's perspective is the setup for God's glory. The impossible position is where the miracle happens.

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### The East Wind — The Breath of God in Judgment and Salvation

- **Exodus 14:21** — "the LORD caused the sea to go back by a strong east wind"
- **Genesis 41:6** — The east wind withers; in Joseph's dream it brings famine
- **Jonah 4:8** — A vehement east wind; God's appointed instrument
- **Ezekiel 19:12** — "the east wind dried up her fruit" — judgment wind
- *Thread:* The east wind in Scripture consistently represents God's sovereign breath — used for both judgment and salvation depending on who it touches. The same wind that parted the sea for Israel destroyed Egypt's army. The Spirit (ruach = wind/breath) is the agent of both salvation and judgment throughout Scripture.
