## CHAPTER SUMMARY

In the third month after the Exodus, Israel arrives at Sinai and camps before the mountain. God calls Moses up: "Ye have seen what I did unto the Egyptians, and how I bare you on eagles' wings, and brought you unto myself." He makes the covenant offer: "If ye will obey my voice indeed, and keep my covenant, then ye shall be a peculiar treasure unto me above all people: for all the earth is mine: And ye shall be unto me a kingdom of priests, and an holy nation." The people answer with one voice: "All that the LORD hath said will we do." God announces He will come in a thick cloud that the people may hear Him speak with Moses and believe Moses forever. The people are to sanctify themselves, wash their clothes, be ready on the third day, and not touch the mountain (penalty: death). On the third day: thunders, lightnings, thick cloud, a great trumpet blast, the whole mount quaking and smoking like a furnace. The people tremble. God descends in fire. Moses speaks; God answers. Moses called up alone.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "I Bare You on Eagles' Wings" — God's Redemptive Carrying of His People

**Exodus 19:4** — "how I bare you on eagles' wings, and brought you unto myself"

- **Deuteronomy 32:10-12** — "He found him in a desert land... he kept him as the apple of his eye. As an eagle stirreth up her nest, fluttereth over her young, spreadeth abroad her wings, taketh them, beareth them on her wings: So the LORD alone did lead him"
- **Isaiah 40:31** — "They that wait upon the LORD shall renew their strength; they shall mount up with wings as eagles"
- **Isaiah 46:3-4** — "I have carried thee from the belly... even I will carry; and will deliver you"
- **Revelation 12:14** — "And to the woman were given two wings of a great eagle, that she might fly into the wilderness, into her place" — at the end of the age, God bears Israel on eagles' wings again into the wilderness for protection
- *Interpretation:* "Eagles' wings" is God's description of His own redemptive act at the Exodus — He did not lead them from a distance; He carried them. The eagle imagery in Deuteronomy 32 describes the mother eagle who stirs the nest, hovers over her young, and bears them up when they fall. God's redemption is not remote management — it is intimate carrying. Revelation 12 applies the same image eschatologically: God will carry Israel on eagles' wings again in the final tribulation.

### "A Kingdom of Priests, an Holy Nation" — Israel's Covenant Calling

**Exodus 19:6** — "ye shall be unto me a kingdom of priests, and an holy nation"

- **Isaiah 61:6** — "But ye shall be named the Priests of the LORD: men shall call you the Ministers of our God"
- **1 Peter 2:5, 9** — "Ye also, as lively stones, are built up a spiritual house, an holy priesthood... But ye are a chosen generation, a royal priesthood, an holy nation, a peculiar people" — Peter quotes Exodus 19:6 directly and applies it to the New Testament church
- **Revelation 1:6** — "And hath made us kings and priests unto God and his Father" — Revelation applies Exodus 19:6 to believers in Christ
- **Revelation 5:10** — "And hast made us unto our God kings and priests: and we shall reign on the earth"
- *Interpretation:* "Kingdom of priests" is Israel's covenant identity — and it was always intended to extend to the nations. A priest mediates between God and man; a kingdom of priests means every member of the covenant community is a mediator, bringing the nations to God. Israel failed this calling consistently (hence the need for a priestly tribe, the Levites, as a subset). 1 Peter 2:9 declares that the church is the fulfillment of this calling — every believer is a priest. Revelation 1:6 and 5:10 confirm: Christ has made the redeemed to be "kings and priests," fulfilling what Exodus 19 called Israel to be.

### "All the Earth Is Mine" — The Universal Sovereignty Behind the Particular Covenant

**Exodus 19:5** — "for all the earth is mine"

- The covenant call to Israel is not because the rest of the earth is irrelevant — it is because God owns all of it
- **Psalm 24:1** — "The earth is the LORD's, and the fulness thereof; the world, and they that dwell therein"
- **Deuteronomy 10:14** — "Behold, the heaven and the heaven of heavens is the LORD's thy God, the earth also, with all that therein is"
- **Romans 11:36** — "For of him, and through him, and to him, are all things: to whom be glory for ever"
- *Interpretation:* Israel's election is not God preferring one part of the world over another — it is the Creator choosing a particular people to be His instrument among all peoples He owns. "All the earth is mine" — therefore choosing Israel is not partiality; it is the owner of all choosing the particular instrument He will use to reach all. This phrase prevents Israel from misunderstanding their election as favoritism; it is missiological — through the kingdom of priests, all the earth that is His will hear of Him.

### The Third Day — Resurrection Pattern at Sinai

**Exodus 19:11** — "Be ready against the third day: for the third day the LORD will come down in the sight of all the people upon mount Sinai"

- **Genesis 22:4** — Abraham sees the place of sacrifice "on the third day" — the resurrection type in the Akedah
- **Jonah 1:17** — Three days in the fish; resurrection on the third day
- **Hosea 6:2** — "After two days will he revive us: in the third day he will raise us up, and we shall live in his sight"
- **Matthew 16:21** — Jesus: "and be raised again the third day"
- **1 Corinthians 15:4** — "He rose again the third day according to the scriptures" — the "scriptures" Paul cites include Hosea 6:2 and patterns like this one
- *Interpretation:* The third-day theophany at Sinai joins the cluster of third-day appearances of God in Scripture. The people are to prepare for two days; on the third day, God comes down. This is not coincidentally resonant with the resurrection — the Spirit uses the third-day pattern consistently across the entire canon to point toward the ultimate third-day event: the resurrection of Christ. Every third-day theophany is a type of the resurrection.

### The Sinai Theophany — Contrasted with Pentecost and Hebrews 12

**Exodus 19:16-19** — Thunder, lightning, thick cloud, the exceeding loud trumpet, smoke, fire, quaking, the people trembling

- **Deuteronomy 4:11-12** — "the mountain burned with fire unto the midst of heaven, with darkness, clouds, and thick darkness. And the LORD spake unto you out of the midst of the fire"
- **Hebrews 12:18-21** — "For ye are not come unto the mount that might be touched, and that burned with fire, nor unto blackness, and darkness, and tempest, And the sound of a trumpet, and the voice of words; which voice they that heard intreated that the word should not be spoken to them any more... And so terrible was the sight, that Moses said, I exceedingly fear and quake"
- **Hebrews 12:22-24** — "But ye are come unto mount Zion, and unto the city of the living God, the heavenly Jerusalem... And to Jesus the mediator of the new covenant, and to the blood of sprinkling, that speaketh better things than that of Abel"
- **Acts 2:1-4** — Pentecost: wind, fire, divided tongues, the disciples filled — the theophany reversed from terror to empowerment
- *Interpretation:* Hebrews 12 is the Spirit's interpretation of Exodus 19. Sinai was the Law covenant — unapproachable, terrifying, the people begging the voice to stop. The New Covenant brings believers not to Sinai but to Zion; not to fire and darkness but to the blood of sprinkling. The same God who descended on Sinai in terrifying holiness descended at Pentecost in empowering fire. The difference is the mediator: at Sinai, Moses stood between God and the people; in the New Covenant, Jesus is the mediator "of better things."

### "My Name Is in Him" — The Angel of God as Pre-Incarnate Christ

**Exodus 23:21** (announced here, elaborated in Ch.23) — "for my name is in him"

- **Exodus 3:2** — The Angel of the LORD appeared in the burning bush — identified with YHWH
- **Exodus 14:19** — "The Angel of God which went before the camp of Israel" — guided at the Red Sea
- **Isaiah 63:9** — "The angel of his presence saved them: in his love and in his pity he redeemed them"
- **John 1:14** — "The Word was made flesh, and dwelt among us" — the Angel in whom God's name dwells is the pre-incarnate Word
- *Interpretation:* The Angel sent before Israel in whom God's name dwells is the pre-incarnate Christ — the same identification made at the burning bush (Exodus 3) and confirmed by John 1. "My name is in him" = the divine name (YHWH) is inherent in this Angel's nature, not merely attributed to Him. This is the basis for Christ's "I AM" statements in John — He is the One who bears the divine name from eternity.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### PECULIAR TREASURE (Hebrew: segullah)
- **Exodus 19:5** — "ye shall be a peculiar treasure unto me" — segullah = a special possession, a personal treasure, a prized property
- **Deuteronomy 7:6; 14:2; 26:18** — Israel is called segullah repeatedly
- **Psalm 135:4** — "For the LORD hath chosen Jacob unto himself, and Israel for his peculiar treasure (segullah)"
- **Malachi 3:17** — "And they shall be mine, saith the LORD of hosts, in that day when I make up my jewels (segullah)"
- **Titus 2:14** — "a peculiar people (periousios — Greek equivalent of segullah), zealous of good works"
- **1 Peter 2:9** — "a peculiar people (peripoiesis)" — same concept applied to the church
- *Definition here:* A prized personal possession — like a king's private treasury, set apart from the national wealth as uniquely his own; Israel is God's personally treasured possession from among all nations He owns

### HOLY NATION (Hebrew: goy qadosh)
- **Exodus 19:6** — goy = nation, people group; qadosh = set apart, holy, consecrated
- The only nation in the world defined not by geography or ethnicity alone but by its holy calling and covenant relationship to God
- *Definition here:* A nation defined by separation to God — its distinctiveness from all other nations is not cultural preference but holy calling; the national identity IS the sanctification

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### Exodus 19:5-6 — "A Kingdom of Priests, an Holy Nation" Used to Flatten All Distinctions in God's People

**Verses 5-6** — "Now therefore, if ye will obey my voice indeed, and keep my covenant, then ye shall be a peculiar treasure unto me above all people: for all the earth is mine: And ye shall be unto me a kingdom of priests, and an holy nation."

**How it is misused:** This verse is sometimes used to argue that if all Israel (and by extension all the church) is a kingdom of priests, then all distinctions in function, calling, and office among God's people are abolished — that there is no legitimate authority structure, no distinct calling, and no representative ministry because "we are all priests." The priesthood of all believers is used to reject the existence of any leadership or specific calling within the body.

**What the text actually says:** The calling of Israel as a kingdom of priests did not abolish the need for specific representatives and ordained roles — the same chapter establishes the need for Moses as the sole mediator and the subsequent chapters establish the Levitical priesthood:
- **Verse 24** — "And the LORD said unto him, Away, get thee down, and thou shalt come up, thou, and Aaron with thee: but let not the priests and the people break through to come up unto the LORD" — even within the "kingdom of priests," Moses and Aaron alone ascend; distinctions in access and function are maintained
- **Exodus 28:1** — God calls Aaron and his sons to serve as priests — a specific office within the kingdom of priests; the national calling does not replace the specific Levitical calling
- **Numbers 3:12** — God takes the Levites in place of Israel's firstborn — a specific tribe set apart within the nation of priests for specific service
- **1 Peter 2:9** — "But ye are a chosen generation, a royal priesthood, an holy nation" — Peter applies Exodus 19:6 to the church; yet the New Testament still contains specific offices (apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors, teachers — Ephesians 4:11) within the royal priesthood
- **Hebrews 5:4** — "No man taketh this honour unto himself, but he that is called of God, as was Aaron" — specific priestly office still requires divine calling, not self-appointment

*Correction:* "A kingdom of priests" means the entire nation has direct covenant access to God as a corporate priestly body — mediating God's knowledge to the nations. It does not abolish distinctions in function within the body. The same chapter that declares all Israel a kingdom of priests also grants only Moses and Aaron access to the summit. The New Testament royal priesthood (1 Peter 2:9) is equally consistent with specific gifts, offices, and callings within the body (Ephesians 4:11-12). The priesthood of all believers is about shared access to God, not the elimination of differing functions.

---

### Exodus 19:8 — "All That the LORD Hath Spoken We Will Do" Used to Claim Israel Agreed to Earn Salvation by Law

**Verse 8** — "And all the people answered together, and said, All that the LORD hath spoken we will do. And Moses returned the words of the people unto the LORD."

**How it is misused:** This verse is sometimes used to argue that at Sinai, Israel chose to earn their standing before God through performance — and that therefore the Mosaic covenant is fundamentally a covenant of works. This reading then contrasts Abraham's covenant of faith with Sinai's covenant of works, creating a theological discontinuity that makes the Law an interruption of grace.

**What the text actually says:** The consent of verse 8 is made in response to a speech (verses 4-6) that begins with redemption already accomplished and identifies the covenant as the expression of the already-redeemed relationship:
- **Verse 4** — "Ye have seen what I did unto the Egyptians, and how I bare you on eagles' wings, and brought you unto myself" — God has already acted; the Exodus is past; Israel is already redeemed before any law is given
- **Verse 5** — "If ye will obey my voice indeed, and keep my covenant, then ye shall be a peculiar treasure" — the "if" is not a condition for redemption; it is the condition for the full expression of the covenant relationship that already exists
- **Galatians 3:17** — "The covenant, that was confirmed before of God in Christ, the law, which was four hundred and thirty years after, cannot disannul" — the Abrahamic covenant (by faith) preceded and was not cancelled by the Sinai law; the law was given to the already-redeemed, not as the means of redemption
- **Deuteronomy 7:6-8** — "The LORD thy God hath chosen thee... Not because ye were more in number than any people... but because the LORD loved you, and because he would keep the oath which he had sworn unto your fathers, hath the LORD brought you out with a mighty hand" — Israel was chosen and redeemed by grace; the law governs the redeemed life

*Correction:* Israel's "we will do" in Exodus 19:8 is the response of the already-redeemed to their Redeemer — not an attempt to earn the redemption that had already taken place. The Mosaic law was given to people God had already brought out of Egypt on eagles' wings. The same pattern applies to the New Covenant: salvation is by grace through faith; the life of the redeemed is governed by God's commands (John 14:15 — "If ye love me, keep my commandments"). Neither Sinai nor the New Covenant makes obedience the basis of redemption; both present obedience as the pattern of the already-redeemed life.
