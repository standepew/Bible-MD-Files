## CHAPTER SUMMARY

God speaks directly to all the people: the Ten Commandments. He opens with His identity rooted in redemption — "I am the LORD thy God, which have brought thee out of the land of Egypt, out of the house of bondage" — then gives ten words that govern Israel's relationship to God (commandments 1-4) and to one another (5-10). The people respond with terror — they see the thunderings, lightning, smoking mountain, and trumpet sound; they draw back and tell Moses to speak for them, lest they die hearing God directly. Moses reassures them: "God is come to prove you, and that his fear may be before your faces, that ye sin not." Moses then draws near to the thick darkness where God was. God instructs Moses on altars: an altar of earth or unhewn stone — no human tool to touch it; no steps.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "I Am the LORD Thy God, Which Have Brought Thee Out" — Redemption Before Law

**Exodus 20:2** — This is the preamble — not a commandment itself, but the foundation for all ten

- God does not begin: "Keep these laws, then I will bring you out of Egypt." He begins: "I already brought you out. Now here is how my redeemed people live."
- **Romans 3:28** — "Therefore we conclude that a man is justified by faith without the deeds of the law" — the Law was given to the already-redeemed, not as the means of redemption
- **Galatians 3:17** — "The covenant, that was confirmed before of God in Christ, the law, which was four hundred and thirty years after, cannot disannul" — the covenant of grace (Abraham) preceded the Law; the Law cannot change the terms of redemption
- **Romans 6:14** — "Ye are not under the law, but under grace" — not law as means of justification
- **John 15:16** — "Ye have not chosen me, but I have chosen you" — election precedes obedience, as Exodus 20:2 precedes the commandments
- *Interpretation:* The structure of Exodus 20 is the grammar of grace: God redeems first ("I brought thee out"), then gives law as the pattern of redeemed living. This is the anti-legalism principle embedded in the Law itself. Israel did not earn the Exodus by keeping commandments — they were brought out before the commandments were given. The Law governs the already-redeemed life; it does not produce redemption. This is the precise theological point Paul makes in Romans and Galatians.

### The First Commandment — No Other Gods

**Exodus 20:3** — "Thou shalt have no other gods before me"

- Hebrew: al-panai = "before my face" / "in my presence" — not merely ranked second to me, but no others in my presence at all
- **Deuteronomy 6:4-5** — The Shema: "Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God is one LORD: And thou shalt love the LORD thy God with all thine heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy might" — the Shema is the positive statement of the first commandment
- **Matthew 22:37-38** — Jesus: "Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy mind. This is the first and great commandment" — Jesus calls the first commandment the greatest
- **Isaiah 44:6** — "I am the first, and I am the last; and beside me there is no God"
- **Isaiah 45:5** — "I am the LORD, and there is none else, there is no God beside me"
- *Interpretation:* The first commandment is not merely the first in a list — it is the foundation of the other nine. If God is not uniquely God, there is no authority behind any of the remaining commandments. The first commandment is the declaration of theological monotheism as the basis for all ethics.

---

### The Second Commandment — No Graven Images

**Exodus 20:4-6** — "Thou shalt not make unto thee any graven image... I the LORD thy God am a jealous God, visiting the iniquity of the fathers upon the children unto the third and fourth generation of them that hate me; And shewing mercy unto thousands of them that love me, and keep my commandments"

- **Deuteronomy 4:15-18** — "Ye saw no manner of similitude on the day that the LORD spake unto you in Horeb... Take ye therefore good heed unto yourselves; for ye saw no manner of form on the day that the LORD spake unto you" — you cannot image what you did not see; the invisible God cannot be confined to visible form
- **Isaiah 40:18-20, 25** — "To whom then will ye liken God?... To whom then will ye liken me, or shall I be equal? saith the Holy One"
- **Romans 1:22-23** — "Professing themselves to be wise, they became fools, and changed the glory of the uncorruptible God into an image made like to corruptible man" — Paul identifies idolatry as the root of all further sin
- **1 John 5:21** — "Little children, keep yourselves from idols" — John's final word in his epistle
- *Interpretation:* The second commandment does not merely prohibit carved statues — it prohibits any fabricated representation of God that reduces Him to human control. A graven image is god-on-demand — you carry it, you control it, you use it when you want. YHWH will not be controlled or confined. The mercy clause ("showing mercy unto thousands") is radically asymmetric: iniquity visits 3-4 generations; mercy visits thousands. The scale is overwhelmingly merciful.

### The Third Commandment — The Name

**Exodus 20:7** — "Thou shalt not take the name of the LORD thy God in vain; for the LORD will not hold him guiltless that taketh his name in vain"

- Hebrew: shav = emptiness, vanity, falsehood, worthlessness; nasa = to lift up, to carry
- Taking the name "in vain" = carrying God's name emptily, insincerely, falsely — including swearing by His name falsely, using it as a curse, or professing His name while living contrary to it
- **Leviticus 19:12** — "And ye shall not swear by my name falsely, neither shalt thou profane the name of thy God"
- **Matthew 6:9** — "Hallowed be thy name" — the Lord's Prayer opens with the opposite of the third commandment violation; we ask that the Name be treated as holy
- **Matthew 7:21-23** — "Not every one that saith unto me, Lord, Lord, shall enter into the kingdom of heaven" — those who use His name while not doing His will are taking His name in vain
- **Ezekiel 36:21-23** — God restores Israel "for mine holy name's sake, which ye have profaned among the heathen" — Israel carried YHWH's name into exile and profaned it by their behavior; the ultimate third-commandment violation
- *Interpretation:* The third commandment is more than a prohibition on profanity. Its deepest application: those who call themselves by God's name (Christians, Israel) but live contrary to His character are "taking His name in vain" — carrying it emptily. Every time a person who bears God's name lives in contradiction to it, the Name is profaned among the nations (Romans 2:24 — "the name of God is blasphemed among the Gentiles through you").

### The Fourth Commandment — The Sabbath

**Exodus 20:8-11** — "Remember the sabbath day, to keep it holy... For in six days the LORD made heaven and earth... and rested the seventh day: wherefore the LORD blessed the sabbath day, and hallowed it"

- "Remember" — not "institute" or "begin"; the Sabbath is rooted in creation (Genesis 2:2-3); Sinai is remembering what was established at creation
- **Genesis 2:2-3** — "God rested on the seventh day... and sanctified it" — first institution of the Sabbath
- **Deuteronomy 5:15** — In Moses' repetition of the commandments, the reason shifts: "And remember that thou wast a servant in the land of Egypt, and that the LORD thy God brought thee out thence through a mighty hand... therefore the LORD thy God commanded thee to keep the sabbath day" — in Deuteronomy the Sabbath is grounded in redemption, not just creation
- **Hebrews 4:9-10** — "There remaineth therefore a rest (sabbatismos) to the people of God. For he that is entered into his rest, he also hath ceased from his own works, as God did from his" — the Sabbath is ultimately fulfilled in the rest of faith in Christ
- **Mark 2:27-28** — "The sabbath was made for man, and not man for the sabbath: Therefore the Son of man is Lord also of the sabbath"
- *Interpretation:* The Sabbath commandment has a double grounding: creation (Exodus 20) and redemption (Deuteronomy 5). Both foundations point to Christ: He completed the work of creation (John 1:3; Hebrews 1:2) and the work of redemption ("It is finished" — John 19:30). The ultimate Sabbath rest is not a day — it is a Person. Hebrews 4 makes the typological interpretation explicit: "we which have believed do enter into rest."

### The Fifth Commandment — Honor Father and Mother

**Exodus 20:12** — "Honour thy father and thy mother: that thy days may be long upon the land which the LORD thy God giveth thee"

- The first commandment with a promise (Ephesians 6:2)
- **Proverbs 6:20** — "My son, keep thy father's commandment, and forsake not the law of thy mother"
- **Proverbs 30:17** — "The eye that mocketh at his father, and despiseth to obey his mother, the ravens of the valley shall pick it out" — the severity of dishonoring parents
- **Matthew 15:4-6** — Jesus rebukes the Pharisees for using religious vows to circumvent caring for parents: "Ye made the commandment of God of none effect by your tradition"
- **Ephesians 6:1-3** — Paul applies the fifth commandment directly to New Testament children, calling it "the first commandment with promise"
- *Interpretation:* The fifth commandment is the bridge between the two tables — it governs the first human authority relationship and thus transitions from duty to God (commandments 1-4) to duty to neighbors (commandments 6-10). The promised land tenure is conditioned on this — a culture that dishonors parents loses its inheritance. The pattern: parental authority is the first delegated authority under God; to honor parents is to honor the structure of authority God established.

### Commandments 6–10 — The Summary of Human Relations

**Exodus 20:13-17**

- **No murder** → Matthew 5:21-22 — "Ye have heard... Thou shalt not kill: But I say unto you, That whosoever is angry with his brother without a cause shall be in danger of the judgment" — Jesus deepens to the heart level
- **No adultery** → Matthew 5:27-28 — "Whosoever looketh on a woman to lust after her hath committed adultery with her already in his heart"
- **No stealing** → Ephesians 4:28 — "Let him that stole steal no more: but rather let him labour... that he may have to give"
- **No false witness** → Revelation 21:8 — "all liars, shall have their part in the lake which burneth with fire and brimstone"
- **No coveting** → Romans 7:7 — "I had not known sin, but by the law: for I had not known lust, except the law had said, Thou shalt not covet" — Paul identifies the tenth commandment as the one that revealed to him the nature of sin in the heart
- **Romans 13:9** — "Thou shalt not commit adultery, Thou shalt not kill, Thou shalt not steal, Thou shalt not bear false witness, Thou shalt not covet; and if there be any other commandment, it is briefly comprehended in this saying, namely, Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself"
- **Matthew 22:39-40** — "Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself... On these two commandments hang all the law and the prophets" — the two love commands (Deuteronomy 6:5 + Leviticus 19:18) summarize all ten

### "Moses Drew Near unto the Thick Darkness Where God Was"

**Exodus 20:21** — "And the people stood afar off, and Moses drew near unto the thick darkness where God was"

- The people retreat; Moses advances toward the terror
- **Hebrews 10:22** — "Let us draw near with a true heart in full assurance of faith" — the New Covenant invitation is to draw near where the people of Sinai could not
- **Hebrews 4:16** — "Let us therefore come boldly unto the throne of grace"
- **Hebrews 9:7-8** — Under the old system, only the high priest could enter the Holy of Holies — the barrier of unapproachability
- **Matthew 27:51** — At Christ's death, "the veil of the temple was rent in twain from the top to the bottom" — the barrier is torn; the way into the thick darkness is now open for all
- *Interpretation:* "Moses drew near unto the thick darkness where God was" — the mediator alone could approach. Under the New Covenant, every believer is invited to draw near. The torn veil of Matthew 27:51 is the completion of what Exodus 20:21 pictures: the one who could approach alone now makes all who are in Him able to draw near together.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### VAIN (Hebrew: shav)
- **Exodus 20:7** — "take the name of the LORD thy God in vain" — shav = emptiness, nothingness, worthlessness, falsehood
- *Definition here:* To carry or lift up the Name in emptiness — either insincerely (swearing falsely by it), meaninglessly (casual profanity), or hypocritically (claiming the Name while living its contradiction)

### COVET (Hebrew: chamad)
- **Exodus 20:17** — "Thou shalt not covet" — chamad = to desire, delight in, take pleasure in; the internal disposition of craving what belongs to another
- **Romans 7:7-8** — Paul: the commandment not to covet is what revealed sin to him; desire itself is the sin, not only the action that follows
- *Definition here:* The sinful internal disposition to desire what God has given to another; the root of all theft, adultery, and murder — the inward form of all outward sins; the only commandment governing thought rather than action

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The Ten Commandments — Jesus' Summary and Fulfillment

- **Matthew 5:17** — "Think not that I am come to destroy the law, or the prophets: I am not come to destroy, but to fulfil"
- **Matthew 22:37-40** — Jesus summarizes the Ten in two: Love God (commandments 1-4) and love neighbor (5-10)
- **Romans 8:3-4** — "For what the law could not do, in that it was weak through the flesh, God sending his own Son in the likeness of sinful flesh, and for sin, condemned sin in the flesh: That the righteousness of the law might be fulfilled in us, who walk not after the flesh, but after the Spirit"
- **Romans 13:9-10** — "Love worketh no ill to his neighbour: therefore love is the fulfilling of the law"
- *The Ten Commandments are not abolished in Christ; they are fulfilled and their inner demand (love) is achieved in those walking in the Spirit.*

### The Unhewn Altar — No Human Achievement in Worship

- **Exodus 20:25** — "if thou lift up thy tool upon it, thou hast polluted it"
- The altar must be of earth or uncut stone — no human craft improves it
- **Hebrews 9:14** — "the blood of Christ, who through the eternal Spirit offered himself without spot to God" — the altar of the cross requires no human improvement; it is complete
- *Type:* Human tools pollute the altar. God's altar of redemption is not improved by human wisdom, achievement, or religious craft. The cross is sufficient exactly as it is. Any human addition to it defiles it. This is the theology of Exodus 20:25 applied to the gospel.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED OR MISAPPLIED PASSAGES

### "Thou Shalt Not Kill" — The Correct Scope

- **Exodus 20:13** — Hebrew: lo tirtsach — ratsach = to murder, specifically premeditated, illegal killing; NOT the word for lawful warfare (hareg) or judicial execution
- **Numbers 35:30-31** — The same law that says "Thou shalt not kill" commands the death penalty for murder: "Whoso killeth any person, the murderer shall be put to death"
- **Romans 13:4** — The governing authority "beareth not the sword in vain: for he is the minister of God, a revenger to execute wrath upon him that doeth evil"
- *The sixth commandment prohibits murder (ratsach), not all killing. Capital punishment and just warfare are regulated within the same Mosaic law and not contradicted by Exodus 20:13.*

### "Visiting the Iniquity of the Fathers upon the Children" — Not Individual Punishment

- **Exodus 20:5** — "visiting the iniquity of the fathers upon the children unto the third and fourth generation of them that hate me"
- **Ezekiel 18:20** — "The son shall not bear the iniquity of the father... the righteousness of the righteous shall be upon him, and the wickedness of the wicked shall be upon him"
- *These are not contradictory.* Exodus 20:5 describes the natural consequence of covenantal unfaithfulness — a family/community that perpetuates idol worship passes its effects down; Ezekiel 18 addresses individual accountability. Neither cancels the other.

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

**The Two Tables / The Two Loves:**
- First appears: Exodus 20:1-17 — Ten Commandments given in two groups: commandments 1-4 govern the relationship to God; commandments 5-10 govern the relationship to neighbor
- Deuteronomy 9:10 — "the LORD delivered unto me two tables of stone written with the finger of God" — physically two tablets; the division was not arbitrary
- Matthew 22:37-40 — Jesus: "Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy mind. This is the first and great commandment. And the second is like unto it, Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself. On these two commandments hang all the law and the prophets" — Jesus reduces ten to two, exactly along the same division
- Romans 13:9 — "For this, Thou shalt not commit adultery, Thou shalt not kill, Thou shalt not steal, Thou shalt not bear false witness, Thou shalt not covet; and if there be any other commandment, it is briefly comprehended in this saying, namely, Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself" — the second table summarized in neighbor-love
- 1 John 4:20 — "If a man say, I love God, and hateth his brother, he is a liar" — the two tables cannot be separated; love of God and love of neighbor are inseparable in Scripture
- *Pattern:* The two-table structure of the Decalogue runs through Scripture as the two-love summary. The entire moral law hangs on two loves; to fail in one table is to fail in both. Jesus' summary in Matthew 22 is not a simplification of the Law — it is the revelation of the Law's inner architecture.

**The Name of the LORD:**
- First established in Exodus 3:14-15 — "I AM THAT I AM... The LORD God of your fathers" — the name is the covenant identity of God
- Exodus 20:7 — "Thou shalt not take the name of the LORD thy God in vain" — the covenant name is protected by the third commandment; bearing the name carries weight and responsibility
- Leviticus 24:16 — "he that blasphemeth the name of the LORD, he shall surely be put to death" — the name carried capital weight under the Mosaic covenant
- Ezekiel 36:21-23 — "I had pity for mine holy name, which the house of Israel had profaned among the heathen... I will sanctify my great name, which was profaned among the heathen... and the heathen shall know that I am the LORD" — Israel carried the name into captivity and profaned it; God acts to vindicate His own name
- Matthew 6:9 — "Hallowed be thy name" — the Lord's Prayer opens with the request that what the third commandment forbids might be replaced by its positive fulfillment: the name treated as holy
- John 17:6, 26 — "I have manifested thy name unto the men which thou gavest me... I have declared unto them thy name" — Jesus declares He came to manifest and declare the Father's name; He is the embodiment of what the name means
- Philippians 2:9-10 — "God also hath highly exalted him, and given him a name which is above every name: That at the name of Jesus every knee should bow" — the name given to Christ is the fullness of what YHWH means; the third commandment finds its ultimate fulfillment in the proper honor given to the name of Jesus
- *Pattern:* The Name of the LORD is the most repeated theme of the Exodus and the Prophets. God acts "for His name's sake" (Psalm 23:3; Ezekiel 36:22; Isaiah 48:9). The covenant name is carried by God's people — and their conduct either hallows or profanes it. Christ is the Name made flesh; to take His name in vain is the same third-commandment violation in its New Covenant form. The whole of Scripture is the story of God vindicating and hallowing His own name through the history of His people.

**Jealousy / Zeal of God:**
- Exodus 20:5 — "For I the LORD thy God am a jealous God" — the second commandment declares God's jealousy over His people's exclusive worship; the same Hebrew word (qanna) is used only for God, never for human jealousy in this covenantal sense
- Exodus 34:14 — "For thou shalt worship no other god: for the LORD, whose name is Jealous, is a jealous God" — jealousy is not merely an attribute; it is embedded in His name
- Numbers 25:11 — Phinehas "was zealous for my sake among them" — the human zeal of Phinehas mirrors God's own jealousy; he acts as God would act against covenant violation
- Isaiah 9:7 — "The zeal of the LORD of hosts will perform this" — God's jealous zeal is the engine of the fulfillment of all covenant promises
- John 2:17 — "The zeal of thine house hath eaten me up" — Jesus' cleansing of the temple; the disciples connect His action to Psalm 69:9; Christ embodies the jealousy of God for pure worship
- 2 Corinthians 11:2 — "For I am jealous over you with godly jealousy: for I have espoused you to one husband, that I may present you as a chaste virgin to Christ" — Paul mirrors God's covenant jealousy in his apostolic care
- *Pattern:* God's jealousy is the covenant form of His love — the fierce exclusive claim of a husband over his wife, of the Creator over His creature's worship. It is not insecurity but righteous covenant demand. Every act of idolatry provokes it; every act of genuine worship satisfies it. Christ's zeal for the Father's house (John 2:17) is the incarnate expression of the same jealousy declared in Exodus 20:5. The jealousy of God runs from Sinai to the cross to Revelation 19:2 where God avenges the blood of His servants.
