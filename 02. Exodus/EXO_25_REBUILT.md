## CHAPTER SUMMARY

God instructs Moses to take a freewill offering from every willing heart: gold, silver, brass, blue/purple/scarlet, fine linen, goat hair, rams' skins dyed red, badger skins, shittim wood, oil, spices, incense, onyx stones. The purpose: "Let them make me a sanctuary; that I may dwell among them. According to all that I shew thee, after the pattern of the tabernacle, and the pattern of all the instruments thereof, even so shall ye make it." Instructions follow for three of the most sacred items: (1) The Ark — shittim wood overlaid with pure gold inside and out; the mercy seat of pure gold; two cherubim of beaten gold facing each other; "there I will meet with thee, and I will commune with thee from above the mercy seat." (2) The Table of Showbread — shittim wood overlaid with gold; twelve loaves of shewbread always before God. (3) The Menorah — one talent of pure gold, seven lamps, shaped like an almond tree in bloom.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "That I May Dwell Among Them" — The Goal of All Redemption

**Exodus 25:8** — "And let them make me a sanctuary; that I may dwell among them"

- This is the stated purpose of the entire Tabernacle project — not worship, not priesthood, not ritual, but God dwelling among His people
- **Leviticus 26:11-12** — "And I will set my tabernacle among you... and I will walk among you, and will be your God, and ye shall be my people"
- **Ezekiel 37:27** — "My tabernacle also shall be with them: yea, I will be their God, and they shall be my people"
- **John 1:14** — "And the Word was made flesh, and dwelt (literally: tabernacled) among us" — Greek skenoo = to tabernacle; the Incarnation is God fulfilling the Exodus 25:8 purpose in person
- **Revelation 21:3** — "Behold, the tabernacle of God is with men, and he will dwell with them, and they shall be his people, and God himself shall be with them, and be their God" — the ultimate fulfillment; Exodus 25:8 fully realized in the new creation
- *Interpretation:* "That I may dwell among them" is the one sentence that explains everything from Exodus 25 to Revelation 21. The Tabernacle, the Temple, the Incarnation, the church as the temple of the Spirit, and the new Jerusalem are all stages in the single divine purpose: God dwelling among His redeemed people. The Tabernacle is the portable dwelling; the Temple is the permanent dwelling; the Incarnation is God Himself becoming the dwelling place (John 2:19 — "destroy this temple"); the new Jerusalem is the eternal dwelling where God and His people are never separated again.

### "After the Pattern Shewed Thee in the Mount" — The Earthly Copies of Heavenly Realities

**Exodus 25:9, 40** — "According to all that I shew thee, after the pattern of the tabernacle... And look that thou make them after their pattern, which was shewed thee in the mount"

- **Hebrews 8:4-5** — "For if he were on earth, he should not be a priest... who serve unto the example and shadow of heavenly things, as Moses was admonished of God when he was about to make the tabernacle: for, See, saith he, that thou make all things according to the pattern shewed to thee in the mount"
- **Hebrews 9:23-24** — "It was therefore necessary that the patterns of things in the heavens should be purified with these; but the heavenly things themselves with better sacrifices than these. For Christ is not entered into the holy places made with hands, which are the figures of the true; but into heaven itself, now to appear in the presence of God for us"
- **Revelation 11:19** — "And the temple of God was opened in heaven, and there was seen in his temple the ark of his testament" — the heavenly ark is real; the earthly ark was its copy
- *Interpretation:* This is one of the most theologically decisive statements in Exodus. The earthly Tabernacle is not original — it is a copy of the heavenly reality that Moses saw on the mountain. Everything in the Tabernacle is a shadow of the heavenly original. Hebrews 8-9 builds its entire argument on this principle: the old covenant priesthood and sacrifices are "shadows" of the heavenly realities accomplished by Christ. The earthly mercy seat is a copy; Christ's blood sprinkled in the heavenly sanctuary is the reality (Hebrews 9:12, 24). The earthly ark was made; the heavenly ark exists (Revelation 11:19). Moses built a copy; Jesus entered the original.

### The Ark of the Testimony — The Throne of God Among Men

**Exodus 25:10-22** — The Ark: wood overlaid with gold, staves, the mercy seat, two cherubim

- **Numbers 10:35** — "Rise up, LORD, and let thine enemies be scattered; and let them that hate thee flee before thee" — Moses' prayer when the Ark set forward; the Ark = God's presence going before Israel in battle
- **1 Samuel 4:3-11** — Israel carries the Ark to battle like a talisman; the Philistines capture it — the Ark is not a magic box; God's presence is not coerced
- **2 Samuel 6:6-7** — Uzzah touches the Ark to steady it and is struck dead — the holiness of the Ark demands the prescribed approach
- **Hebrews 9:4** — The Ark contained: the golden pot of manna (Exodus 16:33), Aaron's rod that budded (Numbers 17:10), and the tables of the covenant (Exodus 25:16)
- **Psalm 80:1** — "Give ear, O Shepherd of Israel, thou that leadest Joseph like a flock; thou that dwellest between the cherubims, shine forth"
- *Interpretation:* The Ark is the earthly throne of God — "I will meet with thee from above the mercy seat, from between the two cherubims" (v.22). The cherubim posture (facing each other, wings covering the mercy seat, faces toward the seat) mirrors the posture of the heavenly cherubim described in Ezekiel and Isaiah 6. The Ark is where God's justice (the Law inside) and God's mercy (the mercy seat over it) meet. This is atonement theology built into furniture: the Law demands death; the blood on the mercy seat covers the Law; God looks down and sees mercy covering justice.

### The Mercy Seat — Kapporeth — The Place of Atonement

**Exodus 25:17-22** — The mercy seat of pure gold; two cherubim of beaten gold; the meeting place

- Hebrew: kapporeth = covering, atonement; from kaphar = to cover, to atone, to make propitiation
- **Leviticus 16:14-15** — On the Day of Atonement, the high priest sprinkles blood on the mercy seat — the blood covers the Law and God accepts the people
- **Romans 3:25** — "Whom God hath set forth to be a propitiation (hilasterion — Greek translation of kapporeth) through faith in his blood" — Paul calls Christ the mercy seat (the kapporeth)
- **Hebrews 4:16** — "Let us therefore come boldly unto the throne of grace" — the throne of grace IS the mercy seat; the same place where God meets with His people
- *Interpretation:* Romans 3:25 is the New Testament interpretation of Exodus 25:17-22. Paul uses hilasterion (the Greek word the Septuagint uses for kapporeth) to describe Christ. Christ IS the mercy seat — the place where God's justice (the Law) is satisfied by blood, and where God meets with His people. The high priest sprinkled blood on the mercy seat once a year (Leviticus 16); Christ entered the heavenly sanctuary with His own blood once for all (Hebrews 9:12). The earthly mercy seat was sprinkled repeatedly; the heavenly mercy seat was covered once and permanently.

### The Table of Showbread — The Bread of the Presence

**Exodus 25:23-30** — The table of shittim wood overlaid with gold; the shewbread always before God

- Hebrew: lechem panim = bread of the face/presence — the bread is always before God's face
- Twelve loaves = twelve tribes; all Israel is perpetually before God
- **Leviticus 24:5-9** — Twelve loaves, changed every Sabbath; only the priests eat the old loaves
- **1 Samuel 21:6** — David eats the showbread in necessity — "there was no bread there but the shewbread"
- **Matthew 12:4** — Jesus cites David eating the showbread to defend His disciples' eating on the Sabbath — "Have ye not read what David did?"
- **John 6:35** — "I am the bread of life" — Christ as the living showbread; always before the Father; the sustenance of His people
- *Interpretation:* The showbread (bread of the Presence) is always before God — twelve loaves representing all twelve tribes perpetually in God's presence. Christ is the antitype: He is the Bread of Life (John 6:35) who is always before the Father interceding for His people (Hebrews 7:25). As the showbread was perpetually present before God in the tabernacle, Christ is perpetually present before the Father as our intercessor.

---

### The Golden Menorah — The Light of the World

**Exodus 25:31-40** — The menorah of pure gold; seven lamps; shaped like an almond tree; one talent of gold

- **Numbers 8:2** — "The seven lamps shall give light over against the candlestick" — the lamps illuminate the Holy Place
- **Zechariah 4:1-6** — Zechariah's vision: the golden menorah with the bowl and two olive trees; the angel explains: "Not by might, nor by power, but by my spirit, saith the LORD of hosts" — the oil is the Spirit
- **Revelation 1:12-13, 20** — "And I turned to see the voice that spake with me. And being turned, I saw seven golden candlesticks; And in the midst of the seven candlesticks one like unto the Son of man... The seven candlesticks which thou sawest are the seven churches"
- **John 8:12** — "I am the light of the world: he that followeth me shall not walk in darkness" — Christ as the fulfillment of the Menorah
- **Matthew 5:14** — "Ye are the light of the world" — the church as the continuing lampstand of Christ's light
- *Interpretation:* The menorah is the light of the Holy Place — without it, the priests work in darkness. It is shaped like an almond tree (Numbers 17:8 — Aaron's rod bloomed almonds, the sign of the legitimate priesthood). The seven-branched menorah becomes in Revelation the symbol of the seven churches — each church is a lampstand bearing Christ's light in its city. Christ is the central shaft; the church is the branches. Zechariah 4:6 gives the theological principle: the oil is the Spirit — the menorah burns by the Holy Spirit, not human effort.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### MERCY SEAT (Hebrew: kapporeth)
- **Exodus 25:17** — kapporeth = covering, lid, place of atonement; from kaphar = to cover, to atone
- **Romans 3:25** — Greek hilasterion = propitiation, mercy seat — the same word used for kapporeth in the Septuagint, and used by Paul to describe Christ
- *Definition here:* The gold lid of the Ark where the blood of atonement was applied and where God met with His people; the place where Law and mercy intersect; the type of Christ as the propitiation (hilasterion) for sin

### SHITTIM WOOD (Hebrew: atzei shittim)
- The wood of the acacia tree — a hard, incorruptible wood that grows in the Sinai desert
- Used for the Ark, the Table, and the Boards of the Tabernacle
- Incorruptibility = Christ's human nature which did not see corruption (Acts 2:27 — "neither wilt thou suffer thine Holy One to see corruption")
- *Definition here:* The incorruptible wood that forms the structural core of the Ark and Tabernacle, overlaid with gold; the combination of wood (humanity) and gold (divinity) is the consistent typological picture of the hypostatic union — fully human, fully divine

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### Exodus 25:8 — "That I May Dwell Among Them" — Reduced to a Building Project

**Verse 8** — "And let them make me a sanctuary; that I may dwell among them."

**How it is misused:** This verse is regularly cited as a justification for elaborate church building programs — "God wants a beautiful house, therefore we must build one." The purpose of the command is reduced to the construction project itself, detached from the theology of divine dwelling.

**What the text actually says:**
- **Acts 7:48-49** — Stephen before the Sanhedrin: "Howbeit the most High dwelleth not in temples made with hands; as saith the prophet, Heaven is my throne, and earth is my footstool: what house will ye build me? saith the Lord"
- **1 Kings 8:27** — Solomon at the Temple dedication: "But will God indeed dwell on the earth? behold, the heaven and heaven of heavens cannot contain thee; how much less this house that I have builded?"
- **John 2:19-21** — "Destroy this temple, and in three days I will raise it up... But he spake of the temple of his body"
- **1 Corinthians 6:19** — "What? know ye not that your body is the temple of the Holy Ghost which is in you"
- **Revelation 21:3** — "Behold, the tabernacle of God is with men, and he will dwell with them"

*Correction:* The tabernacle/temple is never an end in itself — it is the instrument of the one purpose stated in Exodus 25:8: "that I may dwell among them." The physical sanctuary was always a type pointing to God's ultimate dwelling in human flesh (John 1:14), in His people as a corporate temple (1 Corinthians 3:16), and finally in the new creation (Revelation 21:3). Stephen and Solomon both understood that no building actually contains God. The command to build is real; but the point of the building is always God dwelling with His people, not the structure itself.

---

### Exodus 25:40 — "After the Pattern Shewed Thee in the Mount" — Ignored in Worship Design

**Verse 40** — "And look that thou make them after their pattern, which was shewed thee in the mount."

**How it is misused:** This verse is either ignored entirely in discussions of Christian worship — treated as an Old Covenant curiosity with no bearing on how the church approaches God — or it is used selectively to enforce specific furniture arrangements without grasping the theological principle.

**What the text actually says:**
- **Hebrews 8:5** — "Who serve unto the example and shadow of heavenly things, as Moses was admonished of God when he was about to make the tabernacle: for, See, saith he, that thou make all things according to the pattern shewed to thee in the mount"
- **Hebrews 9:23-24** — "It was therefore necessary that the patterns of things in the heavens should be purified with these; but the heavenly things themselves with better sacrifices... For Christ is not entered into the holy places made with hands, which are the figures of the true; but into heaven itself"
- **Revelation 11:19** — "And the temple of God was opened in heaven, and there was seen in his temple the ark of his testament" — the heavenly originals exist; the earthly were copies
- **Colossians 2:17** — "Which are a shadow of things to come; but the body is of Christ"

*Correction:* The theological principle of Exodus 25:40 is that the earthly Tabernacle was a copy of a heavenly original. Hebrews 8-9 builds its entire argument on this: the earthly priesthood, sacrifices, and furniture are "shadows" of the heavenly realities accomplished by Christ. The practical application for New Covenant worship is not about specific furniture but about this: approach God according to His revealed pattern, not human invention. The "pattern in the mount" principle means that worship must be shaped by what God has revealed, not what seems good to human creativity.

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

**Gold:**
- First appears in the Tabernacle: Exodus 25:3 — gold is the first metal listed among the offerings
- Exodus 25:11 — the Ark overlaid with pure gold "within and without"
- Exodus 25:17 — the mercy seat "of pure gold"
- Exodus 25:31 — the menorah "of pure gold"
- Revelation 21:18, 21 — "the city was pure gold, like unto clear glass... the street of the city was pure gold"
- Job 23:10 — "when he hath tried me, I shall come forth as gold"
- 1 Peter 1:7 — "That the trial of your faith, being much more precious than of gold that perisheth, though it be tried with fire, might be found unto praise and honour and glory at the appearing of Jesus Christ"
- *Pattern:* Gold in the Tabernacle is consistently associated with the divine presence and divine nature — the closer to the Most Holy Place, the more gold dominates. The outer court is bronze; the Holy Place is gold-overlaid; the Most Holy Place is pure gold. The New Jerusalem is described as pure gold (Revelation 21:18) — the eschatological completion of what the Tabernacle's gold signified. Gold as a symbol of divinity and refined character is also used by Job and Peter: faith tried in fire comes out as gold.

---

**Shittim Wood (Acacia):**
- First appears: Exodus 25:5 — shittim wood listed among the Tabernacle materials
- Exodus 25:10 — the Ark made of shittim wood, overlaid with gold
- Exodus 25:13 — the staves of shittim wood, overlaid with gold
- Exodus 25:23 — the table of shittim wood, overlaid with gold
- Acts 2:27 — Peter quotes Psalm 16:10 of Christ: "Because thou wilt not leave my soul in hell, neither wilt thou suffer thine Holy One to see corruption"
- *Pattern:* Shittim (acacia) wood is the hardest, most incorruptible wood available in the Sinai region — it resists rot and decay. Every piece of Tabernacle furniture that carries the presence of God is built from this incorruptible wood and overlaid with gold. The wood-and-gold combination is the consistent material picture of the hypostatic union: incorruptible humanity (wood that does not decay) united with divine glory (gold). Acts 2:27 applies the incorruptibility principle directly to Christ's resurrection — His body would not see corruption. The shittim wood is the physical type of that incorruptible human nature.

---

**The Cherubim:**
- First appears: Genesis 3:24 — "So he drove out the man; and he placed at the east of the garden of Eden Cherubims, and a flaming sword which turned every way, to keep the way of the tree of life"
- Exodus 25:18-20 — two cherubim of beaten gold on the mercy seat, wings spread, faces toward the mercy seat
- Ezekiel 1:5-14 — the four living creatures (cherubim) carrying the divine throne; described as having four faces, four wings, and wheels
- Ezekiel 10:20 — "This is the living creature that I saw under the God of Israel by the river of Chebar; and I knew that they were the cherubims"
- Revelation 4:6-8 — "And in the midst of the throne, and round about the throne, were four beasts (living creatures) full of eyes... And the first beast was like a lion, and the second beast like a calf, and the third beast had a face as a man, and the fourth beast was like a flying eagle"
- *Pattern:* The cherubim at Eden guard the way to life; the cherubim on the mercy seat face the place where atonement is made. The change in orientation is the gospel in symbol: what barred the way back to life (Eden's cherubim with flaming sword) now bows over the place where the barrier is removed (the mercy seat's blood). In Revelation 4, the cherubim surround the throne crying "Holy, holy, holy" — they have moved from guardians of exclusion to worshippers of the One who opened the way. The cherubim's posture tracks the entire arc of redemptive history.
