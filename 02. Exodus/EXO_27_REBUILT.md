## CHAPTER SUMMARY

The Altar of Burnt Offering: shittim wood overlaid with brass, five cubits square and three cubits high, a horn on each of its four corners, with a brass grate and brass implements, carried on staves. This is the first thing encountered when entering the Tabernacle courtyard — the place of sacrifice. The Court of the Tabernacle: 100 cubits long by 50 wide, enclosed with fine linen hangings on pillars with brass sockets and silver hooks. The gate on the east side: a 20-cubit hanging of blue, purple, and scarlet on four pillars. Everything visible from outside is linen and brass; everything inside is gold. Command for the perpetual lamp: pure beaten olive oil, kept burning from evening to morning by Aaron and his sons, as a statute forever before the LORD.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### The Altar of Burnt Offering — The Cross of the Tabernacle

**Exodus 27:1-8** — The first object encountered entering the Tabernacle court

- The altar is the threshold of approach — you cannot enter God's presence without passing the place of sacrifice
- **Hebrews 13:10** — "We have an altar, whereof they have no right to eat which serve the tabernacle" — the New Testament altar is Christ's cross
- **John 14:6** — "I am the way, the truth, and the life: no man cometh unto the Father, but by me" — no approach to God except through sacrifice; no entry to the Father except through Christ
- **Romans 3:25** — "Whom God hath set forth to be a propitiation through faith in his blood"
- *Interpretation:* The layout of the Tabernacle is the gospel in geography: you enter the gate (there is only one gate — John 10:9), and the first thing you encounter is the altar of sacrifice. There is no path past the altar to God; the altar must be passed through. This is the irreducible truth of the gospel: no one comes to the Father except through the cross. The altar's position is not incidental — it is the theological claim of the entire system.

---

### The Horns of the Altar — The Place of Refuge

**Exodus 27:2** — "And thou shalt make the horns of it upon the four corners thereof: his horns shall be of the same"

- **Exodus 21:14** — A murderer is taken even from the altar's horns to die — the altar horn is a place of refuge for the innocent but not the guilty
- **1 Kings 1:50** — Adonijah "caught hold on the horns of the altar" when he feared Solomon
- **1 Kings 2:28** — Joab fled and "caught hold on the horns of the altar" — Solomon's judgment reached him there
- **Psalm 118:27** — "Bind the sacrifice with cords, even unto the horns of the altar"
- *Interpretation:* The horns of the altar were the four protruding points at each corner — the place where the blood of atonement was applied (Leviticus 4:7) and the place to which one who feared death fled for refuge. The four corners and four horns = God's protection extending in all four directions. The altar simultaneously executes judgment on the sacrifice and provides refuge for the person; Christ's cross simultaneously executes God's judgment on sin and provides refuge for the sinner.

---

### The Single Gate of the Court — "I Am the Door"

**Exodus 27:16** — "And for the gate of the court shall be an hanging of twenty cubits, of blue, and purple, and scarlet, and fine twined linen"

- One gate on the east side; no other entry into the court
- **John 10:9** — "I am the door: by me if any man enter in, he shall be saved, and shall go in and out, and find pasture"
- **John 14:6** — "No man cometh unto the Father, but by me"
- **Acts 4:12** — "Neither is there salvation in any other: for there is none other name under heaven given among men, whereby we must be saved"
- *Interpretation:* The Tabernacle court had one gate. Not multiple entrances for different traditions or approaches — one gate, facing east. The gate was 20 cubits wide — large enough for all to enter, but only one. This is the architectural statement of Acts 4:12: one way in. Jesus' identification as "the door" (John 10:9) is the New Testament interpretation of the single gate of the Tabernacle court.

---

### The Perpetual Lamp — Evening to Morning

**Exodus 27:20-21** — "pure oil olive beaten for the light, to cause the lamp to burn always... from evening to morning before the LORD"

- **Leviticus 24:2-4** — The lamp burns continually; Aaron tends it from evening to morning in the Holy Place
- **Psalm 119:105** — "Thy word is a lamp unto my feet, and a light unto my path"
- **Zechariah 4:1-6** — The golden lampstand with the oil from the olive trees: "Not by might, nor by power, but by my spirit" — the oil is the Holy Spirit
- **Revelation 4:5** — "And there were seven lamps of fire burning before the throne, which are the seven Spirits of God"
- *Interpretation:* The lamp burns from evening to morning — through the night, the light in the sanctuary never goes out. God's light does not fail in the darkness. The oil (the Holy Spirit) is the fuel; the priests maintain the supply; the light is perpetual. This is the promise of Christ's presence: "lo, I am with you alway, even unto the end of the world" (Matthew 28:20).

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

**ALTAR (mizbeach — מִזְבֵּחַ)**
- From the root "to slaughter" — a place of sacrifice, not merely a raised platform
- Exodus 27:1: The Altar of Burnt Offering — brass, foursquare, the first object in the Tabernacle court
- Hebrews 13:10: "We have an altar" — the NT identifies Christ's cross as the fulfillment
- *Definition here:* The divinely appointed place of substitutionary sacrifice

**HORNS OF THE ALTAR**
- Exodus 27:2: Four horns at the four corners of the altar
- Blood of atonement applied to the horns (Leviticus 4:7, 18, 25, 30, 34)
- Place of refuge (1 Kings 1:50) — but not for the murderer (Exodus 21:14)
- *Definition here:* Both the power of God's judgment and the mercy of His refuge

**STATUTE FOREVER (chuqqah olam)**
- Exodus 27:21: The perpetual lamp is a "statute for ever"
- "Forever" (olam) = age-lasting; continuing throughout the appointed covenant period
- *Definition here:* A binding ordinance for the duration of the covenant era — not necessarily without end

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

**1** And thou shalt make an altar of shittim wood, five cubits long, and five cubits broad; the altar shall be foursquare: and the height thereof shall be three cubits.
**2** And thou shalt make the horns of it upon the four corners thereof: his horns shall be of the same: and thou shalt overlay it with brass.
**3** And thou shalt make his pans to receive his ashes, and his shovels, and his basons, and his fleshhooks, and his firepans: all the vessels thereof thou shalt make of brass.
**4** And thou shalt make for it a grate of network of brass; and upon the net shalt thou make four brasen rings in the four corners thereof.
**5** And thou shalt put it under the compass of the altar beneath, that the net may be even to the midst of the altar.
**6** And thou shalt make staves for the altar, staves of shittim wood, and overlay them with brass.
**7** And the staves shall be put into the rings, and the staves shall be upon the two sides of the altar, to bear it.
**8** Hollow with boards shalt thou make it: as it was shewed thee in the mount, so shall they make it.
**9** And thou shalt make the court of the tabernacle: for the south side southward there shall be hangings for the court of fine twined linen of an hundred cubits long for one side:
**10** And the twenty pillars thereof and their twenty sockets shall be of brass; the hooks of the pillars and their fillets shall be of silver.
**11** And likewise for the north side in length there shall be hangings of an hundred cubits long, and his twenty pillars and their twenty sockets of brass; the hooks of the pillars and their fillets of silver.
**12** And for the breadth of the court on the west side shall be hangings of fifty cubits: their pillars ten, and their sockets ten.
**13** And the breadth of the court on the east side eastward shall be fifty cubits.
**14** The hangings of one side of the gate shall be fifteen cubits: their pillars three, and their sockets three.
**15** And on the other side shall be hangings fifteen cubits: their pillars three, and their sockets three.
**16** And for the gate of the court shall be an hanging of twenty cubits, of blue, and purple, and scarlet, and fine twined linen, wrought with needlework: and their pillars shall be four, and their sockets four.
**17** All the pillars round about the court shall be filleted with silver; their hooks shall be of silver, and their sockets of brass.
**18** The length of the court shall be an hundred cubits, and the breadth fifty every where, and the height five cubits of fine twined linen, and their sockets of brass.
**19** All the vessels of the tabernacle in all the service thereof, and all the pins thereof, and all the pins of the court, shall be of brass.
**20** And thou shalt command the children of Israel, that they bring thee pure oil olive beaten for the light, to cause the lamp to burn always.
**21** In the tabernacle of the congregation without the vail, which is before the testimony, Aaron and his sons shall order it from evening to morning before the LORD: it shall be a statute for ever unto their generations on the behalf of the children of Israel.

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The Tabernacle as the Gospel in Architecture

Moving from outside to inside traces the path of salvation:

1. **The single gate (east)** → John 10:9 — "I am the door"
2. **The Altar of Burnt Offering** → Romans 3:25 — propitiation through faith in His blood
3. **The Laver** (ch.30) → John 13:10 — "he that is washed needeth not save to wash his feet"
4. **The Holy Place: Table, Menorah, Altar of Incense** → Fellowship with God: the Word, the Spirit, prayer
5. **The Veil** → Matthew 27:51 — torn at the cross; access now open
6. **The Most Holy Place: Ark and Mercy Seat** → Hebrews 10:19 — boldness to enter the Holiest

Every piece of furniture, every spatial arrangement preaches the path to God through Christ.

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

**The Single East Gate:**
- East = direction of rising sun
- The Tabernacle gate faces east — entry is from where the sun rises
- Ezekiel 43:2 — the glory of God returning from the east
- Matthew 24:27 — "as the lightning cometh out of the east" — the coming of the Son of Man
- One gate: the architectural preaching of John 14:6 and Acts 4:12

**Brass vs. Gold — Two Zones:**
- Outside (the court): brass — brass sockets, brass altar, brass grate
- Inside the Tabernacle: gold — gold overlaid boards, gold lampstand, gold altar of incense
- Brass = judgment, earthly; Gold = divine, incorruptible
- The path from brass to gold = the path from judgment to grace

**The Perpetual Lamp — Light Through Darkness:**
- Burns from evening to morning — specifically through the night
- The church age is night; Christ is the light (John 8:12)
- Revelation 21:23 — in the new Jerusalem "the Lamb is the light thereof" — the perpetual lamp fulfilled
