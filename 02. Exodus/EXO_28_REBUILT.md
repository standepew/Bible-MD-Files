## CHAPTER SUMMARY

God commands garments for Aaron "for glory and for beauty," made by those filled with the spirit of wisdom. The garments: the ephod (with two onyx stones on the shoulders engraved with the twelve tribes' names — "Aaron shall bear their names before the LORD upon his two shoulders for a memorial"); the breastplate of judgment (twelve stones in four rows, each engraved with a tribe's name — "Aaron shall bear the names of the children of Israel in the breastplate of judgment upon his heart... before the LORD continually"; the Urim and Thummim inside the breastplate "upon Aaron's heart"); the robe of the ephod (all blue, with golden bells and pomegranates alternating on the hem — "his sound shall be heard when he goeth in unto the holy place... that he die not"); and the golden plate (engraved "HOLINESS TO THE LORD," worn on Aaron's forehead "that Aaron may bear the iniquity of the holy things... that they may be accepted before the LORD").

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### Aaron Bears Their Names on His Shoulders and Heart — The High Priest's Intercessory Load

**Exodus 28:12, 29** — "Aaron shall bear their names before the LORD upon his two shoulders for a memorial... Aaron shall bear the names of the children of Israel in the breastplate of judgment upon his heart... before the LORD continually"

- **Hebrews 7:25** — "He ever liveth to make intercession for them" — Christ as the ultimate High Priest bearing His people before the Father
- **Isaiah 9:6** — "the government shall be upon his shoulder" — the Messianic King bears responsibility on His shoulders
- **Isaiah 53:12** — "he bare the sin of many, and made intercession for the transgressors"
- **Romans 8:34** — "Christ Jesus... who also maketh intercession for us"
- **John 17** — The High-Priestly prayer: Jesus bearing each name before the Father
- *Interpretation:* The high priest wore Israel's names on two places: on his shoulders (carrying their weight — the place of burden and strength) and on his heart (carrying their identity — the place of love and relationship). Christ carries His people in both dimensions: He is strong enough to bear their weight (the government on His shoulder) and intimate enough to love each one (He knows His sheep by name — John 10:3). The Aaronic high priest wore twelve stones representing twelve tribes — the same names, every time he entered the presence of God. Christ carries His people before the Father perpetually (Hebrews 7:25).

### "HOLINESS TO THE LORD" — The Golden Plate

**Exodus 28:36-38** — "Thou shalt make a plate of pure gold, and grave upon it, like the engravings of a signet, HOLINESS TO THE LORD... it shall be upon Aaron's forehead, that Aaron may bear the iniquity of the holy things... that they may be accepted before the LORD"

- The golden plate is worn on the forehead — the most visible, prominent position
- It declares the identity of the entire ministry: HOLINESS TO THE LORD
- **Zechariah 14:20** — "In that day shall there be upon the bells of the horses, HOLINESS UNTO THE LORD" — in the Millennium, even the horses' bells will carry this inscription; what was exclusive to the high priest becomes universal
- **1 Peter 1:15-16** — "But as he which hath called you is holy, so be ye holy in all manner of conversation; Because it is written, Be ye holy; for I am holy"
- **Revelation 22:4** — "And they shall see his face; and his name shall be in their foreheads" — the redeemed bear God's name on their foreheads; the ultimate fulfillment of the golden plate
- *Interpretation:* The golden plate declares the purpose and identity of the entire priestly ministry: HOLINESS TO THE LORD. This is not merely a description of the high priest's personal character — it is his identification with the holiness of the One he represents. Aaron bears the iniquity of the holy things (even Israel's best worship is imperfect; the high priest absorbs that imperfection into his mediatorial role). Christ's New Covenant fulfillment: His perfect holiness covers the imperfections of His people's worship. Revelation 22:4 is the eschatological fulfillment: the name of God on the foreheads of the redeemed — every believer becomes a living golden plate.

### "His Sound Shall Be Heard" — The Bells and the Living High Priest

**Exodus 28:35** — "And it shall be upon Aaron to minister: and his sound shall be heard when he goeth in unto the holy place before the LORD, and when he cometh out, that he die not"

- The bells on the hem of the robe served as an audible signal: as long as the bells rang, the high priest was alive in the presence of God
- **Hebrews 7:24-25** — "But this man, because he continueth ever, hath an unchangeable priesthood. Wherefore he is able also to save them to the uttermost that come unto God by him, seeing he ever liveth to make intercession for them"
- *Interpretation:* The bells' sound confirmed the high priest was still alive and ministering. Under the Aaronic priesthood, the silence of the bells meant death. The New Covenant high priest never goes silent — "he ever liveth." Christ's perpetual intercession (Hebrews 7:25) is the silence of those bells broken permanently: the sound of intercession never ceases. "He ever liveth" = the bells are always ringing.

### The Urim and Thummim — God's Direct Judgment

**Exodus 28:30** — "And thou shalt put in the breastplate of judgment the Urim and the Thummim; and they shall be upon Aaron's heart, when he goeth in before the LORD: and Aaron shall bear the judgment of the children of Israel upon his heart before the LORD continually"

- Hebrew: Urim = lights; Thummim = perfections/completions
- The mechanism is not described — the effect is: God's judgment/will is revealed through the high priest's breastplate before the LORD
- **Numbers 27:21** — Joshua to inquire of the Urim before the LORD through Eleazar
- **1 Samuel 28:6** — "And when Saul enquired of the LORD, the LORD answered him not, neither by dreams, nor by Urim, nor by prophets" — the Urim as a recognized channel of divine guidance
- **Ezra 2:63** — After the exile, the governor said there should be no eating of holy things "till there stood up a priest with Urim and with Thummim" — they were lost; awaiting restoration
- *Interpretation:* The Urim and Thummim were the divinely ordained means of accessing God's judgment/decision for the nation. They were carried on the high priest's heart — the locus of love, relationship, and personal knowledge. When direct divine guidance through the Urim ceased (exile period), Israel awaited a new priest with them. The New Covenant fulfillment: the Holy Spirit as the indwelling "Urim and Thummim" — "He will guide you into all truth" (John 16:13). Judgment/guidance is no longer mediated through an external object but through the indwelling Spirit who teaches believers all things (1 John 2:27).

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### EPHOD (Hebrew: efod)
- **Exodus 28:6** — A vestment worn over the robe; constructed of gold, blue, purple, scarlet, and fine linen
- The ephod is the defining outer garment of the high priest; later also a simpler linen ephod worn by priests and even David (2 Samuel 6:14)
- *Definition here:* The outer ceremonial garment of the high priest that holds the breastplate and shoulder pieces; the garment that carries Israel before God on the high priest's body

### URIM AND THUMMIM (Hebrew: Urim ve-Thummim)
- **Exodus 28:30** — "lights and perfections" — objects placed in the breastplate by which God's will was revealed
- *Definition here:* The divinely appointed means of direct inquiry to God through the high priest; the mechanism of God's judgment being made known to His people; lost after the exile; awaiting the coming of the ultimate High Priest who carries perfect light and perfection

## THE ACTUAL VERSES — EXODUS 28 (KJV)

**1** And take thou unto thee Aaron thy brother, and his sons with him, from among the children of Israel, that he may minister unto me in the priest's office, even Aaron, Nadab and Abihu, Eleazar and Ithamar, Aaron's sons.
**2** And thou shalt make holy garments for Aaron thy brother for glory and for beauty.
**3** And thou shalt speak unto all that are wise hearted, whom I have filled with the spirit of wisdom, that they may make Aaron's garments to consecrate him, that he may minister unto me in the priest's office.
**4** And these are the garments which they shall make; a breastplate, and an ephod, and a robe, and a broidered coat, a mitre, and a girdle: and they shall make holy garments for Aaron thy brother, and his sons, that he may minister unto me in the priest's office.
**5** And they shall take gold, and blue, and purple, and scarlet, and fine linen.
**6** And they shall make the ephod of gold, of blue, and of purple, of scarlet, and fine twined linen, with cunning work.
**7** It shall have the two shoulderpieces thereof joined at the two edges thereof; and so it shall be joined together.
**8** And the curious girdle of the ephod, which is upon it, shall be of the same, according to the work thereof; even of gold, of blue, and purple, and scarlet, and fine twined linen.
**9** And thou shalt take two onyx stones, and grave on them the names of the children of Israel:
**10** Six of their names on one stone, and the other six names of the rest on the other stone, according to their birth.
**11** With the work of an engraver in stone, like the engravings of a signet, shalt thou engrave the two stones with the names of the children of Israel: thou shalt make them to be set in ouches of gold.
**12** And thou shalt put the two stones upon the shoulders of the ephod for stones of memorial unto the children of Israel: and Aaron shall bear their names before the LORD upon his two shoulders for a memorial.
**13** And thou shalt make ouches of gold;
**14** And two chains of pure gold at the ends; of wreathen work shalt thou make them, and fasten the wreathen chains to the ouches.
**15** And thou shalt make the breastplate of judgment with cunning work; after the work of the ephod thou shalt make it; of gold, of blue, and of purple, and of scarlet, and of fine twined linen, shalt thou make it.
**16** Foursquare it shall be being doubled; a span shall be the length thereof, and a span shall be the breadth thereof.
**17** And thou shalt set in it settings of stones, even four rows of stones: the first row shall be a sardius, a topaz, and a carbuncle: this shall be the first row.
**18** And the second row shall be an emerald, a sapphire, and a diamond.
**19** And the third row a ligure, an agate, and an amethyst.
**20** And the fourth row a beryl, and an onyx, and a jasper: they shall be set in gold in their inclosings.
**21** And the stones shall be with the names of the children of Israel, twelve, according to their names, like the engravings of a signet; every one with his name shall they be according to the twelve tribes.
**22** And thou shalt make upon the breastplate chains at the ends of wreathen work of pure gold.
**23** And thou shalt make upon the breastplate two rings of gold, and shalt put the two rings on the two ends of the breastplate.
**24** And thou shalt put the two wreathen chains of gold in the two rings which are on the ends of the breastplate.
**25** And the other two ends of the two wreathen chains thou shalt fasten in the two ouches, and put them on the shoulderpieces of the ephod before it.
**26** And thou shalt make two rings of gold, and thou shalt put them upon the two ends of the breastplate in the border thereof, which is in the side of the ephod inward.
**27** And two other rings of gold thou shalt make, and shalt put them on the two sides of the ephod underneath, toward the forepart thereof, over against the other coupling thereof, above the curious girdle of the ephod.
**28** And they shall bind the breastplate by the rings thereof unto the rings of the ephod with a lace of blue, that it may be above the curious girdle of the ephod, and that the breastplate be not loosed from the ephod.
**29** And Aaron shall bear the names of the children of Israel in the breastplate of judgment upon his heart, when he goeth in unto the holy place, for a memorial before the LORD continually.
**30** And thou shalt put in the breastplate of judgment the Urim and the Thummim; and they shall be upon Aaron's heart, when he goeth in before the LORD: and Aaron shall bear the judgment of the children of Israel upon his heart before the LORD continually.
**31** And thou shalt make the robe of the ephod all of blue.
**32** And there shall be an hole in the top of it, in the midst thereof: it shall have a binding of woven work round about the hole of it, as it were the hole of an habergeon, that it be not rent.
**33** And beneath upon the hem of it thou shalt make pomegranates of blue, and of purple, and of scarlet, round about the hem thereof; and bells of gold between them round about:
**34** A golden bell and a pomegranate, a golden bell and a pomegranate, upon the hem of the robe round about.
**35** And it shall be upon Aaron to minister: and his sound shall be heard when he goeth in unto the holy place before the LORD, and when he cometh out, that he die not.
**36** And thou shalt make a plate of pure gold, and grave upon it, like the engravings of a signet, HOLINESS TO THE LORD.
**37** And thou shalt put it on a blue lace, that it may be upon the mitre; upon the forefront of the mitre it shall be.
**38** And it shall be upon Aaron's forehead, that Aaron may bear the iniquity of the holy things, which the children of Israel shall hallow in all their holy gifts; and it shall be always upon his forehead, that they may be accepted before the LORD.
**39** And thou shalt embroider the coat of fine linen, and thou shalt make the mitre of fine linen, and thou shalt make the girdle of needlework.
**40** And for Aaron's sons thou shalt make coats, and thou shalt make for them girdles, and bonnets shalt thou make for them, for glory and for beauty.
**41** And thou shalt put them upon Aaron thy brother, and his sons with him; and shalt anoint them, and consecrate them, and sanctify them, that they may minister unto me in the priest's office.
**42** And thou shalt make them linen breeches to cover their nakedness; from the loins even unto the thighs they shall reach:
**43** And they shall be upon Aaron, and upon his sons, when they come in unto the tabernacle of the congregation, or when they come near unto the altar to minister in the holy place; that they bear not iniquity, and die: it shall be a statute for ever unto him and his seed after him.
