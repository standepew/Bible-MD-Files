## CHAPTER SUMMARY

God commands: (1) The Altar of Incense — shittim wood overlaid with gold, positioned "before the veil" in the Holy Place; Aaron burns sweet incense on it morning and evening, continually; no strange incense, no other offering on it; once a year the high priest makes atonement on its horns with sin-offering blood. (2) The Half-Shekel Ransom — when Israel is numbered, every man from twenty years old gives a half-shekel as a ransom for his soul; the rich give no more and the poor give no less; the money funds the tabernacle service and is "a memorial unto the children of Israel before the LORD." (3) The Laver of Brass — between the tabernacle and the altar; Aaron and his sons wash their hands and feet before entering or ministering or they die; a statute forever. (4) The Holy Anointing Oil — composed of myrrh, cinnamon, calamus, cassia, and olive oil; used to anoint the tabernacle, all its furnishings, and Aaron and his sons; not for use on human flesh outside this purpose; whoever makes its duplicate is cut off. (5) The Holy Incense — stacte, onycha, galbanum, and frankincense; beaten small and placed before the testimony; whoever makes a duplicate to smell it is cut off.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### The Altar of Incense — Prayer in the Presence of God

**Exodus 30:7-8** — "Aaron shall burn thereon sweet incense every morning... and when Aaron lighteth the lamps at even, he shall burn incense upon it, a perpetual incense before the LORD throughout your generations"

- **Psalm 141:2** — "Let my prayer be set forth before thee as incense; and the lifting up of my hands as the evening sacrifice"
- **Luke 1:9-10** — Zacharias burns incense in the temple; "the whole multitude of the people were praying without at the time of incense" — prayer and incense are inseparable in the New Testament temple practice
- **Revelation 5:8** — "the four beasts and four and twenty elders fell down before the Lamb, having every one of them harps, and golden vials full of odours, which are the odours of the prayers of saints"
- **Revelation 8:3-4** — "And another angel came and stood at the altar, having a golden censer; and there was given unto him much incense, that he should offer it with the prayers of all saints upon the golden altar which was before the throne. And the smoke of the incense, which came with the prayers of the saints, ascended up before God"
- *Interpretation:* The altar of incense is the type of prayer. It stands before the veil — between the priesthood and the Most Holy Place — ascending toward God continuously, morning and evening. Revelation 8:3-4 is the Spirit's definitive interpretation: the incense IS the prayers of the saints. The golden altar before God's throne in Revelation is the heavenly reality of which the earthly golden altar was the copy (Hebrews 8:5). When believers pray, their prayers ascend before God as incense — this is not poetic metaphor but literal heavenly reality, revealed in Revelation 8.

### The Half-Shekel Ransom — Every Soul Is Equal Before God

**Exodus 30:12, 15** — "then shall they give every man a ransom for his soul unto the LORD... The rich shall not give more, and the poor shall not give less than half a shekel"

- Hebrew: kofer nefesh = ransom for the soul; kofer = a covering, a price of redemption
- Half a shekel = the same for every Israelite; wealth confers no advantage in covenant standing
- **Matthew 17:24-27** — The temple tax (the half-shekel equivalent); Jesus pays it for Himself and Peter from the fish's mouth — "lest we should offend them"
- **1 Peter 1:18-19** — "Forasmuch as ye know that ye were not redeemed with corruptible things, as silver and gold... But with the precious blood of Christ" — the half-shekel is silver; the true ransom is blood
- **Psalm 49:7-8** — "None of them can by any means redeem his brother, nor give to God a ransom for him: (For the redemption of their soul is precious, and it ceaseth for ever)"
- *Interpretation:* The half-shekel is simultaneously (1) a statement of equality before God — no soul is worth more than another in covenant standing — and (2) a type of the ransom price of the soul. The silver ransom pointed forward to the true price of a soul that silver cannot pay (Psalm 49:7-8; 1 Peter 1:18-19). Jesus paid the temple tax (Matthew 17:24-27) — He, the one who owns all things and owes nothing, paid the ransom for Himself and Peter in a miracle, signaling He is the true Ransom beyond silver.

### The Laver — Washing for Ministry

**Exodus 30:19-21** — "For Aaron and his sons shall wash their hands and their feet thereat: When they go into the tabernacle of the congregation, they shall wash with water, that they die not"

- The laver stood between the burnt offering altar (justification — one-time atonement for sin) and the tabernacle entrance (ongoing ministry)
- **John 13:10** — Jesus to Peter: "He that is washed needeth not save to wash his feet, but is clean every whit: and ye are clean, but not all" — the full bath = regeneration; the feet washing = daily cleansing
- **Ephesians 5:26** — "That he might sanctify and cleanse it with the washing of water by the word"
- **1 John 1:9** — "If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us our sins, and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness" — the ongoing washing
- *Interpretation:* The laver represents ongoing sanctification — the daily cleansing that follows initial justification (the altar). A priest who had approached the altar was already covered by blood atonement, but before he could minister in the tabernacle, he had to wash his hands and feet or die. This is John 13:10: the initial washing (salvation/regeneration) is complete; but the daily contamination of walking in the world requires regular cleansing. 1 John 1:9 is the New Covenant laver — the ongoing confession and cleansing of the believer who is already saved but continually needs daily purification.

### The Holy Anointing Oil — The Seal of the Spirit

**Exodus 30:22-33** — Pure myrrh, sweet cinnamon, sweet calamus, cassia, and olive oil; used to anoint the tabernacle and all its furnishings and Aaron and his sons

- Not to be poured on man's flesh for common use; not to be replicated; whoever uses it improperly is cut off
- **1 Samuel 16:13** — "Samuel took the horn of oil, and anointed him in the midst of his brethren: and the Spirit of the LORD came upon David from that day forward" — anointing = the coming of the Spirit
- **Isaiah 61:1** — "The Spirit of the Lord GOD is upon me; because the LORD hath anointed me" — the Messiah identified by the anointing
- **Acts 10:38** — "God anointed Jesus of Nazareth with the Holy Ghost and with power" — Christ (Messiah = Anointed One) is the ultimate recipient of the anointing
- **2 Corinthians 1:21-22** — "Now he which... hath anointed us, is God; Who hath also sealed us, and given the earnest of the Spirit in our hearts"
- **1 John 2:27** — "But the anointing which ye have received of him abideth in you, and ye need not that any man teach you"
- *Interpretation:* The holy anointing oil is the type of the Holy Spirit. The oil consecrates everything it touches — the tabernacle, the furnishings, the priests — to God's exclusive service. The prohibition on making a duplicate and pouring it on common flesh (v.32-33) is the theological statement: the Spirit is not common; He is holy; He cannot be counterfeited or cheaply replicated; He sets apart exclusively for God's use everything He touches. Christ is the Anointed One (Christos/Messiah); every believer receives this anointing (2 Corinthians 1:21; 1 John 2:27).

### The Holy Incense — The Fragrance That Cannot Be Replicated

**Exodus 30:34-38** — Stacte, onycha, galbanum, and pure frankincense; beaten small; placed before the testimony; not to be replicated for personal use; whoever makes it to smell is cut off

- Four spices in equal measure = the completeness of the holy fragrance
- The incense must be beaten small (v.36) — the process of crushing/humility produces the fragrance
- **Song of Solomon 1:3** — "thine ointments have a pleasing fragrance... therefore do the virgins love thee" — the fragrance of the beloved
- **2 Corinthians 2:14-15** — "Now thanks be unto God, which always causeth us to triumph in Christ, and maketh manifest the savour of his knowledge by us in every place. For we are unto God a sweet savour of Christ, in them that are saved, and in them that perish"
- *Interpretation:* The holy incense is the fragrance of Christ — the savour that God delights in, that cannot be artificially produced, and that arises only from the genuine presence of the Spirit. 2 Corinthians 2:14-15 identifies believers as the savour of Christ — a sweet fragrance to God and a recognized smell in the world, which is either the aroma of life or the aroma of death depending on the recipient's response. The incense must be beaten small — only through suffering and humility does the true fragrance of Christ arise.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### RANSOM (Hebrew: kofer)
- **Exodus 30:12** — "a ransom for his soul unto the LORD" — kofer = covering, price of redemption, propitiation
- From kaphar = to cover, to atone; the same root as kapporeth (mercy seat)
- **Job 33:24** — "Deliver him from going down to the pit: I have found a ransom (kofer)"
- **Matthew 20:28** — "the Son of man came... to give his life a ransom (lutron) for many" — the Greek word picks up the Hebrew kofer concept
- *Definition here:* The price paid to cover a life that is forfeit; the ransom price acknowledges that the soul deserves death; payment of the ransom releases the soul from the death claim

### ANOINTING OIL (Hebrew: shemen mishchah)
- **Exodus 30:25** — "an oil of holy ointment, an ointment compound after the art of the apothecary: it shall be an holy anointing oil"
- mishchah = anointing, spreading/smearing; from mashach = to anoint; root of Messiah (mashiach = anointed one)
- *Definition here:* The sacred compound oil that consecrates to God's exclusive service everything it touches; the type of the Holy Spirit's anointing which sets apart believers and tabernacles for God's holy use; the compound composition (five spices) suggests the fullness of the Spirit's attributes*

**14** Every one that passeth among them that are numbered, from twenty years old and above, shall give an offering unto the LORD.
**15** The rich shall not give more, and the poor shall not give less than half a shekel, when they give an offering unto the LORD, to make an atonement for your souls.

**17** And the LORD spake unto Moses, saying,
**18** Thou shalt also make a laver of brass, and his foot also of brass, to wash withal: and thou shalt put it between the tabernacle of the congregation and the altar, and thou shalt put water therein.
**19** For Aaron and his sons shall wash their hands and their feet thereat:
**20** When they go into the tabernacle of the congregation, they shall wash with water, that they die not; or when they come near to the altar to minister, to burn offering made by fire unto the LORD:
**21** So they shall wash their hands and their feet, that they die not: and it shall be a statute for ever to them, even to him and to his seed throughout their generations.
**22** Moreover the LORD spake unto Moses, saying,
**23** Take thou also unto thee principal spices, of pure myrrh five hundred shekels, and of sweet cinnamon half so much, even two hundred and fifty shekels, and of sweet calamus two hundred and fifty shekels,
**24** And of cassia five hundred shekels, after the shekel of the sanctuary, and of oil olive an hin:
**25** And thou shalt make it an oil of holy ointment, an ointment compound after the art of the apothecary: it shall be an holy anointing oil.
**26** And thou shalt anoint the tabernacle of the congregation therewith, and the ark of the testimony,
**27** And the table and all his vessels, and the candlestick and his vessels, and the altar of incense,
**28** And the altar of burnt offering with all his vessels, and the laver and his foot.
**29** And thou shalt sanctify them, that they may be most holy: whatsoever toucheth them shall be holy.
**30** And thou shalt anoint Aaron and his sons, and consecrate them, that they may minister unto me in the priest's office.
**31** And thou shalt speak unto the children of Israel, saying, This shall be an holy anointing oil unto me throughout your generations.
**32** Upon man's flesh shall it not be poured, neither shall ye make any other like it, after the composition of it: it is holy, and it shall be holy unto you.
**33** Whosoever compoundeth any like it, or whosoever putteth any of it upon a stranger, shall even be cut off from his people.

**37** And as for the perfume which thou shalt make, ye shall not make to yourselves according to the composition thereof: it shall be unto thee holy for the LORD.
**38** Whosoever shall make like unto that, to smell thereto, shall even be cut off from his people.

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The Five Furnishings of Exodus 25-30 — The Gospel Path Through the Tabernacle

| Furnishing | Location | New Testament Fulfillment |
|---|---|---|
| Altar of Burnt Offering (ch.27) | Outer court — first encountered | The Cross — justification through Christ's blood |
| Laver of Brass (ch.30) | Outer court — before entering | Regeneration and ongoing sanctification (John 13:10; 1 John 1:9) |
| Table of Showbread (ch.25) | Holy Place — north | Christ the Bread of Life — John 6:35 |
| Golden Menorah (ch.25) | Holy Place — south | Christ the Light of the World — John 8:12 |
| Altar of Incense (ch.30) | Holy Place — before the veil | The prayers of the saints before God — Revelation 8:3-4 |
| Ark and Mercy Seat (ch.25) | Most Holy Place | Christ the propitiation — Romans 3:25; Hebrews 9:24 |

The path through the Tabernacle is the gospel: Cross → Cleansing → Bread/Light/Prayer → Access to God's Presence.

### The Incense Before the Testimony — Prayer As the Closest Approach to God Before the Cross

- The incense altar was the closest any daily ministry came to the Most Holy Place — right before the veil
- This was the station of Zacharias when the angel appeared (Luke 1:9-11) — the incense altar is where God spoke
- *Thread:* Under the Old Covenant, the closest approach to God in daily ministry was the incense altar — prayer. The Most Holy Place was entered once a year. Under the New Covenant, every believer has immediate access through Christ's blood (Hebrews 10:19-22), but prayer (incense) remains the primary expression of that access.
