## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Bezaleel himself makes the Ark of shittim wood — 2.5 by 1.5 by 1.5 cubits — overlaid inside and out with pure gold, a crown of gold around it, four gold rings for gold-overlaid staves. The Mercy Seat of pure gold, same length and width as the Ark. Two cherubim of beaten gold, made out of the mercy seat itself — one piece — at either end, wings spread upward covering the mercy seat, faces toward each other and toward the mercy seat. The Table of Showbread: shittim wood overlaid with gold, crown, border, rings, staves, all vessels of pure gold. The Menorah: pure gold beaten work, shaft and six branches, seven almond-shaped bowls with knops and flowers on each, all of one piece — one talent of pure gold — with seven lamps, tongs, and snuff dishes of gold. The Altar of Incense: shittim wood overlaid with pure gold, foursquare, two cubits high, crown, two gold rings, gold-overlaid staves. The holy anointing oil and pure incense compounded according to the apothecary's art.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### The Mercy Seat — Kapporeth / Hilasterion

**Exodus 37:6** — "And he made the mercy seat of pure gold"

- Hebrew: kapporeth — from kaphar = to cover, atone, appease; the lid of the Ark; the place where the blood was applied on the Day of Atonement (Leviticus 16:14-15)
- **Romans 3:25** — Greek: hilasterion — "Whom God hath set forth to be a propitiation (hilasterion) through faith in his blood" — Paul uses the exact word for "mercy seat" (LXX: hilasterion = kapporeth)
- **Hebrews 9:5** — "And over it the cherubims of glory shadowing the mercyseat" — the writer of Hebrews explicitly identifies the mercy seat as the type
- **1 John 2:2** — "And he is the propitiation (hilasmos) for our sins" — John uses the cognate word
- *Interpretation:* Christ IS the mercy seat. The gold lid where the high priest sprinkled blood once a year in the Most Holy Place is the type; Christ is the antitype. Romans 3:25 does not say Christ was "like" the mercy seat — it uses the identical Greek term. The blood that was applied to the kapporeth once per year on the Day of Atonement is now applied to the eternal mercy seat (Christ) once for all. The Ark contained the broken Law; the mercy seat covered it. Christ the mercy seat covers the Law the sinner has broken.

---

### The Cherubim Beaten from One Piece with the Mercy Seat

**Exodus 37:7-9** — "out of the mercy seat made he the cherubims on the two ends thereof... out of one piece made he them"

- The cherubim do not sit on the mercy seat — they emerge from it; one mass of gold shaped into three forms
- Their faces are "toward the mercy seat" — their gaze is fixed on the place of atonement
- **Genesis 3:24** — Cherubim guard the way to the tree of life after the Fall; the same creatures now guard the mercy seat
- **Hebrews 1:14** — "Are they not all ministering spirits, sent forth to minister for them who shall be heirs of salvation?" — angelic service is now oriented toward the redeemed, not guarding them out
- *Interpretation:* The cherubim who once barred the way back into Eden now bow over the mercy seat, faces fixed on the place where blood is applied. What changed? Atonement. When the blood is on the mercy seat, the cherubim no longer guard — they witness. The direction of angelic gaze is toward the atonement. 1 Peter 1:12: "which things the angels desire to look into."*

---

### The Menorah — Almond Design and the Resurrection

**Exodus 37:17-24** — The menorah is modeled on the almond tree — almond-shaped bowls, knops, flowers; beaten from one talent of pure gold

- The almond tree (Hebrew: shaqed) is the first tree to blossom after winter — it "wakens" before all others; shaqed shares its root with shaqad = to watch, to be alert, to wake
- **Numbers 17:8** — Aaron's rod: "the rod of Aaron for the house of Levi was budded, and brought forth buds, and bloomed blossoms, and yielded almonds" — the dead rod produces life; resurrection symbol
- **Jeremiah 1:11-12** — God shows Jeremiah an almond branch (shaqed); God says "I will hasten (shaqad) my word to perform it" — the almond = God watching/hastening to fulfill
- **John 8:12** — "I am the light of the world: he that followeth me shall not walk in darkness, but shall have the light of life" — Jesus declares Himself the antitype of the menorah
- **Revelation 1:12-13, 20** — The seven golden lampstands are the seven churches; Christ walks among them
- *Interpretation:* The menorah is the almond tree in gold — the tree of resurrection and divine vigilance. Its light fills the Holy Place (the realm of the Spirit's work). Jesus as the Light of the World (John 8:12) is the fulfillment of the menorah. The churches as lampstands (Revelation 1:20) are the continuation: the redeemed community bearing the light of Christ in the world.*

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### MERCY SEAT (Hebrew: kapporeth)
- **Exodus 37:6** — kapporeth — from kaphar = to cover, atone; the golden lid of the Ark; the place of blood application on the Day of Atonement
- **Romans 3:25** — hilasterion (Greek LXX equivalent) — translated "propitiation" in KJV; Christ is this
- *Definition here:* The place where God meets man over covered sin; the precise location in the Tabernacle where justice and mercy intersect; the type of Christ who is both the covering and the meeting place*

### BEATEN WORK (Hebrew: miqshah)
- **Exodus 37:7, 17, 22** — miqshah — from naqash = to beat out, hammer; the menorah and cherubim are not cast but hammered into shape from one mass
- *Definition here:* Work produced by hammer blows rather than casting in a mold; the cherubim and menorah are shaped by force applied to gold — a detail that suggests the shaping of divine things through the pressure of the refining process (Isaiah 48:10; 1 Peter 1:7)*

**1** And Bezaleel made the ark of shittim wood: two cubits and a half was the length of it, and a cubit and a half the breadth of it, and a cubit and a half the height of it:
**2** And he overlaid it with pure gold within and without, and made a crown of gold to it round about.
**3** And he cast for it four rings of gold, to be set by the four corners of it; even two rings upon the one side of it, and two rings upon the other side of it.
**4** And he made staves of shittim wood, and overlaid them with gold.
**5** And he put the staves into the rings by the sides of the ark, to bear the ark.
**6** And he made the mercy seat of pure gold: two cubits and a half was the length thereof, and one cubit and a half the breadth thereof.
**7** And he made two cherubims of gold, beaten out of one piece made he them, on the two ends of the mercy seat;
**8** One cherub on the end on this side, and another cherub on the other end on that side: out of the mercy seat made he the cherubims on the two ends thereof.
**9** And the cherubims spread out their wings on high, and covered with their wings over the mercy seat, with their faces one to another; even to the mercy seatward were the faces of the cherubims.
**10** And he made the table of shittim wood: two cubits was the length thereof, and a cubit the breadth thereof, and a cubit and a half the height thereof:
**11** And he overlaid it with pure gold, and made thereunto a crown of gold round about.
**12** Also he made thereunto a border of an handbreadth round about; and made a crown of gold for the border thereof round about.
**13** And he cast for it four rings of gold, and put the rings upon the four corners that were in the four feet thereof.
**14** Over against the border were the rings, the places for the staves to bear the table.
**15** And he made the staves of shittim wood, and overlaid them with gold, to bear the table.
**16** And he made the vessels which were upon the table, his dishes, and his spoons, and his bowls, and his covers to cover withal, of pure gold.
**17** And he made the candlestick of pure gold: of beaten work made he the candlestick; his shaft, and his branch, his bowls, his knops, and his flowers, were of the same:
**18** And six branches going out of the sides thereof; three branches of the candlestick out of the one side thereof, and three branches of the candlestick out of the other side thereof:

**20** And in the candlestick were four bowls made like almonds, his knops, and his flowers:
**21** And a knop under two branches of the same, and a knop under two branches of the same, and a knop under two branches of the same, according to the six branches going out of it.
**22** Their knops and their branches were of the same: all of it was one beaten work of pure gold.
**23** And he made his seven lamps, and his snuffers, and his snuffdishes, of pure gold.
**24** Of a talent of pure gold made he it, and all the vessels thereof.
**25** And he made the incense altar of shittim wood: the length of it was a cubit, and the breadth of it a cubit; it was foursquare; and two cubits was the height of it; the horns thereof were of the same.
**26** And he overlaid it with pure gold, both the top of it, and the sides thereof round about, and the horns of it: also he made unto it a crown of gold round about.
**27** And he made two rings of gold for it under the crown thereof, by the two corners of it, upon the two sides thereof, to be places for the staves to bear it withal.
**28** And he made the staves of shittim wood, and overlaid them with gold.
**29** And he made the holy anointing oil, and the pure incense of sweet spices, according to the work of the apothecary.

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### Gold Within and Without — Christ's Inner and Outer Righteousness

**Exodus 37:2** — "he overlaid it with pure gold within and without"

- The Ark is gold inside and out — no wood is visible; it is entirely covered
- **Matthew 23:27** — Jesus contrasts the Pharisees: "whited sepulchres, which indeed appear beautiful outward, but are within full of dead men's bones" — external only, not internal
- *Type:* The Ark overlaid within and without is the type of Christ whose righteousness is total — not a veneer over corruption but gold through and through. The New Covenant requires the same: not external conformity (gold outside) but a new heart (gold inside). 2 Corinthians 5:17 — "if any man be in Christ, he is a new creature."*
