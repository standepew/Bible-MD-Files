## CHAPTER SUMMARY

The priestly garments are made exactly as commanded: the ephod of gold wire woven into blue, purple, scarlet, and fine linen, with onyx stones on the shoulders engraved with the twelve tribes' names. The breastplate of the same materials — foursquare, doubled — set with four rows of three stones, each engraved with a tribe's name, bound to the ephod with blue lace. The all-blue robe with pomegranates and alternating pure gold bells on the hem. Coats of fine linen, the linen mitre, linen breeches, and the needlework girdle. The golden plate: pure gold engraved "HOLINESS TO THE LORD," tied with a blue lace to the front of the mitre. All the work of the Tabernacle is finished. Every piece is brought to Moses. He inspects it all — "they had done it as the LORD had commanded, even so had they done it" — and Moses blessed them.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "HOLINESS TO THE LORD" — On the Forehead of the High Priest

**Exodus 39:30** — "And they made the plate of the holy crown of pure gold, and wrote upon it a writing, like to the engravings of a signet, HOLINESS TO THE LORD"

- The golden plate on the high priest's forehead bore the inscription: Qodesh la-YHWH — "Set apart to YHWH"
- **Exodus 28:36-38** — The function: "And it shall be upon Aaron's forehead, that Aaron may bear the iniquity of the holy things, which the children of Israel shall hallow... that they may be accepted before the LORD"
- **Zechariah 14:20-21** — "In that day shall there be upon the bells of the horses, HOLINESS UNTO THE LORD; and the pots in the LORD's house shall be like the bowls before the altar" — in the Millennium, the inscription that was on the high priest alone will be on everything
- **Revelation 22:4** — "And they shall see his face; and his name shall be in their foreheads" — the redeemed in the New Jerusalem bear God's name on their foreheads; the "HOLINESS TO THE LORD" of the high priest becomes the inheritance of every believer
- *Interpretation:* The golden plate's inscription traces a trajectory across Scripture: under Moses, it belongs to the high priest alone — he bears the holiness-inscription on behalf of all Israel. In the Millennium (Zechariah 14:20), it spreads to the ordinary (horse bells, cooking pots). In eternity (Revelation 22:4), every redeemed person bears the name on their forehead. What was the high priest's exclusive inscription becomes the universal mark of the redeemed. Holiness ceases to be the possession of the few and becomes the inheritance of the whole.*

### Names on the Shoulders and Heart — Strength and Love

**Exodus 39:6-7, 14-17** — The twelve tribe names are engraved on onyx stones on the shoulders of the ephod and on twelve gems on the breastplate over the heart

- Shoulders = the place of bearing weight; strength; carrying
- Heart = the seat of affection; love; relationship
- **Hebrews 7:25** — "He ever liveth to make intercession for them" — Christ carries His people on shoulders (strength to bear them) and heart (love to intercede for them)
- **Isaiah 9:6** — "and the government shall be upon his shoulder" — Messianic bearing; Christ carries what Israel could not carry
- **Isaiah 53:4** — "Surely he hath borne our griefs, and carried our sorrows" — the shoulder-bearing of the Servant
- *Interpretation:* The high priest carried every Israelite into the presence of God twice: on his shoulders (never dropping them — sustained by strength) and on his heart (always bearing them before God in love). This is the dual nature of Christ's intercession: He is powerful enough to sustain (Isaiah 9:6) and loving enough to plead (Romans 8:34). No believer falls because Christ lacks strength; no believer is forgotten because Christ lacks love.*

---

### The Bells on the Hem — The Living Priest

**Exodus 39:25-26** — "And they made bells of pure gold, and put the bells between the pomegranates upon the hem of the robe, round about between the pomegranates; A bell and a pomegranate, a bell and a pomegranate"

- **Exodus 28:35** — "And it shall be upon Aaron to minister: and his sound shall be heard when he goeth in before the LORD, and when he cometh out, that he die not"
- The bells rang while the high priest moved — silence meant death; sound meant life
- **Hebrews 7:25** — "he ever liveth to make intercession for them" — the bells never go silent; Christ is the ever-living High Priest
- *Interpretation:* Under the old covenant, Israel waited at a distance while the high priest went behind the veil. They could not see him. They listened for the bells. Silence = dead high priest; no intercession; no atonement for this year. The bells' ringing was the assurance that their mediator lived. Hebrews 7:25 is the New Covenant proclamation that the bells never stop: "He ever liveth." Christ does not come out exhausted from the Holy Place — He lives eternally to intercede. The high priest's bell-ringing was the type; Christ's eternal life is the unending fulfillment.*

---

### "As the LORD Commanded Moses" — The Refrain of Obedience

**Exodus 39** — The phrase "as the LORD commanded Moses" appears seven times in this chapter alone (vv. 1, 5, 7, 21, 26, 29, 31) and dozens of times in chapters 39-40

- Every component is tied to the divine command; each piece of work is an act of obedience
- **John 14:15** — "If ye love me, keep my commandments"
- **John 15:10** — "If ye keep my commandments, ye shall abide in my love; even as I have kept my Father's commandments, and abide in his love"
- **1 Samuel 15:22** — "to obey is better than sacrifice"
- *Interpretation:* The sevenfold repetition of "as the LORD commanded Moses" in chapter 39 is the Spirit's emphasis on the completeness of Israel's obedience in the construction. Every measurement obeyed. Every material obeyed. Every method obeyed. The obedience is total. This stands in sharp contrast to the golden calf — where Israel departed from the pattern in 40 days. The Tabernacle chapters end with the full restoration of obedience. The refrain "as the LORD commanded" is the sound of a repentant community doing exactly what God said.*

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### EPHOD (Hebrew: ephod)
- **Exodus 39:2-7** — a sacred vestment worn over the high priest's robe; the breastplate was attached to it; the onyx shoulder stones bore the tribes' names
- *Definition here:* The primary identifying garment of the high priest; the structure that unites the breastplate (the heart) with the shoulder stones (strength); the type of Christ who bears His people both in love and in power*

### POMEGRANATE (Hebrew: rimmon)
- **Exodus 39:24-26** — alternating with gold bells on the robe's hem; blue, purple, scarlet, fine linen
- The pomegranate = fruitfulness; its many seeds = abundance and multiplied life
- *Definition here:* The symbol of covenant fruitfulness alternating with the bells of priestly life; together they declare: the living priest produces abundant fruit in those he intercedes for (John 15:5 — "he that abideth in me... beareth much fruit")*

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### Moses Blessed Them — The Benediction at Completion

**Exodus 39:43** — "And Moses did look upon all the work, and, behold, they had done it as the LORD had commanded, even so had they done it: and Moses blessed them"

- Inspection → approval → blessing; the pattern of God's own work at creation: "And God saw that it was good"
- **Genesis 1:31** — "And God saw every thing that he had made, and, behold, it was very good"
- **Matthew 25:21** — "Well done, thou good and faithful servant... enter thou into the joy of thy lord" — the final inspection and blessing
- **Hebrews 12:2** — "who for the joy that was set before him endured the cross" — Christ's obedience completed, the Father's approval given
- *The Tabernacle's completion and Moses' blessing is the type of every completed work of obedience receiving divine approval. The workers labored "as the LORD commanded" — and the blessing follows exact obedience. This is the pattern from creation to consummation: God inspects, God approves, God blesses.*
