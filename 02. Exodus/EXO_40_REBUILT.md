## CHAPTER SUMMARY

God commands Moses: on the first day of the first month of the second year, erect the Tabernacle. Ark in first, veil covering it; then table with bread set in order; menorah lit; gold altar set and incense burned; entrance hanging placed; altar of burnt offering before the entrance; laver between tent and altar with water; court erected; gate hung. Anoint everything — "and it shall be holy." Bring Aaron and his sons, wash them, dress them, anoint them — an everlasting priesthood. Moses does everything exactly according to God's command. The Tabernacle is raised on the first day of the first month of the second year — exactly one year and two weeks after the Exodus. Moses places every piece in its place. The cloud descends. The glory of the LORD fills the Tabernacle. Moses is not able to enter — because the cloud abides on it and the glory fills it. When the cloud lifts, Israel moves. When the cloud remains, Israel stays. The cloud of the LORD is upon the Tabernacle by day; fire on it by night — in the sight of all the house of Israel, throughout all their journeys.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "The Glory of the LORD Filled the Tabernacle" — The Culmination of All Redemptive History

**Exodus 40:34-35** — "Then a cloud covered the tent of the congregation, and the glory of the LORD filled the tabernacle. And Moses was not able to enter into the tent of the congregation, because the cloud abode thereon, and the glory of the LORD filled the tabernacle"

- From Exodus 3 (burning bush — "the place whereon thou standest is holy ground") to Exodus 40: God moves from bush to mountain to Tabernacle — progressive drawing near
- **1 Kings 8:10-11** — Solomon's Temple dedication: "it came to pass, when the priests were come out of the holy place, that the cloud filled the house of the LORD... for the glory of the LORD had filled the house of the LORD" — the same glory, the same cloud, the same inability to stand
- **Ezekiel 10:18-19** — The glory departs from Solomon's Temple because of Israel's sin
- **Ezekiel 43:4-5** — The glory returns to the Millennial Temple from the east
- **John 1:14** — "And the Word was made flesh, and dwelt (skenoo = tabernacled) among us, and we beheld his glory" — the Incarnation IS the glory filling the Tabernacle; the Greek skenoo = to pitch a tent/tabernacle
- **Revelation 21:3** — "Behold, the tabernacle of God is with men, and he will dwell with them" — the final and permanent fulfillment of everything Exodus 40 began
- *Interpretation:* Exodus 40:34 is not the end of a story — it is the arrival at the purpose of everything since Genesis 3. God's stated aim: "that I may dwell among them" (Exodus 25:8). Exodus 40 is the first fulfillment; John 1:14 is the supreme fulfillment (God dwelling in human flesh); Revelation 21:3 is the eternal fulfillment (God dwelling with all His redeemed permanently). Every Tabernacle, every Temple, every church gathering is a station on this one trajectory: God coming to dwell with His people.*

### "Moses Was Not Able to Enter" — Overcome by Glory

**Exodus 40:35** — "And Moses was not able to enter into the tent of the congregation, because the cloud abode thereon, and the glory of the LORD filled the tabernacle"

- Moses — the greatest man under the old covenant — cannot enter when God's glory fully occupies the Tabernacle
- **1 Kings 8:11** — The priests cannot stand to minister when Solomon's Temple is filled with glory
- **Revelation 15:8** — "And the temple was filled with smoke from the glory of God, and from his power; and no man was able to enter into the temple" — at the end of the age, the same principle
- **Isaiah 6:5** — "Woe is me! for I am undone" — the effect of divine glory on fallen humanity
- **Hebrews 10:19-22** — "Having therefore, brethren, boldness to enter into the holiest by the blood of Jesus... Let us draw near with a true heart in full assurance of faith" — what was impossible for Moses (entering where God's glory fills) is now the standing invitation to every believer through Christ's blood
- *Interpretation:* Moses' inability to enter is not failure — it is the revelation of the old covenant's limit. The Tabernacle filled with glory is inaccessible to the mediator of the old covenant. Hebrews 10:19 is the answer: through the blood of Jesus, the way into the holiest is now open. What stopped Moses — the fullness of God's presence — is now the invitation of the New Covenant. "Let us draw near."*

### "So Moses Finished the Work" — A Complete Obedience

**Exodus 40:33** — "So Moses finished the work"

- Seven words in English; the entire project completed in a single clause
- **Genesis 2:1-2** — "Thus the heavens and the earth were finished, and all the host of them. And on the seventh day God ended his work which he had made"
- **John 17:4** — Jesus praying: "I have glorified thee on the earth: I have finished the work which thou gavest me to do"
- **John 19:30** — "It is finished" — tetelestai; the completed work of redemption
- *Interpretation:* "Moses finished the work" deliberately echoes "God finished his work" (Genesis 2:2) and anticipates "It is finished" (John 19:30). Each is the completion of a stage in the one redemptive purpose: creation finished; the Tabernacle finished; redemption finished. The word "finished" is the Spirit's marker for completed acts of God accomplished through human obedience. Moses finishes what God designed; Christ finishes what Moses could only type.*

### The Cloud by Day, Fire by Night — God Governing All Movement

**Exodus 40:36-38** — "And when the cloud was taken up from over the tabernacle, the children of Israel went onward in all their journeys: But if the cloud were not taken up, then they journeyed not till the day that it was taken up. For the cloud of the LORD was upon the tabernacle by day, and fire was on it by night, in the sight of all the house of Israel, throughout all their journeys"

- **Numbers 9:15-23** — The full governing principle: cloud up = move; cloud down = stay; whether two days or a month or a year
- **1 Corinthians 10:1-2** — "Moreover, brethren, I would not that ye should be ignorant, how that all our fathers were under the cloud... And were all baptized unto Moses in the cloud and in the sea"
- **Romans 8:14** — "For as many as are led by the Spirit of God, they are the sons of God" — the New Covenant equivalent: the Spirit leads as the cloud led
- **John 16:13** — "Howbeit when he, the Spirit of truth, is come, he will guide you into all truth" — the Spirit as the New Covenant pillar of cloud and fire
- *Interpretation:* Israel's entire movement through the wilderness was governed by the visible cloud. Not their own strategy, their strength, their calendar, or their preference. Move when it moves. Stop when it stops. The New Covenant transfers the same principle inward: "as many as are led by the Spirit of God, they are the sons of God" (Romans 8:14). The cloud became internal — the indwelling Spirit. But the principle is unchanged: God governs His people's movement. The wilderness generation had the external sign; the New Covenant believer has the internal reality.*

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### GLORY (Hebrew: kavod)
- **Exodus 40:34-35** — kavod = weight, heaviness, honor, splendor; the visible manifestation of God's presence
- The same kavod that fills the Tabernacle departs from Solomon's Temple (Ezekiel 10) and returns in Christ (John 1:14 — skenoo/tabernacle) and finally in the New Jerusalem (Revelation 21:23 — "the glory of God did lighten it")
- *Definition here:* The tangible, overwhelming, space-filling presence of God; the weight of divine reality that no human being in fallen flesh can stand in unmediated contact with; what grace makes accessible through the mediation of Christ*

### SHEKINAH (Hebrew/Aramaic: shekinah — "dwelling/resting")
- Not a word found in the Hebrew text but the rabbinical term for the cloud-glory presence of God dwelling among Israel
- The root: shakan = to dwell, to tabernacle — the same root as mishkan (Tabernacle) and John 1:14 (skenoo)
- *Definition here:* The visible manifestation of God's immanent presence among His covenant people; the cloud-and-fire that both reveals and conceals; it reveals enough to lead, not so much as to consume*

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### "Throughout All Their Journeys" — The Last Words of Exodus

**Exodus 40:38** — "the cloud of the LORD was upon the tabernacle by day, and fire was on it by night, in the sight of all the house of Israel, throughout all their journeys"

- The book that began with Israel in slavery in Egypt ends with Israel in the wilderness — guided by God's presence, moving at His direction, held by His protection
- **Deuteronomy 31:8** — "And the LORD, he it is that doth go before thee; he will be with thee, he will not fail thee, neither forsake thee: fear not, neither be dismayed"
- **Matthew 28:20** — "lo, I am with you alway, even unto the end of the world" — Christ as the cloud and fire; never departing; present throughout all journeys
- *Exodus ends not with arrival but with the guarantee of guidance. Israel has not yet entered Canaan. They are still in the wilderness. But the glory is with them — and where the glory leads, they follow. This is the posture of the covenant people in every age: not yet home, but not alone. The cloud by day. The fire by night. Throughout all our journeys.*
