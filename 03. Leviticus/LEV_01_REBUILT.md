## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Leviticus opens with God speaking to Moses from inside the completed Tabernacle. The first instruction: the burnt offering (olah). Three grades — a bull from the herd, a sheep or goat from the flock, or turtledoves/young pigeons — so that the wealthy, the middling, and the poor can all approach. In each case the procedure establishes the same theological truth: the worshipper brings a male without blemish, lays his hand on the animal's head (transferring identity), kills it, and the priests apply the blood and manage the fire. The whole animal is burned — nothing is eaten, nothing is kept. It is entirely consumed as "a sweet savour unto the LORD." The stated effect: "it shall be accepted for him to make atonement for him."

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### God Speaks from the Tabernacle — The Dwelling Now Functions

**Leviticus 1:1** — "And the LORD called unto Moses, and spake unto him out of the tabernacle of the congregation"

- Exodus ended with the Tabernacle filled with glory so overwhelming that Moses could not enter (Exodus 40:35)
- Now God speaks from inside it — the dwelling is operational; God has taken up residence and summons Moses
- **Numbers 7:89** — "And when Moses was gone into the tabernacle of the congregation to speak with him, then he heard the voice of one speaking unto him from off the mercy seat" — the mercy seat is the throne; God speaks from between the cherubim
- **John 1:14** — The Word tabernacled among us — the pattern is fulfilled in the Incarnation: God dwelling among His people and speaking from within
- *Interpretation:* Leviticus 1:1 is the sequel to Exodus 40:38. The glory filled the Tabernacle; now God speaks from it. The entire sacrificial system of Leviticus originates in the voice of God from His own dwelling — not in human religious invention. This is God providing the means of approach to Himself, from within His own presence.*

---

### "He Shall Put His Hand Upon the Head" — Identification and Substitution

**Leviticus 1:4** — "And he shall put his hand upon the head of the burnt offering; and it shall be accepted for him to make atonement for him"

- Hebrew: samak = to lean, press, lay heavily — not a light touch but a leaning of weight; the transference of identity from the worshipper to the animal
- **Leviticus 16:21** — On the Day of Atonement, Aaron "shall lay both his hands upon the head of the live goat, and confess over him all the iniquities of the children of Israel" — the same gesture, full substitution made explicit
- **Isaiah 53:6** — "the LORD hath laid on him the iniquity of us all" — the divine hand presses upon the Servant what Israel's hand pressed upon the goat
- **2 Corinthians 5:21** — "For he hath made him to be sin for us, who knew no sin" — the New Covenant fulfillment of the hand-on-head substitution
- **1 Peter 2:24** — "Who his own self bare our sins in his own body on the tree"
- *Interpretation:* The laying of the hand is the hinge of the entire sacrificial system. Without it, the offering is merely an animal killed — with it, it is substitution. The worshipper's identity is transferred to the animal; the animal dies as the worshipper's representative. Every burnt offering in Leviticus is a repeated enacted declaration: "This animal dies in my place." Isaiah 53:6 and 2 Corinthians 5:21 are the fulfillment: God Himself performs the great hand-laying on His own Son.*

---

### "Without Blemish" — The Qualification for Substitution

**Leviticus 1:3** — "he shall offer a male without blemish"

- **Leviticus 22:20-22** — "Whatsoever hath a blemish, that shall ye not offer: for it shall not be acceptable for you" — a defective substitute cannot represent a sinless standard
- **1 Peter 1:18-19** — "ye were not redeemed with corruptible things, as silver and gold... But with the precious blood of Christ, as of a lamb without blemish and without spot"
- **Hebrews 9:14** — "Christ, who through the eternal Spirit offered himself without spot to God"
- **Isaiah 53:9** — "neither was any deceit in his mouth" — the fulfillment of "without blemish"
- *Interpretation:* The without-blemish requirement is not agricultural fastidiousness — it is theological precision. The substitute must be without the defect that makes the sinner guilty. An imperfect sacrifice cannot cover a perfect standard. Every rejected blemished animal was the Law saying: "This is not enough." Only one sacrifice met the standard: the Lamb of God (John 1:29), whose perfection was not ceremonial but moral and ontological.*

---

### The Three Grades — All Ranks Can Approach

**Leviticus 1:2-17** — Bull (wealthy), sheep/goat (common), turtledoves/pigeons (poor)

- **Leviticus 5:7-11** — The sliding scale extends to flour if even birds are unaffordable
- **Luke 2:24** — "And to offer a sacrifice according to that which is said in the law of the Lord, A pair of turtledoves, or two young pigeons" — Mary and Joseph at the Temple; they brought the birds, not a bull — they were poor. God had already built provision for them into Leviticus 1.
- **James 2:5** — "Hath not God chosen the poor of this world rich in faith"
- *Interpretation:* The graduated offering system declares that no one is excluded from access to God by poverty. The same atonement — "it shall be accepted for him" — applies to the bull-bringer and the pigeon-bringer. God built economic equity into the sacrificial system from the beginning. Luke 2:24 reveals what family Jesus was born into — a pigeon family, not a bull family. The incarnate Son of God entered the world at the poverty tier of the offering system He Himself designed.*

---

### "A Sweet Savour" — God's Acceptance of the Sacrifice

**Leviticus 1:9, 13, 17** — "an offering made by fire, of a sweet savour unto the LORD" (three times in this chapter)

- Hebrew: reyach nichoach = a soothing/restful aroma; the root nichoach = rest, settling down — God's wrath is appeased, He is at rest
- **Genesis 8:21** — After Noah's burnt offering: "the LORD smelled a sweet savour; and the LORD said in his heart, I will not again curse the ground" — same phrase; same effect
- **Ephesians 5:2** — "Christ also hath loved us, and hath given himself for us an offering and a sacrifice to God for a sweetsmelling savour" — Paul applies the exact Levitical phrase to Christ's sacrifice
- **2 Corinthians 2:15** — "For we are unto God a sweet savour of Christ" — believers in union with Christ carry the fragrance of His accepted sacrifice
- *Interpretation:* The "sweet savour" is the declaration of God's acceptance. When the sacrifice meets the standard, God is "at rest" — His holy wrath against sin is satisfied by the substitute. Ephesians 5:2 makes the application explicit: Christ's sacrifice is the supreme sweet savour, the final and complete satisfaction of God's just demands. In Christ, God smells the fragrance of His Son's perfect offering — and is at rest.*

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### BURNT OFFERING (Hebrew: olah)
- From alah = to go up; the offering that "goes up" entirely to God — nothing eaten, nothing returned; total ascent
- Contrast with the peace offering (partially eaten) and sin offering (partially eaten by priests)
- *Definition here:* Total consecration offering; the worshipper's complete surrender represented in the animal that is entirely consumed before God; the type of Christ's total self-giving (Hebrews 10:7 — "Lo, I come to do thy will, O God")*

### ATONEMENT (Hebrew: kaphar)
- **Leviticus 1:4** — "it shall be accepted for him to make atonement (kaphar) for him"
- Kaphar = to cover, to wipe, to appease; the same root as kapporeth (mercy seat)
- *Definition here:* The covering of sin through an accepted substitute; the mechanism by which a guilty person is made acceptable before God; not the elimination of sin (that requires the blood of Christ) but its covering until the perfect sacrifice arrives (Romans 3:25 — "sins that are past")*

### HAND-LAYING (Hebrew: samak)
- **Leviticus 1:4** — samak = to press, lean heavily upon; the transfer of burden from the person to the substitute
- *Definition here:* The physical act of identification with the substitute; the worshipper declaring "this animal represents me"; the OT enacted form of what faith performs in the NT — resting one's full weight on Christ as substitute*

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The Five Offerings of Leviticus 1-7 — All Fulfilled in Christ

The five offerings introduced in Leviticus 1-7 are five dimensions of the one sacrifice of Christ:

| Offering | Hebrew | What It Covers | Fulfilled in Christ |
|----------|--------|---------------|---------------------|
| Burnt Offering | Olah | Total consecration | Hebrews 10:7 — "I come to do thy will" |
| Grain Offering | Minchah | Perfect humanity | Christ's sinless life; John 6:35 |
| Peace Offering | Shelem | Reconciliation/fellowship | Ephesians 2:14 — "He is our peace" |
| Sin Offering | Chattat | Guilt of sin | 2 Corinthians 5:21; Hebrews 13:12 |
| Guilt/Trespass Offering | Asham | Damage done by sin | Isaiah 53:10 — "his soul an offering for sin" (asham) |

*No single offering captures the full work of Christ. All five are required to express what He accomplished. This is why Hebrews dwells at length on the sacrificial system — not to abolish it but to demonstrate that Christ is simultaneously all five offerings in one perfect, unrepeatable act.*

---

### Romans 12:1 — The Living Burnt Offering

**Leviticus 1:9** — The burnt offering is wholly consumed; nothing remains; total offering
**Romans 12:1** — "I beseech you therefore, brethren, by the mercies of God, that ye present your bodies a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable unto God, which is your reasonable service"

- Paul calls believers to be living burnt offerings — total consecration, holding nothing back
- The language is directly sacrificial: "present" (paristemi = to place beside the altar), "living sacrifice," "holy, acceptable" (euarestos = well-pleasing = the sweet savour)
- *The New Covenant burnt offering is not an animal wholly burned — it is a person wholly yielded. The altar is no longer bronze; it is the daily life of a believer given entirely to God.*

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### Leviticus 1:3 — "Voluntary Will" Does Not Mean Optional Atonement

**The misuse:** "He shall offer it of his own voluntary will" (v.3) is sometimes read to imply that bringing an offering to God is purely optional — that worship and atonement are matters of personal preference.

**What the text actually says:** "Voluntary" (Hebrew: *retsono* = at his own pleasure, freely, of his own accord) describes the spirit of the offering — it must be brought willingly, not under compulsion. It does NOT mean atonement itself is optional for the sinful person who needs it. The entire sacrificial system was God's commanded provision; "voluntary" governs the posture, not the necessity.

**The correction from within Scripture:**
- **Hebrews 10:26** — "For if we sin wilfully after that we have received the knowledge of the truth, there remaineth no more sacrifice for sins" — the one who refuses to come does not escape guilt; he faces worse
- **Romans 3:23** — "For all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God" — the need for atonement is universal, not voluntary
- **John 3:36** — "He that believeth not the Son shall not see life; but the wrath of God abideth on him" — there is no neutral position; refusal to come is not a valid option

---

### "Sweet Savour" — Not God's Emotional Pleasure in Animal Death

**The misuse:** The phrase "sweet savour unto the LORD" (vv.9, 13, 17) is sometimes misread as God taking pleasure in the suffering or death of animals, which is then used to question God's character.

**What the text actually says:** The Hebrew *reyach nichoach* = a soothing, settling aroma; the language of appeasement and rest — God's wrath against sin is satisfied by the accepted substitute. The pleasure is not in the death but in the atonement completed.

**The correction from within Scripture:**
- **Psalm 51:16-17** — "For thou desirest not sacrifice; else would I give it: thou delightest not in burnt offering. The sacrifices of God are a broken spirit" — God's delight is in the heart behind the offering, not the animal itself
- **Isaiah 1:11** — "I am full of the burnt offerings of rams... I delight not in the blood of bullocks" — offerings without the heart are not a sweet savour
- **Ephesians 5:2** — The sweet savour God truly desired was Christ's self-offering in love — the animal offerings were shadows pointing to this

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### Fire on the Altar — First Mention and Ongoing Significance

**First mention principle:** The fire of Leviticus 1 is placed by the sons of Aaron (v.7), but Leviticus 9:24 establishes that the original fire came from the LORD Himself. The altar fire is divine in origin.

**The thread through Scripture:**
- [[01. Genesis/GEN_15_REBUILT|Genesis 15:17]] — the burning lamp passing through the covenant pieces = God's fire of acceptance on a sacrifice
- [[03. Leviticus/LEV_06_REBUILT|Leviticus 6:13]] — "The fire shall ever be burning upon the altar; it shall never go out" — the divine fire as the perpetual symbol of God's ongoing acceptance
- [[11. 1 Kings/1_KINGS_18_REBUILT|1 Kings 18:38]] — Elijah on Carmel: fire from the LORD = divine ratification
- [[44. Acts/ACTS_02_REBUILT|Acts 2:3]] — tongues of fire at Pentecost = the New Covenant altar fire falling on living temples
- [[58. Hebrews/HEBREWS_12_REBUILT|Hebrews 12:29]] — "For our God is a consuming fire" — the fire is God's own nature

**What the Bible establishes:** Fire = God's consuming holiness; when applied to a sacrifice = acceptance; when applied to sin unmediated by blood = destruction. The fire that consumed the burnt offering in Leviticus 1 is the same fire that would have consumed the sinner — redirected to the substitute.

---

### The Altar — The Meeting Point of God's Holiness and Human Need

**The altar (Hebrew: mizbeach = place of slaughter)** runs as a thread from Genesis to Revelation:
- [[01. Genesis/GEN_08_REBUILT|Genesis 8:20]] — Noah builds the first post-flood altar; God smells the sweet savour
- [[01. Genesis/GEN_22_REBUILT|Genesis 22:9]] — Abraham builds the altar on Moriah; Isaac on the wood
- [[02. Exodus/EXO_20_REBUILT|Exodus 20:24]] — "An altar of earth thou shalt make unto me"
- **Leviticus 1** — The Tabernacle altar at the door — the required point of approach
- [[23. Isaiah/ISAIAH_06_REBUILT|Isaiah 6:6]] — The live coal from the altar cleanses Isaiah's lips
- [[66. Revelation/REVELATION_08_REBUILT|Revelation 8:3]] — The golden altar before the throne; incense and prayers
- [[66. Revelation/REVELATION_09_REBUILT|Revelation 9:13]] — The voice from the four horns of the golden altar

**What the Bible establishes (Revelation 1:20 principle — symbols defined by Scripture):** The altar is always the place where heaven's demand and earth's need intersect through blood. It is the geography of atonement. Every altar in Scripture is a marker pointing to Calvary, which is the altar where the final sacrifice was made.
