## CHAPTER SUMMARY

The grain offering (minchah = tribute, gift) is the non-animal offering: fine flour with oil poured on it and frankincense placed on it. The priest takes a memorial handful — flour, oil, and all the frankincense — and burns it on the altar as a sweet savour. The rest belongs to Aaron and his sons to eat without leaven in the holy place. It is "most holy." The offering may be prepared in an oven (unleavened cakes or wafers), on a griddle/pan, or in a frying pan — always fine flour, always unleavened, always with oil. The core prohibitions: no leaven, no honey in any fire-offering to the LORD. The core requirement: every grain offering must be salted — "neither shalt thou suffer the salt of the covenant of thy God to be lacking." The firstfruits form: green ears of corn dried by fire, beaten out, with oil and frankincense.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### The Minchah — Tribute and Labor Presented to God

**Leviticus 2:1** — "And when any will offer a meat offering (minchah) unto the LORD"

- Hebrew: minchah = a gift, tribute, present; in Genesis 4 it is used for both Cain's grain offering and Abel's animal offering — the word itself is neutral; it is what is offered and how that matters
- **Genesis 4:3-5** — Cain brought a minchah of the fruit of the ground; Abel brought of the firstlings of his flock and the fat; God had respect for Abel's but not Cain's — the issue was faith (Hebrews 11:4), not the category of offering
- **Psalm 96:8** — "Give unto the LORD the glory due unto his name: bring an offering (minchah), and come into his courts"
- *Interpretation:* The grain offering is the consecration of human labor and cultivation to God. The animal offering (olah) represents substitution and life given; the grain offering represents the product of work given. Together they declare: both life and labor belong to God. The worshipper offers not only a substitute for himself but the fruit of what his hands have made.*

---

### No Leaven, No Honey — What Cannot Be Offered

**Leviticus 2:11** — "No meat offering, which ye shall bring unto the LORD, shall be made with leaven: for ye shall burn no leaven, nor any honey, in any offering of the LORD made by fire"

- Leaven = fermentation; corruption spreading through the whole (Matthew 16:6, 11-12; 1 Corinthians 5:6-8; Galatians 5:9)
- Honey = natural sweetness; may represent human attractiveness or natural appeal offered in place of Spirit-granted acceptability
- **1 Corinthians 5:7-8** — "Purge out therefore the old leaven, that ye may be a new lump, as ye are unleavened. For even Christ our passover is sacrificed for us: Therefore let us keep the feast, not with old leaven, neither with the leaven of malice and wickedness; but with the unleavened bread of sincerity and truth"
- **Matthew 16:6** — "Beware of the leaven of the Pharisees and of the Sadducees" — religious corruption as leaven
- *Interpretation:* What is offered to God must be free from corruption (no leaven) and from the substitution of natural appeal for genuine holiness (no honey). God does not accept worship that mixes His standard with human corruption or natural sweetness. The prohibition on leaven in offerings is the sacrificial form of the New Testament warnings against leaven in the community of faith and in doctrine.*

---

### "The Salt of the Covenant" — What Must Be in Every Offering

**Leviticus 2:13** — "And every oblation of thy meat offering shalt thou season with salt; neither shalt thou suffer the salt of the covenant of thy God to be lacking from thy meat offering: with all thine offerings thou shalt offer salt"

- Salt = preservation, incorruption, covenant faithfulness; the opposite of leaven (fermentation/corruption)
- **Numbers 18:19** — "It is a covenant of salt for ever before the LORD" — the priestly covenant described as a salt covenant
- **2 Chronicles 13:5** — "Ought ye not to know that the LORD God of Israel gave the kingdom over Israel to David for ever, even to him and to his sons by a covenant of salt?" — the Davidic covenant described as a salt covenant
- **Mark 9:49-50** — "For every one shall be salted with fire... Salt is good: but if the salt have lost his saltness, wherewith will ye season it? Have salt in yourselves, and have peace one with another"
- **Matthew 5:13** — "Ye are the salt of the earth" — believers as the salt-covenant people
- **Colossians 4:6** — "Let your speech be alway with grace, seasoned with salt, that ye may know how ye ought to answer every man"
- *Interpretation:* Salt in every offering is the covenant-faithfulness requirement. No offering can be given to God without the mark of covenant loyalty. Where leaven is the sign of corruption, salt is the sign of preservation and fidelity. The New Covenant equivalents are clear: believers are the salt of the earth (Matthew 5:13); speech is to be salted (Colossians 4:6); the community must have salt in itself (Mark 9:50). All three are the same principle: covenant faithfulness must permeate every dimension of the believer's life just as salt must permeate every offering.*

---

### The Memorial Portion — A Handful for God, the Rest for the Priests

**Leviticus 2:2-3** — "the priest shall burn the memorial of it upon the altar... And the remnant of the meat offering shall be Aaron's and his sons'"

- Hebrew: azkarah = memorial, reminder; the handful burned is God's portion; it represents and is received in place of the whole
- **1 Corinthians 9:13** — "Do ye not know that they which minister about holy things live of the things of the temple? and they which wait at the altar are partakers with the altar?"
- **1 Corinthians 9:14** — "Even so hath the Lord ordained that they which preach the gospel should live of the gospel"
- *Interpretation:* The grain offering establishes the principle that those who serve at the altar live from the altar. The priest who burns the memorial portion eats the rest — not as a perquisite but as God's ordained provision for those who give their lives to His service. Paul cites this exact principle in 1 Corinthians 9 to establish the right of those who minister the gospel to receive support from the community they serve.*

---

### The Grain Offering as Christ's Perfect Humanity

**Leviticus 2** — Fine flour (no lumps — perfect evenness), oil throughout, no leaven

- Fine flour = no coarseness, no roughness, no imperfection; perfectly uniform
- Oil = the Holy Spirit throughout (Isaiah 61:1; Luke 4:18 — Christ anointed with the Spirit)
- No leaven = no sin, no corruption
- Frankincense = the ascending fragrance of perfect worship
- **John 6:35** — "I am the bread of life" — Jesus is the minchah; the perfect grain of humanity offered to God
- **Hebrews 4:15** — "tempted in all points like as we are, yet without sin" — the sinless fine flour
- **Luke 1:35** — "The Holy Ghost shall come upon thee... that holy thing which shall be born of thee" — conceived and filled with the Spirit (oil) from the beginning
- *Interpretation:* The grain offering is the type of Christ's perfect humanity: formed by the Spirit (oil), without sin (no leaven), perfectly uniform (fine flour), fragrant before God (frankincense). Leviticus 2 is the detailed portrait of the human life of the Son of God — the Man without blemish, without the fermenting corruption of sin, filled throughout with the oil of the Spirit.*

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### MINCHAH (Hebrew: minchah)
- A gift, tribute, or present; used of both grain and animal gifts; originally the word for any formal gift to a superior
- **Leviticus 2:1** — in the sacrificial system specifically: the grain/cereal offering of fine flour with oil and frankincense
- *Definition here:* The tribute of human labor and cultivation offered to God; the acknowledgment that the fruit of human hands belongs to the LORD; complementing the animal offering (which represents life given) with the grain offering (labor given)*

### MEMORIAL (Hebrew: azkarah)
- **Leviticus 2:2** — the handful of flour/oil/frankincense burned as the representative portion; it "reminds" God of the whole offering and is received in its place
- *Definition here:* The portion that stands for and is received instead of the whole; the principle that God accepts the consecrated representative portion as equivalent to the total offering — the basis of all memorial theology (including the Lord's Supper — 1 Corinthians 11:24-25 "in remembrance of me")*

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The Grain Offering in the New Covenant

**Leviticus 2** → **Romans 12:1** → **1 Corinthians 10:31**

- The grain offering is the consecration of human work and sustenance to God
- Romans 12:1 — "present your bodies a living sacrifice" — the whole of human life including its labor
- 1 Corinthians 10:31 — "Whether therefore ye eat, or drink, or whatsoever ye do, do all to the glory of God" — even eating and drinking become grain offerings
- Colossians 3:23 — "And whatsoever ye do, do it heartily, as to the Lord, and not unto men" — every work becomes a minchah

*The grain offering is not superseded by Christ's sacrifice — it is fulfilled in the believer who takes the fruit of daily labor and consecrates it wholly to God. The New Covenant believer IS the priest who eats the remainder; the offering of daily work is both given to God (the handful burned) and received back as holy provision for life (the remainder eaten in the holy place).*

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### Leviticus 2:13 — "Salt of the Covenant" Isolated From Its Context

**The misuse:** "The salt of the covenant of thy God" is sometimes cited in isolation to support various practices (dietary laws, preserving specific customs) without anchoring it to its covenantal meaning.

**What the text actually says:** Salt is required in every grain offering because salt = covenant faithfulness and preservation (the opposite of leaven = corruption). The point is not about salt itself but about what salt represents: every offering must be marked by covenant loyalty.

**The correction from within Scripture:**
- **Numbers 18:19** — "It is a covenant of salt for ever before the LORD" — the salt covenant is the preservation of God's covenantal commitments, not a dietary command
- **2 Chronicles 13:5** — "Ought ye not to know that the LORD God of Israel gave the kingdom over Israel to David for ever, even to him and to his sons by a covenant of salt?" — the Davidic covenant is a salt covenant
- **Mark 9:50** — "Have salt in yourselves, and have peace one with another" — the covenant-faithfulness principle applied to relationships in the New Covenant community

---

### Leviticus 2:11 — "No Leaven" Misapplied as Only Dietary

**The misuse:** The prohibition on leaven in grain offerings is sometimes read purely as a dietary regulation rather than a theological statement about what can be brought before God.

**What the text actually says:** Leaven and honey are prohibited specifically in what is burned — what is offered to God by fire. This is a category statement: corruption (leaven) and natural sweetness that does not arise from God's holiness (honey) have no place in what is presented to God as holy.

**The correction from within Scripture:**
- **1 Corinthians 5:6-8** — Paul interprets the leaven prohibition directly: leaven = malice and wickedness; unleavened = sincerity and truth; "Christ our passover is sacrificed for us"
- **Matthew 16:6** — Jesus uses leaven as the symbol of corrupt doctrine: "Beware of the leaven of the Pharisees and of the Sadducees"
- **Galatians 5:9** — "A little leaven leaveneth the whole lump" — the spreading principle

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### Oil — The Holy Spirit Woven Through the Grain Offering

**The thread through Scripture (Revelation 1:20 principle — defined by Scripture):**
- **Exodus 27:20** — "pure oil olive beaten for the light" — the oil that keeps the menorah burning
- **Leviticus 2:1, 4, 6** — oil poured on the grain offering; oil mixed into the dough
- **1 Samuel 16:13** — "Samuel took the horn of oil, and anointed him... and the Spirit of the LORD came upon David from that day forward" — oil = Spirit anointing
- **Isaiah 61:1** — "The Spirit of the Lord GOD is upon me; because the LORD hath anointed me" — oil as the mark of the Spirit's commissioning
- **Zechariah 4:6** — "Not by might, nor by power, but by my spirit, saith the LORD of hosts" — context: the golden candlestick fed by oil
- **Matthew 25:3-4** — The foolish virgins had no oil; the wise had oil — the Spirit's presence as the essential fuel

**What the Bible establishes:** Oil in the grain offering is not a flavoring agent — it is the Spirit's presence woven through the offering. Fine flour without oil = human labor without the Spirit = what God does not receive as a sweet savour. Every oil-soaked grain offering is a confession: "What I bring is only acceptable because the Spirit is in it."

---

### Fine Flour — Perfection and Evenness as a Symbolic Thread

- **Leviticus 2:1** — "his offering shall be of fine flour" — no coarseness, no lumps, perfectly uniform
- **Psalm 81:16** — "He should have fed them also with the finest of the wheat" — fine flour as God's best provision
- **John 6:35** — "I am the bread of life" — Christ as the minchah, the fine-flour offering of perfect humanity
- **Hebrews 4:15** — "tempted in all points like as we are, yet without sin" — the sinless fine flour of Christ's humanity; no impurity, no coarseness of fallen nature
- **Revelation 19:8** — "the fine linen, clean and white: for the fine linen is the righteousness of saints" — the fine-flour standard applied to the bride's garments at the marriage supper

**What the Bible establishes:** Fine flour is the scriptural symbol of moral and spiritual perfection — no lumps, no impurity, uniform throughout. Its presence in the grain offering demands that what is brought to God reflect His standard of wholeness. Christ is the only pure fine-flour offering; believers clothed in His righteousness are the fine linen of Revelation 19.
