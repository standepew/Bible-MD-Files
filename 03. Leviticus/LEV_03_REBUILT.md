## CHAPTER SUMMARY

The peace offering (shelem = wholeness, completeness, peace/shalom) may be from the herd or the flock, male or female, without blemish. The worshipper lays his hand on the head and kills it at the Tabernacle door. The priest sprinkles the blood around the altar. The fat portions — the fat covering the inwards, the kidneys, the caul above the liver — are burned on the altar. For the lamb, also the whole tail. The verdict: "all the fat is the LORD's." A perpetual statute: eat neither fat nor blood. The peace offering is distinctively a shared offering: God receives the fat portions (burned), the priest receives specified portions, and the worshipper and his household eat the rest — it is a fellowship meal eaten in God's presence.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### Shalom — Peace Through Blood

**Leviticus 3:1** — "a sacrifice of peace offering" — Hebrew: zebah sheleimim — sacrifice of completeness/wholeness

- The root shelem shares its root with shalom (peace) and shalem (to be complete, whole, restored)
- **Isaiah 53:5** — "the chastisement of our peace (shalom) was upon him; and with his stripes we are healed" — Christ is the peace offering; the chastisement that makes shalom possible falls on Him
- **Romans 5:1** — "Therefore being justified by faith, we have peace (eirene) with God through our Lord Jesus Christ"
- **Ephesians 2:14-15** — "For he is our peace, who hath made both one, and hath broken down the middle wall of partition between us; Having abolished in his flesh the enmity"
- **Colossians 1:20** — "having made peace through the blood of his cross, by him to reconcile all things unto himself"
- *Interpretation:* The peace offering is the sacrifice that restores fellowship — it is not primarily about guilt removal (that is the sin offering) but about the enjoyment of reconciled relationship. Ephesians 2:14 names Christ as "our peace" — the very thing the shelem embodied. His blood does not merely cancel the legal debt; it opens the door to a shared meal with God. Romans 5:1 is the result: "we have peace with God." The peace offering is the enacted declaration that through blood, the hostility is over and the fellowship meal can begin.*

---

### The Shared Meal — Communion with God

**Leviticus 3** — Structure of the peace offering:
- God receives: the fat (burned on the altar)
- The priest receives: specified portions (given in chapter 7)
- The worshipper receives: the flesh to eat with his household

- **Exodus 24:9-11** — Moses, Aaron, and 70 elders "saw God, and did eat and drink" on the mountain — the covenant communion meal as type
- **1 Corinthians 10:18** — "Behold Israel after the flesh: are not they which eat of the sacrifices partakers of the altar?" — eating the peace offering = communion with the altar = communion with God
- **1 Corinthians 10:16** — "The cup of blessing which we bless, is it not the communion of the blood of Christ? The bread which we break, is it not the communion of the body of Christ?"
- **Revelation 3:20** — "Behold, I stand at the door, and knock: if any man hear my voice, and open the door, I will come in to him, and will sup with him, and he with me" — the invitation to a peace-offering meal
- **Revelation 19:9** — "Blessed are they which are called unto the marriage supper of the Lamb" — the eschatological peace offering; the final fellowship meal
- *Interpretation:* The peace offering is the Old Testament Lord's Supper. The worshipper eats in God's presence, at God's table, on the basis of blood sprinkled on the altar. 1 Corinthians 10:18 makes the parallel explicit: to eat the sacrifice is to be a partaker of the altar. The Lord's Supper is the New Covenant peace offering — the shared meal between God and His people, established through Christ's blood, anticipating the final marriage supper.*

### "All the Fat Is the LORD's" — The Best Belongs to God

**Leviticus 3:16-17** — "all the fat is the LORD's. It shall be a perpetual statute for your generations throughout all your dwellings, that ye eat neither fat nor blood"

- In the ancient world, fat = the richest, best portion; to give someone the fat = to give them the best
- **Genesis 4:4** — Abel brought "of the firstlings of his flock and of the fat thereof" — the fat as the best portion
- **Deuteronomy 32:14** — "the fat of kidneys of wheat" — fat as a metaphor for the richest produce
- **Psalm 63:5** — "My soul shall be satisfied as with marrow and fatness; and my mouth shall praise thee"
- **Isaiah 1:11** — "I am full of the burnt offerings of rams, and the fat of fed beasts" — God's rejection of hollow sacrifices; the fat without the heart is meaningless
- *Interpretation:* God claims the fat — the best — for Himself. The worshipper does not keep the richest portion while offering the remainder to God. This is the anti-Cain principle: not "I'll give God what I have left after keeping the best," but "the best belongs to God first." The prohibition on eating fat in Israel's daily life (not just at the altar) extends this principle beyond worship into all of life: the fat — the first and finest — is always the LORD's.*

---

### No Blood — The Sanctity of Life

**Leviticus 3:17** — "ye shall eat no manner of blood"

- **Leviticus 17:11** — "For the life of the flesh is in the blood: and I have given it to you upon the altar to make an atonement for your souls: for it is the blood that maketh an atonement for the soul"
- **Genesis 9:4** — "But flesh with the life thereof, which is the blood thereof, shall ye not eat" — the prohibition is as old as Noah; it is not merely Mosaic
- **Acts 15:20, 29** — The Jerusalem Council retains this prohibition for Gentile believers: "abstain from blood" — one of only four requirements carried into the New Covenant community
- **Hebrews 9:22** — "without shedding of blood is no remission"
- *Interpretation:* The blood prohibition is the constant reminder that life belongs to God. Blood is not food — it is the medium of atonement. To eat blood is to treat as common what God has set apart as the price of redemption. Every time Israel refrained from eating blood, they enacted the theological truth that the life poured out on the altar is holy — it is not consumed but consecrated. Hebrews 9:22 is the capstone: the blood that cannot be eaten is the blood without which there is no forgiveness.*

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### PEACE OFFERING (Hebrew: zebah sheleimim / shelem)
- From shelem = completeness, wholeness; related to shalom = peace
- *Definition here:* The offering of restored fellowship; the sacrifice through which the broken relationship between God and man is not only legally repaired (sin offering) but experientially enjoyed (shared meal); the type of the Lord's Supper and ultimately the marriage supper of the Lamb*

### FAT (Hebrew: chelev)
- **Leviticus 3:16** — "all the fat is the LORD's" — chelev = the best portions of the animal; specifically the suet, the kidney fat, the caul
- Distinct from the fat within the muscle (marrow); this is the rich overlay fat
- *Definition here:* The richest, best portion of the sacrifice, reserved entirely for God; the principle that God claims the first and finest; no worshipper is to consume what God has claimed*

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The Three-Way Sharing and the Trinity

The peace offering's division — God's portion (fat), priest's portion (Leviticus 7), worshipper's portion (the rest) — reflects the communal nature of the sacrifice:
- God accepts the offering through the fire
- The priest mediates and receives
- The worshipper eats and is satisfied

In the New Covenant:
- The Father accepts the sacrifice of the Son (Ephesians 5:2)
- Christ the High Priest mediates (Hebrews 7:25)
- The believer feeds on Christ (John 6:54-56 — "Whoso eateth my flesh and drinketh my blood, hath eternal life")

*The peace offering is not merely a bilateral transaction between man and God — it is a shared table. This is why Revelation 19:9 describes the final state as a supper. The ultimate peace offering produces the ultimate fellowship meal.*

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Peace" in the Peace Offering — Not Merely Emotional Peace

**The misuse:** "Peace offering" is often read as an offering that produces emotional calm or inner tranquility — as if the peace offering is about the worshipper feeling peaceful.

**What the text actually says:** The Hebrew *shelem* / *shalom* here is relational and covenantal — the restoration of a right standing between two parties where hostility previously existed. This is purchased, blood-established peace, not a feeling.

**The correction from within Scripture:**
- **Romans 5:1** — "Therefore being justified by faith, we have peace WITH GOD through our Lord Jesus Christ" — peace WITH God (objective, relational), not peace IN yourself (subjective, emotional)
- **Isaiah 53:5** — "the chastisement of our peace was upon him" — the peace was purchased by the Servant's suffering; it is not self-generated
- **Ephesians 2:14** — "he IS our peace" — Christ Himself is the peace; the peace is in a Person, not in a state of mind
- **Philippians 4:7** — "the peace of God, which passeth all understanding" — the subjective peace exists, but it flows FROM the objective peace with God; it is downstream, not the thing itself

---

### Leviticus 3:17 — "Perpetual Statute" and the New Covenant

**The misuse:** "It shall be a perpetual statute for your generations" (v.17 — no fat or blood) is sometimes cited to argue that Mosaic dietary laws are still fully binding on New Covenant believers.

**What the text actually says:** The *olam* statute (perpetual/age-lasting for your generations) applies to Israel's covenant generations under the Mosaic economy. The theological principle beneath it (blood = atonement medium; fat = the LORD's portion) is permanent; the dietary application is part of the ceremonial system addressed by the New Covenant.

**The correction from within Scripture:**
- **Acts 15:20, 29** — The Jerusalem Council retains only four requirements for Gentile believers, including abstaining from blood — the blood prohibition is retained because the principle (life is in the blood; it belongs to God) remains; the broader dietary law is not mentioned as binding
- **Colossians 2:16-17** — "Let no man therefore judge you in meat, or in drink... which are a shadow of things to come; but the body is of Christ" — the shadow-system is fulfilled in Christ
- **Mark 7:19** — Jesus "purging all meats" — the food category distinctions are resolved in Christ

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### Blood Sprinkled Around the Altar — A Thread Through Scripture

**Leviticus 3:2, 8, 13** — "Aaron's sons the priests shall sprinkle the blood upon the altar round about" — the blood goes around the altar, not just on one side.

The thread:
- [[02. Exodus/EXO_24_REBUILT|Exodus 24:6]] — Moses sprinkles half the blood of the covenant on the altar, half on the people: "Behold the blood of the covenant"
- **Leviticus 3, 4, 7** — blood sprinkled around the altar in every major offering
- [[58. Hebrews/HEBREWS_09_REBUILT|Hebrews 9:21]] — "he sprinkled with blood both the tabernacle, and all the vessels of the ministry"
- [[60. 1 Peter/1_PETER_01_REBUILT|1 Peter 1:2]] — believers "elect... unto obedience and sprinkling of the blood of Jesus Christ"
- [[58. Hebrews/HEBREWS_12_REBUILT|Hebrews 12:24]] — "the blood of sprinkling, that speaketh better things than that of Abel"

**What the Bible establishes:** The "round about" sprinkling covers every approach to the altar — not just one direction, not just one tribe, not just one kind of sin. The blood that goes around the altar declares that every approach to God from every direction is covered by the one atoning sacrifice.

---

### The Shared Meal — Table Communion as a Symbolic Thread

The peace offering is the only sacrifice where the worshipper eats with God. This shared-meal thread runs through Scripture:

- [[01. Genesis/GEN_18_REBUILT|Genesis 18:1-8]] — Abraham hosts the LORD and His angels; eating together as covenant fellowship
- [[02. Exodus/EXO_24_REBUILT|Exodus 24:11]] — "And upon the nobles of the children of Israel he laid not his hand: also they saw God, and did eat and drink" — covenant communion at Sinai
- **Leviticus 3** — The peace offering: God's portion (fat), priest's portion, worshipper's portion — the three-way shared table
- [[42. Luke/LUKE_22_REBUILT|Luke 22:20]] — "This cup is the new testament in my blood" — the shared cup of the New Covenant peace offering
- [[66. Revelation/REVELATION_03_REBUILT|Revelation 3:20]] — "I will come in to him, and will sup with him, and he with me" — Christ's invitation to the New Covenant peace-offering meal
- [[66. Revelation/REVELATION_19_REBUILT|Revelation 19:9]] — "Blessed are they which are called unto the marriage supper of the Lamb" — the eschatological peace offering; the final shared meal

**What the Bible establishes:** The shared meal is the physical expression of covenant peace. Where there is no meal, there may be treaty but not intimacy. The peace offering moves beyond legal settlement into fellowship. The Lord's Supper and ultimately the marriage supper of the Lamb are the New and Final Covenant peace offerings.
