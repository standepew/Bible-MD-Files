## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Chapter 7 closes the first major section of Leviticus. The trespass offering: most holy, killed in the same place as the burnt offering, blood sprinkled around the altar, fat burned, eaten in the holy place by the officiating priest. The priest who offers a burnt offering keeps its skin. The grain offering portions distributed equally among Aaron's sons. The peace offering: three forms — thanksgiving (eat same day, with leavened bread alongside unleavened), vow or voluntary (eat over two days, third-day remainder burned). Unclean person who eats peace offering flesh is cut off. Any flesh touching an unclean thing is burned. No fat, no blood — cut off from the people for violation. The wave breast belongs to Aaron and his sons; the right shoulder (heave offering) to the officiating priest — "by a statute for ever." The chapter and the opening section close with the summary: "This is the law of the burnt offering, of the meat offering, and of the sin offering, and of the trespass offering, and of the consecrations, and of the sacrifice of the peace offerings; Which the LORD commanded Moses in mount Sinai."

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### Three Forms of the Peace Offering — Thanksgiving, Vow, Voluntary

**Leviticus 7:12-16** — Thanksgiving (todah), vow (neder), voluntary/freewill (nedavah)

- **Psalm 107:22** — "And let them sacrifice the sacrifices of thanksgiving (todah), and declare his works with rejoicing"
- **Psalm 116:17** — "I will offer to thee the sacrifice of thanksgiving (todah), and will call upon the name of the LORD"
- **Jonah 2:9** — "I will sacrifice unto thee with the voice of thanksgiving (todah)" — Jonah in the fish; his promised peace offering
- **Hebrews 13:15** — "By him therefore let us offer the sacrifice of praise to God continually, that is, the fruit of our lips giving thanks to his name" — the todah fulfilled in verbal praise through Christ
- *Interpretation:* The thanksgiving peace offering is the most immediate form: something has happened; God has acted; the worshipper comes with gratitude and a peace offering eaten that very day. The urgency of the same-day eating matches the immediacy of grateful response — thanksgiving should not wait. Psalm 116 and Psalm 107 are full expressions of the todah spirit. Hebrews 13:15 is the New Covenant todah: the "sacrifice of praise" offered "continually" through Christ — the perpetual thanksgiving peace offering of the believer.*

### The Third-Day Flesh — Abomination

**Leviticus 7:17-18** — "But the remainder of the flesh of the sacrifice on the third day shall be burnt with fire. And if any of the flesh of the sacrifice of his peace offerings be eaten at all on the third day, it shall not be accepted, neither shall it be imputed unto him that offereth it: it shall be an abomination (piggul), and the soul that eateth of it shall bear his iniquity"

- Hebrew: piggul = something foul, rotten, ritually offensive; the flesh that has passed its acceptable period becomes a piggul — not merely stale but spiritually offensive
- The same sacrifice that was holy on day one is an abomination on day three
- **1 Corinthians 5:7-8** — "Purge out therefore the old leaven... Christ our passover is sacrificed for us: Therefore let us keep the feast, not with old leaven" — old religious stock that should have been consumed in its proper time becomes corruption
- *Interpretation:* The time limit on the peace offering flesh is a statement about the nature of sacred things: their holiness is not permanent apart from proper use within the appointed time. What is holy at the right moment becomes abomination when held past its season. This is a warning against religious formalism: the peace offering that should produce fresh fellowship — eaten the same day in gratitude — cannot be stored as a religious system and consumed three days later as custom. Religion preserved past its spiritual moment of life becomes piggul.*

### No Fat, No Blood — The Two Perpetual Prohibitions

**Leviticus 7:23-27** — "Ye shall eat no manner of fat, of ox, or of sheep, or of goat... ye shall eat no manner of blood, whether it be of fowl or of beast... even that soul shall be cut off from his people"

- Both prohibitions are "perpetual statutes" — they predate Moses (Genesis 9:4 for blood) and are reaffirmed in the New Covenant (Acts 15:29)
- **Acts 15:20, 29** — The Jerusalem Council's four requirements for Gentile believers include abstaining from blood — the only Mosaic practice carried explicitly into the New Covenant community by apostolic decree
- **Leviticus 17:11** — The theological reason: "For the life of the flesh is in the blood: and I have given it to you upon the altar to make an atonement for your souls: for it is the blood that maketh an atonement for the soul"
- *Interpretation:* The prohibition on blood is not dietary law — it is atonement theology in practice. Blood is not food; it is the medium of redemption. To eat blood is to consume what God has reserved as the price of atonement. The Acts 15 retention of this prohibition in the New Covenant community is not a partial continuation of the Law — it is the recognition that the theological principle beneath the prohibition (blood = life = the price of redemption) remains eternally true. Christ's blood is not consumed — it is applied.*

### The Wave Offering and Heave Offering — The Priest's Ordained Portion

**Leviticus 7:30-34** — "His own hands shall bring the offerings of the LORD made by fire, the fat with the breast, it shall he bring, that the breast may be waved for a wave offering before the LORD... And the right shoulder shall ye give unto the priest for an heave offering"

- Wave offering (tenufah): waved before the LORD — acknowledging He is the owner who gives back to the priest
- Heave offering (terumah): lifted up toward God — the same acknowledgment
- The breast (heart) = closest to the priest; the right shoulder (strength/work) = given to the officiating priest
- **1 Corinthians 9:13-14** — "Do ye not know that they which minister about holy things live of the things of the temple? and they which wait at the altar are partakers with the altar? Even so hath the Lord ordained that they which preach the gospel should live of the gospel"
- *Interpretation:* God ordained from Leviticus 7 that those who serve at the altar eat from the altar. This is not a concession to human need — it is God's design for sustaining those whose lives are given to ministry. 1 Corinthians 9:13-14 is not Paul's innovation; it is his application of the Levitical principle. The New Covenant community that receives the ministry of the gospel is obligated — by the same principle embedded in Leviticus 7 — to sustain those who minister.*

### The Summary Verse — The Law of All Five Offerings

**Leviticus 7:37-38** — "This is the law of the burnt offering, of the meat offering, and of the sin offering, and of the trespass offering, and of the consecrations, and of the sacrifice of the peace offerings; Which the LORD commanded Moses in mount Sinai, in the day that he commanded the children of Israel to offer their oblations unto the LORD, in the wilderness of Sinai"

- The Spirit places a formal closing summary on the entire opening section of Leviticus (chapters 1-7)
- Six categories: burnt offering (1), grain offering (2), sin offering (4), trespass offering (5), consecrations (8), peace offering (3, 7)
- **Hebrews 10:1** — "For the law having a shadow of good things to come, and not the very image of the things, can never with those sacrifices which they offered year by year continually make the comers thereunto perfect"
- **Hebrews 10:12-14** — "But this man, after he had offered one sacrifice for sins for ever, sat down on the right hand of God... For by one offering he hath perfected for ever them that are sanctified"
- *Interpretation:* The summary in Leviticus 7:37-38 says: here is the complete system. Hebrews 10:1-14 says: the complete system was a shadow; here is the reality. One sacrifice replaces the entire six-category system. The system was not wrong — it was preparatory. Its completion in Christ is not its abolition but its arrival.*

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### THANKSGIVING OFFERING (Hebrew: todah)
- **Leviticus 7:12** — todah = praise, thanksgiving, a confession of God's goodness; the specific peace offering of gratitude
- Same root as the modern Hebrew "todah" (thank you)
- **Psalm 50:14, 23** — "Offer unto God thanksgiving... Whoso offereth praise glorifieth me" — God desires the todah above formal sacrifice
- *Definition here:* The peace offering of immediate gratitude for God's specific act of salvation or provision; the most personal form of the peace offering; its New Covenant form = Hebrews 13:15 (the sacrifice of praise)*

### WAVE OFFERING (Hebrew: tenufah)
- **Leviticus 7:30** — tenufah = from nuph = to wave, swing, elevate; the gesture of waving before the LORD and back acknowledges: it was His, He gave it back as provision
- *Definition here:* The ritual acknowledgment that what the priest receives is God's gift returned — not earned, not owned independently; the motion of wave-and-return enacts the theology that all provision flows from God and returns to God in gratitude*

### ABOMINATION / PIGGUL (Hebrew: piggul)
- **Leviticus 7:18** — piggul = something offensive, putrid, foul; used specifically for flesh that has passed its acceptable time
- *Definition here:* The transformation of something once holy into something spiritually repugnant through misuse of time and context; the warning against treating sacred things as indefinitely preserved rather than to be consumed in their proper season*

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### Hebrews 10 — The Shadow and the Substance

**Leviticus 7:37-38** presents the complete five-offering system as a unified whole. Hebrews 10 addresses it as a unified whole:

- **Hebrews 10:1** — "the law having a shadow of good things to come... can never with those sacrifices which they offered year by year continually make the comers thereunto perfect"
- **Hebrews 10:4** — "For it is not possible that the blood of bulls and of goats should take away sins" — the entire system points beyond itself
- **Hebrews 10:5-7** — The Son entering the world says: "Sacrifice and offering thou wouldest not... burnt offerings and sacrifices for sin thou hast had no pleasure in... Lo, I come... to do thy will, O God" — quoting Psalm 40:6-8, which is itself a Davidic meditation on the limits of the Levitical system
- **Hebrews 10:12-14** — "But this man, after he had offered one sacrifice for sins for ever, sat down on the right hand of God... For by one offering he hath perfected for ever them that are sanctified"

*Leviticus 1-7 is not a system that failed. It is a system that worked — but worked precisely by pointing beyond itself to the one Sacrifice that would make all these shadows unnecessary. The five offerings are five sermons preached in blood and fire, all saying the same thing: "Someone is coming who will do this once, perfectly, and sit down."*

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### Leviticus 7:12 — "Thanksgiving" Reduced to Emotional Gratitude

**The misuse:** "If he offer it for a thanksgiving" is read through a modern lens as simply expressing a grateful feeling to God — an emotional posture rather than a covenantal act.

**What the text actually says:** The todah (thanksgiving offering) is a sacrifice — blood was shed, flour was offered, specific breads were brought. The "thanksgiving" here is not a mood but a public, costly declaration before the congregation that God has acted on one's behalf.

**The correction from within Scripture:**
- **Psalm 116:12-14, 17-18** — "What shall I render unto the LORD for all his benefits toward me? I will take the cup of salvation... I will pay my vows unto the LORD now in the presence of all his people. I will offer to thee the sacrifice of thanksgiving" — the todah is linked to making vows public and paying them before witnesses; it is an act of testimony, not just emotion
- **Hebrews 13:15** — "By him therefore let us offer the sacrifice of praise to God continually, that is, the fruit of our lips giving thanks to his name" — the New Covenant todah is vocal, ongoing, costly in that it requires the lips to speak truthfully in every season
- **Jonah 2:9** — "But I will sacrifice unto thee with the voice of thanksgiving; I will pay that that I have vowed" — from inside the fish; the todah offered in extremity before deliverance comes

---

### Leviticus 7:18 — Piggul — "Abomination" Through Delayed Intent

**The misuse:** The piggul concept — eating the peace offering on the third day makes it "an abomination" and "it shall not be accepted" — is sometimes dismissed as merely procedural food safety.

**What the text actually says:** Piggul (*pig-gool* = something abominable, foul, rejected) operates on the principle that the offering's validity is tied to the worshipper's intent AT THE TIME of offering. If the priest or offerer intends from the start to eat it on day three — stretching the offering beyond its proper limits — the entire offering is retroactively voided. Corrupt intent poisons the act at its root.

**The correction from within Scripture:**
- **Isaiah 65:4** — "which eat swine's flesh, and broth of abominable things is in their vessels" — piggul language applied to deliberate covenant violation
- **1 Corinthians 5:8** — "the leaven of malice and wickedness" — corrupt intent embedded in what appears to be a religious act
- **Matthew 23:27-28** — "whited sepulchres... full of dead men's bones" — religious form maintained while the inner intent is corrupt; the piggul principle at the level of the entire Pharisaic system

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### The Three-Day Limit — A Thread Through Scripture

[[03. Leviticus/LEV_07_REBUILT|Leviticus 7:17-18]] — The peace offering flesh must be eaten within two days; day three = piggul = abomination

The three-day boundary is a recurring scriptural marker:
- [[01. Genesis/GEN_22_REBUILT|Genesis 22:4]] — "Then on the third day Abraham lifted up his eyes" — the third day as the day of resolution after the trial; Isaac "alive from the dead" on the third day (Hebrews 11:19)
- **Exodus 19:11, 15-16** — "And be ready against the third day: for the third day the LORD will come down" — the divine appointment at Sinai
- [[28. Hosea/HOSEA_06_REBUILT|Hosea 6:2]] — "After two days will he revive us: in the third day he will raise us up, and we shall live in his sight" — the resurrection pattern
- [[32. Jonah/JONAH_01_REBUILT|Jonah 1:17]] — three days and three nights in the whale's belly
- [[40. Matthew/MATTHEW_12_REBUILT|Matthew 12:40]] — "So shall the Son of man be three days and three nights in the heart of the earth" — Jesus applies the Jonah pattern to His own death and resurrection
- [[46. 1 Corinthians/1_CORINTHIANS_15_REBUILT|1 Corinthians 15:4]] — "he rose again the third day according to the scriptures" — "according to the scriptures" = the entire three-day thread throughout the OT

**What the Bible establishes:** Three days in Scripture consistently marks the boundary between what is valid/alive and what has become corrupt/dead. The peace offering that is eaten within two days participates in the living, accepted sacrifice; the flesh carried to day three has crossed the boundary into corruption. Christ's resurrection on the third day is the supreme enactment of this principle: death could not hold Him past the boundary of the third day.

### The Five Offerings as a Complete Portrait of Christ

[[03. Leviticus/LEV_07_REBUILT|Leviticus 7:37-38]] closes all five offerings as a unified system. The five together form one complete typological portrait:

| Offering | What It Typifies | New Testament Fulfillment |
|---|---|---|
| Burnt Offering (olah) | Total consecration; complete self-giving | Ephesians 5:2 — "gave himself for us, an offering and a sacrifice to God for a sweetsmelling savour" |
| Grain Offering (minchah) | Perfect humanity; the life lived for God | John 6:35 — "I am the bread of life"; Hebrews 4:15 — without sin |
| Peace Offering (shelem) | Covenant communion; reconciliation | Ephesians 2:14 — "he is our peace"; Romans 5:1 — "peace with God" |
| Sin Offering (chattat) | Bearing the guilt of sin | 2 Corinthians 5:21 — "he hath made him to be sin for us" |
| Trespass Offering (asham) | Restitution for specific wrong done | Isaiah 53:10 — "thou shalt make his soul an offering for sin (asham)" |

**What the Bible establishes:** No single offering could show the whole work of Christ. It required five. Hebrews 10:14 declares that the five-offering system — repeated annually, daily — accomplished in one unrepeatable act what all of them together could only shadow: "by one offering he hath perfected for ever them that are sanctified." The five offerings are not rivals or alternatives; they are five facets of one diamond.
