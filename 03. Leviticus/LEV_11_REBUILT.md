## CHAPTER SUMMARY

God speaks to Moses and Aaron with comprehensive food laws. Land animals: permitted if they both split the hoof completely and chew the cud; swine, camel, hare, and coney fail one criterion each and are unclean. Water creatures: permitted if they have fins and scales; all others are abomination. Birds: all of prey and carrion-eaters are listed as forbidden. Leaping insects with jointed legs above their feet (locust, cricket, grasshopper) are permitted; all other flying insects are unclean. Carcass contamination law: touching the carcass of an unclean animal makes a person unclean until evening; an earthen vessel that receives a carcass is broken; a spring or cistern with abundant water remains clean; dry seed is clean but wetted seed that contacts a carcass becomes unclean. The stated theological foundation: "For I am the LORD your God: ye shall therefore sanctify yourselves, and ye shall be holy; for I am holy." The stated practical purpose: "To make a difference between the unclean and the clean, and between the beast that may be eaten and the beast that may not be eaten."

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Be Holy, For I Am Holy" — The Theological Foundation

**Leviticus 11:44-45** — "For I am the LORD your God: ye shall therefore sanctify yourselves, and ye shall be holy; for I am holy... For I am the LORD that bringeth you up out of the land of Egypt, to be your God: ye shall therefore be holy, for I am holy"

- This verse is the theological root of the entire chapter — and, by extension, of the entire section of Leviticus called "the Holiness Code"
- **1 Peter 1:15-16** — "But as he which hath called you is holy, so be ye holy in all manner of conversation; Because it is written, Be ye holy; for I am holy" — Peter quotes this verse directly and applies it to New Covenant believers
- **Matthew 5:48** — "Be ye therefore perfect, even as your Father which is in heaven is perfect" — the Sermon on the Mount equivalent
- **Hebrews 12:14** — "Follow peace with all men, and holiness, without which no man shall see the Lord"
- *Interpretation:* The food laws are not first of all about nutrition or hygiene — they are about identity. God's people eat differently because God's people are different because God Himself is different from everything else. Every meal in which an Israelite made a distinction between clean and unclean was an act of theology: my daily choices reflect who my God is. Peter quotes Leviticus 11:44 without the food law context to apply the same principle to New Covenant ethical life — the motivation for holy living remains identical: I am holy; therefore you are to be holy.*

---

### The Clean/Unclean Distinction as Separation from the Nations

**Leviticus 11:44** — "neither shall ye defile yourselves with any manner of creeping thing that creepeth upon the earth"

- **Acts 10:9-15** — Peter's vision: a sheet with all kinds of animals; "What God hath cleansed, that call not thou common" — the food laws as the dividing wall between Jew and Gentile, now declared clean in Christ
- **Mark 7:18-19** — Jesus: "Do ye not perceive, that whatsoever thing from without entereth into the man, it cannot defile him... purging all meats" — the narrator's parenthetical: "In saying this, Jesus declared all foods clean" (ESV; the Greek construction supports this reading from the KJV "purging all meats")
- **Romans 14:14** — "I know, and am persuaded by the Lord Jesus, that there is nothing unclean of itself"
- **Colossians 2:16-17** — "Let no man therefore judge you in meat, or in drink... which are a shadow of things to come; but the body is of Christ"
- *Interpretation:* The food laws served their purpose in the Mosaic economy: they created a daily, embodied practice of distinction that separated Israel from the nations and taught the principle of holy/common differentiation through every meal. Their New Covenant cancellation (Acts 10; Mark 7:19; Colossians 2:17) is not a repudiation of their purpose but its completion. The wall they built to protect the seed until Christ came is removed by Christ, who is the reality they shadowed. The holiness principle they taught (1 Peter 1:15-16) is fully retained.*

---

### The Carcass Law — Contamination Spreads Outward, Cleanness Does Not

**Leviticus 11:32-38** — Unclean carcass contaminates whatever it falls into; but a spring of running water remains clean; dry seed is clean but wetted seed becomes unclean on contact

- **Haggai 2:12-13** — God uses this exact principle to make a theological point to post-exilic Israel: holiness does not spread by contact the way uncleanness does — "if one that is unclean by a dead body touch any of these, shall it be unclean? And the priests answered and said, It shall be unclean. Then said Haggai, So is this people... before me, saith the LORD"
- **John 4:14** — "the water that I shall give him shall be in him a well of water springing up into everlasting life" — the spring of living water that cannot be defiled (Leviticus 11:36: a spring or pit with plenty of water shall be clean)
- *Interpretation:* Haggai 2 explicitly interprets the carcass contamination law of Leviticus 11. The physical law teaches a spiritual principle: contamination naturally spreads; holiness does not automatically transfer. A holy garment touching an unholy meal does not make the meal holy. But an unholy corpse touching a vessel makes the vessel unclean. This is why the New Covenant requires regeneration — not proximity to the holy, but transformation by it. The spring (Leviticus 11:36) that remains clean because it has abundance of living water is the type of the believer whose inner life of the Spirit is not contaminated by external contact because the source is inexhaustible.*

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### ABOMINATION (Hebrew: sheqetz / toevah)
- **Leviticus 11:10-13** — sheqetz = something detestable, loathsome; used specifically for prohibited food and creeping things
- Distinct from toevah (also abomination) which is used for moral violations; sheqetz is for ritual category violations
- *Definition here:* The category of things that are not morally evil in themselves but are designated as incompatible with the holy status of Israel; their prohibition communicates belonging to a different order; sheqetz items are not sinful to exist but are forbidden to be consumed by those who belong to the holy God*

### UNCLEAN (Hebrew: tameh)
- **Leviticus 11:4, 7, 8** — tameh = ritually impure, defiled; a state that bars access to the sanctuary and requires cleansing procedures
- *Definition here:* A ritual state of impurity that prevents access to God's presence; the temporary or permanent condition of incompatibility with holiness; the Levitical system maintained constant awareness of this state to train Israel that access to God is never presumed — it is always prepared for*

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### Acts 10 — The Vision That Opens the Gentile Mission

Peter's vision in Acts 10 is the direct interpretation of Leviticus 11 for the New Covenant age. The sheet containing all creatures — clean and unclean — and the command "kill and eat" followed by "What God hath cleansed, that call not thou common" (Acts 10:15) is God's own declaration that the wall Leviticus 11 built between Jew and Gentile has been removed in Christ.

- **Ephesians 2:14-15** — "For he is our peace, who hath made both one, and hath broken down the middle wall of partition between us; Having abolished in his flesh the enmity, even the law of commandments contained in ordinances"

The food laws were the embodied, daily-practice form of this wall. Their cancellation is not God changing His standard — it is God declaring that the wall has done its job: it kept the seed line separate until the Seed arrived (Galatians 3:19), and now that Christ has come, the wall comes down and all nations may eat at the same table.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### Leviticus 11:7-8 — "Swine" Used to Bind New Covenant Believers to Dietary Law

**Verses 7-8** — "And the swine, though he divide the hoof, and be clovenfooted, yet he cheweth not the cud; he is unclean to you. Of their flesh shall ye not eat, and their carcase shall ye not touch; they are unclean to you."

**How it is misused:** This verse is frequently quoted to argue that Christians who eat pork are violating God's law — that the dietary laws of Leviticus 11 remain binding on New Covenant believers, and that eating forbidden foods incurs spiritual defilement.

**What the text actually says:**
- **Mark 7:18-19** — "Do ye not perceive, that whatsoever thing from without entereth into the man, it cannot defile him; Because it entereth not into his heart, but into the belly, and goeth out into the draught, purging all meats."
- **Acts 10:15** — "What God hath cleansed, that call not thou common."
- **Romans 14:14** — "I know, and am persuaded by the Lord Jesus, that there is nothing unclean of itself: but to him that esteemeth any thing to be unclean, to him it is unclean."
- **Colossians 2:16-17** — "Let no man therefore judge you in meat, or in drink... which are a shadow of things to come; but the body is of Christ."

*Correction:* The dietary laws were a shadow (Colossians 2:17) — they served as a bodily, daily-practice partition that separated Israel from the nations and taught the clean/unclean distinction until the Seed came (Galatians 3:19). Acts 10:15 is God's own declaration that He has cleansed what was formerly called common. The holiness principle the food laws taught (1 Peter 1:15-16) is fully retained in the New Covenant — but the food laws themselves are not the vehicle of that principle for New Covenant believers.

---

### Leviticus 11:44-45 — "Be Ye Holy" Divorced from Its Covenantal Context

**Verses 44-45** — "For I am the LORD your God: ye shall therefore sanctify yourselves, and ye shall be holy; for I am holy... ye shall therefore be holy, for I am holy."

**How it is misused:** This call to holiness is sometimes quoted in isolation to imply that God requires moral perfection before He will accept a person — used as a standard of exclusion rather than identity formation, or used to demand food-law observance as the content of New Covenant holiness.

**What the text actually says:**
- **1 Peter 1:15-16** — "But as he which hath called you is holy, so be ye holy in all manner of conversation; Because it is written, Be ye holy; for I am holy." — Peter quotes Leviticus 11:44 and applies it to New Covenant ethical conduct, not dietary law.
- **Hebrews 12:14** — "Follow peace with all men, and holiness, without which no man shall see the Lord."
- **Romans 6:22** — "But now being made free from sin, and become servants to God, ye have your fruit unto holiness, and the end everlasting life."

*Correction:* "Be ye holy, for I am holy" is the theological engine of Leviticus, not a dietary code. Peter quotes it directly and applies it to "all manner of conversation" — conduct, behavior, the whole pattern of life — not food. The holiness commanded here is identity-shaped ethics: because God is who He is, and His people belong to Him, their lives reflect His character. The vehicle in Leviticus was food laws; the vehicle in the New Covenant is the Spirit-formed life of 1 Peter 1:15.

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

**Clean and Unclean (Tahor / Tameh):**
- First established: Genesis 7:2 — "Of every clean beast thou shalt take to thee by sevens, the male and his female: and of beasts that are not clean by two, the male and his female." — The clean/unclean distinction precedes Moses by centuries.
- Leviticus 11:47 — "To make a difference between the unclean and the clean, and between the beast that may be eaten and the beast that may not be eaten." — The stated purpose of the entire chapter.
- Leviticus 20:25 — "Ye shall therefore put difference between clean beasts and unclean, and between unclean fowls and clean."
- Acts 10:14-15 — Peter's vision: "Not so, Lord; for I have never eaten any thing that is common or unclean. And the voice spake unto him again the second time, What God hath cleansed, that call not thou common." — God declares the clean/unclean food wall abolished.
- Revelation 21:27 — "And there shall in no wise enter into it any thing that defileth, neither whatsoever worketh abomination, or maketh a lie: but they which are written in the Lamb's book of life." — The clean/unclean distinction reaches its final form: not food categories but moral reality.
- *Pattern:* Clean/unclean is a theological category established before Moses, codified in Leviticus, used to separate Israel from the nations, cancelled as a food distinction in Acts 10, and applied in its ultimate moral sense to the New Jerusalem. The principle — that God's presence requires a qualified approach — is permanent. The food-law vehicle for that principle in Israel's economy is not.

**The Abomination (Sheqetz / Toevah):**
- Leviticus 11:10 — sheqetz applied to forbidden sea creatures; ritual category violation.
- Leviticus 18:22 — toevah applied to moral violations; stronger category.
- Proverbs 6:16-19 — "These six things doth the LORD hate: yea, seven are an abomination unto him" — a proud look, lying tongue, hands that shed innocent blood, a heart that deviseth wicked imaginations, feet swift to mischief, a false witness, and sowing discord.
- Revelation 21:27 — "neither whatsoever worketh abomination" shall enter the New Jerusalem.
- *Pattern:* Scripture uses two Hebrew terms translated "abomination." Sheqetz marks ritual category violations (Leviticus 11 food laws). Toevah marks moral violations (Leviticus 18:22; Proverbs 6:16). The distinction matters: not everything called "abomination" carries the same weight or the same permanence. Sheqetz prohibited foods belong to the ceremonial law that the NT declares fulfilled. Toevah moral violations are re-stated by Paul in Romans 1 and 1 Corinthians 6 as still operative.
