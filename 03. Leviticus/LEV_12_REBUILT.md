## CHAPTER SUMMARY

After bearing a son, the mother is unclean seven days (as in her monthly separation), then continues in the blood of her purifying for thirty-three days — forty days total before she may enter the sanctuary or touch any hallowed thing. After bearing a daughter, the periods double: two weeks of uncleanness, sixty-six days of purification — eighty days total. On the eighth day the male child is circumcised. At the end of the purification period she brings a yearling lamb for a burnt offering and a young pigeon or turtledove for a sin offering; if she cannot afford a lamb, two turtledoves or two young pigeons will serve for both. The priest makes atonement, and she is clean.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### The Law Fulfilled at Jesus' Birth

**Leviticus 12:6, 8** — The lamb or, if poor, two birds

- **Luke 2:22-24** — "And when the days of her purification according to the law of Moses were accomplished, they brought him to Jerusalem, to present him to the Lord... And to offer a sacrifice according to that which is said in the law of the Lord, A pair of turtledoves, or two young pigeons"
- Mary brought the poor-tier offering — two birds; she could not afford a lamb
- The Lamb of God, who would be the final burnt offering and sin offering, was presented at the Temple under the provision for those too poor to bring a lamb
- **Isaiah 53:9** — "he had done no violence, neither was any deceit in his mouth" — He for whom this offering was made was Himself the Lamb the offering pointed to
- *Interpretation:* Luke 2:22-24 is one of the most theologically rich moments in the gospels: the mother of the Lamb of God brings two birds because she is too poor to bring a lamb. God arranged for His own Son to enter the Temple under the provision for the poor — just as He had built into Leviticus 1 and 5 and 12 for those who had nothing. The Lamb was there; He just hadn't yet been offered.*

### Uncleanness and New Life — The Condition of Fallen Creation

**Leviticus 12:2, 5** — The birth of new life produces uncleanness in the mother

- This is not a statement that childbirth is sinful — it is a statement about the condition of the fallen world: even the most glorious act of new life in a fallen creation occurs through processes marked by mortality and blood loss
- **Psalm 51:5** — "Behold, I was shapen in iniquity; and in sin did my mother conceive me" — David is not blaming his mother; he is acknowledging that he was formed within the fallen human condition
- **Romans 8:22** — "For we know that the whole creation groaneth and travaileth in pain together until now" — the groaning of creation includes the pain and blood of childbirth (Genesis 3:16)
- **Romans 5:12** — "as by one man sin entered into the world, and death by sin; and so death passed upon all men" — the uncleanness of childbirth is a marker of the fall's reach into every new human beginning
- *Interpretation:* Leviticus 12 teaches through its laws what the New Testament states directly: every human life begins in a fallen world, and the processes of that world bear the marks of mortality. The purification rites after childbirth are not about the mother's sin or the child's sin — they are about the condition of the creation into which the child is born. The remedy (sacrifice, atonement, purification) is required not because something wicked occurred but because the fall has touched even the holiest human gifts.*

---

### The Eighth Day — Circumcision as the Sign Planted Before Purification Is Complete

**Leviticus 12:3** — "And in the eighth day the flesh of his foreskin shall be circumcised"

- The circumcision occurs on the eighth day — while the mother is still in her first period of uncleanness (the seven days complete on day 7; day 8 is the transition)
- **Genesis 17:12** — "And he that is eight days old shall be circumcised among you" — the original command; the eighth day as covenant sign
- **Colossians 2:11-12** — "In whom also ye are circumcised with the circumcision made without hands, in putting off the body of the sins of the flesh by the circumcision of Christ: Buried with him in baptism, wherein also ye are risen with him through the faith of the operation of God"
- **Philippians 3:3** — "For we are the circumcision, which worship God in the spirit, and rejoice in Christ Jesus, and have no confidence in the flesh"
- *Interpretation:* The eighth-day circumcision while the mother is still technically in the first period of her uncleanness is a statement: the covenant sign is not delayed until everything is ritually resolved. The child is marked on the eighth-day of new creation before the full purification period is complete. The New Covenant circumcision (Colossians 2:11-12) similarly does not wait for the believer to be fully purified before the covenant mark is applied — it is the act that initiates the process of purification.*

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### PURIFICATION (Hebrew: tohorot)
- **Leviticus 12:4-6** — the days of purification; the period during which the mother is in a transitional state — not fully unclean (she can be with her household) but not yet able to enter the sanctuary
- *Definition here:* The process of transitioning from a state of ritual impurity back to full participation in the covenant community; a structured, time-bound process — not indefinite — with a clear endpoint (the sacrifice) that restores full access*

## THE ACTUAL VERSES — LEVITICUS 12 (KJV)

**1** And the LORD spake unto Moses, saying,
**2** Speak unto the children of Israel, saying, If a woman have conceived seed, and born a man child: then she shall be unclean seven days; according to the days of the separation for her infirmity shall she be unclean.
**3** And in the eighth day the flesh of his foreskin shall be circumcised.
**4** And she shall then continue in the blood of her purifying three and thirty days; she shall touch no hallowed thing, nor come into the sanctuary, until the days of her purifying be fulfilled.
**5** But if she bear a maid child, then she shall be unclean two weeks, as in her separation: and she shall continue in the blood of her purifying threescore and six days.
**6** And when the days of her purifying are fulfilled, for a son, or for a daughter, she shall bring a lamb of the first year for a burnt offering, and a young pigeon, or a turtledove, for a sin offering, unto the door of the tabernacle of the congregation, unto the priest:
**7** Who shall offer it before the LORD, and make an atonement for her; and she shall be cleansed from the issue of her blood. This is the law for her that hath born a male or a female.
**8** And if she be not able to bring a lamb, then she shall bring two turtles, or two young pigeons; the one for the burnt offering, and the other for a sin offering: and the priest shall make an atonement for her, and she shall be clean.

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The 40 Days and the 80 Days

Male child: 40 days total (7 + 33). Female child: 80 days total (14 + 66).

Various rabbinic interpretations have been offered for the doubling, but the Bible's own interpretive frame is more reliable than speculation:

- **Numbers 14:33-34** — Israel wanders 40 years for 40 days of unbelieving spying — a day for a year
- **Ezekiel 4:6** — "forty days: I have appointed thee each day for a year"
- The 40/80 structure in Leviticus 12 echoes the 40-day patterns throughout Scripture (Moses on the mountain, Israel in the wilderness, Elijah's journey, Jesus' temptation) — the number of trial, testing, and preparation before entering the presence of God or the promised place

The mother's return to the sanctuary after the purification period is always through sacrifice — the same principle that governs every re-entry into God's presence after any form of uncleanness in Leviticus.
