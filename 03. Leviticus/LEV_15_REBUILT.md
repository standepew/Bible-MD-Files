## CHAPTER SUMMARY

The chapter deals with four categories of bodily discharge. A man with a chronic bodily issue (a running sore or discharge): everything he lies on, sits on, or touches transmits uncleanness; he who touches him must wash and is unclean until evening; his cleansing requires seven days of counting after the discharge stops, washing on the seventh day, and two birds on the eighth day. A male seminal emission: the man and any woman involved are unclean until evening; a garment or skin with seed must be washed; bathing in water restores cleanness by evening. A woman's monthly discharge: she is unclean seven days; her bed and seat transmit uncleanness; anyone who lies with her is unclean seven days. A woman's chronic hemorrhage (beyond the normal cycle): all days of her discharge she remains in the same state as her normal separation; after the discharge stops, seven days and then two birds on the eighth day. Summary verse: separation is required so Israel does not defile the Tabernacle that dwells among them, lest they die.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### The Woman with the Issue of Blood — Mark 5 and Luke 8

**Leviticus 15:25-30** — A woman with a chronic hemorrhage is in a perpetual state of uncleanness as long as it continues

- **Mark 5:25-34; Luke 8:43-48** — "And a woman having an issue of blood twelve years, which had spent all her living upon physicians, neither could be healed of any, came behind him, and touched the border of his garment: and immediately her issue of blood stanched"
- Under Leviticus 15 this woman was perpetually unclean for twelve years; everything she touched became unclean; she could not enter the synagogue or Tabernacle; she was in social and spiritual exile
- She touches Jesus — by Levitical law her touch makes Him unclean; instead, His virtue flows into her and makes her clean
- **Luke 8:47** — She "declared unto him before all the people for what cause she had touched him, and how she was healed immediately" — a public declaration after twelve years of silence; the cry of "Unclean, unclean" reversed
- *Interpretation:* Mark 5 / Luke 8 is the direct New Covenant application of Leviticus 15. The woman whose condition made everything she touched unclean for twelve years touches Christ. The flow is reversed: instead of her uncleanness contaminating Him, His wholeness cleanses her. This is the principle of the New Covenant at its sharpest point: Christ does not become unclean by contact with the unclean — He makes them clean. The law of Leviticus 15 could only describe and quarantine her condition; Christ heals it.*

### The Tabernacle Among Them — The Reason for All Purity Laws

**Leviticus 15:31** — "Thus shall ye separate the children of Israel from their uncleanness; that they die not in their uncleanness, when they defile my tabernacle that is among them"

- The entire elaborate purity system has one stated purpose: the Tabernacle is in the middle of the camp; God is resident; the people must not bring their uncleanness into proximity with His holiness, lest they die
- **1 Corinthians 6:19-20** — "What? know ye not that your body is the temple of the Holy Ghost which is in you, which ye have of God, and ye are not your own? For ye are bought with a price: therefore glorify God in your body, and in your spirit, which are God's"
- **1 Corinthians 3:16-17** — "Know ye not that ye are the temple of God, and that the Spirit of God dwelleth in you? If any man defile the temple of God, him shall God destroy; for the temple of God is holy, which temple ye are"
- **Ephesians 4:30** — "And grieve not the holy Spirit of God, whereby ye are sealed unto the day of redemption"
- *Interpretation:* The purpose statement of Leviticus 15:31 is the New Covenant's driving concern in 1 Corinthians 6. Israel's Tabernacle was external — the purity laws governed what could come near the dwelling. The New Covenant Tabernacle is internal — the Spirit dwells in the believer. The purity concern therefore intensifies rather than diminishes: the believer's body itself is the sanctuary, and what defiles the body defiles the temple where the Spirit dwells.*

---

### The Eighth-Day Atonement After Discharge — the Constant Return

**Leviticus 15:14-15, 29-30** — Both the man and woman with chronic discharges bring two birds on the eighth day; the priest makes atonement before the LORD for the issue of uncleanness

- The same eighth-day new-beginning pattern after seven days of cleansing
- The atonement is not for a sin committed but for a state of uncleanness in a fallen body
- **Hebrews 10:19-22** — "Having therefore, brethren, boldness to enter into the holiest by the blood of Jesus, By a new and living way, which he hath consecrated for us, through the veil... Let us draw near with a true heart in full assurance of faith, having our hearts sprinkled from an evil conscience, and our bodies washed with pure water"
- *Interpretation:* The repeated two-bird offerings for those with chronic conditions reflect the ongoing nature of the problem: in a fallen world, in fallen bodies, uncleanness is not a one-time issue resolved by a single sacrifice. The New Covenant equivalent is not a repeated sacrifice — Christ's one offering is sufficient — but the ongoing application of His blood through the confession of 1 John 1:9 and the continual access of Hebrews 10:19-22.*

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### ISSUE (Hebrew: zov)
- **Leviticus 15:2** — a running flow, discharge; the condition that makes the person a transmitter of uncleanness to everything they contact
- *Definition here:* A chronic bodily discharge that places the person in ongoing ritual uncleanness; the condition Jesus encounters and reverses in Mark 5; the type of the spiritual condition that bars access to God and spreads through contact — and which only Christ can arrest and heal*

### SEPARATION (Hebrew: niddah)
- **Leviticus 15:19-24** — the woman's monthly separation; the period during which sexual relations are forbidden and uncleanness is transmitted through bed and seat
- *Definition here:* The enforced withdrawal from certain forms of contact with others during a period of ritual uncleanness; not a permanent exclusion but a temporary separation with a defined end; the principle that the condition of fallen human physicality requires periodic acknowledgment and temporary withdrawal from sacred things*

**30** And the priest shall offer the one for a sin offering, and the other for a burnt offering; and the priest shall make an atonement for her before the LORD for the issue of her uncleanness.

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### Leviticus 15 and the New Temple of the Holy Spirit

The purity system of Leviticus 15 was designed to protect the external Tabernacle from defilement. The New Covenant makes every believer's body a temple of the Spirit (1 Corinthians 6:19). This does not abolish the principle — it intensifies it. Paul's sexual ethics in 1 Corinthians 6:13-20 are not a departure from Leviticus 15; they are Leviticus 15 applied to the body as the new temple.

- Leviticus 15 governed what the body could not bring near the external Tabernacle
- 1 Corinthians 6:19-20 governs what the body must not bring into the internal sanctuary

The logic is identical; the location of the sanctuary has changed. This is why Paul, when confronting sexual immorality in the Corinthian church, does not appeal to a new ethic — he appeals to temple theology: "Know ye not that your body is the temple of the Holy Ghost?"
