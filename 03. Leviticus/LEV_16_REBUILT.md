## CHAPTER SUMMARY

The Day of Atonement is instituted immediately after the deaths of Nadab and Abihu. The instructions are precisely how Aaron may come near the Most Holy Place without dying. He must not come at all times — only on this day, once a year. He puts on the simple linen garments (not the full glorious vestments). For himself: a bull sin offering and a ram burnt offering. For the people: two male goats and a ram. He casts lots over the two goats: one for the LORD, one for Azazel (the scapegoat). Aaron kills the bull, takes a censer of altar coals and incense into the Most Holy Place — the incense cloud covers the mercy seat. He sprinkles the bull's blood on and before the mercy seat seven times. He kills the goat for the LORD, brings its blood in, does the same. He then cleanses the altar with combined blood. He confesses all Israel's iniquities over the live goat, laying both hands on its head, and sends it into the wilderness with a designated man. He bathes, changes back into his regular garments, then offers the two burnt offerings. The attendants burn the sin offering carcasses outside the camp and bathe before returning. This is to be done once a year, forever. On the tenth day of the seventh month: no work, afflict your souls — a Sabbath of complete rest.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### The Two Goats — The Double Work of Atonement

**Leviticus 16:8-10, 20-22** — One goat killed for the LORD; one goat laden with sins sent into the wilderness

- **Isaiah 53:4-6, 11-12** — "Surely he hath borne our griefs, and carried our sorrows... the LORD hath laid on him the iniquity of us all... he bare the sin of many" — both dimensions in one person: the bearing (goat for the LORD = blood sacrifice) and the carrying away (scapegoat = removal)
- **Psalm 103:12** — "As far as the east is from the west, so far hath he removed our transgressions from us" — the scapegoat principle expressed; the sins are not merely covered but sent to a land uninhabited
- **Micah 7:19** — "he will cast all their sins into the depths of the sea"
- **Hebrews 9:26** — "but now once in the end of the world hath he appeared to put away sin by the sacrifice of himself" — "put away" = the scapegoat action; sin not merely covered but removed
- **Romans 4:25** — "Who was delivered for our offences, and was raised again for our justification" — the two movements; crucifixion (goat for the LORD) and resurrection (the living goat that carries sins away and goes into newness)
- *Interpretation:* The two goats cannot be separated from one another: one alone is insufficient to show what Christ did. The killed goat shows the price of sin — blood shed before God, on the mercy seat. The scapegoat shows the destination of sin — carried away to a place of no return, never to be brought back. Together they declare: the penalty is paid AND the record is cleared. Romans 4:25 is the New Covenant two-goat theology: delivered (killed goat) and raised (scapegoat's life going out carrying sin away into a land not inhabited).*

### The Incense Cloud — No One Sees the Mercy Seat Directly

**Leviticus 16:12-13** — "He shall put the incense upon the fire before the LORD, that the cloud of the incense may cover the mercy seat that is upon the testimony, that he die not"

- The high priest cannot look at the mercy seat directly — the incense cloud is a screen between the priest and the full, unmediated glory
- **Exodus 33:20** — "Thou canst not see my face: for there shall no man see me, and live"
- **1 Timothy 6:16** — "Who only hath immortality, dwelling in the light which no man can approach unto; whom no man hath seen, nor can see"
- **Hebrews 10:19-22** — "Having therefore, brethren, boldness to enter into the holiest by the blood of Jesus, By a new and living way... Let us draw near" — what the incense cloud only screened from Aaron, the blood of Christ permanently resolves
- **Revelation 8:3-4** — "And another angel came and stood at the altar, having a golden censer; and there was given unto him much incense, that he should offer it with the prayers of all saints upon the golden altar which was before the throne" — the eschatological incense offering; incense as the prayers of the saints before the throne
- *Interpretation:* The incense cloud in Leviticus 16 is protection for the priest: a screen of fragrance between fallen humanity and the undiluted presence of the holy God. Without it, even Aaron dies. Hebrews 10:19-22 is the New Covenant answer: the blood of Jesus creates a "new and living way" through the veil — we are not screened from the presence; we are invited in. What incense only obscured from view, Christ's blood has resolved into open access.*

### "No Man in the Tabernacle When He Goes In"

**Leviticus 16:17** — "And there shall be no man in the tabernacle of the congregation when he goeth in to make an atonement in the holy place, until he come out"

- The high priest enters alone — no assistant, no other priest; the work cannot be shared or witnessed from within
- **Isaiah 63:3** — "I have trodden the winepress alone; and of the people there was none with me" — the Servant doing the work alone
- **Hebrews 9:11-12** — "But Christ being come an high priest of good things to come, by a greater and more perfect tabernacle, not made with hands... by his own blood he entered in once into the holy place" — Christ enters alone, with His own blood
- **John 16:32** — "Behold, the hour cometh, yea, is now come, that ye shall be scattered, every man to his own, and shall leave me alone"
- *Interpretation:* The Levitical requirement of absolute solitude in the Most Holy Place is the type of Christ's solitary atoning work. No one could stand with Him in Gethsemane (the disciples slept), and no one could stand with Him on the cross as a fellow sacrifice. The work of atonement is inherently solitary — it is one person's offering for others. Isaiah 63:3 is the prophetic expression: "I have trodden the winepress alone." Leviticus 16:17 is the law that enacts it.*

### Both Hands on the Scapegoat — The Full Weight of All Sins

**Leviticus 16:21** — "And Aaron shall lay both his hands upon the head of the live goat, and confess over him all the iniquities of the children of Israel, and all their transgressions in all their sins"

- Both hands — not one (individual offerings used one hand or the laying of one hand); both hands for the entire nation
- Three categories: iniquities (avon = moral perversity), transgressions (pesha = willful rebellion), sins (chatta'at = missing the mark)
- All three comprehensively cover the entire range of human failure
- **1 John 1:9** — "If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us our sins, and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness" — the New Covenant equivalent of the Day of Atonement confession
- **Isaiah 53:6** — "and the LORD hath laid on him the iniquity of us all" — the divine two-handed laying upon the Son
- *Interpretation:* The both-hands posture for the national confession signals the comprehensiveness of what is being transferred. This is not a partial atonement, not atonement for some sins only, not atonement for this year's known sins only — it is "all the iniquities of the children of Israel, and all their transgressions in all their sins." The scapegoat carries everything. Isaiah 53:6 translates this into the New Covenant: "the LORD hath laid on him the iniquity of us all" — God performs the both-hands transfer on His own Son.*

### "Afflict Your Souls" — The Posture of the Day

**Leviticus 16:29, 31** — "ye shall afflict your souls... It shall be a sabbath of rest unto you, and ye shall afflict your souls, by a statute for ever"

- Hebrew: anah nefesh = to humble, afflict, fast with the soul; the term used for self-denial and fasting
- This is the only annual fast commanded in the Torah
- **Joel 2:12-13** — "Turn ye even to me with all your heart, and with fasting, and with weeping, and with mourning: And rend your heart, and not your garments, and turn unto the LORD your God"
- **Isaiah 58:5** — the false fast vs. the true fast; afflicting the soul that pleases God vs. external performance
- **Luke 18:14** — "every one that exalteth himself shall be abased; and he that humbleth himself shall be exalted" — the posture required on the Day of Atonement is the posture required before God at all times
- *Interpretation:* The affliction of the soul on Yom Kippur is not self-punishment — it is the whole-person acknowledgment that you have nothing to bring before God except your need. The Sabbath of rest (no work) combined with the affliction of the soul (fasting) together declare: I am not working my way to atonement, and I am not pretending I have no need of it. Both postures together — rest and affliction — are the Day of Atonement's theological statement about the human condition before a holy God.*

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### DAY OF ATONEMENT (Hebrew: Yom Kippur)
- **Leviticus 16:29-34** — Yom = day; Kippur from kaphar = to cover, atone; the one day of the year when atonement is made for the entire nation, the priests, and the sanctuary itself
- *Definition here:* The climactic annual event of the Levitical calendar; the day when all accumulated sin of the past year is dealt with comprehensively; the type of the single, unrepeatable atonement accomplished by Christ (Hebrews 9:26 — "once in the end of the world"); the day that all prior daily sacrifices pointed toward and which itself pointed forward to the cross*

### SCAPEGOAT (Hebrew: Azazel)
- **Leviticus 16:8, 10, 26** — the goat upon which the lot fell "for Azazel"; sent into the wilderness carrying all Israel's sins
- The meaning of Azazel is debated; the most natural reading from context: the goat goes "for dismissal/removal" into a land cut off — a remote, uninhabited place
- *Definition here:* The goat that bears and carries away the sins that have been confessed and transferred to it; the symbol of the complete removal of forgiven sin — not merely covered but sent beyond recovery; Psalm 103:12 ("as far as the east is from the west") is the best scriptural gloss on Azazel*

## THE ACTUAL VERSES — LEVITICUS 16 (KJV)

**1** And the LORD spake unto Moses after the death of the two sons of Aaron, when they offered before the LORD, and died;
**2** And the LORD said unto Moses, Speak unto Aaron thy brother, that he come not at all times into the holy place within the vail before the mercy seat, which is upon the ark; that he die not: for I will appear in the cloud upon the mercy seat.
**3** Thus shall Aaron come into the holy place: with a young bullock for a sin offering, and a ram for a burnt offering.
**4** He shall put on the holy linen coat, and he shall have the linen breeches upon his flesh, and shall be girded with a linen girdle, and with the linen mitre shall he be attired: these are holy garments; therefore shall he wash his flesh in water, and so put them on.
**5** And he shall take of the congregation of the children of Israel two kids of the goats for a sin offering, and one ram for a burnt offering.
**6** And Aaron shall offer his bullock of the sin offering, which is for himself, and make an atonement for himself, and for his house.
**7** And he shall take the two goats, and present them before the LORD at the door of the tabernacle of the congregation.
**8** And Aaron shall cast lots upon the two goats; one lot for the LORD, and the other lot for the scapegoat.
**9** And Aaron shall bring the goat upon which the LORD's lot fell, and offer him for a sin offering.
**10** But the goat, on which the lot fell to be the scapegoat, shall be presented alive before the LORD, to make an atonement with him, and to let him go for a scapegoat into the wilderness.
**11** And Aaron shall bring the bullock of the sin offering, which is for himself, and shall make an atonement for himself, and for his house, and shall kill the bullock of the sin offering which is for himself:
**12** And he shall take a censer full of burning coals of fire from off the altar before the LORD, and his hands full of sweet incense beaten small, and bring it within the vail:
**13** And he shall put the incense upon the fire before the LORD, that the cloud of the incense may cover the mercy seat that is upon the testimony, that he die not:
**14** And he shall take of the blood of the bullock, and sprinkle it with his finger upon the mercy seat eastward; and before the mercy seat shall he sprinkle of the blood with his finger seven times.
**15** Then shall he kill the goat of the sin offering, that is for the people, and bring his blood within the vail, and do with that blood as he did with the blood of the bullock, and sprinkle it upon the mercy seat, and before the mercy seat:
**16** And he shall make an atonement for the holy place, because of the uncleanness of the children of Israel, and because of their transgressions in all their sins: and so shall he do for the tabernacle of the congregation, that remaineth among them in the midst of their uncleanness.
**17** And there shall be no man in the tabernacle of the congregation when he goeth in to make an atonement in the holy place, until he come out, and have made an atonement for himself, and for his household, and for all the congregation of Israel.
**18** And he shall go out unto the altar that is before the LORD, and make an atonement for it; and shall take of the blood of the bullock, and of the blood of the goat, and put it upon the horns of the altar round about.
**19** And he shall sprinkle of the blood upon it with his finger seven times, and cleanse it, and hallow it from the uncleanness of the children of Israel.
**20** And when he hath made an end of reconciling the holy place, and the tabernacle of the congregation, and the altar, he shall bring the live goat:
**21** And Aaron shall lay both his hands upon the head of the live goat, and confess over him all the iniquities of the children of Israel, and all their transgressions in all their sins, putting them upon the head of the goat, and shall send him away by the hand of a fit man into the wilderness:
**22** And the goat shall bear upon him all their iniquities unto a land not inhabited: and he shall let go the goat in the wilderness.
**23** And Aaron shall come into the tabernacle of the congregation, and shall put off the linen garments, which he put on when he went into the holy place, and shall leave them there:
**24** And he shall wash his flesh with water in the holy place, and put on his garments, and come forth, and offer his burnt offering, and the burnt offering of the people, and make an atonement for himself, and for the people.
**25** And the fat of the sin offering shall he burn upon the altar.
**26** And he that let go the goat for the scapegoat shall wash his clothes, and bathe his flesh in water, and afterward come into the camp.
**27** And the bullock for the sin offering, and the goat for the sin offering, whose blood was brought in to make atonement in the holy place, shall one carry forth without the camp; and they shall burn in the fire their skins, and their flesh, and their dung.
**28** And he that burneth them shall wash his clothes, and bathe his flesh in water, and afterward he shall come into the camp.
**29** And this shall be a statute for ever unto you: that in the seventh month, on the tenth day of the month, ye shall afflict your souls, and do no work at all, whether it be one of your own country, or a stranger that sojourneth among you:
**30** For on that day shall the priest make an atonement for you, to cleanse you, that ye may be clean from all your sins before the LORD.
**31** It shall be a sabbath of rest unto you, and ye shall afflict your souls, by a statute for ever.
**32** And the priest, whom he shall anoint, and whom he shall consecrate to minister in the priest's office in his father's stead, shall make the atonement, and shall put on the linen clothes, even the holy garments:
**33** And he shall make an atonement for the holy sanctuary, and he shall make an atonement for the tabernacle of the congregation, and for the altar, and he shall make an atonement for the priests, and for all the people of the congregation.
**34** And this shall be an everlasting statute unto you, to make an atonement for the children of Israel for all their sins once a year. And he did as the LORD commanded Moses.

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### Hebrews 9 — The Full Theological Commentary on Leviticus 16

The entire ninth chapter of Hebrews is the Spirit-inspired interpretation of Leviticus 16:

- **Hebrews 9:7** — the high priest entering once a year with blood = Leviticus 16:14-15
- **Hebrews 9:12** — "by his own blood he entered in once into the holy place, having obtained eternal redemption" — not animal blood once a year; His own blood once forever
- **Hebrews 9:22** — "without shedding of blood is no remission" = the entire two-goat structure
- **Hebrews 9:24** — "Christ is not entered into the holy places made with hands... but into heaven itself, now to appear in the presence of God for us" — the true Most Holy Place, not behind an earthly veil
- **Hebrews 9:26** — "but now once in the end of the world hath he appeared to put away sin by the sacrifice of himself" — scapegoat and killed goat in one unrepeatable act
- **Hebrews 10:1-4** — the repeated annual sacrifice was evidence of what it could not do; Christ's single sacrifice did what all of them together could not

*Leviticus 16 is the theological summit of the entire Levitical system. Everything in chapters 1-15 leads here. And Hebrews 9-10 is God's own commentary: the summit of the old system is the base camp of the new. Where Aaron entered once a year and trembled, the believer is invited to "draw near with a true heart in full assurance of faith" (Hebrews 10:22) — because the real Day of Atonement has happened, once, in history, and it holds forever.*
