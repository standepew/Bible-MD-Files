## CHAPTER SUMMARY

All slaughter of ox, lamb, or goat — whether intended as sacrifice or ordinary food — must be brought to the Tabernacle door. The stated reason: Israel has been going whoring after se'irim (goat-demons) in the open field; centralizing sacrifice at the altar eliminates the opportunity for unauthorized worship. Any slaughter outside the Tabernacle incurs bloodguilt — "blood shall be imputed unto that man." Then the foundational declaration: blood is not to be eaten by any person — native, stranger, anyone — because the life of the flesh is in the blood, and God has given the blood upon the altar as the instrument of atonement for human souls. The prohibition is absolute; violation means being cut off from the people. When game animals are hunted (they are not sacrificial), the blood must be poured out and covered with dust — acknowledging that even the blood of non-sacrificial animals belongs to God as the carrier of life. Eating a naturally dead animal or a torn animal makes a person unclean until evening; if no washing occurs, they bear their iniquity.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "The Life of the Flesh Is in the Blood" — The Foundation of All Atonement Theology

**Leviticus 17:11** — "For the life of the flesh is in the blood: and I have given it to you upon the altar to make an atonement for your souls: for it is the blood that maketh an atonement for the soul"

This is the most important single verse in Leviticus for understanding why the sacrificial system exists at all:
- The blood carries the life (nefesh = life/soul)
- God gave the blood to Israel for a specific purpose: as the atonement medium on the altar
- The blood makes atonement because the life is in it — one life poured out covers another life

- **Genesis 9:4** — "But flesh with the life thereof, which is the blood thereof, shall ye not eat" — the prohibition predates Moses; the principle is as old as Noah
- **Hebrews 9:22** — "And almost all things are by the law purged with blood; and without shedding of blood is no remission" — the direct New Covenant commentary on Leviticus 17:11
- **Hebrews 9:12** — "by his own blood he entered in once into the holy place, having obtained eternal redemption for us" — the life (blood) of the Son of God poured out; infinite life for infinite atonement
- **1 John 1:7** — "the blood of Jesus Christ his Son cleanseth us from all sin" — the present continuous tense: the blood keeps on cleansing
- **Revelation 12:11** — "And they overcame him by the blood of the Lamb, and by the word of their testimony" — the eschatological application of Leviticus 17:11; the blood of the Lamb remains the instrument of overcoming
- *Interpretation:* Leviticus 17:11 is the theological engine of the entire sacrificial system. Every burnt offering, sin offering, peace offering, and trespass offering from Leviticus 1-16 flows from this verse: life must be given for life; the blood that carries life must be shed; God alone decides what blood is sufficient for atonement; He designed the altar as the place where His ordained blood meets the soul's need. Hebrews 9:22 draws the straight line from Leviticus 17:11 to the cross: the blood that "maketh an atonement" is ultimately the blood of the one whose life is infinite, whose atonement therefore covers infinitely.*

### No Sacrifice to Se'irim — The Demonic Alternative

**Leviticus 17:7** — "And they shall no more offer their sacrifices unto devils (se'irim), after whom they have gone a whoring. This shall be a statute for ever unto them throughout their generations"

- Se'irim = literally "hairy ones, goat-demons"; the demonic entities behind Canaanite/Egyptian worship involving goats
- **1 Corinthians 10:20** — "But I say, that the things which the Gentiles sacrifice, they sacrifice to devils, and not to God: and I would not that ye should have fellowship with devils" — Paul applies this principle directly to Greek idol worship
- **1 Corinthians 10:21** — "Ye cannot drink the cup of the Lord, and the cup of devils: ye cannot be partakers of the Lord's table, and of the table of devils"
- **Deuteronomy 32:17** — "They sacrificed unto devils, not to God; to gods whom they knew not, to new gods that came newly up, whom your fathers feared not" — the Mosaic context for what Israel was prone to do
- *Interpretation:* Centralizing sacrifice at the Tabernacle was not merely about priestly control — it was about cutting off the channel through which Israelites were feeding demonic entities with blood. Blood offered in the open field did not go to no one; it went to the se'irim. 1 Corinthians 10:20 confirms that this principle operates in the New Covenant: sacrifice offered to idols is not offered to nothing — it is offered to demonic beings who receive it. The altar of God and the table of demons are mutually exclusive.*

### Acts 15 — The Blood Prohibition in the New Covenant

**Leviticus 17:10-14** — The prohibition on eating blood applies to stranger and native alike; "I will even set my face against that soul that eateth blood"

- **Acts 15:20, 29** — The Jerusalem Council retains this prohibition as one of only four requirements for Gentile believers: "abstain from blood"
- **Acts 15:21** — The reason given: "For Moses of old time hath in every city them that preach him, being read in the synagogues every sabbath day" — the blood prohibition is known everywhere; Gentile believers must not give offense by violating what is universally recognized
- *Interpretation:* The Jerusalem Council's retention of the blood prohibition is not a partial continuation of Mosaic law in the New Covenant — it is the recognition that Leviticus 17:11 states a theological principle that does not expire: blood is the medium of atonement; to consume it casually is to treat the medium of redemption as food. The prohibition that was grounded in "the life of the flesh is in the blood" does not become untrue when Christ sheds His blood. It becomes more true. The believer who abstains from blood is enacting the same theology that Leviticus 17 taught: this is not yours to consume; it belongs to God as the price of atonement.*

### Pouring Out Game Blood — Every Life Belongs to God

**Leviticus 17:13** — "which hunteth and catcheth any beast or fowl that may be eaten; he shall even pour out the blood thereof, and cover it with dust"

- Game animals are not brought to the altar — but their blood still cannot be eaten; it must be poured out and covered with earth
- **Genesis 4:10** — "the voice of thy brother's blood crieth unto me from the ground" — blood poured on the earth is not silent before God; it speaks
- **Deuteronomy 12:16, 24** — "Only ye shall not eat the blood; ye shall pour it upon the earth as water" — the same principle in the Promised Land context
- *Interpretation:* Even the blood of a hunted deer is covered with dust — not burned on the altar, but returned to the earth and acknowledged as belonging to God. This is the extension of the Leviticus 17:11 principle beyond the sanctuary and into daily life: every life, even non-sacrificial animal life, carries the blood that the Creator has designated as sacred. The covering of the blood with dust is a daily act of theology: I acknowledge that this life, and all life, belongs to its Maker.*

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### LIFE / SOUL (Hebrew: nefesh)
- **Leviticus 17:11** — "the life (nefesh) of the flesh is in the blood" — nefesh = the living being, the soul, the animating life
- *Definition here:* The living principle that animates flesh; located in the blood; given by God; returned to God through the pouring out of blood; the reason blood is never to be treated as ordinary food — because blood = life = what God gives and what God receives in atonement*

### ATONEMENT (Hebrew: kaphar)
- **Leviticus 17:11** — "the blood that maketh an atonement (yekhapper) for the soul" — the mechanism: blood covers / wipes away guilt
- *Definition here:* See Leviticus 1:4; here Leviticus 17:11 provides the theological rationale that was not explained in chapter 1: it works because life covers life; the shed blood of the substitute covers the forfeited life of the sinner; the mechanism is life-for-life, not merely ritual-for-guilt*

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### Leviticus 17:11 as the Theological Spine of the New Testament

Leviticus 17:11 is cited, applied, and assumed throughout the New Testament. It is not the background to the gospel — it is the grammar of the gospel:

- **Hebrews 9:22** — "without shedding of blood is no remission" = Leviticus 17:11 in its minimal negative form
- **Hebrews 9:12** — "by his own blood" = the same blood principle, now applied to the life of the Son of God
- **John 6:53-56** — "Except ye eat the flesh of the Son of man, and drink his blood, ye have no life in you... for my flesh is meat indeed, and my blood is drink indeed" — Jesus applies Leviticus 17:11 in reverse: where the Law said the blood carries life and must not be consumed, Jesus says His blood carries life and must be received by faith; the type inverts into antitype
- **1 Peter 1:18-19** — "ye were not redeemed with corruptible things, as silver and gold... But with the precious blood of Christ, as of a lamb without blemish"
- **Revelation 12:11** — "they overcame him by the blood of the Lamb"

Every New Testament reference to Christ's blood as the instrument of redemption is an application of Leviticus 17:11. The verse is not merely background context — it is the explanatory principle without which the cross cannot be understood. Why blood? Because life is in the blood. Why Christ's blood? Because His life is infinite and therefore His blood covers infinitely.
