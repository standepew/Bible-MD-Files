## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Leviticus 20 attaches penalties to the prohibitions of Leviticus 18. Molech worship (child sacrifice): death by stoning; if the community hides its eyes, God will set His face against that man AND his family AND all who follow after him. Familiar spirits and wizards: death by stoning. Then the positive call: "Sanctify yourselves therefore, and be ye holy: for I am the LORD your God." Cursing father or mother: death. Adultery: both parties die. Father's wife: death. Daughter-in-law: death. Man with man: death. A man and her mother together: burnt with fire. Bestiality: death for the person and the animal. Incest with sister: cut off. Intercourse during the woman's separation: cut off. Aunt or uncle's wife: they shall bear their iniquity, die childless. Brother's wife: they shall be childless. Summary: keep God's statutes; do not walk in the manners of the nations being cast out; God has separated Israel from other peoples to be His; put difference between clean and unclean; be holy to God for He is holy and has severed Israel from other peoples that they should be His. Wizard and familiar spirit: death by stoning.

- *Interpretation: Leviticus 20:4-5 establishes corporate accountability. The community that looks the other way when Molech-worship occurs bears guilt alongside the worshipper. The New Covenant equivalent is 1 Corinthians 5: the congregation that tolerates what must not be tolerated is itself under judgment. Tolerance of covenant-violating behaviour is not mercy — it is participation in the violation.*

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### "I WILL SET MY FACE AGAINST" (Hebrew: natan panai b-)
- **Leviticus 20:3, 5, 6** — the idiom of active divine opposition; God turning His face toward a person in hostility rather than favour
- *Definition here: The reversal of the Aaronic benediction ("The LORD make his face shine upon thee" — Numbers 6:25). When God's face shines, there is blessing; when He sets His face against, there is the active withdrawal of blessing and the active presence of judgment. This is not mere displeasure but directed covenantal opposition.*

### BLOOD SHALL BE UPON THEM (Hebrew: damam al-)
- **Leviticus 20:9, 11, 12, etc.** — the guilt of the death-worthy deed rests on the one who committed it
- *Definition here: A legal formula declaring that the blood (the life forfeited by the capital crime) belongs to the offender — the community that executes judgment is not guilty of bloodshed; the offender's own guilt is upon their own head. The formula releases the community from blood-guilt for carrying out the sentence.*

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### Leviticus 20 and Matthew 27:25 — "His Blood Be On Us"

The phrase "their blood shall be upon them" (vv.9, 11, 12, 13, 16, 27) runs through Leviticus 20 as the formula of self-assumed guilt. When the crowd at Pilate's court says "His blood be on us, and on our children" (Matthew 27:25), they are using the Leviticus formula — consciously or not — to take the blood-guilt of Christ's death upon themselves.

The irony is complete: in Leviticus 20, "blood shall be upon them" is declared over those who commit capital crimes against God's covenant. At the crucifixion, those words are spoken by the crowd over themselves regarding the one person in human history who committed no crime whatsoever. They took the formula of covenant guilt and applied it to the death of the one in whom there was no guilt.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES PASSAGES

### Leviticus 20:13 — Used to Justify Violence Against Homosexual People

**The misuse:** "They shall surely be put to death" is sometimes cited as a command for individual believers today to personally execute judgment on homosexual persons.

**What the text actually says:** The judicial penalties of Leviticus 20 were civil penalties for the theocratic nation of Israel under the Mosaic covenant administered by the community ("the people of the land" — v.2, 4). They are not self-executing commands given to individuals.

**The correction from within Scripture:**
- **John 8:7** — Jesus to those about to stone the woman taken in adultery: "He that is without sin among you, let him first cast a stone at her" — Jesus does not abolish the principle but redirects who may execute it
- **Romans 12:19** — "Vengeance is mine; I will repay, saith the Lord" — judgment is God's, delegated to governing authorities (Romans 13:4), not to private individuals
- **1 Corinthians 6:9-11** — "such were some of you: but ye are washed, but ye are sanctified, but ye are justified" — the New Covenant response is the gospel, not the stone

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### The Land That Vomiteth — The Earth's Moral Sensitivity

[[03. Leviticus/LEV_20_REBUILT|Leviticus 20:22]] — "that the land, whither I bring you to dwell therein, spue you not out" (same image as Leviticus 18:25, 28)

This image of the land vomiting its inhabitants runs through Scripture as a symbol of creation's moral responsiveness:

- [[01. Genesis/GEN_04_REBUILT|Genesis 4:10-12]] — the ground receives Abel's blood and is cursed; it "hath opened her mouth to receive thy brother's blood"
- **Leviticus 18:25, 28; 20:22** — the land vomits those who defile it
- [[23. Isaiah/ISAIAH_24_REBUILT|Isaiah 24:4-6]] — "The earth mourneth and fadeth away... The earth also is defiled under the inhabitants thereof; because they have transgressed the laws"
- [[45. Romans/ROMANS_08_REBUILT|Romans 8:22]] — "For we know that the whole creation groaneth and travaileth in pain together until now" — creation's groaning under human sin
- [[66. Revelation/REVELATION_11_REBUILT|Revelation 11:18]] — "shouldest destroy them which destroy the earth"

**What the Bible establishes:** The land/creation is not a passive stage for human history. It has a covenant relationship with its Creator and a moral responsiveness to human sin. The land that bears Molech-fire, adultery, and blood-guilt builds up a debt that it eventually pays — not by legislation but by expulsion. This is the Bible's own explanation for the fall of nations: not merely military defeat or economic collapse but the land doing what creation does when the weight of iniquity reaches its limit.
