## CHAPTER SUMMARY

God commands that the priests handle the holy things only when ceremonially clean; any priest who approaches the holy things while unclean shall be cut off. The categories of uncleanness that disqualify a priest from eating the holy food are detailed. No outsider (stranger, sojourner, hired servant) may eat the holy things. The priest's daughter who has married a non-priest may not eat; but if she returns to her father's house as a widow or divorcée without children, she may eat again. If someone eats holy food by mistake they must restore it plus a fifth. Then God addresses the quality of sacrificial animals: no blind, broken, maimed, diseased animal may be offered — not even to fulfil a vow. A freewill offering may include a slightly imperfect animal (something superfluous or lacking in its parts) but not for a vow. Nothing bruised, crushed, broken, or cut may be offered. Foreign-source animals with blemishes are not accepted. Newborn animals must remain seven days with the mother before being eligible for offering — on the eighth day they may be accepted. A cow or ewe and her young may not be killed on the same day. The thanksgiving offering must be eaten on the day it is offered. Summary: "I will be hallowed among the children of Israel: I am the LORD which hallow you."

### God Will Not Receive the Defective — Malachi's Commentary

**Leviticus 22:20-25** — No blemished animal may be offered

- **Malachi 1:8** — "And if ye offer the blind for sacrifice, is it not evil? and if ye offer the lame and sick, is it not evil? offer it now unto thy governor; will he be pleased with thee, or accept thy person? saith the LORD of hosts" — Malachi directly interprets Leviticus 22; the offering you wouldn't give to a human official, you are offering to God
- **Malachi 1:14** — "But cursed be the deceiver, which hath in his flock a male, and voweth, and sacrificeth unto the Lord a corrupt thing: for I am a great King, saith the LORD of hosts, and my name is dreadful among the heathen"
- **Romans 12:1** — "present your bodies a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable unto God" — the New Covenant equivalent: what is presented to God must be the best, not the leftovers
- *Interpretation: Malachi 1 is the prophetic commentary on Leviticus 22. The post-exilic priests were offering what the Law explicitly forbade, and Malachi exposes it with God's own logic: you wouldn't insult a governor this way — why do you insult the King of kings? The blemish prohibition is not about animal health; it is about God's honour.*

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### FREEWILL OFFERING vs. VOW (Hebrew: nedavah vs. neder)
- **Leviticus 22:18-23** — a freewill offering (nedavah) may include a minor imperfection (something superfluous or lacking); a vow offering (neder) must be without blemish
- *Definition here: A freewill offering is spontaneous generosity — God receives it graciously even if not perfect. A vow is a sworn commitment — what you promised, you must deliver without reduction. Numbers 30:2 and Ecclesiastes 5:4-5 apply the vow principle: "When thou vowest a vow unto God, defer not to pay it... Better is it that thou shouldest not vow, than that thou shouldest vow and not pay."*

### HALLOWED (Hebrew: qadash — Niphal: to be made holy / to show oneself holy)
- **Leviticus 22:32** — "I will be hallowed" — the Niphal (passive/reflexive) form: God causes Himself to be shown as holy through what happens at the altar
- *Definition here: God's holiness is not merely an attribute He possesses — it is something that must be manifested, displayed, shown. The offering brought to the altar either hallows God's name (by reflecting His worth) or profanes it (by suggesting He can be satisfied with what a governor would reject). "Hallowed be thy name" in the Lord's Prayer is the same Niphal concept: let Your holiness be displayed.*

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### "Without Blemish" — The Chain to the Lamb of God

Every instance of "without blemish" in the Mosaic system contributes to a single typological chain:

- **Exodus 12:5** — "Your lamb shall be without blemish" — the Passover lamb; the first use of the phrase for a sacrificial animal
- **Leviticus 22:19-25** — the consistent requirement for every offering at the altar
- **Numbers 28-29** — the daily, weekly, monthly, and festival offerings all specified as "without blemish"
- **Isaiah 53:9** — "he had done no violence, neither was any deceit in his mouth" — the Servant without moral blemish
- **Hebrews 9:14** — "who through the eternal Spirit offered himself without spot to God"
- **1 Peter 1:19** — "But with the precious blood of Christ, as of a lamb without blemish and without spot"

What Leviticus 22 repeats chapter after chapter about the altar sacrifice, Peter and the writer of Hebrews apply to Christ in a single, final statement.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES PASSAGES

### Leviticus 22:28 — "Mother and Young Not on the Same Day" — Misread as Animal Rights Law

**The misuse:** "Ye shall not kill it and her young both in one day" is sometimes cited as a proto-animal-rights law or a dietary restriction with ecological motivation.

**What the text actually says:** The law is about not destroying both a generation and its source simultaneously — a principle of not exhausting the created order's capacity to renew itself, rooted in God's ownership of life. It reflects the character of the God who preserves life, not merely a utilitarian conservation rule.

**The correction from within Scripture:**
- **Deuteronomy 22:6-7** — "If a bird's nest chance to be before thee... thou shalt not take the dam with the young... that it may be well with thee" — the same principle applied to birds; the mother is released so the source of life continues
- **Genesis 8:17** — "Bring forth... that they may breed abundantly in the earth" — God's design for created life is abundance and continuation, not exhaustion
- *The law is about reverence for life as God's possession and design, not an animal welfare statute in the modern sense.*

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### The Thanksgiving Offering Eaten Same Day — Urgency as Symbolic Thread

**Leviticus 22:29-30** — The todah (thanksgiving offering) must be eaten the same day; none left until morning.

The "same day" urgency thread:
- **Exodus 12:10** — the Passover lamb: "ye shall let nothing of it remain until the morning"
- **Leviticus 7:15** — the thanksgiving peace offering: eaten the same day, no remainder
- **Leviticus 22:30** — reaffirmed
- **John 6:27** — "Labour not for the meat which perisheth, but for that meat which endureth unto everlasting life" — the New Covenant bread does not perish
- **Matthew 6:11** — "Give us this day our daily bread" — the petition is for today; the manna (Exodus 16:19-20) left until morning bred worms

**What the Bible establishes:** What God provides for immediate sustaining must be received now — not stored, not deferred, not carried over. The thanksgiving offering eaten same-day is a covenant lesson about receptivity: what God gives for today is for today. The accumulation of what was meant for immediate reception becomes corruption. This is the manna principle extended to the offering table.
