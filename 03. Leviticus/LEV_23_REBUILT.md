## CHAPTER SUMMARY

God gives Moses the complete calendar of His appointed times. The weekly Sabbath is listed first — even before the annual feasts — as the foundational appointed time. Then the seven annual feasts in order: (1) Passover — 14th of the first month at evening, the LORD's Passover. (2) Unleavened Bread — begins the 15th, seven days, first and last days are holy convocations with no servile work; eat unleavened bread throughout. (3) Firstfruits — the wave sheaf offered the morrow after the Sabbath during Unleavened Bread; the harvest cannot be eaten until the wave offering is made. (4) Pentecost/Feast of Weeks — count fifty days from the day after the Sabbath of the wave sheaf; offer two wave loaves of fine flour baked with leaven (firstfruits to the LORD); seven lambs, one bullock, two rams as burnt offering; one goat as sin offering; two lambs as peace offering; a holy convocation. (5) Feast of Trumpets — first day of the seventh month; memorial of blowing of trumpets; holy convocation; rest. (6) Day of Atonement — tenth day of the seventh month; afflict your souls; holy convocation; no work whatsoever; from evening to evening. (7) Feast of Tabernacles — fifteenth of the seventh month; seven days; live in booths; take branches of goodly trees, palms, thick trees, willows; rejoice before the LORD; the eighth day is a solemn assembly. The gleaning law appears at verse 22 in the middle of the feast calendar — the poor and the stranger must have access to harvest even during the celebration.

### "These Are MY Feasts" — The LORD's Ownership of Time

**Leviticus 23:2** — "the feasts of the LORD, which ye shall proclaim to be holy convocations, even these are my feasts"

- **Isaiah 1:13-14** — "Your new moons and your appointed feasts my soul hateth" — when Israel corrupts the feasts, God disowns them; they cease to be "my feasts" and become "your feasts"
- **Hosea 2:11** — "I will also cause all her mirth to cease, her feast days, her new moons, and her sabbaths, and all her solemn feasts" — the feasts can be taken away as judgment
- **Colossians 2:16-17** — "Let no man therefore judge you in meat, or in drink, or in respect of an holyday, or of the new moon, or of the sabbath days: Which are a shadow of things to come; but the body is of Christ" — Paul identifies the feast-calendar as shadow; Christ is the substance
- *Interpretation: The feasts belong to the LORD, not to Israel. Israel was the steward of the prophetic calendar. Colossians 2:17 identifies the calendar as a system of shadows pointing to Christ — which means Christ is the substance that makes every shadow intelligible.*

### The Wave Sheaf — Firstfruits and Resurrection

**Leviticus 23:10-11** — "a sheaf of the firstfruits of your harvest unto the priest: And he shall wave the sheaf before the LORD... on the morrow after the sabbath the priest shall wave it"

- **1 Corinthians 15:20** — "But now is Christ risen from the dead, and become the firstfruits of them that slept"
- **1 Corinthians 15:23** — "Christ the firstfruits; afterward they that are Christ's at his coming"
- **Revelation 14:4** — "These were redeemed from among men, being the firstfruits unto God and to the Lamb"
- *Interpretation: The wave sheaf is waved on the morning after the Sabbath — which is the first day of the week. Jesus rose on the first day of the week (John 20:1). He rose on the exact day that the wave sheaf was waved before the LORD. He IS the firstfruits wave sheaf — the first of the harvest of the dead, waved before the Father as the guarantee of the full harvest to follow.*

### Two Wave Loaves Baked with Leaven at Pentecost

**Leviticus 23:17** — "Ye shall bring out of your habitations two wave loaves of two tenth deals: they shall be of fine flour; they shall be baken with leaven; they are the firstfruits unto the LORD"

- This is the ONLY offering in all of Leviticus that is baked WITH leaven and still offered to the LORD
- Acts 2 — the first Pentecost filling of the Spirit includes Jews from every nation; the two loaves have been interpreted as Jew and Gentile brought together into one Spirit-filled community
- **Ephesians 2:14-16** — "For he is our peace, who hath made both one, and hath broken down the middle wall of partition between us"
- *Interpretation: The two leavened loaves do not go on the altar (they cannot — leaven cannot be burned). They are waved before the LORD and belong to the priest. They are not the perfect sacrifice — they are the firstfruit community: real people, still with the leaven of humanity in them, but offered to God as the firstfruits of the new community He is gathering. The leaven in the Pentecost loaves is not sin; it is humanity. Christ is the fine flour without leaven (the grain offering of Leviticus 2); the church is the two leavened loaves offered at Pentecost.*

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### FEAST / APPOINTED TIME (Hebrew: moed — plural: moedim)
- **Leviticus 23:2, 4, 37, 44** — moed = an appointed time, a set meeting, a fixed season
- *Definition here: Not merely a party or celebration but a divine appointment — a time God has scheduled on His calendar for a specific encounter with His people. The Hebrew root means "to meet at an appointed place and time." The feasts are not Israel's idea; they are God's appointments. Missing a moed is like missing a meeting God called.*

### HOLY CONVOCATION (Hebrew: miqra qodesh)
- **Leviticus 23:2, 3, 4, 7, 8, 21, 24, 27, 35, 36** — appears throughout the chapter
- *Definition here: miqra = a called assembly, a public reading (same root as "to call/proclaim"); qodesh = holiness. A holy convocation is a sacred assembly that is called — by God, through the priests, for the whole people. It is not optional attendance; it is a summons.*

### AFFLICT YOUR SOULS (Hebrew: anah nefesh)
- **Leviticus 23:27, 29, 32** — the Day of Atonement requirement
- *Definition here: The Scriptures define this phrase internally. Isaiah 58:3-5 addresses those who "afflict their soul" (fast) while behaving unjustly — God's response defines what He actually requires. The affliction is penitential self-denial — the only annual commanded fast in the Mosaic calendar — expressing that the worshipper is not working toward atonement through personal virtue but acknowledging complete dependence on God's provision of it.*

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The Feast of Tabernacles and John 7 — "The Great Day of the Feast"

**Leviticus 23:36, 39** — The eighth day of Tabernacles is "a solemn assembly" — the climactic day

**John 7:37-38** — "In the last day, that great day of the feast, Jesus stood and cried, saying, If any man thirst, let him come unto me, and drink. He that believeth on me, as the scripture hath said, out of his belly shall flow rivers of living water."

Jesus stands on the eighth day — the great day of Tabernacles — and declares Himself to be what the feast pointed to: the one in whom God fully tabernacles, from whom the living water flows. Zechariah 14:16-19 declares that in the age to come all nations will come up to Jerusalem to keep the Feast of Tabernacles — the feast is both historical and eschatological.

**Revelation 21:3** — "Behold, the tabernacle of God is with men, and he will dwell with them" — the Feast of Tabernacles reaches its ultimate fulfillment when God Himself tabernacles with humanity permanently.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES PASSAGES

### Leviticus 23:24 — "Feast of Trumpets" Used to Predict the Rapture Date

**The misuse:** The Feast of Trumpets is repeatedly used in popular prophecy as the predicted date for the rapture, often linked to the phrase "no man knows the day or hour" (Matthew 24:36) — the reasoning being that Trumpets is the only feast announced by watching for the new moon (making its exact date uncertain).

**What the text actually says:** Leviticus 23:24 gives no such interpretive instruction. The text says: "a memorial of blowing of trumpets, an holy convocation." It prescribes what to do, not what it predicts about the timing of a future event.

**The correction from within Scripture:**
- **Colossians 2:16-17** — the feast days are "a shadow of things to come; but the body is of Christ" — the shadow is interpreted by the body (Christ), not by modern date-speculation
- **Matthew 24:36** — the context of "no man knoweth the day or hour" is the destruction of the Temple and Jerusalem (see vv.1-2), not a feast-calendar calculation; Jesus has already said in v.33 that those who see the signs can know "it is near, even at the doors"
- **Acts 1:7** — "It is not for you to know the times or the seasons, which the Father hath put in his own authority" — the response to a direct question about the timing of the kingdom's restoration

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### The Number Seven in the Feast Calendar

The seven-ness of the feast system is structurally significant:
- **Seven annual feasts** within the calendar
- **Seven days** of Unleavened Bread (vv.6-8)
- **Seven sabbaths** counted to Pentecost (v.15)
- **Seventh month** contains three feasts — Trumpets, Atonement, Tabernacles
- **Seven days** of Tabernacles + the **eighth day** solemn assembly (vv.34-36)

Seven = completion (as the roadmap establishes from the Bible's own repeated usage). The feast calendar is structured around completion-cycles. The weekly Sabbath is the seventh day. The Pentecost count is seven sevens. The seventh month is the climactic month. The Feast of Tabernacles runs seven days and ends with the eighth-day new beginning.

**What the Bible establishes:** The feast calendar is built on the number of completion (seven) and the number of new beginning (eight). The seven feasts tell one complete story — from redemption (Passover) to God dwelling with men (Tabernacles) — and then the eighth day solemn assembly points beyond the complete story to the age that follows: the new creation that does not end.
