## CHAPTER SUMMARY

God commands that pure beaten olive oil be brought for the lampstand, which Aaron must tend from evening to morning before the LORD continually — a perpetual statute. The twelve showbread loaves (two tenth deals of fine flour each) are set on the pure gold table in two rows of six, with pure frankincense upon each row as a memorial offering to the LORD. Every Sabbath the bread is refreshed and eaten by Aaron and his sons in the holy place — it is most holy, a perpetual covenant. Then the narrative: a man of mixed parentage (Israelite mother, Egyptian father) comes out among Israel, strives with an Israelite man, and blasphemes the Name with a curse. He is held in custody until God's mind is declared. God decrees death by stoning — all who heard him lay hands on his head; the whole congregation stones him. God then articulates the governing principles: whoever kills a man shall be put to death; whoever kills a beast shall restore it; if a man causes a blemish in his neighbour, the same shall be done to him — breach for breach, eye for eye, tooth for tooth. One manner of law for stranger and native alike: for I am the LORD your God.

### "Eye for Eye, Tooth for Tooth" — Proportionate Justice

**Leviticus 24:19-20** — "if a man cause a blemish in his neighbour; as he hath done, so shall it be done to him. Breach for breach, eye for eye, tooth for tooth"

- **Exodus 21:24** — the same lex talionis in the civil code
- **Deuteronomy 19:21** — "eye for eye, tooth for tooth, hand for hand, foot for foot"
- **Matthew 5:38-39** — "Ye have heard that it hath been said, An eye for an eye, and a tooth for a tooth: But I say unto you, That ye resist not evil: but whosoever shall smite thee on thy cheek, turn to him the other also"
- *Interpretation: The lex talionis in Leviticus 24 is a proportionate justice principle for civil courts — it caps punishment at equivalence; it forbids disproportionate revenge. Jesus in Matthew 5 is not abolishing civil justice; He is addressing the personal application: the disciple does not demand his legal rights in personal offences. Paul applies the same distinction: Romans 13:4 affirms the state's right to bear the sword for justice; Romans 12:19 says the individual does not take personal vengeance. The two spheres (civil justice and personal ethic) are distinct in the Bible.*

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### THE NAME (Hebrew: HaShem / Shem YHWH)
- **Leviticus 24:11, 16** — "blasphemed the name of the LORD, and cursed"
- *Definition here: The Name (YHWH — the personal covenant name of God revealed to Moses in Exodus 3:14-15) is not merely a title or label. In Hebrew thought the name is the person — to blaspheme the Name is to curse the person of God to His face. The entire offence of verse 11 is the direct, public, cursing invocation of YHWH's name in contempt. Exodus 20:7 ("thou shalt not take the name of the LORD thy God in vain") governs this; Leviticus 24:16 assigns the capital penalty to the ultimate form of the violation.*

### BREACH FOR BREACH (Hebrew: shever tachat shever)
- **Leviticus 24:20** — shever = a breaking, a fracture
- *Definition here: The lex talionis principle — the damage inflicted is the exact measure of the penalty. It is a cap on revenge, not a floor. In ancient Near Eastern law, a rich man could pay money instead of suffering the physical penalty; the lex talionis in the Mosaic system was egalitarian — the powerful could not buy their way out of the proportionate consequence. Every person, regardless of status, bore equal liability.*

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The Blasphemer Outside the Camp — A Type Reversed at Calvary

**Leviticus 24:14** — "Bring forth him that hath cursed without the camp; and... all the congregation stone him"

The blasphemer is taken outside the camp for death. Hebrews 13:12 establishes the principle: "Wherefore Jesus also, that he might sanctify the people with his own blood, suffered without the gate."

At Calvary the pattern is exactly reversed: the one taken outside the camp to be executed for blasphemy was Jesus — who had committed no blasphemy. The blasphemers in the crowd called for His blood while He bore their guilt. The sentence of Leviticus 24 fell on the sinless one so that those who deserved it might go free.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES PASSAGES

### Matthew 5:38-39 — "Eye for Eye" Abolished?

**The misuse:** Jesus' words "But I say unto you, That ye resist not evil" are used to argue that Jesus overturned the lex talionis of Leviticus 24 and that all forms of physical restraint or legal justice are therefore forbidden to Christians.

**What the text actually says:** Jesus in Matthew 5 is addressing personal conduct — specifically the demand for personal revenge in interpersonal offence. He is not addressing civil law or the state's function.

**The correction from within Scripture:**
- **Romans 13:1-4** — the governing authority "beareth not the sword in vain: for he is the minister of God, a revenger to execute wrath upon him that doeth evil" — in the same letter where Paul says "Avenge not yourselves" (Romans 12:19), he says the state is God's instrument of retributive justice (Romans 13:4). These are two different spheres.
- **Matthew 5:17** — "Think not that I am come to destroy the law, or the prophets: I am not come to destroy, but to fulfil" — Jesus is not abolishing the lex talionis; He is elevating the standard of personal ethics beyond what the civil law requires
- **John 18:22-23** — when struck on the face at his trial, Jesus does not strike back — but He does speak: "If I have spoken evil, bear witness of the evil: but if well, why smitest thou me?" — Jesus models the Matthew 5 principle while still speaking truth

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### The Twelve Loaves — Twelve Tribes Before God Always

**Leviticus 24:5-8** — twelve loaves on the table; refreshed every Sabbath

The twelve-loaf showbread is the enacted declaration that all twelve tribes are always before God:

- **Exodus 28:9-12** — the high priest's shoulder stones engraved with six names per stone: "Aaron shall bear their names before the LORD upon his two shoulders for a memorial"
- **Exodus 28:21, 29** — twelve stones on the breastplate: "Aaron shall bear the names of the children of Israel in the breastplate of judgment upon his heart, when he goeth in unto the holy place, for a memorial before the LORD"
- **Leviticus 24:5-8** — twelve loaves before the LORD always; the covenant of the whole people perpetually represented at God's table
- **Revelation 21:12, 14** — New Jerusalem: twelve gates with the names of the twelve tribes; twelve foundations with the names of the twelve apostles — the twelve-fold completeness carried into the eternal state

**What the Bible establishes (twelve = governmental perfection per the roadmap):** The twelve showbread loaves are not random. They represent the complete covenant people always standing before God — carried by the priest's shoulders, engraved on the priest's breastplate, set on the LORD's table. Not one tribe is forgotten. Not one name drops off the table. The New Jerusalem's twelve-gated, twelve-founded structure is the consummation of what the showbread declared weekly: all God's people, complete, present before Him always.
