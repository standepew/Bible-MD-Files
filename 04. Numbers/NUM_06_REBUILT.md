## CHAPTER SUMMARY

God gives Moses the law of the Nazirite — available to any Israelite man or woman who vows a vow of separation unto the LORD. Three prohibitions define the vow: (1) no wine, strong drink, vinegar of wine, grape liquor, moist or dried grapes, grape kernels, or grape husks — total abstinence from the vine; (2) no razor on the head — the hair grows as the visible sign of consecration; (3) no contact with any dead body — not even for parents, siblings, or children; the consecration of God is upon his head and takes precedence over all natural bonds. If someone dies suddenly beside him, he shaves his head on the seventh day, brings two birds on the eighth day (one sin offering, one burnt offering), then brings a lamb for a trespass offering — and the prior days of the vow are lost; he must begin again. When the full days of separation are completed, he comes to the Tabernacle and brings: one lamb for burnt offering, one ewe lamb for sin offering, one ram for peace offering, a basket of unleavened bread and wafers, and drink offerings. After the priest performs the sin and burnt offerings, the Nazirite shaves his consecrated head at the door of the Tabernacle and puts the hair in the fire under the peace offering. The priest waves the shoulder with cake and wafer as a wave offering. After this — and not before — the Nazirite may drink wine. Then the Aaronic Benediction: God commands Aaron and his sons to bless Israel with six specific phrases, and declares: "they shall put my name upon the children of Israel; and I will bless them."

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture) (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### The Nazirites of Scripture — Samson, Samuel, John the Baptist

**Numbers 6** establishes the law; three specific Nazirites confirm it in narrative:

- **Judges 13:3-5** — Samson: "for the child shall be a Nazarite unto God from the womb... and no razor shall come on his head" — a lifelong Nazirite from birth; his hair is the physical sign of consecration and the source of his strength (Judges 16:17-19)
- **1 Samuel 1:11** — Hannah's vow concerning Samuel: "there shall no razor come upon his head" — Samuel as a Nazirite
- **Luke 1:13-15** — the angel concerning John the Baptist: "he shall drink neither wine nor strong drink; and he shall be filled with the Holy Ghost, even from his mother's womb" — John fulfils the Nazirite abstention from wine
- **Matthew 26:29** — Jesus at the Last Supper: "I will not drink henceforth of this fruit of the vine, until that day when I drink it new with you in my Father's kingdom" — Jesus makes a Nazirite-style vow from the Last Supper until the Kingdom banquet; He will not drink wine again until the consummation
- *Interpretation: The Nazirite vow creates a class of persons who are set apart within the ordinary community by voluntary consecration. Samson, Samuel, and John the Baptist are marked Nazirites from birth. Christ's declaration in Matthew 26:29 is a Nazirite-form consecration to the completion of the work — He will not drink wine until it is finished and the Kingdom banquet begins.*

### The Aaronic Benediction — God's Own Words, Placed in Priestly Mouths

**Numbers 6:24-26** — "The LORD bless thee, and keep thee: The LORD make his face shine upon thee, and be gracious unto thee: The LORD lift up his countenance upon thee, and give thee peace."

**Numbers 6:27** — "And they shall put my name upon the children of Israel; and I will bless them."

- **Psalm 4:6** — "LORD, lift thou up the light of thy countenance upon us" — the benediction invoked as prayer
- **Psalm 29:11** — "The LORD will bless his people with peace" — the final word of the Benediction as God's own declaration
- **Psalm 67:1** — "God be merciful unto us, and bless us; and cause his face to shine upon us" — the Aaronic Benediction in psalm form, then immediately: "That thy way may be known upon earth, thy saving health among all nations" — the blessing of Israel is for the blessing of all nations
- **2 Corinthians 13:14** — "The grace of the Lord Jesus Christ, and the love of God, and the communion of the Holy Ghost, be with you all" — the New Covenant equivalent: the same three-movement blessing structure, now Trinitarian
- **Revelation 1:4-5** — "Grace be unto you, and peace, from him which is, and which was, and which is to come... from Jesus Christ" — the apostolic greeting is the Aaronic Benediction fulfilled in the New Covenant
- *Interpretation: The Aaronic Benediction is not merely a nice closing formula. It is God commanding that His name be placed on His people by priestly declaration. "They shall put my name upon them" — the name of the LORD is the content of the blessing. The six clauses of the Benediction are six facets of what it means to have God's name on you: kept, illumined, graced, seen, uplifted, given peace.*

### Three-Part Structure of the Benediction — Divine Witness

**Numbers 6:24-26** — three stanzas, each invoking "the LORD":

Line 1: "The LORD bless thee and keep thee" — provision and protection
Line 2: "The LORD make his face shine upon thee and be gracious unto thee" — illumination and mercy
Line 3: "The LORD lift up his countenance upon thee and give thee peace" — favour and shalom

- **Numbers 6:27** — "And I will bless them" — God's own confirmation of the priestly word
- Three = divine witness (as the roadmap establishes). The three invocations of the LORD in the Benediction are the Trinity's triple self-identification in blessing. What the New Testament makes explicit — Father, Son, Holy Spirit — the Old Testament invokes three times under the singular divine Name.
- **Deuteronomy 6:4** — "The LORD our God is one LORD" — the one LORD invoked three times is the one LORD who is one

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### NAZIRITE (Hebrew: nazir — separated, consecrated)
- **Numbers 6:2, 13, 18, 19, 20, 21** — one who has separated themselves by vow unto the LORD
- *Definition here: From nazar = to separate, to abstain. The Nazirite is not a priest (they are not born into the office) and not a monk (they live in the community). They are an ordinary Israelite who voluntarily takes on a higher standard of separation for a defined period. Any man or woman can take this vow (v.2 — "either man or woman"). The Nazirite makes the holy choice available to the non-priest, the non-Levite — a democratization of intensive consecration within the covenant community.*

### SHALOM (Hebrew: shalom — peace, wholeness, completeness)
- **Numbers 6:26** — "and give thee peace" — the final word of the Aaronic Benediction
- *Definition here: Shalom is not merely the absence of conflict. It is wholeness, completeness, the state in which nothing is lacking and nothing is broken. It is the condition that the Jubilee (Leviticus 25) enacts economically, that the covenant (Leviticus 26) promises to the obedient, and that Christ brings by His cross (Ephesians 2:14 — "he is our peace"). The Benediction ends with shalom because shalom is the comprehensive state of divine blessing — everything the LORD has done in the preceding five clauses culminates in the person being whole.*

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### Jesus' Matthew 26:29 Vow — The Nazirite Consecration to the Kingdom Banquet

**Numbers 6:20** — "after that the Nazarite may drink wine" — the wine is withheld until the vow is complete and the consecration fulfilled.

**Matthew 26:29** — "But I say unto you, I will not drink henceforth of this fruit of the vine, until that day when I drink it new with you in my Father's kingdom."

Jesus speaks these words at the Last Supper — the Passover meal — immediately before His crucifixion. He is placing Himself under a Nazirite-form vow: the wine is suspended until the work is complete and the Kingdom arrives. The consecration is to the cross; the wine waits for the resurrection banquet. The Nazirite's hair burned under the peace offering (Numbers 6:18) corresponds to the sacrifice Christ is about to offer — the completion of the vow makes the wine possible again.

**Revelation 19:9** — "Blessed are they which are called unto the marriage supper of the Lamb" — the Nazirite drinks wine again at the banquet; the Kingdom feast is the moment when the consecration-vow is fulfilled and the celebration begins.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES PASSAGES

### Numbers 6:24-26 — The Aaronic Benediction Used as a Mere Closing Formula

**The misuse:** The Aaronic Benediction is reduced to a liturgical closing, repeated without engagement with its content — a ritual phrase rather than a covenantal declaration.

**What the text actually says:** Numbers 6:27 reveals the mechanism: "they shall put my name upon the children of Israel; and I will bless them." The Benediction is not the priest's blessing — it is God's blessing, delivered through the priest's mouth. The priest is the instrument; God is the source. When the Benediction is spoken, God Himself commits to bless. It is not a wish ("may God bless you") — it is a declaration ("the LORD bless you") — spoken in God's own words, commanding the blessing into being.

**The correction from within Scripture:**
- **Isaiah 55:11** — "So shall my word be that goeth forth out of my mouth: it shall not return unto me void, but it shall accomplish that which I please" — God's own words accomplish what they declare
- **Psalm 115:14-15** — "The LORD shall increase you more and more, you and your children. Ye are blessed of the LORD which made heaven and earth" — the same benediction-confidence

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### "The LORD Bless Thee" — The Name of God as the Benediction's Content

**Numbers 6:24-26** — the LORD (YHWH) appears three times; **Numbers 6:27** — "they shall put MY NAME upon the children of Israel"

The name placed on Israel through the Benediction is YHWH — the personal covenant name revealed at the burning bush (Exodus 3:14-15). The Benediction is not a general good wish; it is the placement of the divine name on God's people. This name-thread runs through Scripture to its culmination:

- **Exodus 3:14-15** — the name first declared to Moses
- **Numbers 6:27** — the name placed on Israel through the priestly Benediction
- **Revelation 3:12** — "I will write upon him the name of my God... and I will write upon him my new name" — the overcomer receives the name written on him
- **Revelation 14:1** — "an hundred forty and four thousand, having his Father's name written in their foreheads"
- **Revelation 22:4** — "and his name shall be in their foreheads"

**What the Bible establishes:** The Aaronic Benediction is the old-covenant form of what Revelation 22:4 describes as the new-creation reality. "They shall put my name upon the children of Israel" is the prototype; "his name shall be in their foreheads" is the consummation. The blessing is not merely received — it is inscribed. The people who are blessed carry the Name.
