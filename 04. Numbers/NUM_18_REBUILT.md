## CHAPTER SUMMARY

After the terror of Numbers 17:12-13 ("we die, we perish"), God answers by clarifying who bears the weight of the sanctuary's holiness: Aaron and his sons bear the iniquity of the sanctuary and the iniquity of the priesthood — the priests absorb the danger on behalf of the people. The Levites are given to Aaron as a gift — and Aaron's family alone may minister before the altar and inside the veil; any stranger who approaches dies. In return: all the holy offerings of the Israelites belong to Aaron and his sons and daughters — every meal, sin, and trespass offering; all wave and heave offerings; the best of the firstfruits of oil, wine, and grain; every devoted thing; all firstborn humans (redeemed at five shekels from one month old); firstborn of clean animals sacrificed with blood on the altar, flesh for Aaron; firstborn of unclean animals redeemed. This entire allotment is a covenant of salt — perpetual, irrevocable. Aaron and the Levites have no land inheritance among Israel. "I am thy part and thine inheritance among the children of Israel." The Levites receive all Israel's tithe as their inheritance for Tabernacle service. They must in turn tithe the tithe — giving the best tenth of what they receive to Aaron.

### "Thou and Thy Sons with Thee Shall Bear the Iniquity of the Sanctuary"

**Numbers 18:1** — the priest bears the iniquity; the people are protected by the priest's bearing

- **Leviticus 10:17** — after Nadab and Abihu: "Wherefore have ye not eaten the sin offering in the holy place, seeing it is most holy, and God hath given it you to bear the iniquity of the congregation" — the priest eats the sin offering as part of bearing the congregation's iniquity
- **Isaiah 53:11** — "he shall bear their iniquities" — the Servant bearing the iniquity; the Aaronic pattern fulfilled in its ultimate form
- **Hebrews 9:28** — "So Christ was once offered to bear the sins of many" — the High Priest who bears iniquity once for all
- **1 Peter 2:24** — "Who his own self bare our sins in his own body on the tree" — the burden Aaron's sons bore in shadow, Christ bore in substance on the cross
- *Interpretation: Numbers 18:1 establishes that the priesthood functions by bearing. The priest does not merely perform rituals — he personally carries the weight of the community's failures before God. This is why the high priest's garments include memorial stones for all twelve tribes (Exodus 28:12, 29) — he carries the people when he enters the holy place. Christ as the ultimate high priest bore not the annual sins of one nation but the sins of all humanity, once, permanently.*

### "I Am Thy Part and Thine Inheritance"

**Numbers 18:20** — "I am thy part and thine inheritance among the children of Israel"

- **Psalm 16:5** — "The LORD is the portion of mine inheritance and of my cup: thou maintainest my lot" — David speaking the same language; the one whose inheritance is the LORD is the richest person in Israel
- **Psalm 73:26** — "My flesh and my heart faileth: but God is the strength of my heart, and my portion for ever" — Asaph in distress finds the same conclusion: God is his portion
- **Lamentations 3:24** — "The LORD is my portion, saith my soul; therefore will I hope in him" — Jeremiah from the rubble of Jerusalem: the inheritance that cannot be destroyed
- **Revelation 21:3** — "and God himself shall be with them, and be their God" — the New Covenant form; having God as your portion is the ultimate eschatological state
- *Interpretation: Aaron receives no land — every tribe gets territory; Aaron gets God. This is not deprivation; it is the highest possible inheritance. The priest who mediates between God and the people is the one whose inheritance IS God. The New Covenant develops this into the promise for all believers: "heirs of God, and joint-heirs with Christ" (Romans 8:17). What was Aaron's unique inheritance becomes, through Christ, the common inheritance of all who are in the Son.*

### Covenant of Salt — The Irrevocable Allotment

**Numbers 18:19** — "It is a covenant of salt for ever before the LORD unto thee and to thy seed with thee"

- **Leviticus 2:13** — "with all thine offerings thou shalt offer salt" — every grain offering must be salted; salt is the covenant preservative in every offering
- **2 Chronicles 13:5** — "the LORD God of Israel gave the kingdom over Israel to David for ever, even to him and to his sons by a covenant of salt" — the Davidic covenant described as a covenant of salt — the same irrevocability
- **Mark 9:49-50** — "every sacrifice shall be salted with salt. Salt is good: but if the salt have lost his saltness, wherewith will ye season it?" — Jesus extends the salt covenant into discipleship: the life salted by the Spirit's covenant faithfulness
- **Colossians 4:6** — "Let your speech be alway with grace, seasoned with salt" — the salt of covenantal faithfulness carried into every word
- *Interpretation: A covenant of salt is permanent because salt preserves and does not rot. What God establishes by a salt covenant cannot be undone by time, circumstance, or human failure. The Aaronic priestly portion, the Davidic throne, and (by extension) the New Covenant in Christ's blood are all covenant-of-salt arrangements: sealed, preserved, irrevocable.*

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### COVENANT OF SALT (Hebrew: berit melach — a salt covenant)
- **Numbers 18:19** — the priestly allotment is sealed with a covenant of salt
- *Definition here: Salt preserves — it prevents decay and corruption. A covenant sealed with salt is one that cannot corrupt or dissolve over time. Every offering in the Tabernacle was salted (Leviticus 2:13) as a material declaration that the covenant does not rot. The opposite of salt in the covenant context is leaven — leaven corrupts and spreads; it is excluded from meal offerings (Leviticus 2:11). What God establishes with salt is permanent; what man brings with leaven is excluded.*

### SERVICE OF GIFT (Numbers 18:7)
- **Numbers 18:7** — "I have given your priest's office unto you as a service of gift"
- *Definition here: The priesthood is not earned, bought, inherited through personal virtue, or seized — it is given as a gift (mattanah). The same word as the Levites given as a gift (mattanah) in Numbers 18:6. Both the Levites and the priesthood itself are gift-structured: God gives the Levites to Aaron as a gift; God gives the priesthood to Aaron as a gift. Ephesians 2:8 — "by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves: it is the gift of God" — the entire New Covenant salvation has the same structure as Numbers 18:7: given, not earned.*

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### Hebrews 7 — The Levitical Tithe and the Melchizedek Priesthood

**Numbers 18:21-28** — Israel tithes to the Levites; the Levites tithe the tithe to Aaron

- **Hebrews 7:5-9** — the writer of Hebrews uses Numbers 18 to argue the superiority of Melchizedek's priesthood: "they that are of the sons of Levi... have a commandment to take tithes of the people according to the law... But he whose descent is not counted from them received tithes of Abraham, and blessed him that had the promises. And without all contradiction the less is blessed of the better." The Levites receive tithes by law; Melchizedek received tithe by stature — Abraham tithed to him before Levi existed (Genesis 14:20). The Numbers 18 tithe-structure is the reference point from which the argument for Melchizedek's superiority is built.
- **Hebrews 7:11-12** — "If therefore perfection were by the Levitical priesthood... what further need was there that another priest should rise after the order of Melchisedec?" — Numbers 18 defines the Levitical system in its fullness; Hebrews 7 declares it was never designed to be permanent.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES PASSAGES

### Numbers 18:20 — "I Am Thy Part and Thine Inheritance" — Applied Only to Full-Time Ministers

**The misuse:** This verse is often restricted to vocational ministers — "God is the pastor's inheritance since he has no secular income." The application is spiritually privatised.

**What the text actually says in its full canonical context:**
- **Psalm 16:5** — David (not a Levite) claims the same inheritance: "The LORD is the portion of mine inheritance"
- **Psalm 73:26** — Asaph: "God is... my portion for ever"
- **Romans 8:17** — "heirs of God, and joint-heirs with Christ" — the New Covenant extends this to all believers

The Numbers 18:20 principle applies uniquely to Aaron in its original context, but Psalms and the New Testament develop it as the universal inheritance of all who know God.

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### No Land Inheritance — The Freedom of Having God as Portion

Every tribe receives territorial inheritance in Canaan. The Levites receive 48 cities scattered among all the tribes (Numbers 35). Aaron and his sons receive no territory at all — only God.

This landlessness creates the most mobile and universally present group in Israel: Levites scattered in every tribe; priests belonging to no territory. The priest serves all Israel precisely because he belongs to none of it.

- **Matthew 8:20** — "The Son of man hath not where to lay his head" — the ultimate Aaronic priest; no territory, no land, belonging entirely to His Father's service
- **Hebrews 13:14** — "For here have we no continuing city, but we seek one to come" — the New Covenant community with the same territorial rootlessness as the Levites: scattered among the nations, belonging to none of them, serving all of them, waiting for the city God has prepared

**What the Bible establishes:** The Levitical landlessness is not a deprivation but a liberation. Having no earthly territory to defend means having no earthly loyalties to divide devotion. The priest belongs entirely to God. The New Covenant community is likewise dispersed, landless in the eschatological sense, "strangers and pilgrims" (1 Peter 2:11) — freed from territorial identity to serve all peoples everywhere.
