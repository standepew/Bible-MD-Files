## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Israel camps in the plains of Moab. Balak son of Zippor, king of Moab, sees what Israel did to the Amorites and is terrified. He consults the elders of Midian: this people will devour everything around us like an ox licks grass. He sends elders of Moab and Midian to Balaam at Pethor with rewards of divination: come curse this people — you have power; he whom you bless is blessed, he whom you curse is cursed. Balaam tells them to lodge overnight; God comes to Balaam and asks who these men are. Balaam explains. God: do not go; do not curse — for they are blessed. Balaam dismisses the princes: the LORD refuses to let me go. Balak sends more prestigious ambassadors with promises of great honour and anything Balaam asks. Balaam's famous reply: if Balak gave me his house full of silver and gold I cannot go beyond the word of the LORD my God to do less or more. He asks them to stay overnight again — perhaps the LORD will say more. God returns: if the men come to call you, go with them — but speak only what I say. Balaam rises and goes. God's anger is kindled when he goes. The angel of the LORD stands three times in the way with a drawn sword. Each time the donkey sees the angel and turns aside or stops; each time Balaam beats her. The third time the donkey falls down. The LORD opens the donkey's mouth: what have I done to you that you beat me three times? Balaam argues with the donkey. The LORD opens Balaam's eyes; he sees the angel, falls on his face. The angel rebukes: your way is perverse before me; the donkey saved your life. Balaam confesses: I have sinned. The angel: go with the men — but speak only what I say. Balak meets Balaam; Balaam warns: I can only speak what God puts in my mouth.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture) (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### Balaam — The Prophet-for-Hire

**Numbers 22:7** — the elders come "with the rewards of divination in their hand"

- **2 Peter 2:15** — "Which have forsaken the right way, and are gone astray, following the way of Balaam the son of Bosor, who loved the wages of unrighteousness" — Peter identifies Balaam's core sin: he loved the wages; the money was the motive
- **Jude 11** — "ran greedily after the error of Balaam for reward" — same diagnosis; the error is not the prophecy but the mercenary motivation
- **Revelation 2:14** — "thou hast there them that hold the doctrine of Balaam, who taught Balac to cast a stumblingblock before the children of Israel" — connects the Numbers 25 seduction to Balaam's counsel; the doctrine of Balaam = using sexual temptation and idolatry to accomplish what a curse could not
- *Interpretation: Balaam is the Bible's archetype of the prophet who possesses genuine prophetic gifts but uses them for personal profit. He cannot curse what God has blessed — his prophecies in Numbers 23-24 are magnificent — but his heart is for hire. The New Testament consistently uses him as the warning for all who minister for money rather than for God.*

### "He Whom Thou Blessest Is Blessed; He Whom Thou Cursest Is Cursed" — The Feared Power

**Numbers 22:6** — Balak's summary of Balaam's power

- **Genesis 12:3** — God to Abraham: "I will bless them that bless thee, and curse him that curseth thee" — the Abrahamic covenant is what Balak is describing without knowing it; the blessing/curse power over Israel that Balak credits to Balaam actually belongs to God and is the Abrahamic covenant
- **Genesis 27:29** — Isaac's blessing of Jacob: "Cursed be every one that curseth thee, and blessed be he that blesseth thee" — the covenant pattern confirmed in the patriarchal blessing
- *Interpretation: Balak hires Balaam to do what only God can do — and what God has already irrevocably done. The covenant of Genesis 12:3 means that Israel's blessing is not available for purchase or reversal. Balak is trying to buy the undoing of a divine oath. He cannot. What he commissions from Balaam will become the instrument of God's further blessing of Israel.*

---

### The Donkey Sees What the Prophet Cannot — Spiritual Blindness

**Numbers 22:23-31** — the donkey sees the angel three times; Balaam cannot see until God opens his eyes

- **2 Kings 6:17** — Elisha prays: "LORD, I pray thee, open his eyes, that he may see" — his servant's eyes opened to see the horses and chariots of fire; the divine army invisible to the natural eye
- **Luke 24:31** — on the Emmaus road: "their eyes were opened, and they knew him" — eyes opened to recognise the risen Christ who had been walking with them
- **2 Peter 2:16** — "the dumb ass speaking with man's voice forbad the madness of the prophet" — Peter calls Balaam's blindness madness; the prophet whose spiritual eyes are focused on the wages of unrighteousness is less perceptive than his own animal
- *Interpretation: The donkey's three sightings of the angel against Balaam's three beatings of the donkey is one of Scripture's most devastating reversals: the man with prophetic credentials cannot see what his pack animal can. Spiritual office does not guarantee spiritual sight. The wages of unrighteousness blind the prophet to the angel standing with a drawn sword directly in his path.*

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### REWARDS OF DIVINATION (Hebrew: qesem — divination, lot-casting)
- **Numbers 22:7** — the elders carry the fee for Balaam's services
- *Definition here: Qesem is the practice of determining hidden or future knowledge through supernatural means — including casting lots, interpreting omens, reading entrails. Deuteronomy 18:10-12 lists it among the abominations: "For all that do these things are an abomination unto the LORD." Balaam is a practitioner of this art — a pagan diviner, not an Israelite prophet. Yet God speaks to him and through him. This is not an endorsement of divination; it is a demonstration that God can commandeer any vessel He chooses to speak His word, while Balaam's character and profession remain condemned.*

### PERVERSE (Hebrew: yarath — precipitate, reckless, headlong)
- **Numbers 22:32** — "thy way is perverse before me"
- *Definition here: The angel's word is not the ordinary word for moral perversity but suggests rushing headlong, acting rashly and precipitously. Balaam's journey is perverse not because going was forbidden (God permitted it) but because his heart was headlong toward the reward — rushing toward the wages of unrighteousness. The permission to go was not a blessing on the motive. God permitted what Balaam's heart was set on (Psalm 106:15 pattern) while standing in the way to correct the trajectory.*

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The Angel with the Drawn Sword — The Divine Adversary

**Numbers 22:23** — the angel of the LORD standing in the way, sword drawn

- **Joshua 5:13-14** — the Captain of the LORD's host appears to Joshua before Jericho with a drawn sword: "Art thou for us, or for our adversaries? And he said, Nay; but as captain of the host of the LORD am I now come" — the same drawn-sword angel; the same posture of standing in the way of human plans
- **1 Chronicles 21:16** — the angel of the LORD standing between earth and heaven with a drawn sword over Jerusalem after David's census; the sword sheathed only when David intercedes
- *Interpretation: The angel with the drawn sword is a consistent divine-intervention image: God personally blocking a path that leads to destruction. In Numbers 22 He blocks Balaam; in Joshua 5 He meets Joshua; in 1 Chronicles 21 He stands in judgment over Israel. In every case the drawn sword is the visible form of divine sovereignty overriding human plans.*

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES PASSAGES

### Numbers 22:20 — "God Said Go" Then Numbers 22:22 — "God's Anger Was Kindled Because He Went"

**The apparent contradiction:** God tells Balaam to go (v.20), then is angry when he goes (v.22).

**What the text establishes:** God permitted Balaam to go under a specific condition: "only the word that I shall say unto thee, that shalt thou do." The anger is not because Balaam obeyed but because of why he went — the wages, the honour, the motive. Numbers 22:32 — "thy way is perverse before me" — confirms the issue is the heart orientation, not the act of going. God permits what He does not endorse; permission is not approval of motive. Psalm 106:15 — "he gave them their request; but sent leanness into their soul" — the same principle.

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### Three Refusals / Three Positions of the Angel — The Pattern of Blocked Paths

The angel blocks three times; the donkey resists three times; Balaam beats three times. The threefold pattern of divine blocking recurs throughout Scripture as the number of complete, unmistakable warning:

- [[04. Numbers/NUM_22_REBUILT|Numbers 22:23-27]] — three blocking positions; three beatings; then eyes opened
- [[09. 1 Samuel/1_SAMUEL_03_REBUILT|1 Samuel 3:4-8]] — God calls Samuel three times before Samuel understands
- [[44. Acts/ACTS_10_REBUILT|Acts 10:16]] — the sheet descends three times to confirm the message to Peter
- [[47. 2 Corinthians/2_CORINTHIANS_12_REBUILT|2 Corinthians 12:8]] — Paul pleads three times for the thorn's removal; the third time God answers definitively

**What the Bible establishes:** Three is the number of complete testimony — the third instance removes all possibility of coincidence or misunderstanding. Balaam needed three blockings to understand that this path was opposed by God. The gracious divine pattern is to block, warn, and speak before destroying — the drawn sword held back while the donkey speaks.
