## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Five daughters of Zelophehad of Manasseh — Mahlah, Noah, Hoglah, Milcah, Tirzah — stand before Moses, Eleazar, the princes, and the whole congregation at the Tabernacle door. Their case: their father died in the wilderness in his own sin (not Korah's rebellion) and had no sons. Why should his name be erased from his family because he had no son? Give us a possession among our father's brothers. Moses brings the case before the LORD. God: the daughters speak right — give them their father's inheritance. And establish as law: if a man dies without a son, his inheritance passes to his daughter; if no daughter, to his brothers; if no brothers, to his father's brothers; if none, to the nearest kinsman. Then God tells Moses: go up Mount Abarim and see the land I am giving Israel — then you will be gathered to your people as Aaron was, because you both rebelled at the waters of Meribah. Moses' response — not protest, not grief for himself, but immediate prayer: Let the LORD, the God of the spirits of all flesh, set a man over the congregation who will go out before them and come in before them, that the congregation of the LORD not be as sheep without a shepherd. God designates Joshua son of Nun — a man in whom is the spirit — and commands Moses to lay his hand on him before Eleazar and all the congregation, give him a charge, and put some of Moses' honour upon him. Joshua will stand before Eleazar the priest and inquire by the Urim before the LORD; at the LORD's word he and all Israel will go out and come in. Moses does exactly as commanded — lays hands on Joshua, gives him a charge.

### "Not as Sheep Without a Shepherd" — Moses' Prayer for Succession

**Numbers 27:17** — Moses prays for a successor who will go before them and come in before them, "that the congregation of the LORD be not as sheep which have no shepherd"

- **Ezekiel 34:5** — "And they were scattered, because there is no shepherd" — when Israel loses faithful leadership, the sheep scatter; Ezekiel's lament for Israel in exile
- **Matthew 9:36** — "But when he saw the multitudes, he was moved with compassion on them, because they fainted, and were scattered abroad, as sheep having no shepherd" — Jesus sees the crowd and uses the exact phrase of Numbers 27:17; the compassion of Moses echoed in Christ
- **John 10:11** — "I am the good shepherd: the good shepherd giveth his life for the sheep" — Christ as the ultimate answer to Moses' prayer; the shepherd who does not merely lead but dies for the flock
- **Hebrews 13:20** — "the great shepherd of the sheep" — Christ designated with the language Moses' prayer anticipated
- *Interpretation: Moses does not ask for self-preservation or contest God's verdict about his own exclusion from the land. His first and only concern is for the people. "That they not be as sheep without a shepherd" — this is the pastoral heart at its highest. Jesus explicitly takes this language as His own self-description. Moses' prayer for a shepherd-leader is answered first by Joshua, ultimately by Christ.*

### Joshua — A Man in Whom Is the Spirit

**Numbers 27:18** — "Take thee Joshua the son of Nun, a man in whom is the spirit"

- **Numbers 11:28** — Joshua first appears: "Joshua the son of Nun, the servant of Moses, one of his young men" — forty years earlier; he has served the entire wilderness period
- **Deuteronomy 34:9** — "And Joshua the son of Nun was full of the spirit of wisdom; for Moses had laid his hands upon him" — the spirit received through the laying on of hands functions as wisdom for leadership
- **Acts 6:3** — the choosing of deacons: "men of honest report, full of the Holy Ghost and wisdom" — the same criteria as Joshua: a man in whom is the spirit
- **Acts 13:3** — "they had fasted and prayed, and laid their hands on them, they sent them away" — the laying-on-of-hands pattern from Numbers 27 becomes the New Testament ordination pattern; the charge given, the honour transferred, the authority conferred
- *Interpretation: The laying on of hands in Numbers 27:18-23 is the foundational ordination act in Scripture. Moses puts his hand on Joshua before Eleazar and the congregation, gives him a charge, and transfers some of his honour. 1 Timothy 4:14 — "Neglect not the gift that is in thee, which was given thee by prophecy, with the laying on of the hands of the presbytery." The Numbers 27 act is the prototype of every ordination in every generation.*

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### URIM (Hebrew: urim — lights; part of the Urim and Thummim)
- **Numbers 27:21** — "he shall ask counsel for him after the judgment of Urim before the LORD"
- *Definition here: The Urim and Thummim (lights and perfections) are the sacred lot-casting/divination instrument kept in the high priest's breastplate (Exodus 28:30). Their exact mechanism is unknown — the text never describes how they work. They function as the official means of divine consultation on major decisions. After Joshua no leader in Scripture is said to use the Urim for decisions — the prophetic word begins to replace the priestly oracle. The Urim is mentioned in Ezra 2:63 / Nehemiah 7:65 as something Israel is waiting to use again; it disappears after the Exile.*

### HONOUR (Hebrew: hod — splendour, majesty, authority)
- **Numbers 27:20** — "thou shalt put some of thine honour upon him"
- *Definition here: Hod is the visible splendour of authority — the dignity that makes others obedient. Numbers 27:20 is the only place where hod is transferred from one person to another by command. Moses does not give Joshua all his honour — "some" of his honour. The full weight of Mosaic authority is not replicated; Joshua leads as the continuation of Moses, not the replacement. Psalm 21:5 — "His glory is great in thy salvation: honour and majesty hast thou laid upon him" — God clothing the king with hod. Moses' partial transfer of hod to Joshua is the human version of what God does for the anointed king.*

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES PASSAGES

### Numbers 27:16 — "The God of the Spirits of All Flesh" Used to Argue That All People Have God as Father

**The misuse:** The title "God of the spirits of all flesh" is sometimes read as a declaration that God is the personal Father of every individual human being without distinction.

**What the text establishes:** The title is a sovereignty claim — God is the one who gives breath/spirit to all living creatures (cf. Genesis 2:7; Job 12:10); He is the source of life in all flesh. This is the basis of Moses' prayer: since You are the one who gives life to all, You can appoint whoever You choose. It is not a statement about universal spiritual fatherhood but about God's sovereignty over all life. John 1:12-13 establishes the distinction between those who receive sonship and those who do not: "to them gave he power to become the sons of God."

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### The Transfer of Authority — Moses to Joshua to Christ

The succession from Moses to Joshua is one of the Bible's clearest succession types:

- **Moses** — prophet, lawgiver, mediator of the Sinai covenant; dies outside the land; intercedes for the people; carries the burden of the flock
- **Joshua (Yehoshua)** — "YHWH saves"; leads the people through the Jordan into the promised land; conquers the enemies; distributes the inheritance
- **Christ (Greek: Iesous = Hebrew: Yehoshua)** — bears the same name as Joshua; leads His people through death into the eternal rest; conquers every enemy; distributes the eternal inheritance

**Hebrews 4:8** — "For if Jesus [Joshua] had given them rest, then would he not afterward have spoken of another day" — the writer of Hebrews names Joshua by his New Testament name (Jesus) to make the comparison unmistakable: the first Joshua's rest was incomplete; the second Joshua (Christ) gives the true rest. Moses laying hands on Joshua is the type of the Father commissioning the Son for His redemptive mission.
