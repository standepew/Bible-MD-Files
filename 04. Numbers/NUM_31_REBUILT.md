## CHAPTER SUMMARY

God tells Moses: avenge Israel of Midian — afterward you will be gathered to your people. Moses arms twelve thousand men — a thousand per tribe — with Phinehas carrying the holy instruments and the trumpets. They war against Midian: all males slain; five kings of Midian killed — Evi, Rekem, Zur, Hur, Reba; Balaam son of Beor killed with the sword. Cities and castles burned; women, children, livestock, and goods taken as spoil, brought to Moses and Eleazar at the camp. Moses goes out to meet them outside the camp and is furious: why have you saved all the women alive? These are the very women who, through Balaam's counsel, caused Israel to transgress at Peor — causing the plague. Kill every male child; kill every woman who has known a man. Keep alive only the virgins. All who killed or touched a slain person must stay outside camp seven days and purify on the third and seventh day. Eleazar instructs on metal purification: whatever can endure fire must go through fire and then through the water of separation; whatever cannot endure fire goes through water only. Wash clothes on the seventh day; then re-enter camp. God instructs on dividing the spoil: half to the warriors; half to the congregation. From the warriors' half — one soul in five hundred (of persons, cattle, donkeys, sheep) to Eleazar as a heave offering to the LORD. From the congregation's half — one in fifty to the Levites. The spoil totals: 675,000 sheep; 72,000 cattle; 61,000 donkeys; 32,000 persons (virgins). The officers then report: not one Israelite soldier is missing. In gratitude they bring a voluntary oblation — all their personal gold jewelry, 16,750 shekels — to Moses and Eleazar as an atonement for their souls before the LORD. Brought into the Tabernacle as a memorial.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture) (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### Balaam Dies by the Sword — His End vs. His Wish

**Numbers 31:8** — "Balaam also the son of Beor they slew with the sword"

- **Numbers 23:10** — Balaam's wish: "Let me die the death of the righteous, and let my last end be like his!" — he wanted to die the death of the covenant people; he dies the death of their enemy
- **2 Peter 2:15** — "Which have forsaken the right way, and are gone astray, following the way of Balaam the son of Bosor, who loved the wages of unrighteousness" — the reward Balaam pursued ends in the sword
- **Revelation 2:14** — "the doctrine of Balaam" identified in Pergamos; the ultimate consequence of that doctrine is judgment
- *Interpretation: Balaam dies where he does not belong — among the enemies of Israel, in the war God commanded against Midian. He had gone back to his own place (Numbers 24:25) and returned to Midian — into the very nation whose counsel to seduce Israel he had provided. Numbers 31:16 identifies his counsel as the cause of the Baal-Peor plague. The man who wished to die the death of the righteous dies by the sword of the righteous. His end is the exact opposite of his wish.*

### "Counsel of Balaam" — The Strategy Identified

**Numbers 31:16** — "Behold, these caused the children of Israel, through the counsel of Balaam, to commit trespass against the LORD in the matter of Peor"

- **Revelation 2:14** — "the doctrine of Balaam, who taught Balac to cast a stumblingblock before the children of Israel, to eat things sacrificed unto idols, and to commit fornication" — Christ's identification of Balaam's strategy as a named doctrine still operating in the first-century church
- *Interpretation: Numbers 31:16 is the interpretive key for Numbers 22-25. The seduction of Numbers 25 was not spontaneous — it was strategically counselled by the prophet who could not curse Israel directly. The "doctrine of Balaam" in Revelation 2:14 is the same strategy applied in the church: introduce sexual immorality and idol-food into the community through internal compromise, since direct external attack has failed.*

### The Purification of Warriors — Contact with Death Defiles

**Numbers 31:19-24** — seven days outside camp; cleansing on third and seventh day; metals through fire and water of separation

- **Numbers 19** — the red heifer water of separation; established for all contact with death; Numbers 31 applies it to the returning warriors
- **Hebrews 9:13-14** — "For if the blood of bulls and of goats, and the ashes of an heifer sprinkling the unclean, sanctifieth to the purifying of the flesh: How much more shall the blood of Christ, who through the eternal Spirit offered himself without spot to God, purge your conscience from dead works to serve the living God?" — the Numbers 31 purification (ashes of the heifer, third-day and seventh-day cleansing) is the type; Christ's blood is the antitype
- **Haggai 2:12-14** — holiness is not contagious; defilement is; the purification law of Numbers 31 underlies Haggai's principle
- *Interpretation: The warriors who did what God commanded still require purification from contact with death. The holiness of the mission does not exempt the person from the defilement of the means. This is the consistent witness of the Levitical system: death defiles, and what defiles must be cleansed before re-entering the holy camp. The three-day and seven-day purification is the shadow of Christ's three-day death and the seven-fold completeness of His atoning work.*

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### HOLY INSTRUMENTS (Hebrew: kele ha-qodesh — vessels/instruments of holiness)
- **Numbers 31:6** — "Phinehas the son of Eleazar the priest, to the war, with the holy instruments, and the trumpets to blow in his hand"
- *Definition here: The holy instruments brought to war with Phinehas are the sacred items of the Tabernacle used in warfare — specifically in connection with the blowing of trumpets. Numbers 10:9 — "if ye go to war in your land against the enemy that oppresseth you, then ye shall blow an alarm with the trumpets; and ye shall be remembered before the LORD your God." The Tabernacle instruments carried into battle are not weapons — they are the instruments of the divine presence that ensures the LORD remembers and fights.*

### ATONEMENT FOR OUR SOULS (Hebrew: kipper et-napshoteinu)
- **Numbers 31:50** — "to make an atonement for our souls before the LORD"
- *Definition here: The officers offer their gold voluntarily to make atonement — not because they sinned (they survived without losing a man) but as a spontaneous response to undeserved mercy. The atonement here is not guilt-covering but gratitude-offering — a recognition that survival was grace, not merit. David's language in 1 Chronicles 29:14 — "of thine own have we given thee" — is the same spirit: the offering is returning to God what already belongs to Him, in acknowledgment that the givers themselves belong to Him.*

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### Fire and Water — The Double Purification

**Numbers 31:22-23** — what can endure fire goes through fire; then through the water of separation. What cannot endure fire goes through water only.

- **Malachi 3:2-3** — "he is like a refiner's fire... And he shall sit as a refiner and purifier of silver: and he shall purify the sons of Levi, and purge them as gold and silver"
- **1 Peter 1:7** — "That the trial of your faith, being much more precious than of gold that perisheth, though it be tried with fire, might be found unto praise and honour and glory at the appearing of Jesus Christ"
- **Zechariah 13:9** — "And I will bring the third part through the fire, and will refine them as silver is refined, and will try them as gold is tried"
- *Interpretation: The metal purification of Numbers 31 is the type of the eschatological refining — the fire that purges what can be purged, the water of the Spirit that cleanses what remains. The metals that go through fire and water emerge purer than before; they are not destroyed but refined. 1 Corinthians 3:13-15 — "the fire shall try every man's work of what sort it is... If any man's work shall be burned, he shall suffer loss: but he himself shall be saved; yet so as by fire."*

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES PASSAGES

### Numbers 31:17-18 — The Command to Kill Used as Evidence That the God of the Old Testament Is Immoral

**The misuse:** Verses 17-18 are presented as arbitrary cruelty — killing males and non-virgin women while keeping virgins.

**What the text establishes:** Moses identifies the specific cause in v.16 — these Midianite women are the same ones Balaam counselled to seduce Israel at Peor, causing the plague that killed 24,000. The command addresses the specific women who were instruments of the seduction-weapon against Israel. The virgin girls who had no part in the Baal-Peor seduction are preserved. This is not arbitrary — it is the direct consequence of the identified harm. The broader theological question of conquest commands belongs to the category of divine judicial action against specific historical wickedness — the Bible addresses this directly in Genesis 15:16 ("the iniquity of the Amorites is not yet full") and Deuteronomy 9:4-5 ("for the wickedness of these nations").

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### Not One Man Lacking — The Commander's Report

[[04. Numbers/NUM_31_REBUILT|Numbers 31:49]] — "there lacketh not one man of us"

This is the most astonishing report in Numbers. Twelve thousand men went to war against five kings, burned their cities, killed all the males of Midian — and not one Israelite died. The officers do not receive this as a military achievement; they receive it as a divine mercy requiring a response of gratitude-atonement. The gold they bring is every man's personal jewelry — chains, bracelets, rings, earrings, tablets — what each man stripped from the enemy for himself (v.53), then gave up to the Tabernacle.

- [[19.Psalm Part 2/PSALM_91_REBUILT|Psalm 91:7]] — "A thousand shall fall at thy side, and ten thousand at thy right hand; but it shall not come nigh thee"
- [[14. 2 Chronicles/2_CHRONICLES_20_REBUILT|2 Chronicles 20:17]] — "Ye shall not need to fight in this battle... stand still, and see the salvation of the LORD"

**What the Bible establishes:** When God goes before His people in commanded warfare, casualty counts are not the measure of military competence — they are the evidence of divine presence. The zero-casualty report of Numbers 31 is the officers' own evidence that God fought this battle. Their response — bringing their gold as atonement for their souls — is the correct response to undeserved survival: not pride in skill but humility before grace.
