## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Moses writes down every departure point of Israel's journeys by the LORD's command. Beginning: Rameses in the first month, fifteenth day — the day after the Passover — Israel went out with a high hand in sight of all Egypt, while Egypt buried the firstborn the LORD had struck; the gods of Egypt were judged. From Rameses: Succoth, Etham, Pi-hahiroth (before Baal-zephon at Migdol), through the sea, three days in Etham wilderness, Marah, Elim (twelve springs, seventy palms), by the Red Sea, wilderness of Sin, Dophkah, Alush, Rephidim (no water), wilderness of Sinai, Kibroth-hattaavah, Hazeroth, Rithmah — then seventeen more stations through the unknown middle wilderness years — Rimmon-parez, Libnah, Rissah, Kehelathah, Mount Shapher, Haradah, Makheloth, Tahath, Tarah, Mithcah, Hashmonah, Moseroth, Bene-jaakan, Hor-hagidgad, Jotbathah, Ebronah, Ezion-gaber, wilderness of Zin (Kadesh). From Kadesh to Mount Hor (edge of Edom). Note: Aaron the priest went up to Mount Hor and died there, the fortieth year, first day of the fifth month. Aaron was 123 years old. King Arad the Canaanite heard they were coming. Then: Zalmonah, Punon, Oboth, Ije-abarim (border of Moab), Dibon-gad, Almon-diblathaim, mountains of Abarim before Nebo, plains of Moab by Jordan — from Beth-jesimoth to Abel-shittim. God speaks: when you cross Jordan, drive out all the land's inhabitants; destroy all their carved images, molten images, and high places; dispossess them and dwell there — I have given you the land. Divide it by lot according to family size. Warning: if you do not drive them out, those left will be pricks in your eyes and thorns in your sides — and I will do to you as I planned to do to them.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture) (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Moses Wrote Their Goings Out" — The Divine Record

**Numbers 33:2** — "Moses wrote their goings out according to their journeys by the commandment of the LORD"

- **Exodus 17:14** — "And the LORD said unto Moses, Write this for a memorial in a book" — the first divine command to write for a memorial; writing as preservation of divine acts
- **Malachi 3:16** — "a book of remembrance was written before him for them that feared the LORD, and that thought upon his name" — the heavenly record mirrors the earthly one; what Moses wrote by divine command is the earthly type of the book of remembrance before God
- **Revelation 20:12** — "And I saw the dead, small and great, stand before God; and the books were opened" — the ultimate divine record; every journey, every station, all recorded
- *Interpretation: The written record of Numbers 33 is not a travel itinerary; it is a divine memorial — a record that God commanded to be kept. Every stage of the wilderness journey was witnessed, noted, and preserved. Psalm 56:8 — "Thou tellest my wanderings: put thou my tears into thy bottle: are they not in thy book?" — God keeps records of His people's journeys; Numbers 33 is the institutional form of that personal promise.*

---

### "With a High Hand" — The Exodus as Public Triumph

**Numbers 33:3** — "the children of Israel went out with an high hand in the sight of all the Egyptians"

- **Exodus 14:8** — "the children of Israel went out with an high hand" — the same phrase at the original departure
- **Micah 7:15** — "According to the days of thy coming out of the land of Egypt will I shew unto him marvellous things" — the Exodus high-hand departure is the model for the eschatological second exodus
- **Isaiah 52:12** — "For ye shall not go out with haste, nor go by flight: for the LORD will go before you; and the God of Israel will be your rereward" — the second exodus is even more deliberate than the first; no flight, no haste
- *Interpretation: The "high hand" departure is the posture of conquest, not flight. Israel does not sneak out of Egypt — they leave openly, publicly, while Egypt buries its dead and acknowledges the supremacy of Israel's God. The forty-two stages recorded in Numbers 33 begin with this public divine triumph.*

---

### The Pricks and Thorns Warning — Incomplete Obedience and Its Consequences

**Numbers 33:55** — "those which ye let remain of them shall be pricks in your eyes, and thorns in your sides, and shall vex you in the land wherein ye dwell"

- **Judges 2:3** — "I will not drive them out from before you; but they shall be as thorns in your sides, and their gods shall be a snare unto you" — God's words to Israel at Bochim after the generation of Joshua dies; the Numbers 33:55 warning is enacted; Israel did not drive them out, and the pricks and thorns arrived
- **Judges 3:1-6** — the nations left in Canaan become both a test and a snare; intermarriage and idol worship follow
- **2 Corinthians 6:14** — "Be ye not unequally yoked together with unbelievers" — the New Covenant application of the separation principle; incomplete separation from what God commanded removed produces the same prick-and-thorn dynamic
- *Interpretation: Numbers 33:55 is a prophecy-warning — if you do not complete the commanded removal, what you leave will become the instrument of your affliction. Judges is the historical demonstration that this warning was accurate. The pricks in the eyes and thorns in the sides become the seven cycles of oppression in Judges, each one produced by the idol-worship that the remaining nations introduced.*

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### PICTURES / CARVED IMAGES (Hebrew: maskiyoth — showpieces, figured stones, carved images)
- **Numbers 33:52** — "destroy all their pictures"
- *Definition here: Maskiyoth are the carved or figured images used in Canaanite worship — relief carvings, standing stones, and figured representations of deities or sacred objects. Leviticus 26:1 — "Ye shall make you no idols nor graven image, neither rear you up a standing image, neither shall ye set up any image of stone in your land" — the same category. The destruction command is not aesthetic censorship but the removal of the physical infrastructure of false worship that would become the snare God warns against in v.55.*

### HIGH PLACES (Hebrew: bamoth — elevated sites of worship)
- **Numbers 33:52** — "quite pluck down all their high places"
- *Definition here: Bamah (high place) is the elevated platform or hill-shrine used for worship outside the Tabernacle/Temple. Israel repeatedly fails to remove the bamoth — 2 Kings 12:3; 14:4; 15:4 — "the high places were not removed." The consistent failure to destroy what Numbers 33:52 commanded is one of the key structural failures of the monarchy period. Even good kings like Joash, Amaziah, and Azariah receive the qualified praise: "he did right in the sight of the LORD — but the high places were not removed."*

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### Forty-Two Stages — The Number of Testing and Completion

Numbers 33 records exactly forty-two stopping points from Rameses to the plains of Moab.

- **Revelation 11:2** — the nations tread the holy city "forty and two months"
- **Revelation 13:5** — the beast is given authority "forty and two months"
- **Matthew 1:17** — the genealogy of Christ organized in three groups of fourteen generations (3 × 14 = 42) — from Abraham to Christ
- *Interpretation: The forty-two stages of the wilderness are the temporal frame of testing and preparation. The number recurs in Revelation as the period of the great tribulation — the compressed eschatological wilderness — and in Matthew's genealogy as the complete sweep of covenant history from promise to fulfilment. The forty-two stages of Numbers 33 are not accidents of geography; they are the complete measure of God's appointed wilderness season.*

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES PASSAGES

### Numbers 33:56 — "I Shall Do unto You, as I Thought to Do unto Them" — Confused with Unconditional Promises

**The misuse:** The warning of Numbers 33:56 is sometimes bypassed in favour of the unconditional covenant promises — as if Israel's security in the land is absolute regardless of obedience.

**What the text establishes:** The covenant with Abraham is unconditional; possession of the land is covenantal and conditional. Numbers 33:56 makes this explicit: the same judgment God intended for the Canaanites will fall on Israel if Israel becomes like the Canaanites. Leviticus 18:28 — "That the land spue not you out also, when ye defile it, as it spued out the nations that were before you." The land itself has a covenantal gag reflex — it expelled the Canaanites and will expel Israel if Israel adopts Canaanite practice. The Babylonian exile is the fulfilment of Numbers 33:56.

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### The Journey Written Down — Every Stage Counts

The command to write the journey in Numbers 33:2 is significant: God values the record of the entire path, not only the destination. The wilderness stages that seem like repetitive entries — pitched here, departed from there — are each a complete human experience: provision, shelter, conflict, or rest at each place.

- **Deuteronomy 8:2-3** — "And thou shalt remember all the way which the LORD thy God led thee these forty years in the wilderness, to humble thee, and to prove thee, to know what was in thine heart... he fed thee with manna, which thou knewest not" — the wilderness journey is to be remembered as a whole; every stage has a purpose
- **Psalm 77:19** — "Thy way is in the sea, and thy path in the great waters, and thy footsteps are not known" — God's path through the wilderness is traceable even when its logic is not visible
- **Hebrews 11:13** — "These all died in faith, not having received the promises, but having seen them afar off... and confessed that they were strangers and pilgrims on the earth" — the forty-two stages are the literal form of the pilgrim identity; every stage a temporary home on the way to the permanent one
