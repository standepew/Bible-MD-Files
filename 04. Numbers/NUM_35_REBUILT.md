## CHAPTER SUMMARY

God commands: the Levites shall receive forty-eight cities from the tribes' inheritances — proportional to each tribe's size. Every city will have a suburb of one thousand cubits in all directions plus two thousand cubits measured outward. Six of the forty-eight will be cities of refuge — three in Canaan, three in the Transjordan. Any person who kills someone unintentionally may flee there and be protected from the blood avenger until standing before the congregation for judgment. The law defines murder: using an instrument of iron, a thrown stone, a hand weapon — the murderer shall be put to death. If with hatred or lying in wait: murderer; blood avenger kills him. The blood avenger acts justly in executing the murderer. Manslaughter: a sudden thrust without enmity, or casting something without seeing the victim, or a stone that may cause death but was not thrown at the person in hatred — the congregation shall judge. If manslaughter, the congregation restores the person to the city of refuge until the high priest's death. If the manslayer leaves the city boundary, the blood avenger may kill him without guilt — because he should have remained until the high priest died. After the high priest's death, the manslayer returns freely to his inheritance. This is a statute for all generations. The rule of evidence: no one is put to death on one witness. No ransom may substitute for a murderer's life — he must die. No ransom may substitute for the manslayer leaving the city of refuge early — he must wait for the high priest's death. Blood defiles the land; the land cannot be cleansed of spilt blood except by the blood of the one who shed it. Defile not the land wherein I dwell — for I the LORD dwell among the children of Israel.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture) (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### Cities of Refuge — The Type of Christ as Refuge

**Numbers 35:11-15** — the manslayer flees to the city of refuge; safe from the avenger while within its borders

- **Hebrews 6:18** — "that by two immutable things, in which it was impossible for God to lie, we might have a strong consolation, who have fled for refuge to lay hold upon the hope set before us" — the writer of Hebrews uses the city of refuge imagery directly: we have "fled for refuge"; the city of refuge is the type of Christ; those who flee to Him are safe from the avenger
- **Psalm 46:1** — "God is our refuge and strength, a very present help in trouble" — the LORD Himself as refuge; the cities of refuge are the institutional type of the divine refuge
- **Psalm 142:4-5** — "I looked on my right hand, and beheld, but there was no man that would know me: refuge failed me; no man cared for my soul. I cried unto thee, O LORD: I said, Thou art my refuge" — the city of refuge failing as the type of the LORD who never fails as refuge
- *Interpretation: The city of refuge is one of the richest types in the Torah. The manslayer — not guilty of intentional murder but guilty of causing a death — needs a place of safety from the legitimate avenger. Christ is that place: those who flee to Him are safe from the divine blood-avenger not because they are innocent but because the High Priest (Christ) has died. Hebrews 6:18 explicitly uses the "fled for refuge" language. The manslayer's freedom after the high priest's death corresponds directly to the believer's freedom from condemnation through Christ's death.*

### The Death of the High Priest Frees the Manslayer

**Numbers 35:25, 28** — the manslayer remains in the city of refuge until the high priest's death; then goes free to his inheritance

- **Hebrews 9:15** — "And for this cause he is the mediator of the new testament, that by means of death, for the redemption of the transgressions that were under the first testament, they which are called might receive the promise of eternal inheritance" — the death of the great High Priest is what releases the people to their eternal inheritance; Numbers 35 is the precise type
- **Hebrews 7:23-24** — "they truly were many priests, because they were not suffered to continue by reason of death: But this man, because he continueth ever, hath an unchangeable priesthood" — the annual high priest dies and frees the manslayer; Christ's priesthood is permanent, meaning His atonement-death operates once for all (Hebrews 9:12)
- *Interpretation: The most precise typological point in Numbers 35: the manslayer goes free specifically through the death of the high priest. This cannot be coincidence — it is the explicit teaching of Hebrews. The Old Covenant high priest dying freed the manslayer in his generation; Christ, the eternal High Priest, died once and freed all who flee to Him in every generation. The city of refuge is valid "throughout your generations" (Numbers 35:29) — because the High Priest whose death effects the release has died once and lives forever.*

### "Blood Defiles the Land" — The Land Covenant

**Numbers 35:33-34** — "So ye shall not pollute the land wherein ye are: for blood it defileth the land... Defile not therefore the land which ye shall inhabit, wherein I dwell: for I the LORD dwell among the children of Israel"

- **Genesis 4:10** — "the voice of thy brother's blood crieth unto me from the ground" — shed blood cries to God; the ground is defiled by it; this is the first recorded blood-defilement of the land
- **Leviticus 18:25, 28** — the land itself vomited out the Canaanites for their sins; the land vomits Israel for the same sins if Israel commits them
- **Isaiah 26:21** — "For, behold, the LORD cometh out of his place to punish the inhabitants of the earth for their iniquity: the earth also shall disclose her blood, and shall no more cover her slain"
- *Interpretation: The land is not morally neutral — it responds to what is done upon it. Blood shed unjustly defiles the land and cries to God. Only the blood of the guilty party can cleanse it. This is why capital punishment in the Torah is not about deterrence but about land-cleansing: unpunished bloodshed accumulates in the land until it is cleansed or until the land expels its inhabitants. Numbers 35:34 closes with the most important reason: I the LORD dwell among you. The holy God cannot coexist with a land full of uncleansed bloodguilt.*

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### AVENGER OF BLOOD (Hebrew: go'el ha-dam — redeemer of the blood)
- **Numbers 35:12, 19, 21, 24, 25, 27** — the one who pursues the manslayer/murderer
- *Definition here: The go'el (redeemer/kinsman-redeemer) is the nearest male relative obligated to restore what was lost to the family. In the case of murder, the go'el ha-dam (redeemer of blood) is obligated to pursue and execute the murderer — the blood of the slain must be redeemed by the death of the killer. Ruth 4 shows the same go'el acting as property-redeemer; Numbers 35 shows the same role as blood-redeemer. Christ as go'el: He is the kinsman-redeemer who redeems blood — His own blood shed to redeem ours (Galatians 4:4-5; Hebrews 2:14-15).*

### UNAWARES / WITHOUT PREMEDITATION (Hebrew: bishgagah — in error, inadvertently)
- **Numbers 35:11, 15** — "killeth any person at unawares"
- *Definition here: Bishgagah is the same word used in Leviticus 4 and Numbers 15 for inadvertent sin — sin committed without full knowledge or intent. The city of refuge is designed for this category — the death that happened in the moment without premeditation, malice, or laying in wait. The law takes seriously that not every death is murder; intent is the dividing line. Acts 17:30 — "the times of this ignorance God winked at; but now commandeth all men every where to repent" — the gospel's application of the bishgagah principle to spiritual matters.*

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The City of Refuge — The Complete Type

The city of refuge contains the most developed and explicit type in the Torah for Christ's atoning work:

| Numbers 35 Feature | Typological Correspondence |
|---|---|
| Manslayer flees to city | Sinner flees to Christ for refuge |
| City open to Israelite, stranger, sojourner alike (v.15) | No distinction in Christ (Galatians 3:28) |
| Safe inside the city; avenger cannot touch him within | "There is therefore now no condemnation" (Romans 8:1) |
| Must remain until high priest dies | Freedom comes through the High Priest's death |
| After high priest's death: free to return to inheritance | After Christ's death: believers enter eternal inheritance |
| Leaves early: loses protection | Apostasy: removes oneself from the refuge (Hebrews 10:26-31) |

**Hebrews 6:18** — "who have fled for refuge to lay hold upon the hope set before us: Which hope we have as an anchor of the soul, both sure and stedfast, and which entereth into that within the veil."

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES PASSAGES

### Numbers 35:30 — "By the Mouth of Witnesses" Applied Without Its Context

**The misuse:** "Two witnesses" (Deuteronomy 17:6; 19:15) is cited from Numbers 35:30 as though the requirement is purely procedural — about number of witnesses regardless of quality.

**What the text establishes:** The two-witness rule is protective — one witness cannot be sufficient to execute a person. The rule is specifically framed here in the context of capital punishment ("cause him to die"). Deuteronomy 19:16-21 addresses false witnesses: if a witness is found to have testified falsely in a capital case, the false witness receives the punishment the accused would have received. The evidentiary standard is not merely quantitative (two people) but qualitative (truthful witnesses whose testimony can withstand challenge).

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### The Levites Distributed Throughout — The Priestly People Among the People

The forty-eight Levitical cities are distributed through all twelve tribes' territories. The tribe with no land of its own (Numbers 18:20 — "I am thy part and thine inheritance") is given cities in every tribe's land — so that no Israelite is far from a Levite, and no region is without priestly presence.

- [[60. 1 Peter/1_PETER_02_REBUILT|1 Peter 2:9]] — "But ye are a chosen generation, a royal priesthood, an holy nation" — the New Covenant people distributed through all nations as the Levites were distributed through all tribes
- [[40. Matthew/MATTHEW_05_REBUILT|Matthew 5:13-14]] — "Ye are the salt of the earth... Ye are the light of the world" — the salt is distributed through all the food; the light spread through all the darkness; the Levitical distribution is the Torah type of the dispersed New Covenant priesthood embedded in every tribe, tongue, and nation
