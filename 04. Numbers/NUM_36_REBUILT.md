## CHAPTER SUMMARY

The chief fathers of the families of Gilead son of Machir son of Manasseh come before Moses and the princes. They raise the problem created by Numbers 27's ruling: the LORD commanded the daughters of Zelophehad to receive their father's inheritance. But if they marry men of other tribes, their inheritance will pass to that tribe's possession. And in the Year of Jubilee — when all property returns to its original tribal allotment — the land will stay with the husband's tribe, not return to Manasseh. The tribal inheritance will be permanently diminished. Moses presents the LORD's response: the tribe of Joseph has said well. The command: the daughters of Zelophehad shall marry whomever they think best — but they must marry within the family of their father's tribe. No inheritance of Israel shall be transferred from tribe to tribe — every Israelite shall keep to the inheritance of the tribe of their fathers. Every daughter in Israel who possesses an inheritance must marry within her father's tribe. The daughters obey: Mahlah, Tirzah, Hoglah, Milcah, and Noah — all five — marry their father's brothers' sons; they marry within Manasseh. Their inheritance remains in their father's tribe. Final verse: these are the commandments and judgments which the LORD commanded by the hand of Moses to the children of Israel in the plains of Moab by Jordan near Jericho.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture) (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### The Inheritance That Must Not Transfer — Tribal Integrity and Jubilee

**Numbers 36:4** — "And when the jubile of the children of Israel shall be, then shall their inheritance be put unto the inheritance of the tribe whereunto they are received: so shall their inheritance be taken away from the inheritance of the tribe of our fathers"

- **Leviticus 25:10-13** — the Year of Jubilee: "ye shall return every man unto his possession, and ye shall return every man unto his family" — the Jubilee returns all property to its original owner and all servants to their families; it is the great reset
- **Numbers 36:4** — but if the inheritance passes to another tribe through marriage, the Jubilee reset returns it to the husband's tribe, not the wife's — the Jubilee cannot restore what has been permanently transferred by tribal-exogamy marriage
- **Ruth 4:3-6** — the kinsman-redeemer system preserves inheritance within the family line; Boaz redeems the land of Elimelech for Naomi; the same tribal-inheritance concern as Numbers 36, resolved through the go'el mechanism
- *Interpretation: The Numbers 36 ruling closes the loop opened in Numbers 27. The daughters have the right to inherit (Numbers 27); the tribe has the right to retain its allotment (Numbers 36). The resolution — marry within the tribe — preserves both rights simultaneously. This is the Bible's model for resolving competing legitimate claims: not abolishing either right but structuring the exercise of both.*

### "Marry Whom They Think Best" — Freedom Within Boundaries

**Numbers 36:6** — "Let them marry to whom they think best; only to the family of the tribe of their father shall they marry"

- **1 Corinthians 7:39** — "The wife is bound by the law as long as her husband liveth; but if her husband be dead, she is at liberty to be married to whom she will; only in the Lord" — the New Covenant parallel structure: "marry whom you will — only in the Lord." The freedom within the covenant structure is the same pattern as Numbers 36:6
- **2 Corinthians 6:14** — "Be ye not unequally yoked together with unbelievers" — the principle of covenant-community marriage; freedom of choice within the covenant boundary
- *Interpretation: The Numbers 36 ruling is neither arranged marriage nor unrestricted choice — it is free choice within a defined boundary. God does not specify which cousin; the daughters choose. But they choose within Manasseh. The New Covenant equivalent: "marry whom you will — only in the Lord" (1 Corinthians 7:39). The pattern is identical: wide freedom within covenant community.*

### The Daughters Obey — Obedience as the Closing Note of Numbers

**Numbers 36:10-12** — "Even as the LORD commanded Moses, so did the daughters of Zelophehad"

- **Numbers 27:7** — God said "the daughters of Zelophehad speak right" — their original plea was validated
- **Numbers 36:10** — their obedience to the subsequent command is equally complete
- **John 2:5** — Mary to the servants at Cana: "Whatsoever he saith unto you, do it" — the posture of complete obedience to divine command regardless of how the instruction develops
- *Interpretation: The daughters of Zelophehad are one of Numbers' most positive examples. They pleaded their case before Moses, before Eleazar, before the princes, before all the congregation (Numbers 27:2). God validated their case. Then when the consequence of their inheritance created a new problem, God addressed that too — and they obeyed without recorded protest. They first spoke right; then they acted right. The closing note of Numbers is an act of obedience by five women who did exactly what the LORD commanded.*

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### JUBILE / JUBILEE (Hebrew: yovel — ram's horn; year of the ram's horn blast)
- **Numbers 36:4** — the Jubilee year referenced as the point when a tribal transfer would become permanent
- *Definition here: The Jubilee (every fiftieth year) is the great covenant reset — all debts cancelled, all enslaved Israelites freed, all land returned to original family owners. Leviticus 25:10 — "And ye shall hallow the fiftieth year, and proclaim liberty throughout all the land unto all the inhabitants thereof." The Jubilee is the economic sabbath-of-sabbaths. It cannot reverse a marriage; it can return land — but only to the tribe it left, not to the tribe of origin if transferred through marriage to another tribe. Numbers 36 identifies the limit of the Jubilee: what the law of marriage effects, the Jubilee cannot undo.*

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### "Their Inheritance Remained" — The Preserved Inheritance

**Numbers 36:12** — "their inheritance remained in the tribe of the family of their father"

The closing image of Numbers is an inheritance that remained — that was not lost, not transferred, not diminished. Five women obeyed, and the inheritance stayed where it belonged.

- **1 Peter 1:4** — "To an inheritance incorruptible, and undefiled, and that fadeth not away, reserved in heaven for you" — the New Covenant inheritance that cannot be transferred, diminished, or lost; it is "reserved" — the same protecting-in-place that Numbers 36 achieves through obedience
- **Romans 8:17** — "heirs of God, and joint-heirs with Christ" — the inheritance is in Christ; what is in Christ does not transfer to the enemy; it remains
- *Interpretation: Numbers ends with an inheritance that remained. The entire book — forty years of wilderness, murmuring, rebellion, judgment, census, offering law, borders, cities, vows, warfare — all of it concludes with a five-word declaration: their inheritance remained. This is the final theological statement of the book: after everything, the inheritance is still there. God's covenantal promises, despite every human failure catalogued in Numbers, remain.*

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES PASSAGES

### Numbers 36:6 — Used to Restrict Marriage to Literal Family Members

**The misuse:** The command for the daughters to marry "within the family of the tribe" is sometimes read as endorsement of cousin marriage generally or restricted kinship marriage as a biblical norm.

**What the text establishes:** The ruling is specific to the unique circumstance of daughters who inherit tribal land — a category created by the Numbers 27 ruling. It is a property law, not a universal marriage law. The general principle throughout Leviticus (Leviticus 18) prohibits various close-kin marriages. The Numbers 36 ruling is a targeted solution to a targeted problem: how to preserve tribal land allocation when a daughter inherits and the Jubilee cannot reverse a tribal transfer. It does not establish a norm of cousin marriage for all Israelites.

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### The Bookends of Numbers — Two Censuses; One Inheritance

Numbers opens with a census (Numbers 1) and closes with the preservation of an inheritance (Numbers 36). Between them: the entire experience of the wilderness generation and the rising of a new generation who will actually enter the land.

The five daughters of Zelophehad appear in both the second census (Numbers 26:33) and the closing chapter (Numbers 36:10-12). They are the connective thread of the book's final movement: women who claimed their right under the law (Numbers 27), were validated by God, and then obeyed the condition placed upon that right (Numbers 36). They are the positive image of the generation that will enter — those who stand before God with their legitimate case, hear His answer, and do exactly what He says.

**What the Bible establishes as the final word of Numbers:** Obedience preserved the inheritance. The forty years of Numbers is the long account of disobedience that lost the inheritance for one generation and preserved it, by grace, for the next. The book closes with five women doing exactly as the LORD commanded — and their inheritance remained.
