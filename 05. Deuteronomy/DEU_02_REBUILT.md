## CHAPTER SUMMARY

After the thirty-eight years of wandering until the generation of fighting men was consumed, Israel turns north from Seir. God instructs Moses to pass through the territory of Esau (Edom), Moab, and Ammon without fighting — He has assigned those lands to those peoples. Israel is to buy food and water from Esau's descendants, recognizing God's provision of their walking through the great wilderness. The chapter inserts historical notes on the giant nations (Emims, Zamzummims, Horims, Avims) that God cleared from those territories for Esau, Moab, and Ammon — establishing that God moves nations and settles peoples according to His sovereign purposes. When Israel reaches the Arnon, God commands engagement: Sihon king of Heshbon is given into their hand. God hardened Sihon's heart so that he refused Israel's peaceful passage offer, provoking battle. Israel defeats Sihon utterly and takes all his cities — their first conquest, already east of Jordan.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### God Assigns National Boundaries — No Territory Is Accident

**Deuteronomy 2:5** — "I will not give you of their land, no, not so much as a foot breadth; because I have given mount Seir unto Esau for a possession."  
**Deuteronomy 2:9** — "I will not give thee of their land for a possession; because I have given Ar unto the children of Lot for a possession."  
**Deuteronomy 2:19** — "I will not give thee of the land of the children of Ammon any possession; because I have given it unto the children of Lot"

- **Deuteronomy 32:8** — "When the Most High divided to the nations their inheritance, when he separated the sons of Adam, he set the bounds of the people according to the number of the children of Israel."
- **Acts 17:26** — "And hath made of one blood all nations of men for to dwell on all the face of the earth, and hath determined the times before appointed, and the bounds of their habitation"
- **Genesis 25:23** — To Rebekah: "Two nations are in thy womb... and the elder shall serve the younger" — Esau/Edom's destiny decreed before birth
- **Obadiah 1:3** — "The pride of thine heart hath deceived thee, thou that dwellest in the clefts of the rock" — addressing Esau/Edom centuries later in their God-assigned territory
  - *Interpretation:* Territorial assignments are God's work, not accidents of history. God gave Seir to Esau and Ar to Lot's descendants, just as He gave Canaan to Israel. Acts 17:26 makes this a universal principle: God has determined the boundaries of all nations. This means no map is merely political — behind all national geography is divine ordering. Israel is forbidden to covet or take what God assigned to another people.

### "Forty Years the LORD Thy God Hath Been with Thee; Thou Hast Lacked Nothing"

**Deuteronomy 2:7** — "For the LORD thy God hath blessed thee in all the works of thy hand: he knoweth thy walking through this great wilderness: these forty years the LORD thy God hath been with thee; thou hast lacked nothing."

- **Deuteronomy 8:4** — "Thy raiment waxed not old upon thee, neither did thy foot swell, these forty years."
- **Nehemiah 9:21** — "Yea, forty years didst thou sustain them in the wilderness, so that they lacked nothing; their clothes waxed not old, and their feet swelled not."
- **Psalm 23:1** — "The LORD is my shepherd; I shall not want." — the same principle stated as personal testimony
- **Philippians 4:19** — "But my God shall supply all your need according to his riches in glory by Christ Jesus."
  - *Interpretation:* God's care for Israel in the wilderness — forty years of provision, clothing that never wore out, feet that never swelled — is the experiential foundation of the promise "thou hast lacked nothing." This is not prosperity theology; it is covenant faithfulness. God provided what was needed for the journey He assigned. The same God who sustained Israel in the wilderness sustains His people through every wilderness He leads them through.

### The Generation of Men of War Consumed — God's Oath Fulfilled

**Deuteronomy 2:14-15** — "And the space in which we came from Kadesh-barnea, until we were come over the brook Zered, was thirty and eight years; until all the generation of the men of war were wasted out from among the host, as the LORD sware unto them. For indeed the hand of the LORD was against them, to destroy them from among the host, until they were consumed."

- **Numbers 14:29-33** — "Your carcases shall fall in this wilderness; and all that were numbered of you, according to your whole number, from twenty years old and upward... But your little ones, which ye said should be a prey, them will I bring in, and they shall know the land which ye have despised."
- **1 Corinthians 10:5-6** — "But with many of them God was not well pleased: for they were overthrown in the wilderness. Now these things were our examples, to the intent we should not lust after evil things, as they also lusted."
- **Hebrews 3:17** — "But with whom was he grieved forty years? was it not with them that had sinned, whose carcases fell in the wilderness?"
  - *Interpretation:* The thirty-eight years from Kadesh-barnea to Zered is the exact fulfillment of God's oath. God counted every one. When the last fighting man of the condemned generation died, then and only then did God move Israel forward. The hand of the LORD was specifically against them — not passively, but actively working to fulfill the sworn judgment. God's oaths are immovable in both directions: the promise to the fathers is kept; the judgment on the rebels is also kept.

### God Cleared Nations Before Esau, Moab, Ammon — Parallel to Israel's Conquest

**Deuteronomy 2:12** — "The Horims also dwelt in Seir beforetime; but the children of Esau succeeded them, when they had destroyed them from before them, and dwelt in their stead; as Israel did unto the land of his possession, which the LORD gave unto them."  
**Deuteronomy 2:20-21** — "That also was accounted a land of giants: giants dwelt therein in old time; and the Ammonites call them Zamzummims; A people great, and many, and tall, as the Anakims; but the LORD destroyed them before them"

- **Acts 17:26** — God "hath determined the times before appointed, and the bounds of their habitation"
- **Amos 9:7** — "Have not I brought up Israel out of the land of Egypt? and the Philistines from Caphtor, and the Syrians from Kir?" — God moves ALL nations, not just Israel
  - *Interpretation:* The parenthetical notes on the Emims, Zamzummims, Horims, and Avims serve a theological purpose: God has been moving nations and clearing territories throughout history, not just for Israel. He cleared Seir for Esau, Ar for Moab, Ammon's territory for Lot's children, and Philistia for the Caphtorims. Israel's conquest of Canaan is one instance of God's universal governance of national history. No nation's history is outside His hand.

### Sihon's Heart Hardened — God's Sovereignty in Judgment

**Deuteronomy 2:30** — "But Sihon king of Heshbon would not let us pass by him: for the LORD thy God hardened his spirit, and made his heart obstinate, that he might deliver him into thy hand"

- **Exodus 4:21** — "And I will harden Pharaoh's heart" — the same pattern: God hardens in judgment those who have already established a course of hostility
- **Exodus 9:16** — "And in very deed for this cause have I raised thee up, for to shew in thee my power; and that my name may be declared throughout all the earth" — Pharaoh's hardening is purposeful: God's glory in judgment
- **Romans 9:17-18** — "For the scripture saith unto Pharaoh, Even for this same purpose have I raised thee up, that I might shew my power in thee, and that my name might be declared throughout all the earth. Therefore hath he mercy on whom he will have mercy, and whom he will he hardeneth."
- **Romans 1:24, 26, 28** — God "gave them up," "gave them over" — hardening as God's judicial act of handing men over to the direction they have already chosen
  - *Interpretation:* Sihon's hardening must be read within the full biblical pattern. God does not harden hearts arbitrarily. Sihon is already an enemy of God's people, already an obstacle who would refuse peaceful passage. God's hardening of Sihon's spirit confirms and completes what Sihon's own character had already established — it is a judicial act, not an arbitrary one. The result is that Sihon's destruction gives Israel their first victory east of Jordan and causes the dread of Israel to fall upon all surrounding nations (verse 25). God is glorified in the hardening; His name goes out to all the nations.

### Israel's Offer of Peace Before War — The Gospel Pattern

**Deuteronomy 2:26-29** — "And I sent messengers out of the wilderness of Kedemoth unto Sihon king of Heshbon with words of peace, saying, Let me pass through thy land: I will go along by the high way, I will neither turn unto the right hand nor to the left..."

- **Deuteronomy 20:10** — "When thou comest nigh unto a city to fight against it, then proclaim peace unto it" — the later law codifies this pattern
- **Luke 14:32** — "Or else, while the other is yet a great way off, he sendeth an ambassage, and desireth conditions of peace" — Jesus' parable uses this exact military logic
- **Romans 5:10** — "For if, when we were enemies, we were reconciled to God by the death of his Son" — God's offer of peace before judgment
- **2 Corinthians 5:20** — "We are ambassadors for Christ... as though God did beseech you by us: we pray you in Christ's stead, be ye reconciled to God"
  - *Interpretation:* Israel's offer of peaceful passage to Sihon mirrors the gospel pattern: peace is offered before judgment. Sihon's rejection of the peace offer removes any claim of injustice from his destruction. The same principle operates in the gospel: God sends "words of peace" to men through His ambassadors; those who refuse the terms of peace receive the judgment they chose. The offer of peace makes the subsequent judgment righteous.

### "This Day Will I Begin to Put the Dread of Thee Upon the Nations"

**Deuteronomy 2:25** — "This day will I begin to put the dread of thee and the fear of thee upon the nations that are under the whole heaven, who shall hear report of thee, and shall tremble, and be in anguish because of thee."

- **Joshua 2:9-11** — Rahab to the spies: "I know that the LORD hath given you the land; and that your terror is fallen upon us, and that all the inhabitants of the land faint because of you... we have heard how the LORD dried up the water of the Red sea for you... and as soon as we had heard these things, our hearts did melt"
- **Joshua 5:1** — "And it came to pass, when all the kings of the Amorites, which were on the side of Jordan westward... heard that the LORD had dried up the waters of Jordan... their heart melted, neither was there spirit in them any more"
- **Exodus 15:14-16** — The Song of Moses anticipates this: "The people shall hear, and be afraid: sorrow shall take hold on the inhabitants of Palestina... all the inhabitants of Canaan shall melt away"
  - *Interpretation:* God's declaration in Deuteronomy 2:25 is a covenant promise that precedes the conquest by decades. It is fulfilled precisely in Joshua 2 (Rahab's testimony), Joshua 5 (the melting of the kings' hearts), and throughout the conquest. God's word went before Israel as a sword, striking fear into hearts before a single arrow was fired. The sequence is always: God speaks → God acts → God's reputation spreads → the nations fear.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### "Hardened His Spirit / Made His Heart Obstinate" (chazaq ruach / kabed lev)

- **Deuteronomy 2:30** — God's hardening of Sihon is judicial, completing what Sihon's own character had established
- Compare **Exodus 4:21** (God hardens Pharaoh), **Romans 1:24** (God "gave them up") — hardening is always God's judicial ratification of a human heart's chosen direction
- Hardening is NOT God forcing innocent men to sin; it is God confirming in judgment the direction already chosen. Sihon was already hostile (he refused the peaceful passage). God's hardening sealed the outcome.

### "Devoted to Destruction" (charam — to utterly destroy, to put under the ban)

- **Deuteronomy 2:34** — "we... utterly destroyed the men, and the women, and the little ones, of every city, we left none to remain"
- Charam appears throughout the conquest — it is total consecration to destruction, the opposite of taking spoil for oneself
- **Achan's sin in Joshua 7** is the violation of charam — he took what was devoted to destruction, bringing judgment on the camp
- The theological basis: the Canaanite nations had filled up the measure of their iniquity (Genesis 15:16 — "the iniquity of the Amorites is not yet full"). Charam is God's judicial sentence executed through Israel as His instrument.

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### Giants Cleared by God — Pattern of the Seed of the Serpent

The repeated mention of the Emims, Zamzummims, Anakims, Horims — giant nations cleared by God before various peoples — connects to the Genesis 6 account of the Nephilim and the ongoing thread of the supernatural corruption of the human line.

- **Genesis 6:4** — "There were giants in the earth in those days; and also after that" — the giant presence continues after the flood
- **Numbers 13:33** — The spies report "the sons of Anak, which come of the giants" at Kadesh-barnea
- **Joshua 11:21-22** — Joshua destroys the Anakim from the hill country; remnants survive in Gaza, Gath, Ashdod — Goliath of Gath is a later descendant (1 Samuel 17)
- *The giants represent the ongoing attempt to corrupt the human line that would produce the Seed of the woman (Genesis 3:15). God systematically removes them from the lands He assigns to the peoples He governs.*

### Sihon = First of the Two Giant-Kings East of Jordan

Sihon (chapter 2) and Og (chapter 3) are always paired in Scripture as the prototypical pagan kings defeated by Israel. After the conquest, their defeat becomes the standard reference point for celebrating God's power:

- **Psalm 135:11** — "Sihon king of the Amorites, and Og king of Bashan, and all the kingdoms of Canaan"
- **Psalm 136:19-20** — "Sihon king of the Amorites... and Og the king of Bashan: for his mercy endureth for ever"
- **Nehemiah 9:22** — Rehearsed in Israel's national prayer of repentance
- Their defeat is a permanent part of Israel's testimony of God's faithfulness, rehearsed in worship for generations.
