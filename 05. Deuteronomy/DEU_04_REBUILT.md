## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Moses issues the solemn command: do not add to or take away from God's word. The Baal-Peor incident is cited as a living object lesson — those who followed false gods are dead; those who cleaved to the LORD are alive this day. Israel's statutes, received from God, are their wisdom in the sight of all nations. Moses rehearses the Horeb experience: no visible form, only a voice — the foundation of the prohibition against images. God is described as a consuming fire, a jealous God. Moses prophesies that when Israel corrupts herself and makes images, God will scatter them among the nations, where they will serve man-made gods of wood and stone. But in the latter days, if they seek the LORD from there with all their heart and soul, they will find Him — for He is merciful and will not forsake the covenant of the fathers. The chapter closes with the declaration that there is none else beside the LORD, and that keeping His commandments is for Israel's good. Three cities of refuge are then set apart east of Jordan.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Ye Shall Not Add... Neither Shall Ye Diminish" — The Complete Word

**Deuteronomy 4:2** — "Ye shall not add unto the word which I command you, neither shall ye diminish ought from it, that ye may keep the commandments of the LORD your God which I command you."

- **Deuteronomy 12:32** — "What thing soever I command you, observe to do it: thou shalt not add thereto, nor diminish from it." — repeated for emphasis
- **Proverbs 30:5-6** — "Every word of God is pure: he is a shield unto them that put their trust in him. Add thou not unto his words, lest he reprove thee, and thou be found a liar."
- **Revelation 22:18-19** — "For I testify unto every man that heareth the words of the prophecy of this book, If any man shall add unto these things, God shall add unto him the plagues that are written in this book: And if any man shall take away from the words of the book of this prophecy, God shall take away his part out of the book of life"
- **Mark 7:13** — "Making the word of God of none effect through your tradition, which ye have delivered"
- **Galatians 1:8-9** — "But though we, or an angel from heaven, preach any other gospel unto you than that which we have preached unto you, let him be accursed."
  - *Interpretation:* The add-not/diminish-not command bookends the entire Bible (Deuteronomy 4:2 at the opening of the covenant; Revelation 22:18-19 at the close of the canon). It is not a suggestion but a covenant boundary. Any theological framework that requires adding to Scripture (tradition as equal authority, extra-biblical prophecy as binding doctrine) or diminishing from it (dismissing whole testaments, allegorizing away literal prophecy) violates this foundational command. The Bible is complete as given. This is the foundational Sola Scriptura verse.

### Baal-Peor — Idolatry's Death Toll as Object Lesson

**Deuteronomy 4:3-4** — "Your eyes have seen what the LORD did because of Baal-peor: for all the men that followed Baal-peor, the LORD thy God hath destroyed them from among you. But ye that did cleave unto the LORD your God are alive every one of you this day."

- **Numbers 25:1-9** — The original incident: Israel joined to Baal-peor, committed harlotry with Moab, ate the sacrifices of the dead; a plague killed 24,000
- **Psalm 106:28-29** — "They joined themselves also unto Baal-peor, and ate the sacrifices of the dead. Thus they provoked him to anger with their inventions: and the plague brake in upon them."
- **1 Corinthians 10:8** — "Neither let us commit fornication, as some of them committed, and fell in one day three and twenty thousand" — Paul draws from Baal-peor as a warning to New Testament believers
  - *Interpretation:* Moses presents Baal-peor not as ancient history but as a living demonstration before the eyes of this new generation: "your eyes have seen." The lesson is binary — those who followed Baal-peor are dead; those who cleaved to the LORD are alive. Idolatry kills. Faithfulness preserves. The simplicity of the evidence is the point. Paul extends this exact lesson to the church.

### "Ye Heard the Voice of the Words, But Saw No Similitude"

**Deuteronomy 4:12** — "And the LORD spake unto you out of the midst of the fire: ye heard the voice of the words, but saw no similitude; only ye heard a voice."
**Deuteronomy 4:15** — "Take ye therefore good heed unto yourselves; for ye saw no manner of similitude on the day that the LORD spake unto you in Horeb out of the midst of the fire"

- **Exodus 20:4** — "Thou shalt not make unto thee any graven image, or any likeness of any thing that is in heaven above, or that is in the earth beneath" — the commandment grounded in this very fact
- **John 1:18** — "No man hath seen God at any time; the only begotten Son, which is in the bosom of the Father, he hath declared him."
- **John 4:24** — "God is a Spirit: and they that worship him must worship him in spirit and in truth."
- **Isaiah 40:18** — "To whom then will ye liken God? or what likeness will ye compare unto him?"
- **Acts 17:29** — "Forasmuch then as we are the offspring of God, we ought not to think that the Godhead is like unto gold, or silver, or stone, graven by art and man's device."
  - *Interpretation:* The prohibition of images is rooted in epistemology — you cannot make an image of what you have not seen. Israel heard a voice at Horeb; they saw no form. Therefore any image is a lie — it claims to represent what has not been revealed visually. The second commandment is not arbitrary; it is the direct application of what happened at Horeb. God chose to reveal Himself through His Word (voice/words), not through visual form. Any image substitutes human invention for divine self-revelation.

### "The LORD Thy God Is a Consuming Fire, Even a Jealous God"

**Deuteronomy 4:24** — "For the LORD thy God is a consuming fire, even a jealous God."

- **Exodus 3:2** — The burning bush: fire that burns but does not consume — the paradox of God's holy fire
- **Exodus 19:18** — Sinai in fire: "And mount Sinai was altogether on a smoke, because the LORD descended upon it in fire"
- **Exodus 24:17** — "And the sight of the glory of the LORD was like devouring fire on the top of the mount in the eyes of the children of Israel."
- **Isaiah 33:14** — "Who among us shall dwell with the devouring fire? who among us shall dwell with everlasting burnings?" — the terror of God's holiness
- **Hebrews 12:29** — "For our God is a consuming fire." — the New Testament directly quotes Deuteronomy 4:24 and applies it to the God of the New Covenant
- **Hebrews 12:25-29** — Context: even more fearful under the new covenant because the revelation is greater; if they who refused Moses escaped not, how shall we escape who refuse Him who speaks from heaven?
  - *Interpretation:* "Consuming fire" is not a metaphor to be softened. Hebrews 12:29 applies it directly to New Testament believers worshipping the same God. God's holiness has not diminished between Testaments. The fire that will consume all that is not of God (1 Corinthians 3:13 — "the fire shall try every man's work") is the same fire that descended on Sinai. Jealousy in God is not a flaw; it is the perfection of His covenant love — He cannot share His people's loyalty with non-gods without violating His own nature.

### Prophecy of Scattering — Fulfilled Literally

**Deuteronomy 4:26-28** — "I call heaven and earth to witness against you this day, that ye shall soon utterly perish from off the land... And the LORD shall scatter you among the nations, and ye shall be left few in number among the heathen, whither the LORD shall lead you. And there ye shall serve gods, the work of men's hands, wood and stone"

- **Deuteronomy 28:64** — The fuller elaboration: "And the LORD shall scatter thee among all people, from the one end of the earth even unto the other"
- **Leviticus 26:33** — "And I will scatter you among the heathen, and will draw out a sword after you: and your land shall be desolate, and your cities waste."
- **2 Kings 17:18-20** — Fulfilled in the northern kingdom: "Therefore the LORD was very angry with Israel, and removed them out of his sight: there was none left but the tribe of Judah only."
- **2 Kings 25:11** — Fulfilled in Judah: "Now the rest of the people that were left in the city... did Nebuzaradan the captain of the guard carry away."
- **Luke 21:24** — Jesus: "And they shall fall by the edge of the sword, and shall be led away captive into all nations" — fulfilled in AD 70
  - *Interpretation:* Moses prophesies the scattering in Deuteronomy 4 as a future certainty. It was fulfilled first in the Assyrian captivity of the northern kingdom (2 Kings 17), then in the Babylonian captivity (2 Kings 25), and again in the Roman dispersion after AD 70. The prophecy was not vague — Moses specified nations (plural), few in number, serving man-made gods. These details were fulfilled exactly.

### "In the Latter Days... If Thou Seek the LORD" — Return Prophesied

**Deuteronomy 4:29-31** — "But if from thence thou shalt seek the LORD thy God, thou shalt find him, if thou seek him with all thy heart and with all thy soul. When thou art in tribulation, and all these things are come upon thee, even in the latter days, if thou turn to the LORD thy God, and shalt be obedient unto his voice; (For the LORD thy God is a merciful God;) he will not forsake thee, neither destroy thee, nor forget the covenant of thy fathers which he sware unto them."

- **Jeremiah 29:13** — "And ye shall seek me, and find me, when ye shall search for me with all your heart." — same condition, same promise
- **Hosea 5:15** — "I will go and return to my place, till they acknowledge their offence, and seek my face: in their affliction they will seek me early."
- **Zechariah 12:10** — "And I will pour upon the house of David, and upon the inhabitants of Jerusalem, the spirit of grace and of supplications: and they shall look upon me whom they have pierced, and they shall mourn for him"
- **Romans 11:25-27** — "blindness in part is happened to Israel, until the fulness of the Gentiles be come in. And so all Israel shall be saved: as it is written, There shall come out of Sion the Deliverer, and shall turn away ungodliness from Jacob"
  - *Interpretation:* The phrase "in the latter days" (be'acharit hayyamim) is a technical prophetic phrase used throughout the Prophets for the end-time period. Moses places Israel's ultimate return to God in that timeframe: tribulation → seeking the LORD → finding Him → covenant restoration. This is not yet exhaustively fulfilled. Romans 11 identifies it as the "all Israel shall be saved" event that occurs when the fullness of the Gentiles has come in. The sequence Moses gives matches the prophetic outline: scattering → tribulation → seeking → finding → mercy → covenant kept.

### "The LORD He Is God in Heaven Above and Upon the Earth Beneath: There Is None Else"

**Deuteronomy 4:39** — "Know therefore this day, and consider it in thine heart, that the LORD he is God in heaven above, and upon the earth beneath: there is none else."

- **Isaiah 45:5-6** — "I am the LORD, and there is none else, there is no God beside me... That they may know from the rising of the sun, and from the west, that there is none beside me."
- **Isaiah 46:9** — "I am God, and there is none else; I am God, and there is none like me"
- **1 Kings 8:60** — Solomon's prayer at the temple dedication: "That all the people of the earth may know that the LORD is God, and that there is none else."
- **Mark 12:29-30** — Jesus quotes the Shema (Deuteronomy 6:4) as the greatest commandment, rooted in this same exclusivity: there is one LORD
- **1 Corinthians 8:4-6** — "we know that an idol is nothing in the world, and that there is none other God but one... to us there is but one God, the Father, of whom are all things"
  - *Interpretation:* "There is none else" is the bedrock confession of biblical monotheism. Heaven above and earth beneath — the complete created order — is under one God. This is not religious preference; it is ontological fact as Scripture presents it. All idolatry is therefore not merely wrong worship — it is worshipping what does not exist (1 Corinthians 8:4 — "an idol is nothing in the world"). The emptiness of idols is not relative; they are literally nothing before the one God who fills heaven and earth.
