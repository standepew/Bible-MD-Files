## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Moses gives Israel the foundational declaration of their faith: "Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God is one LORD." This is followed by the commandment to love the LORD with all the heart, soul, and might — a total-being love that extends into every moment of life. These words are to be internalized in the heart, taught diligently to children at every occasion, bound as a sign on the hand and between the eyes, and written on doorposts and gates. Moses warns that when Israel enters the land of abundance — cities, houses, wells, vineyards they did not build or plant — they must beware of forgetting the LORD who brought them out of Egypt. They must not tempt the LORD as at Massah. When future children ask about the testimonies and statutes, parents are to narrate the salvation story: "We were Pharaoh's bondmen in Egypt, and the LORD brought us out." Observance of the commandments will be Israel's righteousness.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### The Shema — "The LORD Our God Is One LORD"

**Deuteronomy 6:4** — "Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God is one LORD"

- **Mark 12:28-30** — A scribe asks Jesus "Which is the first commandment of all?" Jesus answers by quoting the Shema: "Hear, O Israel; The Lord our God is one Lord: And thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy mind, and with all thy strength" — Jesus identifies this as the first and greatest commandment
- **Mark 12:32-34** — The scribe agrees, adding that this is "more than all whole burnt offerings and sacrifices" — Jesus tells him he is "not far from the kingdom of God"
- **James 2:19** — "Thou believest that there is one God; thou doest well: the devils also believe, and tremble." — bare intellectual assent to the Shema is not enough
- **Galatians 3:20** — "now a mediator is not a mediator of one, but God is one" — Paul's argument on the oneness of God
- **1 Corinthians 8:4-6** — "there is none other God but one... to us there is but one God, the Father, of whom are all things, and we in him; and one Lord Jesus Christ, by whom are all things, and we by him"
  - *Interpretation:* The Shema is Israel's foundational confession of faith, the bedrock of biblical monotheism, and the foundation of all ethics — if the LORD is one, then love of Him is total and undivided. Jesus quotes the Shema as the first commandment, establishing that the entire Law and Prophets hang on it (Matthew 22:40). The "one" (echad in Hebrew — a compound unity, as in Genesis 2:24 "they shall be one flesh") does not rule out the plurality within the Godhead established in Genesis 1:26 ("Let us") and throughout Scripture.

### "Thou Shalt Love the LORD Thy God with All Thine Heart, and with All Thy Soul, and with All Thy Might"

**Deuteronomy 6:5** — The Great Commandment

- **Matthew 22:37-40** — Jesus quotes and identifies this as "the first and great commandment... And the second is like unto it, Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself. On these two commandments hang all the law and the prophets."
- **Luke 10:27-28** — The lawyer answers Jesus' question with this exact verse; Jesus: "Thou hast answered right: this do, and thou shalt live."
- **Romans 13:10** — "Love worketh no ill to his neighbour: therefore love is the fulfilling of the law."
- **1 John 4:19** — "We love him, because he first loved us." — the source of this commanded love
- **Deuteronomy 10:12** — Moses restates the same requirement: "what doth the LORD thy God require of thee, but to fear the LORD thy God, to walk in all his ways, and to love him, and to serve the LORD thy God with all thy heart and with all thy soul"
  - *Interpretation:* The command to love is not a request for an emotion; it is a covenant obligation that encompasses the whole person: heart (lev — will, mind, center of decision), soul (nephesh — the whole being, desires), and might (me'od — strength, force, resources). Total love leaves nothing compartmentalized, nothing held back. Jesus adds "mind" in the Synoptic Gospels, drawing out the cognitive dimension already implied in "heart." The entirety of a person's existence — every thought, desire, resource, and strength — is the field of this love.

### "These Words Shall Be in Thine Heart... Teach Them Diligently unto Thy Children"

**Deuteronomy 6:6-7** — "And these words, which I command thee this day, shall be in thine heart: And thou shalt teach them diligently unto thy children, and shalt talk of them when thou sittest in thine house, and when thou walkest by the way, and when thou liest down, and when thou risest up."

- **Deuteronomy 11:18-20** — Repeated nearly verbatim — Moses doubles down on this command
- **Psalm 78:3-7** — "Which we have heard and known, and our fathers have told us. We will not hide them from their children, shewing to the generation to come the praises of the LORD... That the generation to come might know them, even the children which should be born; who should arise and declare them to their children: That they might set their hope in God"
- **Proverbs 22:6** — "Train up a child in the way he should go: and when he is old, he will not depart from it."
- **Ephesians 6:4** — "And, ye fathers, provoke not your children to wrath: but bring them up in the nurture and admonition of the Lord."
- **2 Timothy 3:15** — "And that from a child thou hast known the holy scriptures, which are able to make thee wise unto salvation through faith which is in Christ Jesus." — Timothy as the product of this type of instruction (2 Timothy 1:5 — his grandmother Lois and mother Eunice)
  - *Interpretation:* The Hebrew word "diligently" (shinantam) comes from shanan — to sharpen, to incise. The image is of sharpening a blade — teaching that cuts, repeats, and penetrates. The four occasions (sitting, walking, lying down, rising up) cover the entire rhythm of daily life. God's word is not to be compartmentalized into a religious hour — it is the fabric of all of life's conversations. This is the biblical model of discipleship and education: immersive, daily, relational, conversational, all-of-life engagement with the word.

### Bind for a Sign / Frontlets / Write on Doorposts — Word Saturating All Space

**Deuteronomy 6:8-9** — "And thou shalt bind them for a sign upon thine hand, and they shall be as frontlets between thine eyes. And thou shalt write them upon the posts of thy house, and on thy gates."

- **Exodus 13:9** — "And it shall be for a sign unto thee upon thine hand, and for a memorial between thine eyes" — first mention in context of the Passover memorial
- **Revelation 13:16-17** — The mark of the beast on the hand and forehead — a counterfeit of God's sign; Satan mimics the divine pattern
- **Revelation 14:1** — The 144,000 have the Father's name written on their foreheads — those marked by God vs. those marked by the beast
  - *Interpretation:* The hand represents actions; the forehead (between the eyes) represents thoughts and identity. God's word is to mark what we do and what we think — the full person. Physical phylacteries/mezuzot are the literal application; their spiritual meaning is that God's word saturates both private space (hand, forehead — the individual person) and public/household space (doorposts, gates). The beast's counterfeit mark in Revelation uses the identical body-geography — hand and forehead — because it is a counterfeit of God's claim on His people.

### Warning Against Forgetting in Prosperity

**Deuteronomy 6:10-12** — "houses full of all good things, which thou filledst not, and wells digged, which thou diggedst not... when thou shalt have eaten and be full; Then beware lest thou forget the LORD, which brought thee forth out of the land of Egypt"

- **Deuteronomy 8:11-14** — Extended warning: "Beware that thou forget not the LORD thy God... lest when thou hast eaten and art full, and hast built goodly houses, and dwelt therein... Then thine heart be lifted up, and thou forget the LORD thy God"
- **Hosea 13:6** — "According to their pasture, so were they filled; they were filled, and their heart was exalted; therefore have they forgotten me."
- **Proverbs 30:8-9** — "Feed me with food convenient for me: Lest I be full, and deny thee, and say, Who is the LORD?"
- **Revelation 3:17** — Laodicea: "Because thou sayest, I am rich, and increased with goods, and have need of nothing; and knowest not that thou art wretched, and miserable, and poor, and blind, and naked"
  - *Interpretation:* Moses identifies prosperity, not poverty, as the primary spiritual danger. The crisis of the wilderness was unbelief in the face of need. The crisis of Canaan would be forgetfulness in the face of abundance. Both are failures of the same thing: trust in the LORD rather than in circumstances. The Laodicean church in Revelation is the New Testament example of exactly what Moses warns against here — material fullness producing spiritual amnesia.

### "Ye Shall Not Tempt the LORD Your God, as Ye Tempted Him in Massah"

**Deuteronomy 6:16** — "Ye shall not tempt the LORD your God, as ye tempted him in Massah."

- **Exodus 17:1-7** — The Massah incident: Israel quarreled with Moses demanding water, saying "Is the LORD among us, or not?" — they tested God's presence
- **Psalm 95:8-9** — "Harden not your heart, as in the provocation, and as in the day of temptation in the wilderness: When your fathers tempted me, proved me, and saw my work."
- **Matthew 4:7** — Satan tempts Jesus to throw Himself from the temple: "thou shalt not tempt the Lord thy God" — Jesus quotes this exact verse (Deuteronomy 6:16) as His response
- **1 Corinthians 10:9** — "Neither let us tempt Christ, as some of them also tempted, and were destroyed of serpents."
  - *Interpretation:* Tempting the LORD means testing whether He is who He says He is — demanding proof of His presence or power in a context of unbelief. At Massah, Israel had already seen ten plagues, the Red Sea, manna, and quail — yet they demanded "Is the LORD among us?" Jesus quotes this verse when Satan demands He prove God's protection. Both situations have the same structure: demanding God perform to prove Himself rather than trusting what He has already revealed. This is the definition of tempting God.

### "And It Shall Be Our Righteousness If We Observe to Do All These Commandments"

**Deuteronomy 6:25** — "And it shall be our righteousness, if we observe to do all these commandments before the LORD our God, as he hath commanded us."

- **Romans 10:5** — "For Moses describeth the righteousness which is of the law, That the man which doeth those things shall live by them."
- **Galatians 3:10** — "For as many as are of the works of the law are under the curse: for it is written, Cursed is every one that continueth not in all things which are written in the book of the law to do them."
- **Romans 3:20** — "Therefore by the deeds of the law there shall no flesh be justified in his sight: for by the law is the knowledge of sin."
- **Romans 10:3-4** — "For they being ignorant of God's righteousness, and going about to establish their own righteousness, have not submitted themselves unto the righteousness of God. For Christ is the end of the law for righteousness to every one that believeth."
  - *Interpretation:* Deuteronomy 6:25 states the law's terms for righteousness within the covenant — obedience produces covenant standing. Paul in Romans 10 does not contradict this; he shows that the perfect standard of the law can only be fully met by One — Christ, who is the end (goal and completion) of the law for righteousness. The law's righteousness points to the righteousness that only Christ can provide. Israel's history proved that keeping all these commandments was beyond fallen humanity — hence the need for the new covenant (Jeremiah 31:33) and the Spirit within (Ezekiel 36:26-27).

### "When Thy Son Asketh Thee" — Covenant Transmission

**Deuteronomy 6:20-25** — The child's question and the parent's answer

- **Exodus 12:26-27** — The Passover: "And it shall come to pass, when your children shall say unto you, What mean ye by this service? That ye shall say, It is the sacrifice of the LORD's passover, who passed over the houses of the children of Israel in Egypt"
- **Exodus 13:14** — "And it shall be when thy son asketh thee in time to come, saying, What is this? that thou shalt say unto him, By strength of hand the LORD brought us out from Egypt, from the house of bondage"
- **Joshua 4:21-22** — The Jordan stones: "When your children shall ask their fathers in time to come, saying, What mean these stones? Then ye shall let your children know, saying, Israel came over this Jordan on dry land."
  - *Interpretation:* The repeated pattern of "when your child asks" throughout the Torah establishes that covenant transmission happens through questions and narrative answers. Faith is not automatic — it is taught. The parent's obligation is to give the salvation story as the answer: "We were Pharaoh's bondmen... the LORD brought us out." The personal pronoun is critical — "we were," "the LORD brought us." Every generation is to identify personally with the exodus narrative. This is why Paul says in 1 Corinthians 10:1 "all our fathers" — he owns the exodus story as his own, across generations.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### "One LORD" (echad)

- **Deuteronomy 6:4** — echad is a compound unity, not an absolute numerical singularity (yachid)
- **Genesis 2:24** — "they shall be one (echad) flesh" — two persons becoming one; the word naturally accommodates plurality in unity
- **Genesis 1:5** — "the evening and the morning were one (echad) day" — two components constituting one unit
- The Shema's "one LORD" does not rule out the plurality within the Godhead — it declares the unity of the one God who is Father, Son (the Word), and Spirit throughout Scripture. The Lord is not many gods; He is one God revealed in three persons.

### "Diligently" (shinantam — from shanan)

- **Deuteronomy 6:7** — the root meaning is to sharpen, to whet, to cut into
- The image: teaching that repeats, sharpens, cuts deep — not casual mention but incisive, constant, penetrating instruction
- This is the opposite of the modern model of compartmentalized religious education (one hour per week). It is all-of-life, all-day, immersive teaching through conversation, action, and storytelling.

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The Shema and the Incarnation — "One LORD" Who Became Flesh

**Deuteronomy 6:4** declares the LORD is one. **John 1:14** declares the Word became flesh.

- **Philippians 2:6-8** — Christ, being in the form of God, took the form of a servant
- **Colossians 2:9** — "For in him dwelleth all the fulness of the Godhead bodily"
- The Shema is not overturned by the Incarnation — it is fulfilled. The one LORD entered His own creation in the person of the Son, revealing the compound unity of the one God more fully than Sinai's voice could.

### "Bind on Hand / Between Eyes" → Mark of the Beast — The Counterfeit

**Deuteronomy 6:8** — God's sign on hand and forehead  
**Revelation 13:16** — The beast's mark on hand and forehead

- Satan's strategy is always imitation of divine pattern, not invention of new patterns. The mark of the beast is a counterfeit of the Shema's claim — ownership of the total person (hand = actions, forehead = identity/allegiance)
- Those who refuse the beast's mark are those whose allegiance is already marked by the Lamb (Revelation 14:1 — the Father's name on their foreheads). One cannot serve two masters (Matthew 6:24).

### Jesus Quoting Deuteronomy 6 in the Wilderness — The Faithful Son

**Matthew 4:7, 10** — Jesus quotes Deuteronomy 6:16 and 6:13 in response to Satan's temptations:
- "Thou shalt not tempt the Lord thy God" (Deuteronomy 6:16)
- "Thou shalt worship the Lord thy God, and him only shalt thou serve" (Deuteronomy 6:13)
- Jesus, the true Israel, succeeds in the wilderness where Israel failed, using the very words of Moses' covenant charge. He is the faithful Son who loves the Father with all His heart, soul, and might — the Shema perfectly embodied.
