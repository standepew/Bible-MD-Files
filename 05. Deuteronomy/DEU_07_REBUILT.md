## CHAPTER SUMMARY

When the LORD brings Israel into Canaan, He will cast out seven nations greater and mightier than Israel. God's commands are total: smite them, utterly destroy them, make no covenant with them, show no mercy, and make no marriages with them — because intermarriage will turn away Israel's children to serve other gods. Israel is to destroy every altar, pillar, grove, and graven image. The basis for all this is election: Israel is a holy people, chosen as the LORD's special treasure above all nations — not because of their numbers (they are the fewest) but because the LORD loved them and kept the oath to the fathers. The faithful God keeps covenant and mercy to a thousand generations for those who love Him and repay those who hate Him to their face. Obedience produces blessing: fruitfulness, health, and the diseases of Egypt removed. God will drive out the nations little by little (lest the beasts of the field multiply). Israel must burn the graven images of their gods — even the silver and gold on them is not to be desired, for it is an abomination and a cursed thing.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### Election by Love, Not by Merit

**Deuteronomy 7:6-8** — "For thou art an holy people unto the LORD thy God: the LORD thy God hath chosen thee to be a special people unto himself, above all people that are upon the face of the earth. The LORD did not set his love upon you, nor choose you, because ye were more in number than any people; for ye were the fewest of all people: But because the LORD loved you, and because he would keep the oath which he had sworn unto your fathers"

- **Romans 9:11-13** — "(For the children being not yet born, neither having done any good or evil, that the purpose of God according to election might stand, not of works, but of him that calleth;) It was said unto her, The elder shall serve the younger. As it is written, Jacob have I loved, but Esau have I hated."
- **Romans 9:15-16** — "For he saith to Moses, I will have mercy on whom I will have mercy, and I will have compassion on whom I will have compassion. So then it is not of him that willeth, nor of him that runneth, but of God that sheweth mercy."
- **Ephesians 1:4-5** — "According as he hath chosen us in him before the foundation of the world... Having predestinated us unto the adoption of children by Jesus Christ to himself, according to the good pleasure of his will"
- **John 15:16** — "Ye have not chosen me, but I have chosen you"
- **1 Corinthians 1:27-28** — "God hath chosen the foolish things of the world to confound the wise; and God hath chosen the weak things of the world to confound the things which are mighty; And base things of the world, and things which are despised, hath God chosen"
  - *Interpretation:* Deuteronomy 7:7-8 is the clearest Old Testament statement of unconditional election. The reason for Israel's chosenness is not in Israel — not in their numbers, their merit, or their character (as chapter 9 will make explicit: "thou art a stiffnecked people"). The reason is in God: His love, His oath, His faithfulness. Paul draws directly from this same passage in Romans 9 to establish the doctrine of election in the New Testament context. Election is not a New Testament invention; it is Deuteronomy 7's explicit statement.

### "A Special People" (am segullah) — Treasured Possession

**Deuteronomy 7:6** — "a special people unto himself"

- **Exodus 19:5** — "then ye shall be a peculiar treasure unto me above all people" — first use of segullah
- **Deuteronomy 14:2** — Repeated: "thou art an holy people unto the LORD thy God, and the LORD hath chosen thee to be a peculiar people unto himself"
- **Deuteronomy 26:18** — "And the LORD hath avouched thee this day to be his peculiar people"
- **Psalm 135:4** — "For the LORD hath chosen Jacob unto himself, and Israel for his peculiar treasure."
- **Titus 2:14** — Christ "gave himself for us, that he might redeem us from all iniquity, and purify unto himself a peculiar people, zealous of good works" — the church as the new covenant segullah
- **1 Peter 2:9** — "But ye are a chosen generation, a royal priesthood, an holy nation, a peculiar people; that ye should shew forth the praises of him who hath called you out of darkness into his marvellous light" — directly echoes Exodus 19:5-6 and Deuteronomy 7:6
  - *Interpretation:* Segullah means a personal, valued possession — not common property but a king's private treasure. Both Israel and the church are called God's segullah. This does not mean the two are identical — Israel's national election and the church's spiritual election are distinct — but both carry the same status: they belong to God personally, as His most treasured possession.

### "Faithful God Which Keepeth Covenant and Mercy unto a Thousand Generations"

**Deuteronomy 7:9** — "Know therefore that the LORD thy God, he is God, the faithful God, which keepeth covenant and mercy with them that love him and keep his commandments to a thousand generations"

- **Exodus 20:6** — "shewing mercy unto thousands of them that love me, and keep my commandments" — in the ten commandments
- **Psalm 89:34** — "My covenant will I not break, nor alter the thing that is gone out of my lips."
- **Psalm 100:5** — "For the LORD is good; his mercy is everlasting; and his truth endureth to all generations."
- **Lamentations 3:22-23** — "It is of the LORD's mercies that we are not consumed, because his compassions fail not. They are new every morning: great is thy faithfulness."
- **Hebrews 10:23** — "Let us hold fast the profession of our faith without wavering; (for he is faithful that promised)"
- **2 Timothy 2:13** — "If we believe not, yet he abideth faithful: he cannot deny himself."
  - *Interpretation:* The faithfulness of God — that He keeps covenant to a thousand generations — is not conditioned on Israel's faithfulness. It is conditioned on God's own character ("he cannot deny himself"). The thousand generations applies to those who love Him; the repayment to those who hate Him is "to their face" — swift, not multi-generational. The asymmetry is deliberate: God's faithfulness in blessing extends far further than His faithfulness in judgment. Mercy triumphs over judgment (James 2:13).

### No Intermarriage with Canaanites — Separation of God's People

**Deuteronomy 7:3-4** — "Neither shalt thou make marriages with them; thy daughter thou shalt not give unto his son, nor his daughter shalt thou take unto thy son. For they will turn away thy son from following me, that they may serve other gods"

- **1 Kings 11:1-4** — Solomon's thousand wives and concubines from foreign nations: "For it came to pass, when Solomon was old, that his wives turned away his heart after other gods" — exact fulfillment of this warning
- **Nehemiah 13:23-27** — Nehemiah confronts intermarriage after the return from exile; cites Solomon: "Did not Solomon king of Israel sin by these things? yet among many nations was there no king like him... nevertheless even him did outlandish women cause to sin."
- **2 Corinthians 6:14** — "Be ye not unequally yoked together with unbelievers: for what fellowship hath righteousness with unrighteousness?"
- **Ezra 9-10** — The crisis of intermarriage in the post-exilic community; Ezra's prayer of corporate confession
  - *Interpretation:* The prohibition is not ethnic but theological — the danger is idolatry, not bloodline. Ruth the Moabitess is incorporated into Israel because she embraces the LORD (Ruth 1:16). Rahab the Canaanite is spared and incorporated. The issue is worship, not race. Those who bring their foreign gods are the danger; those who abandon their foreign gods are welcome. Solomon's failure (1 Kings 11) is the proof-text for this warning's accuracy — exactly as Moses predicted.

### "The LORD Thy God Will Put Out Those Nations... by Little and Little"

**Deuteronomy 7:22** — "And the LORD thy God will put out those nations before thee by little and little: thou mayest not consume them at once, lest the beasts of the field increase upon thee."

- **Exodus 23:29-30** — The same principle given earlier: "I will not drive them out from before thee in one year; lest the land become desolate, and the beast of the field multiply against thee. By little and little I will drive them out from before thee, until thou be increased, and inherit the land."
  - *Interpretation:* God's "little by little" is not weakness — it is wisdom. The land requires cultivation and management; sudden depopulation would create ecological disaster (beasts filling the vacuum). God's timing of the conquest is calibrated to Israel's actual capacity to possess and manage the territory. He gives them what they can steward. The principle extends spiritually: sanctification also proceeds "little by little" — God does not demand instantaneous perfection but steady, Spirit-empowered growth in the life of His people.

---

### "Thou Shalt Utterly Detest It / Thou Shalt Utterly Abhor It"

**Deuteronomy 7:25-26** — "The graven images of their gods shall ye burn with fire: thou shalt not desire the silver or gold that is on them, nor take it unto thee, lest thou be snared therein: for it is an abomination to the LORD thy God. Neither shalt thou bring an abomination into thine house, lest thou be a cursed thing like it"

- **Joshua 7:1, 20-21** — Achan took "a goodly Babylonish garment, and two hundred shekels of silver, and a wedge of gold" — the silver and gold of devoted things; brought curse on all Israel
- **Acts 19:18-19** — New Testament application: converts to Christ burned their books of curious arts — did not sell them, burned them
- **2 Corinthians 6:17** — "Wherefore come out from among them, and be ye separate, saith the Lord, and touch not the unclean thing; and I will receive you"
  - *Interpretation:* The command to utterly abhor the abominable thing — not even to profit from it economically — is demonstrated by Achan's failure and its consequences. Taking the silver from a devoted thing does not neutralize the curse; it transfers it. The New Testament application is Acts 19: the Ephesian believers didn't sell their occult books at profit — they burned them in public. Separation from idolatrous things is not accomplished by monetizing them.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### SEVEN NATIONS (Hittites, Girgashites, Amorites, Canaanites, Perizzites, Hivites, Jebusites)
- **Identity:** The pre-Israelite inhabitants of Canaan whose iniquity had reached fullness (Genesis 15:16)
- **Command:** Complete destruction (*cherem* — devoted to destruction); no treaties, no intermarriage, no sparing their altars
- **Theological basis:** Not racial superiority but covenantal judgment — God used Israel as His instrument of judgment as He later used Assyria and Babylon
- **Cross-references:** Genesis 15:16; Leviticus 18:24-28; Deuteronomy 20:16-18; Amos 2:9

### CHOSEN PEOPLE (Segullah — סְגֻלָּה)
- **Hebrew meaning:** *segullah* — treasured possession, special property; a king's personal treasure
- **Basis of election:** Not Israel's greatness or righteousness but God's love and His oath to the fathers (vv.6-8) — sovereign grace, not merit
- **Significance:** The smallest of all peoples (v.7); God's choice was entirely free and unconditional from Israel's side
- **Cross-references:** Exodus 19:5; Deuteronomy 14:2; 26:18; Titus 2:14; 1 Peter 2:9

### INTERMARRIAGE PROHIBITION (vv.3-4)
- **Scope:** No marriage with the seven Canaanite nations specifically — the prohibition is religious, not racial (Rahab the Canaanite and Ruth the Moabite were both incorporated)
- **Reason stated:** "For they will turn away thy son from following me" (v.4) — the danger is spiritual corruption, not ethnic contamination
- **New Testament parallel:** 2 Corinthians 6:14 — "Be ye not unequally yoked together with unbelievers" — same principle, broader application
- **Cross-references:** Ezra 9-10; Nehemiah 13:23-27; 1 Corinthians 7:39; 2 Corinthians 6:14

### ABOMINATION (Toevah — תּוֹעֵבָה)
- **Hebrew meaning:** Something utterly detestable, ritually or morally revolting to God
- **Application here:** The graven images of the Canaanite nations (v.25-26) — not just wrong but contagious and defiling
- **Bring not an abomination into thine house:** The physical objects of idolatry carry a spiritual contamination; complete separation required
- **Cross-references:** Deuteronomy 12:31; 18:12; Proverbs 11:1; Revelation 21:27

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### Israel's Election — Type of the Church's Election

God's election of the smallest of all nations on the basis of His own sovereign love (vv.6-8) establishes the pattern for all divine election — grace-based, not merit-based.

- **Romans 9:11-13** — Jacob and Esau: "that the purpose of God according to election might stand, not of works, but of him that calleth"
- **1 Corinthians 1:26-29** — "Not many wise men after the flesh, not many mighty, not many noble, are called... That no flesh should glory in his presence"
- **Ephesians 1:4-5** — "He hath chosen us in him before the foundation of the world... having predestinated us unto the adoption"
- **1 Peter 2:9-10** — "But ye are a chosen generation, a royal priesthood, an holy nation, a peculiar people" — direct echo of Deuteronomy 7:6, applied to the church

---

### "By Little and Little" (v.22) — God's Pace of Sanctification

God's decision to drive out the nations gradually, not at once (v.22), mirrors how He works in sanctification. The reason given is practical — sudden complete victory would create a worse problem (wild animals).

- **Proverbs 13:11** — "Wealth gotten by vanity shall be diminished: but he that gathereth by labour shall increase"
- **2 Corinthians 3:18** — "We all, with open face beholding as in a glass the glory of the Lord, are changed into the same image from glory to glory" — progressive transformation
- **Romans 6:19** — Paul's acknowledgment: "I speak after the manner of men because of the infirmity of your flesh: for as ye have yielded your members servants to uncleanness... even so now yield your members servants to righteousness unto holiness"

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED / MISAPPLIED PASSAGES

### "He Will Love Thee, and Bless Thee, and Multiply Thee" (Deuteronomy 7:13)

**Common misapplication:** Used as a universal prosperity promise — that obedience to God guarantees material blessing, health, and increase for all believers in all circumstances.

**Correct exegetical context:** This promise was covenantal and conditional, addressed to Israel as a national covenant community in the land of Canaan. The blessings (agricultural productivity, livestock increase, freedom from disease) were specifically Canaanite-land blessings tied to the Mosaic covenant's land provisions. The New Testament explicitly does not promise material wealth as a standard covenant blessing — Paul was beaten, shipwrecked, and hungry (2 Corinthians 11:23-27). The principle that faithfulness to God brings blessing is true, but the specific form of blessing varies according to the covenant context and God's sovereign purposes for each person.

---

### "Thou Shalt Utterly Destroy Them" (Deuteronomy 7:2)

**Common misapplication:** Used to argue that God commanded ethnic cleansing as a permanent template for religious warfare — that this establishes a principle of violence against unbelievers.

**Correct exegetical context:** The *cherem* command was specific, bounded, and unrepeatable — applied to the seven nations of Canaan at a specific moment when their iniquity was full (Genesis 15:16). God had waited 400 years (Genesis 15:13). No subsequent biblical command repeats this mandate. Jesus explicitly reversed the spirit of religious violence against opponents (Luke 9:54-55; Matthew 26:52). The New Testament calls believers to "destroy" strongholds (2 Corinthians 10:4) and to put to death the deeds of the body (Romans 8:13) — spiritual warfare, not physical. The Canaanite destruction is unique historical-covenantal judgment, not a template.

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### The Smallest Nation Chosen — Grace Over Merit

Israel's election as "the fewest of all people" (v.7) is one of Scripture's clearest statements that divine choice operates in complete freedom from human qualification.

- **Genesis 12:1-3** — Abraham called from paganism; no prior merit
- **1 Samuel 16:7** — "for man looketh on the outward appearance, but the LORD looketh on the heart" — God consistently chooses what human wisdom would not
- **Matthew 5:3** — "Blessed are the poor in spirit: for theirs is the kingdom of heaven" — the principle of Deuteronomy 7:7 in the beatitudes
- **1 Corinthians 1:27-29** — "God hath chosen the weak things of the world to confound the things which are mighty"

---

### The Snare of What Is Devoted to Destruction

The warning against taking the silver and gold from Canaanite idols (vv.25-26) — "lest thou be snared therein" — establishes a recurring pattern: the things God has devoted to destruction are dangerous, not merely wrong.

- **Joshua 7:1, 20-21** — Achan takes a Babylonian garment, silver, and gold from the *cherem*; Israel defeated at Ai; Achan and his household destroyed
- **1 Samuel 15:9** — Saul spares the best of the devoted things from Amalek; loses the kingdom
- **2 Corinthians 6:17** — "Wherefore come out from among them, and be ye separate, saith the Lord, and touch not the unclean thing" — the separation principle carried forward
- **Revelation 18:4** — "Come out of her, my people, that ye be not partakers of her sins, and that ye receive not of her plagues" — the eschatological application of the snare warning
