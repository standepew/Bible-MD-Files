## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Moses calls Israel to remember all the forty-year wilderness journey — its purpose was to humble them, prove them, and know what was in their heart. God fed them with manna (which they did not know, nor their fathers knew) to show that man does not live by bread alone but by every word from God's mouth. Their clothes did not wear out; their feet did not swell. As a man chastens his son, so the LORD chastened Israel. The good land ahead overflows with water, grain, fruit, oil, honey, iron, and brass. When they eat and are full, they must bless the LORD. The danger: when houses are built, herds multiply, silver and gold accumulate, the heart is lifted up and they forget the LORD. The deadly lie: "My power and the might of mine hand hath gotten me this wealth." The truth: "It is he that giveth thee power to get wealth, that he may establish his covenant." If they forget the LORD, they shall perish — as the nations that preceded them.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Man Doth Not Live by Bread Only, but by Every Word That Proceedeth Out of the Mouth of the LORD"

**Deuteronomy 8:3** — "And he humbled thee, and suffered thee to hunger, and fed thee with manna, which thou knewest not, neither did thy fathers know; that he might make thee know that man doth not live by bread only, but by every word that proceedeth out of the mouth of the LORD doth man live."

- **Matthew 4:4** — Jesus in the wilderness, tempted to turn stones to bread: "But he answered and said, It is written, Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that proceedeth out of the mouth of God." — Jesus quotes Deuteronomy 8:3 directly
- **John 6:31-35** — "Our fathers did eat manna in the desert... Then Jesus said unto them, Verily, verily, I say unto you, Moses gave you not that bread from heaven; but my Father giveth you the true bread from heaven... I am the bread of life: he that cometh to me shall never hunger"
- **John 6:48-51** — "I am that bread of life. Your fathers did eat manna in the wilderness, and are dead. This is the bread which cometh down from heaven, that a man may eat thereof, and not die. I am the living bread which came down from heaven: if any man eat of this bread, he shall live for ever"
- **Revelation 2:17** — "To him that overcometh will I give to eat of the hidden manna"
  - *Interpretation:* Deuteronomy 8:3 is the key verse Jesus uses against Satan in His wilderness temptation. The parallel is exact: Israel in the wilderness forty years; Jesus in the wilderness forty days. Israel failed the food test (Numbers 11 — lusting for meat); Jesus quotes Deuteronomy 8:3 and refuses to use divine power for personal physical satisfaction. More than that, Jesus identifies Himself as the fulfillment of the manna — not just the bread that sustained temporarily, but the living bread that gives eternal life. The manna of the wilderness was always a type pointing to Him.

### The Purpose of Wilderness: Humble / Prove / Know What Is in the Heart

**Deuteronomy 8:2** — "And thou shalt remember all the way which the LORD thy God led thee these forty years in the wilderness, to humble thee, and to prove thee, to know what was in thine heart, whether thou wouldest keep his commandments, or no."

- **James 1:2-4** — "My brethren, count it all joy when ye fall into divers temptations; Knowing this, that the trying of your faith worketh patience. But let patience have her perfect work, that ye may be perfect and entire, wanting nothing."
- **Romans 5:3-4** — "we glory in tribulations also: knowing that tribulation worketh patience; And patience, experience; and experience, hope"
- **1 Peter 1:6-7** — "the trial of your faith, being much more precious than of gold that perisheth, though it be tried with fire, might be found unto praise and honour and glory at the appearing of Jesus Christ"
- **Proverbs 3:11-12** — "My son, despise not the chastening of the LORD; neither be weary of his correction: For whom the LORD loveth he correcteth; even as a father the son in whom he delighteth."
- **Hebrews 12:5-11** — Quotes Proverbs 3:11-12 and expands: "what son is he whom the father chasteneth not?... He chasteneth us for our profit, that we might be partakers of his holiness. Now no chastening for the present seemeth to be joyous, but grievous: nevertheless afterward it yieldeth the peaceable fruit of righteousness"
  - *Interpretation:* The forty years of wilderness was not punishment — it was fatherly education. The three purposes (humble, prove, know) are not punitive; they are diagnostic and formative. Humbling removes the pride that would lead to the very prosperity-forgetting warned about later in the chapter. Proving reveals what is truly there. Knowing (on God's part) completes the assessment. The same purposes are at work in every trial a believer faces. Hebrews 12 is the direct New Testament commentary on Deuteronomy 8:2-5: the wilderness experience is the pattern of fatherly discipline that all God's children undergo.

### "As a Man Chasteneth His Son, So the LORD Thy God Chasteneth Thee"

**Deuteronomy 8:5** — "Thou shalt also consider in thine heart, that, as a man chasteneth his son, so the LORD thy God chasteneth thee."

- **Proverbs 13:24** — "He that spareth his rod hateth his son: but he that loveth him chasteneth him betimes."
- **Proverbs 3:11-12** — "For whom the LORD loveth he correcteth; even as a father the son in whom he delighteth."
- **Hebrews 12:6-8** — "For whom the Lord loveth he chasteneth, and scourgeth every son whom he receiveth. If ye endure chastening, God dealeth with you as with sons; for what son is he whom the father chasteneth not? But if ye be without chastisement, whereof all are partakers, then are ye bastards, and not sons."
  - *Interpretation:* The father-son relationship is the interpretive key for understanding God's chastening. A father who loves disciplines; a father who does not discipline does not love — or has no actual child relationship with the person. Hebrews 12 draws the inverse conclusion: if you are not being chastened at all, examine whether you are actually in the family. The wilderness was the school of sonship: discomfort, need, dependence, and correction — all marks of the genuine father-child relationship.

### The Good Land — Covenant Abundance

**Deuteronomy 8:7-9** — "For the LORD thy God bringeth thee into a good land, a land of brooks of water, of fountains and depths that spring out of valleys and hills; A land of wheat, and barley, and vines, and fig trees, and pomegranates; a land of oil olive, and honey; A land wherein thou shalt eat bread without scarceness, thou shalt not lack any thing in it; a land whose stones are iron, and out of whose hills thou mayest dig brass."

- **Exodus 3:8** — "a land flowing with milk and honey" — the first description of Canaan
- **Numbers 13:23-27** — The spies' report confirms the land's abundance: a single cluster of grapes requires two men to carry it
- **Joel 2:19, 22-26** — The restoration of covenant blessing: "the LORD will answer and say unto his people, Behold, I will send you corn, and wine, and oil... Be glad then, ye children of Zion, and rejoice in the LORD your God: for he hath given you the former rain moderately, and he will cause to come down for you the rain, the former rain, and the latter rain in the first month. And the floors shall be full of wheat, and the vats shall overflow with wine and oil."
  - *Interpretation:* The seven fruits of the land (wheat, barley, grape, fig, pomegranate, olive, date-honey) are not incidental agriculture — they represent the fullness of covenant blessing. Lack of them (drought, famine, blight) signals covenant curse (Deuteronomy 28:38-42). Their restoration is always a sign of covenant restoration. Joel 2 and Zechariah 8:12 both promise the return of these specific covenant blessings as part of the ultimate restoration.

### "Beware That Thou Forget Not the LORD Thy God" — The Prosperity Trap

**Deuteronomy 8:11-14** — "Beware that thou forget not the LORD thy God... Lest when thou hast eaten and art full, and hast built goodly houses, and dwelt therein... Then thine heart be lifted up, and thou forget the LORD thy God, which brought thee forth out of the land of Egypt, from the house of bondage"

- **Hosea 13:6** — "According to their pasture, so were they filled; they were filled, and their heart was exalted; therefore have they forgotten me." — exact fulfillment
- **Nehemiah 9:25-26** — "And they took strong cities, and a fat land, and possessed houses full of all goods, wells digged, vineyards, and oliveyards, and fruit trees in abundance: so they did eat, and were filled, and became fat, and delighted themselves in thy great goodness. Nevertheless they were disobedient" — almost quoting Deuteronomy 8
- **Revelation 3:17** — Laodicea: "thou sayest, I am rich, and increased with goods, and have need of nothing; and knowest not that thou art wretched, and miserable, and poor, and blind, and naked" — prosperity as spiritual danger
- **Luke 12:16-21** — The parable of the rich fool: "the ground of a certain rich man brought forth plentifully... I will pull down my barns, and build greater... thou hast much goods laid up for many years... But God said unto him, Thou fool, this night thy soul shall be required of thee"
  - *Interpretation:* Moses accurately identifies the primary spiritual danger of Canaan: not hardship but prosperity. The wilderness formed them through need; Canaan would test them through abundance. Every form of worldly success contains within it the seed of the same lie: "my power and the might of mine hand hath gotten me this wealth" (verse 17). From Hosea's testimony of exact fulfillment to Laodicea to the rich fool, the pattern is consistent: abundance without gratitude and active remembrance of God produces spiritual amnesia and then destruction.

### "My Power and the Might of Mine Hand Hath Gotten Me This Wealth"

**Deuteronomy 8:17-18** — "And thou say in thine heart, My power and the might of mine hand hath gotten me this wealth. But thou shalt remember the LORD thy God: for it is he that giveth thee power to get wealth, that he may establish his covenant which he sware unto thy fathers, as it is this day."

- **1 Corinthians 4:7** — "For who maketh thee to differ from another? and what hast thou that thou didst not receive? now if thou didst receive it, why dost thou glory, as if thou hadst not received it?"
- **James 1:17** — "Every good gift and every perfect gift is from above, and cometh down from the Father of lights, with whom is no variableness, neither shadow of turning."
- **Proverbs 10:22** — "The blessing of the LORD, it maketh rich, and he addeth no sorrow with it."
- **John 15:5** — "without me ye can do nothing" — the foundational dependency principle
  - *Interpretation:* "It is he that giveth thee power to get wealth" is the correction to the lie of self-sufficiency. The very capacity to labor, think, and acquire is itself a gift of God. This does not mean the lazy are spiritual; it means the productive are to acknowledge the source. Paul's question in 1 Corinthians 4:7 is the most concise expression: "what hast thou that thou didst not receive?" If all is received, there is no room for the pride of self-achievement. Note the purpose: God gives the power to get wealth "that he may establish his covenant" — wealth is instrumental to covenant purposes, not an end in itself.
