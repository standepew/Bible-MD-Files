## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Moses confronts Israel's potential pride about the coming conquest: you are not going in for your righteousness or the uprightness of your heart. You are going in because of the wickedness of these nations AND because God is keeping His oath to the fathers. Understand: you are a stiffnecked people. Moses then rehearses the Horeb catastrophe in detail: forty days and nights on the mountain receiving the tablets, the LORD's report of the golden calf, Moses descending to find the calf, breaking the tablets, destroying the calf, falling before the LORD a second forty days and nights in intercession, praying for Aaron also. He then catalogs the other rebellions: Taberah, Massah, Kibroth-hattaavah, Kadesh-barnea. His conclusion: "Ye have been rebellious against the LORD from the day that I knew you." His intercession is explicit: he prays on the basis of God's redemptive act, the covenant with the fathers, and the concern for God's reputation among the nations.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Not for Thy Righteousness" — Grace, Not Merit

**Deuteronomy 9:4-6** — "Speak not thou in thine heart, after that the LORD thy God hath cast them out from before thee, saying, For my righteousness the LORD hath brought me in to possess this land... Not for thy righteousness, or for the uprightness of thine heart, dost thou go to possess their land... for thou art a stiffnecked people."

- **Deuteronomy 7:7-8** — Already established: not because of numbers but because of God's love and oath
- **Titus 3:5** — "Not by works of righteousness which we have done, but according to his mercy he saved us"
- **Romans 3:10, 23** — "There is none righteous, no, not one... for all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God"
- **Ephesians 2:8-9** — "For by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves: it is the gift of God: Not of works, lest any man should boast."
- **Isaiah 64:6** — "But we are all as an unclean thing, and all our righteousnesses are as filthy rags"
  - *Interpretation:* Deuteronomy 9 is the Old Testament's most explicit anti-merit statement. The land is not given for Israel's righteousness — the text says this three times in four verses. The reason for the gift is twofold: the wickedness of the nations and the covenant with the fathers (grace and judgment together). Paul's doctrine of grace in Ephesians 2 and Romans 3 is not new; it is the consistent teaching of Deuteronomy 9. The Old Testament believer had no ground for boasting any more than the New Testament believer does.

### The Golden Calf — The First Great Apostasy

**Deuteronomy 9:12-17** — "And the LORD said unto me, Arise, get thee down quickly from hence; for thy people which thou hast brought forth out of Egypt have corrupted themselves; they are quickly turned aside out of the way which I commanded them; they have made them a molten image."

- **Exodus 32:1-6** — The original account: the people gathered to Aaron saying "make us gods"; Aaron made the golden calf; they said "These be thy gods, O Israel, which brought thee up out of the land of Egypt"
- **Exodus 32:10** — God offers to destroy Israel and make Moses a great nation: "Now therefore let me alone, that my wrath may wax hot against them"
- **Psalm 106:19-20** — "They made a calf in Horeb, and worshipped the molten image. Thus they changed their glory into the similitude of an ox that eateth grass."
- **Acts 7:40-41** — Stephen rehearses this: "saying unto Aaron, Make us gods to go before us... And they made a calf in those days, and offered sacrifice unto the idol, and rejoiced in the works of their own hands."
- **Romans 1:23** — "And changed the glory of the uncorruptible God into an image made like to corruptible man, and to birds, and fourfooted beasts, and creeping things." — Paul describes the Gentile version of the same exchange
- **1 Corinthians 10:7** — "Neither be ye idolaters, as were some of them; as it is written, The people sat down to eat and drink, and rose up to play."
  - *Interpretation:* The golden calf is the paradigm of idolatry: substituting a man-made image for the living God, and then attributing God's works to the image. Psalm 106:20 frames it as an exchange — "they changed their glory into the similitude of an ox." Romans 1:23 applies the same exchange to Gentile idolatry. The pattern is universal: the human heart gravitates toward visible, controllable representations of the divine. Paul uses the golden calf in 1 Corinthians 10 as a warning directly applicable to the church.

### Moses' Intercession — The Pattern of the True Mediator

**Deuteronomy 9:18-20, 25-29** — Moses falls before the LORD forty days and forty nights, neither eating bread nor drinking water, interceding for Israel and for Aaron:

**Deuteronomy 9:26-29** — "And I prayed unto the LORD, and said, O Lord GOD, destroy not thy people and thine inheritance, which thou hast redeemed through thy greatness, which thou hast brought forth out of Egypt with a mighty hand. Remember thy servants, Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob; look not unto the stubbornness of this people, nor to their wickedness, nor to their sin: Lest the land whence thou broughtest us out say, Because the LORD was not able to bring them into the land which he promised them, and because he hated them, he hath brought them out to slay them in the wilderness. Yet they are thy people and thine inheritance, which thou broughtest out by thy mighty power and by thy stretched out arm."

- **Exodus 32:11-13, 31-32** — The original intercession: Moses even offers himself: "And if not, blot me, I pray thee, out of thy book which thou hast written." — Moses' ultimate identification with the people he intercedes for
- **Numbers 14:13-19** — Moses again intercedes after the spies' failure, using the same arguments: God's reputation among the nations, His declared character (slow to anger), the covenant with the fathers
- **Romans 8:34** — "Who is he that condemneth? It is Christ that died, yea rather, that is risen again, who is even at the right hand of God, who also maketh intercession for us."
- **Hebrews 7:25** — "he is able also to save them to the uttermost that come unto God by him, seeing he ever liveth to make intercession for them."
- **John 17** — Jesus' high priestly prayer: "I pray for them: I pray not for the world, but for them which thou hast given me" — the ultimate intercession on the basis of the Father's ownership and glory
  - *Interpretation:* Moses' intercession is one of Scripture's most powerful types of Christ's intercessory work. Note the structure of Moses' prayer:
    1. Appeal to God's prior redemptive act ("which thou hast redeemed")
    2. Appeal to the covenant with the fathers ("Remember Abraham, Isaac, Jacob")
    3. Concern for God's reputation among the nations
    4. Identification of the people as God's own ("yet they are thy people and thine inheritance")
    
    These same four elements appear in Jesus' high priestly prayer (John 17) and in Paul's argument for Israel's preservation (Romans 11). Moses points to the Greater Intercessor who ever lives to make intercession for those the Father has given Him.

### "Ye Have Been Rebellious Against the LORD from the Day That I Knew You"

**Deuteronomy 9:24** — "Ye have been rebellious against the LORD from the day that I knew you."

- **Isaiah 48:8** — "thou wast called a transgressor from the womb" — Israel's constitutionally rebellious nature
- **Romans 3:9-12** — "both Jews and Gentiles, that they are all under sin; As it is written, There is none righteous, no, not one: There is none that understandeth, there is none that seeketh after God."
- **Jeremiah 17:9** — "The heart is deceitful above all things, and desperately wicked: who can know it?"
- **Genesis 8:21** — "for the imagination of man's heart is evil from his youth" — established before Moses, before the Law
  - *Interpretation:* Moses' indictment covers the entire period of his knowledge of Israel — from Egypt onward. The catalog in verses 22-23 (Taberah, Massah, Kibroth-hattaavah, Kadesh-barnea) demonstrates the persistence of the pattern. This is not a charge unique to a particularly bad generation; it is the diagnosis of the human condition that necessitates the new covenant of Jeremiah 31 and the Spirit within of Ezekiel 36. The Law cannot fix what the Law was not designed to fix — it can only reveal it.

### The Broken Tables — Judgment Before Grace

**Deuteronomy 9:17** — "And I took the two tables, and cast them out of my two hands, and brake them before your eyes."

- **Exodus 32:15-19** — The original account: Moses saw the calf and the dancing and his anger waxed hot and he threw the tables
- **Exodus 34:1-4** — God commands new tables to be made; He writes on them again — restoration after judgment
- *Interpretation:* The breaking of the tablets is a prophetic act: Israel had already broken the covenant before Moses reached the bottom of the mountain. The tablets stated the terms; Israel had violated them while Moses was still receiving them. The broken tablets are the visible testimony of a broken covenant. God's grace immediately follows: He commands new tablets and writes on them again. The pattern is judgment → broken covenant → gracious restoration of the covenant terms. This is the pattern of all covenant history.
