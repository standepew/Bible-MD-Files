## CHAPTER SUMMARY

God commands Moses to hew two new stone tablets and come up to the mountain; also to make an ark of wood. God writes the ten commandments again on the new tablets; Moses places them in the ark. After a note on Israel's journeying and Aaron's death, the chapter records the separation of the tribe of Levi to bear the ark, to stand before the LORD to minister, and to bless in His name — with the LORD as their inheritance rather than a territorial portion. Moses then shifts to exhortation: Israel has seen all of God's acts, and now the LORD asks only this — to fear Him, walk in His ways, love Him, and serve Him with all heart and soul. The pivotal command: "Circumcise therefore the foreskin of your heart, and be no more stiffnecked." The basis for all compliance: the LORD is God of gods and Lord of lords — terrible, impartial, caring for the fatherless and widow and stranger. The chapter closes with the rehearsal of the seventy who went to Egypt, now become as the stars of heaven — another fulfillment of the Abrahamic promise.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### The Ark of Wood — A Box to Contain God's Word

**Deuteronomy 10:1-5** — Moses hews two tablets and makes an ark of shittim wood to hold them

- **Exodus 25:10-22** — The full ark commanded: acacia (shittim) wood, overlaid with gold, with the mercy seat and cherubim above — the place where God would meet Moses and speak
- **Hebrews 9:4** — "Which had the golden censer, and the ark of the covenant overlaid round about with gold, wherein was the golden pot that had manna, and Aaron's rod that budded, and the tables of the covenant"
- **Revelation 11:19** — "And the temple of God was opened in heaven, and there was seen in his temple the ark of his testament"
  - *Interpretation:* In Deuteronomy 10, Moses makes a simple wooden box — the plain ark before the gold overlay. The contents are what matter: God's own word (the tablets) placed inside the ark, to be carried by the Levites before the LORD's presence. The ark is the container of the covenant terms; the mercy seat above it is where blood was applied on the Day of Atonement — covering the law that condemns. The law inside, the blood above: this is the gospel in type. In the new covenant, believers are the living ark — God's law written on the heart (Jeremiah 31:33), covered by Christ's blood.

### "The LORD Said Unto Me... Arise, Take Thy Journey" — Restoration After Failure

**Deuteronomy 10:11** — "And the LORD said unto me, Arise, take thy journey before the people, that they may go in and possess the land, which I sware unto their fathers to give unto them."

- **Genesis 22:1-2** — "And God said... Arise, get thee into the land of Moriah" — arise signals a new divine initiative, a new stage of the journey
- The pattern: radical failure (golden calf) → intercession → restoration of the word (new tablets) → forward movement. The command "arise, take thy journey" comes after the covenant is restored. God does not remain stuck in the failure; He moves forward through it.
- **Isaiah 43:18-19** — "Remember ye not the former things... Behold, I will do a new thing" — God's consistent movement forward after judgment and restoration

### "What Doth the LORD Thy God Require of Thee"

**Deuteronomy 10:12-13** — "And now, Israel, what doth the LORD thy God require of thee, but to fear the LORD thy God, to walk in all his ways, and to love him, and to serve the LORD thy God with all thy heart and with all thy soul, To keep the commandments of the LORD, and his statutes, which I command thee this day for thy good?"

- **Micah 6:8** — "He hath shewed thee, O man, what is good; and what doth the LORD require of thee, but to do justly, and to love mercy, and to walk humbly with thy God?" — Micah echoes the same formula
- **Matthew 22:37-40** — Jesus summarizes the whole law: love God with all, love neighbor as self — the same simplification
- **Ecclesiastes 12:13** — "Fear God, and keep his commandments: for this is the whole duty of man."
- **1 Samuel 15:22** — "to obey is better than sacrifice, and to hearken than the fat of rams"
  - *Interpretation:* The "what doth the LORD require" formula appears three times in Scripture (Deuteronomy 10:12-13, Micah 6:8, and implied in Jesus' summary of Matthew 22:37-40). Each time, the answer reduces the whole law to its essential posture: fear, love, walk, serve. The list is not a replacement for the specific commands but their distillation into motivational foundation. Obedience flows from this posture; ritual without this posture is hollow.

### "Circumcise the Foreskin of Your Heart"

**Deuteronomy 10:16** — "Circumcise therefore the foreskin of your heart, and be no more stiffnecked."

- **Deuteronomy 30:6** — "And the LORD thy God will circumcise thine heart, and the heart of thy seed, to love the LORD thy God with all thine heart and with all thy soul, that thou mayest live." — in Deuteronomy 10, the command is given to Israel; in Deuteronomy 30, God promises to do it Himself
- **Jeremiah 4:4** — "Circumcise yourselves to the LORD, and take away the foreskins of your heart, ye men of Judah and inhabitants of Jerusalem"
- **Jeremiah 9:26** — "all the house of Israel are uncircumcised in the heart"
- **Romans 2:28-29** — "For he is not a Jew, which is one outwardly; neither is that circumcision, which is outward in the flesh: But he is a Jew, which is one inwardly; and circumcision is that of the heart, in the spirit, and not in the letter; whose praise is not of men, but of God."
- **Colossians 2:11** — "In whom also ye are circumcised with the circumcision made without hands, in putting off the body of the sins of the flesh by the circumcision of Christ"
  - *Interpretation:* The physical circumcision given to Abraham (Genesis 17) was always pointing to something deeper. Deuteronomy 10:16 issues the demand for heart circumcision — cutting away the spiritual foreskin of hardness and resistance. Deuteronomy 30:6 promises God will do this Himself in the new covenant. Romans 2:28-29 and Colossians 2:11 confirm: the circumcision that matters is the circumcision of Christ applied to the heart by the Spirit. Physical circumcision was the outward sign; heart circumcision is the inward reality it always signified.

### "God of Gods and Lord of Lords" — The Incomparable LORD

**Deuteronomy 10:17** — "For the LORD your God is God of gods, and Lord of lords, a great God, a mighty, and a terrible, which regardeth not persons, nor taketh reward"

- **Psalm 136:2-3** — "O give thanks unto the God of gods: for his mercy endureth for ever. O give thanks to the Lord of lords: for his mercy endureth for ever."
- **Daniel 2:47** — "Of a truth it is, that your God is a God of gods, and a Lord of kings, and a revealer of secrets" — Nebuchadnezzar confesses this after Daniel interprets his dream
- **Revelation 17:14** — The Lamb who overcomes is called "King of kings, and Lord of lords"
- **Revelation 19:16** — On His vesture and thigh: "KING OF KINGS, AND LORD OF LORDS"
  - *Interpretation:* The title "God of gods and Lord of lords" in Deuteronomy 10:17 is the same title applied to Christ in Revelation 17:14 and 19:16. The One who is incomparable among all so-called gods in Deuteronomy is the LORD Jesus Christ who returns as conquering King. This is not coincidence but the fulfillment of the Shema's exclusive claim: the LORD (YHWH) who is God of gods is identified in the New Testament as the glorified Christ.

### God's Care for the Fatherless, Widow, and Stranger

**Deuteronomy 10:18-19** — "He doth execute the judgment of the fatherless and widow, and loveth the stranger, in giving him food and raiment. Love ye therefore the stranger: for ye were strangers in the land of Egypt."

- **Psalm 68:5** — "A father of the fatherless, and a judge of the widows, is God in his holy habitation."
- **Psalm 146:9** — "The LORD preserveth the strangers; he relieveth the fatherless and widow"
- **Zechariah 7:9-10** — "Thus speaketh the LORD of hosts, saying, Execute true judgment, and shew mercy and compassions every man to his brother: And oppress not the widow, nor the fatherless, the stranger, nor the poor; and let none of you imagine evil against his brother in your heart."
- **Matthew 25:35-36** — Jesus: "I was a stranger, and ye took me in... I was in prison, and ye came unto me" — care for the vulnerable as service to Christ
- **James 1:27** — "Pure religion and undefiled before God and the Father is this, To visit the fatherless and widows in their affliction, and to keep himself unspotted from the world."
  - *Interpretation:* The God who is God of gods and Lord of lords is simultaneously the defender of the most vulnerable. The connection is deliberate: His supreme power does not make Him remote from the powerless — it makes Him their defender. Israel's obligation to love the stranger is grounded in their own experience: "ye were strangers in the land of Egypt." Empathy born from shared experience is the basis for covenant ethics toward the outsider.

### Seventy Souls to Egypt / Now Stars of Heaven — Promise Kept

**Deuteronomy 10:22** — "Thy fathers went down into Egypt with threescore and ten persons; and now the LORD thy God hath made thee as the stars of heaven for multitude."

- **Genesis 46:27** — "All the souls of the house of Jacob, which came into Egypt, were threescore and ten."
- **Genesis 15:5** — God's promise to Abraham: "So shall thy seed be" (as the stars)
- **Deuteronomy 1:10** — Moses already cited this: "ye are this day as the stars of heaven for multitude"
  - *Interpretation:* Seventy people → a nation as the stars of heaven. Moses bookends his exhortation with this arithmetic miracle. The same God who took seventy and made millions is the God asking Israel to trust Him going into Canaan. The fulfillment of the star-promise is the evidence that God keeps every word. "The LORD thy God hath made thee" — the multiplication is His work, not Israel's.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### "Circumcise Your Heart" (mul levav — to cut, to circumcise the heart)

- **Deuteronomy 10:16** — the command is Israel's responsibility (circumcise!)
- **Deuteronomy 30:6** — the promise is God's work (the LORD will circumcise your heart)
- Both are true and together form the pattern of sanctification: God commands what only He can ultimately accomplish, creating the posture of dependence and yieldedness in the believer who takes the command seriously. The command drives the believer to the Giver who can fulfill it.

### "LORD Is His Inheritance" (YHWH nachalato — the LORD is his portion)

- **Deuteronomy 10:9** — "the LORD is his inheritance" — said of Levi's tribe
- **Numbers 18:20** — God to Aaron: "I am thy part and thine inheritance among the children of Israel"
- **Psalm 16:5** — David: "The LORD is the portion of mine inheritance and of my cup"
- **Psalm 73:26** — "God is the strength of my heart, and my portion for ever"
- **Lamentations 3:24** — "The LORD is my portion, saith my soul; therefore will I hope in him."
- The Levitical principle extends to all who find their fullness in God rather than in earthly portions. The one who has the LORD has the ultimate inheritance; all earthly portions are secondary.

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The Ark Holding the Law — The Believer as Living Ark

**Deuteronomy 10:5** — tablets placed inside the wooden ark  
**Jeremiah 31:33** — new covenant: law written on the heart  
**2 Corinthians 3:3** — "written... not in tables of stone, but in fleshy tables of the heart"

The progression: stone tablets → wooden ark → heart of flesh. Each stage moves the same law (God's covenant terms) closer to the center of the person. In the new covenant, the believer becomes the living ark — the dwelling place of the Spirit (1 Corinthians 6:19 — "your body is the temple of the Holy Ghost"), the one in whom God's word lives and moves.

### "God of Gods and Lord of Lords" → The Returning Christ

**Deuteronomy 10:17** — the foundation title  
**Revelation 17:14** — "he is Lord of lords, and King of kings: and they that are with him are called, and chosen, and faithful"  
**Revelation 19:16** — "KING OF KINGS, AND LORD OF LORDS"

The title ascribed to YHWH in Deuteronomy 10:17 is the title given to the returning Christ in Revelation 19. The same LORD who declared His incomparable sovereignty at Horeb is the same LORD who rides the white horse at the end of the age. Scripture's testimony is internally consistent across both testaments.
