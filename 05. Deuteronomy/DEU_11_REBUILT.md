## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Moses commands Israel to love the LORD and keep His commandments always, reminding them that their children have not seen God's mighty acts in Egypt, the Red Sea crossing, or the wilderness judgments like Dathan and Abiram. He contrasts Egypt's irrigation system with Canaan's dependence on divine rain, promising blessing for obedience (rain in season, abundant crops) and curse for disobedience (no rain, no fruit). Moses instructs them to bind God's words on their hands, between their eyes, teach them constantly to children, and write them on doorposts and gates. He sets before them the choice of blessing (obedience) and curse (disobedience), to be pronounced on Mount Gerizim and Mount Ebal when they enter the land, promising that obedience will bring fear of them upon all nations and possession of the land from the wilderness to Lebanon and the Euphrates to the sea.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Love the LORD your God" — The Greatest Commandment

**Deuteronomy 11:1** — "Therefore thou shalt love the LORD thy God, and keep his charge, and his statutes, and his judgments, and his commandments, alway."

- **Matthew 22:37-38** — "Jesus said unto him, Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy mind. This is the first and great commandment."
  - *Interpretation:* Jesus quotes Deuteronomy as the foundation of all commandments. Love for God comes first, then obedience flows from that love.

- **Deuteronomy 6:5** — "And thou shalt love the LORD thy God with all thine heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy might."
  - *Interpretation:* Moses repeats this command multiple times. This is not optional—it's the foundation of the covenant relationship.

- **John 14:15** — "If ye love me, keep my commandments."
  - *Interpretation:* Jesus directly links love to obedience, exactly as Moses does in Deuteronomy 11:1. Love is not mere emotion but demonstrated through keeping God's commands.

### "Bind them for a sign upon your hand" — Physical Reminders

**Deuteronomy 11:18** — "Therefore shall ye lay up these my words in your heart and in your soul, and bind them for a sign upon your hand, that they may be as frontlets between your eyes."

- **Deuteronomy 6:8-9** — "And thou shalt bind them for a sign upon thine hand, and they shall be as frontlets between thine eyes. And thou shalt write them upon the posts of thy house, and on thy gates."
  - *Interpretation:* Moses repeats this command exactly. Physical reminders are meant to keep God's words constantly before them.

- **Exodus 13:9** — "And it shall be for a sign unto thee upon thine hand, and for a memorial between thine eyes, that the LORD's law may be in thy mouth"
  - *Interpretation:* This principle began with the Passover command. God's deliverance and His law are to be constantly remembered.

- **Proverbs 7:3** — "Bind them upon thy fingers, write them upon the table of thine heart"
  - *Interpretation:* Wisdom literature carries forward this principle of constant physical and mental reminders of God's truth.

---

### "Blessing and curse" — The Choice Before Israel

**Deuteronomy 11:26-28** — "Behold, I set before you this day a blessing and a curse; A blessing, if ye obey the commandments of the LORD your God, which I command you this day: And a curse, if ye will not obey the commandments of the LORD your God"

- **Deuteronomy 28:1-2** — "And it shall come to pass, if thou shalt hearken diligently unto the voice of the LORD thy God, to observe and to do all his commandments which I command thee this day, that the LORD thy God will set thee on high above all nations of the earth: And all these blessings shall come on thee"
  - *Interpretation:* Deuteronomy 28 expands this blessing and curse in detail. The principle introduced here is foundational to the entire covenant.

- **Joshua 24:15** — "And if it seem evil unto you to serve the LORD, choose you this day whom ye will serve"
  - *Interpretation:* Joshua presents the same choice—serve the LORD or serve other gods. The choice is always before God's people.

- **Psalm 1:1-6** — "Blessed is the man that walketh not in the counsel of the ungodly... but his delight is in the law of the LORD"
  - *Interpretation:* The psalmist presents the same two paths—blessing for obedience, curse for disobedience.

---

### "Mount Gerizim and Mount Ebal" — The Ceremony of Choice

**Deuteronomy 11:29** — "And it shall come to pass, when the LORD thy God hath brought thee in unto the land whither thou goest to possess it, that thou shalt put the blessing upon mount Gerizim, and the curse upon mount Ebal."

- **Joshua 8:30-35** — "Then Joshua built an altar unto the LORD God of Israel in mount Ebal... And he wrote there upon the stones a copy of the law of Moses... And all Israel, and their elders, and officers, and their judges, stood on this side the ark and on that side... to bless and curse according to all that is written in the book of the law."
  - *Interpretation:* Joshua fulfills this command exactly as Moses gave it. The ceremony actually happens, showing this is not symbolic but literal instruction.

- **Judges 9:7** — "And when they told it to Jotham, he went and stood in the top of mount Gerizim, and lifted up his voice, and cried"
  - *Interpretation:* Mount Gerizim becomes known as the mount of blessing. Jotham's parable is delivered from this significant location.

### "Rain in due season" — Agricultural Blessing

**Deuteronomy 11:14-15** — "That I will give you the rain of your land in his due season, the first rain and the latter rain, that thou mayest gather in thy corn, and thy wine, and thine oil. And I will send grass in thy fields for thy cattle, that thou mayest eat and be full."

- **Joel 2:23-24** — "Be glad then, ye children of Zion, and rejoice in the LORD your God: for he hath given you the former rain moderately, and he will cause to come down for you the rain, the former rain, and the latter rain in the first month. And the floors shall be full of wheat, and the vats shall overflow with wine and oil."
  - *Interpretation:* Joel prophesies restoration using the same agricultural blessings Moses promised. The "former rain" and "latter rain" become prophetic terms.

- **James 5:7** — "Be patient therefore, brethren, unto the coming of the Lord. Behold, the husbandman waiteth for the precious fruit of the earth, and hath long patience for it, until he receive the early and latter rain."
  - *Interpretation:* James uses this agricultural principle as a spiritual metaphor. The early and latter rain pattern established in Deuteronomy becomes a picture of spiritual patience and God's timing.

- **1 Kings 8:35-36** — "When heaven is shut up, and there is no rain, because they have sinned against thee; if they pray toward this place, and confess thy name, and turn from their sin, when thou dost afflict them: Then hear thou in heaven, and forgive the sin of thy servants"
  - *Interpretation:* Solomon's prayer recognizes this principle exactly as Moses stated it—no rain comes because of sin, rain returns with repentance.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### FRONTLETS
- **Deuteronomy 11:18 usage:** Physical reminders worn on the forehead containing God's words
- **Not just jewelry:** These are teaching tools to keep Scripture constantly in mind
- **Modern application:** The principle is constant meditation on Scripture, not necessarily physical phylacteries

### FIRST RAIN AND LATTER RAIN
- **Agricultural meaning:** Early rain for planting, later rain for harvesting
- **Prophetic meaning:** Outpouring of God's Spirit in different seasons (Joel 2:23, James 5:7)
- **Conditional blessing:** Comes only with obedience to God's commands

### MOUNT GERIZIM AND MOUNT EBAL
- **Geographical reality:** Actual mountains near Shechem where the ceremony took place (Joshua 8:30-35)
- **Symbolic meaning:** Gerizim = blessing, Ebal = curse
- **Later significance:** Samaritans built their temple on Mount Gerizim, claiming it as the proper place of worship

### DAYS OF HEAVEN UPON EARTH
- **Deuteronomy 11:21 meaning:** Extended life in the land as long as heaven continues
- **Not eternal life:** This is about longevity in the promised land, not heavenly existence
- **Conditional promise:** Depends on obedience to God's commands

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The Choice Before All Generations
- **Adam and Eve's choice:** Tree of life vs tree of knowledge (Genesis 2-3)
- **Cain and Abel's choice:** Faithful sacrifice vs worthless offering (Genesis 4)
- **Joshua's choice:** "Choose you this day whom ye will serve" (Joshua 24:15)
- **New Testament choice:** "Wide gate and broad way vs strait gate and narrow way" (Matthew 7:13-14)

### Rain as Spiritual Blessing
- **Early Church outpouring:** Former rain at Pentecost (Acts 2)
- **End-times revival:** Latter rain before harvest (Joel 2:28-32)
- **Spiritual drought:** When heaven is shut up by sin (1 Kings 17:1)

### Mountains as Places of Decision
- **Mount Sinai:** Law given (Exodus 19-20)
- **Mount of Transfiguration:** Jesus glorified (Matthew 17:1-5)
- **Mount of Olives:** Jesus' agony and ascension (Matthew 26:30, Acts 1:9-12)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Bind them on your hand"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about wearing literal phylacteries today
**Biblical context:** The principle is constant meditation and remembrance of God's Word. The physical objects were teaching tools for a largely illiterate society. The deeper principle is keeping Scripture always before your mind and actions.

### "The days of heaven upon earth"
**Common misinterpretation:** This means eternal life in heaven
**Biblical context:** This refers to extended life in the promised land as long as obedience continues. It's about longevity in Canaan, not heavenly existence. The promise is conditional and earthly.

- **Mount Sinai:** Law and covenant (Exodus 19-24)
- **Mount Gerizim/Ebal:** Blessing and curse (Deuteronomy 11, Joshua 8)

- **Heart not deceived:** Warning against internal corruption (Deuteronomy 11:16)
- **Write on heart:** New covenant promise (Jeremiah 31:33)
- **Heart circumcision:** True spirituality (Romans 2:29)
