## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Moses commands Israel to completely destroy all pagan worship sites—high places, hills, green trees, altars, pillars, groves, and graven images—when they enter Canaan. They must not worship the LORD as the pagans worship their gods. Instead, they must seek the place where God chooses to put His name, bringing all burnt offerings, sacrifices, tithes, heave offerings, vows, and freewill offerings there to eat and rejoice before God. While they may eat regular meat in their towns, they must not eat the blood, for the blood is the life. Holy things and vows must be eaten at the chosen place. Moses warns against being snared by pagan practices, especially child sacrifice, and commands strict obedience without adding to or diminishing from God's commands.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "The place which the LORD shall choose" — Centralized Worship

**Deuteronomy 12:5, 11, 14, 18, 21, 26** — "But unto the place which the LORD your God shall choose out of all your tribes to put his name there"

- **2 Chronicles 7:16** — "For now have I chosen and sanctified this house, that my name may be there for ever: and mine eyes and mine heart shall be there perpetually."
  - *Interpretation:* Solomon's temple becomes the fulfillment of this promise. God chooses Jerusalem as the place for His name to dwell.

- **1 Kings 8:29** — "That thine eyes may be open toward this house night and day, even toward the place of which thou hast said, My name shall be there"
  - *Interpretation:* Solomon's dedication prayer recognizes that the temple is the place where God has chosen to put His name.

- **John 4:21-24** — "Woman, believe me, the hour cometh, when ye shall neither in this mountain, nor yet at Jerusalem, worship the Father... But the hour cometh, and now is, when the true worshippers shall worship the Father in spirit and in truth"
  - *Interpretation:* Jesus transitions from physical location worship to spiritual worship. The principle of one place of worship moves from Jerusalem to the heart of the believer.

### "Destroy all the places" — Removing Idolatry

**Deuteronomy 12:2-3** — "Ye shall utterly destroy all the places, wherein the nations which ye shall possess served their gods... And ye shall overthrow their altars, and break their pillars, and burn their groves with fire"

- **Exodus 23:24** — "Thou shalt not bow down to their gods, nor serve them, nor do after their works: but thou shalt utterly overthrow them, and quite break down their images."
  - *Interpretation:* This command was given earlier in the wilderness. Moses repeats it because they're about to enter the land where these places actually exist.

- **Judges 2:2-3** — "And ye shall make no league with the inhabitants of this land; ye shall throw down their altars: but ye have not obeyed my voice: why have ye done this? Wherefore I also said, I will not drive them out from before you; but they shall be as thorns in your sides"
  - *Interpretation:* Israel's failure to destroy these places leads to their downfall. The command is not optional—it's essential for survival.

- **2 Kings 23:4-20** — "And the king commanded Hilkiah the high priest... to bring forth out of the temple of the LORD all the vessels that were made for Baal... And he brought out the grove from the house of the LORD... And he brake down the altars"
  - *Interpretation:* Josiah's reforms show what should have been done initially. The command is finally fulfilled centuries later.

### "Not eat the blood" — The Sanctity of Life

**Deuteronomy 12:16, 23-25** — "Only ye shall not eat the blood; ye shall pour it upon the earth as water... for the blood is the life; and thou mayest not eat the life with the flesh"

- **Genesis 9:4** — "But flesh with the life thereof, which is the blood thereof, shall ye not eat."
  - *Interpretation:* This principle began with Noah after the flood. Blood represents life, which belongs to God alone.

- **Leviticus 17:11** — "For the life of the flesh is in the blood: and I have given it to you upon the altar to make an atonement for your souls: for it is the blood that maketh an atonement for the soul."
  - *Interpretation:* The blood prohibition is tied to atonement. Blood saves lives when properly applied on the altar, but must not be consumed.

- **Acts 15:20, 29** — "But that we write unto them, that they abstain from pollutions of idols, and from fornication, and from things strangled, and from blood"
  - *Interpretation:* The Jerusalem council maintains this prohibition for Gentile believers. It's not just ceremonial but a moral principle about the sanctity of life.

- **Hebrews 9:22** — "And almost all things are by the law purged with blood; and without shedding of blood is no remission."
  - *Interpretation:* The blood principle points to Christ's sacrifice. Blood's sacred value culminates in Jesus' blood providing true atonement.

### "Add not, diminish not" — Complete Obedience

**Deuteronomy 12:32** — "What thing soever I command you, observe to do it: thou shalt not add thereto, nor diminish from it."

- **Deuteronomy 4:2** — "Ye shall not add unto the word which I command you, neither shall ye diminish ought from it"
  - *Interpretation:* Moses repeats this warning multiple times. God's Word is complete as given.

- **Proverbs 30:6** — "Add thou not unto his words, lest he reprove thee, and thou be found a liar."
  - *Interpretation:* Wisdom literature carries forward this principle. Adding to God's words makes one a liar in God's eyes.

- **Revelation 22:18-19** — "For I testify unto every man that heareth the words of the prophecy of this book, If any man shall add unto these things, God shall add unto him the plagues that are written in this book: And if any man shall take away from the words of the book of this prophecy, God shall take away his part out of the book of life"
  - *Interpretation:* The Bible ends with the same warning it began with. This principle covers all of Scripture from Genesis to Revelation.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### HIGH PLACES
- **Pagan meaning:** Elevated locations used for idol worship
- **Israel's failure:** These became stumbling blocks throughout their history (1 Kings 14:23, 2 Kings 17:10-11)
- **Spiritual principle:** Any place we elevate above God's chosen place of worship

### GROVES (ASHERAH)
- **Canaanite worship:** Sacred trees or poles dedicated to the goddess Asherah
- **God's hatred:** Called "abomination" and repeatedly condemned (Exodus 34:13, Deuteronomy 16:21)
- **Modern application:** Any naturalistic worship that replaces God with creation

### THE BLOOD IS THE LIFE
- **Biological reality:** Blood carries life throughout the body
- **Theological meaning:** Life belongs to God, blood represents atonement (Leviticus 17:11)
- **Christological fulfillment:** Jesus' blood gives eternal life (John 6:53-56)

### RIGHT IN HIS OWN EYES
- **Deuteronomy 12:8 meaning:** Doing what seems right without God's standard
- **Book of Judges theme:** "In those days there was no king in Israel: every man did that which was right in his own eyes" (Judges 17:6, 21:25)
- **New Testament warning:** "There is a way which seemeth right unto a man, but the end thereof are the ways of death" (Proverbs 14:12)

14 But in the place which the LORD shall choose in one of thy tribes, there thou shalt offer thy burnt offerings, and there thou shalt do all that I command thee. 

15 Notwithstanding thou mayest kill and eat flesh in all thy gates, whatsoever thy soul lusteth after, according to the blessing of the LORD thy God which he hath given thee: the unclean and the clean may eat thereof, as of the roebuck, and as of the hart.

17 Thou mayest not eat within thy gates the tithe of thy corn, or of thy wine, or of thy oil, or the firstlings of thy herds or of thy flock, nor any of thy vows which thou vowest, nor thy freewill offerings, or heave offering of thine hand: 

18 But thou must eat them before the LORD thy God in the place which the LORD thy God shall choose, thou, and thy son, and thy daughter, and thy manservant, and thy maidservant, and the Levite that is within thy gates: and thou shalt rejoice before the LORD thy God in all that thou puttest thine hands unto. 

19 Take heed to thyself that thou forsake not the Levite as long as thou livest upon the earth. 

20 When the LORD thy God shall enlarge thy border, as he hath promised thee, and thou shalt say, I will eat flesh, because thy soul longeth to eat flesh; thou mayest eat flesh, whatsoever thy soul lusteth after. 

21 If the place which the LORD thy God hath chosen to put his name there be too far from thee, then thou shalt kill of thy herd and of thy flock, which the LORD hath given thee, as I have commanded thee, and thou shalt eat in thy gates whatsoever thy soul lusteth after. 

22 Even as the roebuck and the hart is eaten, so thou shalt eat them: the unclean and the clean shall eat of them alike. 

23 Only be sure that thou eat not the blood: for the blood is the life; and thou mayest not eat the life with the flesh. 

24 Thou shalt not eat it; thou shalt pour it upon the earth as water. 

25 Thou shalt not eat it; that it may go well with thee, and with thy children after thee, when thou shalt do that which is right in the sight of the LORD. 

26 Only thy holy things which thou hast, and thy vows, thou shalt take, and go unto the place which the LORD shall choose: 

27 And thou shalt offer thy burnt offerings, the flesh and the blood, upon the altar of the LORD thy God: and the blood of thy sacrifices shall be poured out upon the altar of the LORD thy God, and thou shalt eat the flesh. 

28 Observe and hear all these words which I command thee, that it may go well with thee, and with thy children after thee for ever, when thou doest that which is good and right in the sight of the LORD thy God. 

29 When the LORD thy God shall cut off the nations from before thee, whither thou goest to possess them, and thou succeedest them, and dwellest in their land;

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Every man whatsoever is right in his own eyes"
**Common misinterpretation:** This promotes individual freedom and personal truth
**Biblical context:** This is presented as the problem, not the solution. Doing what seems right without God's standard leads to chaos and judgment. The solution is centralized worship and obedience to God's revealed commands.

### "The unclean and the clean may eat thereof"
**Common misinterpretation:** This abolishes the clean/unclean distinction
**Biblical context:** This refers specifically to eating common meat in towns, not sacrificial meat. The clean/unclean laws still apply elsewhere. This is about practical provision, not abolishing dietary distinctions.

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### Mountains as Worship Places
- **Mount Sinai:** God's law given (Exodus 19-20)
- **Mount Gerizim/Ebal:** Blessing and curse (Deuteronomy 11, Joshua 8)
- **Mount Moriah:** Temple site (2 Chronicles 3:1)
- **Mount of Olives:** Jesus' teaching and ascension (Matthew 24, Acts 1)

### Blood as Sacred
- **Noahic covenant:** Blood prohibition (Genesis 9:4)
- **Passover protection:** Blood on doorposts (Exodus 12)
- **Atonement system:** Blood on altar (Leviticus 16)
- **Christ's sacrifice:** Blood for salvation (Matthew 26:28)

### Trees as Religious Symbols
- **Tree of life:** Original provision (Genesis 2:9)
- **Tree of knowledge:** Original prohibition (Genesis 2:17)
- **Asherah groves:** Pagan corruption (Deuteronomy 12:3)
- **Cross:** Tree of redemption (Galatians 3:13)

---

**Chapter 12 establishes the principle of centralized worship:** God determines the place and manner of worship, not human preference. The complete removal of idolatry and strict adherence to God's commands without addition or subtraction becomes foundational for Israel's relationship with God. The blood prohibition teaches the sanctity of life, pointing ultimately to Christ's perfect sacrifice.
