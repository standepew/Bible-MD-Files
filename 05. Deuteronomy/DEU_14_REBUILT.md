## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Moses reminds Israel they are children and holy people of God, chosen to be peculiar above all nations. They must not practice pagan mourning rites (cutting themselves, baldness) or eat abominable things. Dietary laws are reviewed: clean land animals must both chew cud and divide hoof (ox, sheep, goat, deer, wild ox, chamois), excluding camel, hare, coney (chew cud but don't divide hoof) and swine (divides hoof but doesn't chew cud). Clean water creatures must have fins and scales. Clean birds are permitted but lists unclean birds of prey and scavengers (eagle, vulture, raven, owl, etc.) and all flying creeping things. They must not eat animals that die of themselves (give to strangers or sell), nor seethe a kid in its mother's milk. Tithing instructions: eat tithes before God at the chosen place; if distance is too far, convert to money and buy desired food there, rejoicing with household and Levites. Every third year, store tithes locally for Levites, strangers, fatherless, and widows.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Holy people... peculiar people" — Chosen Nation Status

**Deuteronomy 14:2** — "For thou art an holy people unto the LORD thy God, and the LORD hath chosen thee to be a peculiar people unto himself, above all the nations that are upon the earth."

- **Exodus 19:5-6** — "Now therefore, if ye will obey my voice indeed, and keep my covenant, then ye shall be a peculiar treasure unto me above all people: for all the earth is mine: And ye shall be unto me a kingdom of priests, and an holy nation."
  - *Interpretation:* This promise began at Sinai. The "peculiar treasure" status is conditional on obedience to the covenant.

- **1 Peter 2:9** — "But ye are a chosen generation, a royal priesthood, an holy nation, a peculiar people; that ye should shew forth the praises of him who hath called you out of darkness into his marvellous light"
  - *Interpretation:* Peter applies this language to the Church. The "peculiar people" status transfers from national Israel to spiritual Israel—the Church.

- **Titus 2:14** — "Who gave himself for us, that he might redeem us from all iniquity, and purify unto himself a peculiar people, zealous of good works."
  - *Interpretation:* Christ's death creates the new "peculiar people" who are purified and zealous for good works.

---

### "Cut yourselves, nor make any baldness" — Forbidden Mourning Practices

**Deuteronomy 14:1** — "Ye shall not cut yourselves, nor make any baldness between your eyes for the dead."

- **Leviticus 19:28** — "Ye shall not make any cuttings in your flesh for the dead, nor print any marks upon you: I am the LORD."
  - *Interpretation:* This prohibition was given earlier. Self-mutilation for the dead was associated with pagan worship practices.

- **1 Kings 18:28** — "And they cried aloud, and cut themselves after their manner with knives and lancets, till the blood gushed out upon them."
  - *Interpretation:* Elijah's contest on Mount Carmel shows Baal worshippers practicing exactly what Moses forbids. This is pagan, not Israelite, worship.

- **Jeremiah 16:6** — "Both the great and the small shall die in this land: they shall not be buried, neither shall men lament for them, nor cut themselves, nor make themselves bald for them"
  - *Interpretation:* Jeremiah prophesies judgment so severe that even forbidden mourning practices won't occur because no one will be left to mourn.

---

### "Chew the cud, and cleaveth the cleft into two claws" — Clean Animal Criteria

**Deuteronomy 14:6-8** — "And every beast that parteth the hoof, and cleaveth the cleft into two claws, and cheweth the cud among the beasts, that ye shall eat... the swine, because it divideth the hoof, yet cheweth not the cud, it is unclean unto you"

- **Leviticus 11:2-8** — "Speak unto the children of Israel, saying, These are the beasts which ye shall eat among all the beasts that are on the earth... Whatsoever parteth the hoof, and is clovenfooted, and cheweth the cud, among the beasts, that ye shall eat... And the swine, though he divide the hoof, and be clovenfooted, yet he cheweth not the cud; he is unclean to you."
  - *Interpretation:* This is almost identical to Leviticus 11. Moses repeats the laws for the new generation about to enter the land.

- **Isaiah 65:4** — "Which remain among the graves, and lodge in the monuments, which eat swine's flesh, and broth of abominable things is in their vessels"
  - *Interpretation:* Isaiah uses eating swine as a symbol of rebellion and impurity. The dietary law has spiritual significance beyond health.

- **Matthew 8:31-32** — "So the devils besought him, saying, If thou cast us out, suffer us to go away into the herd of swine. And he said unto them, Go. And when they were come out, they went into the herd of swine: and, behold, the whole herd of swine ran violently down a steep place into the sea, and perished in the waters."
  - *Interpretation:* Jesus allows demons to enter swine, showing these animals are unclean and suitable for demonic occupation. The dietary laws have spiritual reality.

---

### "Seethe a kid in his mother's milk" — Prohibited Practice

**Deuteronomy 14:21** — "Thou shalt not seethe a kid in his mother's milk."

- **Exodus 23:19** — "...Thou shalt not seethe a kid in his mother's milk."
- **Exodus 34:26** — "...Thou shalt not seethe a kid in his mother's milk."
  - *Interpretation:* This command appears three times in Scripture, showing its importance. It's likely connected to Canaanite fertility rituals where this practice had magical significance.

- **Leviticus 22:28** — "And whether it be cow or ewe, ye shall not kill it and her young both in one day."
  - *Interpretation:* The principle extends to not killing mother and offspring on the same day, showing sensitivity to natural relationships and preventing cruelty.

---

### "Turn it into money" — Practical Tithing

**Deuteronomy 14:24-26** — "Then shalt thou turn it into money, and bind up the money in thine hand, and shalt go unto the place which the LORD thy God shall choose: And thou shalt bestow that money for whatsoever thy soul lusteth after... and thou shalt eat there before the LORD thy God, and thou shalt rejoice"

- **Nehemiah 10:37-38** — "And that we should bring the firstfruits of our dough, and our offerings, and the fruit of all manner of trees, of wine and oil; unto the priests, to the chambers of the house of our God... And the priest the son of Aaron shall be with the Levites, when the Levites take tithes"
  - *Interpretation:* Nehemiah's reforms show this system was actually practiced. Tithes could be converted to money for practical transportation.

- **Malachi 3:10** — "Bring ye all the tithes into the storehouse, that there may be meat in mine house, and prove me now herewith, saith the LORD of hosts, if I will not open you the windows of heaven, and pour you out a blessing, that there shall not be room enough to receive it."
  - *Interpretation:* Malachi addresses failure to bring tithes to God's house. The system was still in place but being neglected.

- **Matthew 23:23** — "Woe unto you, scribes and Pharisees, hypocrites! for ye pay tithe of mint and anise and cummin, and have omitted the weightier matters of the law, judgment, mercy, and faith: these ought ye to have done, and not to leave the other undone."
  - *Interpretation:* Jesus affirms tithing but condemns doing it while neglecting justice and mercy. The system remains valid but must be balanced with other commandments.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### PECULIAR PEOPLE
- **Hebrew concept:** "Segullah" - treasured possession, private property
- **Not "strange":** Modern usage differs from biblical meaning
- **Covenant relationship:** Belongs exclusively to God for special purposes

### CHEW THE CUD
- **Scientific observation:** Ruminants regurgitate and re-chew food
- **Spiritual symbolism:** Meditation on God's Word (Psalm 1:2)
- **Digestive process:** Represents thorough processing of truth

### DIVIDE THE HOOF
- **Physical characteristic:** Split hooves for stability and cleanliness
- **Spiritual symbolism:** Separation from worldly contamination (2 Corinthians 6:17)
- **Both required:** Complete criteria, not partial compliance

### SEETHE A KID IN MOTHER'S MILK
- **Canaanite practice:** Fertility ritual involving mother's milk
- **Prohibition reason:** Prevents pagan worship infiltration
- **Principle:** Avoid practices associated with idolatry

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 Ye are the children of the LORD your God: ye shall not cut yourselves, nor make any baldness between your eyes for the dead. 

2 For thou art an holy people unto the LORD thy God, and the LORD hath chosen thee to be a peculiar people unto himself, above all the nations that are upon the earth. 

3 Thou shalt not eat any abominable thing. 

4 These are the beasts which ye shall eat: the ox, the sheep, and the goat, 

5 The hart, and the roebuck, and the fallow deer, and the wild goat, and the pygarg, and the wild ox, and the chamois. 

6 And every beast that parteth the hoof, and cleaveth the cleft into two claws, and cheweth the cud among the beasts, that ye shall eat. 

7 Nevertheless these ye shall not eat of them that chew the cud, or of them that divide the cloven hoof; as the camel, and the hare, and the coney: for they chew the cud, but divide not the hoof; therefore they are unclean unto you. 

8 And the swine, because it divideth the hoof, yet cheweth not the cud, it is unclean unto you: ye shall not eat of their flesh, nor touch their dead carcase. 

9 These ye shall eat of all that are in the waters: all that have fins and scales shall ye eat: 

10 And whatsoever hath not fins and scales ye may not eat; it is unclean unto you. 

11 Of all clean birds ye shall eat. 

12 But these are they of which ye shall not eat: the eagle, and the ossifrage, and the ospray, 

13 And the glede, and the kite, and the vulture after his kind, 

14 And every raven after his kind, 

15 And the owl, and the night hawk, and the cuckow, and the hawk after his kind, 

16 The little owl, and the great owl, and the swan, 

17 And the pelican, and the gier eagle, and the cormorant, 

18 And the stork, and the heron after her kind, and the lapwing, and the bat. 

19 And every creeping thing that flieth is unclean unto you: they shall not be eaten. 

20 But of all clean fowls ye may eat. 

21 Ye shall not eat of any thing that dieth of itself: thou shalt give it unto the stranger that is in thy gates, that he may eat it; or thou mayest sell it unto an alien: for thou art an holy people unto the LORD thy God. Thou shalt not seethe a kid in his mother's milk. 

22 Thou shalt truly tithe all the increase of thy seed, that the field bringeth forth year by year. 

23 And thou shalt eat before the LORD thy God, in the place which he shall choose to place his name there, the tithe of thy corn, of thy wine, and of thine oil, and the firstlings of thy herds and of thy flocks; that thou mayest learn to fear the LORD thy God always. 

24 And if the way be too long for thee, so that thou art not able to carry it; or if the place be too far from thee, which the LORD thy God shall choose to set his name there, when the LORD thy God hath blessed thee: 

25 Then shalt thou turn it into money, and bind up the money in thine hand, and shalt go unto the place which the LORD thy God shall choose: 

26 And thou shalt bestow that money for whatsoever thy soul lusteth after, for oxen, or for sheep, or for wine, or for strong drink, or for whatsoever thy soul desireth: and thou shalt eat there before the LORD thy God, and thou shalt rejoice, thou, and thine household, 

27 And the Levite that is within thy gates; thou shalt not forsake him; for he hath no part nor inheritance with thee. 

28 At the end of three years thou shalt bring forth all the tithe of thine increase the same year, and shalt lay it up within thy gates: 

29 And the Levite, (because he hath no part nor inheritance with thee,) and the stranger, and the fatherless, and the widow, which are within thy gates, shall come, and shall eat and be satisfied; that the LORD thy God may bless thee in all the work of thine hand which thou doest.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Whatsoever thy soul lusteth after"
**Common misinterpretation:** This promotes indulgence and following every desire
**Biblical context:** This specifically refers to buying food with tithe money at the chosen place of worship. It's about practical logistics for worship, not general license to follow any desire. The context is still within God's parameters.

### "Thou mayest sell it unto an alien"
**Common misinterpretation:** This permits exploiting foreigners with unclean food
**Biblical context:** This refers specifically to animals that died of themselves, which Israelites couldn't eat but foreigners weren't bound by the same dietary restrictions. It's about not wasting resources while maintaining holiness standards.
