## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Moses commands the seven-year release: at the end of every seven years, creditors must cancel debts owed by fellow Israelites (though foreigners can still be repaid). This is called "the LORD's release." God promises blessing and prosperity if they obey—no poor among them, lending to many nations but not borrowing, ruling over others but not being ruled. When a poor brother asks for help, they must open their hands wide, not harden their hearts or refuse because the release year is near. God promises to bless generous giving. Since the poor will never cease, they must always be open-handed. Hebrew servants serve six years and go free in the seventh, not empty-handed but furnished liberally from flocks, threshing floor, and winepress. They must remember they were bondmen in Egypt whom God redeemed. If a servant loves his master and refuses to leave, his ear is pierced with an aul to the door, making him a perpetual servant. Firstling males are sanctified to the LORD—no working with them or shearing sheep. They are eaten yearly at the chosen place; if blemished, they're eaten locally like common meat, but blood must be poured on the ground.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "The LORD's release" — Sabbatical Economics

**Deuteronomy 15:1-3** — "At the end of every seven years thou shalt make a release... Every creditor that lendeth ought unto his neighbour shall release it; he shall not exact it of his neighbour, or of his brother; because it is called the LORD's release."

- **Leviticus 25:1-4** — "And the LORD spake unto Moses in mount Sinai, saying, Speak unto the children of Israel, and say unto them, When ye come into the land which I give you, then shall the land keep a sabbath unto the LORD. Six years thou shalt sow thy field, and six years thou shalt prune thy vineyard, and gather in the fruit thereof; But in the seventh year shall be a sabbath of rest unto the land"
  - *Interpretation:* The seven-year cycle includes both land rest and debt cancellation. This is part of the broader Sabbatical system that acknowledges God as ultimate owner.

- **Nehemiah 10:31** — "If the people of the land bring ware or any victuals on the sabbath day to sell, that we would not buy it of them on the sabbath, or on the holy day: and that we would leave the seventh year, and the exaction of every debt."
  - *Interpretation:* Nehemiah's covenant renewal includes this exact provision. The system was actually practiced in Israel's history.

- **Isaiah 58:6** — "Is not this the fast that I have chosen? to loose the bands of wickedness, to undo the heavy burdens, and to let the oppressed go free, and that ye break every yoke?"
  - *Interpretation:* Isaiah connects economic liberation with true fasting. The Sabbatical release is part of God's justice system.

### "Open thine hand wide" — Generosity to the Poor

**Deuteronomy 15:7-11** — "If there be among you a poor man of one of thy brethren... thou shalt not harden thine heart, nor shut thine hand from thy poor brother: But thou shalt open thine hand wide unto him... Beware that there be not a thought in thy wicked heart, saying, The seventh year, the year of release, is at hand"

- **Proverbs 19:17** — "He that hath pity upon the poor lendeth unto the LORD; and that which he hath given will he pay him again."
  - *Interpretation:* Wisdom literature confirms that giving to the poor is lending to God Himself, who guarantees repayment.

- **Psalm 112:5-6** — "A good man sheweth favour, and lendeth: he will guide his affairs with discretion. Surely he shall not be moved for ever: the righteous shall be in everlasting remembrance."
  - *Interpretation:* The righteous person is characterized by generosity and lending, which leads to stability and eternal remembrance.

- **2 Corinthians 9:7-8** — "Every man according as he purposeth in his heart, so let him give; not grudgingly, or of necessity: for God loveth a cheerful giver. And God is able to make all grace abound toward you; that ye, always having all sufficiency in all things, may abound to every good work"
  - *Interpretation:* Paul builds on this principle—God loves cheerful givers and provides abundance so they can continue giving.

### "Thou wast a bondman in the land of Egypt" — Redemption Motivation

**Deuteronomy 15:15** — "And thou shalt remember that thou wast a bondman in the land of Egypt, and the LORD thy God redeemed thee: therefore I command thee this thing to day."

- **Exodus 21:2** — "If thou buy an Hebrew servant, six years he shall serve: and in the seventh he shall go out free for nothing."
  - *Interpretation:* This law was given at Sinai. Moses repeats it because they're about to enter the land where it will be practiced.

- **Leviticus 25:42-43** — "For they are my servants, which I brought forth out of the land of Egypt: they shall not be sold as bondmen. Thou shalt not rule over him with rigour; but shalt fear thy God."
  - *Interpretation:* God claims ownership of all Israelites because He redeemed them from Egypt. No human can have absolute ownership of another Israelite.

- **Luke 4:18-19** — "The Spirit of the Lord is upon me, because he hath anointed me to preach the gospel to the poor; he hath sent me to heal the brokenhearted, to preach deliverance to the captives, and recovering of sight to the blind, to set at liberty them that are bruised"
  - *Interpretation:* Jesus reads Isaiah's prophecy about liberation, fulfilling the redemption principle that began with Egypt and continues through Him.

### "Thrust it through his ear unto the door" — Voluntary Servitude

**Deuteronomy 15:16-17** — "And it shall be, if he say unto thee, I will not go away from thee; because he loveth thee and thine house... Then thou shalt take an aul, and thrust it through his ear unto the door, and he shall be thy servant for ever."

- **Exodus 21:5-6** — "And if the servant shall plainly say, I love my master, my wife, and my children; I will not go out free: His master shall bring him unto the judges; he shall also bring him to the door, or unto the door post; and his master shall bore his ear through with an aul; and he shall serve him for ever."
  - *Interpretation:* This is identical to Exodus 21. The ear piercing signifies permanent, voluntary commitment based on love, not coercion.

- **Psalm 40:6** — "Sacrifice and offering thou didst not desire; mine ears hast thou opened: burnt offering and sin offering hast thou not required."
  - *Interpretation:* David uses this imagery to describe his willingness to hear and obey God. The opened ear represents readiness to serve.

- **John 10:27** — "My sheep hear my voice: and I know them, and they follow me"
  - *Interpretation:* Jesus describes the relationship between Himself and His followers as one of hearing and following. The ear is the organ of reception for divine guidance.

### "Firstling males... sanctify unto the LORD" — Firstborn Principle

**Deuteronomy 15:19-23** — "All the firstling males that come of thy herd and of thy flock thou shalt sanctify unto the LORD thy God: thou shalt do no work with the firstling of thy bullock, nor shear the firstling of thy sheep."

- **Exodus 13:2, 12** — "Sanctify unto me all the firstborn, whatsoever openeth the womb among the children of Israel, both of man and of beast: it is mine... That thou shalt set apart unto the LORD all that openeth the womb, and every firstling that cometh of a beast which thou hast; the males shall be the LORD's."
  - *Interpretation:* The firstborn principle began at Passover in Egypt. Firstborns belong to God because He spared Israel's firstborns.

- **Numbers 18:15-18** — "Every thing that openeth the matrix in all flesh, which they bring unto the LORD, whether it be of men or beasts, shall be thine: nevertheless the firstborn of man shalt thou surely redeem, and the firstling of unclean beasts shalt thou redeem. And those of the firstborn of the cows, the firstling of the sheep, and the firstling of the goats, thou shalt not redeem; they are holy"
  - *Interpretation:* Numbers clarifies that animal firstlings cannot be redeemed—they belong permanently to God.

- **1 Corinthians 15:20, 23** — "But now is Christ risen from the dead, and become the firstfruits of them that slept... But every man in his own order: Christ the firstfruits; afterward they that are Christ's at his coming."
  - *Interpretation:* Christ fulfills the firstborn/firstfruits principle as the first to rise from the dead, guaranteeing resurrection for His people.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### RELEASE (HEBREW: SHMITTAH)
- **Meaning:** "Release," "remission," "dropping"
- **Economic impact:** Complete debt cancellation for Israelites
- **Social purpose:** Prevents permanent poverty and generational debt
- **Modern parallel:** Bankruptcy protection, debt forgiveness programs

### AUL
- **Tool:** Sharp instrument for piercing
- **Symbolic meaning:** Permanent commitment through physical marking
- **Location significance:** Door represents household entrance and authority
- **Voluntary nature:** Based on love, not coercion

### FIRSTLING
- **Definition:** Firstborn male of animals
- **Divine claim:** Belongs to God as His portion
- **Redemption exception:** Animals cannot be redeemed, unlike human firstborns
- **Prophetic fulfillment:** Christ as "firstfruits" of resurrection (1 Corinthians 15:20)

### EVIL EYE
- **Cultural concept:** Stingy, envious gaze
- **Biblical usage:** Opposite of "good eye" (generous) in Proverbs 22:9
- **Spiritual meaning:** Withholding due to selfishness or fear of loss
- **New Testament connection:** "If thine eye be evil, thy whole body shall be full of darkness" (Matthew 6:23)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 At the end of every seven years thou shalt make a release. 

2 And this is the manner of the release: Every creditor that lendeth ought unto his neighbour shall release it; he shall not exact it of his neighbour, or of his brother; because it is called the LORD's release. 

3 Of a foreigner thou mayest exact it again: but that which is thine with thy brother thine hand shall release; 

4 Save when there shall be no poor among you; for the LORD shall greatly bless thee in the land which the LORD thy God giveth thee for an inheritance to possess it: 

5 Only if thou carefully hearken unto the voice of the LORD thy God, to observe to do all these commandments which I command thee this day. 

6 For the LORD thy God blesseth thee, as he promised thee: and thou shalt lend unto many nations, but thou shalt not borrow; and thou shalt reign over many nations, but they shall not reign over thee. 

7 If there be among you a poor man of one of thy brethren within any of thy gates in thy land which the LORD thy God giveth thee, thou shalt not harden thine heart, nor shut thine hand from thy poor brother: 

8 But thou shalt open thine hand wide unto him, and shalt surely lend him sufficient for his need, in that which he wanteth. 

9 Beware that there be not a thought in thy wicked heart, saying, The seventh year, the year of release, is at hand; and thine eye be evil against thy poor brother, and thou givest him nought; and he cry unto the LORD against thee, and it be sin unto thee. 

10 Thou shalt surely give him, and thine heart shall not be grieved when thou givest unto him: because that for this thing the LORD thy God shall bless thee in all thy works, and in all that thou puttest thine hand unto. 

11 For the poor shall never cease out of the land: therefore I command thee, saying, Thou shalt open thine hand wide unto thy brother, to thy poor, and to thy needy, in thy land. 

12 And if thy brother, an Hebrew man, or an Hebrew woman, be sold unto thee, and serve thee six years; then in the seventh year thou shalt let him go free from thee. 

13 And when thou sendest him out free from thee, thou shalt not let him go away empty: 

14 Thou shalt furnish him liberally out of thy flock, and out of thy floor, and out of thy winepress: of that wherewith the LORD thy God hath blessed thee thou shalt give unto him. 

15 And thou shalt remember that thou wast a bondman in the land of Egypt, and the LORD thy God redeemed thee: therefore I command thee this thing to day. 

16 And it shall be, if he say unto thee, I will not go away from thee; because he loveth thee and thine house, because he is well with thee; 

17 Then thou shalt take an aul, and thrust it through his ear unto the door, and he shall be thy servant for ever. And also unto thy maidservant thou shalt do likewise. 

18 It shall not seem hard unto thee, when thou sendest him away free from thee; for he hath been worth a double hired servant to thee, in serving thee six years: and the LORD thy God shall bless thee in all that thou doest. 

19 All the firstling males that come of thy herd and of thy flock thou shalt sanctify unto the LORD thy God: thou shalt do no work with the firstling of thy bullock, nor shear the firstling of thy sheep. 

20 Thou shalt eat it before the LORD thy God year by year in the place which the LORD shall choose, thou and thy household. 

21 And if there be any blemish therein, as if it be lame, or blind, or have any ill blemish, thou shalt not sacrifice it unto the LORD thy God. 

22 Thou shalt eat it within thy gates: the unclean and the clean person shall eat it alike, as the roebuck, and as the hart. 

23 Only thou shalt not eat the blood thereof; thou shalt pour it upon the ground as water.

- **Creation Sabbath:** Seventh day rest (Genesis 2:2-3)
- **Land Sabbath:** Seventh year rest (Leviticus 25:1-7)
- **Jubilee:** Fifty-year cycle (Leviticus 25:8-55)
- **Millennial Sabbath:** Seventh thousand years (Hebrews 4:9, Revelation 20)

- **Egyptian slavery:** Original bondage (Exodus 1-14)
- **Christ's payment:** Cancellation of sin debt (Colossians 2:14)
- **Future restoration:** Release from all oppression (Romans 8:21)
- **Eternal freedom:** No more debt or curse (Revelation 21:4)

- **Passover firstborns:** Spared by blood (Exodus 12)

- **Church as firstborn:** "Church of the firstborn" (Hebrews 12:23)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "The poor shall never cease out of the land"
**Common misinterpretation:** This promotes fatalism about poverty and discourages efforts to eliminate it
**Biblical context:** This is a realistic statement within a command to be continuously generous. It's not an excuse for indifference but motivation for ongoing compassion. The very next verse commands open-handedness.

### "Thou shalt lend unto many nations, but thou shalt not borrow"
**Common misinterpretation:** This applies to all believers for all time
**Biblical context:** This is specifically conditional on Israel's obedience to the covenant. It's a national promise, not an individual financial principle. Israel's history shows they often borrowed when disobedient.

The Hebrew servant regulations balance freedom with provision, and the firstborn dedication acknowledges God's ownership of all. This system combines economic justice with spiritual devotion, showing that true worship includes how we handle resources and treat the vulnerable.
