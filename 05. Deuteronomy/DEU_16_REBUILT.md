## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Moses commands the three annual feasts: Passover (month of Abib, commemorating Egypt deliverance), Feast of Weeks (seven weeks after harvest begins), and Feast of Tabernacles (after gathering corn and wine). All sacrifices must be at the place God chooses. Passover includes eating unleavened bread seven days, remembering the hasty departure from Egypt, with no leaven seen in their territory and no sacrifice remaining overnight. Feast of Weeks involves freewill offerings and rejoicing before God with all household members, Levites, strangers, fatherless, and widows. Feast of Tabernacles is seven days of rejoicing after harvest. All males must appear before God three times yearly with gifts as God has blessed them. Moses commands appointing judges and officers in all gates to judge with just judgment, prohibiting perverting justice, showing partiality, or taking bribes (which blind the wise and pervert the righteous). They must follow what is altogether just to live and inherit the land. Finally, Moses forbids planting groves near God's altar or setting up images, which God hates.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Keep the passover unto the LORD thy God" — Passover Remembrance

**Deuteronomy 16:1-8** — "Observe the month of Abib, and keep the passover unto the LORD thy God: for in the month of Abib the LORD thy God brought thee forth out of Egypt by night"

- **Exodus 12:1-14** — "And the LORD spake unto Moses and Aaron in the land of Egypt, saying, This month shall be unto you the beginning of months: it shall be the first month of the year to you... And this day shall be unto you for a memorial; and ye shall keep it a feast to the LORD throughout your generations; ye shall keep it a feast by an ordinance for ever."
  - *Interpretation:* Moses repeats the Passover command originally given in Egypt. The purpose remains perpetual remembrance of redemption.

- **Leviticus 23:5-8** — "In the fourteenth day of the first month at even is the LORD's passover. And on the fifteenth day of the same month is the feast of unleavened bread unto the LORD: seven days ye must eat unleavened bread."
  - *Interpretation:* Leviticus provides the priestly calendar with the same dates and requirements. Moses is giving the civil application.

- **Luke 22:15-20** — "And he said unto them, With desire I have desired to eat this passover with you before I suffer... This cup is the new testament in my blood, which is shed for you."
  - *Interpretation:* Jesus transforms the Passover into the Lord's Supper, showing the continuity between Old Testament feasts and New Testament sacraments.

### "Feast of weeks" — Pentecost Celebration

**Deuteronomy 16:9-12** — "Seven weeks shalt thou number unto thee: begin to number the seven weeks from such time as thou beginnest to put the sickle to the corn... And thou shalt keep the feast of weeks unto the LORD thy God with a tribute of a freewill offering"

- **Leviticus 23:15-21** — "And ye shall count unto you from the morrow after the sabbath, from the day that ye brought the sheaf of the wave offering; seven sabbaths shall be complete: Even unto the morrow after the seventh sabbath shall ye number fifty days"
  - *Interpretation:* Leviticus calls it the "feast of weeks" and specifies the 50-day count. Deuteronomy focuses on the celebration and generosity aspects.

- **Acts 2:1-4** — "And when the day of Pentecost was fully come, they were all with one accord in one place... And they were all filled with the Holy Ghost"
  - *Interpretation:* The Holy Spirit comes on the Day of Pentecost (Feast of Weeks), fulfilling the feast's prophetic significance. The firstfruits of the harvest become the firstfruits of the Church.

- **Exodus 34:22** — "And thou shalt observe the feast of weeks, of the firstfruits of wheat harvest, and the feast of ingathering at the year's end."
  - *Interpretation:* Exodus connects this feast to wheat harvest firstfruits. It celebrates God's provision through agricultural bounty.

### "Feast of tabernacles" — Booth Dwelling

**Deuteronomy 16:13-17** — "Thou shalt observe the feast of tabernacles seven days, after that thou hast gathered in thy corn and thy wine: And thou shalt rejoice in thy feast"

- **Leviticus 23:33-43** — "Speak unto the children of Israel, saying, The fifteenth day of this seventh month shall be the feast of tabernacles for seven days unto the LORD... Ye shall dwell in booths seven days; all that are Israelites born shall dwell in booths"
  - *Interpretation:* Leviticus adds the requirement to live in temporary booths, commemorating wilderness wandering. Deuteronomy focuses on the harvest celebration aspect.

- **Nehemiah 8:14-18** — "And they found written in the law which the LORD had commanded by Moses, that the children of Israel should dwell in booths in the feast of the seventh month... And all the congregation of them that were come again out of the captivity made booths, and sat under the booths"
  - *Interpretation:* Ezra and Nehemiah restore this feast after the exile, showing its continued importance in Israel's worship.

- **Zechariah 14:16** — "And it shall come to pass, that every one that is left of all the nations which came against Jerusalem shall even go up from year to year to worship the King, the LORD of hosts, and to keep the feast of tabernacles."
  - *Interpretation:* Zechariah prophesies that all nations will celebrate this feast in the messianic kingdom, showing its universal significance.

### "Three times in a year shall all thy males appear" — Pilgrimage Requirement

**Deuteronomy 16:16-17** — "Three times in a year shall all thy males appear before the LORD thy God in the place which he shall choose; in the feast of unleavened bread, and in the feast of weeks, and in the feast of tabernacles: and they shall not appear before the LORD empty"

- **Exodus 23:14-17** — "Three times thou shalt keep a feast unto me in the year... Three times in the year all thy males shall appear before the Lord GOD."
  - *Interpretation:* This requirement began at Sinai. Moses repeats it for the generation entering the land.

- **2 Chronicles 8:13** — "Even after a certain rate every day, offering according to the commandment of Moses, on the sabbaths, and on the new moons, and on the solemn feasts, three times in the year, even in the feast of unleavened bread, and in the feast of weeks, and in the feast of tabernacles."
  - *Interpretation:* Solomon's temple follows this three-feast pattern exactly, showing this was actually practiced in Israel's history.

- **John 7:2-14** — "Now the Jews' feast of tabernacles was at hand... In the last day, that great day of the feast, Jesus stood and cried, saying, If any man thirst, let him come unto me, and drink."
  - *Interpretation:* Jesus attends the Feast of Tabernacles and uses its symbolism to offer Himself as living water, fulfilling the feast's prophetic meaning.

### "Judges and officers shalt thou make thee" — Justice System

**Deuteronomy 16:18-20** — "Judges and officers shalt thou make thee in all thy gates, which the LORD thy God giveth thee... Thou shalt not wrest judgment; thou shalt not respect persons, neither take a gift: for a gift doth blind the eyes of the wise, and pervert the words of the righteous"

- **Exodus 18:21-22** — "Moreover thou shalt provide out of all the people able men, such as fear God, men of truth, hating covetousness; and place such over them, to be rulers of thousands, and rulers of hundreds, rulers of fifties, and rulers of tens: And let them judge the people at all seasons"
  - *Interpretation:* Jethro advised Moses to establish this judicial system. Moses now codifies it for national implementation.

- **2 Chronicles 19:6-7** — "And said to the judges, Take heed what ye do: for ye judge not for man, but for the LORD, who is with you in the judgment. Wherefore now let the fear of the LORD be upon you; take heed and do it: for there is no iniquity with the LORD our God, nor respect of persons, nor taking of gifts."
  - *Interpretation:* Jehoshaphat's reforms echo these exact principles when reorganizing the judicial system.

- **James 2:1-4, 9** — "My brethren, have not the faith of our Lord Jesus Christ, the Lord of glory, with respect of persons... But if ye have respect to persons, ye commit sin, and are convinced of the law as transgressors."
  - *Interpretation:* James applies this principle to the Church, showing that partiality is still sinful in the New Testament era.

### "Gift doth blind the eyes of the wise" — Bribery Warning

**Deuteronomy 16:19** — "Neither take a gift: for a gift doth blind the eyes of the wise, and pervert the words of the righteous."

- **Proverbs 17:23** — "A wicked man taketh a gift out of the bosom to pervert the ways of judgment."
  - *Interpretation:* Wisdom literature confirms that bribes corrupt justice. This is a universal principle, not just ceremonial law.

- **Isaiah 1:23** — "Thy princes are rebellious, and companions of thieves: every one loveth gifts, and followeth after rewards: they judge not the fatherless, neither doth the cause of the widow come unto them."
  - *Interpretation:* Isaiah condemns Judah's leaders for taking bribes, showing this was a real problem in Israel's history.

- **1 Samuel 8:3** — "And his sons walked not in his ways, but turned aside after lucre, and took bribes, and perverted judgment."
  - *Interinterpretation:* Samuel's sons were corrupt judges, demonstrating the danger Moses warned about.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### ABIB
- **Meaning:** "Green ears" of grain
- **Timing:** First month of religious year (March-April)
- **Significance:** Marks beginning of harvest and redemption
- **New Testament name:** Nisan (Esther 3:7, Nehemiah 2:1)

### FEAST OF WEEKS
- **Hebrew name:** Shavuot
- **Greek name:** Pentecost ("fiftieth day")
- **Agricultural meaning:** Wheat harvest firstfruits
- **Christian significance:** Holy Spirit's coming (Acts 2)

### FEAST OF TABERNACLES
- **Hebrew name:** Sukkot ("booths")
- **Duration:** Seven days plus eighth day
- **Symbolism:** Wilderness wandering, divine protection
- **Prophetic fulfillment:** Messianic kingdom celebration (Zechariah 14)

### GIFT (BRIBE)
- **Hebrew concept:** "Shochad" - present that influences judgment
- **Judicial impact:** Corrupts justice regardless of wisdom
- **Moral principle:** Justice must be blind to wealth and status
- **Modern application:** Campaign contributions, judicial corruption

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 Observe the month of Abib, and keep the passover unto the LORD thy God: for in the month of Abib the LORD thy God brought thee forth out of Egypt by night. 

2 Thou shalt therefore sacrifice the passover unto the LORD thy God, of the flock and the herd, in the place which the LORD shall choose to place his name there. 

3 Thou shalt eat no leavened bread with it; seven days shalt thou eat unleavened bread therewith, even the bread of affliction; for thou camest forth out of the land of Egypt in haste: that thou mayest remember the day when thou camest forth out of the land of Egypt all the days of thy life. 

4 And there shall be no leavened bread seen with thee in all thy coast seven days; neither shall there any thing of the flesh, which thou sacrificedst the first day at even, remain all night until the morning. 

5 Thou mayest not sacrifice the passover within any of thy gates, which the LORD thy God giveth thee: 

6 But at the place which the LORD thy God shall choose to place his name in, there thou shalt sacrifice the passover at even, at the going down of the sun, at the season that thou camest forth out of Egypt. 

7 And thou shalt roast and eat it in the place which the LORD thy God shall choose: and thou shalt turn in the morning, and go unto thy tents. 

8 Six days thou shalt eat unleavened bread: and on the seventh day shall be a solemn assembly to the LORD thy God: thou shalt do no work therein. 

9 Seven weeks shalt thou number unto thee: begin to number the seven weeks from such time as thou beginnest to put the sickle to the corn. 

10 And thou shalt keep the feast of weeks unto the LORD thy God with a tribute of a freewill offering of thine hand, which thou shalt give unto the Lord thy God, according as the LORD thy God hath blessed thee: 

11 And thou shalt rejoice before the LORD thy God, thou, and thy son, and thy daughter, and thy manservant, and thy maidservant, and the Levite that is within thy gates, and the stranger, and the fatherless, and the widow, that are among you, in the place which the LORD thy God hath chosen to place his name there. 

12 And thou shalt remember that thou wast a bondman in Egypt: and thou shalt observe and do these statutes. 

13 Thou shalt observe the feast of tabernacles seven days, after that thou hast gathered in thy corn and thy wine: 

14 And thou shalt rejoice in thy feast, thou, and thy son, and thy daughter, and thy manservant, and thy maidservant, and the Levite, the stranger, and the fatherless, and the widow, that are within thy gates. 

15 Seven days shalt thou keep a solemn feast unto the LORD thy God in the place which the LORD shall choose: because the LORD thy God shall bless thee in all thine increase, and in all the works of thine hands, therefore thou shalt surely rejoice. 

16 Three times in a year shall all thy males appear before the LORD thy God in the place which he shall choose; in the feast of unleavened bread, and in the feast of weeks, and in the feast of tabernacles: and they shall not appear before the LORD empty: 

17 Every man shall give as he is able, according to the blessing of the LORD thy God which he hath given thee. 

18 Judges and officers shalt thou make thee in all thy gates, which the LORD thy God giveth thee, throughout thy tribes: and they shall judge the people with just judgment. 

19 Thou shalt not wrest judgment; thou shalt not respect persons, neither take a gift: for a gift doth blind the eyes of the wise, and pervert the words of the righteous. 

20 That which is altogether just shalt thou follow, that thou mayest live, and inherit the land which the LORD thy God giveth thee. 

21 Thou shalt not plant thee a grove of any trees near unto the altar of the LORD thy God, which thou shalt make thee. 

22 Neither shalt thou set thee up any image; which the LORD thy God hateth.

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### Feasts as Prophetic Calendar
- **Passover:** Christ's crucifixion (1 Corinthians 5:7)
- **Feast of Weeks:** Holy Spirit's coming (Acts 2)
- **Feast of Tabernacles:** Messianic kingdom (Zechariah 14:16)
- **Fulfillment pattern:** Christ fulfills all three feasts

### Centralized Worship
- **Tabernacle:** Mobile central sanctuary (Exodus 25-40)
- **Temple:** Permanent central place (1 Kings 6-8)
- **Christ:** True temple (John 2:19-21)
- **Church:** Spiritual temple (1 Corinthians 3:16)

### Justice as Divine Characteristic
- **Abraham's intercession:** Justice for righteous (Genesis 18)
- **David's kingship:** Justice for all (2 Samuel 8:15)
- **Christ's reign:** Perfect justice (Isaiah 9:7)
- **Final judgment:** Ultimate justice (Revelation 20)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Three times in a year shall all thy males appear"
**Common misinterpretation:** This applies to modern church attendance requirements
**Biblical context:** This specifically refers to the three mandatory pilgrimage feasts at the central sanctuary in ancient Israel. It's not about weekly worship but about national unity and covenant renewal at specific times and places.

### "Thou shalt not wrest judgment"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is only for formal judicial systems
**Biblical context:* This applies to all forms of judgment and decision-making. Every person in positions of authority must be impartial. The principle extends beyond courts to all leadership and personal interactions involving judgment.

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### Seven as Completeness
- **Seven days:** Passover/Tabernacles duration (Deuteronomy 16:3, 13)
- **Seven weeks:** Count to Pentecost (Deuteronomy 16:9)
- **Three times seven:** Annual pilgrimage cycle
- **Seventh day:** Sabbath rest (Exodus 20:10)

### Bread as Life Symbol
- **Unleavened bread:** Affliction and haste (Deuteronomy 16:3)
- **Bread of life:** Christ's self-description (John 6:35)
- **Daily bread:** Provision (Matthew 6:11)
- **Living bread:** Spiritual sustenance (John 6:51)

### Gates as Authority Places
- **City gates:** Judicial location (Deuteronomy 16:18)
- **Gates of hell:** Spiritual opposition (Matthew 16:18)
- **Gates of righteousness:** Worship entrance (Psalm 118:19)
- **Pearly gates:** New Jerusalem entrance (Revelation 21:21)

**Chapter 16 establishes Israel's worship calendar and judicial system:** The three pilgrimage feasts create rhythm for national life centered on God's chosen place of worship, while the judicial system ensures righteousness in community life. The combination shows that true worship involves both celebration of redemption and practice of justice. The prohibitions against idolatry (groves and images) protect the purity of both worship and justice.
