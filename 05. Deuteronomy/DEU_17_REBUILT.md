## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Moses commands that sacrifices must be without blemish, for defective offerings are abominations to God. Those who worship other gods (sun, moon, or heavenly host) must be executed after thorough investigation, requiring two or three witnesses—the witnesses must strike first, then all the people. For difficult judicial cases, people must go to the place God chooses and consult priests and Levites, who will render judgment based on the law. Anyone who presumptuously rejects their decision must die to remove evil from Israel and deter others. When Israel desires a king like other nations, God must choose him from among their brethren (not foreigners). The king must not multiply horses (which would require returning to Egypt), multiply wives (which would turn his heart), or greatly multiply silver and gold. He must write a personal copy of God's law, read it all his life, and follow it carefully so his heart isn't lifted above his brethren, ensuring prolonged reign for him and his children.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Wherein is blemish" — Perfect Sacrifices Required

**Deuteronomy 17:1** — "Thou shalt not sacrifice unto the LORD thy God any bullock, or sheep, wherein is blemish, or any evilfavouredness: for that is an abomination unto the LORD thy God."

- **Leviticus 22:20-25** — "Whatsoever hath a blemish, that shall ye not offer... Ye shall not offer unto the LORD that which is bruised, or crushed, or broken, or cut... neither shall ye offer any bread of them unto the LORD your God."
  - *Interpretation:* Leviticus provides detailed regulations about blemished sacrifices. Moses repeats this principle for the new generation.

- **Malachi 1:8, 13-14** — "And if ye offer the blind for sacrifice, is it not evil?... Ye said also, Behold, what a weariness is it! and ye have puffed at it, saith the LORD of hosts; and ye brought that which was torn, and the lame, and the sick; thus ye brought an offering: should I accept this of your hand? saith the LORD."
  - *Interpretation:* Malachi condemns Israel for offering defective sacrifices, showing this command was violated in later history.

- **Hebrews 9:14** — "How much more shall the blood of Christ, who through the eternal Spirit offered himself without spot to God, purge your conscience from dead works to serve the living God?"
  - *Interpretation:* Christ fulfills this principle as the perfect, blemish-free sacrifice. He had no moral or physical defects.

### "Two witnesses, or three witnesses" — Judicial Evidence

**Deuteronomy 17:6** — "At the mouth of two witnesses, or three witnesses, shall he that is worthy of death be put to death; but at the mouth of one witness he shall not be put to death."

- **Numbers 35:30** — "Whoso killeth any person, the murderer shall be put to death by the mouth of witnesses: but one witness shall not testify against any person to cause him to die."
  - *Interpretation:* Numbers establishes this principle for murder cases. Deuteronomy applies it to all capital cases, especially idolatry.

- **Matthew 18:16** — "But if he will not hear thee, then take with thee one or two more, that in the mouth of two or three witnesses every word may be established."
  - *Interpretation:* Jesus applies this principle to church discipline. The requirement for multiple witnesses continues in the New Testament.

- **Hebrews 10:28** — "He that despised Moses' law died without mercy under two or three witnesses"
  - *Interpretation:* Hebrews references this as a well-known principle of justice. The severity of punishment under the old covenant highlights the seriousness of rejecting Christ.

- **1 Timothy 5:19** — "Against an elder receive not an accusation, but before two or three witnesses."
  - *Interpretation:* Paul applies this principle to church leadership, showing its enduring validity for protecting against false accusations.

### "The priest that standeth to minister" — Priestly Authority

**Deuteronomy 17:9-12** — "Thou shalt come unto the priests the Levites, and unto the judge that shall be in those days, and enquire; and they shall shew thee the sentence of judgment... And the man that will do presumptuously, and will not hearken unto the priest... even that man shall die"

- **Deuteronomy 33:10** — "They shall teach Jacob thy judgments, and Israel thy law: they shall put incense before thee, and whole burnt sacrifice upon thine altar."
  - *Interpretation:* Moses blesses the Levites with teaching authority. This judicial role is part of their priestly function.

- **2 Chronicles 19:8-11** — "Moreover in Jerusalem did Jehoshaphat set of the Levites, and of the priests, and of the chief of the fathers of Israel, for the judgment of the LORD, and for controversies... And charged them, saying, Thus shall ye do in the fear of the LORD, faithfully, and with a perfect heart."
  - *Interpretation:* Jehoshaphat implements this system exactly as Moses commanded, with priests and Levites serving as judges.

- **Ezekiel 44:24** — "And in controversy they shall stand in judgment; and they shall judge it according to my judgments: and they shall keep my laws and my statutes in all mine assemblies; and they shall hallow my sabbaths."
  - *Interpretation:* Ezekiel's vision of the restored temple includes priestly judicial authority, showing this system continues in the messianic age.

### "Set a king over me" — Monarchy Regulations

**Deuteronomy 17:14-20** — "When thou art come unto the land... and shalt say, I will set a king over me, like as all the nations that are about me... Thou shalt in any wise set him king over thee, whom the LORD thy God shall choose"

- **1 Samuel 8:5-7** — "And said unto him, Behold, thou art old, and thy sons walk not in thy ways: now make us a king to judge us like all the nations... And the LORD said unto Samuel, Hearken unto the voice of the people in all that they say unto thee: for they have not rejected thee, but they have rejected me, that I should not reign over them."
  - *Interpretation:* Israel requests a king exactly as Moses anticipated. God grants it but notes it's a rejection of His direct kingship.

- **1 Samuel 16:13** — "Then Samuel took the horn of oil, and anointed him in the midst of his brethren: and the Spirit of the LORD came upon David from that day forward."
  - *Interpretation:* David is God's chosen king, fulfilling the requirement that God selects the king from among the brethren.

- **1 Kings 10:26-29** — "And Solomon gathered together chariots and horsemen: and he had a thousand and four hundred chariots, and twelve thousand horsemen, which he placed in the chariot cities, and with the king at Jerusalem... And a chariot came up and went out of Egypt for six hundred shekels of silver"
  - *Interpretation:* Solomon violates the prohibition against multiplying horses and trading with Egypt, showing the wisdom of Moses' restrictions.

- **1 Kings 11:1-4** — "But king Solomon loved many strange women... and his wives turned away his heart. For it came to pass, when Solomon was old, that his wives turned away his heart after other gods: and his heart was not perfect with the LORD his God"
  - *Interpretation:* Solomon violates the prohibition against multiplying wives, exactly as Moses warned would turn the king's heart away from God.

### "Write him a copy of this law" — King's Personal Scripture

**Deuteronomy 17:18-20** — "And it shall be, when he sitteth upon the throne of his kingdom, that he shall write him a copy of this law in a book... And it shall be with him, and he shall read therein all the days of his life"

- **Joshua 1:8** — "This book of the law shall not depart out of thy mouth; but thou shalt meditate therein day and night, that thou mayest observe to do according to all that is written therein"
  - *Interpretation:* Joshua receives the same command to constantly meditate on God's law. This principle applies to all leaders, not just kings.

- **Psalm 1:2-3** — "But his delight is in the law of the LORD; and in his law doth he meditate day and night. And he shall be like a tree planted by the rivers of water"
  - *Interpretation:* The psalmist describes the blessed person as one who constantly meditates on God's law, the same principle required of kings.

- **2 Timothy 3:16-17** — "All scripture is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness: That the man of God may be perfect, throughly furnished unto all good works."
  - *Interpretation:* Paul affirms that Scripture equips God's people for every good work, including leadership. The king's copy of law prefigures this principle.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### EVILFAVOUREDNESS
- **Meaning:** Physical defects, deformities, abnormalities
- **Sacrificial context:** Any imperfection disqualifies animals for sacrifice
- **Spiritual application:** Points to need for perfection in Christ (Hebrews 9:14)
- **Modern parallel:** Offering God our best, not leftovers

### HOST OF HEAVEN
- **Definition:** Sun, moon, stars, celestial bodies
- **Idolatrous worship:** Astrology, zodiac, celestial deification
- **Biblical prohibition:** Creation worship instead of Creator worship (Romans 1:25)
- **Modern forms:** Horoscopes, astrology, nature worship

### PRESUMPTUOUSLY
- **Hebrew meaning:** "Zadon" - insolence, arrogance, defiance
- **Context:** Willful rejection of established divine authority
- **Severity:** Warrants death penalty as treason against God
- **New Testament parallel:** "Sinning willfully" (Hebrews 10:26)

### COPY OF THIS LAW
- **Contents:** Likely Deuteronomy or entire Pentateuch
- **Purpose:** Personal study reference, not just public reading
- **Method:** Handwritten by the king himself
- **Significance:** Leader must internalize God's standards

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 Thou shalt not sacrifice unto the LORD thy God any bullock, or sheep, wherein is blemish, or any evilfavouredness: for that is an abomination unto the LORD thy God. 

2 If there be found among you, within any of thy gates which the LORD thy God giveth thee, man or woman, that hath wrought wickedness in the sight of the LORD thy God, in transgressing his covenant, 

3 And hath gone and served other gods, and worshipped them, either the sun, or moon, or any of the host of heaven, which I have not commanded; 

4 And it be told thee, and thou hast heard of it, and enquired diligently, and, behold, it be true, and the thing certain, that such abomination is wrought in Israel: 

5 Then shalt thou bring forth that man or that woman, which have committed that wicked thing, unto thy gates, even that man or that woman, and shalt stone them with stones, till they die. 

6 At the mouth of two witnesses, or three witnesses, shall he that is worthy of death be put to death; but at the mouth of one witness he shall not be put to death. 

7 The hands of the witnesses shall be first upon him to put him to death, and afterward the hands of all the people. So thou shalt put the evil away from among you. 

8 If there arise a matter too hard for thee in judgment, between blood and blood, between plea and plea, and between stroke and stroke, being matters of controversy within thy gates: then shalt thou arise, and get thee up into the place which the LORD thy God shall choose; 

9 And thou shalt come unto the priests the Levites, and unto the judge that shall be in those days, and enquire; and they shall shew thee the sentence of judgment: 

10 And thou shalt do according to the sentence, which they of that place which the LORD shall choose shall shew thee; and thou shalt observe to do according to all that they inform thee: 

11 According to the sentence of the law which they shall teach thee, and according to the judgment which they shall tell thee, thou shalt do: thou shalt not decline from the sentence which they shall shew thee, to the right hand, nor to the left. 

12 And the man that will do presumptuously, and will not hearken unto the priest that standeth to minister there before the LORD thy God, or unto the judge, even that man shall die: and thou shalt put away the evil from Israel. 

13 And all the people shall hear, and fear, and do no more presumptuously. 

14 When thou art come unto the land which the LORD thy God giveth thee, and shalt possess it, and shalt dwell therein, and shalt say, I will set a king over me, like as all the nations that are about me; 

15 Thou shalt in any wise set him king over thee, whom the LORD thy God shall choose: one from among thy brethren shalt thou set king over thee: thou mayest not set a stranger over thee, which is not thy brother. 

16 But he shall not multiply horses to himself, nor cause the people to return to Egypt, to the end that he should multiply horses: forasmuch as the LORD hath said unto you, Ye shall henceforth return no more that way. 

17 Neither shall he multiply wives to himself, that his heart turn not away: neither shall he greatly multiply to himself silver and gold. 

18 And it shall be, when he sitteth upon the throne of his kingdom, that he shall write him a copy of this law in a book out of that which is before the priests the Levites: 

19 And it shall be with him, and he shall read therein all the days of his life: that he may learn to fear the LORD his God, to keep all the words of this law and these statutes, to do them: 

20 That his heart be not lifted up above his brethren, and that he turn not aside from the commandment, to the right hand, or to the left: to the end that he may prolong his days in his kingdom, he, and his children, in the midst of Israel.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Like as all the nations that are about me"
**Common misinterpretation:** This approves wanting to be like other nations
**Biblical context:** This is presented as Israel's desire that God accommodates while regulating. The context shows God allows but restricts monarchy to prevent the abuses common in surrounding nations. The regulations are specifically designed to make Israel's kingship different from pagan kingships.

### "Not multiply wives to himself"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about polygamy generally
**Biblical context:** This specifically addresses kings, not common people. The concern is political alliance marriages that would turn the king's heart to foreign gods. The principle applies to leaders whose decisions affect the whole nation.
