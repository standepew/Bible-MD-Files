## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Moses outlines priestly support: Levites have no land inheritance because the LORD is their inheritance, eating fire offerings and firstfruits (shoulder, two cheeks, maw from sacrifices; firstfruits of corn, wine, oil, and sheep fleece). Levites who come to the chosen place may minister there and receive equal portions. Moses then forbids Canaanite abominations: child sacrifice, divination, observing times, enchantment, witchcraft, charming, consulting familiar spirits, wizardry, and necromancy—all practices that caused the Canaanites' expulsion. Israel must be perfect with the LORD, unlike the nations that followed diviners. Moses then prophesies a Prophet like Moses from among Israel's brethren, fulfilling their request at Horeb to not hear God's voice or see fire again. God will put His words in this Prophet's mouth, and people must hearken to Him. Anyone who rejects the Prophet's words will be held accountable. False prophets who speak presumptuously or in other gods' names must die. The test of a true prophet: if their prediction doesn't come to pass, they spoke presumptuously and should not be feared.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "The LORD is their inheritance" — Priestly Provision

**Deuteronomy 18:1-2** — "The priests the Levites, and all the tribe of Levi, shall have no part nor inheritance with Israel: they shall eat the offerings of the LORD made by fire, and his inheritance. Therefore shall they have no inheritance among their brethren: the LORD is their inheritance, as he hath said unto them."

- **Numbers 18:20-24** — "And the LORD spake unto Aaron, Thou shalt have no inheritance in their land, neither shalt thou have any part among them: I am thy part and thine inheritance among the children of Israel... But the tithes of the children of Israel, which they offer as an heave offering unto the LORD, I have given to the Levites to inherit"
  - *Interpretation:* Numbers provides the detailed explanation of this principle. Moses repeats it for the new generation entering the land.

- **Joshua 13:14** — "Only unto the tribe of Levi he gave none inheritance; the sacrifices of the LORD God of Israel made by fire are their inheritance, as he said unto them."
  - *Interpretation:* Joshua's allocation of the land fulfills this command exactly—Levites receive no territorial inheritance.

- **1 Peter 2:5** — "Ye also, as lively stones, are built up a spiritual house, an holy priesthood, to offer up spiritual sacrifices, acceptable to God by Jesus Christ."
  - *Interpretation:* Peter applies priesthood language to the Church. New Testament believers become a spiritual priesthood with God as their inheritance.

### "Shoulder, and the two cheeks, and the maw" — Priestly Portions

**Deuteronomy 18:3** — "And this shall be the priest's due from the people, from them that offer a sacrifice, whether it be ox or sheep; and they shall give unto the priest the shoulder, and the two cheeks, and the maw."

- **Leviticus 7:28-34** — "And the LORD spake unto Moses, saying, Speak unto the children of Israel, saying, He that offereth the sacrifice of his peace offerings for thanksgiving shall bring his oblation unto the LORD... And the wave breast and the heave shoulder shall the priest bring for a wave offering and heave offering before the LORD"
  - *Interpretation:* Leviticus provides detailed regulations for priestly portions. Moses summarizes the key portions here.

- **1 Samuel 9:23-24** — "And the cook took up the shoulder, and that which was upon it, and set it before Saul. And Samuel said, Behold that which is left! set it before thee, and eat: for unto this time hath it been kept for thee since I said, I have invited the people."
  - *Interpretation:* Samuel follows this custom when hosting Saul, showing the priestly portion was also reserved for honored guests.

- **John 19:36** — "For these things were done, that the scripture should be fulfilled, A bone of him shall not be broken."
  - *Interpretation:* Christ's sacrifice fulfills the sacrificial system. As the perfect sacrifice, He becomes the believer's portion and provision.

### "Pass through the fire" — Child Sacrifice Prohibition

**Deuteronomy 18:10** — "There shall not be found among you any one that maketh his son or his daughter to pass through the fire"

- **Leviticus 18:21** — "And thou shalt not let any of thy seed pass through the fire to Molech, neither shalt thou profane the name of thy God: I am the LORD."
  - *Interpretation:* Leviticus specifically names Molech worship. Moses repeats the prohibition as part of the broader condemnation of Canaanite practices.

- **2 Kings 23:10** — "And he defiled Topheth, which is in the valley of the children of Hinnom, that no man might make his son or his daughter to pass through the fire to Molech."
  - *Interpretation:* Josiah's reforms include destroying Topheth, the place of child sacrifice, showing this practice had corrupted Israel.

- **Jeremiah 7:31** — "And they have built the high places of Tophet, which is in the valley of the son of Hinnom, to burn their sons and their daughters in the fire; which I commanded not, neither came it into my mind."
  - *Interpretation:* Jeremiah condemns Judah for adopting this abomination, showing God's horror at this practice.

### "A Prophet from the midst of thee, like unto me" — Messianic Prophecy

**Deuteronomy 18:15, 18** — "The LORD thy God will raise up unto thee a Prophet from the midst of thee, of thy brethren, like unto me... I will raise them up a Prophet from among their brethren, like unto thee"

- **Acts 3:22-26** — "For Moses truly said unto the fathers, A prophet shall the Lord your God raise up unto you of your brethren, like unto me; him shall ye hear in all things whatsoever he shall say unto you... Yea, and all the prophets from Samuel and those that follow after, as many as have spoken, have likewise foretold of these days."
  - *Interpretation:* Peter explicitly applies this prophecy to Jesus Christ, identifying Him as the Prophet like Moses.

- **Acts 7:37** — "This is that Moses, which said unto the children of Israel, A prophet shall the Lord your God raise up unto you of your brethren, like unto me; him shall ye hear."
  - *Interpretation:* Stephen also applies this prophecy to Christ in his defense before the Sanhedrin.

- **John 5:46** — "For had ye believed Moses, ye would have believed me: for he wrote of me."
  - *Interpretation:* Jesus claims Moses wrote about Him, with this prophecy being a key reference point.

### "If the thing follow not, nor come to pass" — Testing Prophets

**Deuteronomy 18:21-22** — "When a prophet speaketh in the name of the LORD, if the thing follow not, nor come to pass, that is the thing which the LORD hath not spoken, but the prophet hath spoken it presumptuously"

- **Jeremiah 28:9** — "The prophet which prophesieth of peace, when the word of the prophet shall come to pass, then shall the prophet be known, that the LORD hath truly sent him."
  -Interpretation:* Jeremiah applies this test when confronting Hananiah's false prophecy.

- **Ezekiel 33:33** — "And when this cometh to pass, (lo, it will come,) then shall they know that a prophet hath been among them."
  - *Interpretation:* Ezekiel acknowledges that fulfilled prophecy validates the prophet's authenticity.

- **Matthew 7:15-20** — "Beware of false prophets, which come to you in sheep's clothing, but inwardly they are ravening wolves. Ye shall know them by their fruits."
  - *Interpretation:* Jesus provides a different test—character and results rather than just prediction accuracy, though the principle of testing remains.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### INHERITANCE
- **Priestly meaning:** No land portion, God Himself as provision
- **Tribal meaning:** Terrorial allotment, family possession
- **Spiritual application:** Believers inherit God Himself (Ephesians 1:11)
- **Eternal dimension:** Heavenly inheritance (1 Peter 1:4)

### DIVINATION
- **Definition:** Attempting to discover hidden knowledge through supernatural means
- **Methods:** Scrying, astrology, omens, lots (except Urim and Thummim)
- **Distinction:** Seeking knowledge apart from God's revelation
- **New Testament forms:** Fortune-telling, horoscopes, psychic readings

### OBSERVER OF TIMES
- **Hebrew:** "M'onen" - cloud observer, astrologer
- **Practice:** Observing celestial patterns for prediction
- **Prohibition:** Trusting creation rather than Creator
- **Modern equivalent:** Astrology, zodiac signs, horoscopes

### NECROMANCER
- **Definition:** Consulting the dead, spiritism
- **Practice:** Séances, mediumship, contacting deceased
- **Biblical view:** Forbidden communication with spirits
- **New Testament warning:** Witchcraft condemned (Galatians 5:20)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "The LORD is their inheritance"
**Common misinterpretation:** This means priests shouldn't work or receive any material support
**Biblical context:** This means they receive no land inheritance but are provided for through offerings and portions. God Himself provides for them through the people's worship. It's a different economic system, not poverty.

### "A Prophet like unto me"
**Common misinterpretation:** This refers to a succession of prophets
**Biblical context:** The singular "a Prophet" and New Testament application show this points specifically to Christ. While other prophets came, this prophecy finds its ultimate fulfillment in Jesus as the ultimate Prophet-Mediator.

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### Fire as Divine Presence
- **Mount Sinai fire:** God's presence (Exodus 19:18)
- **Offerings by fire:** Acceptable worship (Leviticus 1-7)
- **Pentecost fire:** Holy Spirit's coming (Acts 2)
- **Lake of fire:** Final judgment (Revelation 20)

### Mouth as Prophetic Instrument
- **God speaks:** Creation and law (Genesis 1, Exodus 20)
- **Prophetic mouth:** God's words in human mouth (Deuteronomy 18:18)
- **Christ's teaching:** Authority in speech (Matthew 7:29)
- **Great commission:** Speak the word (Mark 16:15)

### Fire as Testing Element
- **Refiner's fire:** Purification (Malachi 3:2)
- **Baptism of fire:** Holy Spirit power (Matthew 3:11)
- **Trial by fire:** Testing of faith (1 Peter 1:7)
- **Eternal fire:** Hell's punishment (Mark 9:43)

---

**Chapter 18 establishes the proper channels of divine communication:** Priests receive provision through the sacrificial system, forbidden practices are condemned as counterfeit spirituality, and the Prophet like Moses is promised as the ultimate divine messenger. The test of true prophecy protects against deception, while the prohibition against occult practices preserves the purity of Israel's relationship with God. This chapter shows that God provides legitimate means of guidance and provision while condemning all counterfeits.
