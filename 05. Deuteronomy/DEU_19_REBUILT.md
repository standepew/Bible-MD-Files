## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Moses commands establishing three cities of refuge in Canaan for accidental manslayers, with roads prepared and land divided into three parts for equal access. The case example: a man chopping wood with his neighbor when the axe head slips and kills his neighbor—he may flee to a refuge city to escape the blood avenger whose heart is hot. If God enlarges their territory through obedience, they must add three more cities, totaling six, to prevent innocent bloodshed. However, intentional murderers who hate their neighbor and lie in wait must be delivered to the blood avenger after elders investigate—show no pity to remove innocent blood guilt. Moses also prohibits moving ancient landmarks that define inheritance boundaries. He reiterates the two-or-three-witness requirement for establishing guilt. For false witnesses, both parties stand before priests and judges; if false testimony is proven, the false witness receives the punishment they intended for their neighbor—life for life, eye for eye, etc.—to deter future evil and make others fear.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Cities of refuge" — Protection for Accidental Killers

**Deuteronomy 19:2-10** — "Thou shalt separate three cities for thee in the midst of thy land... that every slayer may flee thither... Whoso killeth his neighbour ignorantly, whom he hated not in time past"

- **Numbers 35:9-34** — "And the LORD spake unto Moses, saying, Command the children of Israel, that they give unto the Levites cities for habitation; and suburbs for the cities round about... And among the cities shall ye give six cities for refuge... that the slayer that killeth any person unawares may flee thither"
  - *Interpretation:* Numbers provides the detailed regulations for cities of refuge, including the six total cities (three on each side of Jordan). Moses summarizes the key principles here.

- **Joshua 20:1-9** — "The LORD also spake unto Joshua, saying, Speak to the children of Israel, saying, Appoint out for you cities of refuge, which I spake unto you by the hand of Moses: That the slayer that killeth any person accidentally and unawares may flee thither"
  - *Interpretation:* Joshua actually implements this command, establishing the six cities of refuge exactly as Moses commanded.

- **Hebrews 6:18** — "That by two immutable things, in which it was impossible for God to lie, we might have a strong consolation, who have fled for refuge to lay hold upon the hope set before us"
  - *Interpretation:* Hebrews uses the city of refuge concept as a picture of Christ as our refuge from sin's consequences.

### "Avenger of the blood" — Family Justice System

**Deuteronomy 19:6, 12** — "Lest the avenger of the blood pursue the slayer, while his heart is hot... deliver him into the hand of the avenger of blood, that he may die"

- **Genesis 9:5-6** — "And surely your blood of your lives will I require; at the hand of every beast will I require it: and at the hand of man; at the hand of every man's brother will I require the life of man. Whoso sheddeth man's blood, by man shall his blood be shed"
  - *Interpretation:* The blood avenger system began after the Flood. God delegates capital punishment to human society, specifically to the victim's nearest kin.

- **Numbers 35:19** — "The revenger of blood himself shall slay the murderer: when he meeteth him, he shall slay him."
  - *Interpretation:* Numbers confirms this is the family's right and responsibility, not just custom but divine institution.

- **2 Samuel 14:6-7** — "For we must needs die, and are as water spilt on the ground, which cannot be gathered up again: neither doth God respect any person: yet doth he devise means, that his banished be not expelled from him."
  - *Interpretation:* The wise woman of Tekoa uses this principle to appeal to David for Absalom's return, showing the system allowed for mercy in appropriate cases.

### "Remove not thy neighbour's landmark" — Property Rights Protection

**Deuteronomy 19:14** — "Thou shalt not remove thy neighbour's landmark, which they of old time have set in thine inheritance"

- **Proverbs 22:28** — "Remove not the ancient landmark, which thy fathers have set."
- **Proverbs 23:10** — "Remove not the old landmark; and enter not into the fields of the fatherless"
  - *Interpretation:* Wisdom literature treats this as a fundamental principle of justice and property rights.

- **Job 24:2** — "Some remove the landmarks; they violently take away flocks, and feed thereof."
  - *Interpretation:* Job lists landmark removal among the wicked's oppressions, showing this was a real problem in ancient society.

- **Hosea 5:10** — "The princes of Judah were like them that remove the bound: therefore I will pour out my wrath upon them like water."
  - *Interpretation:* Hosea uses this as a metaphor for Judah's leaders violating God's boundaries, showing the principle's symbolic application.

### "Two witnesses, or three witnesses" — Evidentiary Standard

**Deuteronomy 19:15** — "One witness shall not rise up against a man for any iniquity, or for any sin... at the mouth of two witnesses, or at the mouth of three witnesses, shall the matter be established"

- **Deuteronomy 17:6** — "At the mouth of two witnesses, or three witnesses, shall he that is worthy of death be put to death; but at the mouth of one witness he shall not be put to death."
  - *Interpretation:* Moses repeats this principle from the previous chapter, showing its fundamental importance in Israel's justice system.

- **Matthew 18:16** — "But if he will not hear thee, then take with thee one or two more, that in the mouth of two or three witnesses every word may be established."
  - *Interpretation:* Jesus applies this principle to church discipline, showing its enduring validity beyond the civil judicial system.

- **1 Timothy 5:19** — "Against an elder receive not an accusation, but before two or three witnesses."
  - *Interpretation:* Paul applies this to protect church leaders from false accusations, maintaining the principle's protective function.

### "Life for life, eye for eye" — Retribution Justice

**Deuteronomy 19:21** — "And thine eye shall not pity; but life shall go for life, eye for eye, tooth for tooth, hand for hand, foot for foot"

- **Exodus 21:23-25** — "And if any mischief follow, then thou shalt give life for life, Eye for eye, tooth for tooth, hand for hand, foot for foot, Burning for burning, wound for wound, stripe for stripe"
  - *Interpretation:* Exodus establishes this principle for civil justice. Moses repeats it here specifically for false witness cases.

- **Leviticus 24:17-20** — "And he that killeth any man shall surely be put to death... And he that killeth a beast shall make it good; beast for beast. And if a man cause a blemish in his neighbour; as he hath done, so shall it be done to him; breach for breach, eye for eye, tooth for tooth"
  - *Interpretation:* Leviticus applies this principle to various offenses, showing its comprehensive application.

- **Matthew 5:38-39** — "Ye have heard that it hath been said, An eye for an eye, and a tooth for a tooth: But I say unto you, That ye resist not evil: but whosoever shall smite thee on thy right cheek, turn to him the other also."
  - *Interpretation:* Jesus doesn't abolish the principle but transforms it from civil justice to personal ethics. The state still maintains justice, but individuals practice mercy.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### CITIES OF REFUGE
- **Purpose:** Protection for accidental manslayers from blood avenger
- **Number:** Six total (three initially, three more if territory expands)
- **Location:** Equal access throughout the land
- **Duration:** Until high priest's death (Numbers 35:25)

### AVENGER OF THE BLOOD
- **Hebrew:** "Goel hadam" - redeemer of blood
- **Role:** Nearest kin responsible for executing justice
- **Authority:** Delegated by God for capital punishment
- **Limitation:** Cannot harm those in cities of refuge

### LANDMARK
- **Definition:** Boundary stones marking property lines
- **Significance:** Permanent inheritance boundaries
- **Violation:** Serious theft affecting family security
- **Spiritual application:** Respecting God-ordained boundaries

### FALSE WITNESS
- **Crime:** Deliberate false testimony in legal proceedings
- **Punishment:** Receives penalty intended for victim
- **Purpose:** Maintains judicial integrity
- **Modern equivalent:** Perjury, false accusation

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 When the LORD thy God hath cut off the nations, whose land the LORD thy God giveth thee, and thou succeedest them, and dwellest in their cities, and in their houses; 

2 Thou shalt separate three cities for thee in the midst of thy land, which the LORD thy God giveth thee to possess it. 

3 Thou shalt prepare thee a way, and divide the coasts of thy land, which the LORD thy God giveth thee to inherit, into three parts, that every slayer may flee thither. 

4 And this is the case of the slayer, which shall flee thither, that he may live: Whoso killeth his neighbour ignorantly, whom he hated not in time past; 

5 As when a man goeth into the wood with his neighbour to hew wood, and his hand fetcheth a stroke with the axe to cut down the tree, and the head slippeth from the helve, and lighteth upon his neighbour, that he die; he shall flee unto one of those cities, and live: 

6 Lest the avenger of the blood pursue the slayer, while his heart is hot, and overtake him, because the way is long, and slay him; whereas he was not worthy of death, inasmuch as he hated him not in time past. 

7 Wherefore I command thee, saying, Thou shalt separate three cities for thee. 

8 And if the LORD thy God enlarge thy coast, as he hath sworn unto thy fathers, and give thee all the land which he promised to give unto thy fathers; 

9 If thou shalt keep all these commandments to do them, which I command thee this day, to love the LORD thy God, and to walk ever in his ways; then shalt thou add three cities more for thee, beside these three: 

10 That innocent blood be not shed in thy land, which the LORD thy God giveth thee for an inheritance, and so blood be upon thee. 

11 But if any man hate his neighbour, and lie in wait for him, and rise up against him, and smite him mortally that he die, and fleeth into one of these cities; 

12 Then the elders of his city shall send and fetch him thence, and deliver him into the hand of the avenger of blood, that he may die. 

13 Thine eye shall not pity him, but thou shalt put away the guilt of innocent blood from Israel, that it may go well with thee. 

14 Thou shalt not remove thy neighbour's landmark, which they of old time have set in thine inheritance, which thou shalt inherit in the land that the LORD thy God giveth thee to possess it. 

15 One witness shall not rise up against a man for any iniquity, or for any sin, in any sin that he sinneth: at the mouth of two witnesses, or at the mouth of three witnesses, shall the matter be established. 

16 If a false witness rise up against any man to testify against him that which is wrong; 

17 Then both the men, between whom the controversy is, shall stand before the LORD, before the priests and the judges, which shall be in those days; 

18 And the judges shall make diligent inquisition: and, behold, if the witness be a false witness, and hath testified falsely against his brother; 

19 Then shall ye do unto him, as he had thought to have done unto his brother: so shalt thou put the evil away from among you. 

20 And those which remain shall hear, and fear, and shall henceforth commit no more any such evil among you. 

21 And thine eye shall not pity; but life shall go for life, eye for eye, tooth for tooth, hand for hand, foot for foot.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Eye for eye, tooth for tooth"
**Common misinterpretation:** This promotes personal revenge and retaliation
**Biblical context:** This is a principle for civil justice, not personal ethics. It limits punishment to match the crime, preventing excessive retaliation. Jesus transforms this to personal mercy while maintaining the principle for civil authorities.

### "Thine eye shall not pity"
**Common misinterpretation:** This promotes cruelty and lack of compassion
**Biblical context:** This specifically applies to judicial execution of justice for proven crimes, particularly false testimony. It's about maintaining judicial integrity, not general lack of compassion. Mercy is shown elsewhere (cities of refuge, debt release, etc.).
