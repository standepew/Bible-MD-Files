## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Moses gives miscellaneous laws covering various aspects of life: (1) Property responsibility—return lost animals or belongings to neighbors, or care for them until claimed; help fallen animals. (2) Moral prohibitions—no cross-dressing (abomination to God). (3) Environmental protection—when finding a bird's nest, release the mother bird but take the young, for blessing and long life. (4) Safety regulations—build parapets on roofs to prevent bloodguilt. (5) Agricultural prohibitions—no mixed seeds in vineyards, no plowing with ox and ass together, no mixed fabrics (wool and linen). (6) Clothing requirement—tassels on garments. (7) Marriage and sexual laws: If a man accuses his wife of not being a virgin, her parents present evidence of virginity to elders; if true, the man is fined and cannot divorce her. If false, the woman is stoned for prostitution. Adultery—both parties die. Betrothed virgin violated in city—both die (she didn't cry out). In field—only man dies (she cried but no one helped). Unbetrothed virgin violated—man pays bride price and marries her, cannot divorce. Incest prohibition—no taking father's wife or uncovering father's nakedness.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Stone them with stones that they die"
**Common misinterpretation:** This promotes harsh punishment for all sexual offenses
**Biblical context:** These punishments apply specifically within Israel's covenant community and include important distinctions based on consent and circumstances. The field scenario shows consideration for lack of consent, while the city scenario assumes consent through silence. These are civil laws, not universal moral commands.

### "Thou shalt not wear a garment of divers sorts"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about fashion rules generally
**Biblical context:** This specifically prohibits mixing wool and linen, fabrics from different sources (animal vs plant). The principle symbolizes spiritual purity and avoiding mixed allegiances, not general fashion regulations.
