## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Moses lists assembly restrictions: those with wounded genitals or cut off private parts, bastards (to tenth generation), and Ammonites/Moabites (forever) cannot enter the LORD's congregation. Ammonites and Moabites are excluded for not providing bread and water during Israel's wilderness journey and for hiring Balaam to curse Israel, though God turned the curse to blessing. Israel must never seek their peace or prosperity. Edomites and Egyptians are not to be abhorred (brother and former host respectively); their children may enter in third generation. Military camps must remain clean: those with nocturnal uncleanness must stay outside until evening, wash, and return. A designated area outside camp with a paddle on weapons must be used for sanitation, covering excrement because God walks in the camp. Fugitive slaves must not be returned to masters but may dwell where they choose without oppression. No Israelite prostitutes or sodomites. Money from prostitution or "price of a dog" cannot be brought as vows. No usury to brothers (but permitted with foreigners) for blessing. Vows to God must be promptly paid; not vowing is better than vowing and not paying. Gleaning rights: eat grapes and standing corn in neighbor's field to satisfaction but not harvest with tools or containers.

### "Shall not enter into the congregation of the LORD" — Assembly Restrictions

**Deuteronomy 23:1-8** — "He that is wounded in the stones, or hath his privy member cut off, shall not enter into the congregation of the LORD... A bastard shall not enter... An Ammonite or Moabite shall not enter"

- **Leviticus 21:17-23** — "Speak unto Aaron, saying, Whosoever he be of thy seed in their generations that hath any blemish, let him not approach to offer the bread of his God... No man that hath a blemish of the seed of Aaron the priest shall come nigh to offer the offerings of the LORD made by fire"
  - *Interpretation:* Leviticus establishes priestly restrictions for physical perfection. Moses extends similar principles to broader assembly participation.

- **Leviticus 22:24** — "Ye shall not offer unto the LORD that which is bruised, or crushed, or broken, or cut; neither shall ye make any offering thereof in your land."
  - *Interpretation:* The principle of offering only what is whole extends to human participation in worship.

- **Isaiah 56:3-5** — "Neither let the son of the stranger, that hath joined himself to the LORD, speak, saying, The LORD hath utterly separated me from his people... Even unto them will I give in mine house and within my walls a place and a name better than of sons and daughters"
  - *Interpretation:* Isaiah prophesies inclusion of eunuchs and foreigners, showing these restrictions have a future dimension of grace.

- **Acts 8:26-39** — "And the angel of the Lord spake unto Philip, saying, Arise, and go toward the south unto the way that goeth down from Jerusalem unto Gaza, which is desert... And Philip ran thither to him, and heard him read the prophet Esaias, and said, Understandest thou what thou readest?"
  - *Interpretation:* The Ethiopian eunuch's baptism shows the New Testament inclusion of those previously excluded.

### "Thou shalt not deliver unto his master the servant which is escaped" — Fugitive Protection

**Deuteronomy 23:15-16** — "Thou shalt not deliver unto his master the servant which is escaped from his master unto thee: He shall dwell with thee, even among you, in that place which he shall choose in one of thy gates"

- **Philemon 1:15-16** — "For perhaps he therefore departed for a season, that thou shouldest receive him for ever; Not now as a servant, but above a servant, a brother beloved, specially to me, but how much more unto thee, both in the flesh, and in the Lord?"

  - *Interpretation:* Paul's spiritual application shows the principle of freedom from bondage extends to spiritual liberty in Christ.

**Deuteronomy 23:19-20** — "Thou shalt not lend upon usury to thy brother; usury of money, usury of victuals, usury of any thing that is lent upon usury: Unto a stranger thou mayest lend upon usury; but unto thy brother thou shalt not lend upon usury"

- **Luke 6:34-35** — "And if ye lend to them of whom ye hope to receive, what thank have ye? for sinners also lend to sinners, to receive as much again. But love ye your enemies, and do good, and lend, hoping for nothing again"

### "When thou comest into thy neighbour's vineyard" — Gleaning Rights

**Deuteronomy 23:24-25** — "When thou comest into thy neighbour's vineyard, then thou mayest eat grapes thy fill at thine own pleasure: but thou shalt not put any in thy vessel.   - *Interpretation:* Leviticus establishes the principle of leaving portions for the poor. Moses provides the corresponding right of the poor to partake.

- **Ruth 2:2-3** — "And Ruth the Moabitess said unto Naomi, Let me now go to the field, and glean ears of corn after him in whose sight I shall find grace. And she went, and came, and gleaned in the field after the reapers"
  - *Interpretation:* Ruth's gleaning demonstrates this system in practice, showing how God provided for the poor through this principle.

- **Matthew 12:1-8** — "At that time Jesus went on the sabbath day through the corn; and his disciples were an hungred, and began to pluck the ears of corn, and to eat... But he said unto them, Have ye not read what David did, when he was an hungred, and they that were with him"
  - *Interpretation:* Jesus defends his disciples' grain plucking by appealing to David's example, showing the principle extends to meeting basic human needs.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### WOUNDED IN THE STONES
- **Meaning:** Crushed testicles, emasculation
- **Cultural context:** Physical perfection for worship participation
- **New Testament application:** Spiritual wholeness in Christ (Matthew 19:12)
- **Prophetic fulfillment:** Inclusion through grace (Isaiah 56:3-5)

### BASTARD
- **Hebrew:** "Mamzer" - child of forbidden union
- **Generational impact:** Exclusion to tenth generation
- **Social stigma:** Family dishonor and community exclusion
- **New Testament principle:** All can become children of God (John 1:12)

### USURY
- **Definition:** Interest charged on loans
- **Principle distinction:** Brothers (no interest) vs foreigners (permitted)
- **Economic justice:** Preventing exploitation of vulnerable
- **New Testament extension:** Lend without expectation (Luke 6:35)

### PRICE OF A DOG
- **Likely meaning:** Male prostitute's fee
- **Alternative:** Dog meat sold for sacrifice (unlikely)
- **Prohibition reason:** Money from sexual exploitation
- **Temple purity:** No profane money in sacred vows

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "A bastard shall not enter into the congregation"
**Common misinterpretation:** This applies to all illegitimate children permanently
**Biblical context:** This specifically refers to assembly participation in Israel's worship system, not eternal salvation. The limitation is "to his tenth generation," showing it's temporal, not eternal. Isaiah 56 prophesies inclusion of eunuchs and foreigners, showing these restrictions have a future dimension of grace.

### "Thou shalt not lend upon usury to thy brother"
**Common misinterpretation:** This prohibits all interest on loans universally
**Biblical context:** This specifically applies to fellow Israelites ("brother"), while permitting usury with foreigners. The principle is preventing exploitation of vulnerable community members, not prohibiting all financial interest. The New Testament extends this to lending without expectation of return (Luke 6:35).
