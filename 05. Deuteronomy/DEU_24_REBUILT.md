## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Moses gives various laws: (1) Divorce—if a man finds "uncleanness" in his wife, he writes a bill of divorcement and sends her away; she may remarry, but her former husband cannot take her back even if the second husband dies, for this is abomination and causes the land to sin. (2) Newlywed exemption—men with new wives are exempt from military service and business for one year to cheer up their wives. (3) Essential items protection—no taking millstones as pledges since they represent livelihood. (4) Kidnapping—those who steal and sell Israelites must die. (5) Leprosy—follow priestly instructions, remembering Miriam's punishment. (6) Pledge regulations—lenders cannot enter homes to collect pledges; pledges must be returned before sunset so the poor can sleep in their own clothing, bringing righteousness before God. (7) Worker protection—pay hired servants daily before sunset since they depend on it, preventing cries to God. (8) Generational justice—each person dies for their own sin, not fathers for children or vice versa. (9) Vulnerable protection—don't pervert judgment for strangers, fatherless, or widows; don't take widow's clothing as pledge, remembering Egypt slavery and redemption. (10) Harvest gleaning—leave forgotten sheaves, olive tree remnants, and vineyard gleanings for strangers, fatherless, and widows, remembering Egyptian bondage.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Bill of divorcement" — Divorce Regulations

**Deuteronomy 24:1-4** — "When a man hath taken a wife, and married her, and it come to pass that she find no favour in his eyes, because he hath found some uncleanness in her: then let him write her a bill of divorcement... Her former husband... may not take her again to be his wife, after that she is defiled; for that is abomination before the LORD"

- **Leviticus 21:7, 14** — "They shall not take a wife that is a whore, or profane... A widow, or a divorced woman, or profane, or an harlot, these shall he not take: but he shall take a virgin of his own people to wife."
  - *Interpretation:* Leviticus restricts priests from marrying divorced women, showing divorce was known but regulated. Moses provides the civil procedure.

- **Jeremiah 3:8** — "And I saw, when for all the causes whereby backsliding Israel committed adultery I had put her away, and given her a bill of divorce; yet her treacherous sister Judah feared not, but went and played the harlot also."
  - *Interpretation:* God uses divorce as a picture of Israel's spiritual unfaithfulness, showing the cultural understanding of divorce as ending covenant relationship.

- **Matthew 19:7-9** — "They say unto him, Why did Moses then command to give a writing of divorcement, and to put her away? He saith unto them, Moses because of the hardness of your hearts suffered you to put away your wives: but from the beginning it was not so. And I say unto you, Whosoever shall put away his wife, except it be for fornication, and shall marry another, committeth adultery"
  - *Interpretation:* Jesus explains that divorce was a concession to human hardness, not God's original design. He restricts the grounds to sexual immorality.

- **Malachi 2:16** — "For the LORD, the God of Israel, saith that he hateth putting away"
  - *Interpretation:* Malachi reveals God's true attitude toward divorce—He hates it, even though He regulated it to protect people.

### "Free at home one year" — Newlywed Protection

**Deuteronomy 24:5** — "When a man hath taken a new wife, he shall not go out to war, neither shall he be charged with any business: but he shall be free at home one year, and shall cheer up his wife which he hath taken"

- **Deuteronomy 20:7** — "And what man is there that hath betrothed a wife, and hath not taken her? let him go and return unto his house, lest he die in the battle, and another man take her."
  - *Interpretation:* Moses provides a similar exemption for betrothed men in warfare regulations. This extends the principle to newly married men.

- **1 Corinthians 7:32-34** — "But I would have you without carefulness. He that is unmarried careth for the things that belong to the Lord, how he may please the Lord: But he that is married careth for the things that are of the world, how he may please his wife."
  - *Interpretation:* Paul acknowledges the divided interests of married people, showing the wisdom of Moses' one-year exemption for establishing the marriage relationship.

- **Ephesians 5:25, 31-33** — "Husbands, love your wives, even as Christ also loved the church, and gave himself for it... For this cause shall a man leave his father and mother, and shall be joined unto his wife, and they two shall be one flesh... Nevertheless let every one of you in particular so love his wife even as himself"
  - *Interpretation:* Paul's teaching on marital love builds on the principle that marriage requires dedicated attention and care.

### "Taketh a man's life to pledge" — Essential Protection

**Deuteronomy 24:6** — "No man shall take the nether or the upper millstone to pledge: for he taketh a man's life to pledge"

- **Job 31:38-40** — "If my land cry against me, or all the furrows thereof weep; If I have eaten the fruits thereof without money, or have caused the owners thereof to lose their life: Let thistles grow instead of wheat, and cockle instead of barley."
  - *Interpretation:* Job shows awareness that taking someone's livelihood is equivalent to taking their life.

- **Matthew 24:41** — "Two women shall be grinding at the mill; the one shall be taken, and the other left."
  - *Interpretation:* Jesus references millstones as common household items, showing their essential role in daily life.

- **Revelation 18:22-23** — "And the voice of harpers, and musicians, and of pipers, and trumpeters, shall be heard no more at all in thee; and no craftsman, of whatsoever craft he be, shall be found any more in thee... For thy merchants were the great men of the earth; for by thy sorceries were all nations deceived."
  - *Interpretation:* Revelation's description of Babylon's fall includes the cessation of normal economic activities, showing the importance of essential trades.

### "That thief shall die" — Kidnapping Capital Crime

**Deuteronomy 24:7** — "If a man be found stealing any of his brethren of the children of Israel, and maketh merchandise of him, or selleth him; then that thief shall die"

- **Exodus 21:16** — "And he that stealeth a man, and selleth him, or if he be found in his hand, he shall surely be put to death."
  - *Interpretation:* Exodus establishes the same penalty for kidnapping. Moses repeats it for the new generation.

- **Genesis 37:28** — "And there passed by Midianites merchantmen; and they drew and lifted up Joseph out of the pit, and sold Joseph to the Ishmeelites for twenty pieces of silver: and they brought Joseph into Egypt."
  - *Interpretation:* Joseph's brothers technically committed this crime, though divine providence turned it to good. This shows the seriousness of their action.

- **1 Timothy 1:9-10** — "Knowing this, that the law is not made for a righteous man, but for the lawless and disobedient, for the ungodly and for sinners... for menstealers"
  - *Interpretation:* Paul lists "menstealers" (kidnappers) among those the law addresses, showing this crime remains serious in the New Testament era.

### "Remember what the LORD thy God did unto Miriam" — Leprosy Warning

**Deuteronomy 24:8-9** — "Take heed in the plague of leprosy... Remember what the LORD thy God did unto Miriam by the way, after that ye were come forth out of Egypt"

- **Numbers 12:10-15** — "And the cloud departed from off the tabernacle; and, behold, Miriam became leprous, white as snow: and Aaron looked upon Miriam, and, behold, she was leprous. And Aaron said unto Moses, Alas, my lord, I beseech thee, lay not the sin upon us... And Miriam was shut out of the camp seven days"
  - *Interpretation:* Numbers provides the historical reference for Moses' warning. Miriam's leprosy resulted from challenging Moses' authority.

- **Leviticus 13-14** — Detailed regulations for diagnosing and cleansing leprosy, showing the serious nature of this disease and the need for priestly supervision.

- **2 Kings 15:5** — "And the LORD smote the king, so that he was a leper unto the day of his death, and dwelt in a several house."
  - *Interpretation:* King Azariah's leprosy shows this remained a understood divine punishment throughout Israel's history.

### "Deliver him the pledge again when the sun goeth down" — Creditor Protection

**Deuteronomy 24:12-15** — "When thou dost lend thy brother any thing, thou shalt not go into his house to fetch his pledge... In any case thou shalt deliver him the pledge again when the sun goeth down, that he may sleep in his own raiment, and bless thee: and it shall be righteousness unto thee before the LORD thy God"

- **Exodus 22:26-27** — "If thou at all take thy neighbour's raiment to pledge, thou shalt deliver it unto him by that the sun goeth down: For that is his covering only, it is his raiment for his skin: wherein shall he sleep? and it shall come to pass, when he crieth unto me, that I will hear; for I am gracious."
  - *Interpretation:* Exodus gives the same command with God's promise to hear the poor's cries. Moses adds the blessing aspect for the lender.

- **Nehemiah 5:1-13** — "Then there arose a great cry of the people and of their wives against their brethren the Jews... And I was very angry when I heard their cry and these words... Restore, I pray you, to them, even this day, their lands, their vineyards, their oliveyards, and their houses"

- **James 2:15-16** — "If a brother or sister be naked, and destitute of daily food, And one of you say unto them, Depart in peace, be ye warmed and filled; notwithstanding ye give them not those things which are needful to the body; what doth it profit?"
  - *Interpretation:* James applies this principle of practical compassion to the Church, showing its enduring moral value.

### "Every man shall be put to death for his own sin" — Individual Responsibility

**Deuteronomy 24:16** — "The fathers shall not be put to death for the children, neither shall the children be put to death for the fathers: every man shall be put to death for his own sin"

- **Ezekiel 18:4, 20** — "Behold, all souls are mine; as the soul of the father, so also the soul of the son is mine: the soul that sinneth, it shall die... The son shall not bear the iniquity of the father, neither shall the father bear the iniquity of the son: the righteousness of the righteous shall be upon him, and the wickedness of the wicked upon him"
  - *Interpretation:* Ezekiel strongly emphasizes this principle of individual responsibility, using it to correct Israel's misunderstanding of generational punishment.

- **Jeremiah 31:29-30** — "In those days they shall say no more, The fathers have eaten a sour grape, and the children's teeth are set on edge. But every one shall die for his own iniquity"
  - *Interpretation:* Jeremiah prophesies the New Covenant where individual responsibility replaces the pattern of children suffering for parents' sins.

- **2 Kings 14:6** — "But the children of the murderers he slew not: according unto that which is written in the book of the law of Moses, wherein the LORD commanded, saying, The fathers shall not be put to death for the children, nor the children be put to death for the fathers"
  - *Interpretation:* King Amaziah applies this principle specifically, showing it was actually practiced in Israel's history.

### "It shall be for the stranger, for the fatherless, and for the widow" — Gleaning Rights

**Deuteronomy 24:19-22** — "When thou cuttest down thine harvest... thou shalt not go again to fetch it: it shall be for the stranger, for the fatherless, and for the widow... When thou beatest thine olive tree... it shall be for the stranger, for the fatherless, and for the widow... When thou gatherest the grapes of thy vineyard... it shall be for the stranger, for the fatherless, and for the widow"

- **Leviticus 19:9-10** — "And when ye reap the harvest of your land, thou shalt not wholly reap the corners of thy field, neither shalt thou gather every grape of thy vineyard: thou shalt leave them for the poor and stranger"
  - *Interpretation:* Leviticus establishes the principle of leaving portions for the poor. Moses expands it to include forgotten harvests and various crops.

- **Ruth 2:2-3, 15-16** — "And Ruth the Moabitess said unto Naomi, Let me now go to the field, and glean ears of corn after him in whose sight I shall find grace... And when she was risen up to glean, Boaz commanded his young men, saying, Let her glean even among the sheaves, and reproach her not"
  - *Interpretation:* Ruth's story demonstrates this system in practice, with Boaz going beyond the minimum requirement.

- **Luke 12:15** — "And he said unto them, Take heed, and beware of covetousness: for a man's life consisteth not in the abundance of the things which he possesseth."
  - *Interpretation:* Jesus teaches against hoarding, building on the principle that God provides for all through community sharing.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Every man shall be put to death for his own sin"
**Common misinterpretation:** This contradicts the idea that Adam's sin affects all humanity
**Biblical context:** This refers to civil justice, not spiritual consequences. While civil courts punish individuals for their own crimes, Adam's sin brought spiritual death and corruption to all humanity (Romans 5:12). The two realms operate on different principles.

### "It shall be for the stranger, for the fatherless, and for the widow"
**Common misinterpretation:** This supports socialism or forced redistribution
**Biblical context:* This specifically refers to leaving forgotten harvest remnants, not systematic redistribution. It's about generosity and leaving portions for the vulnerable to gather, not government-mandated wealth redistribution.
