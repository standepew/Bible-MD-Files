## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Moses gives instructions for offerings in the promised land: (1) Firstfruits—when Israel enters Canaan, they must take firstfruits in a basket to the place God chooses, declare to the priest they've come to the land God swore to their fathers. The priest sets the basket before the altar as Israel recites their history: "A Syrian ready to perish was my father" (Jacob), who went to Egypt, became a great nation, suffered Egyptian bondage, cried to God, who delivered them with mighty hand, outstretched arm, signs and wonders, and brought them to this land of milk and honey. They present the firstfruits, worship God, and rejoice with Levites and strangers. (2) Third-year tithe—after giving tithes to Levites, strangers, fatherless, and widows, they declare before God they've obeyed all commands, not eating sacred portions in mourning or for unclean use. They ask God's blessing from heaven. (3) Covenant declaration—Moses declares that Israel has avouched God as their God to walk in His ways, and God has avouched Israel as His peculiar people, holy and high above all nations, to keep His commandments and bring Him honor.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "A Syrian ready to perish was my father" — Historical Confession

**Deuteronomy 26:5** — "A Syrian ready to perish was my father, and he went down into Egypt, and sojourned there with a few, and became there a nation, great, mighty, and populous"

- **Genesis 28:10-15** — "And Jacob went out from Beersheba, and went toward Haran... And the LORD stood above it, and said, I am the LORD God of Abraham thy father, and the God of Isaac: the land whereon thou liest, to thee will I give it, and to thy seed"
  - *Interpretation:* Jacob's experience at Bethel shows his "Syrian" status as a wanderer. God's promise to Abraham and Isaac continues through Jacob.

- **Genesis 46:1-7** — "And Israel took his journey with all that he had, and came to Beersheba, and offered sacrifices unto the God of his father Isaac... And his sons and his sons' sons with him, his daughters, and his sons' daughters, and all his seed brought he with him into Egypt."
  - *Interpretation:* Jacob's actual journey to Egypt with his family fulfills this historical reference, showing the transition from a small family to a nation.

- **Acts 7:14-15** — "Then Joseph sent and called his father Jacob to him, and all his kindred, threescore and fifteen souls. So Jacob went down into Egypt, and died, he, and our fathers"
  - *Interpretation:* Stephen's sermon confirms this historical progression from Jacob's small family to their growth in Egypt.

### "The Egyptians evil entreated us, and afflicted us" — Egyptian Bondage

**Deuteronomy 26:6-9** — "And the Egyptians evil entreated us, and afflicted us, and laid upon us hard bondage: And when we cried unto the LORD God of our fathers, the LORD heard our voice, and looked on our affliction... - **Exodus 1:13-14** — "And the Egyptians made the children of Israel to serve with rigour: And they made their lives bitter with hard bondage, in morter, and in brick, and in all manner of service in the field"
  - *Interpretation:* Exodus provides the detailed account of the hard bondage Moses summarizes in this confession.

- **Exodus 2:23-25** — "And it came to pass in process of time, that the king of Egypt died: and the children of Israel sighed by reason of the bondage, and they cried, and their cry came up unto God by reason of the bondage. And God heard their groaning, and God remembered his covenant with Abraham, with Isaac, and with Jacob."
  - *Interpretation:* Exodus shows God hearing Israel's cries and remembering His covenant, exactly as Moses recounts.

- **Exodus 15:6** — "Thy right hand, O LORD, is become glorious in power: thy right hand, O LORD, hath dashed in pieces the enemy."
  - *Interpretation:* Miriam's song celebrates God's mighty hand and outstretched arm in delivering Israel from Egypt.

### "Land that floweth with milk and honey" — Promised Land Description

**Deuteronomy 26:9, 15** — "And he hath brought us into this place, and hath given us this land, even a land that floweth with milk and honey... And now, behold, I have brought the firstfruits of the land, which thou, O LORD, hast given me"

- **Exodus 3:8** — "And I am come down to deliver them out of the hand of the Egyptians, and to bring them up out of that land unto a good land and a large, unto a land flowing with milk and honey"
  - *Interpretation:* God first described Canaan this way when calling Moses at the burning bush. Moses repeats this throughout Deuteronomy.

- **Numbers 13:27** — "And they told him, and said, We came unto the land whither thou sentest us, and surely it floweth with milk and honey; and this is the fruit of it."
  - *Interpretation:* The spies confirm the land's abundance, though most focus on the difficulties rather than God's promise.

- **Joshua 5:6** — "For the children of Israel walked forty years in the wilderness, till all the people that were men of war, which came out of Egypt, were consumed, because they obeyed not the voice of the LORD"
  - *Interpretation:* Joshua shows that the generation that doubted this promise died in the wilderness, while the new generation would possess it.

### "Thou hast avouched the LORD this day to be thy God" — Mutual Covenant

**Deuteronomy 26:17-19** — "Thou hast avouched the LORD this day to be thy God, and to walk in his ways... And the LORD hath avouched thee this day to be his peculiar people, as he hath promised thee, and that thou shouldest keep all his commandments"

  - *Interpretation:* God's original proposal at Sinai becomes reality through this mutual avouching. Israel accepts God as their God, and God accepts them as His people.

- **Joshua 24:24** — "And the people said unto Joshua, The LORD our God will we serve, and his voice will we obey."
  - *Interpretation:* Joshua's renewal of the covenant at Shechem follows this same pattern of mutual commitment.

- **1 Peter 2:9-10** — "But ye are a chosen generation, a royal priesthood, an holy nation, a peculiar people; that ye should shew forth the praises of him who hath called you out of darkness into his marvellous light: Which in time past were not a people, but are now the people of God: which had not obtained mercy, but now have obtained mercy."
  - *Interpretation:* Peter applies this "peculiar people" status to the Church, showing the covenant relationship continues through Christ.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### SYRIAN READY TO PERISH
- **Historical reference:** Jacob's status as wandering Aramean
- **Cultural context:** "Syrian" refers to Aramean region, not modern Syria
- **Theological significance:** From nothing to great nation through God's promise
- **New Testament application:** Spiritual transformation through Christ (2 Corinthians 5:17)

### MIGHTY HAND AND OUTSTRETCHED ARM
- **Divine power imagery:** God's strength in deliverance
- **Historical fulfillment:** Ten plagues and Red Sea crossing
- **Prophetic usage:** Continued divine intervention (Isaiah 51:9)
- **Christological fulfillment:** Christ's power over death (1 Corinthians 15)

### HOLY HABITATION
- **Meaning:** God's dwelling place, originally the temple
- **Heavenly perspective:** God looks down from heaven to bless
- **New Testament expansion:** God dwells among His people (Revelation 21:3)
* **Spiritual application:** Believers as temples (1 Corinthians 3:16)

### AVOUCHED
- **Legal term:** Formal declaration or affirmation
- **Mutual commitment:** Both parties declare allegiance
- **Covenant language:** Binding agreement between God and Israel
- **New Testament parallel:** Mutual indwelling (John 17:21)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "A Syrian ready to perish was my father"
**Common misinterpretation:** This refers to Abraham or someone else
**Biblical context:** This specifically refers to Jacob, who became a "Syrian" by living in Paddan-Aram (modern Syria/Turkey region) with Laban. He was "ready to perish" when fleeing Esau with nothing, yet God made him the father of a great nation.

### "Land that floweth with milk and honey"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is just metaphorical language
**Biblical context:** While metaphorical, this describes Canaan's actual agricultural abundance—milk from livestock and honey from wild bees and date cultivation. The spies confirmed this literal abundance (Numbers 13:27).

**Chapter 26 establishes the pattern of worship through remembrance:** The firstfruits offering combines historical confession with present gratitude, acknowledging God's faithfulness from Jacob's wandering to Israel's settlement. The third-year tithe declaration confirms obedience to God's commands, especially regarding the vulnerable. The mutual avouching creates a covenant relationship where Israel commits to God's ways and God commits to them as His holy people. This chapter shows that true worship involves remembering God's past faithfulness, obeying His present commands, and trusting His future promises.
