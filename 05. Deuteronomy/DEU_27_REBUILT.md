## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Moses commands Israel to keep all God's commandments when they cross Jordan into Canaan. They must set up great stones on Mount Ebal, plaster them, and write all the law on them plainly after crossing Jordan. They must build an altar of whole stones (no iron tools) to the LORD on Mount Ebal, offering burnt and peace offerings, eating and rejoicing before God. Moses and the Levitical priests declare Israel has become God's people and must obey His voice. Moses assigns six tribes (Simeon, Levi, Judah, Issachar, Joseph, Benjamin) to stand on Mount Gerizim for blessings, and six tribes (Reuben, Gad, Asher, Zebulun, Dan, Naphtali) to stand on Mount Ebal for curses. The Levites pronounce twelve curses: for idolatry, dishonoring parents, moving landmarks, misleading the blind, perverting justice for vulnerable, sexual sins (father's wife, beasts, sisters, mother-in-law), secret murder, taking bribes to kill innocents, and not confirming all the law. After each curse, all the people respond "Amen."

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Set thee up great stones, and plaister them with plaister" — Memorial Stones

**Deuteronomy 27:2-8** — "And it shall be on the day when ye shall pass over Jordan... that thou shalt set thee up great stones, and plaister them with plaister: And thou shalt write upon them all the words of this law... in mount Ebal... And there shalt thou build an altar unto the LORD thy God, an altar of stones: thou shalt not lift up any iron tool upon them"

- **Joshua 8:30-35** — "Then Joshua built an altar unto the LORD God of Israel in mount Ebal... And he wrote there upon the stones a copy of the law of Moses, which he wrote in the presence of the children of Israel... And afterward he read all the words of the law, the blessings and cursings, according to all that is written in the book of the law"
  - *Interpretation:* Joshua fulfills this command exactly, building the altar on Mount Ebal and writing the law on stones, then reading blessings and curses.

- **Exodus 24:4** — "And Moses wrote all the words of the LORD, and rose up early in the morning, and builded an altar under the hill, and twelve pillars, according to the twelve tribes of Israel."
  - *Interpretation:* Moses previously built an altar and pillars at Sinai, establishing the pattern of stone memorials for covenant events.

- **1 Kings 18:31-32** — "And Elijah took twelve stones, according to the number of the tribes of the sons of Jacob, unto whom the word of the LORD came, saying, Israel shall be thy name: And with the stones he built an altar in the name of the LORD"
  - *Interpretation:* Elijah uses twelve stones representing Israel's tribes, showing the ongoing significance of stone memorials in Israel's worship.

### "Mount Gerizim to bless... Mount Ebal to curse" — Dual Mountains

**Deuteronomy 27:11-13** — "These shall stand upon mount Gerizim to bless the people... Simeon, and Levi, and Judah, and Issachar, and Joseph, and Benjamin: And these shall stand upon mount Ebal to curse; Reuben, Gad, and Asher, and Zebulun, Dan, and Naphtali"

- **Joshua 8:33** — "And all Israel, and their elders, and officers, and their judges, stood on this side the ark and on that side before the priests the Levites, which bare the ark of the covenant of the LORD, as well the stranger as he that was born among them; half of them over against mount Gerizim; and half of them over against mount Ebal"
  - *Interpretation:* Joshua fulfills this arrangement exactly, with half Israel on each mountain as the law is read.

- **Judges 9:7** — "And when they told it to Jotham, he went and stood in the top of mount Gerizim, and lifted up his voice, and cried, and said unto them, Hearken unto me, ye men of Shechem, that God may hearken unto you"
  - *Interpretation:* Jotham delivers his parable from Mount Gerizim, showing its continued significance as a place of proclamation.

- **John 4:20** — "Our fathers worshipped in this mountain; and ye say, that in Jerusalem is the place where men ought to worship."
  - *Interpretation:* The Samaritan woman references Mount Gerizim as their place of worship, showing these mountains had lasting religious significance.

### "Cursed be the man that maketh any graven or molten image" — Idolatry Prohibition

**Deuteronomy 27:15** — "Cursed be the man that maketh any graven or molten image, an abomination unto the LORD, the work of the hands of the craftsman, and putteth it in a secret place. And all the people shall answer and say, Amen"

- **Exodus 20:4-5** — "Thou shalt not make unto thee any graven image, or any likeness of any thing that is in heaven above, or that is in the earth beneath, or that is in the water under the earth: Thou shalt not bow down thyself to them, nor serve them"
  - *Interpretation:* This curse reinforces the second commandment, showing that violating it brings divine judgment.

- **Jeremiah 10:14-15** — "Every man is brutish in his knowledge: every founder is confounded by the graven image: for his molten image is falsehood, and there is no breath in them. They are vanity, and the work of errors: in the time of their visitation they shall perish."
  - *Interpretation:* Jeremiah confirms that graven images are falsehood and vanity, deserving of destruction.

- **Revelation 9:20** — "And the rest of the men which were not killed by these plagues yet repented not of the works of their hands, that they should not worship devils, and idols of gold, and silver, and brass, and stone, and of wood"
  - *Interpretation:* Revelation shows that idolatry persists until the end times, with people still worshipping material objects instead of God.

### "Cursed be he that confirmeth not all the words of this law" — Complete Obedience

**Deuteronomy 27:26** — "Cursed be he that confirmeth not all the words of this law to do them. And all the people shall say, Amen"

- **Galatians 3:10** — "For as many as are of the works of the law are under the curse: for it is written, Cursed is every one that continueth not in all things which are written in the book of the law to do them."
  - *Interpretation:* Paul quotes this verse to prove that no one can be justified by the law because complete obedience is required, and all fail somewhere.

- **James 2:10** — "For whosoever shall keep the whole law, and yet offend in one point, he is guilty of all."
  - *Interpretation:* James confirms this principle—the law requires complete obedience, and breaking any part makes one guilty of breaking it all.

- **Romans 3:19-20** — "Now we know that what things soever the law saith, it saith to them who are under the law: that every mouth may be stopped, and all the world may become guilty before God. Therefore by the deeds of the law there shall no flesh be justified in his sight"
  - *Interpretation:* Paul explains that the law's purpose is to show universal guilt, making justification impossible through law-keeping.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### PLAISTER WITH PLAISTER
- **Material:** Lime or gypsum-based covering
- **Purpose:** Create smooth surface for writing
- **Durability:** Makes inscription permanent and readable
- **Archaeological evidence:** Ancient inscribed stelae discovered

### MOUNT GERIZIM AND MOUNT EBAL
- **Location:** Near Shechem in central Canaan
- **Significance:** Blessing and curse locations
- **Geography:** Twin mountains forming natural amphitheater
- **Historical importance:** Site of covenant renewal (Joshua 8)

### WHOLE STONES
- **Requirement:** No cutting or shaping with metal tools
- **Symbolism:** Natural, unaltered creation for worship
- **Principle:** God's creation sufficient for worship
- **New Testament parallel:** Living stones (1 Peter 2:5)

### AMEN
- **Hebrew meaning:** "So be it," "truly," "certainly"
- **Function:** Corporate agreement and affirmation
- **Covenant significance:** Binding communal commitment
- **New Testament usage:** Christ's "verily, verily" (John 3:3)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Cursed be he that confirmeth not all the words of this law"
**Common misinterpretation:** This applies to specific laws people can choose to follow
**Biblical context:** This refers to complete obedience to the entire law. Paul uses this verse in Galatians 3:10 to prove that no one can be justified by law-keeping because complete obedience is required, and all fail somewhere. This shows the law's purpose is to reveal our need for grace, not provide a means of salvation.

### "Mount Gerizim to bless... Mount Ebal to curse"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about geographic spiritual power
**Biblical context:** These mountains serve as a natural amphitheater for covenant renewal. The location is practical for tribal gatherings and public proclamation, not inherently spiritual. The Samaritans later made Mount Gerizim their holy place, but Moses assigns both mountains equal importance in the ceremony.

**Chapter 27 establishes the covenant renewal ceremony:** Memorial stones with God's law provide permanent testimony, the altar of whole stones represents pure worship, and the dual mountains create a dramatic setting for blessing and curse. The twelve curses cover major covenant violations, each followed by communal "Amen," showing corporate responsibility. This ceremony would be fulfilled by Joshua, demonstrating that Moses' commands were not theoretical but practical instructions for Israel's national life in Canaan. The chapter emphasizes that covenant with God involves both blessing for obedience and curse for disobedience, with the entire community sharing in both.
