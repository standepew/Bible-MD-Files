## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Moses renews the covenant in Moab, distinct from the Horeb covenant. He reviews Israel's history: they saw God's works in Egypt, great signs and miracles, yet God hasn't given them perceiving hearts. For forty years in wilderness, their clothes and shoes didn't wear out, they didn't eat bread or drink wine, learning God is their provider. They defeated Sihon and Og, taking their land for Reuben, Gad, and half-Manasseh. Moses calls all Israel—captains, elders, officers, men, children, wives, strangers, woodcutters, water-drawers—to enter covenant with God that day, establishing them as God's people and He as their God, fulfilling promises to Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob. This covenant includes those present and future generations. Moses warns against "root bearing gall and wormwood"—those whose hearts turn to serve other gods, deceiving themselves with false peace while adding drunkenness to thirst. God's anger and jealousy will bring all written curses, blotting out their names, separating them to evil. Future generations and foreigners will see the land's desolation—brimstone, salt, burning, no sowing or growth, like Sodom, Gomorrah, Admah, and Zeboim. Nations will ask why God did this, and the answer will be Israel's covenant violation through serving unknown gods. Moses concludes that secret things belong to God, but revealed things belong to Israel and their children forever, to obey the law.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Beside the covenant which he made with them in Horeb" — Covenant Distinction

**Deuteronomy 29:1** — "These are the words of the covenant, which the LORD commanded Moses to make with the children of Israel in the land of Moab, beside the covenant which he made with them in Horeb"

- **Exodus 19-24** — The original Sinai covenant with detailed laws, sacrificial system, and the ten commandments
  - *Interpretation:* The Horeb (Sinai) covenant was the foundational covenant. This Moab covenant is a renewal specifically for those entering Canaan.

- **Jeremiah 31:31-34** — "Behold, the days come, saith the LORD, that I will make a new covenant with the house of Israel, and with the house of Judah... I will put my law in their inward parts, and write it in their hearts"
  - *Interpretation:* Jeremiah prophesies the New Covenant, building on the pattern of covenant renewals like this one in Moab.

- **Hebrews 8:6-13** — "But now hath he obtained a more excellent ministry, by how much also he is the mediator of a better covenant, which was established upon better promises... In that he saith, A new covenant, he hath made the first old"
  - *Interpretation:* Hebrews shows Christ as mediator of a better covenant, building on the covenant progression from Sinai to Moab to New Covenant.

---

### "Your clothes are not waxen old upon you" — Wilderness Provision

**Deuteronomy 29:5-6** — "And I have led you forty years in the wilderness: your clothes are not waxen old upon you, and thy shoe is not waxen old upon thy foot. Ye have not eaten bread, neither have ye drunk wine or strong drink: that ye might know that I am the LORD your God"

- **Nehemiah 9:20-21** — "Thou gavest also thy good spirit to instruct them, and withheldest not thy manna from their mouth, and gavest them water for their thirst. Yea, forty years didst thou sustain them in the wilderness, so that they lacked nothing; their clothes waxed not old, and their feet swelled not."
  - *Interpretation:* Nehemiah's prayer recounts these same wilderness miracles, confirming they were remembered throughout Israel's history.

- **Psalm 105:40-41** — "He spread a cloud for a covering; and fire to give light in the night. He asked, and he brought quails, and satisfied them with the bread of heaven."
  - *Interpretation:* The psalmist celebrates God's wilderness provision, including manna and quail, showing these miracles were part of Israel's worship heritage.

- **John 6:31-35** — "Our fathers did eat manna in the desert; as it is written, He gave them bread from heaven to eat... I am the bread of life: he that cometh to me shall never hunger"
  - *Interpretation:* Jesus uses the wilderness manna as a type of Himself as the true bread from heaven, showing the spiritual significance of these physical miracles.

### "A root that beareth gall and wormwood" - Spiritual Poison

**Deuteronomy 29:18-19** — "Lest there should be among you man, or woman, or family, or tribe, whose heart turneth away this day from the LORD our God... lest there should be among you a root that beareth gall and wormwood: And it come to pass, when he heareth the words of this curse, that he bless himself in his heart, saying, I shall have peace, though I walk in the imagination of mine heart"

- **Jeremiah 9:15** — "Therefore thus saith the LORD of hosts, the God of Israel; Behold, I will feed them, even this people, with wormwood, and give them water of gall to drink."
  - *Interpretation:* Jeremiah uses the same imagery to describe God's judgment on Judah, showing this metaphor became part of prophetic language.

- **Lamentations 3:15, 19** — "He hath filled me with bitterness, he hath made me drunken with wormwood... Remembering mine affliction and my misery, the wormwood and the gall."
  - *Interpretation:* Lamentations uses this imagery to describe Jerusalem's suffering, showing the bitter consequences of spiritual apostasy.

- **Revelation 8:11** — "And the name of the star is called Wormwood: and the third part of the waters became wormwood; and many men died of the waters, because they were made bitter."
  - *Interpretation:* Revelation uses Wormwood as a star that poisons waters, showing the ultimate fulfillment of spiritual poison leading to death.

### "Like the overthrow of Sodom, and Gomorrah" — Historical Judgment Pattern

**Deuteronomy 29:23-25** — "And that the whole land thereof is brimstone, and salt, and burning... like the overthrow of Sodom, and Gomorrah, Admah, and Zeboim, which the LORD overthrew in his anger, and in his wrath: Even all nations shall say, Wherefore hath the LORD done thus unto this land?"

- **Genesis 19:24-28** — "Then the LORD rained upon Sodom and upon Gomorrah brimstone and fire from the LORD out of heaven... And Abraham got up early in the morning to the place where he stood before the LORD: And he looked toward Sodom and Gomorrah... the smoke of the country went up as the smoke of a furnace."
  - *Interpretation:* Genesis provides the historical account of Sodom and Gomorrah's destruction, which Moses uses as the pattern for what will happen to disobedient Israel.

- **Isaiah 1:9** — "Except the LORD of hosts had left unto us a very small remnant, we should have been as Sodom, and we should have been like unto Gomorrah."
  - *Interpretation:* Isaiah warns that Judah was close to experiencing Sodom's judgment due to their sin, showing this remained a relevant warning.

- **Luke 17:28-30** — "Likewise also as it was in the days of Lot: they did eat, they drank, they bought, they sold, they planted, they builded; But the same day that Lot went out of Sodom it rained fire and brimstone from heaven, and destroyed them all... Even thus shall it be in the day when the Son of man is revealed."
  - *Interpretation:* Jesus uses Sodom's judgment as a pattern for end-times judgment, showing this historical event has prophetic significance.

### "The secret things belong unto the LORD" — Divine Sovereignty

**Deuteronomy 29:29** — "The secret things belong unto the LORD our God: but those things which are revealed belong unto us and to our children for ever, that we may do all the words of this law"

- **Psalm 25:14** — "The secret of the LORD is with them that fear him; and he will shew them his covenant."
  - *Interpretation:* The psalmist shows that while some things remain secret, God reveals His covenant to those who fear Him.

- **Daniel 2:22, 28** — "He revealeth the deep and secret things: he knoweth what is in the darkness, and the light dwelleth with him... But there is a God in heaven that revealeth secrets, and maketh known to the king Nebuchadnezzar what shall be in the latter days."
  - *Interpretation:* Daniel demonstrates that God reveals secrets to accomplish His purposes, showing the balance between divine mystery and revelation.

- **1 Corinthians 2:10-11** — "But God hath revealed them unto us by his Spirit: for the Spirit searcheth all things, yea, the deep things of God... For what man knoweth the things of a man, save the spirit of man which is in him? even so the things of God knoweth no man, but the Spirit of God."
  - *Interpretation:* Paul shows that through the Holy Spirit, believers can know spiritual truths that remain secret to natural man, building on this Deuteronomy principle.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### GALL AND WORMWOOD
- **Botanical meaning:** Bitter plants producing poisonous substances
- **Spiritual application:** Spiritual poison that corrupts from within
- **Prophetic usage:** Symbol of divine judgment and spiritual bitterness
- **New Testament fulfillment:** Revelation's star called Wormwood (Revelation 8:11)

### BRIMSTONE AND SALT
- **Geological meaning:** Sulphur deposits and salted earth
- **Agricultural result:** Complete inability to grow crops
- **Historical reference:** Sodom and Gomorrah's destruction pattern
- **Prophetic significance:** Total divine judgment rendering land uninhabitable

### SECRET THINGS
- **Divine mysteries:** Things known only to God
- **Human limitation:** Finite understanding of infinite God
- **Revelation principle:** God reveals what is necessary for obedience
- **Spiritual application:** Trusting God for what we cannot understand

### REVEALED THINGS
- **Divine disclosure:** What God makes known to humanity
- **Covenant requirements:** Commands necessary for relationship
- **Generational responsibility:** Teaching children what God has revealed
- **Eternal relevance:** Truth that belongs to us forever

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "The secret things belong unto the LORD"
**Common misinterpretation:** This means we shouldn't seek to understand God's mysteries
**Biblical context:** This acknowledges divine sovereignty while emphasizing responsibility for revealed truth. Paul shows that through the Spirit, believers can know "the deep things of God" (1 Corinthians 2:10). The principle is about trusting God with what we don't know while obeying what we do know.

### "I shall have peace, though I walk in the imagination of mine heart"
**Common misinterpretation:** This promotes positive thinking or self-deception
**Biblical context:** This describes false security among those who turn from God. Moses warns that self-blessing while walking in sinful imagination brings God's judgment, not peace. True peace comes from obedience, not positive thinking.

**Chapter 29 renews the covenant for the new generation:** Moses reviews God's faithfulness in Egypt and wilderness, then calls all Israel—including children, women, strangers, laborers—to covenant commitment. He warns against the "root of gall and wormwood" of secret idolatry and self-deception, which brings divine judgment and land desolation like Sodom. The chapter concludes with the important principle that secret things belong to God while revealed things belong to us for obedience. This covenant renewal emphasizes that relationship with God is both corporate and generational, with consequences extending beyond the individual to the community and future generations.
