## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Moses blesses Israel before his death, describing God's revelation at Sinai, Seir, and Paran, coming with ten thousands of saints and fiery law. God loved His people, who sat at His feet receiving His words. Moses gave the law as Jacob's inheritance, and God was king in Jeshurun when Israel gathered. Moses blesses each tribe: Reuben to live and not die with many men; Judah to be heard by God with sufficient hands and help against enemies; Levi with Urim and Thummim, who chose God over family, teaching Jacob and offering sacrifices; Benjamin dwelling safely between God's shoulders; Joseph with blessed land receiving precious things from heaven, sun, moon, mountains, hills, earth, and God's presence, having strength like unicorns; Zebulun and Issachar rejoicing in going out and tents, calling people to mountain sacrifices, enjoying sea abundance and sand treasures; Gad enlarged and dwelling as a lion, executing justice; Dan as a lion's whelp leaping from Bashan; Naphtali satisfied with favor and blessing, possessing west and south; Asher blessed with children, acceptable to brethren, dipping feet in oil with iron and brass shoes and strength matching days. Moses declares none is like God Jeshurun, who rides on heavens as help and excellency. Eternal God is Israel's refuge with everlasting arms, thrusting out enemies. Israel dwells safely alone with fountains of corn and wine and dewy heavens. Israel is happy, saved by God as shield and sword, with enemies becoming liars as Israel treads their high places.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "The LORD came from Sinai" — Divine Revelation

**Deuteronomy 33:2-5** — "The LORD came from Sinai, and rose up from Seir unto them; he shined forth from mount Paran, and he came with ten thousands of saints: from his right hand went a fiery law for them... And he was king in Jeshurun"

- **Exodus 19:16-19** — "And it came to pass on the third day in the morning, that there were thunders and lightnings, and a thick cloud upon the mount, and the voice of the trumpet exceeding loud... And mount Sinai was altogether on a smoke, because the LORD descended upon it in fire"
  - *Interpretation:* Exodus provides the historical account of God's dramatic appearance at Sinai, which Moses summarizes poetically.

- **Habakkuk 3:3-4** — "God came from Teman, and the Holy One from mount Paran. Selah. His glory covered the heavens, and the earth was full of his praise. And his brightness was as the light; he had horns coming out of his hand: and there was the hiding of his power."
  - *Interpretation:* Habakkuk uses similar imagery to describe God's glorious appearance, building on Moses' foundation.

- **Galatians 3:19** — "Wherefore then serveth the law? It was added because of transgressions, till the seed should come to whom the promise was made; and it was ordained by angels in the hand of a mediator."
  - *Interpretation:* Paul refers to the law being ordained by angels, connecting to Moses' "ten thousands of saints" accompanying God at Sinai.

### "Let thy Thummim and thy Urim be with thy holy one" — Priestly Blessing

**Deuteronomy 33:8-11** — "And of Levi he said, Let thy Thummim and thy Urim be with thy holy one... Who said unto his father and to his mother, I have not seen him; neither did he acknowledge his brethren, nor knew his own children: for they have observed thy word, and kept thy covenant"

- **Exodus 28:30** — "And thou shalt put in the breastplate of judgment the Urim and the Thummim; and they shall be upon Aaron's heart, when he goeth in before the LORD: and Aaron shall bear the judgment of the children of Israel upon his heart before the LORD continually."
  - *Interpretation:* Exodus establishes the Urim and Thummim as priestly instruments for divine guidance, which Moses references in Levi's blessing.

- **Numbers 20:2-13** — "And there was no water for the congregation: and they gathered themselves together against Moses and against Aaron... And the LORD spake unto Moses and Aaron, Because ye believed me not, to sanctify me in the eyes of the children of Israel, therefore ye shall not bring this congregation into the land which I have given them"
  - *Interpretation:* Numbers records the waters of Meribah incident where Levi (through Aaron and Moses) stood with God, confirming Moses' reference.

- **1 Samuel 28:6** — "And when Saul inquired of the LORD, the LORD answered him not, neither by dreams, nor by Urim, nor by prophets."
  - *Interpretation:* 1 Samuel shows the Urim was still used for divine guidance in Israel's later history.

### "The beloved of the LORD shall dwell in safety by him" — Benjamin's Blessing

**Deuteronomy 33:12** — "And of Benjamin he said, The beloved of the LORD shall dwell in safety by him; and the Lord shall cover him all the day long, and he shall dwell between his shoulders"

- **Genesis 35:16-18** — "And they journeyed from Bethel; and there was but a little way to come to Ephrath: and Rachel travailed, and she had hard labour... And it came to pass, as her soul was in departing, (for she died) that she called his name Benoni: but his father called him Benjamin."
  - *Interpretation:* Benjamin's birth was marked by Rachel's death, making him "son of my sorrow" (Benoni) but Jacob renamed him "son of my right hand" (Benjamin), showing special status.

- **Psalm 68:27** — "There is little Benjamin with their ruler, the princes of Judah and their council, the princes of Zebulun, and the princes of Naphtali."
  - *Interpretation:* The psalmist lists Benjamin among the tribes, showing their continued presence and leadership.

- **Esther 2:5** — "Now in Shushan the palace there was a certain Jew, whose name was Mordecai, the son of Jair, the son of Shimei, the son of Kish, a Benjamite"
  - *Interpretation:* Mordecai and Esther from Benjamin played crucial roles in Israel's preservation, showing God's protection over this tribe.

### "There is none like unto the God of Jeshurun" — God's Uniqueness

**Deuteronomy 33:26-29** — "There is none like unto the God of Jeshurun, who rideth upon the heaven in thy help, and in his excellency on the sky. The eternal God is thy refuge, and underneath are the everlasting arms... Israel then shall dwell in safety alone"

- **Exodus 15:11** — "Who is like unto thee, O LORD, among the gods? who is like thee, glorious in holiness, fearful in praises, doing wonders?"
  - *Interpretation:* Moses' song after the Red Sea uses similar language about God's uniqueness, showing this was a consistent theme in his theology.

- **Psalm 89:6-8** — "For who in the heaven can be compared unto the LORD? who among the sons of the mighty can be likened unto the LORD? God is greatly to be feared in the assembly of the saints, and to be had in reverence of all them that are about him."
  - *Interpretation:* The psalmist echoes this theme of God's incomparability, building on Moses' foundation.

- **Jeremiah 10:6-7** — "Forasmuch as there is none like unto thee, O LORD; thou art great, and thy name is great in might... Who would not fear thee, O King of nations? for to thee doth it appertain"
  - *Interpretation:* Jeremiah applies this principle to all nations, showing God's universal sovereignty.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### JESHURUN
- **Meaning:** "Upright one," poetic name for Israel
- **Usage:** Term of endearment for ideal Israel
- **Context:** Used when Israel is in right relationship with God
- **Significance:** Shows God's desire for Israel's uprightness

### THUMMIM AND URIM
- **Meaning:** "Perfections and Lights"
- **Function:** Priestly instruments for divine guidance
- **Location:** Breastplate of the High Priest
- **Significance:** Means of knowing God's will in difficult situations

### UNICORNS
- **Hebrew:** "Re'em" - likely wild ox or aurochs
- **Strength symbol:** Power and untamable nature
- **Context:** Joseph's military and political strength
- **Modern understanding:** Not mythical unicorn but powerful animal

### EVERLASTING ARMS
- **Meaning:** God's eternal support and protection
- **Security image:** Complete divine protection
- **Refuge concept:** God as ultimate safety
* **New Testament parallel:** Christ's eternal protection (John 10:28)

23 And of Naphtali he said, O Naphtali, satisfied with favour, and full with the blessing of the LORD: possess thou the west and the south.

26 There is none like unto the God of Jeshurun, who rideth upon the heaven in thy help, and in his excellency on the sky.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "His horns are like the horns of unicorns"
**Common misinterpretation:** This refers to mythical unicorns
**Biblical context:** The Hebrew "re'em" likely refers to a wild ox or aurochs, a powerful, extinct animal. The imagery emphasizes Joseph's strength and military power, not mythical creatures.

### "There is none like unto the God of Jeshurun"
**Common misinterpretation:** This promotes national pride or exclusivism
**Biblical context:** This is about God's unique character and incomparability, not Israel's superiority. Paul applies this principle to show that all gods are idols compared to the true God (1 Corinthians 8:4-6).

**Chapter 33 presents Moses' final blessing over Israel:** He begins with God's glorious revelation at Sinai, then blesses each tribe according to their character and future role. Reuben receives life, Judah leadership, Levi priestly service, Benjamin divine protection, Joseph abundant prosperity, Zebulun and Issachar joy and calling, Gad strength and justice, Dan boldness, Naphtali favor, and Asher blessing and strength. The chapter concludes with declaring God's uniqueness as eternal refuge with everlasting arms, ensuring Israel's security and happiness. This poetic blessing provides a positive vision of Israel's future, contrasting with the warnings of previous chapters, and emphasizes that God's unique character and protection ensure Israel's ultimate security and prosperity.
