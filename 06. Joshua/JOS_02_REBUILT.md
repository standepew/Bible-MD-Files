## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Joshua sends two spies from Shittim to secretly scout Jericho. They lodge at Rahab the harlot's house. The king of Jericho learns of the spies and orders Rahab to surrender them, but she hides them on her roof under flax stalks, deceiving the king's men that the spies have already left. After the pursuers search toward Jordan and return, Rahab reveals to the spies that she knows God has given Israel the land, and that terror has fallen on Canaan's inhabitants because they heard of God drying the Red Sea and destroying Sihon and Og. She declares that God is God in heaven and earth, and asks for kindness to her family in exchange for helping them. The spies agree to save her family if she keeps their mission secret, instructing her to bind a scarlet thread in her window as a sign. They promise safety for anyone in her house but death to those outside. Rahab sends them to hide in the mountains for three days, then lets them down by the scarlet cord. The spies return to Joshua, reporting that God has delivered the land into their hands since all inhabitants are terrified of Israel.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Rahab the harlot" — Faith of Gentile

**Joshua 2:1, 9-11** — "And Joshua the son of Nun sent out of Shittim two men to spy secretly, saying, Go view the land, even Jericho. And they went, and came into an harlot's house, named Rahab... And she said unto the men, I know that the LORD hath given you the land... for the LORD your God, he is God in heaven above, and in earth beneath"

- **Hebrews 11:31** — "By faith the harlot Rahab perished not with them that believed not, when she had received the spies with peace."
  - *Interpretation:* Hebrews lists Rahab among the heroes of faith, showing her profession of faith in Israel's God was genuine and saving.

- **James 2:25** — "Likewise also was not Rahab the harlot justified by works, when she had received the messengers, and had sent them out another way?"
  - *Interpretation:* James uses Rahab as an example of faith demonstrated by works, showing that her actions proved the reality of her faith.

- **Matthew 1:5** — "And Salmon begat Booz of Rachab; and Booz begat Obed of Ruth; and Obed begat Jesse"
  - *Interpretation:* Matthew includes Rahab in Christ's genealogy, showing that God incorporated this Gentile prostitute into the lineage of the Messiah.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### HARLOT
- **Cultural context:** Innkeeper or prostitute, possibly both
- **Social status:** Marginalized figure in society
- **Spiritual significance:** God chooses unlikely people for redemption
* **New Testament parallel:** Sinners saved by grace (Luke 7:37)

### SCARLET THREAD
- **Physical meaning:** Red cord for identification
- **Symbolic meaning:** Blood of Christ for redemption
- **Protective function:** Sign of divine protection
* **Typological significance:** Passover blood parallel (Exodus 12)

### TERROR
- **Divine terror:** Fear caused by God's presence
- **Psychological effect:** Hearts melting, courage failing
- **Military advantage:** Enemies defeated by fear
* **Spiritual application:** Reverence for God (Psalm 33:8)

### BLOOD UPON HEAD
- **Legal principle:** Responsibility for death
- **Judicial concept:** Bloodguilt and accountability
- **Protective boundary:** Inside/outside distinction
* **New Testament principle:** Spiritual responsibility (Acts 20:26)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 And Joshua the son of Nun sent out of Shittim two men to spy secretly, saying, Go view the land, even Jericho. And they went, and came into an harlot's house, named Rahab, and lodged there. 

2 And it was told the king of Jericho, saying, Behold, there came men in hither to night of the children of Israel to search out the country. 

3 And the king of Jericho sent unto Rahab, saying, Bring forth the men that are come to thee, which are entered into thine house: for they be come to search out all the country. 

4 And the woman took the two men, and hid them, and said thus, There came men unto me, but I wist not whence they were: 

5 And it came to pass about the time of shutting of the gate, when it was dark, that the men went out: whither the men went I wot not: pursue after them quickly; for ye shall overtake them. 

6 But she had brought them up to the roof of the house, and hid them with the stalks of flax, which she had laid in order upon the roof. 

7 And the men pursued after them the way to Jordan unto the fords: and as soon as they which pursued after them were gone out, they shut the gate. 

8 And before they were laid down, she came up unto them upon the roof; 

9 And she said unto the men, I know that the LORD hath given you the land, and that your terror is fallen upon us, and that all the inhabitants of the land faint because of you. 

10 For we have heard how the LORD dried up the water of the Red sea for you, when ye came out of Egypt; and what ye did unto the two kings of the Amorites, that were on the other side Jordan, Sihon and Og, whom ye utterly destroyed. 

11 And as soon as we had heard these things, our hearts did melt, neither did there remain any more courage in any man, because of you: for the LORD your God, he is God in heaven above, and in earth beneath. 

12 Now therefore, I pray you, swear unto me by the LORD, since I have shewed you kindness, that ye will also shew kindness unto my father's house, and give me a true token: 

13 And that ye will save alive my father, and my mother, and my brethren, and my sisters, and all that they have, and deliver our lives from death. 

14 And the men answered her, Our life for yours, if ye utter not this our business. And it shall be, when the LORD hath given us the land, that we will deal kindly and truly with thee. 

15 Then she let them down by a cord through the window: for her house was upon the town wall, and she dwelt upon the wall. 

16 And she said unto them, Get you to the mountain, lest the pursuers meet you; and hide yourselves there three days, until the pursuers be returned: and afterward may ye go your way. 

17 And the men said unto her, We will be blameless of this thine oath which thou hast made us swear. 

18 Behold, when we come into the land, thou shalt bind this line of scarlet thread in the window which thou didst let us down by: and thou shall bring thy father, and thy mother, and thy brethren, and all thy father's household, home unto thee. 

19 And it shall be, that whosoever shall go out of the doors of thy house into the street, his blood shall be upon his head, and we will be guiltless: and whosoever shall be with thee in the house, his blood shall be on our head, if any hand be upon him. 

20 And if thou utter this our business, then we will be quit of thine oath which thou hath made us swear. 

21 And she said, According unto your words, so be it. And she sent them away, and they departed: and she bound the scarlet line in the window. 

22 And they went, and came unto the mountain, and abode there three days, until the pursuers were returned: and the pursuers sought them throughout all the way, but found them not. 

23 So the two men returned, and descended from the mountain, and passed over, and came to Joshua the son of Nun, and told him all things that befell them: 

24 And And they said unto Joshua, Truly the LORD hath delivered into our hands all the land; for even all the inhabitants of the country do faint because of us.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Rahab the harlot"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is used to condone prostitution
**Biblical context:** Rahab is identified as a harlot to show God's grace reaches even the most marginalized. Her inclusion is not approval of her profession but demonstration that faith, not social status, determines salvation. She later married Salmon and became ancestor of Christ.

### "His blood shall be upon his head"
**Common misinterpretation:** This promotes personal vengeance
**Biblical context:** This is a legal principle about responsibility for consequences, not permission for revenge. It establishes that those who reject divine protection bear responsibility for their fate, while those who provide protection bear responsibility for those under their care.

**Chapter 2 demonstrates God's sovereignty and grace:** The spies' mission succeeds through the unexpected faith of Rahab, a Canaanite prostitute who recognizes Israel's God as "God in heaven above, and in earth beneath." Her declaration of faith, based on hearing of God's mighty works, shows that divine testimony creates faith even among Gentiles. The scarlet thread serves as both a practical identification sign and a prophetic symbol of Christ's blood for redemption. The chapter establishes the principle that divine protection comes through covenant relationship—those inside the house marked by the scarlet thread are saved, while those outside bear responsibility for their fate. Rahab's inclusion in Israel and Christ's genealogy demonstrates that God's salvation extends beyond ethnic boundaries to all who exercise faith.
