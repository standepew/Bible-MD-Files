## CHAPTER SUMMARY

After Israel crosses Jordan, God commands Joshua to select twelve men (one from each tribe) to take twelve stones from where priests stood in Jordan and carry them to their lodging place as a memorial. Joshua commands the men to take stones from Jordan's midst before the ark, explaining these stones will be a sign for future generations to ask about their meaning. The answer: Jordan's waters were cut off before the ark of the covenant, and these stones memorialize this forever. Israel obeys, taking twelve stones to their lodging place. Joshua also sets up twelve stones in Jordan where priests stood, which remain there. The priests stand in Jordan until all is finished as Moses commanded, then the ark and priests cross over. The eastern tribes (Reuben, Gad, half-Manasseh) pass over armed as Moses instructed, about 40,000 prepared for battle to Jericho plains. God magnifies Joshua before Israel, and they fear him as they feared Moses. Joshua commands priests to come up from Jordan; when they reach dry land, Jordan's waters return to their place, flowing over all banks as before. Israel comes up from Jordan on the tenth day of the first month and encamps at Gilgal east of Jericho. Joshua pitches the twelve stones from Jordan at Gilgal, instructing Israel that when children ask about the stones, they should explain that Israel crossed Jordan on dry land because God dried up the waters as He did the Red Sea, so all peoples might know God's mighty hand and fear Him forever.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Twelve stones... for a memorial" — Generational Teaching

**Joshua 4:3-7, 21-24** — "Take you hence out of the midst of Jordan... twelve stones... That this may be a sign among you, that when your children ask their fathers in time to come, saying, What mean ye by these stones?... Then ye shall let your children know, saying, Israel came over this Jordan on dry land"

- **Exodus 12:14** — "And this day shall be unto you for a memorial; and ye shall keep it a feast to the LORD throughout your generations; ye shall keep it a feast by an ordinance for ever."
  - *Interpretation:* The Passover memorial establishes the pattern of using physical objects to teach future generations about God's deliverance.

- **Exodus 13:14-16** — "And it shall be when thy son asketh thee in time to come, saying, What is this? that thou shalt say unto him, By strength of hand the LORD brought us out from Egypt... And it shall be for a token upon thine hand, and for frontlets between thine eyes: for by strength of hand the LORD brought us forth out of Egypt."
  - *Interpretation:* Moses establishes the principle of answering children's questions about God's works, which Joshua applies to the Jordan stones.

- **Psalm 78:4-7** — "We will not hide them from their children, shewing to the generation to come the praises of the LORD, and his strength, and his wonderful works that he hath done. For he established a testimony in Jacob, and appointed a law in Israel, which he commanded our fathers, that they should make them known to their children"
  - *Interpretation:* The psalmist emphasizes the importance of telling God's works to the next generation, exactly what Joshua establishes with the memorial stones.

### "The LORD magnified Joshua in the sight of all Israel" — Leadership Validation

**Joshua 4:14** — "On that day the LORD magnified Joshua in the sight of all Israel; and they feared him, as they feared Moses, all the days of his life"

- **Deuteronomy 34:9** — "And Joshua the son of Nun was full of the spirit of wisdom; for Moses had laid his hands upon him: and the children of Israel hearkened unto him, and did as the LORD commanded Moses."
  - *Interpretation:* Moses' laying on of hands and the spirit of wisdom prepared Israel to follow Joshua, which the Jordan miracle now confirms.

### "As the LORD your God did to the Red sea" — Parallel Miracles

**Joshua 4:23** — "For the LORD your God dried up the waters of Jordan from before you, until ye were passed over, as the LORD your God did to the Red sea, which he dried up from before us, until we were gone over"

- **Exodus 14:21-22** — "And Moses stretched out his hand over the sea; and the LORD caused the sea to go back by a strong east wind all that night, and made the sea dry land, and the waters were divided. And the children of Israel went into the midst of the sea upon the dry ground"
  - *Interpretation:* The Red Sea crossing provides the direct parallel that Joshua references, showing God's consistent pattern of delivering His people through water miracles.

- **Psalm 106:9-12** — "He rebuked the Red sea also, and it was dried up: so he led them through the depths, as through the wilderness. And he saved them from the hand of him that hated them, and redeemed them from the hand of the enemy. And the waters covered their enemies: there was not one of them left. Then believed they his words; they sang his praise."
  - *Interpretation:* The psalmist celebrates both water miracles as demonstrations of God's saving power.

- **1 Corinthians 10:1-2** — "Moreover, brethren, I would not that ye should be ignorant, how that all our fathers were under the cloud, and all passed through the sea; And were all baptized unto Moses in the cloud and in the sea"
  - *Interpretation:* Paul connects both water crossings to baptism, showing their spiritual significance as symbols of salvation.

**Joshua 4:24** — "That all the people of the earth might know the hand of the LORD, that it is mighty: that ye might fear the LORD your God for ever"

- **Exodus 9:16** — "And in very deed for this cause have I raised thee up, for to shew in thee my power; and that my name may be declared throughout all the earth."
  - *Interpretation:* God told Pharaoh that His power would be declared throughout the earth, which the Red Sea and Jordan crossings accomplish.

  - *Interpretation:* The psalmist prays that God's ways and salvation would be known among all nations, exactly what the Jordan crossing demonstrates.

  - *Interpretation:* Peter shows that Christ's resurrection validates His divine authority before all nations, continuing the pattern of God making His power known universally.

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 And it came to pass, when all the people were clean passed over Jordan, that the LORD spake unto Joshua, saying, 

2 Take you twelve men out of the people, out of every tribe a man, 

3 And command ye them, saying, Take you hence out of the midst of Jordan, out of the place where the priests' feet stood firm, twelve stones, and ye shall carry them over with you, and leave them in the lodging place, where ye shall lodge this night. 

4 Then Joshua called the twelve men, whom he had prepared of the children of Israel, out of every tribe a man: 

5 And Joshua said unto them, Pass over before the ark of the LORD your God into the midst of Jordan, and take ye up every man of you a stone upon his shoulder, according unto the number of the tribes of the children of Israel: 

6 That this may be a sign among you, that when your children ask their fathers in time to come, saying, What mean ye by these stones? 

7 Then ye shall answer them, That the waters of Jordan were cut off before the ark of the covenant of the LORD; when it passed over Jordan, the waters of Jordan were cut off: and these stones shall be for a memorial unto the children of Israel for ever. 

8 And the children of Israel did so as Joshua commanded, and took up twelve stones out of the midst of Jordan, as the LORD spake unto Joshua, according to the number of the tribes of the children of Israel, and carried them over with them unto the place where they lodged, and laid them down there. 

9 And Joshua set up twelve stones in the midst of Jordan, in the place where the feet of the priests which bare the ark of the covenant stood: and they are there unto this day. 

10 For the priests which bare the ark stood in the midst of Jordan, until every thing was finished that the LORD commanded Joshua to speak unto the people, according to all that Moses commanded Joshua: and the people hasted and passed over. 

11 And it came to pass, when all the people were clean passed over, that the ark of the LORD passed over, and the priests, in the presence of the people. 

12 And the children of Reuben, and the children of Gad, and half the tribe of Manasseh, passed over armed before the children of Israel, as Moses spake unto them: 

13 About forty thousand prepared for war passed over before the LORD unto battle, to the plains of Jericho. 

14 On that day the LORD magnified Joshua in the sight of all Israel; and they feared him, as they feared Moses, all the days of his life. 

15 And the LORD spake unto Joshua, saying, 

16 Command the priests that bear the ark of the testimony, that they come up out of Jordan. 

17 Joshua therefore commanded the priests, saying, Come ye up out of Jordan. 

18 And it came to pass, when the priests that bare the ark of the covenant of the LORD were come up out of the midst of Jordan, and the soles of the priests' feet were lifted up unto the dry land, that the waters of Jordan returned unto their place, and flowed over all his banks, as they did before. 

19 And the people came up out of Jordan on the tenth day of the first month, and encamped in Gilgal, in the east border of Jericho. 

20 And those twelve stones, which they took out of Jordan, did Joshua pitch in Gilgal. 

21 And he spake unto the children of Israel, saying, When your children shall ask their fathers in time to come, saying, What mean these stones? 

22 Then ye shall let your children know, saying, Israel came over this Jordan on dry land. 

23 For the LORD your God dried up the waters of Jordan from before you, until ye were passed over, as the LORD your God did to the Red sea, which he dried up from before us, until we were gone over: 

24 That all the people of the earth might know the hand of the LORD, that it is mighty: that ye might fear the LORD your God for ever.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "That all the people of the earth might know the hand of the LORD"
**Common misinterpretation:** This means God wants to show off His power
**Biblical context:** This is about God revealing Himself so people might know Him and respond in reverent fear. The purpose is relational knowledge leading to worship, not mere demonstration of power. The goal is that people might "fear the LORD your God forever."

### "The LORD magnified Joshua"
**Common misinterpretation:** This promotes human pride or self-glory
**Biblical context:* This is God's public validation of His chosen leader, not Joshua's self-promotion. The magnification serves to establish Joshua's authority so Israel will follow God's commands through him. It's about God's glory, not Joshua's.

The parallel between Jordan and Red Sea crossings demonstrates God's consistent pattern of deliverance. Joshua's magnification validates his leadership as God's chosen successor to Moses. The eastern tribes' armed participation shows their fulfillment of Moses' command. The restoration of Jordan's waters demonstrates that God's miracles are purposeful, not permanent alterations of nature. Gilgal becomes Israel's base camp, symbolizing the new beginning in the promised land. The chapter emphasizes that God's works serve both immediate needs (crossing Jordan) and eternal purposes (teaching future generations and revealing His power to all nations).
