## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Adoni-zedek king of Jerusalem, hearing Joshua took Ai and destroyed it, and that Gibeon made peace with Israel, fears greatly because Gibeon is a great royal city with mighty men. He sends to Hoham of Hebron, Piram of Jarmuth, Japhia of Lachish, and Debir of Eglon, asking them to help attack Gibeon for making peace with Israel. The five Amorite kings gather their armies and camp against Gibeon. The Gibeonites send to Joshua for help, saying all Amorite kings are gathered against them. Joshua ascends from Gilgal with all warriors. God tells Joshua not to fear, promising victory. Joshua marches all night and attacks suddenly. God discomfits the Amorites before Israel, slaughtering them at Gibeon and chasing them to Beth-horon, Azekah, and Makkedah. As they flee down Beth-horon, God casts hailstones from heaven that kill more than Israel's sword. Joshua speaks to God, commanding the sun to stand still over Gibeon and the moon over Ajalon. The sun stands still for about a whole day until Israel avenges themselves. There was never a day like this when God hearkened to a man's voice, for God fought for Israel. Joshua returns to Gilgal. The five kings hide in a cave at Makkedah. Joshua rolls stones over the cave mouth and sets guards, telling his men to pursue and not let enemies enter cities. After slaughtering the enemy, Israel returns peacefully to Makkedah. Joshua has the five kings brought out, commands Israel's captains to put their feet on the kings' necks, and encourages them with God's promise. Joshua kills and hangs the kings on trees until evening, then takes them down and casts them back into their cave with stones over the mouth. Joshua takes Makkedah, destroys it and its king, then conquers Libnah, Lachish, Eglon, Hebron, and Debir, destroying all inhabitants. Horam king of Gezer tries to help Lachish but is destroyed. Joshua conquers all southern Canaan from Kadesh-barnea to Gaza, leaving none alive, because God fought for Israel. Joshua returns to Gilgal.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Sun, stand thou still upon Gibeon; and thou, Moon, in the valley of Ajalon" — The Long Day

**Joshua 10:12-14** — "Then spake Joshua to the LORD in the day when the LORD delivered up the Amorites before the children of Israel, and he said in the sight of Israel, Sun, stand thou still upon Gibeon; and thou, Moon, in the valley of Ajalon. And the sun stood still, and the moon stayed, until the people had avenged themselves upon their enemies... And there was no day like that before it or after it, that the LORD hearkened unto the voice of a man: for the LORD fought for Israel"

- **Habakkuk 3:11** — "The sun and moon stood still in their habitation: at the light of thine arrows they went, and at the shining of thy glittering spear."
  - *Interpretation:* Habakkuk references Joshua's long day as evidence of God's mighty works, showing this was remembered throughout Israel's history.

- **Isaiah 38:8** — "Behold, I will bring again the shadow of the degrees, which is gone down in the sun dial of Ahaz, ten degrees backward. So the sun returned ten degrees, by which degrees it was gone down."
  - *Interpretation:* Hezekiah's sign of the sun returning backward parallels Joshua's long day, showing God's control over celestial bodies.

- **Mark 13:25** — "And the stars of heaven shall fall, and the powers that are in heaven shall be shaken."
  - *Interpretation:* Jesus speaks of celestial disturbances in end times, showing that God's control over heavenly bodies continues.

### "The LORD cast down great stones from heaven upon them" — Divine Weapons

**Joshua 10:11** — "And it came to pass, as they fled from before Israel, and were in the going down to Beth-horon, that the LORD cast down great stones from heaven upon them unto Azekah, and they died: they were more which died with hailstones than they whom the children of Israel slew with the sword"

- **Exodus 9:23-25** — "And Moses stretched forth his rod toward heaven: and the LORD sent thunder and hail, and fire ran along upon the ground; and the LORD sent hail upon the land of Egypt... And the hail smote throughout all the land of Egypt all that was in the field, both man and beast"
  - *Interpretation:* The hailstones on Egypt parallel the hailstones on the Amorites, showing God's consistent use of weather as weapons.

- **Revelation 16:21** — "And there fell upon men a great hail out of heaven, every stone about the weight of a talent: and men blasphemed God because of the plague of the hail; for the plague thereof was exceeding great."
  *Interpretation:* Revelation shows that hailstones will be used in end-times judgment, continuing this divine weapon.

### "Put your feet upon the necks of these kings" — Victory Symbolism

**Joshua 10:24-25** — "And Joshua called for all the men of Israel, and said unto the captains of the men of war which went with him, Come near, put your feet upon the necks of these kings... And Joshua said unto them, Fear not, nor be dismayed, be strong and of a good courage: for thus shall the LORD do to all your enemies against whom ye fight"

- **Psalm 110:1** — "The LORD said unto my Lord, Sit thou at my right hand, until I make thine enemies thy footstool."
  - *Interpretation:* The psalmist uses the same imagery of enemies as footstools, showing this was a recognized symbol of complete victory.

- **Romans 16:20** — "And the God of peace shall bruise Satan under your feet shortly."
  - *Interpretation:* Paul applies this principle to spiritual victory, showing that Christ's victory over Satan makes enemies a footstool.

- **1 Corinthians 15:25** — "For he must reign, till he hath put all enemies under his feet."
  *Interpretation:* Paul shows that Christ's reign involves putting all enemies under His feet, building on this victory symbolism.

### "And Joshua smote them from Kadesh-barnea even unto Gaza" — Complete Conquest

**Joshua 10:40-42** — "So Joshua smote all the country of the hills, and of the south, and of the vale, and of the springs, and all their kings: he left none remaining, but utterly destroyed all that breathed, as the LORD God of Israel commanded... And Joshua smote them from Kadesh-barnea even unto Gaza, and all the country of Goshen, even unto Gibeon. And all these kings and their land did Joshua take at one time, because the LORD God of Israel fought for Israel"

- **Deuteronomy 7:2** — "And when the LORD thy God shall deliver them before thee; thou shalt smite them, and utterly destroy them; thou shalt make no covenant with them, nor shew mercy unto them"
  - *Interpretation:* Moses commanded complete destruction of Canaanite nations, which Joshua now fulfills in southern Canaan.

- **Deuteronomy 20:16-17** — "But of the cities of these people, which the LORD thy God doth give thee for an inheritance, thou shalt save alive nothing that breatheth: But thou shalt utterly destroy them"
  *Interpretation:* Moses' law required complete destruction of Canaanite cities, which Joshua executes perfectly.

- **Function:** Historical record of Israel's deeds
- **Reference:** Source for Joshua's long day account

- **Natural phenomenon:** Ice falling from sky
- **Divine weapon:** God's judgment tool
- **Effectiveness:** Killed more than Israel's sword

- **Meaning:** Place where feet rest
- **Symbolic meaning:** Complete victory over enemies
- **Cultural context:** Sign of total domination

16 But these five kings fled, and hid themselves in a cave at Makkedah. 

17 And it was told Joshua, saying, The five kings are found hid in a cave at Makkedah. 

18 And Joshua said, Roll great stones upon the mouth of the cave, and set men by it for to keep them: 

19 And stay ye not, but pursue after your enemies, and smite the hindmost of them; suffer them not to come into their cities: for the LORD your God hath delivered them into your hand. 

20 And it came to pass, when Joshua and the children of Israel had made an end of slaying them with a very great slaughter, till they were consumed, that the rest which remained of them entered into fenced cities. 

21 And all the people returned to the camp to Joshua at Makkedah in peace: none moved his tongue against any of the children of Israel. 

22 Then said Joshua, Open the mouth of the cave, and bring out those five kings unto me out of the' cave.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Sun, stand thou still"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about stopping the earth's rotation
**Biblical context:** This is a divine miracle where God controls celestial bodies for Israel's military victory. The focus is on God's power and His fighting for Israel, not astronomical mechanics. The miracle demonstrates that God controls creation for His purposes.

### "There was no day like that before it or after it"
**Common misinterpretation:** This means no other miracles compare
**Biblical context:** This specifically refers to God hearkening to a man's voice to control celestial bodies. Other miracles may be greater in different ways, but this was unique in God responding to human command over heavenly bodies.

**Chapter 10 demonstrates God's sovereign power in conquest:** The Amorite coalition forms to attack Gibeon for making peace with Israel, but God promises victory to Joshua. The battle involves divine miracles: God discomfits the enemy, casts hailstones that kill more than Israel's sword, and stops the sun and moon for a whole day. This unprecedented miracle shows God fighting for Israel. The capture and execution of the five kings demonstrates complete victory over opposition. Joshua's encouragement to his captains shows that this victory pattern applies to all future battles. The systematic conquest of southern Canaan demonstrates thorough obedience to God's command to destroy all inhabitants. The chapter emphasizes that military success comes from divine intervention rather than human strength. The return to Gilgal shows that victory leads back to the worship base. This chapter establishes that God controls all creation for His people's benefit and that complete obedience brings complete victory.
