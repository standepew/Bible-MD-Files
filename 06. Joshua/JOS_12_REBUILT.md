## CHAPTER SUMMARY

This chapter lists all the kings defeated by Israel, dividing them into those conquered east of Jordan by Moses and those west of Jordan by Joshua. East of Jordan, from Arnon River to Mount Hermon, Moses and Israel defeated Sihon king of the Amorites who ruled from Aroer to half Gilead, including the plains to Chinneroth and Salt Sea. They also defeated Og king of Bashan, a remnant of giants, who ruled from Mount Hermon to the Geshurite border, including half Gilead. Moses gave these lands to Reuben, Gad, and half-Manasseh. West of Jordan, from Baal-gad to Mount Halak, Joshua and Israel defeated thirty-one kings in various terrains and among all Canaanite tribes. The list includes: Jericho, Ai, Jerusalem, Hebron, Jarmuth, Lachish, Eglon, Gezer, Debir, Geder, Hormah, Arad, Libnah, Adullam, Makkedah, Beth-el, Tappuah, Hepher, Aphek, Lasharon, Madon, Hazor, Shimron-meron, Achshaph, Taanach, Megiddo, Kedesh, Jokneam of Carmel, Dor in the coast of Dor, the nations of Gilgal, and Tirzah. Joshua gave these lands to the tribes of Israel according to their divisions.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Sihon king of the Amorites" — East Jordan Conquest

**Joshua 12:2-5** — "Sihon king of the Amorites, who dwelt in Heshbon, and ruled from Aroer, which is upon the bank of the river Arnon, and from the middle of the river, and from half Gilead, even unto the river Jabbok, which is the border of the children of Ammon"

- **Numbers 21:21-25** — "And Israel sent messengers unto Sihon king of the Amorites, saying, Let me pass, I pray thee, through thy country... But Sihon trusted not Israel to pass through his coast: but Sihon gathered all his people together, and went out against Israel into the wilderness... And Israel smote him with the edge of the sword, and possessed his land from Arnon unto Jabbok"
  - *Interpretation:* Numbers provides the historical account of Sihon's defeat, which Joshua now summarizes as part of the complete conquest record.

- **Deuteronomy 2:30-33** — "But Sihon king of Heshbon would not let us pass by him: for the LORD thy God hardened his spirit, and made his heart obstinate, that he might deliver him into thy hand... And the LORD our God delivered him before us, and we smote him, and his sons, and all his people."
  - *Interpretation:* Deuteronomy explains that God hardened Sihon's heart to bring about his defeat, showing divine sovereignty in conquest.

- **Psalm 135:10-11** — "Who smote great nations, and slew mighty kings; Sihon king of the Amorites, and Og king of Bashan"
  *Interpretation:* The psalmist celebrates God's victory over these kings as evidence of His power.

### "Og king of Bashan, which was of the remnant of the giants" — Giant King Defeat

**Joshua 12:4-6** — "And the coast of Og king of Bashan, which was of the remnant of the giants, that dwelt at Ashtaroth and at Edrei... Them did Moses the servant of the LORD and the children of Israel smite"

- **Deuteronomy 3:11** — "For only Og king of Bashan remained of the remnant of giants; behold, his bedstead was a bedstead of iron; is it not in Rabbath of the children of Ammon? nine cubits was the length thereof, and four cubits the breadth of it, after the cubit of a man."
  - *Interpretation:* Deuteronomy provides details about Og's giant stature, showing that Israel defeated even seemingly invincible enemies through God's power.

- **Numbers 21:33-35** — "And they turned and went up by the way of Bashan: and Og king of Bashan came out against them, he, and all his people, to the battle at Edrei.   *Interpretation:* Numbers records God's promise of victory over Og, showing that even giant kings fall before divine power.

### "Thirty and one kings" — Complete Conquest Record

**Joshua 12:9, 24-26** — "And these are the kings of the country which Joshua and the children of Israel smote on this side Jordan on the west... all the kings thirty and one"

- **Deuteronomy 7:1** — "When the LORD thy God shall bring thee into the land whither thou goest to possess it, and hath cast out many nations before thee, the Hittites, and the Girgashites, and the Amorites, and the Canaanites, and the Perizzites, and the Hivites, and the Jebusites; seven nations greater and mightier than thou"
  - *Interpretation:* Moses mentioned seven nations, but Joshua's list shows thirty-one individual kings, demonstrating the thoroughness of the conquest.

- **Judges 1:21-36** — "And the children of Benjamin did not drive out the Jebusites that inhabited Jerusalem... Neither did Manasseh drive out the inhabitants of Beth-shean and her towns... Neither did Ephraim drive out the Canaanites that dwelt in Gezer... Neither did Zebulun drive out the inhabitants of Kitron"
  - *Interpretation:* Judges shows that while Joshua conquered the kings, some inhabitants remained in cities, explaining why continued conflict occurred.

- **Acts 13:19** — "And when he had destroyed seven nations in the land of Chanaan, he divided their land to them by lot."
  *Interpretation:* Paul's sermon refers to the conquest of seven nations, showing this was recognized in New Testament times as a significant historical event.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### REMNANT OF THE GIANTS
- **Meaning:** Descendants of the Nephilim/Anakim
- **Historical context:** Survivors of pre-flood giants
- **Significance:** Seemingly invincible enemies defeated by God
* **Spiritual application:** No obstacle too great for God (Jeremiah 32:27)

### COAST
- **Meaning:** Border, boundary, territory
- **Geographic usage:** Kingdom boundaries and extent
- **Political significance:** Sovereign control over territory
* **Spiritual meaning:** Boundaries of God's kingdom (Psalm 74:17)

### DIVISIONS
- **Meaning:** Tribal allocations of land
- **Purpose:** Orderly distribution of inheritance
- **Method:** By lot and divine direction
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual gifts distribution (1 Corinthians 12)

### INHERITANCE
- **Meaning:** Permanent possession from God
- **Context:** Promised land as divine gift
- **Significance:** Covenant fulfillment
* **Eternal application:** Eternal inheritance (1 Peter 1:4)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Thirty and one kings"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is just a historical list
**Biblical context:** This is a divine record of complete victory, showing that God's promise to give Israel the land was fulfilled through the defeat of all organized resistance. The specific number demonstrates the thoroughness of the conquest.

### "Which was of the remnant of the giants"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about mythological creatures
**Biblical context:* This refers to historical giants like the Anakim who survived from before the flood. Og's giant status (bedstead 9 cubits long) shows these were real people of unusual size, not mythical beings.
