## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Eleazar the priest, Joshua, and tribal heads distribute the Canaan land inheritance by lot to nine tribes and half-Manasseh, as the LORD commanded Moses. Moses had already given two and a half tribes inheritance east of Jordan, giving Levites no land inheritance but cities to dwell in with suburbs for their cattle. Joseph's two tribes (Manasseh and Ephraim) gave no part to Levites except dwelling cities. Israel obeyed the LORD's command through Moses in dividing the land. Judah comes to Joshua at Gilgal, and Caleb the Kenezite reminds Joshua of God's word to Moses at Kadesh-barnea about them. Caleb was forty when Moses sent him to spy the land, and he gave an honest report while others caused Israel's heart to melt. Because Caleb wholly followed the LORD, Moses swore that the land he trod on would be his inheritance forever. Now forty-five years later, Caleb at eighty-five declares he's as strong as when Moses sent him, able for war. He requests the mountain with Anakim and great fenced cities, trusting that if God is with him, he can drive them out as promised. Joshua blesses Caleb and gives him Hebron as inheritance. Hebron becomes Caleb's inheritance because he wholly followed the LORD God of Israel. Hebron was formerly called Kirjath-arba after Arba, a great Anakim. The land rests from war.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "I wholly followed the LORD my God" — Faithful Devotion

**Joshua 14:8-9, 14** — "Forty years old was I when Moses the servant of the LORD sent me from Kadesh-barnea to espy out the land; and I brought him word again as it was in mine heart. Nevertheless my brethren that went up with me made the heart of the people melt: but I wholly followed the LORD my God... As yet I am as strong this day as I was in the day that Moses sent me: as my strength was then, even so is my strength now, for war, both to go out, and to come in"

- **Numbers 13:30** — "And Caleb stilled the people before Moses, and said, Let us go up at once, and possess it; for we are well able to overcome it."
  - *Interpretation:* Numbers records Caleb's faithful report, contrasting with the other spies who brought discouragement. His confidence came from wholly following the LORD.

- **Numbers 14:24** — "But my servant Caleb, because he had another spirit with him, and hath followed me fully, him will I bring into the land whereinto he went; and his seed shall possess it."
  - *Interpretation:* God promised Caleb the land because he had "another spirit" and followed Him fully, which Joshua now fulfills.

- **Deuteronomy 1:36** — "Save Caleb the son of Jephunneh; he shall see it, and to him will I give the land that he hath trodden upon, and to his children, because he hath wholly followed the LORD."
  *Interpretation:* Moses repeated God's promise to Caleb, showing this was a well-established covenant promise.

### "Forty and five years, even since the LORD spake this word unto Moses" — Faithful Waiting

**Joshua 14:10** — "And now, behold, the LORD hath kept me alive, as he said, these forty and five years, even since the LORD spake this word unto Moses, while the children of Israel wandered in the wilderness: and now, lo, I am this day fourscore and five years old"

- **Psalm 91:16** — "With long life will I satisfy him, and shew him my salvation."
  - *Interpretation:* The psalmist promises long life to those who dwell in God's protection, which Caleb experienced through forty-five years of wilderness wandering.

- **Isaiah 46:4** — "And even to your old age I am he; and even to hoar hairs will I carry you: I have made, and I will bear; even I will carry, and will deliver you."
  - *Interpretation:* Isaiah promises God's care through old age, which Caleb experienced as he remained strong at eighty-five.

- **Luke 2:36** — "And there was one Anna, a prophetess, the daughter of Phanuel, of the tribe of Aser: she was of a great age, and had lived with an husband seven years from her virginity"
  *Interpretation:* Anna also served God faithfully into old age, showing that elderly believers remain valuable in God's service.

  - *Interpretation:* God originally promised that only Caleb and Joshua would enter the land, which Caleb now claims specifically the mountain area with Anakim.

- **Joshua 15:13** — "And unto Caleb the son of Jephunneh he gave a part among the children of Judah, according to the commandment of the LORD to Joshua, even the city of Arba the father of Anak, which city is Hebron."
  *Interpretation:* Joshua's later chapter confirms that Caleb received Hebron, specifically the city of Arba (Hebron), fulfilling his request for the mountain with Anakim.

- **Hebrews 6:12** — "That ye be not slothful, but followers of them who through faith and patience inherit the promises."
  *Interpretation:* Hebrews uses Caleb as an example of inheriting promises through faith and patience, showing his forty-five year wait demonstrates this principle.

- **Spiritual meaning:** God's promised blessing

- **Challenge context:** Home of Anakim giants

- **Meaning:** Military campaign completed

7 Forty years old was I when Moses the servant of the LORD sent me from Kadesh-barnea to espy out the land; and I brought him word again as it was in mine heart. 

8 Nevertheless my brethren that went up with me made the heart of the people melt: but I wholly followed the LORD my God. 

9 And Moses sware on that day, saying, Surely the land whereon thy feet have trodden shall be thine inheritance, and thy children's for ever, because thou hast wholly followed the LORD my God. 

10 And now, behold, the LORD hath kept me alive, as he said, these forty and five years, even since the LORD spake this word unto Moses, while the children of Israel wandered in the wilderness: and now, lo, I am this day fourscore and five years old. 

11 As yet I am as strong this day as I was in the day that Moses sent me: as my strength was then, even so is my strength now, for war, both to go out, and to come in. 

12 Now therefore give me this mountain, whereof the LORD spake in that day; for thou heardest in that day how the Anakims were there, and that the cities were great and fenced: if so be the LORD will be with me, then I shall be able to drive them out, as the LORD said. 

13 And Joshua blessed him, and gave unto Caleb the son of Jephunneh Hebron for an inheritance. 

14 Hebron therefore became the inheritance of Caleb the son of Jephunneh the Kenezite unto this day, because that he wholly followed the LORD God of Israel. 

15 And the name of Hebron before was Kirjath-arba; which Arba was a great man among the Anakims. And the land had rest from war.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "I wholly followed the LORD my God"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about perfect sinlessness
**Biblical context:** This is about complete devotion and loyalty to God's direction, not claiming moral perfection. Caleb's contrast is with the other spies who brought fear, not about being sinless. It's about following God's leading faithfully.

### "As yet I am as strong this day as I was in the day that Moses sent me"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about physical fitness
**Biblical context:* This is about divine preservation and readiness for God's service. Caleb's strength at 85 shows God's faithfulness in keeping His servants capable for His purposes, not natural health.
