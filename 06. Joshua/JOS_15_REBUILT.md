## CHAPTER SUMMARY

This chapter details Judah's tribal inheritance boundaries. The south border extends from Edom's wilderness of Zin to the Salt Sea shore, going south to Maaleh-acrabbim, Zin, Kadesh-barnea, Hezron, Adar, Karkaa, Azmon, and the River of Egypt. The east border is the Salt Sea to Jordan's end. The north border goes from the sea bay at Jordan's end to Beth-hogla, north of Beth-arabah, to the stone of Bohan (Reuben's son), up from Achor valley toward Debir, north to Gilgal before Adummim ascent (south of the river), to En-shemesh waters, ending at En-rogel. The border goes up Hinnom valley to Jebusite (Jerusalem) south side, to the mountain top before Hinnom valley westward, ending at the valley of giants northward. From the hill top to Nephtoah waters, to mount Ephron cities, to Baalah (Kirjath-jearim), compassing westward to mount Seir, along mount Jearim's north side (Chesalon), down to Beth-shemesh, to Timnah, to Ekron's north side, to Shicron, mount Baalah, to Jabneel, ending at the sea. The west border is the great sea. Caleb receives Hebron (city of Arba, Anak's father) among Judah, driving out the three Anakim sons (Sheshai, Ahiman, Talmai). Caleb offers his daughter Achsah to whoever takes Kirjath-sepher (Debir). Othniel (Caleb's brother's son) takes it and receives Achsah. She asks Caleb for a field and springs of water, receiving upper and nether springs. The chapter lists Judah's cities by region: 29 cities toward Edom, 14 valley cities, 11 mountain cities, 9 cities including Hebron, 10 cities, 6 cities, 2 cities, and 6 wilderness cities. The Jebusites in Jerusalem could not be driven out and dwell with Judah.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Caleb drove thence the three sons of Anak" — Giant Defeat

**Joshua 15:14** — "And Caleb drove thence the three sons of Anak, Sheshai, and Ahiman, and Talmai, the children of Anak"

- **Numbers 13:22, 28** — "And they ascended by the south, and came unto Hebron; where Ahiman, Sheshai, and Talmai, the children of Anak, were... Nevertheless the people be strong that dwell in the land, and the cities are walled, and very great: and moreover we saw the children of Anak there."
  - *Interpretation:* Numbers first mentions these three Anakim sons at Hebron, causing the spies' fear. Caleb now drives them out, fulfilling his faith and demonstrating that what once caused fear is now defeated.

- **Joshua 14:12** — "Now therefore give me this mountain, whereof the LORD spake in that day; for thou heardest in that day how the Anakims were there, and that the cities were great and fenced"
  - *Interpretation:* Caleb specifically requested the mountain with Anakim, and now drives them out, showing that faith leads to action and victory.

- **1 Samuel 17:4** — "And a champion went out out of the camp of the Philistines, named Goliath, of Gath, whose height was six cubits and a span."
  *Interpretation:* Goliath from Gath shows that Anakim descendants remained in Philistine cities, exactly as Joshua 11:22 recorded.

---

### "He that smiteth Kirjath-sepher, and taketh it, to him will I give Achsah my daughter to wife" — Marriage as Reward

**Joshua 15:16** — "And Caleb said, He that smiteth Kirjath-sepher, and taketh it, to him will I give Achsah my daughter to wife"

- **Judges 1:12-13** — "And Caleb said, He that smiteth Kirjath-sepher, and taketh it, to him will I give Achsah my daughter to wife. And Othniel the son of Kenaz, Caleb's younger brother, took it: and he gave him Achsah his daughter to wife."
  - *Interpretation:* Judges repeats this account, confirming its historical accuracy and Othniel's victory.

- **Judges 3:7-11** — "And the children of Israel did evil in the sight of the LORD, and forgat the LORD their God... And the anger of the LORD was hot against Israel, and he sold them into the hand of Chushan-rishathaim king of Mesopotamia: and the children of Israel served Chushan-rishathaim eight years. And when the children of Israel cried unto the LORD, the LORD raised up a deliverer to the children of Israel, who delivered them, even Othniel the son of Kenaz, Caleb's younger brother."
  - *Interpretation:* Judges shows that Othniel later became Israel's first judge, demonstrating that military victory led to spiritual leadership.

- **Deuteronomy 7:3-4** — "Neither shalt thou make marriages with them; thy daughter thou shalt not give unto his son, nor his daughter shalt thou take unto thy son. For they will turn away thy son from following me, that they may serve other gods"
  *Interpretation:* Deuteronomy forbade intermarriage with Canaanites, but Caleb's offer to Othniel (an Israelite) shows marriage within the covenant community was encouraged.

---

### "Give me a blessing; for thou hast given me a south land; give me also springs of water" — Wisdom and Provision

**Joshua 15:19** — "Who answered, Give me a blessing; for thou hast given me a south land; give me also springs of water. And he gave her the upper springs, and the nether springs"

- **Proverbs 19:14** — "House and riches are the inheritance of fathers: and a prudent wife is from the LORD."
  - *Interpretation:* Proverbs shows that a wise wife is from the Lord, which Achsah demonstrates by requesting necessary water for her land.

- **John 4:10** — "Jesus answered and said unto her, If thou knewest the gift of God, and who it is that saith to thee, Give me to drink; thou wouldest have asked of him, and he would have given thee living water."
  - *Interpretation:* Jesus uses water as a metaphor for spiritual provision, building on the principle that water is essential for life and blessing.

- **Isaiah 44:3** — "For I will pour water upon him that is thirsty, and floods upon the dry ground: I will pour my spirit upon thy seed, and my blessing upon thine offspring"
  *Interpretation:* Isaiah promises water and blessing to the thirsty, which Achsah receives through her wise request.

---

### "The children of Judah could not drive them out: but the Jebusites dwell with the children of Judah at Jerusalem unto this day" — Unfinished Conquest

**Joshua 15:63** — "As for the Jebusites the inhabitants of Jerusalem, the children of Judah could not drive them out: but the Jebusites dwell with the children of Judah at Jerusalem unto this day"

- **Judges 1:21** — "And the children of Benjamin did not drive out the Jebusites that inhabited Jerusalem; but the Jebusites dwell with the children of Benjamin in Jerusalem unto this day."
  - *Interpretation:* Judges attributes Jerusalem's Jebusite problem to Benjamin, showing the city was on the tribal border between Judah and Benjamin.

- **Judges 19:10-12** — "But the man would not tarry that night, but rose up and departed, and came over against Jebus, which is Jerusalem... And his master said unto him, We will not turn aside hither into the city of the Jebusites... but we will go on to Gibeah."
  *Interpretation:* Judges shows that Jebus (Jerusalem) was still considered Jebusite territory after Joshua's time.

- **2 Samuel 5:6-9** — "And the king and his men went to Jerusalem unto the Jebusites, the inhabitants of the land: which spake unto David, Thou shalt not come hither... Nevertheless David took the strong hold of Zion: the same is the city of David."
  *Interpretation:* David finally conquered Jerusalem centuries later, showing that some conquest tasks required future generations to complete.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### LOT
- **Meaning:** Portion determined by divine casting
- **Method:** Urim and Thummim or casting lots
- **Significance:** God's sovereign choice in allocation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual gifts distribution (1 Corinthians 12)

### ANAKIM
- **Meaning:** Descendants of Anak, giants
- **Historical significance:** Caused Israel's fear in wilderness
- **Geographic location:** Mountain regions, especially Hebron
* **Spiritual meaning:** Seemingly insurmountable obstacles

### SPRINGS
- **Physical meaning:** Water sources essential for agriculture
- **Symbolic meaning:** Blessing and provision
- **Strategic importance:** Made land productive and valuable
* **Spiritual application:** Living water (John 4:14)

### JEBUSITES
- **Meaning:** Inhabitants of Jerusalem (Jebus)
- **Historical problem:** Not driven out by Judah
- **Future conquest:** Conquered by David (2 Samuel 5)
* **Spiritual application:** Persistent spiritual strongholds

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "As for the Jebusites the inhabitants of Jerusalem, the children of Judah could not drive them out"
**Common misinterpretation:** This shows Israel's failure or weakness
**Biblical context:** This shows that some conquest tasks require future generations to complete. David later conquered Jerusalem (2 Samuel 5), showing that God's timing may span generations, not indicating Israel's failure.

### "Give me a blessing; for thou hast given me a south land; give me also springs of water"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about material greed
**Biblical context:* This is wise planning. Achsah recognizes that land without water is less valuable, so she requests necessary resources to make her inheritance productive. This demonstrates practical wisdom in stewardship.

**Chapter 15 establishes Judah's prominent inheritance:** Judah receives the largest and most detailed inheritance, including the strategically important southern border from Edom to Egypt and the western border to the great sea. The precise boundaries demonstrate God's orderly planning in land distribution. Caleb's inheritance within Judah fulfills God's promise to the faithful spy, showing that divine rewards come to those who wholly follow God. Caleb's driving out the three Anakim sons demonstrates that faith conquers even the most intimidating obstacles. Othniel's capture of Kirjath-sepher and marriage to Achsah shows that military valor brings family and social rewards. Achsah's wise request for water springs demonstrates practical wisdom in making inheritance productive. The extensive city list shows the thoroughness of Judah's possession. The note about Jebusites remaining in Jerusalem shows that complete conquest may require future generations, as David later conquered the city. This chapter establishes Judah's preeminent position among tribes, foreshadowing the Davidic and Messianic lineage coming from Judah.
