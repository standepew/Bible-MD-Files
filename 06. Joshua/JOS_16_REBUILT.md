## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Joseph's inheritance extends from Jordan by Jericho eastward to Jericho waters, then to the wilderness rising from Jericho through mount Beth-el. The border goes from Beth-el to Luz, along Archi borders to Ataroth, then westward to Japhleti coast, to lower Beth-horon, and to Gezer, ending at the sea. Joseph's children (Manasseh and Ephraim) take their inheritance. Ephraim's eastern border is Ataroth-addar to upper Beth-horon. The border goes to Michmethah on the north side by the sea, then eastward to Taanath-shiloh, passing east to Janohah. From Janohah it goes down to Ataroth, Naarath, Jericho, and ends at Jordan. From Tappuah westward to Kanah river, ending at the sea. This is Ephraim's inheritance. Ephraim has separate cities among Manasseh's inheritance, all cities with villages. The Ephraimites did not drive out the Canaanites in Gezer; the Canaanites dwell among Ephraimites to this day, serving under tribute.

### "The lot of the children of Joseph fell" — Joseph's Promised Preeminence

**Joshua 16:1, 6** — "And the lot of the children of Joseph fell from Jordan by Jericho... So the children of Joseph, Manasseh and Ephraim, took their inheritance"

- **Genesis 48:5-6** — "And now thy two sons, Ephraim and Manasseh, which were born unto thee in the land of Egypt before I came unto thee into Egypt, are mine; as Reuben and Simeon, they shall be mine. Thy issue, which thou begettest after them, shall be thine, and shall be called after the name of their brethren in their inheritance."
  - *Interpretation:* Jacob adopted Joseph's sons as his own, giving them equal status with his own sons and promising them inheritance in the promised land, which Joshua now fulfills.

- **Genesis 49:22-26** — "Joseph is a fruitful bough, even a fruitful bough by a well; whose branches run over the wall...   - *Interpretation:* Jacob's blessing promised Joseph preeminence and abundance, which is reflected in Joseph's descendants receiving a large, central inheritance.

- **1 Chronicles 5:1-2** — "Now the sons of Reuben the firstborn of Israel, (for he was the firstborn; but, forasmuch as he defiled his father's bed, his birthright was given unto the sons of Joseph the son of Israel: and the genealogy is not to be reckoned after the birthright.   *InterInterpretation:* Chronicles confirms that Joseph received Reuben's birthright, explaining why Joseph's descendants received a double portion inheritance.

- **1 Kings 9:16** — "For Pharaoh king of Egypt had gone up, and taken Gezer, and burnt it with fire, and slain the Canaanites that dwelt in the city, and given it for a present unto his daughter, Solomon's wife, which had brought her up out of David's city: which he burned unto the ground."

  *Interpretation:* Chronicles shows that Gezer remained part of Ephraim's territory even with Canaanite inhabitants, demonstrating the complexity of the settlement.
