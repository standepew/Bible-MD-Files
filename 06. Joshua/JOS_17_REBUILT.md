## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Manasseh receives inheritance as Joseph's firstborn. Machir, Manasseh's firstborn and Gilead's father, being a man of war, receives Gilead and Bashan. The rest of Manasseh's children (Abiezer, Helek, Asriel, Shechem, Hepher, Shemida) receive inheritance by families. Zelophehad son of Hepher, descended from Manasseh, had no sons but five daughters (Mahlah, Noah, Hoglah, Milcah, Tirzah). They approach Eleazar, Joshua, and princes, claiming God's command through Moses for inheritance among brethren. They receive inheritance among their father's brothers. Manasseh gets ten portions besides Gilead and Bashan east of Jordan, because Zelophehad's daughters received inheritance among Manasseh's sons while other sons got Gilead. Manasseh's border goes from Asher to Michmethah before Shechem, along to En-tappuah inhabitants. Manasseh has Tappuah land, but Tappuah on Manasseh's border belongs to Ephraim. The coast descends to Kanah river southward; Ephraim's cities are among Manasseh's cities. Manasseh's coast is north of the river, ending at the sea. Southward is Ephraim, northward Manasseh, with sea as border, meeting Asher north and Issachar east. Manasseh has cities in Issachar and Asher: Beth-shean, Ibleam, Dor, Endor, Taanach, and Megiddo with their towns—three countries. Manasseh cannot drive out these cities' inhabitants, so Canaanites dwell there. When Israel becomes strong, they put Canaanites to tribute but don't drive them out completely. Joseph's children complain to Joshua about receiving only one lot despite being a great people blessed by God. Joshua responds that if they're great people, they should go to the wood country among Perizzites and giants if mount Ephraim is too narrow. Joseph says the hill isn't enough and Canaanites in the valley have iron chariots. Joshua tells the house of Joseph (Ephraim and Manasseh) that they're a great people with great power and won't have only one lot; the mountain is theirs to clear, and they'll drive out Canaanites despite their iron chariots and strength.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Zelophehad... had no sons, but daughters" — Female Inheritance

**Joshua 17:3, 6** — "But Zelophehad, the son of Hepher, the son of Gilead, the son of Machir, the son of Manasseh, had no sons, but daughters... And they came near before Eleazar the priest, and before Joshua the son of Nun, and before the princes, saying, The LORD commanded Moses to give us an inheritance among our brethren"

- **Numbers 27:1-11** — "Then came the daughters of Zelophehad... And they stood before Moses, and before Eleazar the priest, and before the princes and all the congregation, by the door of the tabernacle of the congregation, saying... And Moses brought their cause before the LORD. And the LORD spake unto Moses, saying... Thou shalt surely give them a possession of an inheritance among their father's brethren"
  - *Interpretation:* Numbers provides the original account where Moses brought their case before God, who established that daughters could inherit when no sons existed, preserving the family name and inheritance.

- **Numbers 36:1-12** — "And the heads of the fathers of the Levites... came, and spake unto Moses... And every daughter, that possesseth an inheritance in any tribe of the children of Israel, shall be wife unto one of the family of the tribe of her father, that the children of Israel may enjoy every man the inheritance of his fathers"
  *Interpretation:* Numbers adds the restriction that daughters inheriting must marry within their father's tribe to keep inheritance within that tribe, which Joshua now implements.

**Joshua 17:12-13** — "Yet the children of Manasseh could not drive out the inhabitants of those cities; but the Canaanites would dwell in that land... ## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### FIRSTBORN
- **Meaning:** Preeminent son with birthright privileges
- **Context:** Manasseh as Joseph's firstborn
- **Significance:** Double portion inheritance
* **Christological application:** Christ as firstborn over all (Colossians 1:15)

### INHERITANCE
- **Physical meaning:** Land possession
- **Legal innovation:** Female inheritance rights
- **Tribal significance:** Preserving family name and property
* **Eternal application:** Spiritual inheritance (1 Peter 1:4)

### IRON CHARIOTS
- **Military technology:** Advanced war chariots
- **Strategic advantage:** Superior military equipment
- **Spiritual challenge:** Seemingly insurmountable obstacles
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual warfare (Ephesians 6:12)

### TRIBUTE
- **Meaning:** Forced payment to conquering power
- **Context:** Canaanites serving Israel
- **Significance:** Incomplete but beneficial conquest
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual bondage vs. freedom (Galatians 5:1)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "If thou be a great people, then get thee up to the wood country"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is harsh criticism
**Biblical context:** This is Joshua challenging Joseph to take initiative rather than complaining. It's a call to action: if you claim to be great, prove it by conquering new territory. It's empowerment, not criticism.

### "Though they have iron chariots, and though they be strong"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about military technology
**Biblical context:* This is about trusting God's power over human military advantages. Joshua is saying that God's power is greater than iron chariots and human strength, encouraging faith over fear.
