## CHAPTER SUMMARY

The whole Israel congregation assembles at Shiloh and sets up the tabernacle there. The land is subdued before them, but seven tribes haven't received inheritance. Joshua asks why they're slack to possess the land God gave their fathers. He commands three men from each tribe to survey and describe the land for inheritance division. Judah will stay south, Joseph's house north. They should divide land into seven parts and bring descriptions to Joshua for casting lots before God. Levites have no part; the priesthood is their inheritance. Gad, Reuben, and half-Manasseh already have inheritance east of Jordan from Moses. The men arise and Joshua charges them to survey the land and return for casting lots at Shiloh. The men survey the land, describing it by cities into seven parts in a book, and return to Joshua at Shiloh. Joshua casts lots before the LORD and divides the land to Israel by divisions. Benjamin's lot comes up between Judah and Joseph. Benjamin's northern border starts at Jordan, goes up Jericho's north side, through mountains westward to Beth-aven wilderness. From there toward Luz (Beth-el) southward, descending to Ataroth-adar near lower Beth-horon hill. The border compasses the sea's corner southward from Beth-horon hill, ending at Kirjath-baal (Kirjath-jearim, Judah's city) as west quarter. The south quarter starts at Kirjath-jearim's end, goes west to Nephtoah waters, down to the mountain before Hinnom valley, in the giants' valley north, descending to Hinnom valley, Jebusi south side, to En-rogel. From north it goes to En-shemesh, toward Geliloth opposite Adummim ascent, down to Bohan (Reuben's son) stone, along Arabah side northward, down to Arabah, along Beth-hogla side northward, ending at Salt Sea's north bay at Jordan's south end. Jordan is the east border. Benjamin's cities: Jericho, Beth-hoglah, Keziz valley, Beth-arabah, Zemaraim, Beth-el, Avim, Parah, Ophrah, Chephar-haammonai, Ophni, Gaba (12 cities with villages); Gibeon, Ramah, Beeroth, Mizpeh, Chephirah, Mozah, Rekem, Irpeel, Taralah (9 cities with villages); Zelah, Eleph, Jebusi (Jerusalem), Gibeath, Kirjath (5 cities with villages). This is Benjamin's inheritance.

  - *Interpretation:* James teaches that knowing what to do but not doing it is sin, which applies to Israel's failure to possess their inheritance.

- **Proverbs 16:33** — "The lot is cast into the lap; but the whole disposing thereof is of the LORD."

  - *Interpretation:* The early church used lots to select Matthias as Judas' replacement, showing this method continued in New Testament times for divine guidance.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "How long are ye slack to go to possess the land"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is just impatience
**Biblical context:** This is a legitimate rebuke for procrastination in claiming God's promises. Seven tribes had not claimed their inheritance despite the land being subdued. It's about spiritual lethargy, not impatience.

### "The lot of the tribe of the children of Benjamin came up"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about random chance
**Biblical context:* This is about divine sovereignty. While lots were cast, Proverbs 16:33 teaches that the whole disposing is of the LORD. Benjamin's strategic position between Judah and Joseph was God's sovereign choice.
