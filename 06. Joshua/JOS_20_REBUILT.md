## CHAPTER SUMMARY

The LORD commands Joshua to speak to Israel about appointing cities of refuge as previously commanded through Moses. These cities are for those who kill someone unintentionally and unwittingly, providing refuge from the blood avenger. When someone flees to one of these cities, they must stand at the city gate entrance and declare their case to the city elders, who will take them in and provide a place to dwell. If the blood avenger pursues, the city must not deliver the slayer because the killing was unintentional without prior hatred. The slayer dwells there until standing before the congregation for judgment and until the current high priest's death, then may return to their own city and house. Israel appoints three cities west of Jordan: Kedesh in Galilee (mount Naphtali), Shechem in mount Ephraim, and Kirjath-arba (Hebron) in Judah's mountain. East of Jordan by Jericho, they appoint Bezer in the wilderness plain (Reuben's tribe), Ramoth in Gilead (Gad's tribe), and Golan in Bashan (Manasseh's tribe). These cities are for all Israel and strangers among them, so anyone who kills unintentionally may flee there and not die by the blood avenger's hand until standing before the congregation.

### "Cities of refuge, whereof I spake unto you by the hand of Moses" — Mosaic Command Fulfilled

**Joshua 20:2-4** — "Speak to the children of Israel, saying, Appoint out for you cities of refuge, whereof I spake unto you by the hand of Moses: That the slayer that killeth any person unawares and unwittingly may flee thither: and they shall be your refuge from the avenger of blood"

- **Numbers 35:9-15** — "And the LORD spake unto Moses, saying, Speak unto the children of Israel, and say unto them, When ye be come over Jordan into the land of Canaan; Then ye shall appoint you cities to be cities of refuge for you... that the slayer that killeth any person unawares may flee thither: and they shall be your refuge from the avenger of blood."
  - *Interpretation:* Numbers provides the original command for cities of refuge, which Joshua now fulfills, showing continuity between Moses' and Joshua's leadership.

- **Deuteronomy 19:1-10** — "When the LORD thy God hath cut off the nations, whose land the LORD thy God giveth thee, and thou succeedest them, and dwellest in their cities, and in their houses; Thou shalt separate three cities for thee in the midst of thy land... that the slayer may flee thither, which killeth his neighbour unawares, and hated him not in times past"
  - *Interpretation:* Deuteronomy repeats the command, adding that this should be done after conquering the land, which Joshua now implements.

- **Hebrews 6:18** - "That by two immutable things, in which it was impossible for God to lie, we might have a strong consolation, who have fled for refuge to lay hold upon the hope set before us"
  *Interpretation:* Hebrews uses the concept of refuge as a metaphor for Christ as our refuge, building on the cities of refuge principle.

### "The avenger of blood" — Family Justice System

**Joshua 20:3, 5** — "And they shall be your refuge from the avenger of blood... And if the avenger of blood pursue after him, then they shall not deliver the slayer up into his hand: because he smote his neighbour unwittingly, and hated him not beforetime"

- **Genesis 9:5-6** — "And surely your blood of your lives will I require; at the hand of every beast will I require it, and at the hand of man; at the hand of every man's brother will I require the life of man. Whoso sheddeth man's blood, by man shall his blood be shed: for in the image of God made he man."
  - *Interpretation:* Genesis establishes the principle that shedding human blood requires bloodshed in return, which the blood avenger system implements within legal bounds.

- **Numbers 35:19** — "The revenger of blood himself shall kill the murderer: when he meeteth him, he shall kill him."
  - *Interpretation:* Numbers confirms that the blood avenger has the right to kill murderers, but distinguishes between intentional and unintentional killing.

- **Deuteronomy 19:6** - "Lest the avenger of the blood pursue the slayer, while his heart is hot, and overtake him, because the way is long, and slay him; whereas he was not worthy of death, inasmuch as he hated him not in time past"
  *Interpretation:* Deuteronomy explains the purpose of cities of refuge: to prevent hot-blooded revenge against those who killed unintentionally.

### "Until the death of the high priest that shall be in those days" — Legal Resolution

**Joshua 20:6** — "And he shall dwell in that city, until he stand before the congregation for judgment, and until the death of the high priest that shall be in those days: then shall the slayer return, and come unto his own city, and unto his own house, unto the city from whence he fled"

- **Numbers 35:25** — "And the congregation shall deliver the slayer out of the hand of the revenger of blood, and the congregation shall restore him to the city of his refuge, whither he was fled: and he shall abide in it unto the death of the high priest, which was anointed with the holy oil."
  - *Interpretation:* Numbers explains that the high priest's death provides atonement, allowing the slayer to return home, showing that the high priest represents the people before God.

- **Numbers 35:28** - "Because he should have remained in the city of his refuge until the death of the high priest: but after the death of the high priest the slayer shall return into the land of his possession."
  *Interpretation:* Numbers confirms that the high priest's death is the legal marker for when the slayer may return, showing the connection between priestly ministry and legal resolution.
