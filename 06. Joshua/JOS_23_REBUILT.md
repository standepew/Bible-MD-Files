## CHAPTER SUMMARY

A long time after God gave Israel rest from enemies, Joshua, now old and stricken in age, calls all Israel's leaders together. He reminds them they have seen God defeat nations because He fought for them. Joshua divided the remaining nations by lot as inheritance from Jordan to the great sea, and God will expel them so Israel can possess the land as promised. Joshua charges them to be very courageous to keep and do all written in Moses' law, not turning aside. They must not associate with remaining nations, mention their gods, swear by them, serve them, or bow to them, but must cleave to the LORD as they have done. God has driven out great nations; no one can stand against Israel because God fights for them—one man will chase a thousand. Joshua warns them to love God carefully. If they turn back and associate with remaining nations, intermarrying and serving their gods, God will no longer drive out those nations, which will become snares, traps, scourges, and thorns until Israel perishes from the good land. Joshua declares he's going the way of all the earth, and they know that not one thing has failed of God's good promises—all have come to pass. Just as good things came because of God's promises, evil things will come if they transgress the covenant by serving other gods, causing God's anger and quick destruction from the good land.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "One man of you shall chase a thousand"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about human strength
**Biblical context:** This is about God's power working through His people. The next clause clarifies "for the LORD your God, he it is that fighteth for you"—it's divine power, not human ability.

### "Not one thing hath failed of all the good things which the LORD your God spake"
**Common misinterpretation:** This means life was perfect
**Biblical context:* This refers specifically to God's covenant promises about the land and conquest. It doesn't mean there were no challenges, but that God's specific promises were completely fulfilled.
