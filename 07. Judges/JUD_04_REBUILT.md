## CHAPTER SUMMARY

After Ehud's death, Israel again does evil, so the LORD sells them to Jabin, Canaanite king ruling in Hazor. Jabin's army captain is Sisera, who has nine hundred iron chariots and oppresses Israel for twenty years. Israel cries to the LORD. Deborah, a prophetess and Lapidoth's wife, judges Israel under a palm tree between Ramah and Beth-el in mount Ephraim, where Israel comes for judgment. She sends for Barak (son of Abinoam) from Kedesh-naphtali, commanding him to go to mount Tabor with ten thousand men from Naphtali and Zebulun, promising to draw Sisera to Kishon river and deliver him into Barak's hand. Barak insists Deborah go with him, refusing to go otherwise. Deborah agrees but says Barak's journey won't bring him honor since the LORD will sell Sisera into a woman's hand. Deborah goes with Barak to Kedesh. Barak calls Zebulun and Naphtali to Kedesh, gathering ten thousand men with Deborah. Heber the Kenite, descendant of Moses' father-in-law, had separated from other Kenites and pitched his tent near Kedesh. They show Sisera that Barak has gone to mount Tabor. Sisera gathers his nine hundred iron chariots and army from Harosheth to Kishon river. Deborah tells Barak to attack since this is the day the LORD has delivered Sisera. Barak descends from Tabor with ten thousand men. The LORD discomfits Sisera's army before Barak, who pursues them to Harosheth, killing all. Sisera flees on foot to Jael's tent (Heber's wife), as there's peace between Jabin and Heber's house. Jael meets Sisera, inviting him into her tent without fear. She covers him with a mantle. When he asks for water, she gives him milk and covers him. He asks her to stand guard at the door, denying any man is present when asked. Jael takes a tent nail and hammer, approaches quietly, and drives the nail through his temples into the ground while he sleeps. He dies. When Barak pursues, Jael meets him and shows him Sisera dead with the nail in his temples. That day God subdues Jabin before Israel. Israel's hand prospers until they destroy Jabin king of Canaan.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "If thou wilt go with me, then I will go: but if thou wilt not go with me, then I will not go"
**Common misinterpretation:** This shows proper spiritual dependence
**Biblical context:** This shows lack of faith. Barak should have trusted God's promise without requiring Deborah's presence. His hesitation cost him the honor of killing Sisera.

### "The LORD shall sell Sisera into the hand of a woman"
**Common misinterpretation:* This refers to Deborah
**Biblical context:** This prophecy is fulfilled through Jael, not Deborah. God precisely predicted that a woman would kill Sisera, showing His detailed knowledge of future events.
