## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Israel does evil, and the LORD delivers them to Philistines for forty years. A Danite man named Manoah from Zorah has a barren wife. The angel of the LORD appears to her, saying she'll conceive and bear a son. She must avoid wine, strong drink, and unclean food, for the child will be a Nazarite to God from the womb and will begin delivering Israel from Philistines. The woman tells her husband a "man of God" with terrifying angelic countenance appeared, but she didn't ask his name or origin. He said she'd conceive a son who must be a Nazarite from womb to death. Manoah prays for the man to return and teach them how to raise the child. God hears, and the angel appears to the woman in the field. She runs to get Manoah, who follows and asks if he's the man who spoke to his wife. The angel confirms and tells Manoah to ensure his wife observes all instructions: no wine, strong drink, or unclean food. Manoah asks to detain the angel for a meal, but the angel says he won't eat their bread - any offering must be to the LORD. Manoah doesn't realize he's speaking to an angel. He asks the angel's name to honor him when his words come true. The angel says his name is secret. Manoah offers a kid with meat offering on a rock to the LORD. The angel performs wondrously as Manoah and his wife watch. When flame ascends from the altar, the angel ascends in the flame. Manoah and his wife fall face down. The angel doesn't appear again. Manoah realizes he saw an angel and fears they'll die for seeing God. His wife reasons that if the LORD wanted to kill them, He wouldn't have accepted their offering or shown them these things. The woman bears a son named Samson. The child grows, and the LORD blesses him. The Spirit of the LORD begins to move him at times in Dan's camp between Zorah and Eshtaol.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### NAZARITE
- **Meaning:** One consecrated to God with special vows
- **Context:** Samson's lifelong consecration from birth
- **Significance:** Divine calling and separation for God's purposes
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual consecration (Romans 12:1)

### BARREN
- **Meaning:** Unable to have children
- **Context:** Samson's mother's condition before divine intervention
- **Significance:** Human inability requiring divine power
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual fruitlessness requiring divine life (John 15:5)

### ANGEL OF THE LORD
- **Meaning:** Divine messenger, often pre-incarnate Christ
- **Context:** Messenger who announces Samson's birth
- **Significance:** Direct divine intervention in human affairs
* **New Testament parallel:** Angelic announcements (Luke 1)

### SECRET
- **Meaning:** Hidden or mysterious
- **Context:** Angel's name being secret
- **Significance:** Divine mystery beyond human understanding
* **Spiritual application:** Divine mysteries (Deuteronomy 29:29)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "No razor shall come on his head: for the child shall be a Nazarite unto God from the womb"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about hair length being spiritually significant
**Biblical context:** This is about lifelong consecration to God, not hair itself. The uncut hair is the outward sign of inward dedication, similar to how baptism signifies inward faith.

### "If the LORD were pleased to kill us, he would not have received a burnt offering"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about manipulating God through offerings
**Biblical context:** This is the wife's correct theological reasoning that God's acceptance of their offering shows His favor, not that offerings control God.
