## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Samson goes to Gaza and visits a harlot. The Gazites learn he's there and surround him, waiting all night in the city gate to kill him in the morning. Samson lies until midnight, then awakens and removes the city gates, doors, posts, and bar, carrying them on his shoulders to a hill before Hebron. Later he loves Delilah in the Sorek valley. Philistine lords offer her eleven hundred silver pieces each to entice Samson to reveal his strength's secret so they can bind and afflict him. Delilah asks Samson to reveal his strength, and he lies that seven green withs would bind him. The Philistines bring the withs, bind him, and hide men in the chamber. When she warns him, he breaks the withs like thread. She accuses him of lying and asks again. He lies that new ropes would bind him. She binds him with new ropes, but when warned, he breaks them like thread. She accuses him again of lying. He says weaving his seven locks with a web would bind him. She weaves his hair and fastens it with a pin. When warned, he awakens and leaves with the pin and web. She accuses him of not loving her since he won't tell his secret after three times of lying. She presses him daily until his soul is vexed to death. Finally he reveals his Nazarite vow - no razor has touched his head from his mother's womb, and if shaved, his strength will leave him. Delilah calls the Philistine lords with money. She makes Samson sleep on her knees, has a man shave his seven locks, and begins to afflict him as his strength leaves. When she warns him, he awakens thinking he'll shake himself free as before, not knowing the LORD has departed. The Philistines capture him, put out his eyes, bring him to Gaza, bind him with brass fetters, and make him grind in prison. His hair begins growing back. The Philistine lords gather to sacrifice to Dagon, rejoicing that their god delivered Samson. The people praise Dagon for delivering their enemy who destroyed their country and killed many. When merry, they call Samson from prison to entertain them, setting him between pillars. Samson asks the lad holding him to feel the pillars so he can lean on them. The house is full of men and women, with all Philistine lords present and about three thousand on the roof watching. Samson calls to the LORD to remember him and strengthen him one last time to avenge his two eyes. He takes hold of the two middle pillars, one with each hand, and says "Let me die with the Philistines." He bows with all his might, and the house falls on the lords and people inside. The dead at his death exceed those he killed in life. His brothers and father's house take his body and bury him between Zorah and Eshtaol in Manoah's tomb. He judged Israel twenty years.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### HARLOT
- **Meaning:** Prostitute or sexually immoral woman
- **Context:** Samson's moral failure in Gaza
- **Significance:** Spiritual compromise leading to downfall
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual adultery (James 4:4)

### DELILAH
- **Meaning:** "Delicate" or "languishing"
- **Context:** Samson's treacherous lover
- **Significance:** Betrayal through intimate relationship
* **Spiritual application:** False intimacy (Proverbs 2:16)

### NAZARITE
- **Meaning:** One consecrated to God with special vows
- **Context:** Samson's lifelong consecration violated
- **Significance:** Divine calling through separation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual consecration (Romans 12:1)

### DAGON
- **Meaning:** Philistine fish-god
- **Context:** False god credited with Samson's capture
- **Significance:** Idolatry opposing true God
* **Spiritual application:** False gods (1 Corinthians 10:20)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Let me die with the Philistines"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is suicide
**Biblical context:** This is sacrificial death for God's purposes. Samson dies to deliver Israel from Philistine oppression, making his death a martyrdom rather than suicide.

### "He wist not that the LORD was departed from him"
**Common misinterpretation:* This shows God abandoning Samson
**Biblical context:** This shows Samson's spiritual blindness. God didn't abandon Samson; Samson abandoned God through his sin and didn't recognize the Spirit's departure.

The shaving of his head represents the violation of his lifelong consecration and loss of divine power. His unawareness that the LORD departed shows spiritual blindness - he doesn't recognize when God's presence leaves. His death destroying more Philistines than he killed in life shows that God can bring greater victory through death than through life. His burial in his father's tomb shows restoration to family and proper place. The twenty-year judgeship shows that despite his failures, he fulfilled his calling to begin delivering Israel from Philistines. It demonstrates that Samson's story is both a warning about spiritual compromise and an example of final redemption. The chapter shows that God's purposes can be fulfilled even through flawed servants, but that human weakness still has serious consequences.
