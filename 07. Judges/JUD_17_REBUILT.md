## CHAPTER SUMMARY

A man named Micah from mount Ephraim tells his mother he took the eleven hundred shekels of silver she had cursed about. She blesses him. When he returns the silver, his mother says she had wholly dedicated it to the LORD for her son to make a graven and molten image, then restores it to him. Micah returns the money, and his mother takes two hundred shekels to a founder who makes a graven and molten image placed in Micah's house. Micah makes a house of gods with ephod and teraphim, consecrating one son as priest. The narrator notes that in those days Israel had no king, and everyone did what was right in their own eyes. A young Levite from Bethlehem-judah leaves his city to sojourn elsewhere, journeying to Micah's house in mount Ephraim. Micah asks where he's from, and the Levite explains he's from Bethlehem-judah seeking a place to sojourn. Micah invites him to dwell as father and priest for ten shekels of silver yearly, plus clothing and food. The Levite agrees and becomes like a son. Micah consecrates the Levite, who becomes his priest. Micah expresses confidence that the LORD will do him good since he has a Levite priest.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "I had wholly dedicated the silver unto the LORD... to make a graven image"
**Common misinterpretation:** This shows proper worship
**Biblical context:** This is blasphemy - dedicating something to the LORD for the explicit purpose of violating His commands against graven images. It shows complete spiritual confusion.

### "In those days there was no king in Israel, but every man did that which was right in his own eyes"
**Common misinterpretation:* This describes good individual freedom
**Biblical context:** This describes spiritual anarchy and moral relativism. The narrator is explaining why such idolatry and confusion occurred - lack of proper spiritual leadership.
