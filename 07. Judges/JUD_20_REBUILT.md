## CHAPTER SUMMARY

All Israel gathers as one man from Dan to Beer-sheba with Gilead, coming to the LORD at Mizpeh. Four hundred thousand sword-drawing chiefs from all tribes present themselves. Benjamin hears Israel went to Mizpeh. Israel asks how the wickedness happened. The Levite whose concubine was slain testifies he came to Gibeah in Benjamin to lodge. Gibeah's men rose against him, besieging his house by night intending to kill him. They forced his concubine to death. He cut her in pieces and sent her throughout Israel's inheritance because they committed lewdness and folly. He asks Israel's advice and counsel. All the people arise as one man, vowing none will go to tent or house until Gibeah is dealt with. They plan to go against Gibeah by lot, taking ten men per hundred throughout Israel to fetch provisions for battle against Gibeah's folly. All Israel's men gather against Gibeah as one. Israel sends men to Benjamin asking what wickedness was done among them, demanding the sons of Belial in Gibeah for death to remove evil from Israel. Benjamin won't listen to their brethren, gathering from cities to Gibeah to battle Israel. Benjamin has 26,000 sword-drawing men plus 700 chosen Gibeah inhabitants. Among them are 700 chosen left-handed men who can sling stones at hairbreadth without missing. Israel (besides Benjamin) has 400,000 sword-drawing men of war. Israel goes to God's house asking counsel about who should battle Benjamin first. The LORD says Judah first. Israel encamps against Gibeah. Israel battles Benjamin at Gibeah. Benjamin comes forth, destroying 22,000 Israelites that day. Israel encourages themselves and arrays again. Israel weeps before the LORD until evening, asking counsel about battling Benjamin their brother. The LORD says to go up. Israel nears Benjamin the second day. Benjamin comes forth again, destroying 18,000 Israelites. All Israel goes to God's house, weeping, fasting until evening, offering burnt and peace offerings before the LORD. Israel enquires of the LORD (the ark of covenant is there with Phinehas son of Eleazar son of Aaron standing before it), asking whether to battle Benjamin again or cease. The LORD says to go up, for tomorrow He'll deliver Benjamin into their hand. Israel sets ambush around Gibeah. Israel battles Benjamin the third day, arraying as before. Benjamin comes against Israel, drawn from the city, smiting and killing about 30 Israelites on highways to God's house and Gibeah field. Benjamin thinks they're smitten as before. Israel flees to draw Benjamin from city to highways. All Israel rises and arrays at Baal-tamar. Israel's ambush comes from Gibeah meadows. Ten thousand chosen Israelites attack Gibeah in fierce battle; Benjamin doesn't know evil is near. The LORD smites Benjamin before Israel; Israel destroys 25,100 Benjamites that day. Benjamin sees they're smitten as Israel gives place to them, trusting their ambush. The ambush rushes and smites Gibeah with swords. There's a sign between Israel and ambush - great flame with smoke from city. When Israel retreats, Benjamin smites about 30 Israelites, thinking they're winning as before. When flame rises with smoke pillar, Benjamites look back and see city flame ascending to heaven. When Israel turns, Benjamin is amazed seeing evil upon them. They turn backs toward wilderness way, but battle overtakes them; those from cities are destroyed among them. Israel encloses Benjamites, chasing and treading them down from Gibeah toward sunrise. 18,000 Benjamites fall - all men of valor. They flee toward wilderness rock Rimmon; Israel gleans 5,000 on highways, pursuing to Gidom and killing 2,000. Total Benjamin dead that day: 25,000 sword-drawing men of valor. 600 men flee to wilderness rock Rimmon, staying there four months. Israel turns against Benjamin, smiting with sword - men of every city, beasts, and all they find, setting fire to all cities they come to.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "All the children of Israel went out, and the congregation was gathered together as one man"
**Common misinterpretation:** This shows perfect spiritual unity
**Biblical context:** This is unity in judgment against wickedness, not necessarily spiritual unity with God. Israel is united in anger and vengeance, not necessarily in righteousness.

### "The LORD said, Go up; for to morrow I will deliver them into thine hand"
**Common misinterpretation:* This shows unconditional divine approval
**Biblical context:** This comes after Israel's proper repentance, fasting, and inquiry through the ark and Phinehas. God's promise follows their appropriate response to previous defeats.
