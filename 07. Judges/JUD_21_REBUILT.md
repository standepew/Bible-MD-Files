## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Israel's men swore at Mizpeh not to give daughters to Benjamin as wives. The people come to God's house, staying until evening, weeping sorely before God, asking why Israel now lacks one tribe. Next morning they build an altar and offer burnt and peace offerings. Israel asks which tribe didn't come to the congregation at Mizpeh, having sworn death on anyone who didn't come to the LORD at Mizpeh. Israel repents for Benjamin, seeing one tribe cut off that day. They ask how to provide wives for remaining Benjamites since they swore not to give their daughters. They discover no one from Jabesh-gilead came to the assembly. The congregation sends twelve thousand valiant men to smite Jabesh-gilead's inhabitants with sword, including women and children, commanding them to destroy every male and woman who has lain with man. They find four hundred young virgins who haven't known any man, bringing them to Shiloh camp in Canaan. The congregation sends to Benjamin at rock Rimmon, calling peaceably. Benjamin comes and receives wives saved from Jabesh-gilead women, but they're insufficient. The people repent for Benjamin because the LORD made a breach in Israel's tribes. The elders ask how to provide wives for remaining Benjamites since Benjamin's women are destroyed, needing inheritance for escaped Benjamites so the tribe isn't destroyed. They can't give their daughters due to the oath that curses anyone giving a wife to Benjamin. They note there's a yearly LORD feast in Shiloh, north of Beth-el, east of the highway from Beth-el to Shechem, south of Lebonah. They command Benjamin to lie in wait in vineyards, and when Shiloh's daughters come out to dance, catch wives from them and go to Benjamin's land. When fathers or brethren complain, they'll say to be favorable since they didn't reserve wives in war, and the complainers didn't give wives then, avoiding guilt. Benjamin does this, taking wives according to their number from the dancing girls, returning to their inheritance, repairing cities, and dwelling there. Israel departs, each man to his tribe, family, and inheritance. The chapter concludes that in those days there was no king in Israel; every man did what was right in his own eyes.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "There shall not any of us give his daughter unto Benjamin to wife" — Rash Vow

**Judges 21:1** — "Now the men of Israel had sworn in Mizpeh, saying, There shall not any of us give his daughter unto Benjamin to wife"

- **Ecclesiastes 5:4-5** — "When thou vowest a vow unto God, defer not to pay it; for he hath no pleasure in fools: pay that which thou hast vowed. Better is it that thou shouldest not vow, than that thou shouldest vow and not pay."
  - *Interpretation:* Ecclesiastes warns against making vows rashly, which Israel does by swearing not to give daughters to Benjamin, then having to find ways around their own oath.

- **Numbers 30:2** - "If a man vow a vow unto the LORD, or swear an oath to bind his soul with a bond; he shall not break his word, he shall do according to all that proceedeth out of his mouth."
  -Interpretation:* Numbers emphasizes keeping vows, which creates Israel's dilemma when they repent but are bound by their oath.

**Judges 21:22-23** — "Therefore they commanded the children of Benjamin, saying, Go and lie in wait in the vineyards; And see, and, behold, if the daughters of Shiloh come out to dance in dances, then come ye out of the vineyards, and catch you every man his wife of the daughters of Shiloh, and go to the land of Benjamin"

- **Exodus 22:16-17** — "And if a man entice a maid that is not betrothed, and lie with her, he shall surely endow her to be his wife. If her father utterly refuse to give her unto him, he shall pay money according to the dowry of virgins."
  - *Interpretation:* Exodus provides proper procedure for taking a wife, which Benjamin's abduction violates.

- **Deuteronomy 22:28-29** - "If a man find a damsel that is a virgin, which is not betrothed, and lay hold on her, and lie with her, and they be found; Then the man that lay with her shall give unto the damsel's father fifty shekels of silver, and she shall be his wife; because he hath humbled her, he may not put her away all his days."
  -Interpretation:* Deuteronomy requires payment to the father for taking a virgin, which Benjamin's plan bypasses.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### OATH
- **Meaning:** Solemn promise or vow
- **Context:** Israel's vow not to give daughters to Benjamin
- **Significance:** Rash promises create difficult dilemmas
* **New Testament parallel:** Careful speech (James 5:12)

### JABESH-GILEAD
- **Meaning:** "Dry heap of witness"
- **Context:** City punished for not assembling at Mizpeh
- **Significance:** Collective punishment for failure to participate
* **Spiritual application:** Church participation (Hebrews 10:25)

### SHILOH
- **Meaning:** "Tranquil" or "peaceful"
- **Context:** Location of annual feast and tabernacle
- **Significance:** Center of Israelite worship
* **New Testament parallel:** Christ as Shiloh (Genesis 49:10)

### VINEYARDS
- **Meaning:** Agricultural areas for growing grapes
- **Context:** Location for Benjamin's wife abduction
- **Significance:** Places of celebration become places of sin
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual fruitfulness (John 15)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 Now the men of Israel had sworn in Mizpeh, saying, There shall not any of us give his daughter unto Benjamin to wife. 

2 And the people came to the house of God, and abode there till even before God, and lifted up their voices, and wept sore; 

3 And said, O LORD God of Israel, why is this come to pass in Israel, that there should be to day one tribe lacking in Israel? 

4 And it came to pass on the morrow, that the people rose early, and built there an altar, and offered burnt offerings and peace offerings. 

5 And the children of Israel said, Who is there among all the tribes of Israel that came not up with the congregation unto the LORD? For they had made a great oath concerning him that came not up to the LORD to Mizpeh, saying, He shall surely be put to death. 

6 And the children of Israel repented them for Benjamin their brother, and said, There is one tribe cut off from Israel this day. 

7 How shall we do for wives for them that remain, seeing we have sworn by the LORD that we will not give them of our daughters to wives? 

8 And they said, What one is there of the tribes of Israel that came not up to Mizpeh to the LORD? And, behold, there came none to the camp from Jabesh-gilead to the assembly. 

9 For the people were numbered, and, behold, there were none of the inhabitants of Jabesh-gilead there. 

10 And the congregation sent thither twelve thousand men of the valiantest, and commanded them, saying, Go and smite the inhabitants of Jabesh-gilead with the edge of the sword, with the women and the children. 

11 And this is the thing that ye shall do, Ye shall utterly destroy every male, and every woman that hath lain by man. 

12 And they found among the inhabitants of Jabesh-gilead four hundred young virgins, that had known no man by lying with any male: and they brought them unto the camp to Shiloh, which is in the land of Canaan. 

13 And the whole congregation sent some to speak to the children of Benjamin that were in the rock Rimmon, and to call peaceably unto them. 

14 And Benjamin came again at that time; and they gave them wives which they had saved alive of the women of Jabesh-gilead: and yet so they sufficed them not. 

15 And the people repented them for Benjamin, because that the LORD had made a breach in the tribes of Israel. 

16 Then the elders of the congregation said, How shall we do for wives for them that remain, seeing the women are destroyed out of Benjamin? 

17 And there must be an inheritance for them that be escaped of Benjamin, that a tribe be not destroyed out of Israel. 

18 Howbeit we may not give them wives of our daughters: for the children of Israel has sworn, saying, Cursed be he that giveth a wife to Benjamin. 

19 Then they said, Behold, there is a feast of the LORD in Shiloh yearly in a place which is on the north side of Beth-el, on the east side of the highway that goeth up from Beth-el to Shiloh, and on the south of Lebonah. 

20 Therefore they commanded the children of Benjamin, saying, Go and lie in wait in the vineyards; 

21 And see, and, behold, if the daughters of Shiloh come out to dance in dances, then come ye out of the vineyards, and catch you every man his wife of the daughters of Shiloh, and go to the land of Benjamin. 

22 And it should be, when their fathers or their brethren come unto us to complain, that we will say unto them, Be favourable unto them for our sakes: because we reserved not to each man his wife in the war: for ye did not give unto them at this time, that ye should be guilty. 

23 And the children of Benjamin did so, and took them wives, according to their number, of them that danced, whom they caught: and they went and returned unto their inheritance, and repaired the cities, and dwelt in them. 

24 And the children of Israel departed thence at that time, every man to his tribe and to his family, and they went out from thence every man to his inheritance. 

25 In those days there was no king in Israel: every man did that which was right in his own eyes.

**Chapter 21 demonstrates the complications of rash vows and the moral compromises that follow repentance:** Israel's oath not to give daughters to Benjamin shows how emotional decisions in anger can create future problems. Their repentance and weeping before God shows proper remorse for nearly destroying a tribe. The building of an altar and offerings shows appropriate worship in seeking divine guidance. The discovery that Jabesh-gilead didn't assemble provides a "solution" through punishing those who failed to participate in national assembly. The destruction of Jabesh-gilead except for virgins shows collective punishment and the tragic cost of Israel's dilemma. The giving of these virgins to Benjamin provides partial solution but shows the moral compromise of using conquered people as wives. The continued repentance shows Israel's ongoing concern about the tribal breach. The plan to abduct wives at Shiloh shows further moral compromise - using deception and abduction to solve a self-created problem. The justification offered to fathers shows how people rationalize sin when trapped by previous decisions. Benjamin's compliance shows their desperation for survival and restoration. The restoration of Benjamin's cities and inheritance shows the importance of preserving all twelve tribes. The final statement about no king and everyone doing what's right in their own eyes provides the book's concluding explanation for the chaos and moral confusion that characterized this period. This chapter serves as a powerful warning about making rash vows, the danger of collective punishment, the moral compromises that follow emotional decisions, and the chaos that results from lack of proper spiritual leadership. It demonstrates that even sincere repentance can lead to sinful solutions when people are trapped by previous poor decisions. The chapter shows that tribal loyalty can override moral considerations, that religious festivals can become opportunities for sin, and that the absence of proper leadership leads to moral anarchy. It illustrates that the book of Judges ends not with victory but with compromise - Benjamin is preserved but through sinful means, showing the complete breakdown of Israel's spiritual condition before the rise of Samuel. The final statement about everyone doing what's right in their own eyes serves as the book's thesis statement, explaining why the period was characterized by such chaos, violence, and moral confusion. It points forward to the need for proper kingship that will provide the spiritual leadership Israel lacks.
