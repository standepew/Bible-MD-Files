## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Boaz went to the gate and sat down. The kinsman he had spoken of came by, and Boaz called him to turn aside and sit down. He took ten elders of the city and had them sit. Boaz told the kinsman that Naomi, returned from Moab, sells a parcel of land belonging to their brother Elimelech. He thought to advertise him to buy it before inhabitants and elders. If he would redeem it, he should; if not, tell Boaz since there was none to redeem it besides him, and Boaz was after him. The kinsman said he would redeem it. Boaz said that when he buys the field from Naomi, he must also buy Ruth the Moabitess, wife of the dead, to raise up the dead's name on his inheritance. The kinsman said he couldn't redeem it himself lest he mar his own inheritance, telling Boaz to redeem his right for himself since he couldn't redeem it. This was the former manner in Israel for redeeming and changing to confirm things - a man plucked off his shoe and gave it to his neighbor as testimony. The kinsman told Boaz to buy it for himself and drew off his shoe. Boaz told the elders and all people they were witnesses he had bought all that was Elimelech's, Chilion's, and Mahlon's from Naomi's hand. Moreover, he purchased Ruth the Moabitess, Mahlon's wife, to be his wife to raise up the dead's name on his inheritance, that the dead's name not be cut off from his brethren and gate. All the people and elders witnessed. They prayed the LORD make the woman who came to his house like Rachel and Leah who built Israel's house, and that he do worthily in Ephratah and be famous in Bethlehem. May his house be like Pharez's house, whom Tamar bore to Judah, of the seed the LORD would give him of this young woman. Boaz took Ruth as his wife; when he went in to her, the LORD gave her conception, and she bore a son. The women told Naomi the LORD had not left her without a kinsman that day, making his name famous in Israel. He would be a restorer of her life and nourisher of her old age, as her daughter-in-law who loved her, better than seven sons, had born him. Naomi took the child, laid it in her bosom, and became nurse. The women neighbors named him, saying a son was born to Naomi, calling him Obed, father of Jesse, father of David. The generations of Pharez: Pharez begat Hezron, Hezron begat Ram, Ram begat Amminadab, Amminadab begat Nahshon, Nahshon begat Salmon, Salmon begat Boaz, Boaz begat Obed, Obed begat Jesse, Jesse begat David.

### "A man plucked off his shoe, and gave it to his neighbour: and this was a testimony in Israel" — Shoe Testimony

**Ruth 4:7** — "Now this was the manner in former time in Israel concerning redeeming and concerning changing, for to confirm all things; a man plucked off his shoe, and gave it to his neighbour: and this was a testimony in Israel"

- **Deuteronomy 25:9-10** — "Then shall his brother's wife come unto him in the presence of the elders, and loose his shoe from off his foot, and spit in his face, and shall answer and say, So shall it be done unto that man that will not build up his brother's house. And his name shall be called in Israel, The house of him that hath his shoe loosed."
  - *Interpretation:* Deuteronomy establishes the shoe-loosing ceremony for refusing levirate marriage, which Ruth adapts for redemption transactions.

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 Then went Boaz up to the gate, and sat him down there: and, behold, the kinsman of whom Boaz spake came by; unto whom he said, Ho, such a one! turn aside, sit down here. And he turned aside, and sat down. 

2 And he took ten men of the elders of the city, and said, Sit ye down here. And they sat down. 

3 And he said unto the kinsman, Naomi, that is come again out of the country of Moab, selleth a parcel of land, which was our brother Elimelech’s: 

4 And I thought to advertise thee, saying, Buy it before the inhabitants, and before the elders of my people. If thou wilt redeem it, redeem it: but if thou wilt not redeem it, then tell me, that I may know: for there is none to redeem it beside thee; and I am after thee. And he said, I will redeem it. 

5 Then said Boaz, What day thou buyest the field of the hand of Naomi, thou must buy it also of Ruth the Moabitess, the wife of the dead, to raise up the name of the dead upon his inheritance. 

6 And the kinsman said, I cannot redeem it for myself, lest I mar mine own inheritance: redeem thou my right to thyself; for I cannot redeem it. 

7 Now this was the manner in former time in Israel concerning redeeming and concerning changing, for to confirm all things; a man plucked off his shoe, and gave it to his neighbour: and this was a testimony in Israel. 

8 Therefore the kinsman said unto Boaz, Buy it for thee. So he drew off his shoe. 

9 And Boaz said unto the elders, and unto all the people, Ye are witnesses this day, that I have bought all that was Elimelech’s, and all that was Chilion’s and Mahlon’s, of the hand of Naomi. 

10 Moreover Ruth the Moabitess, the wife of Mahlon, have I purchased to be my wife, to raise up the name of the dead upon his inheritance, that the name of the dead be not cut off from among his brethren, and from the gate of his place: ye are witnesses this day. 

11 And all the people that were in the gate, and the elders, said, We are witnesses. The LORD make the woman that is come into thine house like Rachel and like Leah, which two did build the house of Israel: and do thou worthily in Ephratah, and be famous in Beth-lehem: 

12 And let thy house be like the house of Pharez, whom Tamar bare unto Judah, of the seed which the LORD shall give thee of this young woman. 

13 So Boaz took Ruth, and she was his wife: and when he went in unto her, the LORD gave her conception, and she bare a son. 

14 And the women said unto Naomi, Blessed be the LORD, which hath not left thee this day without a kinsman, that his name may be famous in Israel. 

15 And he shall be unto thee a restorer of thy life, and a nourisher of thine old age: for thy daughter in law, which loveth thee, which is better to thee than seven sons, hath born him. 

16 And Naomi took the child, and laid it in her bosom, and became nurse unto it. 

17 And the women her neighbours gave it a name, saying, There is a son born to Naomi; and they called his name Obed: he is the father of Jesse, the father of David. 

18 Now these are the generations of Pharez: Pharez begat Hezron, 

19 And Hezron begat Ram, and Ram begat Amminadab, 

20 And Amminadab begat Nahshon, and Nahshon begat Salmon, 

21 And Salmon begat Boaz, and Boaz begat Obed, 

22 And Obed begat Jesse, and Jesse begat David.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "A man plucked off his shoe, and gave it to his neighbour"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about footwear
**Biblical context:** This is a legal ceremony confirming transactions and transferring authority, similar to modern notarization or signing documents.

### "He is the father of Jesse, the father of David"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is just genealogical information
**Biblical context:** This establishes the crucial connection between Ruth's story and the Davidic line that leads to Christ, showing how God's providence works through ordinary people to accomplish His redemptive plan.
