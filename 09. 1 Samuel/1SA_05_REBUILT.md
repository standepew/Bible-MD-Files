## CHAPTER SUMMARY

The Philistines took the ark of God from Eben-ezer to Ashdod, bringing it into Dagon's house and setting it by Dagon. When Ashdod arose early the next morning, Dagon was fallen face down before the ark. They set Dagon in his place again. The next morning, Dagon was fallen face down before the ark, with head and both palms cut off on the threshold, leaving only the stump. Therefore neither Dagon's priests nor anyone entering his house tread on Dagon's threshold in Ashdod to this day. The LORD's hand was heavy upon Ashdod, destroying and smiting them with emerods (tumors) in Ashdod and its coasts. When Ashdod's men saw this, they said the ark of Israel's God couldn't abide with them since His hand was sore upon them and Dagon their god. They sent and gathered all Philistine lords, asking what to do with Israel's ark. They answered to carry the ark to Gath. They carried Israel's ark to Gath. After carrying it, the LORD's hand was against Gath with very great destruction, smiting men small and great with emerods in their secret parts. They sent the ark to Ekron. When the ark came to Ekron, Ekronites cried out that Israel's ark was brought to slay them and their people. They gathered all Philistine lords, saying to send away Israel's ark to its place to avoid killing them and their people, as there was deadly destruction throughout the city with God's hand very heavy there. Men who didn't die were smitten with emerods, and the city's cry went up to heaven.

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 And the Philistines took the ark of God, and brought it from Eben-ezer unto Ashdod. 

2 When the Philistines took the ark of God, they brought it into the house of Dagon, and set it by Dagon. 

3 And when they of Ashdod arose early on the morrow, behold, Dagon was fallen upon his face to the earth before the ark of the LORD. And they took Dagon, and set him in his place again. 

4 And when they arose early on the morrow morning, behold, Dagon was fallen upon his face to the ground before the ark of the LORD; and the head of Dagon and both the palms of his hands were cut off upon the threshold; only the stump of Dagon was left to him. 

5 Therefore neither the priests of Dagon, nor any that come into Dagon's house, tread on the threshold of Dagon in Ashdod unto this day. 

6 But the hand of the LORD was heavy upon them of Ashdod, and he destroyed them, and smote them with emerods, even Ashdod and the coasts thereof. 

7 And when the men of Ashdod saw that it was so, they said, The ark of the God of Israel shall not abide with us: for his hand is sore upon us, and upon Dagon our god. 

8 They sent therefore and gathered all the lords of the Philistines unto them, and said, What shall we do with the ark of the God of Israel? And they answered, Let the ark of the God of Israel be carried about unto Gath. And they carried the ark of the God of Israel about thither. 

9 And it was so, that, after they had carried it about, the judgment of the LORD was against the city with a very great destruction: and he smote the men of the city, both small and great, and they had emerods in their secret parts. 

10 Wherefore they sent the ark of God to Ekron. And it came to pass, as the ark of God came to Ekron, that the Ekronites cried out, saying, They have brought about the ark of the God of Israel to us, to slay us and our people. 

11 So they sent and gathered together all the lords of the Philistines, and said, Send away the ark of the God of Israel, and let it go again to his own place, that it slay us not, and our people: for there was a deadly destruction throughout all the city; the hand of God was very heavy there. 

12 And the men that died not were smitten with the emerods: and the cry of the the city went up to heaven.

**Chapter 5 demonstrates God's power over false gods and His ability to defend His honor even when His people fail:** The Philistines' capture of the ark seems like victory, but God immediately demonstrates His power. Bringing the ark into Dagon's temple shows the Philistines' attempt to incorporate Israel's God into their religious system, treating Him as just another deity. Dagon falling face down before the ark shows that false gods cannot stand in God's presence. The second day's destruction of Dagon - head and hands cut off - shows complete defeat, removing the idol's ability to think and act. The threshold superstition shows the Philistines' recognition of God's power but misunderstanding of His nature. The emerods in Ashdod show God's judgment on those who disrespect His presence. The progression of the ark through Philistine cities shows that God's judgment follows wherever His presence goes among those who oppose Him. The increasing severity of judgment - from tumors to death - shows God's escalating response to continued disrespect. The Philistines' final decision to return the ark shows their recognition that they cannot control or contain God's power. The cry going up to heaven shows human desperation when facing divine judgment. This chapter establishes crucial themes: God defends His own honor, false gods are powerless before the true God, divine judgment falls on those who disrespect God, and God's presence cannot be contained or controlled by human means. It demonstrates that God is sovereign even when His people are defeated, that His power works independently of human faithfulness, and that He will judge those who treat Him lightly. The chapter shows the complete failure of the Philistine religious system - their god falls, their people suffer, and they must return what they captured. It illustrates the principle that God's presence brings either blessing or judgment depending on human response. The chapter points forward to Christ's victory over all powers and authorities, demonstrating that God's presence defeats all opposition. The progressive movement of the ark through Philistine cities shows that God's power cannot be contained or controlled by human geography or politics.
