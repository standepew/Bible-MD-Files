## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Nahash the Ammonite encamped against Jabesh-gilead. Jabesh men asked Nahash to make covenant and serve him. Nahash agreed on condition of thrusting out all their right eyes as reproach upon Israel. Jabesh elders requested seven days' respite to send messengers throughout Israel's coasts; if no man saved them, they'd come out. Messengers came to Gibeah of Saul, telling tidings to the people who lifted voices and wept. Saul came from the field after the herd, asking why people wept. They told him Jabesh's tidings. The Spirit of God came upon Saul when he heard, and his anger was greatly kindled. He took a yoke of oxen, hewed them in pieces, and sent them throughout Israel's coasts by messengers, saying whoever didn't come after Saul and Samuel would have their oxen treated likewise. The fear of the LORD fell on the people, and they came out with one consent. When numbered at Bezek, Israel had three hundred thousand and Judah thirty thousand. They told Jabesh messengers that tomorrow by sun's heat they'd have help. Messengers showed this to Jabesh men who were glad. Jabesh men said tomorrow they'd come out and the messengers could do what seemed good. The next day, Saul put people in three companies; they came into the host's midst in morning watch, slew Ammonites until heat of day, and scattered the remaining so that two weren't left together. The people told Samuel to bring those who said "Shall Saul reign over us" to put them to death. Saul said no man would die that day since the LORD wrought salvation in Israel. Samuel told people to go to Gilgal and renew the kingdom there. All people went to Gilgal, made Saul king before the LORD, sacrificed peace offerings, and Saul and all Israel rejoiced greatly.

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 Then Nahash the Ammonite came up, and encamped against Jabesh-gilead: and all the men of Jabesh said unto Nahash, Make a covenant with us, and we will serve thee.  

2 And Nahash the Ammonite answered them, On this condition will I make a covenant with you, that I may thrust out all your right eyes, and lay it for a reproach upon all Israel.  

3 And the elders of Jabesh said unto him, Give us seven days' respite, that we may send messengers unto all the coasts of Israel: and then, if there be no man to save us, we will come out to thee.  

4 Then came the messengers to Gibeah of Saul, and told the tidings in the ears of the people: and all the people lifted up their voices, and wept.  

5 And, behold, Saul came after the herd out of the field; and Saul said, What aileth the people that they weep? And they told him the tidings of the men of Jabesh.  

6 And the Spirit of God came upon Saul when he heard those tidings, and his anger was kindled greatly.  

7 And he took a yoke of oxen, and hewed them in pieces, and sent them throughout all the coasts of Israel by the hands of messengers, saying, Whosoever cometh not forth after Saul and after Samuel, so shall it be done unto his oxen. And the fear of the LORD fell on the people, and they came out with one consent.  

8 And when he numbered them in Bezek, the children of Israel were were three hundred thousand, and the men of Judah thirty thousand.  

9 And they said unto the opposers that came, Thus shall ye say unto the men of Jabesh-gilead, To morrow, by that time the sun be hot, ye shall have help. And the messengers came and shewed it to the men of Jabesh; and they were glad.  

10 Therefore the men of Jabesh said, To morrow we will come out unto you, strategy shall do with us all that seemeth good unto you.  

11 And it was so on the morrow, that Saul put the people in three companies; and they came into the midst of the host in the morning watch, and slew the Ammonites until the heat of the day: and it came to pass, that they which remained were scattered, so that two of them were not left together.  

12 And the people said unto Samuel, Who is he that said, Shall Saul reign over us? bring the men, that we may put them to death.  

13 And Saul said, There shall not a man be put to death this day: for to day the LORD hath wrought salvation in Israel.  

14 Then said Samuel to the people, Come, and let us go to Gilgal, and renew the kingdom there.  

15 And all the people went to Gilgal; and there they made Saul king before the LORD in Gilgal; and there they sacrificed sacrifices of peace offerings before the LORD; and the LORD; and there Saul and all the men of Israel rejoiced greatly.

The dramatic action of cutting oxen shows urgent mobilization and serious consequences for non-participation. The fear of the LORD falling on people shows divine response to decisive leadership. The large army numbering shows Israel's unity and strength under proper leadership. The promise of help by sun's heat shows confidence in divine timing. The three-company strategy shows military wisdom. The morning watch attack shows tactical advantage. The complete victory shows divine blessing on the effort. The scattering so none remained together shows total defeat of the enemy. The people's desire to execute opponents shows loyalty to successful leadership. Saul's mercy shows recognition that the day was about God's salvation, not personal vengeance. Samuel's call to Gilgal shows proper spiritual response to victory. The kingdom renewal shows formal acknowledgment of God's working through human leadership. The sacrifices show proper worship and gratitude. The great rejoicing shows national unity and celebration.
