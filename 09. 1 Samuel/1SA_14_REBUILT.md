## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Jonathan son of Saul told his armourbearer to go to Philistine garrison on other side without telling his father. Saul tarried in Gibeah's uttermost part under pomegranate tree in Migron with about six hundred men. Ahiah son of Ahitub, I-chabod's brother, Phinehas' son, Eli's son, LORD's priest in Shiloh, wore ephod. People didn't know Jonathan was gone. Between passages Jonathan sought to cross to Philistine garrison, there were sharp rocks - Bozez and Seneh. One forefront was northward over against Michmash, other southward over against Gibeah. Jonathan told his armourbearer to go to uncircumcised garrison, saying LORD might work for them since no restraint to LORD to save by many or few. Armourbearer said to do what was in his heart and he'd go according to his heart. Jonathan said they'd pass over and discover themselves. If Philistines said "Tarry until we come," they'd stand still. If they said "Come up," they'd go up for LORD delivered them into their hand - this would be sign. Both discovered themselves to Philistine garrison. Philistines said Hebrews came from holes where they hid. Garrison answered Jonathan and armourbearer to come up and show them something. Jonathan told armourbearer to come up after him for LORD delivered them into Israel's hand. Jonathan climbed on hands and feet, armourbearer after him. They fell before Jonathan; armourbearer slew after him. First slaughter Jonathan and armourbearer made was about twenty men within half acre land that yoke of oxen might plow. There was trembling in host, field, and among all people - garrison and spoilers also trembled, earth quaked in very great trembling. Saul's watchmen in Gibeah looked; multitude melted away beating one another. Saul told people with him to number and see who was gone. When numbered, Jonathan and armourbearer weren't there. Saul told Ahiah to bring ark of God (ark was with Israel's children). While Saul talked to priest, noise in Philistine host increased. Saul told priest to withdraw hand. Saul and people assembled and came to battle; every man's sword was against fellow in very great discomfiture. Hebrews with Philistines before that time, who went up with them into camp from country, also turned to be with Israelites with Saul and Jonathan. All Israel men who hid in mount Ephraim, when they heard Philistines fled, also followed hard after them in battle. LORD saved Israel that day; battle passed over to Beth-aven. Israel was distressed that day for Saul had adjured people saying "Cursed be man that eateth any food until evening, that I may be avenged on mine enemies." No people tasted food. All land came to wood; there was honey on ground. When people came into wood, honey dropped but no man put hand to mouth for people feared oath. Jonathan didn't hear father charge people with oath; he put forth rod end in hand, dipped in honeycomb, put hand to mouth, and eyes were enlightened. One of people answered that father straitly charged people with oath saying "Cursed be man that eateth any food this day." People were faint. Jonathan said father troubled land; see how eyes enlightened because he tasted little honey. How much more if people had eaten freely of spoil from enemies? Would there not have been much greater slaughter among Philistines? They smote Philistines from Michmash to Aijalon; people were very faint. People flew upon spoil, took sheep, oxen, calves, slew them on ground, and ate with blood. They told Saul saying people sinned against LORD eating with blood. He said they transgressed; roll great stone to him that day. Saul told people to disperse and bring every man his ox and sheep, slay them there and eat, not sin against LORD eating with blood. All people brought every man his ox that night and slew them there. Saul built altar to LORD - first altar he built to LORD. Saul said to go down after Philistines by night, spoil them until morning light, not leave man of them. They said do what seemed good to him. Priest said draw near to God. Saul asked counsel of God about going after Philistines and deliverance. God answered him not that day. Saul told chief people to know and see wherein sin was that day. For as LORD liveth which saveth Israel, though it be in Jonathan his son, he shall surely die. No man among people answered him. He told all Israel to be on one side, he and Jonathan son on other side. People said do what seemed good to Saul. Saul told LORD God of Israel to give perfect lot. Saul and Jonathan were taken; people escaped. Saul said cast lots between him and Jonathan son. Jonathan was taken. Saul told Jonathan to tell what he had done. Jonathan told him he tasted little honey with rod end in hand, and must die. Saul answered God do so and more also; Jonathan shall surely die. People told Saul shall Jonathan die who wrought this great salvation in Israel? God forbid - as LORD liveth, not one hair of his head shall fall to ground for he wrought with God that day. People rescued Jonathan that he died not. Saul went up following Philistines; Philistines went to their own place. Saul took kingdom over Israel, fought against all enemies on every side - Moab, children of Ammon, Edom, kings of Zobah, and Philistines. Wheresoever he turned, he vexed them. He gathered host and smote Amalekites, delivering Israel from their hands that spoiled them. Saul's sons were Jonathan, Ishui, and Melchi-shua. His two daughters: firstborn Merab, younger Michal. Saul's wife was Ahinoam, daughter of Ahimaaz. Captain of his host was Abner, son of Ner, Saul's uncle. Kish was father of Saul; Ner father of Abner was son of Abiel. There was sore war against Philistines all Saul's days; when Saul saw strong or valiant man, he took him to him.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### BOZEZ AND SENEH
- **Meaning:** "Sharpness" and "Sharp tooth"
- **Context:** Two sharp rocks Jonathan climbed
- **Significance:** Difficult obstacles overcome through faith
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual obstacles (Ephesians 6:12)

### MIGRON
- **Meaning:** "Precipice"
- **Context:** Location where Saul waited
- **Significance:** Position of inaction while God worked
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual positioning (Psalm 1:1)

### HONEYCOMB
- **Meaning:** Wax structure containing honey
- **Context:** Source of Jonathan's enlightenment
- **Significance:** Natural provision bringing understanding
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sweetness (Psalm 119:103)

### PERFECT LOT
- **Meaning:** Urim and Thummim for divine decision
- **Context:** Method to determine guilt
- **Significance:** Divine guidance in difficult decisions
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual discernment (James 1:5)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 Now it came to pass upon a day, that Jonathan the son of Saul said unto the young man that bare his armour, Come, and let us go over to the Philistines' garrison, that is on the other side. But he told not his father. 

2 And Saul tarried in the uttermost part of Gibeah under a pomegranate tree which is in Migron: and the people that were with him were about six hundred men; 

3 And Ahiah, the son of Ahitub, I-chabod's brother, the son of Phinehas, the son of Eli, the LORD's priest in Shiloh, wearing an ephod. And the people knew not that Jonathan was gone. 

4 And between the passages, by which Jonathan sought to go over unto the Philistines' garrison, there was a sharp rock on the one side, and a sharp rock on the other side: and the name of the one was Bozez, and the name of the other Seneh. 

5 The forefront of the one was situate northward over against Michmash, and the other southward over against Gibeah. 

6 And Jonathan said to the young man that bare his armour, Come, and let us go over unto the garrison of these uncircumcised: it may be that the LORD will work for us: for there is no restraint to the LORD to save by many or by few. 

7 And his armourbearer said unto him, Do all that is in thine heart: turn thee; behold, I am with thee according to thy heart. 

8 Then said Jonathan, Behold, we will pass over unto these men, and we will discover ourselves unto them. 

9 If they say thus unto us, Tarry until we come to you; then we will stand still in our place, and will not go up unto them. 

10 But if they say thus, Come up unto us; then we will go up: for the LORD hath delivered them into our hand: and this shall be a sign unto us. 

11 And both of them discovered themselves unto the garrison of the Philistines: and the Philistines said, Behold, the Hebrews come forth out of the holes where they had hid themselves. 

12 And the men of the garrison answered Jonathan and his armourbearer, and said, Come up to us, and we will shew you a thing. And Jonathan said unto his armourbearer, Come up after me: for the LORD hath delivered them into the hand of Israel. 

13 And Jonathan climbed up upon his hands and upon his feet, and his armourbearer after him: and they fell before Jonathan; and his armourbearer slew after him. 

14 And that first slaughter, which Jonathan and his armourbearer made, was about twenty men, within as it were an half acre of land, which a yoke of oxen might plow. 

15 And there was trembling in the host, in the field, and among all the people: the garrison, and the spoilers, they also trembled, and the earth quaked: so it was a very great trembling. 

16 And the watchmen of Saul in Gibeah of Benjamin looked; and, behold, the multitude melted away, and they went on beating down one another. 

17 Then said Saul unto the people that were with him, Number now, and see who is gone from us. And when they had numbered, behold, Jonathan and his armourbearer were not there. 

18 And Saul said unto Ahiah, Bring hither the ark of God. For the ark of God was at that time with the children of Israel. 

19 And it came to pass, while Saul talked unto the priest, that the noise that was in the host of the Philistines went on and increased: and Saul said unto the priest, Withdraw thine hand. 

20 And Saul and all the people that were with him assembled themselves, and they came to the battle: and, behold, every man's sword was against his fellow, and there was a very great discomfiture. 

21 Moreover the Hebrews that were with the Philistines before that time, which went up with them into the camp from the country round about, even they also turned to be with the Israelites that were with Saul and Jonathan. 

22 Likewise all the men of Israel which had hid themselves in mount Ephraim, when they heard that the Philistines fled, even they also followed hard after them in the battle. 

23 So the LORD saved Israel that day: and the battle passed over unto Beth-aven. 

24 And the men of Israel were distressed that day: for Saul had adjured the people, saying, Cursed be the man that eateth any food until evening, that I may be avenged on mine enemies. So none of the people tasted any food. 

25 And all they of the land came to a wood; and there was honey upon the ground. 

26 And when the people were come into the wood, behold, the honey dropped; but no man put his hand to his mouth: for the people feared the oath. 

27 But Jonathan heard not when his father charged the people with an oath: wherefore he put forth the end of the rod that was in his hand, and dipped it in an honeycomb, and put his hand to his mouth; and his eyes were enlightened. 

28 Then answered one of the people, and said, Thy father straitly charged the people with an oath, saying, Cursed be the man that eateth any food this day. And the people were faint. 

29 Then said Jonathan, My father hath troubled the land: see, I pray you, how mine eyes have been enlightened, because I tasted a little of this honey. 

30 How much more, if haply the people had eaten freely to day of the spoil of their enemies which they found? for had there not been now a much greater slaughter among the Philistines? 

31 And they smote the Philistines that day from Michmash to Aijalon: and the people were very faint. 

32 And the people flew upon the spoil, and took sheep, and oxen, and calves, and slew them on the ground: and the people did eat them with the blood. 

33 Then they told Saul, saying, Behold, the people sin against the LORD, in that they eat with the blood. And he said, Ye have transgressed: roll a great stone unto me this day. 

34 And Saul said, Disperse yourselves among the people, and say unto them, Bring me hither every man his ox, and every man his sheep, and slay them here, and eat; and sin not against the LORD in eating with the blood. And all the people brought every man his ox with him that night, and slew them there. 

35 And Saul built an altar unto the LORD: the same was the first altar that he built unto the LORD. 

36 And Saul said, Let us go down after the Philistines by night, and spoil them until the morning light, and let us not leave a man of them. And they said, Do whatsoever seemeth good unto thee. Then said the priest, Let us draw near hither unto God. 

37 And Saul asked counsel of God, Shall I go down after the Philistines? wilt thou deliver them into the hand of Israel? But he answered him not that day. 

38 And Saul said, Draw ye near hither, all the chief of the people: and know and see wherein this sin hath been this day. 

39 For, as the LORD liveth, which saveth Israel, though it be in Jonathan my son, he shall surely die. But there was not a man among all the people that answered him. 

40 Then said he unto all Israel, Be ye on one side, and I and Jonathan my son will be on the other side. And the people said unto Saul, Do what seemeth good unto thee. 

41 Therefore Saul said unto the LORD God of Israel, Give a perfect lot. And Saul and Jonathan were taken: but the people escaped. 

42 And Saul said, Cast lots between me and Jonathan my son. And Jonathan was taken. 

43 Then Saul said to Jonathan, Tell me what thou hast done. And Jonathan told him, and said, I did but taste a little honey with the end of the rod that was in mine hand, and, lo, I must die. 

44 And Saul answered, God do so and more also: for thou shalt surely die, Jonathan. 

45 And the people said unto Saul, Shall Jonathan die, who hath wrought this great salvation in Israel? God forbid: as the LORD liveth, there shall not one hair of his head fall to the ground: for he hath wrought with God this day. So the people rescued Jonathan, that he died not.  

46 Then Saul went up from following the Philistines: and the Philistines went to their own place.  

47 So Saul took the kingdom over Israel, and fought against all his enemies on every side, against Moab, and against the children of Ammon, and against Edom, and against the kings of Zobah, and against the Philistines: and whithersoever he turned himself, he vexed them.  

48 And he gathered an host, and smote the Amalekites, and delivered Israel out of the hands of them that spoiled them.  

49 Now the sons of Saul were Jonathan, and Ishui, and Melchi-shua: and the names of his two daughters were these; the name of the firstborn Merab, and the name of the younger Michal:  

50 And the name of Saul's wife was Ahinoam, the daughter of Ahimaaz: and the name of the captain of his host was Abner, the son of Ner, Saul's uncle.  

51 And Kish was the father of Saul; and Ner the father of Abner was the son of Abiel.  

52 And there was sore war against the Philistines all the days of Saul: and when Saul saw any strong man, or any valiant man, he took him unto him.

**Chapter 14 demonstrates the power of faith contrasted with human weakness and foolishness:** Jonathan's initiative to attack Philistine garrison shows faith that acts without waiting for human permission. His statement that God can save by many or few shows proper understanding of divine power. The armourbearer's support shows faithful companionship. The plan to discover themselves and use Philistine response as divine sign shows wisdom in seeking God's guidance. The difficult climb up sharp rocks shows obstacles overcome through faith. The victory over twenty men in small space shows God's power working through faithful few. The great trembling throughout host shows God's supernatural intervention affecting both sides. Saul's inaction under pomegranate tree shows missed opportunity through fear. The watchmen seeing multitude melt away shows divine intervention beyond human control. Saul's concern about missing people shows leadership awareness but also anxiety. Calling for the ark shows religious response but lack of spiritual understanding. The noise increase shows enemy confidence turning to fear through divine action. Saul's withdrawal of priest shows impatience in spiritual matters. The discomfiture among Israel shows confusion when acting without divine guidance. The Hebrews and hidden men joining battle shows God's people responding to divine victory. The distress over Saul's foolish oath shows human weakness in leadership. The honey availability shows God's provision during battle. Jonathan's enlightenment through tasting honey shows how small disobedience can bring understanding. His reasoning about greater victory if people had eaten shows wisdom beyond human restrictions. The people's faintness shows consequences of foolish leadership. The sin of eating with blood shows failure to follow divine law. Saul's altar building shows religious response to crisis. The silence from God when seeking counsel shows divine displeasure. The perfect lot showing Jonathan guilty confirms his disobedience. The people's rescue of Jonathan shows recognition of God's work through him. Saul's subsequent military success shows that God can work through flawed leaders. The chapter establishes crucial themes: faith in God brings victory regardless of numbers, human restrictions can hinder divine work, small disobedience can bring understanding, and God protects those who work with Him. It demonstrates that initiative faith often precedes divine blessing, that human wisdom can limit God's power, and that God's purposes continue despite human failure. The chapter shows Jonathan as model of faith - acting on divine opportunity, trusting God's power, and experiencing supernatural victory. It illustrates the principle that God works through those who trust Him, that human restrictions often hinder divine work, and that divine protection extends to those who work with God. The chapter points forward to Christ as the perfectly faithful One who always acts in divine timing and power, whose obedience brings complete victory. The honey enlightenment points forward to Christ as the true enlightenment that brings spiritual understanding. The chapter demonstrates that faith combined with divine power brings supernatural victory, that human foolishness can hinder but not prevent God's purposes, and that God protects and uses those who work with Him regardless of human limitations.
