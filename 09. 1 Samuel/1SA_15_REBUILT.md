## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Samuel told Saul the LORD sent him to anoint Saul king over Israel, so hearken to LORD's words. The LORD of hosts said He remembered what Amalek did to Israel, laying wait in way when Israel came from Egypt. He told Saul to go smite Amalekites, utterly destroying all and sparing none - slay man, woman, infant, suckling, ox, sheep, camel, and ass. Saul gathered people, numbering them in Telaim: two hundred thousand footmen and ten thousand men of Judah. Saul came to Amalekite city, laid wait in valley. Saul told Kenites to depart from among Amalekites lest he destroy them with them, for they showed kindness to Israel's children when they came from Egypt. Kenites departed from among Amalekites. Saul smote Amalekites from Havilah to Shur over against Egypt. He took Agag king of Amalekites alive, utterly destroying all people with sword edge. But Saul and people spared Agag and best of sheep, oxen, fatlings, and lambs - all that was good, refusing to utterly destroy them, but utterly destroying vile and refuse things. The LORD's word came to Samuel saying it repented Him that He set up Saul as king, for he turned back from following Him and didn't perform His commandments. Samuel grieved and cried to LORD all night. When Samuel rose early to meet Saul in morning, he was told Saul came to Carmel, set up place, gone about, passed on, and gone down to Gilgal. Samuel came to Saul; Saul said "Blessed be thou of LORD: I have performed LORD's commandment." Samuel asked what meant bleating of sheep in his ears and lowing of oxen he heard. Saul said they brought them from Amalekites, for people spared best of sheep and oxen to sacrifice to LORD his God, and rest they utterly destroyed. Samuel told Saul to stay and he'd tell what LORD said that night. He said "Say on." Samuel asked when Saul was little in his own sight, wasn't he made head of Israel's tribes, and LORD anointed him king over Israel? The LORD sent him on journey saying "Go and utterly destroy sinning Amalekites, fight against them until consumed." Therefore he didn't obey LORD's voice but flew upon spoil and did evil in LORD's sight. Saul said he obeyed LORD's voice, went way LORD sent him, brought Agag king of Amalekites, and utterly destroyed Amalekites. But people took of spoil sheep and oxen, chief things which should have been utterly destroyed, to sacrifice to LORD his God in Gilgal. Samuel said "Has LORD as great delight in burnt offerings and sacrifices, as in obeying LORD's voice? Behold, to obey is better than sacrifice, and to hearken than fat of rams. For rebellion is as sin of witchcraft, and stubbornness as iniquity and idolatry. Because you rejected LORD's word, He has also rejected you from being king." Saul said "I have sinned: I have transgressed LORD's commandment and your words: because I feared people and obeyed their voice. Now therefore, I pray thee, pardon my sin, and turn again with me, that I may worship LORD." Samuel said "I will not return with thee: for you have rejected LORD's word, and LORD has rejected you from being king over Israel." As Samuel turned to go away, he laid hold on his mantle skirt and it rent. Samuel said "The LORD has rent kingdom of Israel from you this day, and has given it to a neighbour of yours, that is better than you. Also Strength of Israel will not lie nor repent: for He is not a man, that He should repent." Then he said "I have sinned: yet honour me now, I pray thee, before elders of my people, and before Israel, and turn again with me, that I may worship LORD your God." So Samuel turned again after Saul; and Saul worshipped LORD. Then Samuel said "Bring hither Agag king of Amalekites." Agag came to him delicately. Agag said "Surely bitterness of death is past." Samuel said "As your sword made women childless, so shall your mother be childless among women." Samuel hewed Agag in pieces before LORD in Gilgal. Then Samuel went to Ramah; Saul went up to his house to Gibeah of Saul. Samuel came no more to see Saul until his death; nevertheless Samuel mourned for Saul, and LORD repented that He had made Saul king over Israel.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Go and smite Amalek, and utterly destroy all that they have, and spare them not" — Complete Destruction

**1 Samuel 15:3** — "Now go and smite Amalek, and utterly destroy all that they have, and spare them not; but slay both man and woman, infant and suckling, ox and sheep, camel and ass"

- **Deuteronomy 25:17-19** — "But thou shalt utterly destroy them; namely, the Hittites, and the Girgashites, and the Amorites, and the Canaanites, and the Perizzites, and the Hivites, and the Jebusites; as the LORD thy God hath commanded thee... Therefore thou shalt keep all this commandment which I command thee this day, to do them... But thou shalt utterly destroy them; namely, the Hittites, and the Girgashites, and the Amorites, and the Canaanites, and the Perizzites, and the Hivites, and the Jebusites: lest they teach you to do after all their abominations, which they have done unto their gods; lest ye sin against the LORD your God."
  - *Interpretation:* Deuteronomy commands complete destruction of Canaanite nations including Amalek, showing this as divine command.

- **Exodus 17:14-16** - "But I will be at thy side, and the Amalekites and the Canaanites shall be cut off... And it shall be a token upon thine hand, and a memorial between thine eyes; that the LORD's law may be in thy mouth: for with a strong hand hath the LORD brought thee out of Egypt."
  -Interpretation:* God promised to destroy Amalek as memorial of His deliverance from Egypt.

### "It repenteth me that I have set up Saul to be king" — Divine Regret

**1 Samuel 15:11** — "And it came to pass, that night, that the word of the LORD came unto Samuel, saying, It repenteth me that I have set up Saul to be king: for he is turned back from following me, and hath not performed my commandments"

- **Genesis 6:6** — "And it repented the LORD that he had made man on the earth, and it grieved him at his heart."
  - *Interpretation:* God also repented making man before the flood, showing divine sorrow over human failure.

- **Jonah 3:10** - "And God saw their works, that they turned from their evil way; and God repented of the evil, that he had said that he would do unto them; and he did it not."
  -Interpretation:* God repented of judgment when Nineveh repented, showing divine response to human change.

### "To obey is better than sacrifice, and to hearken than the fat of rams" — Obedience Over Ritual

**1 Samuel 15:22** — "And Samuel said, Hath the LORD as great delight in burnt offerings and sacrifices, as in obeying the voice of the LORD? Behold, to obey is better than sacrifice, and to hearken than the fat of rams"

- **Psalm 51:16-17** — "For thou desirest not sacrifice; else would I give it: thou delightest not in burnt offering. The sacrifices of God are a broken spirit: a broken and a contrite heart, O God, thou wilt not despise."
  - *Interpretation:* Psalm teaches that God desires broken heart over sacrifices, confirming Samuel's principle.

- **Isaiah 1:11** - "To what purpose is the multitude of your sacrifices unto me? saith the LORD: I am full of the burnt offerings of rams, and the fat of fed beasts; and I delight not in the blood of bullocks, or of lambs, or of he goats."
  -Interpretation:* Isaiah also teaches that God rejects sacrifices without obedience, confirming this principle.

### "The LORD hath rent the kingdom of Israel from thee this day, and hath given it to a neighbour of thine" — Kingdom Transfer

**1 Samuel 15:28** — "And Samuel said unto him, The LORD hath rent the kingdom of Israel from thee this day, and hath given it to a neighbour of thine, that is better than thou"

- **1 Samuel 16:13** — "Then Samuel took the horn of oil, and anointed him in the midst of his brethren: and the Spirit of the LORD came upon David from that day forward. So Samuel rose up, and went to Ramah."
  - *Interpretation:* Samuel immediately anoints David as replacement, fulfilling the transfer to "neighbour better than thou."

- **Acts 13:22** - "And when he had removed him, he raised up unto them David to be their king; to whom also he gave testimony, and said, I have found David the son of Jesse, a man after mine own heart, which shall fulfil all my will."
  -Interpretation:* Paul confirms God chose David as man after His own heart to replace Saul.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### AMALEKITES
- **Meaning:** Descendants of Amalek, Esau's grandson
- **Context:** Nation commanded to be utterly destroyed
- **Significance:** Represents ongoing spiritual opposition
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual enemies (Ephesians 6:12)

### AGAG
- **Meaning:** "Flame"
- **Context:** King of Amalekites spared by Saul
- **Significance:** Represents incomplete obedience
* **Spiritual application:** Partial obedience (James 2:10)

### REBELLION
- **Meaning:** Willful resistance to authority
- **Context:** Compared to witchcraft and idolatry
- **Significance:** Seriousness of disobedience
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual rebellion (1 Samuel 15:23)

### WITCHCRAFT
- **Meaning:** Sorcery, occult practices
- **Context:** Compared to rebellion against God
- **Significance:** Spiritual danger of disobedience
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual warfare (Ephesians 6:12)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 Samuel also said unto Saul, The LORD sent me to anoint thee to be king over his people, over Israel: now therefore hearken thou unto the voice of the words of the LORD.  

2 Thus saith the LORD of hosts, I remember that which Amalek did to Israel, how he laid wait for him in the way, when he came up from Egypt.  

3 Now go and smite Amalek, and utterly destroy all that they have, and spare them not; but slay both man and woman, infant and suckling, ox and sheep, camel and ass.  

4 And Saul gathered the people together, and numbered them in Telaim, two hundred thousand footmen, and ten thousand men of Judah.  

5 And Saul came to a city of Amalek, and laid wait in the valley.  

6 And Saul said unto the Kenites, Go, depart, get you down from among the Amalekites, lest I destroy you with them: for ye shewed kindness to all the children of Israel, when they came up out of Egypt. So the Kenites departed from among the Amalekites.  

7 And Saul smote the Amalekites from Havilah until thou comest to Shur, that is over against Egypt.  

8 And he took Agag the king of the Amalekites alive, and utterly destroyed all the people with the edge of the sword.  

9 But Saul and the people spared Agag, and the best of the sheep, and of the oxen, and of the fatlings, and of the lambs, and all that was good, and would not utterly destroy them: but every thing that was vile and refuse, that they destroyed utterly.  

10 Then came the word of the LORD unto Samuel, saying,  

11 It repenteth me that I have set up Saul to be king: for he is turned back from following me, and hath not performed my commandments. And it grieved Samuel; and he cried unto the LORD all night.  

12 And when Samuel rose early to meet Saul in the morning, it was told Samuel, saying, Saul came to Carmel, and, behold, he set him up a place, and is gone about, and passed on, and gone down to Gilgal.  

13 And Samuel came to Saul: and Saul said unto him, Blessed be thou of the LORD: I have performed the commandment of the LORD.  

14 And Samuel said, What meaneth then this bleating of the sheep in mine ears, and the lowing of the oxen which I hear?  

15 And Saul said, They have brought them from the Amalekites: for the people spared the best of the sheep and of the oxen, to sacrifice unto the LORD thy God; and the rest we have utterly destroyed.  

16 Then Samuel said unto Saul, Stay, and I will tell thee what the LORD hath said to me this night. And he said unto him, Say on.  

17 And Samuel said, When thou wast little in thine own sight, wast thou not made the head of the tribes of Israel, and the LORD anointed thee king over Israel?  

18 And the LORD sent thee on a journey, and said, Go and utterly destroy the sinners the Amalekites, and fight against them until they be consumed.  

19 Wherefore then didst thou not obey the voice of the LORD, but didst fly upon the spoil, and didst evil in the sight of the LORD?  

20 And Saul said unto Samuel, Yea, I have obeyed the voice of the LORD, and have gone the way which the LORD sent me, and have brought Agag the king of the Amalekites, and have utterly destroyed the Amalekites.  

21 But the people took of the spoil, sheep and oxen, the chief of the things which should have been utterly destroyed, to sacrifice unto the LORD thy God in Gilgal.  

22 And Samuel said, Hath the LORD as great delight in burnt offerings and sacrifices, as in obeying the voice of the LORD? Behold, to obey is better than sacrifice, and to hearken than the fat of rams.  

23 For rebellion is as the sin of witchcraft, and stubbornness is as iniquity and idolatry. Because thou hast rejected the word of the LORD, he hath also rejected thee from being king.  

24 And Saul said unto Samuel, I have sinned: for I have transgressed the commandment of the LORD, and thy words: because I feared the people, and obeyed their voice.  

25 Now therefore, I pray thee, pardon my sin, and turn again with me, that I may worship the LORD.  

26 And Samuel said unto Saul, I will not return with thee: for thou hast rejected the word of the LORD, and the LORD hath rejected thee from being king over Israel.  

27 And as Samuel turned about to go away, he laid hold upon the skirt of his mantle, and it rent.  

28 And Samuel said unto him, The LORD hath rent the kingdom of Israel from thee this day, and hath given it to a neighbour of thine, that is better than thou.  

29 And also the Strength of Israel will not lie nor repent: for he is not a man, that he should repent.  

30 Then he said, I have sinned: yet honour me now, I pray thee, before the elders of my people, and before Israel, and turn again with me, that I may worship the LORD thy God.  

31 So Samuel turned again after Saul; and Saul worshipped the LORD.  

32 Then said Samuel, Bring ye hither to me Agag the king of the Amalekites. And Agag came unto him delicately. And Agag said, Surely the bitterness of death is past.  

33 And Samuel said, As thy sword hath made women childless, so shall thy mother be childless among women. And Samuel hewed Agag in pieces before the LORD in Gilgal.  

34 Then Samuel went to Ramah; and Saul went up to his house to Gibeah of Saul.  

35 And Samuel came no more to see Saul until the day of his death: nevertheless Samuel mourned for Saul: and the LORD repented that he had made Saul king over Israel.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "To obey is better than sacrifice, and to hearken than the fat of rams"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about preferring one over the other
**Biblical context:** This is about God's priority - obedience to His commands matters more than religious rituals, showing that heart attitude matters more than external worship.

### "The LORD hath rent the kingdom of Israel from thee this day"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about political change
**Biblical context:** This is about divine judgment - God removing His blessing due to disobedience, showing spiritual consequences of rebellion.

**Chapter 15 demonstrates the serious consequences of partial obedience and rebellion against God's commands:** Samuel's reminder to Saul about his anointing shows divine calling requires complete obedience. The command to utterly destroy Amalek shows God's judgment on those who opposed Israel in their weakness. Saul's gathering of large army shows his military capability but also his tendency to rely on human strength. The protection of Kenites shows recognition of past kindness and distinction between different groups. Saul's partial victory and sparing of Agag shows incomplete obedience - keeping what seemed good while destroying only what seemed vile. The bleating sheep and lowing oxen show evidence of disobedience reaching Samuel. Saul's claim of obedience shows self-deception and rationalization of sin. Samuel's rebuke reveals that God values obedience over sacrifice, rebellion as witchcraft, and stubbornness as idolatry. The rent kingdom shows divine judgment is irrevocable - once rejected, the blessing doesn't return. The statement that Strength of Israel won't repent shows God's unchangeable character. Saul's confession shows recognition of sin but too late for kingdom restoration. Samuel's continued mourning shows his compassion despite judgment. Agag's execution shows complete fulfillment of God's command through Samuel. Samuel's final separation from Saul shows the painful necessity of divine judgment. This chapter establishes crucial themes: partial obedience equals disobedience, God's commands require complete fulfillment, rebellion has serious spiritual consequences, and divine judgment is final. It demonstrates that religious activity without obedience is worthless, that human rationalization doesn't excuse sin, and that God's standards don't change. The chapter shows Samuel as faithful prophet who delivers both God's commands and judgment, that leadership requires complete obedience not partial compliance, and that divine calling can be removed through disobedience. It illustrates the principle that God's standards are absolute, that partial obedience is rebellion, and that divine judgment comes when leaders persist in disobedience. The chapter points forward to Christ as the perfectly obedient King who completely fulfills God's will, whose obedience provides perfect salvation. The statement "to obey is better than sacrifice" points forward to Christ's perfect obedience that brings eternal redemption. The chapter demonstrates that when leaders persist in partial obedience and rebellion, God removes His blessing and transfers it to those who will obey Him completely.
