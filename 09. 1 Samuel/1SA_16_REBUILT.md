## CHAPTER SUMMARY

The LORD told Samuel how long he'd mourn for Saul since He rejected him from reigning over Israel, telling him to fill horn with oil and go to Jesse the Beth-lehemite for He had provided a king among his sons. Samuel asked how he could go since Saul would kill him if he heard. The LORD told him to take a heifer and say he came to sacrifice to the LORD, call Jesse to sacrifice, and He would show him what to do and whom to anoint. Samuel did as LORD spoke, came to Beth-lehem. Town elders trembled at his coming, asking if he came peaceably. He said peaceably, came to sacrifice to the LORD, told them to sanctify themselves and come with him to sacrifice. He sanctified Jesse and his sons, called them to sacrifice. When they came, he looked on Eliab and said surely the LORD's anointed was before him. But the LORD told Samuel not to look on countenance or height of stature since He had refused him; the LORD doesn't see as man sees - man looks on outward appearance, but the LORD looks on the heart. Jesse called Abinadab, made him pass before Samuel. Samuel said the LORD hadn't chosen him. Jesse made Shammah pass by. Samuel said the LORD hadn't chosen him. Again Jesse made seven of his sons pass before Samuel. Samuel told Jesse the LORD hadn't chosen these. Samuel asked if all Jesse's children were there. Jesse said the youngest remained, keeping sheep. Samuel told Jesse to send and fetch him since they wouldn't sit down till he came. He sent and brought him in. He was ruddy with beautiful countenance and goodly to look to. The LORD said to arise and anoint him, for this was he. Samuel took horn of oil and anointed him in midst of his brethren. The Spirit of the LORD came upon David from that day forward. Samuel rose up and went to Ramah. But the Spirit of the LORD departed from Saul, and an evil spirit from the LORD troubled him. Saul's servants told him an evil spirit from God troubled him. They asked their lord to command servants to seek a cunning harp player, so when evil spirit from God was upon him, he would play with his hand and Saul would be well. Saul told servants to provide a man who could play well and bring him. One servant answered he had seen a son of Jesse the Beth-lehemite who was cunning in playing, a mighty valiant man, a man of war, prudent in matters, comely person, and the LORD was with him. Saul sent messengers to Jesse saying send David his son who was with sheep. Jesse took an ass laden with bread, bottle of wine, and a kid, sending them by David his son to Saul. David came to Saul and stood before him; Saul loved him greatly and he became his armourbearer. Saul sent to Jesse saying let David stand before him since he found favor in his sight. When evil spirit from God was upon Saul, David took harp and played with his hand, so Saul was refreshed and was well, and the evil spirit departed from him.

- **Context:** David's physical appearance

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 And the LORD said unto Samuel, How long wilt thou mourn for Saul, seeing I have rejected him from reigning over Israel? fill thine horn with oil, and go, I will send thee to Jesse the Beth-lehemite: for I have provided me a king among his sons.  

2 And Samuel said, How can I go? if Saul hear it, he will kill me. And the LORD said, Take an heifer with thee, and say, I am come to sacrifice to the LORD.  

3 And call Jesse to the sacrifice, and I will shew thee what thou shalt do: and thou shalt anoint unto me him whom I name unto thee.  

4 And Samuel did that which the LORD spake, and came to Beth-lehem. And the elders of the town trembled at his coming, and said, Comest thou peaceably?  

5 And he said, Peaceably: I am come to sacrifice unto the LORD: sanctify yourselves, and come with me to the sacrifice. And he sanctified Jesse and his sons, and called them to the sacrifice.  

6 And it came to pass, when they were come, that he looked on Eliab, and said, Surely the LORD's anointed is before him.  

7 But the LORD said unto Samuel, Look not on his countenance, or on the height of his stature; because I have refused him: for the Lord seeth not as man seeth; for man looketh on the outward appearance, but the Lord looketh on the heart.  

8 Then Jesse called Abinadab, and made him pass before Samuel. And he said, Neither hath the LORD chosen this.  

9 Then Jesse made Shammah to pass by. And he said, Neither hath the LORD chosen this.  

10 Again, Jesse made seven of his sons to pass before Samuel. And Samuel said unto Jesse, The LORD hath not chosen these.  

11 And Samuel said unto Jesse, Are here all thy children? And he said, There remaineth yet the youngest, and, behold, he keepeth the sheep. And Samuel said unto Jesse, Send and fetch him: for we will not sit down till he come hither.  

12 And he sent, and brought him in. Now he was ruddy, and withal of a beautiful countenance, and goodly to look to. And the LORD said, Arise, anoint him: for this is he.  

13 Then Samuel took the horn of oil, and anointed him in the midst of his brethren: and the Spirit of the LORD came upon David from that day forward. So Samuel rose up, and went to Ramah.  

14 But the Spirit of the LORD departed from Saul, and an evil spirit from the LORD troubled him.  

15 And Saul's servants said unto him, Behold now, an evil spirit from God troubleth thee.  

16 Let our lord now command thy servants, which are before thee, to seek out a man, who is a cunning player on an harp: and it shall come to pass, when the evil spirit from God is upon thee, that he shall play with his hand, and thou shalt be well.  

17 And Saul said unto his servants, Provide me now a man that can play well, and bring him to me.  

18 Then answered one of the servants, and said, Behold, I have seen a son of Jesse the Beth-lehemite, that is cunning in playing, and a mighty valiant man, and a man of war, and prudent in matters, and a comely person, and the LORD is with him.  

19 Wherefore Saul sent messengers unto Jesse, and said, Send me David thy son, which is with the sheep.  

20 And Jesse took an ass laden with bread, and a bottle of wine, and a kid, and sent them by David his son unto Saul.  

21 And David came to Saul, and stood before him: and he loved him greatly; and he became his armourbearer.  

22 And Saul sent to Jesse, saying, Let David, I pray thee, stand before me; for he hath found favour in my sight.  

23 And it came to pass, when the evil spirit from God was upon Saul, that David took an harp, and played with his hand: so Saul was refreshed, and was well, and the evil spirit departed from him.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "The Lord seeth not as man seeth; for man looketh on the outward appearance, but the Lord looketh on the heart"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about ignoring physical appearance
**Biblical context:** This is about divine election based on spiritual character, not external qualifications - God chooses based on heart condition, not human standards.

### "An evil spirit from the LORD troubled him"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about mental illness
**Biblical context:** This is divine judgment - God sends troubling spirit as consequence of disobedience, not natural affliction.

**Chapter 16 demonstrates God's sovereign choice of David based on heart condition rather than external appearance:** The LORD's question to Samuel about mourning for Saul shows divine timing in leadership transitions. The command to fill horn with oil and go to Jesse shows preparation for new anointing. Samuel's fear of Saul shows the danger of divine transitions. The sacrificial cover shows wisdom in approaching sensitive divine appointments. The elders trembling shows Samuel's reputation for divine authority. The sequential presentation of Jesse's seven sons shows human standards of leadership - appearance, height, strength. God's rejection of all seven shows that external qualifications don't determine divine choice. The statement about God looking on heart rather than appearance reveals divine selection criteria. David's absence at first shows that God's chosen may not be obvious to human observation. His keeping sheep shows humility and responsibility. His ruddy and beautiful appearance shows that God can choose those with good appearance but not based on it. The immediate Spirit coming shows divine empowerment upon anointing. The Spirit's departure from Saul shows transfer of divine blessing. The evil spirit troubling Saul shows divine judgment and consequences of disobedience. The servants' recognition of the evil spirit shows that spiritual problems are evident to others. Their suggestion of music therapy shows human attempts to solve spiritual problems. The servant's recommendation of David shows God's providence in bringing solution. The description of David's abilities shows that God prepares His chosen for service. Saul's immediate acceptance shows need for divine help. David's role as armourbearer shows service before prominence. The musical healing shows that God-given talents can bring spiritual relief. This chapter establishes crucial themes: God chooses based on heart condition, divine empowerment follows anointing, disobedience brings spiritual consequences, and God-given talents serve divine purposes. It demonstrates that God's timing is perfect in leadership transitions, that human standards differ from divine criteria, and that God prepares His chosen for their calling. The chapter shows David as God's choice - humble, skilled, and empowered. It illustrates the principle that divine blessing transfers from disobedient to obedient, that God equips those He calls, and that spiritual gifts serve divine purposes. The chapter points forward to Christ as the perfectly chosen King who combines divine selection with perfect character, whose anointing brings eternal Spirit, and whose service brings perfect healing. The statement about God looking on the heart points forward to Christ's perfect heart that fulfills all divine requirements. The chapter demonstrates that when God removes His Spirit from disobedient leaders, He transfers it to those whose hearts are right, and that God-given talents find their perfect purpose in divine service.
