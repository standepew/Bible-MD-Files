## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Philistines gathered armies for battle, gathered at Shochoh belongeth Judah, pitched between Shochoh and Azekah in Ephes-dammim. Saul and Israel's men gathered, pitched by valley of Elah, set battle in array against Philistines. Philistines stood on mountain on one side, Israel on mountain on other side with valley between them. A champion went from Philistine camp named Goliath of Gath, height six cubits and span. He had brass helmet on head, armed with coat of mail weighing five thousand shekels of brass. He had brass greaves on legs and brass target between shoulders. His spear staff like weaver's beam; spear head weighed six hundred shekels iron, with shield bearer before him. He stood and cried to Israel's armies, asking why they came to set battle array, saying he was Philistine and they Saul's servants, telling them to choose a man to fight him. If he could fight and kill him, they'd be Saul's servants; if he prevailed and killed him, they'd be their servants and serve them. Philistine defied Israel's armies that day, telling them to give a man to fight together. When Saul and Israel heard Philistine's words, they were dismayed and greatly afraid. David was son of Ephrathite of Beth-lehem-judah, whose name was Jesse, with eight sons. The man went among men for old man in Saul's days. Three eldest sons of Jesse followed Saul to battle: Eliab firstborn, then Abinadab, third Shammah. David was youngest; three eldest followed Saul. David went and returned from Saul to feed father's sheep at Beth-lehem. Philistine drew near morning and evening, presented himself forty days. Jesse told David to take ephah of parched corn and ten loaves to his brethren in camp, run to camp, see how brethren fared, take their pledge. Saul, they, and Israel's men were in valley of Elah fighting Philistines. David rose early, left sheep with keeper, took as Jesse commanded, came to trench as host went to fight, shouting for battle. Israel and Philistines had put battle in array, army against army. David left carriage in keeper's hand, ran into army, came and saluted brethren. As he talked with them, champion came from Philistine camp, Goliath of Gath, spoke same words; David heard. Israel's men, seeing man, fled from him, sore afraid. Israel's men said they'd seen man come up to defy Israel; whoever killed him, king would enrich with great riches, give his daughter, make father's house free in Israel. David spoke to men standing by him, asking what would be done to man who killed Philistine and took reproach from Israel, asking who was uncircumcised Philistine defying living God's armies. People answered him after same manner, saying it would be done to man who killed him. Eliab his eldest brother heard when he spoke to men; Eliab's anger kindled against David, asking why he came down, left few sheep in wilderness, knowing his pride and naughtiness of heart, coming to see battle. David asked what he had done, if there was cause. He turned from him toward another, spoke after same manner; people answered him again after former manner. When words David spoke were heard, they rehearsed them before Saul and sent for him. David told Saul not to let man's heart fail because of him; his servant would go fight Philistine. Saul told David he couldn't go against Philistine to fight him, being youth and Philistine man of war from youth. David told Saul his servant kept father's sheep; lion came and bear, took lamb from flock. He went after them, smote them, delivered from mouth; when it arose against him, caught by beard, smote and slew. His servant slew both lion and bear; this uncircumcised Philistine would be as one of them, defying living God's armies. David said LORD who delivered him from lion's paw and bear's paw would deliver him from Philistine's hand. Saul told David to go, LORD be with him. Saul armed David with his armour, put brass helmet on head, armed him with coat of mail. David girded sword on his armour, assayed to go but hadn't proved them. David told Saul he couldn't go with these, not having proved them, and put them off. He took staff in hand, chose five smooth stones from brook, put them in shepherd's bag in scrip, sling in hand, drew near to Philistine. Philistine came and drew near to David; man bearing shield before him. When Philistine looked and saw David, he disdained him, being youth, ruddy, fair countenance. Philistine told David he was a dog coming with staves, cursed David by his gods. Philistine told David to come, he'd give his flesh to fowls of air and beasts of field. David told Philistine he came with sword, spear, and shield, but came in name of LORD of hosts, God of Israel's armies whom he defied. That day LORD would deliver him into his hand, smite him, take his head, give carcases of Philistine host to fowls of air and wild beasts of field, that all earth might know there was God in Israel. Assembly would know LORD saved not with sword or spear; battle was LORD's and would give them into their hands. Philistine arose, came and drew near to meet David; David hasted, ran toward army to meet Philistine. David put hand in bag, took stone, slang it, smote Philistine in forehead, stone sunk into forehead; he fell on face to earth. David prevailed over Philistine with sling and stone, smote Philistine, slew him, but no sword in David's hand. David ran, stood on Philistine, took his sword, drew from sheath, slew him, cut off head with it. When Philistines saw champion dead, they fled. Israel's men and Judah arose, shouted, pursued Philistines to valley, gates of Ekron. Wounded Philistines fell by way to Shaaraim to Gath and Ekron. Israel's children returned from chasing Philistines, spoiled their tents. David took Philistine's head, brought to Jerusalem, put his armour in his tent. When Saul saw David go against Philistine, asked Abner captain of host whose son this youth was. Abner said as king's soul lived, he couldn't tell. King told him to inquire whose son the stripling was. As David returned from Philistine slaughter, Abner took him, brought before Saul with Philistine's head in hand. Saul asked whose son he was, young man. David answered he was son of Jesse servant, Beth-lehemite.

### GOLIATH
- **Meaning:** "Splendor" or "Exile"
- **Context:** Philistine champion's name
- **Significance:** Represents seemingly insurmountable opposition

- **Meaning:** Weight measure, about 8-10 pounds

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 Now the Philistines gathered together their armies to battle, and were gathered together at Shochoh, which belongeth to Judah, and pitched between Shochoh and Azekah, in Ephes-dammim.  

2 And Saul and the men of Israel were gathered together, and pitched by the valley of Elah, and set the battle in array against the Philistines.  

3 And the Philistines stood on a mountain on the one side, and Israel stood on a mountain on the other side: and there was a valley between them.  

4 And there went out a champion out of the camp of the Philistines, named Goliath, of Gath, whose height was six cubits and a span.  

5 And he had an helmet of brass upon his head, and he was armed with a coat of mail; and the weight of the coat was five thousand shekels of brass.  

6 And he had greaves of brass upon his legs, and a target of brass between his shoulders.  

7 And the staff of his spear was like a weaver's beam; and his spear's head weighed six hundred shekels of iron: and one bearing a shield went before him.  

8 And he stood and cried unto the armies of Israel, and said unto them, Why are ye come out to set your battle in array? am not I a Philistine, and ye servants to Saul? choose you a man for you, and let him come down to me.  

9 If he be able to fight with me, and to kill me, then will we be your servants: but if I prevail against him, and kill him, then shall ye be our servants, and serve us.  

10 And the Philistine said, I defy the armies of Israel this day; give me a man, that we may fight together.  

11 When Saul and all Israel heard those words of the Philistine, they were dismayed, and greatly afraid.  

12 Now David was the son of that Ephrathite of Beth-lehem-judah, whose name was Jesse; and he had eight sons: and the man went among men for an old man in the days of Saul.  

13 And the three eldest sons of Jesse went and followed Saul to battle: and the names of his three sons that went to battle were Eliab the firstborn, and next unto him Abinadab, and the third Shammah.  

14 And David was the youngest: and the three eldest followed Saul.  

15 But David went and returned from Saul to feed his father's sheep at Beth-lehem.  

16 And the Philistine drew near morning and evening, and presented himself forty days.  

17 And Jesse said unto David his son, Take now for thy brethren an ephah of this parched corn, and these ten loaves, and run to the camp to thy brethren;  

18 And carry these ten cheeses unto the captain of their thousand, and look how thy brethren fare, and take their pledge.  

19 Now Saul, and they, and all the men of Israel, were in the valley of Elah, fighting with the Philistines.  

20 And David rose up early in the morning, and left the sheep with a keeper, and took, and went, as Jesse had commanded him; and he came to the trench, as the host was going forth to the fight, and shouted for the battle.  

21 For Israel and the Philistines had put the battle in array, army against army.  

22 And David left his carriage in the hand of the keeper of the carriage, and ran into the army, and came and saluted his brethren.  

23 And as he talked with them, behold, there came up the champion, the Philistine of Gath, Goliath by name, out of the armies of the Philistines, and spake according to the same words: and David heard them.  

24 And all the men of Israel, when they saw the man, fled from him, and were sore afraid.  

25 And the men of Israel said, Have ye seen this man that is come up? surely to defy Israel is he come up: and it shall be, that the man who killeth him, the king will enrich him with great riches, and will give him his daughter, and make his father's house free in Israel.  

26 And David spake to the men that stood by him, saying, What shall be done to the man that killeth this Philistine, and taketh away the reproach from Israel? for who is this uncircumcised Philistine, that he should defy the armies of the living God?  

27 And the people answered him after this manner, saying, So shall it be done to the man that killeth him.  

28 And Eliab his eldest brother heard when he spake unto the men; and Eliab's anger was kindled against David, and he said, Why camest thou down hither? and with whom hast thou left those few sheep in the wilderness? I know thy pride, and the naughtiness of thine heart; for thou art come down that thou mightest see the battle.  

29 And David said, What have I now done? Is there not a cause?  

30 And he turned from him toward another, and spake after the same manner: and the people answered him again after the former manner.  

31 And when the words were heard which David spake, they rehearsed them before Saul: and he sent for him.  

32 And David said to Saul, Let no man's heart fail because of him; thy servant will go and fight with this Philistine.  

33 And Saul said to David, Thou art not able to go against this Philistine to fight with him: for thou art but a youth, and he a man of war from his youth.  

34 And David said unto Saul, Thy servant kept his father's sheep, and there came a lion, and a bear, and took a lamb out of the flock:  

35 And I went out after him, and smote him, and delivered it out of his mouth: and when he arose against me, I caught him by his beard, and smote him, and slew him.  

36 Thy servant slew both the lion and the bear: and this uncircumcised Philistine shall be as one of them, seeing he hath defied the armies of the living God.  

37 David said moreover, The LORD that delivered me out of the paw of the lion, and out of the paw of the bear, he will deliver me out of the hand of this Philistine.  

38 And Saul said unto David, Go, and the LORD be with thee.  

39 And Saul armed David with his armour, and he put an helmet of brass upon his head; also he armed him with a coat of mail.  

40 And David girded his sword upon his armour, and he assayed to go; for he had not proved them. And David said unto Saul, I cannot go with these; for I have not proved them. And David put them off him.  

41 And he took his staff in his hand, and chose him five smooth stones out of the brook, and put them in a shepherd's bag which he had, even in a scrip; and his sling was in his hand: and he drew near to the Philistine.  

42 And the Philistine came on and drew near unto David; and the man that bare the shield went before him.  

43 And when the Philistine looked about, and saw David, he disdained him: for he was but a youth, and ruddy, and of a fair countenance.  

44 And the Philistine said unto David, Am I a dog, that thou comest to me with staves? And the Philistine cursed David by his gods.  

45 And the Philistine said to David, Come to me, and I will give thy flesh unto the fowls of the air, and to the beasts of the field.  

46 Then said David to the Philistine, Thou comest to me with a sword, and with a spear, and with a shield: but I come to thee in the name of the LORD of hosts, the God of the armies of Israel, whom thou hast defied.  

47 This day will the LORD deliver thee into mine hand; and I will smite thee, and take thine head from thee; and I will give the carcases of the host of the Philistines this day unto the fowls of the air, and to the wild beasts of the earth: that all the earth may know that there is a God in Israel.  

48 And all this assembly shall know that the LORD saveth not with sword and spear: for the battle is the LORD's, and he will give you into our hands.  

49 And it came to pass, when the Philistine arose, and came and drew nigh to meet David, that David hasted, and ran toward the army to meet the Philistine.  

50 And David put his hand in his bag, and took thence a stone, and slang it, and smote the Philistine in his forehead, that the stone sunk into his forehead; and he fell upon his face to the earth.  

51 So David prevailed over the Philistine with a sling and with a stone, and smote the Philistine, and slew him; but there was no sword in the hand of David.  

52 Therefore David ran, and stood upon the Philistine, and took his sword, and drew it out of the sheath thereof, and slew him, and cut off his head therewith. And when the Philistines saw their champion was dead, they fled.  

53 And the men of Israel and of Judah arose, and shouted, and pursued the Philistines, until thou come to the valley, and to the gates of Ekron. And the wounded of the Philistines fell down by the way to Shaaraim, even unto Gath, and unto Ekron.  

54 And the children of Israel returned from chasing after the Philistines, and they spoiled their tents.  

55 And David took the head of the Philistine, and brought it to Jerusalem; but he put his armour in his tent.  

56 And when Saul saw David go forth against the Philistine, he said unto Abner, the captain of the host, Abner, whose son is this youth? And Abner said, As thy soul liveth, O king, I cannot tell.  

57 And the king said, Inquire thou whose son the stripling is.  

58 And as David returned from the slaughter of the Philistine, Abner took him, and brought him before Saul with the head of the Philistine in his hand.  

59 And Saul said to him, Whose son art thou, thou young man? And David answered, I am the son of thy servant Jesse the Beth-lehemite.

**Chapter 17 demonstrates the power of faith in God against seemingly insurmountable opposition:** Philistines' gathering shows ongoing conflict with Israel. The mountain positions show natural barriers between opposing forces. Goliath's impressive appearance and armor represents seemingly unbeatable opposition. His formal challenge follows ancient warfare customs, avoiding full-scale battle through single combat. The forty-day defiance shows prolonged intimidation. Israel's fear shows natural response to overwhelming threat. David's arrival shows God's provision of unexpected deliverer. His youth and inexperience contrast with Goliath's strength and experience. The reward offer shows human motivation for heroism. David's question about reproach to Israel shows spiritual understanding of the threat.
