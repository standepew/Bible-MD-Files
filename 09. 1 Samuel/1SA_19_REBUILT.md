## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Saul spoke to Jonathan his son and all servants that they should kill David. But Jonathan delighted much in David, told David Saul his father sought to kill him, told him to take heed until morning, abide in secret place, hide himself. He would go out and stand beside father in field where David was, commune with father of David, and tell what he saw. Jonathan spoke good of David to Saul his father, telling king not to sin against servant David, since David hadn't sinned against him and his works were very good. David put his life in hand, slew Philistine, LORD wrought great salvation for all Israel, Saul saw and rejoiced, so why sin against innocent blood to slay David without cause? Saul hearkened to Jonathan's voice, swore as LORD liveth, David shall not be slain. Jonathan called David, showed him all things, brought David to Saul, and he was in his presence as in times past. War came again; David went out, fought Philistines, slew them with great slaughter, and they fled from him. Evil spirit from LORD was upon Saul as he sat in house with javelin in hand; David played with his hand. Saul sought to smite David to wall with javelin, but David slipped away from Saul's presence, javelin smote into wall, David fled and escaped that night. Saul sent messengers to David's house to watch him and slay him in morning. Michal David's wife told him to save his life that night or he'd be slain next morning. Michal let David down through window; he went, fled, and escaped. Michal took an image, laid it in bed, put pillow of goats' hair for bolster, covered with cloth. When Saul sent messengers to take David, she said he was sick. Saul sent messengers again to see David, saying bring him up in bed to slay him. When messengers came, there was image in bed with pillow of goats' hair for bolster. Saul asked Michal why she deceived him and sent enemy away who escaped. Michal answered Saul, David told her to let him go, why should she kill him? David fled, escaped, came to Samuel at Ramah, told him all Saul had done. He and Samuel went and dwelt in Naioth. It was told Saul, David was at Naioth in Ramah. Saul sent messengers to take David; when they saw company of prophets prophesying and Samuel standing appointed over them, Spirit of God was upon Saul's messengers, and they also prophesied. When told Saul, he sent other messengers, they prophesied likewise. Saul sent messengers again third time, they prophesied also. Then Saul went to Ramah, came to great well in Sechu, asked where Samuel and David were, one said they were at Naioth in Ramah. He went to Naioth in Ramah; Spirit of God was upon him also, he went on and prophesied until he came to Naioth in Ramah. He stripped off clothes, prophesied before Samuel in like manner, lay down naked all day and night. Therefore they say, "Is Saul also among the prophets?"

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Let not the king sin against his servant, against David; because he hath not sinned against thee" — Defense of Innocent

**1 Samuel 19:4-5** — "And Jonathan spake good of David unto Saul his father, and said unto him, Let not the king sin against his servant, against David; because he hath not sinned against thee, and because his works have been to thee-ward very good: For he did put his life in his hand, and slew the Philistine, and the LORD wrought a great salvation for all Israel"

- **Genesis 4:9-10** — "And the LORD said unto Cain, Where is Abel thy brother? And he said, I know not: Am I my brother's keeper? And he said, What hast thou done? the voice of thy brother's blood crieth unto me from the ground."
  - *Interpretation:* God questioned Cain about killing innocent Abel, showing protection of innocent blood.

- **Proverbs 31:8-9** - "Open thy mouth for the dumb in the cause of all such as are appointed to destruction. Open thy mouth, judge righteously, and plead the cause of the poor and needy."

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### NAIOth
- **Meaning:** "Dwellings" or "Habitations"
- **Context:** Location where Samuel and prophets gathered
- **Significance:** Place of divine protection and prophetic activity
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual refuge (Psalm 91:1)

### IMAGE
- **Meaning:** Idol or household god
- **Context:** Michal's deception to protect David
- **Significance:** False appearance for protection
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual deception (2 Corinthians 11:14)

### PROPHESYING
- **Meaning:** Speaking divine message
- **Context:** Divine Spirit coming upon even hostile messengers
- **Significance:** God's power overcomes human hostility
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual gifts (1 Corinthians 12)

### JAVELIN
- **Meaning:** Throwing spear
- **Context:** Saul's weapon against David
- **Significance:** Human attempt to destroy God's chosen
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual warfare (Ephesians 6:16)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Let not the king sin against his servant, against David; because he hath not sinned against thee"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about political loyalty
**Biblical context:** This is about defending innocent blood - Jonathan speaks against unjust killing, showing moral responsibility to protect the innocent.

### "Is Saul also among the prophets?"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about religious conversion
**Biblical context:** This is about divine intervention - even hostile enemies are overcome by God's Spirit, showing God's power over human hostility.

**Chapter 19 demonstrates God's protection of His chosen through various means and the futility of human opposition:** Saul's command to kill David shows the depth of his jealousy and hatred. Jonathan's delight in David shows true friendship that transcends family loyalty. Jonathan's warning to David shows faithful protection of the innocent. His defense before Saul shows courage to speak truth to power. His reminder of David's service and God's salvation shows proper perspective on divine blessing. Saul's temporary repentance shows how truth can temporarily overcome jealousy. David's continued military success shows God's blessing despite opposition. Saul's renewed attempt to kill David shows persistence of evil jealousy. The evil spirit's influence shows spiritual source of Saul's hostility. David's escape shows divine protection through wisdom. Michal's warning shows God's protection through relationships. Her deception shows righteous protection of innocent life. The image in bed shows creative protection through false appearance. Saul's questioning shows his frustration at failed plans. David's flight to Samuel shows seeking spiritual refuge. The prophetic company shows divine presence as protection. The Spirit coming upon hostile messengers shows God's power overcomes human hostility. Saul's own prophesying shows divine intervention that humbles even enemies. The naked prophesying shows complete divine control over human dignity. This chapter establishes crucial themes: God protects His chosen through various means, true friendship defends the innocent, divine power overcomes human hostility, and human opposition cannot thwart God's purposes. It demonstrates that God uses both spiritual and human means for protection, that righteous deception can save lives, and that divine Spirit overcomes even hostile intent. The chapter shows multiple forms of protection - Jonathan's advocacy, Michal's deception, Samuel's refuge, and divine Spirit's intervention. It illustrates the principle that God's chosen are protected through diverse means, that true friendship involves risk for righteousness, and that divine power can transform even hostile enemies. The chapter points forward to Christ as the perfect protector of His people, whose advocacy brings complete salvation, and whose Spirit transforms the most hostile hearts. The prophesying of hostile messengers points forward to the power of the Holy Spirit to transform even the most resistant hearts. The chapter demonstrates that when human opposition intensifies against God's chosen, divine protection intensifies as well, showing that God's purposes prevail through multiple means of protection.
