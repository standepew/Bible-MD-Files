## CHAPTER SUMMARY

David departed, escaped to cave Adullam; when his brethren and father's house heard, they went down to him. Everyone in distress, debt, discontented gathered to him; he became captain over them with about four hundred men. David went to Mizpeh of Moab, told king of Moab to let his father and mother come forth and be with them until he knew what God would do for him. He brought them before king of Moab; they dwelt with him all while David was in hold. Prophet Gad told David not to abide in hold, depart and get into land of Judah. David departed, came to forest of Hareth. When Saul heard David was discovered and men with him (Saul abode in Gibeah under tree in Ramah with spear in hand, servants standing about him), Saul told servants standing about him to hear, Benjamites, would son of Jesse give them fields, vineyards, make them captains of thousands and hundreds, that all conspired against him, none showed him that Jonathan made league with son of Jesse, none sorry for him or showed him that Jonathan stirred up his servant to lie in wait. Doeg the Edomite, set over Saul's servants, answered that he saw son of Jesse coming to Nob to Ahimelech son of Ahitub. He enquired of LORD for him, gave victuals, gave sword of Goliath the Philistine. King sent to call Ahimelech priest, son of Ahitub, and all his father's house, priests in Nob; they all came to king. Saul told Ahitub to hear; he answered "Here I am, my lord." Saul asked why he conspired against him with son of Jesse, giving him bread, sword, enquiring of God for him, that he should rise against him to lie in wait. Ahimelech answered king, who was faithful among servants as David, king's son-in-law, went at bidding, honorable in house? Did he begin to enquire of God for him? Far from him; let not king impute anything to servant or father's house; servant knew nothing of this. King said Ahimelech would surely die, he and all father's house. King told footmen standing about to turn and slay priests of LORD because their hand was with David, they knew when he fled and didn't show him. King's servants wouldn't put forth hand to fall upon priests of LORD. King told Doeg to turn and fall upon priests. Doeg Edomite turned, fell upon priests, slew that day fourscore and five persons wearing linen ephod. He smote Nob, city of priests, with sword edge, men, women, children, sucklings, oxen, asses, sheep with sword edge. One of Ahimelech's sons, Ahitub, named Abiathar, escaped, fled after David. Abiathar showed David that Saul slew LORD's priests. David told Abiathar he knew it that day when Doeg Edomite was there, that he would surely tell Saul; he occasioned death of all persons of father's house. Abiathar should abide with him, fear not; he who sought David's life sought Abiathar's life; with David he'd be in safeguard.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### ADULLAM
- **Meaning:** "Justice of the people"
- **Context:** Cave where David gathered followers
- **Significance:** Place of refuge and leadership development
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual refuge (Psalm 91:1)

### MIZPEH
- **Meaning:** "Watchtower"
- **Context:** Location in Moab where David sought help
- **Significance:** Place of observation and strategy
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual watchfulness (1 Peter 5:8)

### GAD
- **Meaning:** "Troop" or "Good fortune"
- **Context:** Prophet who guided David
- **Significance:** Divine guidance in difficult circumstances
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual guidance (Psalm 32:8)

### ABIATHAR
- **Meaning:** "Father of abundance"
- **Context:** Priest who escaped Saul's massacre
- **Significance:** Continuation of priestly line
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual priesthood (1 Peter 2:5)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 David therefore departed thence, and escaped to the cave Adullam: and when his brethren and all his father's house heard it, they went down thither to him.  

2 And every one that was in distress, and every one that was in debt, and every one that was discontented, gathered themselves unto him; and he became a captain over them over them: and there were with him about four hundred men.  

3 And David went thence to Mizpeh of Moab: and he said unto the king of Moab, Let my father and my mother, I pray thee, come forth, and be with you, till I know what God will do for me.  

4 And he brought them before the king of Moab: and they dwelt with him all the while that David was in the hold.  

5 And the prophet Gad said unto David, Abide not in the hold; depart, and get thee into the land of Judah. Then David departed, and came into the forest of Hareth.  

6 When Saul heard that David was discovered, and the men that were with him, (now Saul abode in Gibeah under a tree in Ramah, having his spear in his hand, and all his servants were standing about him;)  

7 Then Saul said unto his servants that stood about him, Hear now, ye Benjamites; will the son of Jesse give every one of you fields and vineyards, and make you all captains of thousands, and captains of hundreds;  

8 That all of you have conspired against me, and there is none that sheweth me that my son hath made a league with the son of Jesse, and there is none of you that is sorry for me, or sheweth unto me that my son hath stirred up my servant against me, to lie in wait, as at this day?  

9 Then answered Doeg the Edomite, which was set over the servants of Saul, and said, I saw the son of Jesse coming to Nob, to Ahimelech the son of Ahitub.  

10 And he enquired of the LORD for him, and gave him victuals, and gave him the sword of Goliath the Philistine.  

11 Then the king sent to call Ahimelech the priest, the son of Ahitub, and all his father's house, the priests that were in with him: and they came all of them to the king.  

12 And the king said, Hear now, thou son of Ahitub. And he told him, Here I am, my lord.  

13 And the king said unto him, Why have ye conspired against me, thou and the son of Jesse, in that thou hast given him bread, and a sword, and hast enquired of God for him, that he should rise against me, to lie in wait, as at this day?  

14 Then Ahimelech answered the king, and said, And who is so faithful among all thy servants as David, which the the king's son in law, and goeth at thy bidding, and is honourable in thine house?  

15 Did I then begin to enquire of God for him? be it far from me: let not the king impute any thing unto his servant, nor to all the house of my father: for thy servant knew nothing of all this, less or more.  

16 And the king said, Thou shalt surely die, Ahimelech, thou, and all thy father's house.  

17 And the king said unto the footmen that stood about him, Turn, and slay the priests of the LORD; because their hand also is with David, and because they knew when he fled, and did not shew it to me. But the servants of the king would not put forth their hand to fall upon the priests of the LORD.  

18 And the king said to Doeg, Turn thou, and fall upon the priests. And Doeg the Edomite turned, and he fell upon the priests, and slew on that day fourscore and five persons that did wear a linen ephod.  

19 And Nob, the city of the priests, smote he with the edge of the sword, both men and women, children and sucklings, and oxen, and asses, and sheep, with the's edge of the sword.  

20 And one of the sons of Ahimelech the son of Ahitub, named Abiathar, escaped, and fled after David.  

21 And Abiathar shewed David that Saul had slain the LORD's priests.  

22 And David said unto Abiathar, I knew it that day, when Doeg the Edomite was there, that he would surely tell Saul: I have occasioned the death of all the persons of thy father's house.  

23 Abide thou with me, fear not: for he that seeketh my life seeketh thy life: but with me thou shalt be in safeguard.

**Common misinterpretation:* This is about changing location
