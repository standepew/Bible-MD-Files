## CHAPTER SUMMARY

When Saul returned from following Philistines, it was told him David was in wilderness of En-gedi. Saul took three thousand chosen men from Israel, went to seek David and men on rocks of wild goats. He came to sheepcotes by way where was a cave; Saul went in to cover his feet; David and men remained in sides of cave. David's men told him the day LORD said He would deliver enemy into his hand to do as seemed good. David arose, cut off skirt of Saul's robe privily. David's heart smote him because he cut off Saul's skirt. He told men LORD forbid doing this to master, LORD's anointed, stretching hand against him since he was LORD's anointed. David stayed servants with these words, suffered them not to rise against Saul. Saul rose from cave, went on his way. David also arose afterward, went out of cave, cried after Saul saying "My lord the king." When Saul looked behind, David stooped with face to earth, bowed. David told Saul why he heard men's words saying David sought his hurt. That day his eyes saw LORD delivered him into his hand in cave; some bade him kill him, but his eye spared him; he wouldn't put forth hand against lord for he was LORD's anointed. His father should see skirt of robe in his hand; in that he cut off skirt of robe and didn't kill him, know and see there was neither evil nor transgression in his hand, hadn't sinned against him, yet he hunted his soul to take it. LORD judge between them, LORD avenge him, but his hand wouldn't be upon him. As proverb of ancients, wickedness proceeds from wicked, but his hand wouldn't be upon him. After whom did king of Israel come out? After whom did he pursue? After dead dog, after flea. LORD therefore be judge, judge between them, see, plead his cause, deliver him from his hand. When David finished speaking, Saul asked if this was his voice, son David. Saul lifted voice, wept. He told David he was more righteous, rewarded him good whereas he rewarded David evil. David showed that day he dealt well with him; when LORD delivered him into his hand, he didn't kill him. If man finds enemy, will he let him go well away? LORD reward David good for what he did that day. Now he knew David would surely be king, kingdom of Israel established in his hand. Swear now by LORD that David wouldn't cut off his seed after him, nor destroy his name from father's house. David swore to Saul. Saul went home; David and men went up to hold.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### EN-GEDI
- **Meaning:** "Spring of the goat"
- **Context:** Wilderness area where David hid
- **Significance:** Place of divine protection and testing
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual refuge (Hebrews 6:18)

### CAVE
- **Meaning:** Natural shelter
- **Context:** Where David had opportunity to kill Saul
- **Significance:** Place of moral testing and divine restraint
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual refuge (Psalm 57:1)

### SKIRT OF ROBE
- **Meaning:** Edge of garment
- **Context:** Evidence David could have killed Saul
- **Significance:** Symbol of restraint and respect
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual testimony (2 Corinthians 3:2)

### DEAD DOG
- **Meaning:** Term of humility
- **Context:** David's description of himself
- **Significance:** Recognition of lowliness before authority
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual humility (Philippians 2:8)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 And it came to pass, when Saul was returned from following the Philistines, that it was told him, saying, Behold, David is in the wilderness of En-gedi.  

2 Then Saul took three thousand chosen men out of all Israel, and went to seek David and his men upon the rocks of the wild goats.  

3 And he came to the sheepcotes by the way, where was a cave; and Saul went in to cover his feet: and David and his men remained in the sides of the cave.  

4 And the men of David said unto him, Behold the day of which the LORD said unto thee, Behold, I will deliver thine enemy into thine hand, that thou mayest do to him as it shall seem good unto thee.  

5 Then David arose, and cut off the skirt of Saul's robe privily.  

6 And it came to pass afterward, that David's heart smote him, because he had cut off Saul's skirt.  

7 And he said unto his men, The LORD forbid that I should do this thing unto my master, the LORD's anointed, to stretch forth mine hand against him, seeing he is the anointed of the LORD.  

8 So David stayed his servants with these words, and suffered them not to rise against Saul. But Saul rose up out of the cave, and went on his way.  

9 David also arose afterward, and went out of the cave, and cried after Saul, saying, My lord the king. And when Saul looked behind him, he looked behind him, David stooped with his face to the earth, and bowed himself.  

10 And David said to Saul, Wherefore hearest thou men's words, saying, Behold, David seeketh thy hurt?  

11 Behold, this day thine eyes have seen how that the LORD had delivered thee to day into mine hand in the cave: and some bade me kill thee: but mine eye spared thee; and I said, I will not put forth mine hand against my lord; for he is the LORD's anointed.  

12 Moreover, my father, see, yea, see the skirt of thy robe in my hand: for in that I cut off the skirt of thy robe, and killed thee not, know thou and see that there is neither evil nor transgression in my hand, and I have not sinned against thee; yet thou huntest my soul to take it.  

13 The LORD judge between me and thee, and the LORD avenge me of thee: but mine hand shall not be upon thee.  

14 As saith the proverb of the ancients, Wickedness proceeds from the wicked: but mine hand shall not be upon thee.  

15 After whom is the king of Israel come out? after whom dost thou pursue? after a dead dog, after a flea.  

16 The LORD therefore be judge, and judge between me and thee, and see, and plead my cause, and deliver me out of thine hand.  

17 And it came to pass, when David had made an end of speaking these words unto Saul, that Saul said, Is this thy voice, my son David? And Saul lifted up his encounter, and wept.  

18 And he said to David, Thou art more righteous than I: for thou hast rewarded me good, whereas I have rewarded thee evil.  

19 And thou hast shewed this day how that thou hast dealt well with me: forasmuch as when the LORD had delivered me into thine hand, thou killedst me not.  

20 For if a man find his enemy, will he let him go well away? wherefore the LORD reward thee good for that thou hath done unto me this day.  

21 And went now, behold, I know well that thou shalt surely be king, and that the kingdom of Israel shall be established in thine hand.  

22 Swear now therefore unto me by the LORD, that thou wilt not cut off my seed after me, and that thou wilt not destroy my name out of my father's house.  

23 And David sware unto Saul. And Saul went home; but David and his men gat them up unto the hold.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "The LORD forbid that I should do this thing unto my master, the LORD's anointed"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about political loyalty
**Biblical context:** This is about respect for divine authority - David refuses to harm God's anointed, showing reverence for divine appointment over personal advantage.

### "The LORD judge between me and thee, and the LORD avenge me of thee"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about personal vengeance
**Biblical context:** This is about divine justice - David commits his case to God rather than taking personal revenge, showing trust in divine justice.

**Chapter 24 demonstrates David's integrity in respecting divine authority and the power of mercy to transform hearts:** Saul's pursuit with three thousand men shows the intensity of his obsession with destroying David. The cave setting provides opportunity for David to eliminate his enemy. The men's encouragement shows human perspective that sees opportunity for revenge. David's cutting of Saul's robe shows restraint rather than violence. His heart smiting him shows moral sensitivity to even minor violations of respect. His explanation to men shows principle-based leadership that respects divine authority. His restraint of men shows moral courage against peer pressure. Saul's vulnerability in the cave shows how God places enemies in positions of weakness. David's pursuit of Saul shows courage to confront rather than flee. His bowing shows proper respect for authority despite persecution. His explanation of the situation shows wisdom in presenting evidence of his integrity. His reference to God's deliverance shows acknowledgment of divine sovereignty. His refusal to harm God's anointed shows reverence for divine appointment. His appeal to divine justice shows trust in God rather than personal vengeance. His humble description as dead dog shows proper perspective on his position. His appeal for divine judgment shows confidence in God's righteousness. Saul's emotional response shows that mercy can penetrate even hardened hearts. His confession of David's righteousness shows recognition of moral superiority. His acknowledgment of David's mercy shows understanding of grace. His recognition of David's future kingship shows acceptance of divine purpose. His request for mercy for his descendants shows concern for legacy. David's oath shows commitment to keep his word. This chapter establishes crucial themes: respect for divine authority transcends personal advantage, mercy transforms hearts, integrity builds trust, and divine justice prevails over human vengeance. It demonstrates that true leadership respects divine authority, that mercy demonstrates spiritual strength, and that integrity provides testimony that transforms opponents. The chapter shows David as model leader who respects divine authority, exercises mercy, maintains integrity, and trusts divine justice. It illustrates the principle that reverence for God's anointed shows spiritual maturity, that mercy demonstrates divine character, and that integrity provides powerful witness. The chapter points forward to Christ as the perfect leader who respects divine authority, shows perfect mercy, maintains perfect integrity, and provides perfect salvation. The cutting of the robe points forward to Christ's restraint in using His divine power. The chapter demonstrates that when God's chosen have opportunity to eliminate opponents, divine character calls for mercy and respect for authority, showing how integrity transforms hearts and builds testimony for divine purposes.
