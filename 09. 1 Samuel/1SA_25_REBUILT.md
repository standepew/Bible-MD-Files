## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Samuel died; all Israelites gathered, lamented him, buried him in his house at Ramah. David arose, went down to wilderness of Paran. There was a man in Maon, possessions in Carmel; man was very great, had three thousand sheep, thousand goats; was shearing sheep in Carmel. Man's name was Nabal; wife's name Abigail; she was woman of good understanding, beautiful countenance; man was churlish and evil in doings, of house of Caleb. David heard in wilderness Nabal sheared sheep. David sent ten young men, told them to go to Carmel, greet Nabal in his name, say peace to him, house, and all he had. He heard Nabal had shearers; his shepherds with them, they hurt them not, nothing missing while in Carmel. Ask young men, they'll show; let young men find favor in eyes; they came in good day; give whatsoever came to hand to servants and son David. David's young men came, spoke to Nabal according to words in David's name, ceased. Nabal answered David's servants, asking who was David, son of Jesse; many servants break away from masters. Should he take bread, water, flesh killed for shearers, give to men he didn't know? David's young men turned way, went again, told him all sayings. David told men to gird on swords; they girded swords; David girded sword; four hundred men went after David, two hundred abode by stuff. One young man told Abigail Nabal's wife, David sent messengers from wilderness to salute master, he railed on them. Men were very good to them, not hurt, nothing missing while conversant in fields. They were wall to them both night and day while with them keeping sheep. Know and consider what to do; evil determined against master and household; he was son of Belial, man couldn't speak to him. Abigail made haste, took two hundred loaves, two bottles wine, five sheep ready dressed, five measures parched corn, hundred clusters raisins, two hundred cakes figs, laid on asses. She told servants to go before, she came after, didn't tell husband Nabal. As she rode ass, came down by covert of hill; David and men came down against her, she met them. David said surely in vain he kept all Nabal had in wilderness, nothing missed of all pertaining to him; he requited evil for good. So and more also do God to David's enemies if he left of all pertaining to Nabal by morning light any that pisseth against wall. When Abigail saw David, she hasted, lighted off ass, fell before David on face, bowed to ground, fell at feet, said upon her, my lord, upon her let iniquity be; let handmaid speak in audience, hear words of handmaid. Let not lord regard man of Belial, Nabal; as his name is, so is he; Nabal his name, folly with him; handmaid didn't see young men of lord sent. Therefore, as LORD lives and soul lives, seeing LORD withheld from coming to shed blood and avenging with own hand, let lord's enemies and those seeking evil to lord be as Nabal. Blessing handmaid brought to lord, give to young men following lord. Forgive trespass of handmaid; LORD will make lord sure house; lord fights LORD's battles, evil not found all days. Man risen to pursue, seek soul; but lord's soul bound in bundle of life with LORD God; souls of enemies, he'll sling out as from middle of sling. When LORD does to lord according to good spoken concerning him, appoints ruler over Israel, this shall be no grief to lord, nor offense of heart, either shed blood causeless, or lord avenged himself; but when LORD deals well with lord, remember handmaid. David told Abigail, blessed be LORD God of Israel who sent her that day to meet him. Blessed her advice, blessed her who kept him from shedding blood and avenging with own hand. As LORD God of Israel lives who kept him from hurting her, except she had hasted and come to meet him, surely none left to Nabal by morning light. David received from her hand what she brought, told her to go up in peace to house; he hearkened to voice, accepted her person. Abigail came to Nabal; he held feast in house like feast of king; Nabal's heart merry within, very drunken; she told him nothing until morning light. Morning, wine gone from Nabal, wife told him these things, his heart died within, became as stone. About ten days after, LORD smote Nabal, he died. When David heard Nabal dead, said blessed be LORD who pleaded cause of his reproach from Nabal's hand, kept servant from evil; LORD returned Nabal's wickedness upon his own head. David sent, communed with Abigail, to take her to wife. When David's servants came to Abigail at Carmel, spoke to her, David sent to take her to wife. She arose, bowed face to earth, said let handmaid be servant to wash feet of servants of lord. Abigail hasted, arose, rode ass with five damsels after her, went after David's messengers, became his wife. David also took Ahinoam of Jezreel; both were his wives. Saul had given Michal his daughter, David's wife, to Phalti son of Laish of Gallim.

  - *Interpretation:* Proverbs teaches that wickedness returns upon wicked, fulfilled in Nabal.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### PARAN
- **Meaning:** "Glory" or "Ornament"
- **Context:** Wilderness where David went after Samuel's death
- **Significance:** Place of transition and testing
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual wilderness (Hebrews 3:8)

### NABAL
- **Meaning:** "Fool"
- **Context:** Wealthy but foolish man
- **Significance:** Character matches name, showing folly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual foolishness (1 Corinthians 3:18)

### ABIGAIL
- **Meaning:** "Father of joy" or "Source of joy"
- **Context:** Wise wife who prevented disaster
- **Significance:** Wisdom brings joy and deliverance
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual wisdom (Proverbs 4:7)

### BUNDLE OF LIFE
- **Meaning:** Collection of lives under divine protection
- **Context:** Abigail's description of divine protection
- **Significance:** God's preservation of His chosen
* **New Testament parallel:** Eternal life (John 3:16)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 And Samuel died; and all the Israelites were gathered together, and lamented him, and buried him in his house at Ramah. And David arose, and went down to the wilderness of Paran.  

2 And there was a man in Maon, whose possessions were in Carmel; and the man was very great, and he had three thousand sheep, and a thousand goats: and he was shearing his sheep in Carmel.  

3 Now the name of the man was Nabal; and the name of his wife Abigail: and she was a woman of good understanding, and of a beautiful countenance: but the man was churlish and evil in his doings; and he was of the house of Caleb.  

4 And David heard in the wilderness that Nabal did shear his sheep.  

5 And David sent out ten young men, and David said unto the young men, Get you up to Carmel, and go to Nabal, and greet him in my name:  

6 And thus shall ye say to him that liveth in prosperity, Peace be both to thee, and peace be to thine house, and peace be unto all that thou hast.  

7 And now I have heard that thou hast shearers: now thy shepherds which were with us, we hurt them not, neither was there ought missing unto them, all the while they were in Carmel.  

8 Ask thy young men, and they will shew thee. Wherefore let the young men find favour in thine eyes: for we come in a good day: give, I pray thee, whatsoever cometh to thine hand unto thy servants, and to thy son David.  

9 And when David's young men came, they spake to Nabal according to all those words in the name of David, and ceased.  

10 And Nabal answered David's servants, and said, Who is David? and who is the son of Jesse? there be many servants now a days that break away every man from his master.  

11 Shall I then take my bread, and my water, and my flesh that I have killed for my shearers, and to give it unto men, whom I know not whence they be?  

12 So David's young men turned their way, and went again, and came and told him all those sayings.  

13 And David said unto his men, Gird ye on every man his sword. And they girded on every man his sword; and David also girded on his sword: and there went up after David about four hundred men; and two hundred abide by the stuff.  

14 But one of the young men told Abigail, Nabal's wife, saying, Behold, David sent messengers out of the wilderness to salute our master; and he railed on them.  

15 But the men were very good unto us, and we were not hurt, neither missed we any thing, as long as we were conversant with them, when we were in the fields:  

16 They were a wall unto us both by night and day, all the while they were with them bringing the sheep.  

17 Now therefore know and consider what thou wilt do; for evil is determined against our master, and against all his household: for he is such a son of Belial, that a man cannot speak to him.  

18 Then Abigail made haste, and took two hundred loaves, and two bottles of wine, and five sheep ready dressed, and five measures of parched corn, and an hundred clusters of raisins, and two hundred cakes of figs, and laid them on asses.  

19 And she said unto her servants, Go on before me; behold, I come after you. But she told not her husband Nabal.  

20 And it was so, as she rode on the ass, that she came down by the covert of the hill, and, behold, David and his men came down against her; and she met them.  

21 Now David had said, Surely in vain have I kept all that this fellow hath in the wilderness, so that nothing was missed of all that pertained unto him: and he hath requited me evil for good.  

22 So and more also do God unto the enemies of David, if I leave of all that pertain to him by the morning light any that pisseth against the wall.  

23 And when Abigail saw David, she hasted, and lighted off the ass, and fell before David on her face, and bowed herself to the ground,  

24 And fell at his feet, and said, Upon me, my lord, upon me let this iniquity be: and let thine handmaid, I pray thee, speak in thine audience, and hear the words of thine thine handmaid.  

25 Let not my lord, I pray thee, regard this man of Belial, even Nabal: for as his name is, so is he; Nabal is his name, and brings folly is with him: but I thine handmaid saw not the young men of my lord, whom thou didst send.  

26 Now therefore, my lord, as the LORD liveth, and as thy soul liveth, seeing the LORD hath withholden thee from coming to shed blood, and from avenging thyself with thine own hand, now let thine enemies, and they that seek evil to my lord, be as Nabal.  

27 And now this blessing which thine handmaid hath brought unto my lord, let it even be given unto the young men that follow my lord.  

28 I pray thee, forgive the trespass of thine handmaid: for the LORD will certainly make my lord a sure house; because my lord fighteth the battles of the LORD, and evil hath not been found in thee all thy days.  

29 Yet a man is risen to pursue thee, and to seek thy soul: but the soul of my lord shall be bound in the bundle of life with the LORD thy God; and the souls of thine enemies, them shall he sling out, as out of the middle of a sling.  

30 And it shall come to pass, when the LORD shall have done to my lord according to all the good that he hath spoken concerning thee, and and shall have appointed thee ruler over Israel;  

31 That this shall be no grief unto thee, nor offence of heart unto my lord, either that thou hast shed blood causeless, or that my lord hath avenged himself: but when the LORD shall have dealt well with for my lord, then remember thine handmaid.  

32 And David said to Abigail, Blessed be the LORD God of Israel, which sent thee this day to meet me:  

33 And blessed be thy advice, and blessed be thou, which hast kept me this day from coming to shed blood, and from avenging myself with mine own hand.  

34 For in very deed, as the LORD God of Israel liveth, which hath kept me back from hurting thee, except thou hadst hasted and come to meet me, surely there had not been left unto Nabal by the morning light any that pisseth against the wall.  

35 So David received of her hand that which she had brought him, and said unto her, Go up in peace to thine house; see, I have hearkened to thy voice, and have accepted thy person.  

36 And Abigail came to Nabal; and, behold, he held a feast in his house, like the feast of a king; and the king's heart was merry within him, for he was very drunken: wherefore she told him nothing, less or more, until the morning light.  

37 But it came to pass in the morning, when the wine was gone out of Nabal, and his wife had told him these things, that his heart died within him, and he became as a stone.  

38 And it came to pass about ten days after, that the LORD smote Nabal, that he died.  

39 And when David heard that Nabal was dead, he said, Blessed be the LORD, that hath pleaded the cause of my reproach from the hand of Nabal, and hath kept his servant from evil: for the LORD hath returned the wickedness of Nabal upon his own head.  

40 And David sent and communed with Abigail, to take her to him to wife.  

41 And when the servants of David were come to Abigail to Carmel, they spoke unto her, saying, David sent us unto thee, to take thee to him to wife. 

42 And she arose, and bowed herself on her face to the earth, and said, Behold, let thine handmaid be a servant to wash the feet of the servants of my lord.  

43 And Abigail hasted, and arose, and rode upon an ass, with five damsels of hers that went after her; and preparation to the messengers of David, and became his wife.  

44 David also took Ahinoam of Jezreel; and they were also both of them his wives.  

45 But Saul had given Michal his daughter, David's wife, to Phalti the son of Laish, which was of Gallim.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Who is David? and who is the son of Jesse?"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about ignorance
**Biblical context:** This is about contempt for authority - Nabal shows deliberate disrespect for David's divine calling, showing the folly of rejecting God's chosen.

### "The soul of my lord shall be bound in the bundle of life with the LORD thy God"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about physical protection
**Biblical context:** This is about divine preservation - Abigail speaks of eternal security under God's protection, showing spiritual truth about divine care.

**Chapter 25 demonstrates the power of wisdom to prevent disaster and the certainty of divine justice:** Samuel's death marks transition from prophetic to kingly leadership. David's movement to wilderness shows continued fugitive status. Nabal's wealth and folly contrast material prosperity with spiritual poverty. His wife Abigail's wisdom and beauty show God's provision through righteous people. David's request for provisions shows reasonable expectation of hospitality. Nabal's contemptuous response shows how wealth can produce arrogance and blindness to divine purposes. David's anger shows righteous response to injustice and ingratitude. The young man's report to Abigail shows how wisdom seeks to prevent disaster. Abigail's preparation of generous gifts shows wisdom in action. Her haste shows urgency in preventing bloodshed. Her meeting with David shows courage to confront dangerous situations. Her humble posture shows proper respect for authority. Her appeal shows wisdom in acknowledging David's divine calling while taking responsibility. Her reference to bundle of life shows spiritual insight into divine protection. Her recognition of David's future kingship shows prophetic understanding. David's acceptance of her wisdom shows openness to divine guidance. Nabal's drunken feast shows how sin leads to spiritual blindness. His death shows divine justice against wickedness. David's recognition of divine justice shows proper understanding of God's ways. His marriage to Abigail shows how wisdom brings blessing. This chapter establishes crucial themes: wisdom prevents disaster, divine justice is certain, wealth without character leads to destruction, and God uses righteous people to protect His chosen. It demonstrates that God's chosen face both opposition and protection, that wisdom requires courage and action, and that divine justice prevails over human wickedness. The chapter shows Abigail as model of wisdom - discerning, courageous, humble, and spiritually insightful. It illustrates the principle that wisdom prevents disaster, that divine justice is certain, and that God uses righteous people to accomplish His purposes. The chapter points forward to Christ as the perfect wisdom who prevents eternal disaster, brings perfect justice, and provides eternal salvation. The bundle of life points forward to eternal security in Christ. The chapter demonstrates that when God's chosen face injustice, wisdom prevents disaster, divine justice prevails, and God uses righteous people to protect and bless His chosen, showing His comprehensive care for His purposes.
