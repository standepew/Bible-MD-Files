## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Ziphites came to Saul at Gibeah, saying David hid in hill of Hachilah before Jeshimon. Saul arose, went down to wilderness of Ziph with three thousand chosen men of Israel to seek David in wilderness of Ziph. Saul pitched in hill of Hachilah before Jeshimon by way. David abode in wilderness, saw Saul came after him into wilderness. David sent spies, understood Saul came in very deed. David arose, came to place where Saul pitched; David beheld place where Saul lay, and Abner son of Ner, captain of his host; Saul lay in trench, people pitched round about him. David answered Ahimelech Hittite and Abishai son of Zeruiah, brother to Joab, asking who would go down with him to Saul's camp. Abishai said he would go down. David and Abishai came to people by night; Saul lay sleeping in trench, spear stuck in ground at bolster; Abner and people lay round about him. Abishai told David God delivered enemy into his hand that day, let him smite him with spear to earth at once, not smite second time. David told Abishai not to destroy him; who could stretch hand against LORD's anointed and be guiltless? David said as LORD lives, LORD would smite him, or his day come to die, or descend into battle and perish. LORD forbid stretching hand against LORD's anointed; take spear at bolster and cruse of water, let them go. David took spear and cruse of water from Saul's bolster; they got away, no man saw or knew, neither awaked; deep sleep from LORD fell upon them. David went to other side, stood on hill top afar off, great space between them. David cried to people and Abner son of Ner, asking if Abner wouldn't answer. Abner answered asking who was crying to king. David told Abner he was valiant man, none like him in Israel, why didn't he keep lord king; one of people came in to destroy king lord. This wasn't good; as LORD lives, they were worthy to die for not keeping master, LORD's anointed. See where king's spear and cruse of water at bolster. Saul knew David's voice, asked if this was his voice, son David. David said it was his voice, lord king. He asked why lord pursued after servant, what he had done, what evil in his hand. Let lord king hear servant's words. If LORD stirred up against him, let him accept offering; if children of men, cursed before LORD; they drove him from abiding in LORD's inheritance, saying go serve other gods. Let not his blood fall to earth before LORD; king of Israel came out to seek flea as hunting partridge in mountains. Saul said he sinned, return son David, would no more harm him because his soul was precious in David's eyes that day; he played fool, erred exceedingly. David answered, behold king's spear, let young man come fetch it. LORD render to every man his righteousness and faithfulness; LORD delivered him into his hand that day, but wouldn't stretch hand against LORD's anointed. As his life was precious in his eyes that day, let his life be precious in LORD's eyes, deliver him from all tribulation. Saul told David, blessed be son David; he would both do great things and still prevail. David went on his way, Saul returned to his place.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### HACHILAH
- **Meaning:** "Dark" or "Sickness"
- **Context:** Hill where David hid from Saul
- **Significance:** Place of testing and divine protection
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual testing (1 Peter 1:7)

### TRENCH
- **Meaning:** Defensive ditch or pit
- **Context:** Where Saul slept vulnerably
- **Significance:** Place of human weakness and divine opportunity
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual vulnerability (2 Corinthians 12:10)

### CRUSE
- **Meaning:** Small jar or vessel
- **Context:** Container for water taken from Saul
- **Significance:** Evidence of David's restraint
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual testimony (2 Corinthians 3:2)

### PARTRIDGE
- **Meaning:** Game bird
- **Context:** David's description of himself as prey
- **Significance:** Humility in face of persecution
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual humility (Philippians 2:8)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 And the Ziphites came unto Saul to Gibeah, saying, Doth not David hide himself in the hill of Hachilah, which is before Jeshimon?  

2 Then Saul arose, and went down to the wilderness of Ziph, having three thousand chosen men of Israel with him, to seek David in the wilderness of Ziph.  

3 And Saul pitched in the hill of Hachilah, which is before Jeshimon, by the way. But David abode in the wilderness, and he saw that Saul came after him into the wilderness.  

4 David therefore sent out spies, and understood that Saul was come in very deed.  

5 And David arose, and came to the place where Saul had pitched: and David beheld the place where Saul lay, and Abner the son of Ner, the captain of his host: and Saul lay in the trench, and the people pitched round about him.  

6 Then answered David and said to Ahimelech the Hittite, and to Abishai the son of Zeruiah, brother to Joab, saying, Who will go down with me to Saul to the camp? And Abishai said, I will go down with thee.  

7 So David and Abishai came to the people by night: and, behold, Saul lay sleeping within the trench, and his spear stuck in the ground at his bolster: but Abner and the people lay round about him.  

8 Then said Abishai to David, God hath delivered thine enemy into thine hand this day: now therefore let me smite him, I pray thee, with the spear even to the earth at once, and I will not smite him the second time.  

9 And David said to Abishai, Destroy him not: for who can stretch forth his hand against the LORD's anointed, and be guiltless?  

10 David said furthermore, As the LORD liveth, the LORD shall smite him; or his day shall come to die; or he shall descend into battle, and perish.  

11 The LORD forbid that I should stretch forth mine hand against the LORD's anointed: but, I pray thee, take thou now the spear that is at his bolster, and the cruse of water, and let us go.  

12 So David took the spear and the cruse of water from Saul's bolster; and they gat them away, and no man saw it, nor knew it, it, neither awaked: for they were all asleep; because a deep sleep from the LORD was fallen upon them.  

13 Then David went over to the other side, and stood on the top of an hill afar off; a great space being between them:  

14 And David cried to the people, and to Abner the son of Ner, saying, Answerest thou not, Abner? Then Abner answered and said, Who art thou that criest to the king?  

15 And David said to Abner, Art not thou a valiant man? and who is like to thee in Israel? wherefore then hast thou not kept thy lord the king? for there came one of the people in to destroy the king thy lord.  

16 This thing is not good that thou hast done. As the LORD liveth, ye are worthy to die, because ye have not kept your master, the LORD's anointed. And now see where the king's spear is, and the cruse of water that was at his bolster.  

17 And Saul knew David's voice, and said, Is this thy voice, my son David? And David said, It is my voice, my lord, O king.  

18 And he said, Wherefore doth my lord thus pursue after his servant? for what have I done? or what evil is in mine hand?  

19 Now therefore, I pray thee, let my lord the king hear the words of his servant. If the LORD have stirred thee up against me, let him accept an offering: but if they be the children of men, cursed be they before the LORD; for they have driven me out this day from abiding in the inheritance of the LORD, saying, Go, serve other gods.  

20 Now therefore, let not my blood fall to the earth before the face of the LORD: for the king of Israel is come out to seek a flea, as when one doth hunt a partridge in the mountains.  

21 Then said Saul, I have sinned: return, my son David: for I will no more do thee harm, because my soul was precious in thine eyes this day: because I have played the fool, and have erred exceedingly.  

22 And David answered and said, Behold the king's spear! and let one of the young men come over and fetch it.  

23 The LORD render to every man his righteousness and his faithfulness: for the LORD delivered thee into my hand to day, but I would not stretch forth mine hand against the LORD's anointed.  

24 And, behold, as thy life was much set by this day in mine eyes, so let my life be much set by in the eyes of the LORD, and let him deliver me out of all tribulation.  

25 And Saul said to David, Blessed be thou, my son David: thou shalt both do great things, and also shalt still prevail.  

26 And David went on his way, and Saul returned to his place.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Destroy him not: for who can stretch forth his hand against the LORD's anointed, and be guiltless?"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about political loyalty
**Biblical context:** This is about respect for divine authority - David consistently refuses to harm God's anointed, showing reverence for divine appointment over personal advantage.

### "Because a deep sleep from the LORD was fallen upon them"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about natural sleep
**Biblical context:** This is about divine intervention - God caused supernatural sleep to protect His purposes, showing His control over human circumstances.

David's appeal for divine justice shows trust in God rather than human vengeance. His desire for life to be precious to God shows proper spiritual priorities. Saul's blessing shows temporary recognition of David's divine calling.
