## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Philistines gathered armies for warfare to fight Israel. Achish told David assuredly he'd go out to battle with him and his men. David told Achish he'd know what servant could do. Achish told David he'd make him keeper of his head forever. Samuel was dead, Israel lamented him, buried him in Ramah in his city. Saul had put away familiar spirits and wizards from land. Philistines gathered, pitched in Shunem; Saul gathered Israel, pitched in Gilboa. When Saul saw Philistine host, he was afraid, heart greatly trembled. When Saul enquired of LORD, LORD answered not by dreams, Urim, or prophets. Saul told servants to seek woman with familiar spirit to enquire of her. Servants said there was woman with familiar spirit at Endor. Saul disguised himself, put on other raiment, went with two men by night to woman, asked her to divine by familiar spirit and bring up whom he'd name. Woman said Saul knew what he did, cut off familiar spirits and wizards from land, why lay snare for her life to die. Saul swore to her by LORD, no punishment would happen for this. Woman asked whom to bring up; he said Samuel. When woman saw Samuel, cried with loud voice, told Saul he deceived her, he was Saul. King told her not to fear, what did she see? Woman told Saul she saw gods ascending from earth. He asked what form; she said old man coming up, covered with mantle. Saul perceived it was Samuel, stooped with face to ground, bowed. Samuel told Saul why he disquieted him to bring him up. Saul answered he was sore distressed; Philistines made war against him, God departed from him, answered no more by prophets or dreams; he called to know what to do. Samuel asked why he asked him, seeing LORD departed from him and become his enemy. LORD had done to him as spoken by Samuel; LORD rent kingdom from his hand, given to neighbor David. Because he didn't obey LORD's voice, execute fierce wrath upon Amalek, LORD did this to him that day. LORD would also deliver Israel with him into Philistines' hand; tomorrow he and sons would be with Samuel; LORD would deliver Israel's host into Philistines' hand. Saul fell straightway on earth, sore afraid because of Samuel's words; no strength in him, eaten no bread all day or night. Woman came to Saul, saw he was sore troubled, said handmaid obeyed his voice, put life in hand, hearkened to words. Hearken to handmaid's voice, set morsel of bread before him to eat for strength when going way. He refused, wouldn't eat. Servants with woman compelled him; he hearkened to their voice, arose from earth, sat on bed. Woman had fat calf in house, hastened, killed it, took flour, kneaded, baked unleavened bread, brought before Saul and servants; they ate, rose up, went away that night.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### ENDOR
- **Meaning:** "Fountain" or "Spring"
- **Context:** Location of the witch Saul consulted
- **Significance:** Place of forbidden spiritual practices
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual deception (Revelation 22:15)

### FAMILIAR SPIRIT
- **Meaning:** Medium or necromancer
- **Context:** Woman who contacted the dead
- **Significance:** Forbidden means of seeking guidance
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual deception (2 Corinthians 11:14)

### URIM
- **Meaning:** "Lights"
- **Context:** Priestly means of divine guidance
- **Significance:** Legitimate means of seeking God's will
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual discernment (James 1:5)

### MANTLE
- **Meaning:** Cloak or outer garment
- **Context:** Samuel's appearance
- **Significance:** Symbol of prophetic authority
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual authority (Hebrews 1:3)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 And it came to pass in those days, that the Philistines gathered their armies together for warfare, to fight with Israel. And Achish said unto David, Know thou assuredly, that thou shalt go out with me to battle, thou and thy men.  

2 And David said to Achish, Surely thou shalt know what thy servant can do. And Achish said to David, Therefore will I make thee keeper of mine head for ever.  

3 Now Samuel was dead, and all Israel had lamented him, and buried him in Ramah, even in his own city. And Saul had put away those that had familiar spirits, and the wizards, out of the land.  

4 And the Philistines gathered themselves together, and came and pitched in Shunem: and Saul gathered all Israel together, and they pitched in Gilboa.  

5 And when Saul saw the host of the Philistines, he was afraid, and his heart greatly trembled.  

6 And when Saul enquired of the LORD, the LORD answered him not, neither by dreams, nor by Urim, nor by prophets.  

7 Then said Saul unto his servants, Seek me a woman that hath a familiar spirit, that I may go to her, and enquire of her. And his servants said to him, Behold, there is at Endor a woman that hath a familiar spirit.  

8 And Saul disguised himself, and put on other raiment, and he went, and two men with him, and they came to the woman by night: and he said, I pray thee, divine unto me by the familiar spirit, and bring me him up, whom I shall name unto thee.  

9 And the woman said unto him, Behold, thou knowest what Saul hath done, how he hath cut off those that had familiar spirits, and the wizards, out of the land: wherefore then layest thou a snare for my life, to cause me to die?  

10 And Saul sware to her by the LORD, saying, As the LORD liveth, there shall no punishment happen to thee for this thing.  

11 Then said the woman, Whom shall I bring up unto thee? And he said, Bring me up Samuel.  

12 And the woman saw Samuel, she cried with a loud voice: and the woman spake to Saul, saying, Why hast thou deceived me? for thou art Saul.  

13 And the king said unto her, Be not afraid: for what sawest thou? And the woman said unto Saul, I saw gods ascending out of the earth.  

14 And he said unto her, What form is he of? And she said, An old man cometh up; and he's covered with a mantle. And Saul perceived that it was Samuel, and he stooped with his face to the earth, and bowed himself.  

15 And Samuel said to Saul, Why hast thou disquieted me, to bring me up? And Saul answered, I am sore distressed; for the Philistines make war against them, and God is departed from me, and answereth me no more, neither by prophets, nor by dreams: therefore I have called thee, that thou mayest make known unto me what I shall do.  

16 Then said Samuel, Wherefore then dost thou ask of me, seeing the LORD is departed from thee, and is become thine enemy?  

17 And the LORD hath done to him, as he spake by me: for the LORD hath rent the kingdom out of thine hand, and given it to thy neighbour, even to David:  

18 Because thou obeyedst not the voice of the LORD, nor executedst his fierce wrath upon Amalek, therefore hath the LORD done this thing unto thee this day.  

19 Moreover the LORD will also deliver Israel with thee into the hand of the Philistines: and to morrow shalt thou and thy sons be with me: the LORD also shall deliver the host of Israel into the hand of the Philised.  

20 Then Saul fell straightway all along on the earth, and was sore afraid, because of the words of Samuel: and there was no strength in him; for he had eaten no bread all the day, nor all the night.  

21 And the woman came unto Saul, and saw that he was sore troubled, and said unto him, at the same time, Behold, thine handmaid hath obeyed thy voice, and I have put my life in my hand, and have hearkened unto the words which thou spakest unto me.  

22 Now therefore, I's pray thee, hearken thou also unto the voice of thine handmaid, and let me set a morsel of bread before thee; and eat, that thou mayest have strength, when thou goest on thy way.  

23 But he refused, and said, I will not eat. But his servants, together with the woman, compelled him; and he hearkened to their voice. So he arose from the earth, and of the earth, and sat upon the bed.  

24 And the woman had a fat calf in the house; and she hasted, and killed it, and took flour, and kneaded it, and did bake unleavened bread thereof:  

25 And she brought it before Saul, and before his servants; and they ate. Then they rose up, and went away that night.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "When Saul enquired of the LORD, the LORD answered him not"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about God not hearing
**Biblical context:** This is about divine judgment - God's silence comes from Saul's disobedience, showing that sin blocks divine communication.

### "To morrow shalt thou and thy sons be with me"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about going to heaven
**Biblical context:** This is about death - Samuel predicts Saul's death, not eternal destiny, showing final judgment.
