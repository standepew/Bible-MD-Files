## CHAPTER SUMMARY

David enquired of LORD about going to cities of Judah; LORD said go up. David asked where; LORD said Hebron. David went up with two wives Ahinoam Jezreelitess and Abigail Nabal's wife Carmelite. David brought men with him, every man with household; they dwelt in cities of Hebron. Men of Judah came, anointed David king over house of Judah. They told David men of Jabesh-gilead buried Saul. David sent messengers to Jabesh-gilead, blessed them of LORD for showing kindness to lord Saul, burying him. LORD show kindness and truth to them; David would requite kindness for doing this. Hands strengthened, be valiant; master Saul dead, house of Judah anointed David king. Abner son of Ner, captain of Saul's host, took Ish-bosheth Saul's son, brought to Mahanaim; made him king over Gilead, Ashurites, Jezreel, Ephraim, Benjamin, all Israel. Ish-bosheth Saul's son forty years old when began to reign over Israel, reigned two years. House of Judah followed David. David king in Hebron over house of Judah seven years six months. Abner son of Ner, servants of Ish-bosheth Saul's son, went from Mahanaim to Gibeon. Joab son of Zeruiah, servants of David, went, met at pool of Gibeon; sat down, one side pool, other side pool. Abner told Joab to let young men arise, play before them. Joab said let them arise. Twelve of Benjamin belonging to Ish-bosheth Saul, twelve servants of David arose, went over. Each caught fellow by head, thrust sword in fellow's side; fell down together; place called Helkath-hazzurim in Gibeon. Very sore battle that day; Abner beaten, men of Israel before servants of David. Three sons of Zeruiah there: Joab, Abishai, Asahel; Asahel as light of foot as wild roe. Asahel pursued Abner; turned not right or left from following Abner. Abner looked behind, asked if Asahel; answered yes. Abner told Asahel to turn aside right or left, lay hold on young man, take armour. Asahel wouldn't turn aside from following. Abner told Asahel to turn aside from following; why smite to ground, how hold face to Joab brother? He refused to turn aside; Abner with hinder end of spear smote under fifth rib, spear came out behind; fell there, died same place; as many as came to place Asahel fell stood still. Joab and Abishai pursued Abner; sun went down when came to hill Ammah before Giah by way of wilderness of Gibeon. Children of Benjamin gathered after Abner, became one troop, stood on hill top. Abner called to Joab, shall sword devour forever? Knowest not bitterness in latter end? How long ere bid people return from following brethren? Joab said as God lives, unless spoken, morning people gone from following brother. Joab blew trumpet, all people stood still, pursued Israel no more, fought no more. Abner and men walked all night through plain, passed over Jordan, went through Bithron, came to Mahanaim. Joab returned from following Abner; gathered all people, lacked of David's servants nineteen men and Asahel. Servants of David smitten of Benjamin and Abner's men, three hundred threescore men died. They took up Asahel, buried in sepulchre of father in Bethlehem. Joab and men went all night, came to Hebron at break of day.

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 And it came to pass after this, that David enquired of the LORD, saying, Shall I go up into any of the cities of Judah? And the LORD said unto him, Go up. And David said, Whither shall I go up? And he said, Unto Hebron.  

2 So David went up thither, and his two wives also, Ahinoam the Jezreelitess, and Abigail Nabal's wife the Carmelite.  

3 And his men that were with him did David bring up, every man with his household: and they dwelt in the cities of Hebron.  

4 And the men of Judah came, and there they anointed David king over the house of Judah. And they told David, saying, That the men of Jabesh-gilead were they that buried Saul.  

5 And David sent messengers unto the men of Jabesh-gilead, and said unto them, Blessed be ye of the LORD, that ye have shewed this kindness unto your lord, even unto Saul, and have buried him.  

6 And now the LORD shew kindness and truth unto you: and I also will requite you this kindness, because ye have done this thing.  

7 Therefore now let your hands be strengthened, and be ye valiant: for your master Saul is dead, and also the house of Judah have anointed me king over them.  

8 But Abner the son of Ner, captain of Saul's host, took Ish-bosheth the son of Saul, and brought him over to Mahanaim;  

9 And made him king over Gilead, and over the Ashurites, and over Jezreel, and over Ephraim, and over Benjamin, and over all Israel.  

10 Ish-bosheth Saul's son was forty years old when he began to reign over Israel, and reigned two years. But the house of Judah followed David.  

11 And the time that David was king in Hebron over the house of Judah was seven years and six months.  

12 And Abner the son of Ner, and the servants of Ish-bosheth the son of Saul, went out from Mahanaim to Gibeon.  

13 And Joab the son of Zeruiah, and the servants of David, went out, and met together by the pool of Gibeon: and they sat down, the one on the one side of the pool, and the said, and the other on the other side of the pool.  

14 And Abner said to Joab, Let the young men now arise, and play before us. And Joab said, Let them arise.  

15 Then there arose and went over by number twelve of Benjamin, which pertained to Ish-bosheth the son of Saul, and twelve of the servants of David.  

16 And they caught every one his fellow by the head, and thrust his sword in his fellow's side; so they fell down together: wherefore that place was called Helkath-hazzurim, which is in Gibeon.  

17 And there was a very sore battle that day; and Abner was beaten, and the men of Israel, before the servants of David.  

18 And there were three sons of Zeruiah there, Joab, and Abishai, and Asahel: and Asahel was as light of foot as a wild roe.  

19 And Asahel pursued after Abner; and in going he turned not to the right hand nor to the left from following Abner.  

20 Then Abner looked behind him, and said, Art thou Asahel? And he answered, I am.  

21 And Abner said to him, Turn thee aside to thy right hand or to thy left, and lay thee hold on one of the young men, and take thee his armour. But Asahel would not turn aside from following of him.  

22 And Abner said again to Asahel, Turn thee aside from following me: wherefore should I smite thee to his ground? how then should I hold up my face to Joab thy brother?  

23 Howbeit he refused to turn aside: wherefore Abner with the hinder end of the spear smote him under the fifth rib, that the spear came out behind him; and he fell down there, and died in the same place: and it came to pass, that as many as came to the place where Asahel fell down and died stood still.  

24 Joab also and Abishai pursued after Abner: and the sword went down when they were come to the hill of Ammah, that lieth before Giah by the way of the wilderness of Gibeon.  

25 And the children of Benjamin gathered themselves together after Abner, and became one troop, and stood on the top of an hill.  

26 Then Abner called to Joab, and said, Shall the sword devour for ever? knowest thou not that it will be bitterness in the latter end? how long shall it be then, ere thou bid the people return from following their brethren?  

27 And Joab said, As God liveth, unless thou hadst spoken, surely then in the morning the people had gone up every one from following his brother.  

28 So Joab blew a trumpet, and all the people stood still, and pursued after Israel no more, neither fought they any more.  

29 And Abner and his men walked all that night through the plain, and passed over Jordan, and went through all Bithron, and they came to Mahanaim.  

30 And Joab returned from following Abner: and when he had gathered all the people together, there lacked of David's servants nineteen men and Asahel.  

31 But the servants of David had smitten of Benjamin, and of Abner's men, so that three hundred and threescore men died.  

32 And they took up Asahel, and buried him in the sepulchre of his father, which was in Beth-lehem. And Joab and his men went all night, and they came to Hebron at break of day.

### "Shall the sword devour for ever?"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about military strategy
**Biblical context:** This is about civil war - Abner recognizes the futility and bitterness of internal conflict, showing wisdom in seeking peace.

### "Asahel was as light of foot as a wild roe"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about athletic ability
**Biblical context:** This is about youthful ambition - Asahel's speed leads to his death, showing how ambition without wisdom brings destruction.

**Chapter 2 demonstrates the beginning of David's reign and the tragedy of civil war:** David's enquiry of LORD shows continued dependence on divine guidance. His movement to Hebron shows obedience to divine direction. The inclusion of households shows concern for family and followers. The anointing by Judah shows recognition of divine calling. His gratitude to Jabesh-gilead shows character of honoring kindness. His promise of requital shows leadership that values loyalty. Abner's establishment of Ish-bosheth shows political opposition to divine purposes. The division of kingdom shows tragic consequences of rejecting God's chosen. The gathering at Gibeon shows confrontation between opposing forces. The contest of champions shows attempt to limit bloodshed. The escalation to full battle shows how conflict spreads. Asahel's pursuit shows youthful ambition without wisdom. Abner's warnings show attempts to prevent unnecessary death. Asahel's death shows tragic cost of pride. Joab and Abishai's pursuit shows loyalty to family. Abner's call for peace shows recognition of futility. Joab's response shows willingness to end conflict. The truce shows temporary peace. The casualties show cost of internal division. This chapter establishes crucial themes: divine guidance requires obedience, division brings tragedy, pride leads to destruction, and peace requires wisdom. It demonstrates that David's reign began with proper dependence on God but faced immediate opposition, that internal conflict brings greater cost than external enemies, and that wisdom can temporarily halt tragedy. The chapter shows David as leader who seeks divine guidance, honors kindness, values loyalty, and faces opposition with principle. It illustrates the principle that civil war brings greater tragedy than external conflict, that youthful pride leads to destruction, and that wisdom can bring temporary peace. The chapter points forward to Christ as the perfect king who brings perfect unity, whose reign ends all conflict, and whose wisdom brings eternal peace. The division points forward to how Christ unites divided humanity. The chapter demonstrates that when God's chosen begin their reign, they often face internal opposition that brings tragedy, requiring wisdom and principle to navigate division while maintaining divine purposes.
