Long war between house of Saul and house of David; David waxed stronger, house of Saul weaker. Saul had concubine Rizpah daughter of Aiah; Ish-bosheth told Abner why he went into father's concubine. Abner very wroth for Ish-bosheth's words, said dog's head, against Judah showed kindness to house of Saul father, brethren, friends, didn't deliver to David, charged with fault concerning woman. He couldn't answer Abner word again, feared him. Abner sent messengers to David, whose is land, make league, hand with him, bring all Israel to David. David said well, make league, but require not see face unless bring Michal Saul's daughter when come to see face. David sent messengers to Ish-bosheth Saul's son, deliver wife Michal espoused for hundred foreskins of Philistines. Ish-bosheth sent, took her from husband Phaltiel son of Laish. Husband went with her weeping to Bahurim. Abner told him go, return; he returned. Abner had communication with elders of Israel, sought David in times past to be king; do it; LORD spoken of David, by hand of servant David save people Israel from Philistines and enemies. Abner spoke to Benjamin, went to David in Hebron all good to Israel, good to house of Benjamin. Abner came to David in Hebron, twenty men with him. David made Abner and men feast. Abner told David would arise, go, gather all Israel to lord king, make league, reign over heart's desire. David sent Abner away, went in peace. Servants of David and Joab came from pursuing troop, brought great spoil; Abner not with David in Hebron, sent away, gone in peace. Joab and host came, told Joab Abner came to king, sent away, gone in peace. Joab came to king, what done? Abner came, why sent away, gone? Knowest Abner came to deceive, know going out, coming in, know all done. Joab came from David, sent messengers after Abner, brought from well of Sirah; David knew not. Abner returned to Hebron, Joab took aside in gate to speak quietly, smote under fifth rib, died, for blood of Asahel brother. David heard, said I and kingdom guiltless before LORD forever from blood of Abner son of Ner; let rest on head of Joab, father's house; not fail from house of Joab one with issue, leper, leaning on staff, falling on sword, lacking bread. Joab and Abishai brother slew Abner, slew brother Asahel at Gibeon in battle. David told Joab, people, rend clothes, gird sackcloth, mourn before Abner. King David followed bier. Buried Abner in Hebron; king lifted voice, wept at grave; all people wept. King lamented over Abner, died Abner as fool dieth? Hands not bound, feet in fetters; as man falleth before wicked men, fallest thou. All people wept again. People came to cause David eat meat while day, David swore, so do God to me, more also, if taste bread or ought till sun down. All people took notice, pleased; whatsoever king did pleased all people. All people and Israel understood that day not of king to slay Abner son of Ner. King told servants, know ye not prince and great man fallen this day in Israel? I am this day weak, though anointed king; sons of Zeruiah too hard for me; LORD shall reward doer of evil according to wickedness.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### DAN TO BEER-SHEBA
- **Meaning:** "From north to south"
- **Context:** Complete extent of Israel
- **Significance:** Full territory under David's future reign
* **Spiritual application:** Complete spiritual dominion (Colossians 1:18)

### RIZPAH
- **Meaning:** "Hot coal"
- **Context:** Saul's concubine
- **Significance:** Source of conflict between Abner and Ish-bosheth
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual purity (1 Timothy 4:12)

### SIRAH
- **Meaning:** "Baldness"
- **Context:** Well where Abner was intercepted
- **Significance:** Place of treachery and death
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual danger (1 Peter 5:8)

### SACKCLOTH
- **Meaning:** "Coarse cloth"
- **Context:** Garment of mourning
- **Significance:** Expression of grief and repentance
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual mourning (Matthew 5:4)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 Now there was long war between the house of Saul and the house of David: but David waxed stronger and stronger, and the house of Saul waxed weaker and weaker.  

2 And unto David were sons born in Hebron: and his firstborn was Amnon, of Ahinoam the Jezreelitess;  

3 And his second, Chileab, of Abigail the wife of Nabal the Carmelite; and the third, Absalom the son of Maacah the daughter of Talmai king of Geshur;  

4 And the fourth, Adonijah the son of Haggith; and the fifth, Shephatiah the son of Abital;  

5 And the sixth, Ithream, by Eglah David's wife. These were born to David in Hebron.  

6 And it came to pass, while there was war between the house of Saul and the house of David, that Abner made himself strong for the house of Saul.  

7 And Saul had a concubine, whose name was Rizpah, the daughter of Aiah: and Ish-bosheth said to Abner, Wherefore hast thou gone in unto my father’s concubine?  

8 Then was Abner very wroth for the words of Ish-bosheth, and said, Am I a dog's head, which against Judah do shew kindness this day unto the house of Saul thy father, to his brethren, and to his friends, and have not delivered thee into the hand of David, that thou chargest me to day with a fault concerning this woman?  

9 So do God to Abner, and more also, except, as the LORD hath sworn to David, even so I do to him;  

10 To translate the kingdom from the house of Saul, and to set up the throne of David over Israel and over Judah, from Dan even to Beer-sheba.  

11 And he could not answer Abner a word again, because he feared him.  

12 And Abner sent messengers to David on his behalf, saying, Whose is the land? saying also, Make thy league with me, and, behold, my hand shall be with thee, to bring about all Israel unto thee.  

13 And he said, Well; I will make a league with thee: but one thing I require of thee, that is, Thou shalt not see my face, except thou first bring Michal Saul’s daughter, when thou comest to see my face.  

14 And David sent messengers to Ish-bosheth Saul's son, saying, Deliver me my wife Michal, which I espoused to me for an hundred foreskins of the Philistines.  

15 And Ish-bosheth sent, and took her from her husband, even from Phaltiel the son of Laish.  

16 And her husband went with her along weeping behind her to Bahurim. Then said Abner unto him, Go, return. And he returned.  

17 And Abner had communication with the elders of Israel, saying, Ye sought for David in times past to be king over you:  

18 Now then do it: for the LORD hath spoken of David, saying, By the hand of my servant David I will save my people Israel out of the hand of the Philistines, and out of the hand of all their enemies.  

19 And Abner also spake in the ears of Benjamin: and Abner went also to speak in the ears of David in Hebron all that seemed good to Israel, and that seemed good to the whole house of Benjamin.  

20 So Abner came to David to Hebron, and twenty men with him. And David made Abner and the men that were with him a feast.  

21 And Abner said unto David, I will arise and go, and will gather all Israel unto my lord the king, that they may make a league with thee, and that thou mayest reign over all that thine heart desireth. And David sent Abner away; and he went in peace.  

22 And, behold, the servants of David and Joab came from pursuing a troop, and brought in a great spoil with them: but Abner was not with David in Hebron; for he had sent him away, and he was gone in peace.  

23 When Joab and all the host that was with him were come, they told Joab, saying, Abner the son of Ner came to the king, and he hath sent him away, and he is gone in peace.  

24 Then Joab came to the king, and said, What hast thou done? behold, Abner came unto thee; why is it that thou hast sent him away, and he is quite gone?  

25 Thou knowest Abner the son of Ner, that he came to deceive thee, and to know thy going out and thy coming in, and to know all that thou doest.  

26 And when Joab was come out from David, he sent messengers after Abner, which brought him again from the well of Sirah: but David knew it not.  

27 And when Abner was returned to Hebron, Joab took him aside in the gate to speak with him quietly, and smote him there under the fifth rib, that he died, for the blood of Asahel his brother.  

28 And afterward when David heard it, he said, I and my kingdom are guiltless before the LORD for ever from the blood of Abner the son of Ner:  

29 Let it rest on the head of Joab, and on all his father's house; and let there not fail from the house of Joab one that hath an issue, or that is a leper, or that leaneth on a staff, or that falleth on the sword, or that lacketh bread.  

30 So Joab and Abishai his brother slew Abner, because he had slain their brother Asahel at Gibeon in the battle.  

31 And David said to Joab, and to all the people that were with him, Rend your clothes, and gird you with sackcloth, and mourn before Abner. And king David himself followed the bier.  

32 And they buried Abner in Hebron: and the king lifted up his voice, and wept at the grave of Abner, and all the people wept.  

33 And the king lamented over Abner, and said, Died Abner as a fool dieth?  

34 Thy hands were not bound, nor thy feet put into fetters: as a man falleth before wicked men, so fallest thou. And all the people wept again over him.  

35 And when all the people came to cause David to eat meat while it was yet day, David sware, saying, So do God to me, and more also, if I taste bread, or ought else, till the sun be down.  

36 And all the people took notice of it, and it was pleased them: as whatsoever the king did pleased all the people.  

37 For all the people and all Israel understood that day that it was not of the king to slay Abner the son of Ner.  

38 And the king said unto his servants, Know ye not that there is a prince and a great man fallen this day in Israel?  

39 And I am this day weak, though anointed king; and these men the sons of Zeruiah be too hard for me: the LORD shall reward the doer of evil according to his wickedness.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "David waxed stronger and stronger, and the house of Saul waxed weaker and weaker"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about military strategy
**Biblical context:** This is about divine sovereignty - God's chosen grows stronger while opposition weakens, showing divine purpose in human affairs.

### "I am this day weak, though anointed king"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about personal failure
**Biblical context:** This is about royal humility - David acknowledges his limitations despite divine calling, showing proper recognition of human weakness.
