## CHAPTER SUMMARY

David gathered chosen men of Israel, thirty thousand. David arose, went with people from Baale of Judah to bring ark of God, name called by name of LORD of hosts dwelling between cherubims. Set ark of God on new cart, brought from house of Abinadab in Gibeah; Uzzah and Ahio, sons of Abinadab, drove new cart. Brought from house of Abinadab at Gibeah, accompanying ark of God; Ahio went before ark. David and all house of Israel played before LORD on instruments of fir wood: harps, psalteries, timbrels, cornets, cymbals. When came to Nachon's threshingfloor, Uzzah put forth hand to ark of God, took hold; oxen shook. Anger of LORD kindled against Uzzah; God smote him for error; died by ark of God. David displeased because LORD made breach upon Uzzah; called place Perez-uzzah. David afraid of LORD that day, said how shall ark of LORD come to me? David wouldn't remove ark of LORD to city of David; carried aside to house of Obed-edom the Gittite. Ark of LORD continued in house of Obed-edom the Gittite three months; LORD blessed Obed-edom and household. Told king David, LORD blessed house of Obed-edom and all pertaining because of ark of God. David went, brought up ark of God from house of Obed-edom into city of David with gladness. When they that bare ark of LORD had gone six paces, sacrificed oxen and fatlings. David danced before LORD with all might; girded with linen ephod. David and all house of Israel brought up ark of LORD with shouting, sound of trumpet. Ark of LORD came into city of David; Michal Saul's daughter looked through window, saw king David leaping and dancing before LORD; despised him in heart. Brought in ark of LORD, set in place in midst of tabernacle David pitched; David offered burnt offerings and peace offerings before LORD. When David made end of burnt offerings and peace offerings, blessed people in name of LORD of hosts. Dealt among all people, whole multitude of Israel, women and men, to each one cake of bread, good piece of flesh, flagon of wine. All people departed to houses. David returned to bless household. Michal daughter of Saul came to meet David, said how glorious was king of Israel today, who uncovered himself today in eyes of handmaids of servants, as one of vain fellows shamelessly uncovereth himself! David told Michal, was before LORD, which chose me before father and all his house, appoint me ruler over people of LORD, over Israel: therefore will play before LORD. Will yet be more vile than thus, be base in own sight; of maidservants spoken of, shall be had in honour. Michal daughter of Saul had no child to day of death.

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 Again, David gathered together all the chosen men of Israel, thirty thousand.  

2 And David arose, and went with all the people that were with him from Baale of Judah, to bring up from thence the ark of God, whose name is called by the name of the LORD of hosts that dwelleth between the cherubims.  

3 And they set the ark of God upon a new cart, and brought it out of the house of Abinadab that was in Gibeah: and Uzzah and Ahio, the sons of Abinadab, drave the new cart.  

4 And they brought it out of the house of Abinadab which was at Gibeah, accompanying the ark of God: and Ahio went before the ark.  

5 And David and all the house of Israel played before the LORD on all manner of instruments made of fir wood, even on harps, and on psalteries, and on timbrels, and on cornets, and on cymbals.  

6 And when they came to Nachon's threshingfloor, Uzzah put forth his hand to the ark of God, and took hold of it; for the oxen shook it.  

7 And the anger of the LORD was kindled against Uzzah; and God smote him there for his error; and there he died by the ark of God.  

8 And David was displeased, because the LORD had made a breach upon Uzzah: and he called the name of the place Perez-uzzah to this day.  

9 And David was displeased, because the LORD had made a breach upon Uzzah: and he called the name of the place Perez-uzzah to this day.  

10 And David was afraid of the LORD that day, and said, How shall the ark of the LORD come to me?  

11 So David would not remove the ark of the LORD unto him into the city of David: but David carried it aside into the house of Obed-edom the Gittite.  

12 And the ark of the LORD continued in the house of Obed-edom the Gittite three months: and the LORD blessed Obed-edom, and all his household.  

13 And it was told king David, saying, The LORD hath blessed the house of Obed-edom, and all that pertaineth unto him, because of the ark of God. So David went and brought up the ark of God from the house of Obed-edom into the city of David with gladness.  

14 And it was so, that when they that bare the ark of the LORD had gone six paces, he sacrificed oxen and fatlings.  

15 And David danced before the LORD with all his might; and David was girded with a linen ephod.  

16 So David and all the house of Israel brought up the ark of the LORD with shouting, and with the sound of the trumpet.  

17 And as the ark of the LORD came into the city of David, Michal Saul's daughter looked through a window, and saw king David leaping and dancing before the LORD; and she despised him in her heart.  

18 And they brought in the ark of the LORD, and set it in its place, in the midst of the tabernacle that David had pitched for it: and David offered burnt offerings and peace offerings before the LORD.  

19 And as soon as David had made an end of offering burnt offerings and peace offerings, the LORD, he blessed the people in the name of the LORD of hosts.  

20 And he dealt among all the people, even among the whole multitude of Israel, as well to the women as men, to every one a cake of bread, and a good piece of flesh, and a flagon of wine. So all the people departed every one to his house.  

21 Then David returned to bless his household. And Michal the daughter of Saul came out to meet David, and said, How glorious was the king of Israel to day, who uncovered himself to day in the eyes of the handmaids of his servants, as one of the vain fellows shamelessly uncovereth himself!  

22 And David said unto Michal, It was before the LORD, which chose me before thy father, and before all his house, to appoint me ruler over the people of the LORD, over Israel: therefore will I play before the LORD.  

23 And I will yet be more vile than thus, and will be base in mine own sight: and of the maidservants which thou hast spoken of, of them shall I be had in honour.  

24 Therefore Michal the daughter of Saul had no child unto the day of her death.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "David danced before the LORD with all his might"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about emotional expression
**Biblical context:** This is about proper worship - David's uninhibited worship shows proper response to God's presence, contrasting with improper reverence.

### "Michal the daughter of Saul had no child unto the day of her death"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about infertility
**Biblical context:** This is about divine judgment - Michal's barrenness results from contempt for proper worship, showing spiritual consequences.

**Chapter 6 demonstrates proper and improper approaches to divine presence:** David's gathering shows leadership in spiritual matters. The ark transportation shows desire for God's presence. The use of new cart shows human method rather than divine instruction. The musical celebration shows proper joy in approaching God. Uzzah's action shows irreverence toward holy things. The sudden judgment shows God's holiness requires proper approach. David's displeasure shows understanding of divine justice. The naming of place shows memorial of lesson. David's fear shows proper reverence after judgment. The temporary placement shows wisdom in learning from mistakes. Obed-edom's blessing shows divine presence brings blessing. David's renewed attempt shows persistence in seeking God. The proper method shows learning from past errors. The six paces and sacrifice shows proper approach with reverence. David's uninhibited worship shows proper response to God's presence. Michal's observation shows human perspective on divine worship. Her contempt shows pride and misunderstanding. David's explanation shows proper priority of God over human opinion. His statement about being more vile shows commitment to God over reputation. The generous provision shows worship includes blessing others. Michal's barrenness shows consequences of contempt for divine things. This chapter establishes crucial themes: divine presence requires proper approach, human methods may be inadequate, reverence is essential in worship, and contempt for divine things brings consequences. It demonstrates that God's presence brings blessing but requires proper approach, that human enthusiasm without reverence brings judgment, and that true worship prioritizes God over human opinion. The chapter shows David as model worshipper who learns from mistakes, approaches God with reverence, expresses uninhibited joy, and prioritizes divine approval over human opinion. It illustrates the principle that divine presence is both blessing and responsibility, that proper worship requires both joy and reverence, and that contempt for divine things brings spiritual consequences. The chapter points forward to Christ as the perfect presence of God, whose approach requires perfect reverence, whose worship brings perfect blessing, and whose presence transforms all who properly approach. The uninhibited worship points forward to perfect spiritual worship. The chapter demonstrates that when God's people seek His presence, they must approach with proper reverence, learn from mistakes, express joy appropriately, and prioritize divine approval over human opinion, showing the pattern for true worship that honors God in both method and attitude.
