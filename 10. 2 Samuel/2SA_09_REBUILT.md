## CHAPTER SUMMARY

David asked if any left of house of Saul to show kindness for Jonathan's sake. House of Saul had servant named Ziba; called to David, king asked if Ziba; answered servant. King asked if any left of house of Saul to show kindness of God to him. Ziba said Jonathan had son lame on feet. King asked where; Ziba said in house of Machir son of Ammiel in Lo-debar. King David sent, fetched from house of Machir son of Ammiel from Lo-debar. Mephibosheth son of Jonathan son of Saul came to David, fell on face, did reverence. David said Mephibosheth; answered behold servant. David said fear not, would show kindness for Jonathan father's sake, restore all land of Saul father; eat bread at table continually. Bowed, said what is servant that look upon dead dog. King called Ziba Saul's servant, gave master's son all that pertained to Saul and house. Ziba, sons, servants till land for him, bring fruits for master's son food; Mephibosheth master's son eat bread always at table. Ziba had fifteen sons, twenty servants. Ziba told king, as lord king commanded servant, so will servant do. Mephibosheth shall eat at table as one of king's sons. Mephibosheth had young son Micha; all that dwelt in house of Ziba servants to Mephibosheth. Mephibosheth dwelt in Jerusalem; ate continually at king's table; lame on both feet.

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 And David said, Is there yet any that is left of the house of Saul, that I may shew him kindness for Jonathan's sake?  

2 And there was of the house of Saul a servant whose name was Ziba. And when they had called him unto David, the king said unto him, Art thou Ziba? And he said, Thy servant is he.  

3 And the king said, Is there not yet any of the house of Saul, that I may shew the kindness of God unto him? And Ziba said unto the king, Jonathan hath yet a son, which is lame on his feet.  

4 And the king said unto him, Where is he? And Ziba said unto the king, Behold, he is in the house of Machir, the son of Ammiel, in Lo-debar.  

5 Then king David sent, and fetched him out of the house of Machir, the son of Ammiel, from Lo-debar.  

6 Now when Mephibosheth, the son of Jonathan, the son of Saul, was come unto David, he fell on his face, and did reverence. And David said, Mephibosheth. And he answered, Behold thy servant!  

7 And David said unto him, Fear not: for I will surely shew thee kindness for Jonathan thy father's sake, and will restore thee all the land of Saul thy father; and thou shalt eat bread at my table continually.  

8 And he bowed himself, and said, What is thy servant, that thou shouldest look upon such a dead dog as I am?  

9 Then the king called to Ziba, Saul's servant, and said unto him, I have given unto thy master's son all that pertained to Saul and to all his house.  

10 Thou therefore, and thy sons, and thy servants, shall till the land for him, and thou shalt bring in the fruits, that thy master's son may have food to eat: but Mephibosheth thy master's son shall eat bread alway at my table. Now Ziba had fifteen sons and twenty servants.  

11 Then said Ziba unto the king, According to all that my lord the king hath commanded his servant, so shall thy servant do. As for Mephibosheth, said the king, he shall eat at my table, as one of the king's sons.  

12 And Mephibosheth had a young son, whose name was Micha. And all that dwelt in the house of Ziba were servants unto Mephibosheth.  

13 So Mephibosheth dwelt in Jerusalem: for he did eat continually at the king's table; and was lame on both his feet.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Is there yet any that is left of the house of Saul, that I may shew him kindness for Jonathan's sake?"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about political maneuvering
**Biblical context:** This is about covenant faithfulness - David seeks to honor his covenant with Jonathan, showing true loyalty extends beyond death.

### "Mephibosheth shall eat at my table, as one of the king's sons"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about social status
**Biblical context:** This is about royal honor - David elevates Mephibosheth to family status, showing true kindness restores honor and provision.
