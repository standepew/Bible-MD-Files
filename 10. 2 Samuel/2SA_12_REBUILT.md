## CHAPTER SUMMARY

LORD sent Nathan to David. Nathan came, said two men in one city; one rich, one poor. Rich man had exceeding many flocks and herds. Poor man had nothing, save one little ewe lamb, bought and nourished; grew up with him, children; ate own meat, drank own cup, lay in bosom, as daughter. Traveler came to rich man; spared to take of own flock and herd to dress for wayfaring man; took poor man's lamb, dressed for man. David's anger greatly kindled against man; told Nathan, as LORD lives, man done this shall die; restore lamb fourfold, no pity. Nathan told David, thou art the man. Thus says LORD God of Israel, anointed king over Israel, delivered from Saul; gave master's house, master's wives into bosom, house of Israel and Judah; if too little, would have given more. Why despised commandment of LORD, do evil in sight? killed Uriah Hittite with sword, taken his wife, slain with sword of children of Ammon. Sword shall never depart from house; despised, taken wife of Uriah Hittite. Raise up evil against from own house, take wives before eyes, give to neighbor, lie with wives in sight of sun. Did secretly; will do before all Israel, before sun. David told Nathan, sinned against LORD. Nathan said LORD put away sin; not die. By deed gave great occasion to enemies of LORD to blaspheme, child born shall die. Nathan departed to house. LORD struck child Uriah's wife bare David, very sick. David besought God for child; fasted, went in, lay all night upon earth. Elders of house arose, went to raise from earth; wouldn't, didn't eat bread with them. Seventh day, child died. Servants of David feared to tell child dead; while child alive, spoke, wouldn't hearken; how vex if dead? David saw servants whisper, perceived child dead; asked servants, child dead? They said dead. David arose from earth, washed, anointed, changed apparel, came to house of LORD, worshipped; came to house, required, set bread before, ate. Servants asked, what done? fasted and wept for child alive; child dead, rose and ate bread. David said, while child alive, fasted and wept; said, who can tell whether GOD gracious, child may live? Now dead, why fast? can bring back? shall go to him, not return. David comforted Bath-sheba wife, went in, lay with her; bore son, called Solomon; LORD loved him. Sent by Nathan prophet; called Jedidiah, because of LORD. Joab fought Rabbah of children of Ammon, took royal city. Joab sent messengers to David, fought Rabbah, taken city of waters. Gather rest of people, encamp against city, take; lest take city, called after name. David gathered all people, went to Rabbah, fought, took. Took king's crown from head, weight talent of gold with precious stones; set on David's head. Brought forth spoil of city in great abundance. Brought forth people, put under saws, harrows of iron, axes of iron, made pass through brickkiln; did to all cities of children of Ammon. David and all people returned to Jerusalem.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### NATHAN
- **Meaning:** "Gift of God"
- **Context:** Prophet who confronted David
- **Significance:** Divine messenger of judgment and grace
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual confrontation (Galatians 6:1)

### EWES LAMB
- **Meaning:** "Young female sheep"
- **Context:** Poor man's precious possession
- **Significance:** Symbol of innocent victim
* **New Testament parallel:** Christ as Lamb (John 1:29)

### SOLOMON
- **Meaning:** "Peaceful"
- **Context:** Son born to David and Bathsheba
- **Significance:** Future king and temple builder
* **New Testament parallel:** Christ as Prince of Peace (Isaiah 9:6)

### JEDIDIAH
- **Meaning:** "Beloved of the LORD"
- **Context:** Alternative name for Solomon
- **Significance:** God's love despite circumstances
* **Spiritual application:** Divine love (Romans 5:8)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 And the LORD sent Nathan unto David. And he came unto him, and said unto him, There were two men in one city; the one rich, and the other poor.  

2 The rich man had exceeding many flocks and herds:  

3 But the poor man had nothing, save one little ewe lamb, which he had bought and nourished up: and it grew up together with him, and with his children; it did eat of his own meat, and drank of his own cup, and lay in his bosom, and was unto him as a daughter.  

4 And there came a traveller unto the rich man, and he spared to take of his own flock and of his own herd, to dress for the wayfaring man that was come unto him; but took the poor man's lamb, and dressed it for the man that was come to him.  

5 And David's anger was greatly kindled against the man; and he said to Nathan, As the LORD liveth, the man that hath done this thing shall surely die:  

6 And he shall restore the lamb fourfold, because he did this thing, and because he had no pity.  

7 And Nathan said to David, Thou art the man. Thus saith the LORD God of Israel, I anointed thee king over Israel, and I delivered thee out of the hand of Saul;  

8 And I gave thee thy master's house, and thy master's wives into thy bosom, and gave thee the house of Israel and of Judah; and if that had been too little, I would moreover have given unto thee such and such things.  

9 Wherefore hast thou despised the commandment of the LORD, to do evil in his sight? thou hast killed Uriah the in the Hittite with the sword, and hast taken his wife to be thy wife, and hast slain him with the sword of the children of Ammon.  

10 Now therefore the sword shall never depart from thine house; because thou hast despised me, and hath taken the wife of Uriah the Hittite to be thy wife.  

11 Thus saith the LORD, Behold, I is will raise up evil against thee out of thine own house, and I will take thy wives before thine eyes, and give them unto thy neighbour, and he shall lie with thy wives in the sight of this sun.  

12 For thou didst it secretly: but I will do this thing before all Israel, and before the sun.  

13 And David said unto Nathan, I have sinned against the LORD. And Nathan said unto David, The LORD also hath put away thy sin; thou shalt not die.  

14 Howbeit, because by this deed thou hast given great occasion to the enemies of the LORD to blaspheme, the child also that is born unto thee shall surely die.  

15 And Nathan departed unto his house. And the LORD struck the child that Uriah's wife bare unto David, and it was very sick.  

16 David therefore besought God for the child; and David fasted, and went in, and lay all night upon the earth.  

17 And the elders of his house arose, and went to him, to raise him up from the earth: but he would not, neither did he eat bread with them.  

18 And it came to pass on the seventh day, that the child died. And the servants of David feared to tell him that the child was dead: for they said, shall Behold, while the child was yet alive, we spake unto him, and he would not hearken unto our voice: how will he then vex himself, if we tell him that the child is dead?  

19 But when David saw that his servants whispered, David perceived that the child was dead: therefore David said unto his servants, Life is the child dead? And they said, He is dead.  

20 Then David arose from the earth, and wearing, and anointed himself, and changed his apparel, and came into the house of the LORD, and worshipped: then he came to his own house; and when he required, they set bread before him, and he did eat.  

21 Then said his servants unto him, What thing is this that thou hast done? thou didst fast and weep for the child, while it was brings alive; but when the child was dead, thou didst rise and eat bread.  

22 And he said, While the was child was yet alive, I fasted and wept: for I said, Who can tell whether GOD will be gracious to me, that the child may live?  

23 But now he is dead, wherefore should I fast? can I bring him back again? I shall go to him, but he shall not return to me.  

24 And David comforted Bath-sheba his wife, and went in unto her, and lay with her: and she bare a son, and he called his name Solomon: and the LORD loved him.  

25 And he sent by the hand of Nathan the prophet; and he called his name Jedidiah, because of the LORD.  

26 And Joab fought against Rabbah of the children of Ammon, and took the royal city.  

27 And Joab sent messengers to David, and said, I have fought against Rabbah, and have taken the city of waters.  

28 Now therefore gather the rest of the people together, and encamp against the city, and and take it: lest I take the city, and it be called after my name.  

29 And David gathered all the people together, and went to Rabbah, and fought against it, and took it.  

30 And he took their king's crown from off his head, the weight whereof was a talent of gold with the precious stones: and it was set on David's head.  

31 And he brought forth the spoil of the city in great abundance.  

32 And he brought forth the people that were therein, and put them under saws, and under harrows of iron, and under axes of iron, and made them pass through the brickkiln: and thus did he unto all the cities of the children of Ammon. So David and all the people returned unto Jerusalem.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Thou art the man"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about personal accusation
**Biblical context:** This is about divine conviction - Nathan's confrontation brings David to self-recognition of sin, showing how God uses prophets to bring conviction.

### "I shall go to him, but he shall not return to me"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about reincarnation
**Biblical context:** This is about hope after death - David expresses confidence in seeing the child again, showing hope beyond death.

This chapter establishes crucial themes: sin requires divine confrontation, confession brings forgiveness, consequences remain despite grace, and God's purposes continue through human failure. It demonstrates that David's sin brought divine confrontation leading to confession and forgiveness, but consequences remained, showing both grace and justice in divine dealing. The chapter shows David as model penitent who responds to conviction with confession, accepts consequences with worship, and continues in God's purposes despite failure. It illustrates the principle that sin brings divine confrontation, confession brings forgiveness, consequences remain, but God's purposes continue through grace. The chapter points forward to Christ as the perfect sacrifice who takes away sin completely, whose confession is unnecessary, whose death brings perfect forgiveness, and whose kingdom continues despite human failure. The confession points forward to Christ's perfect righteousness. The consequences point forward to Christ bearing all consequences. The chapter demonstrates that when God's people sin, He confronts them through prophets, brings them to confession, extends forgiveness while allowing consequences, and continues His purposes through grace, showing both His justice and His mercy in dealing with human failure.
