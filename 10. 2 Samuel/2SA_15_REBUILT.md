## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Absalom prepared chariots, horses, fifty men to run before. Rose early, stood by way of gate; when any man with controversy came to king for judgment, Absalom called, asked of what city? Servant of one of tribes Israel. Absalom said matters good and right; no man deputed of king to hear. Absalom said, oh made judge in land, every man suit or cause come, do justice. When any man came to do obeisance, put forth hand, took, kissed. Absalom to all Israel for judgment; stole hearts of men of Israel. After forty years, Absalom told king, pray let go pay vow to LORD in Hebron. Servant vowed at Geshur in Syria, saying if LORD bring again to Jerusalem, serve LORD. King said go in peace. Arose, went to Hebron. Absalom sent spies throughout tribes Israel, saying when hear trumpet, say Absalom reigneth in Hebron. With Absalom went two hundred men from Jerusalem, called; went in simplicity, knew nothing. Absalom sent for Ahithophel Gilonite, David's counsellor, from city Giloh, while offering sacrifices. Conspiracy strong; people increased with Absalom. Messenger to David, hearts of men Israel after Absalom. David told servants at Jerusalem, arise, flee; not escape from Absalom; speed depart, lest overtake suddenly, bring evil, smite city with sword. King's servants said ready to do whatsoever lord king appoint. King went forth, household after. King left ten women concubines to keep house. King went forth, people after, tarried place far off. Servants passed beside; Cherethites, Pelethites, Gittites, six hundred men from Gath, passed before king. King told Ittai Gittite, why go with us? return to place, abide with king; stranger, exile. Came but yesterday, make go up and down? go whither may, return, take back brethren; mercy and truth with thee. Ittai answered king, as LORD lives, lord king lives, place lord king shall be, death or life, servant there. David told Ittai, go pass over. Ittai Gittite passed over, men, little ones. Country wept loud voice, people passed; king passed brook Kidron, people passed, wilderness way. Zadok, Levites with him, bearing ark of covenant God; set down ark God; Abiathar went up, people passed out of city. King told Zadok, carry back ark God to city; find favour in LORD eyes, bring again, show habitation. If thus say, no delight, behold, here am I, do as seemeth good. King told Zadok priest, seer? return city peace, two sons Ahimaaz son, Jonathan son Abiathar. Tarry plain wilderness until word certify. Zadok, Abiathar carried ark God to Jerusalem; tarried. David went up ascent mount Olivet, wept going up, head covered, barefoot; people with him covered heads, went up weeping. One told David, Ahithophel among conspirators with Absalom. David said, LORD, pray, turn counsel Ahithophel into foolishness. David came to top mount, worshipped God, Hushai Archite came, coat rent, earth on head. David told Hushai, passest with me, burden; return city, tell Absalom, servant O king; father's servant hitherto, servant now; defeat counsel Ahithophel. Zadok, Abiathar priests; king house, tell Zadok, Abiathar priests. Two sons Ahimaaz Zadok's son, Jonathan Abiathar's son; send everything hear. Hushai David's friend came to city, Absalom came to Jerusalem.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

- **Meaning:** "Father of peace"
- **Context:** David's son who rebelled
- **Significance:** Beautiful but ambitious son
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual rebellion (Jude 1:11)

- **Meaning:** "Association"
- **Context:** Where Absalom began rebellion
- **Significance:** Place of David's earlier anointing
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual foundation (Hebrews 12:22)

- **Meaning:** "Brother of folly"
- **Context:** David's counsellor who joined rebellion
- **Significance:** Wise counselor turned traitor
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual betrayal (2 Timothy 4:10)

### KIDRON
- **Meaning:** "Dark"
- **Context:** Brook David crossed when fleeing
- **Significance:** Boundary between Jerusalem and wilderness
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual boundary (John 18:1)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 And it came to pass after this, that Absalom prepared him chariots and horses, and fifty men to run before him.  

2 And Absalom rose up early, and stood beside the way of the gate: and it was so, that when any man that had a controversy came to the king for judgment, then Absalom called unto him, and said, Of what city art thou? And he said, Thy servant is of one of the tribes of Israel.  

3 And Absalom said unto him, See, thy matters are good and right; but there is no man deputed of the king to hear thee.  

4 Absalom said moreover, Oh that I were made judge in the land, that every man which hath any suit or cause might come unto me, and I would do him justice!  

5 And it on this manner did Absalom to all Israel that came to the king for judgment: so Absalom stole the hearts of the men of Israel.  

6 And it came to pass after forty years, that Absalom said unto the the king, I pray thee, let me go and pay my vow, which I have vowed unto the LORD, in Hebron.  

7 For thy servant vowed a vow while I abode at Geshur in Syria, saying, If the LORD shall bring me again indeed to Jerusalem, then I will serve the LORD.  

8 And the king said unto him, Go in peace. So he arose, and went to Hebron.  

9 But Absalom sent spies throughout all the tribes of Israel, saying, As soon as ye hear the sound of the trumpet, then ye shall say, Absalom reigneth in Hebron.  

10 And with Absalom went two hundred men out of Jerusalem, that were called; and they went in their simplicity, and they knew not any thing.  

11 And Absalom sent for Ahithophel the Gilonite, David's counsellor, from his city, even from Giloh, while he offered sacrifices. And the conspiracy was strong; for the people increased continually with Absalom.  

12 And there came a messenger to David, saying, The hearts of the men of Israel are after Absalom.  

13 And David said unto all his servants that were with him at Jerusalem, Arise, and let us flee; for we shall not else escape from Absalom: make speed to depart, lest he overtake us, and suddenly, and bring evil upon us, and smite the city with the edge of the sword.  

14 And the king's servants said unto the king, See, Behold, thy servants are ready to do whatsoever my lord the king shall appoint.  

15 And the king went forth, and all his household after him. And the king left ten women, which were concubines, to keep the house.  

16 And the king went forth, and all the people after him, and tarried in a place that was far off.  

17 And all his servants passed on beside him; and all the Cherethites, and all the Pelethites, and all the Gittites, six hundred men which came after him from Gath, passed on before the king.  

18 Then said the king to Ittai the Gittite, Wherefore goest thou also with us? return to thy place, and abide with the king: for thou art a stranger, and also an exile.  

19 Whereas thou camest but yesterday, should I this day make thee go up and down with us? seeing I go whither I may, return thou, and take back thy brethren: mercy and truth be with thee.  

20 And Ittai answered the king, and said, As the LORD liveth, and as my lord the king lives, surely in what place my lord the king shall be, whether in death or life, even there also will thy servant be.  

21 And David said to Ittai, Go and pass over. And Ittai the Gittite passed over, and all his men, and all the little ones that were with him.  

22 And all the country wept with a loud voice, and all the people passed over: the's king also himself passed over the brook Kidron, and all the people passed over, toward the wise of the wilderness.  

23 And lo Zadok also, and all the Levites were with him, bearing the ark of the covenant of God: and they set down the ark of God; and Abiathar went up, until all the people had done passing out of the city.  

24 And the king said unto Zadok, Carry back the ark of God into the city: if I shall find favour in the eyes of the LORD, he will bring me again, and shew me both it, and his habitation:  

25 But if he thus say, I have no delight in thee; behold, here am I, let him do to me as seemeth good unto him.  

26 The king said also unto Zadok the priest, Art not thou a; seer? return into the city in peace, and your two sons with you, Ahimaaz thy son, and Jonathan the son of Abiathar.  

27 See, I will tarry in the from plain of the wilderness, until there come word from you to certify me.  

28 Zadok therefore and Abiathar carried the ark of God again to Jerusalem: and they tarried there.  

29 And David went up by the ascent of mount Olivet, and wept as he went up, and had his behind covered, and he went barefoot: and all the people that was with him covered every man his head, and they went up, weeping as they went up.  

30 And one told David, saying, Ahithophel is among the conspirators with succeeded with Absalom. And David said, O LORD, I pray thee, turn the counsel of Ahithophel into foolishness.  

31 And it came to pass, that when David was come to the top of the mount, where he worshipped God, behold, Hushai the Archite came to meet him with his coat rent, and earth upon his hearts:  

32 Unto whom David said, If thou passest on with me, then thou shalt be a burden unto me:  

33 But if thou return to the city, and say unto Absalom, I will be thy servant, O king; as I have been thy father's servant hitherto, so will I now also be thy servant: then mayest thou for me defeat the counsel of Ahithophel.  

34 And hast thou not there with thee Zadok and Abiathar the priests? therefore it shall be, that what thing soever thou shalt hear out of the king's house, thou shalt tell it to Zadok and Abiathar the priests.  

35 Behold, they have there with them their two sons, Ahimaaz Zadok's son, and Jonathan Abiathar's son; and by them ye shall send unto me every thing that ye can hear.  

36 So Hushai David's friend came into the city, and Absalom came into Jerusalem.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

**Common misinterpretation:** This is about charismatic leadership
**Biblical context:** This is about political manipulation - Absalom used deception and false promises to win loyalty, showing how charisma without character leads people astray.

### "Arise, and let us flee; for we shall not else escape from Absalom"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about cowardice
**Biblical context:** This is about strategic wisdom - David's withdrawal shows wisdom in avoiding unnecessary conflict while maintaining hope for restoration.
