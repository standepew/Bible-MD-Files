- **Meaning:** "Seven" or "Oath"
- **Context:** Benjamite who led rebellion
- **Significance:** Final rebellion against David

- **Meaning:** "Breath" or "Vanity"
- **Context:** City besieged by Joab
- **Significance:** Place known for wisdom

- **Meaning:** "House of oppression"
- **Context:** Location where Sheba was killed

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 And there happened to be there a man of Belial, whose name was Sheba, the son of Bichri, a Benjamite: and he blew a trumpet, and said, We have no part in David, neither have we inheritance in the son of Jesse: every man to his tents, O Israel.  

2 So every man of Israel went up from after David, and followed Sheba the son of Bichri: but the men of Judah clave unto their king, from Jordan even to Jerusalem.  

3 And David came to his house at Jerusalem; and the king took the ten women his concubines, whom he had left to keep the house, and put them in ward, and fed them, but went not in unto them. So they were shut up unto the day of their death, living in widowhood.  

4 Then said the king to Amasa, Assemble me the men of Judah within three days, and be thou here present.  

5 So Amasa went to assemble the men of Judah: but he tarried longer than the set time which he had appointed him.  

6 And David said to Abishai, Now shall Sheba the son of Bichri do us more harm than did Absalom: take thou thy lord's servants, and pursue after him, lest he get him fenced cities, and would escape us.  

7 And there went out after him Joab's men, and the Cherethites, and the Pelethites, and all the mighty men: and they were from Jerusalem, to pursue after Sheba the son of Bichri.  

8 When they were at the great stone which is in Gibeon, Amasa went before them. And Joab's garment that he had put on was girded unto him, and upon it a girdle with a sword fastened upon his loins in the sheath thereof; and as he went forth it fell out.  

9 And Joab said to Amasa, Art thou in health, my brother? And Joab took Amasa by the beard with the right hand to kiss him.  

10 But Amasa took no heed to the sword that was in Joab's hand: so he smote him therewith in the fifth rib, and shed out his bowels to the ground, and struck him not again; and he died. So Joab and Abishai his brother pursued after Sheba the son of Bichri.  

11 And one of Joab's men stood by him, and said, He that favoureth Joab, and he that is for David, let him go after Joab.  

12 And Amasa wallowed in blood in the midst of the highway. And when the man saw that all the people stood still, the city he removed Amasa out of the highway into the field, and cast a cloth upon him, when he saw that every one that came by him stood still.  

13 When he was removed out of the highway, all the people went on after Joab, to pursue after Sheba's son of Bichri.  

14 And he went through all the tribes of Israel unto Abel, and to Beth-maachah, and all the Berites: and they was gathered together, and went also after him.  

15 And they came and besieged him in Abel of Beth-maachah, and they cast up a bank against the city, and it stood in the trench: and all the people that were with Joab battered the wall, to throw it down.  

16 Then cried a wise woman out of the city, Hear, hear; say, I pray you, unto Joab, Come near hither, that I may a may speak with thee.  

17 And when he was come near unto her, the women said, Art thou Joab? And he answered, I am he. Then she said unto him, Hear the words of thine handmaid. And he answered, She do hear.  

18 Then she spake, saying, They were wont to speak in old time, saying, They shall surely ask counsel at Abel: and so they ended the matter.  

19 I am one of them that are peaceable and faithful in Israel: thou seekest to destroy a city and a mother in Israel: why wilt thou swallow up the inheritance of the LORD?  

20 And Joab answered and said, Far be it, far be it from me, that I should swallow up or destroy.  

21 The matter is not so: but a man of mount Ephraim, Sheba the son of Bichri by name, hath lifted up his hand against the king, even against David: deliver him only, and I will depart from the city. And the woman said unto Joab, Behold, his head shall be thrown to thee over the wall.  

22 Then the woman went unto all the people in her wisdom. And they cut off the head of Sheba the son of Bichri, and cast it out to Joab. And he blew a a trumpet, and they retired from the city, every man to his tent. And Joab returned to Jerusalem unto the king.  

23 Now Joab was over all the host of Israel: and Benaiah the son of Jehoiada was over the Cherethites and over the Pelethites:  

24 And Andoram was over the tribute: and Jehoshaphat the son of Ahilud was recorder:  

25 And Sheva was scribe: and Zadok and Abiathar were the priests:  

26 And Ira also the Jairite was a chief ruler about David.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "We have no part in David, neither have we inheritance in the son of Jesse"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about political disagreement
**Biblical context:** This is about rebellion against God's anointed - Sheba's rejection shows opposition to divine authority, demonstrating how rebellion spreads through false leadership.

### "They shall surely ask counsel at Abel: and so they ended the matter"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about human wisdom
**Biblical context:** This is about divine wisdom - The reputation of Abel for wisdom shows how God's wisdom brings resolution, demonstrating that true wisdom comes from God.

**Chapter 20 demonstrates final rebellion, treacherous murder, and wisdom's triumph:** Sheba's rebellion shows ongoing opposition after Absalom's defeat. The trumpet call shows gathering of rebels. The rejection of David shows denial of divine authority. The tribal division shows political fragility. The concubines' fate shows consequences of Absalom's sin. Amasa's appointment shows attempt at reconciliation. His failure shows incompetence. Joab's initiative shows decisive action. The pursuit shows military response. Amasa's death shows Joab's treachery and ambition. The beard kiss shows deception. The sword strike shows calculated murder. The removal of body shows respect for the living. The pursuit continues shows focus on mission. The siege shows military pressure. The wise woman's intervention shows wisdom's power. Her reputation shows established wisdom. Her appeal shows concern for innocent life. Joab's denial shows limited objective. The woman's wisdom shows practical solution. The execution of Sheba shows rebellion's end. The trumpet call shows victory. The administrative list shows restored order. This chapter establishes crucial themes: rebellion continues until dealt with decisively, wisdom saves innocent lives, treachery exists even among leaders, and divine order ultimately prevails. It demonstrates that David's kingdom faced continued opposition, that Joab's ambition led to murder, and that wisdom preserved innocent lives. The chapter shows David as restored king who deals with rebellion, maintains order, and establishes administration. It illustrates the principle that rebellion requires decisive response, that wisdom preserves life, and that order must be maintained for peace. The chapter points forward to Christ as the perfect king whose kingdom faces no rebellion, whose wisdom is perfect, and whose order is eternal. The rebellion points forward to Christ's perfect victory over all opposition. The wisdom points forward to Christ's perfect understanding. The chapter demonstrates that when God's kingdom faces rebellion, decisive action must be taken, wisdom must be employed to preserve innocent life, and order must be maintained for peace, showing how divine authority ultimately prevails over human rebellion through wisdom and justice.
