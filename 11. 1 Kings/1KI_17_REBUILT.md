## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### ELIJAH
- **Meaning:** "My God is the LORD"
- **Context:** Prophet who confronted Ahab
- **Significance:** Divine messenger of judgment and mercy
* **New Testament parallel:** John the Baptist (Matthew 11:14)

### CHERITH
- **Meaning:** "Cutting" or "Separation"
- **Context:** Brook where Elijah hid
- **Significance:** Place of divine provision
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual provision (Philippians 4:19)

### ZAREPHATH
- **Meaning:** "Refining" or "Melting"
- **Context:** City where widow lived
- **Significance:** Place of testing and blessing
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual refining (1 Peter 1:7)

### RAVENS
- **Meaning:** "Black birds"
- **Context:** Birds that fed Elijah
- **Significance:** Unusual provision method
* **Spiritual application:** Divine provision (Matthew 6:26)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 And Elijah the Tishbite, who was of the inhabitants of Gilead, said unto Ahab, As the LORD God of Israel liveth, before whom I stand, there shall not be dew nor rain these years, but according to my word.  

2 And the word of the LORD came unto him, saying,  

3 Get thee hence, and turn thee eastward, and hide thyself by the brook Cherith, that is before Jordan.  

4 And it shall be, that thou shalt drink of the brook; and I have commanded the ravens to feed thee there.  

5 So he went and did according unto the word of the LORD: for he went and dwelt by the brook Cherith, that is before Jordan.  

6 And the ravens brought him bread and flesh in the morning, and bread and flesh in the evening, and he drank of the brook.  

7 And it came to pass after a while, that the brook dried up, because there had been no rain in the land.  

8 And the word of the LORD came unto him, saying,  

9 Arise, get thee to Zarephath, which belongeth to Zidon, and dwell there: behold, I have commanded a widow woman there to sustain thee.  

10 So he arose and went to Zarephath. And when he came to the gate of the city, behold, the widow woman was there gathering of sticks: and he called to her, and said, Fetch me, I pray thee, a little water in a vessel, that I may drink.  

11 And as she was going to fetch it, he called to her, and said, Bring me, I pray thee, a morsel of bread in thine hand.  

12 And she said, As the LORD thy God liveth, I have not a cake, but an handful of meal in a barrel, and a little oil in a cruse: and, behold, I am gathering two times sticks, that I may go in and dress it for me and my son, that we may eat it, and eat, and die.  

13 And Elijah said unto her, Fear not; go and do as thou hast said: but make me thereof a little cake first, and bring it unto me, and after make for thee and for thy son.  

14 For thus saith the LORD God of Israel, The barrel of meal shall not waste, neither shall the cruse of oil fail, until the day that the LORD sendeth rain upon the earth.  

15 And she went and did according to the saying of Elijah: and she, and he, and her house, did eat many days.  

16 And the barrel of meal wasted not, neither did the cruse of oil fail, according to the word of the LORD, which he spake by Elijah.  

17 And it came to pass after these things, that the sent of the woman, the mistress of the house, fell sick; and his sickness was so sore, that there was no breath left in him.  

18 And she said unto Elijah, What have I to do with thee, O thou man of God? art thou come unto me to call my sin to remembrance, and to slay my son?  

19 And himself; and he took him out of her bosom, and carried him up into a loft, where he abode, and laid him upon his own bed.  

20 And he cried unto the LORD, and said, O LORD my God, hast thou also brought evil upon the widow with whom I sojourn, by slaying her son?  

21 And he stretched himself upon the child three times, and cried unto the LORD, and said, O LORD my God, I pray thee, let this child's soul come into him again.  

22 And the the LORD heard the voice of Elijah; and the soul of the child came into him again, and he revived.  

23 And Elijah took the child, and brought him down out of the chamber into the house, and led him unto his mother: and Elijah said, See, thy son liveth.  

24 And the woman said to Elijah, Now by this I know that thou art a man of God, and that the word of the LORD in thy mouth is truth.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "There shall not be dew nor rain these years, but according to my word"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about Elijah's power
**Biblical context:** This is about God's judgment - The drought was God's judgment on Ahab's idolatry, spoken through Elijah as God's messenger.

### "The barrel of meal shall not waste, neither shall the cruse of oil fail"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about magical multiplication
**Biblical context:** This is about divine provision - The miracle was God's provision for faithful obedience, showing how God provides for those who trust Him.

**Chapter 17 demonstrates Elijah's prophetic ministry through drought, divine provision, and resurrection power:** Elijah Tishbite confronted Ahab, prophesied drought by God's word. Commanded to hide by brook Cherith, drink water, ravens feed. Obeyed, dwelt by brook; ravens brought bread and flesh morning and evening. Brook dried from drought. Commanded to Zarephath, widow commanded to sustain. Found widow gathering sticks, requested water and bread. Widow confessed last meal and oil, preparing to die. Elijah requested cake first, promised meal and oil would not fail until rain. Widow obeyed, made cake; meal and oil sustained many days. Widow's son fell sick, died. Widow blamed Elijah for bringing sin remembrance. Elijah took son to loft, stretched on child three times, prayed for soul to return. LORD heard, child revived. Elijah returned son to mother; woman confessed Elijah as man of God, word of LORD truth. This chapter establishes crucial themes: divine judgment requires prophetic courage, God provides supernaturally for His servants, faith brings miraculous provision, and prayer brings resurrection power. It demonstrates that Elijah's ministry began with bold confrontation, continued through divine provision, and culminated in resurrection power. The chapter shows Elijah as bold prophet who spoke God's word despite opposition, who trusted God's provision in hiding, who encouraged faith in desperate circumstances, and who demonstrated resurrection power through prayer. It shows the widow as faithful believer who gave her last in obedience, who received miraculous provision, who experienced restoration through prayer, and who recognized God's truth. It illustrates the principle that God's servants must be bold in confrontation, trust in divine provision, encourage faith in others, and demonstrate God's power through prayer. The chapter points forward to Christ as the perfectly bold prophet whose word is truth, whose provision is perfect, whose compassion is complete, and whose resurrection power is absolute. The bold confrontation points forward to Christ's perfect courage. The divine provision points forward to Christ's perfect supply. The resurrection power points forward to Christ's perfect victory over death. The chapter demonstrates that when God's servants speak His word boldly, trust His provision completely, encourage faith in others, and pray with faith, they demonstrate God's power and bring people to recognize His truth and experience His miraculous provision and restoration.
