## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### BAAL-ZEBUB
- **Meaning:** "Lord of the flies"
- **Context:** Philistine deity Ahaziah consulted
- **Significance:** Idolatrous inquiry
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual uncleanness (Matthew 10:25)

### EKRON
- **Meaning:** "Uprooting" or "Removal"
- **Context:** Philistine city where Baal-zebub was worshipped
- **Significance:** Place of idolatry
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual uprooting (John 15:6)

### LATTICE
- **Meaning:** "Network" or "Framework"
- **Context:** Structure through which Ahaziah fell
- **Significance:** Accident leading to divine confrontation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual weakness (2 Corinthians 12:9)

### GIRDLE
- **Meaning:** "Belt" or "Sash"
- **Context:** Elijah's leather belt
- **Significance:** Prophetic identification
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual readiness (Ephesians 6:14)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 Then Moab rebelled against Israel after the death of Ahab.  

2 And Ahaziah fell down through a lattice in his upper chamber that was in Samaria, and was sick: and he sent messengers, and said unto them, Go, enquire of Baal-zebub the god of Ekron whether I shall recover of this disease.  

3 But the angel of the LORD said to Elijah the Tishbite, Arise, go up to meet the messengers of the king of Samaria, and say unto them, Is it not because there is not a God in Israel, that ye go to enquire of Baal-zebub the god of Ekron?  

4 Now therefore thus saith the LORD, Thou shalt not come down from that bed on which thou art gone up, but shalt surely die. And Elijah departed.  

5 And when the messengers turned back unto him, he said unto them, Why are ye now turned back?  

6 And they said unto him, There came a man up to meet us, and said unto us, Go, turn again unto the king that sent you, and say unto him, Thus saith the LORD, Is it not because there is not a God in Israel, that thou sendest to enquire of Baal-zebub the god of Ekron? therefore thou shalt not come down from that bed on which thou art gone up, but shalt surely die.  

7 And he said unto them, What manner of man was he which came up to meet you, and told you these words?  

8 And they answered him, He was an hairy man, and girt with a girdle of leather about his loins. And he said, It is Elijah the Tishbite.  

9 Then the king sent unto him a captain of fifty with his fifty. And he went up to him: and, behold, he sat on the top of an hill. And he spake unto him, Thou man of God, the king hath said, Come down.  

10 And Elijah answered and said to the captain of fifty, If I be a man of God, then let fire come down from heaven, and consume thee and thy fifty. And there came down fire from heaven, and consumed him and his fifty.  

11 Again also he sent unto him another captain of fifty with his fifty. And he answered and said unto him, O man of God, thus hath the king said, Come down quickly.  

12 And Elijah answered and said unto them, If I be a man of God, let fire come down from heaven, and consume thee and thy fifty. And the fire of God came down from heaven, and consumed him and his fifty.  

13 And he sent again a captain of the third fifty with his fifty. And the third captain of fifty went up, and came and fell on his knees before Elijah, and besought him, and said unto him, O man of God, I pray thee, let my life, and the life of these fifty thy servants, be precious in thy sight.  

14 Behold, there came fire down from heaven, and burnt up the two captains of the former fifties with their fifties: therefore let my life now be precious in thy sight.  

15 And the angel of the LORD said unto Elijah, Go down with him: be not afraid of him. And he arose, and went down with him unto the king.  

16 And he said unto him, Thus saith the LORD, Forasmuch as thou hast sent messengers to enquire of Baal-zebub the god of Ekron, is it not because there is no God in Israel to enquire of his word? therefore thou shalt not come down off that bed on which thou are gone up, but shalt surely die.  

17 So he died according to the word of the LORD which Elijah had spoken. And Jehoram reigned in his stead in the second year of Jehoram the son of Jehoshaphat king of Judah; because he had no son.  

18 Now the rest of the acts of Ahaziah which he did, are they not written in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Israel?  

19 And the rest of the acts of Ahaziah, which he did, are they not written in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Israel?

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Go, enquire of Baal-zebub the god of Ekron"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about seeking medical advice
**Biblical context:** This is about idolatrous inquiry - Ahaziah consulted a false god instead of the true God, showing how sin leads to seeking answers in wrong places.

### "If I be a man of God, then let fire come down from heaven"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about proving spiritual power
**Biblical context:** This is about divine authority - Elijah's challenge demonstrated that true prophetic authority comes from God's power, not human position.

**Chapter 1 demonstrates divine authority and judgment through Elijah's confrontation with Ahaziah:** Moab rebelled Israel after Ahab's death. Ahaziah fell through lattice in Samaria upper chamber, sick; sent messengers to enquire of Baal-zebub god Ekron about recovery. Angel LORD told Elijah to meet messengers, confront idolatry: no God in Israel to consult? LORD prophesied Ahaziah would not rise from bed but die. Messengers returned to Ahaziah, reported man's message. Ahaziah asked about man's appearance; described hairy man with leather girdle; identified Elijah. Ahaziah sent captain with fifty men to summon Elijah; Elijah on hill. Captain called "man of God," king said "Come down." Elijah challenged: if man of God, fire consume them; fire from heaven consumed captain and fifty. Second captain sent, same message; Elijah repeated challenge; fire consumed them. Third captain sent, fell on knees, begged for mercy, acknowledged previous judgments. Angel LORD told Elijah go with captain, not afraid. Elijah went to Ahaziah, repeated divine judgment for consulting Baal-zebub instead of Israel's God. Ahaziah died according to Elijah's word. Jehoram (different from Judah's Jehoram) reigned in his place, second year of Jehoram son of Jehoshaphat king Judah; Ahaziah had no son. This chapter establishes crucial themes: idolatry brings divine judgment, prophetic authority is confirmed by divine power, humility brings mercy, and God's word is fulfilled. It demonstrates that Ahaziah's idolatry led to seeking false gods, that Elijah's authority was confirmed by fire from heaven, that the third captain's humility brought mercy, and that divine prophecy was fulfilled exactly. The chapter shows Ahaziah as idolatrous king who rejected Israel's God for false gods. It shows Elijah as authoritative prophet whose power came from God. It shows the third captain as humble man who received mercy. It illustrates the principle that idolatry brings judgment, that divine authority is confirmed by God's power, that humility receives mercy, and that God's word is certain. The chapter points forward to Christ as the perfectly authoritative prophet whose word is truth, whose power is absolute, whose judgment is righteous, and whose mercy is perfect. The divine authority points forward to Christ's perfect power. The prophetic fulfillment points forward to Christ's perfect truth. The chapter demonstrates that when leaders reject the true God for idols, they bring divine judgment, that true prophetic authority is confirmed by divine power, that humility receives mercy, and that God's word is always fulfilled.
