## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### HEZEKIAH
- **Meaning:** "The LORD strengthens"
- **Context:** Righteous king who was healed and given 15 more years
- **Significance:** Model of faithful prayer and divine healing
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual healing (James 5:15)

### ISAIAH
- **Meaning:** "The LORD is salvation"
- **Context:** Prophet who delivered God's messages to Hezekiah
- **Significance:** Faithful prophetic messenger
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual prophecy (1 Corinthians 14:3)

### DIAL OF AHAZ
- **Meaning:** "Ahaz's steps"
- **Context:** Sundial used for the shadow sign
- **Significance:** Instrument of divine miracle
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual time (2 Peter 3:8)

### BERODACH-BALADAN
- **Meaning:** "Given by Baladan"
- **Context:** King of Babylon who sent embassy to Hezekiah
- **Significance:** Beginning of Babylonian influence
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual influence (1 Corinthians 15:33)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 In those days was Hezekiah sick unto death. And the prophet Isaiah the son of Amoz came to him, and said unto him, Thus saith the LORD, Set thine house in order; for thou shalt die, and not live.  

2 Then he turned his face to the wall, and prayed unto the LORD, saying,  

3 I beseech thee, O LORD, remember now how I have walked before thee in truth and with a perfect heart, and have done that which is good in thy sight. And Hezekiah wept sore.  

4 And it came to pass, afore Isaiah was gone out into the middle court, that the word of the LORD came to him, saying,  

5 Turn again, and tell Hezekiah the captain of my people, Thus saith the LORD, the God of David thy father, I have heard thy prayer, I have seen thy tears: behold, I will heal thee: on the third day thou shalt go up unto the house of the LORD.  

6 And I will add unto thy days fifteen years; and I will deliver thee and this city out of the hand of the king of Assyria; and I will defend this city for mine own sake, and for my servant David's sake.  

7 And Isaiah said, Take a lump of figs. And they took and laid it on the boil, and he recovered.  

8 And Hezekiah said unto Isaiah, What shall be the sign that the LORD will heal me, and that I shall go up into the house of the LORD the third day?  

9 And Isaiah said, This sign shalt thou have of the LORD, that the LORD will do the thing that he hath spoken: shall the shadow go forward ten degrees, or go back ten degrees?  

10 And Hezekiah answered, It is a light thing for the shadow to go down ten degrees: nay, but let the shadow return backward ten degrees.  

11 And Isaiah the prophet cried unto the LORD: and he brought the shadow ten degrees backward, by which it had gone down in the dial of Ahaz.  

12 At that time Berodach-baladan, the son of Baladan, king of Babylon, sent letters and a present unto Hezekiah: for he had heard that Hezekiah had been sick.  

13 And Hezekiah hearkened unto them, and shewed them all the house of his precious things, the silver, and the gold, and the spices, and the precious ointment, and all the house of his armour, and all that was found in his treasures: there was nothing in his house, nor in all his dominion, that Hezekiah shewed them not.  

14 Then came Isaiah the prophet unto king Hezekiah, and said unto him, What said these men? and from whence came they unto thee? And Hezekiah said, They are come from a far country, even from Babylon.  

15 And he said, What have they seen in thine house? And Hezekiah answered, All the things that are in mine house have they seen: there is nothing among my treasures that I have not shewed them.  

16 And Isaiah said unto Hezekiah, Hear the word of the LORD.  

17 Behold, the days come, that all that is in thine house, and that which thy fathers have laid up in store unto this day, shall be carried into Babylon: nothing shall be left, saith the LORD.  

18 And of thy sons that shall issue from thee, which thou shalt beget, shall they take away; and they shall be eunuchs in the palace of the king of Babylon.  

19 Then said Hezekiah unto Isaiah, Good is the word of the LORD which thou hast spoken. And he said, Is it not good, if peace and truth be in my days?  

20 And the rest of the acts of Hezekiah, and all his might, and how he made a pool, and a conduit, and brought water into the city, are they not written in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Judah?  

21 And Hezekiah slept with his fathers: and Manasseh his son reigned in his stead.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Set thine house in order; for thou shalt die, and not live"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about estate planning
**Biblical context:** This is about mortality warning - The warning was about impending death, showing how God uses mortality to draw people to Himself.

### "Shall the shadow go forward ten degrees, or go back ten degrees?"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about astronomy
**Biblical context:** This is about divine miracle - The shadow miracle showed God's power over time and creation, demonstrating how divine power transcends natural laws.

This chapter establishes crucial themes: faithful prayer brings divine healing, divine power transcends natural laws, human pride brings future judgment, and divine mercy gives temporary relief. It demonstrates that Hezekiah's prayer brought healing and fifteen more years, that the shadow miracle showed God's power over time, that showing treasures brought Babylonian exile prophecy, and that Hezekiah's pride led to future judgment. The chapter shows Hezekiah as faithful king whose prayer brought healing. It shows Isaiah as prophetic messenger who delivered both healing and judgment. It shows Berodach-baladan as Babylonian king who began future oppression. It shows Manasseh as successor who would bring wickedness. It illustrates the principle that faithful prayer brings divine healing, that divine power transcends natural laws, that human pride brings future judgment, and that divine mercy gives temporary relief. The chapter points forward to Christ as the perfectly faithful intercessor whose healing is complete, whose power is absolute, whose judgment is righteous, and whose mercy is eternal. The faithful prayer points forward to Christ's perfect intercession. The divine power points forward to Christ's perfect authority. The chapter demonstrates that when people pray faithfully, receive divine healing, witness divine power, and respond with pride, they experience both divine blessing and future judgment, showing how God works through faithful prayer but holds people accountable for their response to His blessings.
