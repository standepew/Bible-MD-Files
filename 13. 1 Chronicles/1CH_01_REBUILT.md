## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### ADAM
- **Meaning:** "Man" or "red"
- **Context:** First man created in God's image
- **Significance:** Origin of humanity
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual Adam (Romans 5:14)

### ENOCH
- **Meaning:** "Dedicated"
- **Context:** Man who walked with God and was translated
- **Significance:** Model of faithfulness
* **New Testament parallel:** Faithful believer (Hebrews 11:5)

### NOAH
- **Meaning:** "Rest" or "comfort"
- **Context:** Man who found grace and saved humanity
- **Significance:** Instrument of divine salvation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual salvation (1 Peter 3:20)

### ABRAHAM
- **Meaning:** "Father of a multitude"
- **Context:** Father of faith and covenant
- **Significance:** Model of faith
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual father (Romans 4:11)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "And Enoch walked with God"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about righteous living
**Biblical context:** This is about intimate fellowship - Enoch walked with God in close relationship, showing how faith involves intimate communion with God.

### "And Noah found grace in the eyes of the LORD"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about personal favor
**Biblical context:** This is about divine selection - Noah found grace because God chose him for salvation, showing how divine grace is unmerited favor.
