## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 The Second Book of Chronicles.

2 Chapter 1.

3 And Solomon son of David was strengthened in his kingdom, and LORD his God was with him, and magnified him exceedingly.  

4 Then Solomon spake unto all Israel, to the captains of thousands and of hundreds, and to the judges, and to every governor in all Israel, and chief of fathers.  

5 So Solomon, and all the congregation with him, went to the high place that was at Gibeon; for there was the tabernacle of the congregation of the LORD, which Moses the servant of the LORD had made in the wilderness.  

6 But the ark of God had David brought up from Kirjath-jearim to the place which David had prepared for it: for he had pitched a tent for it at Jerusalem.  

7 Moreover the brasen altar, that Bezalel the son of Uri the son of Hur, had made, he put before the tabernacle of the congregation of the LORD: and Solomon and the congregation sought unto it.  

8 And Solomon went up thither to the brasen altar before the LORD, which was at the tabernacle of the congregation, and offered a thousand burnt offerings upon it.  

9 In that night did God appear unto Solomon, and said unto him, Ask what I shall give thee.  

10 And Solomon said unto God, Thou hast shewed great mercy unto David my father, and hast made me to reign in his stead.  

11 Now, O LORD God, let thy promise unto David my father be established: for thou hast made me to reign over a people like to the dust of the earth in multitude.  

12 Give me now wisdom and knowledge, that I may go out and come in before this people: for who can judge this thy people, that is so great?  

13 And God said to Solomon, Because this was in thine heart, and thou hast not asked riches, wealth, or honour, nor long life; but hast asked for thyself wisdom and knowledge, that thou mayest judge my people, over whom I have made thee king.  

14 Wisdom and knowledge is granted unto thee; and I will give thee riches, and wealth, and honour, such as none of the kings have had that have been before thee, neither shall there any after thee have any like.  

15 And Solomon came from his journey to the high place that was at Gibeon to Jerusalem, from before the tabernacle of the congregation of the LORD, and reigned over Israel.  

16 And Solomon gathered chariots and horsemen: and he had a thousand and four hundred chariots, and twelve thousand horsemen, which he placed in the chariot cities, and with the king at Jerusalem.  

17 And Solomon made silver and gold at Jerusalem as plenteous as stones, and cedar trees made he in abundance, like the sycamore trees that are in the vale for abundance.  

18 And Solomon had horses brought out of Egypt, and linen yarn: the king's merchants received the linen yarn at a price.  

19 And they fetched up, and brought forth out of Egypt a chariot for six hundred shekels of silver, and an horse for an hundred and fifty shekel: and so brought they out horses for all the kings of the Hittites, and for the kings of Syria, and for the kings of Egypt, by their means.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "And Solomon son of David was strengthened in his kingdom, and LORD his God was with him"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about political power
**Biblical context:** This is about divine strengthening - Solomon's strength came from God's presence, showing how divine sovereignty empowers human leadership.

### "Ask what I shall give thee"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about human desire
**Biblical context:** This is about divine invitation - God offered Solomon a choice, showing how divine initiative precedes human requests.
