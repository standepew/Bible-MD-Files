## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### SHECHANIAH
- **Meaning:** "Yahweh dwells"
- **Context:** Leader who proposed covenant reform
- **Significance:** Model of faithful reformer
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual reformer (2 Timothy 4:2)

### JONATHAN
- **Meaning:** "Yahweh has given"
- **Context:** Official who handled divorce cases
- **Significance:** Model of faithful administrator
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual administration (1 Corinthians 12:28)

### MESHULLAM
- **Meaning:** "Friend"
- **Context:** Levite who helped in reform
- **Significance:** Model of faithful helper
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual helper (Romans 16:1)

### STRANGE WIVES
- **Meaning:** "Foreign spouses"
- **Context:** Pagan wives who led to compromise
- **Significance:** Model of spiritual danger
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual danger (2 Corinthians 6:14)

1 Now when Ezra had prayed, and when he had confessed, weeping and casting himself down before the house of God, there assembled unto him out of Israel a very great congregation of men and women and children: for the people wept very sore.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Now therefore let us make a covenant with our God to put away all the wives, and such as are born of them"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about harsh divorce
**Biblical context:** This is about spiritual purity - The covenant was necessary to restore covenant relationship with God, showing how spiritual purity requires difficult obedience.

### "All these had taken strange wives: and some of them had wives by whom they had children"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about family destruction
**Biblical context:** This is about covenant faithfulness - The reform affected families because spiritual compromise affects all relationships, showing how sin has real consequences.
