## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### ANOINTED
- **Meaning:** "Set apart, consecrated"
- **Context:** God's chosen king
- **Significance:** Model of divine appointment
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual anointing (Acts 10:38)

### SON
- **Meaning:** "Divine offspring"
- **Context:** God's begotten one
- **Significance:** Model of divine relationship
* **New Testament parallel:** Divine Sonship (John 3:16)

### DECREE
- **Meaning:** "Divine declaration"
- **Context:** God's unchangeable purpose
- **Significance:** Model of divine sovereignty
* **New Testament parallel:** Divine decree (Ephesians 1:11)

### INHERITANCE
- **Meaning:** "Portion, possession"
- **Context:** Messiah's universal dominion
- **Significance:** Model of spiritual inheritance
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual inheritance (Romans 8:17)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 Why do the heathen rage, and the people imagine a vain thing?  

2 The kings of the earth set themselves, and the rulers take counsel together, against the LORD, and against his anointed, saying,  

3 Let us break their bands asunder, and cast away their cords from us.  

4 He that sitteth in the heavens shall laugh: the Lord shall have them in derision.  

5 Then shall he speak unto them in his wrath, and vex them in his sore displeasure.  

6 Yet have I set my king upon my holy hill of Zion.  

7 I will declare the decree: the LORD hath said unto me, Thou art my Son; this day have I begotten thee.  

8 Ask of me, and I shall give thee the heathen for thine inheritance, and the uttermost parts of the earth for thy possession.  

9 Thou shalt break them with a rod of iron; thou shalt dash them in pieces like a potter's vessel.  

10 Be wise now therefore, O ye kings: be instructed, ye judges of the earth.  

11 Serve the LORD with fear, and rejoice with trembling.  

12 Kiss the Son, lest he be angry, and ye perish from the way, when his wrath is kindled but a little. Blessed are all they that put their trust in him.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Why do the heathen rage, and the people imagine a vain thing?"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about political unrest
**Biblical context:** This is about spiritual rebellion - The nations rage against God's authority, showing human opposition to divine rule.

### "Kiss the Son, lest he be angry"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about affection
**Biblical context:** This is about submission - Kissing the Son represents submission to Christ's authority, showing need for reverence.
