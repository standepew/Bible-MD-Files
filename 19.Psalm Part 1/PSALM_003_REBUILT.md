## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 A Psalm of David, when he fled from Absalom his son. LORD, how are they increased that trouble me! many are they that rise up against me.  

2 Many there be which say of my soul, There is no help for him in God. Selah.  

3 But thou, O LORD, art a shield for me; my glory, and the lifter up of mine head.  

4 I cried unto the LORD with my voice, and he heard me out of his holy hill. Selah.  

5 I laid me down and slept; I awaked; for the LORD sustained me.  

6 I will not be afraid of ten thousands of people, that have set themselves against me round about.  

7 Arise, O LORD; save me, O my God: for thou hast smitten all mine enemies upon the cheek bone; thou hast broken the teeth of the ungodly.  

8 Salvation belongeth unto the LORD: thy blessing is upon thy people. Selah.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "I laid me down and slept; I awaked; for the LORD sustained me"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about physical rest
**Biblical context:** This is about divine trust - David sleeps peacefully despite danger, showing confidence in God's protection.

### "Salvation belongeth unto the LORD"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about future salvation only
**Biblical context:** This is about present deliverance - God saves now, showing His immediate involvement in human affairs.
