## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### EXCELLENT
- **Meaning:** "Supreme, magnificent"
- **Context:** God's glorious name
- **Significance:** Model of divine majesty
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual excellence (Philippians 2:9)

### BABES
- **Meaning:** "Infants"
- **Context:** Simple praise to God
- **Significance:** Model of spiritual innocence
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual babes (1 Peter 2:2)

### DOMINION
- **Meaning:** "Authority, rule"
- **Context:** Human authority over creation
- **Significance:** Model of spiritual authority
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual dominion (1 Corinthians 15:27)

### SON OF MAN
- **Meaning:** "Human being"
- **Context:** Humanity in relation to God
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's humanity
* **New Testament parallel:** Christ as Son of Man (Matthew 8:20)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 To the chief Musician upon Gittith, A Psalm of David. O LORD our Lord, how excellent is thy name in all the earth! who hast set thy glory above the heavens.  

2 Out of the mouth of babes and sucklings hast thou ordained strength because of thine enemies, that thou mightest still the enemy and the avenger.  

3 When I consider thy heavens, the work of thy fingers, the moon and the stars, which thou hast ordained;  

4 What is man, that thou art mindful of him? and the son of man, that thou visitest him?  

5 For thou hast made him a little lower than the angels, and hast crowned him with glory and honour.  

6 Thou madest him to have dominion over the works of thy hands; thou hast put all things under his feet:  

7 All sheep and oxen, yea, and the beasts of the field;  

8 The fowl of the air, and the fish of the sea, and whatsoever passeth through the paths of the seas.  

9 O LORD our Lord, how excellent is thy name in all the earth!

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "What is man, that thou art mindful of him?"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about human insignificance
**Biblical context:** This is about divine honor - David marvels that God cares for humans, showing human dignity in God's plan.

### "Thou hast made him a little lower than the angels"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about human limitation
**Biblical context:** This is about human honor - Being lower than angels shows humans' high position, not their lowliness.
