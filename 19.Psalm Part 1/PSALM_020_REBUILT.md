## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### SANCTUARY
- **Meaning:** "Holy place"
- **Context:** God's dwelling place
- **Significance:** Model of spiritual presence
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sanctuary (Hebrews 8:2)

### BURNT SACRIFICE
- **Meaning:** "Offering consumed by fire"
- **Context:** Complete dedication to God
- **Significance:** Model of spiritual devotion
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sacrifice (Romans 12:1)

### BANNERS
- **Meaning:** "Standards, flags"
- **Context:** Symbols of victory and identity
- **Significance:** Model of spiritual triumph
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual victory (1 Corinthians 15:57)

### ANOINTED
- **Meaning:** "Set apart, consecrated"
- **Context:** God's chosen king
- **Significance:** Model of spiritual consecration
* **New Testament parallel:** Christ as Anointed (Acts 10:38)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 To the chief Musician, A Psalm of David. The LORD hear thee in the day of trouble; the name of the God of Jacob defend thee;  

2 Send thee help from the sanctuary, and strengthen thee out of Zion;  

3 Remember all thy offerings, and accept thy burnt sacrifice; Selah.  

4 Grant thee according to thine own heart, and fulfil all thy counsel.  

5 We will rejoice in thy salvation, and in the name of our God we will set up our banners: the LORD fulfil all thy petitions.  

6 Now know I that the LORD saveth his anointed; he will hear him from his holy heaven with the saving strength of his right hand.  

7 Some trust in chariots, and some in horses: but we will remember the name of the LORD our God.  

8 They are brought down and fallen: but we are risen, and stand upright.  

9 Save, LORD: let the king hear us when we call.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Some trust in chariots, and some in horses: but we will remember the name of the LORD our God"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about military strategy
**Biblical context:** This is about spiritual dependence - Trusting in human strength vs. trusting in God, showing complete reliance on divine power.

### "Now know I that the LORD saveth his anointed"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about David's personal victory
**Biblical context:** This is about Messianic prophecy - God saves His anointed one, pointing forward to Christ's salvation.
