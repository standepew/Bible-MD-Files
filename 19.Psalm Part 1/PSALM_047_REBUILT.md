* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual triumph (1 Corinthians 15:54)

- **Significance:** Model of spiritual reverence
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual reverence (Hebrews 12:28)

- **Meaning:** "Supreme quality"
- **Context:** God's chosen inheritance
- **Significance:** Model of spiritual blessing
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual excellency (Philippians 4:8)

### UNDERSTANDING
- **Meaning:** "Discernment, insight"
- **Context:** Intelligent worship
- **Significance:** Model of spiritual wisdom
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual understanding (Colossians 1:9)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 To the chief Musician, A Psalm for the sons of Korah. O clap your hands, all ye people; shout unto God with the voice of triumph.  

2 For the LORD most high is terrible; he is a great King over all the earth.  

3 He shall subdue the people under us, and the nations under our feet.  

4 He shall choose our inheritance for us, the excellency of Jacob whom he loved. Selah.  

5 God is gone up with a shout, the LORD with the sound of a trumpet.  

6 Sing praises to God, sing praises: sing praises unto our King, sing praises.  

7 For God is the King of all the earth: sing ye praises with understanding.  

8 God reigneth over the heathen: God sitteth upon the throne of his holiness.  

9 The princes of the people are gathered together, even the people of the God of Abraham: for the shields of the earth belong unto God: he is greatly exalted.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "God is gone up with a shout, the LORD with the sound of a trumpet"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about earthly victory
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's ascension - Going up with trumpet points to Christ's return, showing Messianic fulfillment.

### "For God is the King of all the earth: sing ye praises with understanding"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about emotional worship
**Biblical context:** This is about intelligent worship - Understanding in worship shows spiritual discernment.
