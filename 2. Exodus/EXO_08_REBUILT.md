## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Three more plagues strike Egypt. Plague 2: Frogs cover all of Egypt; even the magicians can replicate. Pharaoh begs Moses to remove them, promises to let Israel go — Moses prays, the frogs die, Pharaoh hardens his heart again. Plague 3: Lice from the dust throughout Egypt — the magicians cannot replicate and tell Pharaoh: "This is the finger of God." Pharaoh's heart is hardened. Plague 4: Flies throughout Egypt — but not in Goshen where Israel lives. God makes a division (separation) between His people and Egypt. Pharaoh bargains: sacrifice in the land. Moses refuses (it would be an abomination before Egyptians), demands three days into the wilderness. Pharaoh relents (only not far), Moses prays, flies removed — Pharaoh hardens his heart again.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "There Shall None Like It in All the Land of Egypt Since It Became a Nation" — Escalating Judgment

**Exodus 8:6** (frogs), **8:17** (lice), **8:24** (flies) — Each plague covers the land completely

- **Joel 2:2** — "A great people and a strong; there hath not been ever the like, neither shall be any more after it, even to the years of many generations" — the pattern of unprecedented judgment that escalates
- **Matthew 24:21** — "For then shall be great tribulation, such as was not since the beginning of the world to this time, no, nor ever shall be" — Jesus uses the same "never before, never after" language for the end-time tribulation; the Exodus plagues establish the grammar of eschatological judgment
- *Interpretation:* The "unprecedented" nature of each plague is not hyperbole — it is a declaration that this is divine judgment outside normal providence. God is acting at a level that transcends natural explanation. The same language in Matthew 24 signals that the end-time tribulation is the global equivalent of the Exodus plagues.

### "This Is the Finger of God" — Magicians Reach Their Limit

**Exodus 8:19** — "Then the magicians said unto Pharaoh, This is the finger of God: and Pharaoh's heart was hardened"

- **Exodus 31:18** — "And he gave unto Moses, when he had made an end of communing with him upon mount Sinai, two tables of testimony, tables of stone, written with the finger of God" — the same phrase; the finger of God writes the Law
- **Luke 11:20** — Jesus: "But if I with the finger of God cast out devils, no doubt the kingdom of God is come upon you" — Jesus uses "finger of God" as equivalent to the Spirit of God (Matthew 12:28 parallels "finger of God" with "Spirit of God")
- *Interpretation:* The magicians of Egypt — pagan sorcerers — correctly identify what they cannot replicate: the direct act of God. Their testimony is ironic: the most resistant court on earth is forced by its own magicians to acknowledge the activity of God. "This is the finger of God" is the testimony of people who have encountered something beyond all occult capability. Luke 11:20 makes the connection explicit: the finger of God = the Spirit of God working directly.

### "I Will Put a Division Between My People and Thy People" — Goshen Separated

**Exodus 8:22-23** — "I will sever in that day the land of Goshen, in which my people dwell, that no swarms of flies shall be there... I will put a division between my people and thy people"

- Hebrew: peduт (division/redemption) — "I will put a ransom/separation between my people and thy people"
- **Genesis 15:17** — The covenant smoking furnace passes between the pieces — God separating Himself to His covenant
- **Isaiah 43:2** — "When thou walkest through the fire, thou shalt not be burned" — God's protection of His people amid judgment
- **Psalm 91:7** — "A thousand shall fall at thy side, and ten thousand at thy right hand; but it shall not come nigh thee"
- **Revelation 9:4** — Locusts commanded: "hurt not the grass of the earth... but only those men which have not the seal of God in their foreheads" — the same separation principle at end of the age
- **Revelation 7:3** — The 144,000 sealed before the trumpets begin — God sealing His own before judgment falls
- *Interpretation:* The separation of Goshen from Egypt's plagues is the first explicit demonstration in Exodus of God's protective distinction for His people within judgment. This is not that His people are removed from the world — they are in the middle of Egypt — but they are sheltered by God's protective hand. Revelation 7 and 9 apply this same principle: the seal of God on His servants protects them from specific judgments that fall on the rest. The Exodus Goshen principle is the template for the sealing of the saints in Revelation.

### Pharaoh's Pattern of Bargaining — The Compromiser's Offers

**Exodus 8:25** — "Go ye, sacrifice to your God in the land" (offer 1 — compromise within Egypt)
**Exodus 8:28** — "I will let you go... only ye shall not go very far away" (offer 2 — near compromise)

- **Genesis 3:1** — "Hath God said?" — the serpent's first move is always to question and then modify God's command
- **Matthew 4:9** — "All these things will I give thee, if thou wilt fall down and worship me" — Satan offers a compromise kingdom to avoid the cross
- **Hebrews 11:25-26** — Moses "choosing rather to suffer affliction with the people of God, than to enjoy the pleasures of sin for a season"
- *Interpretation:* Pharaoh's bargaining strategy is consistent throughout the plague narrative and it reveals the enemy's tactic: offer a modified version of obedience that still keeps God's people under control. "Sacrifice in the land" = worship in bondage. "Not go very far" = freedom on a leash. Moses refuses both. The pattern: Satan never asks for total, immediate rebellion — he offers compromised obedience that gradually erodes the full command of God. Moses' refusals demonstrate that partial obedience to God is disobedience.

### Moses Intercedes for Pharaoh — Prayer for Enemies

**Exodus 8:12, 30** — Moses cries unto the LORD for Pharaoh; the plague is removed

- **Matthew 5:44** — "But I say unto you, Love your enemies, bless them that curse you, do good to them that hate you, and pray for them which despitefully use you, and persecute you"
- **Luke 23:34** — "Father, forgive them; for they know not what they do" — Christ interceding for those who crucify Him
- **Romans 12:20** — "if thine enemy hunger, feed him"
- *Interpretation:* Moses intercedes for Pharaoh — the oppressor, the murderer of Hebrew children, the enemy of God's people — because Pharaoh asks. This is the pattern of the deliverer-as-intercessor. The deliverer does not come only to judge but to offer mercy when mercy is sought. The plague is removed in response to prayer. That Pharaoh then hardens his heart does not invalidate the intercession — it establishes that mercy was offered and rejected, making the subsequent judgment without excuse.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### DIVISION / SEPARATION (Hebrew: peduт / peduth)
- **Exodus 8:23** — "I will put a division between my people and thy people"
- peduth = ransom, distinction, redemption; related to padah (to ransom/redeem)
- *Definition here:* The separation between Goshen and Egypt is not merely geographical — it is covenantal; God draws a redemptive line around His people that the plagues cannot cross; this "division" is an act of redemption as much as protection

### ABOMINATION (Hebrew: toevah)
- **Exodus 8:26** — "we shall sacrifice the abomination of the Egyptians to the LORD our God"
- toevah = something morally disgusting, detestable, contrary to divine/natural order
- Moses means: what Israel sacrifices (cattle, sheep) Egyptians worship as sacred; sacrificing them publicly in Egypt would provoke violence
- **Deuteronomy 7:25-26** — the idols of the Canaanites are called toevah — to be utterly destroyed
- **Proverbs 11:1** — "A false balance is abomination to the LORD"
- *Definition here:* Here used to mean something that is religiously offensive to a pagan audience; Moses uses the real conflict of religious cultures as the reason Israel must leave Egypt to worship

### INTREAT (Hebrew: atar)
- **Exodus 8:8, 28, 29, 30** — "intreat the LORD" / "I will intreat the LORD"
- atar = to pray earnestly, to make supplication, to intercede
- *Definition here:* The specific kind of prayer Moses makes for Pharaoh — earnest intercession on behalf of an enemy; this word is used almost exclusively in Exodus 8-10 for Moses' plague-intercessions

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The Three Compromise Offers of Pharaoh — End-Time Application

Pharaoh's compromise strategy through the plagues runs in four stages:
1. "Sacrifice in the land" (Exodus 8:25) — worship Me but stay in the world
2. "Not very far away" (Exodus 8:28) — go, but stay close enough to return
3. "Only the men go" (Exodus 10:11) — partial obedience
4. "Leave your flocks" (Exodus 10:24) — go but give up your substance

Each offer keeps some part of Israel in Egypt's control. Moses refuses all four. The pattern maps onto Satan's strategy toward believers: incremental compromise, not frontal assault. **1 John 2:15** — "Love not the world, neither the things that are in the world" — the command that Pharaoh's compromises would have violated.

### "That There Is None Like Unto the LORD Our God" — The Exclusive God

- **Exodus 8:10** — Moses: "that thou mayest know that there is none like unto the LORD our God"
- **Deuteronomy 4:35** — "Unto thee it was shewed, that thou mightest know that the LORD he is God; there is none else beside him"
- **Isaiah 46:9** — "I am God, and there is none else; I am God, and there is none like me"
- *Thread:* The plague narrative is a sustained demonstration of exclusivity — not "our God is bigger" but "there is no other God." The Exodus is the historical declaration of monotheism against the polytheism of Egypt.
