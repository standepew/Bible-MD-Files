## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Six weeks after the Exodus, the whole congregation murmurs against Moses and Aaron in the wilderness of Sin: "Would to God we had died in the land of Egypt, when we sat by the flesh pots." God responds: He will rain bread from heaven — a daily provision that tests whether they will obey His instructions. In the evening quails cover the camp; in the morning manna covers the ground — small, round, white, tasting of honey wafers. Each person gathers an omer; those who gather more or less find their omer is exactly right. Those who keep it overnight (disobeying Moses) find it breeds worms and stinks. On the sixth day a double portion is given; it keeps through the Sabbath without spoiling. Some go out on the Sabbath and find nothing. God: "How long refuse ye to keep my commandments?" The Sabbath is formally established in practice. Manna falls for 40 years until they reach Canaan. An omer is kept before the LORD for all generations.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### Manna — "What Is It?" — Bread from Heaven

**Exodus 16:15** — "They said one to another, It is manna: for they wist not what it was"

- Hebrew: man hu = What is it? The food was named by the question
- **Deuteronomy 8:3** — "He humbled thee, and suffered thee to hunger, and fed thee with manna, which thou knewest not, neither did thy fathers know; that he might make thee know that man doth not live by bread only, but by every word that proceedeth out of the mouth of God" — Moses interprets the manna spiritually: it teaches dependence on God's word
- **Matthew 4:4** — Jesus quotes Deuteronomy 8:3 when Satan tempts Him to turn stones to bread — Jesus applies the manna lesson: life comes from God's word, not from bread alone
- **John 6:31-35** — The crowd quotes "He gave them bread from heaven to eat." Jesus responds: "I am the bread of life... I am the living bread which came down from heaven: if any man eat of this bread, he shall live for ever" — Jesus explicitly identifies Himself as the true Manna; the Exodus manna was the type
- **John 6:49-51** — "Your fathers did eat manna in the wilderness, and are dead. This is the bread which cometh down from heaven, that a man may eat thereof, and not die"
- **Revelation 2:17** — "To him that overcometh will I give to eat of the hidden manna" — the manna in the ark (Exodus 16:33-34) kept before the LORD is the hidden manna; Christ is the hidden manna given to overcomers
- *Interpretation:* John 6 is the Spirit-breathed interpretation of Exodus 16. Jesus does not merely compare Himself to manna — He says the manna was pointing to Him. Every detail of the manna is typological: it came from heaven (Christ's origin), it was given freely (grace), it had to be gathered daily (daily dependence on Christ), it tasted like honey (the sweetness of God's word — Psalm 119:103), it was white (purity/holiness), it came with the dew (the Holy Spirit's presence). Those who ate the Exodus manna still died; those who eat the true Bread live forever.

### "Your Murmurings Are Not Against Us, but Against the LORD"

**Exodus 16:8** — "your murmurings are not against us, but against the LORD"

- **Numbers 11:1** — "And when the people complained, it displeased the LORD: and the LORD heard it; and his anger was kindled"
- **1 Corinthians 10:10** — "Neither murmur ye, as some of them also murmured, and were destroyed of the destroyer" — Paul explicitly applies the Exodus murmuring to New Testament believers as a warning
- **Philippians 4:11** — Paul: "I have learned, in whatsoever state I am, therewith to be content" — the opposite of murmuring
- **James 5:9** — "Grudge not one against another, brethren, lest ye be condemned"
- *Interpretation:* The murmuring against Moses is identified as murmuring against God — the appointed leader and the God who appointed him are treated as one in covenant terms. This principle recurs throughout Scripture: those who reject God's appointed servants reject God (1 Samuel 8:7 — "they have not rejected thee, but they have rejected me"). The pattern: murmuring in difficulty = distrust of God's provision and character, not merely impatience with circumstances.

### "He That Gathered Much Had Nothing Over, and He That Gathered Little Had No Lack"

**Exodus 16:18**

- **2 Corinthians 8:13-15** — Paul quotes this verse directly in the context of the collection for the Jerusalem saints: "As it is written, He that had gathered much had nothing over; and he that had gathered little had no lack" — Paul applies the manna principle to economic sharing among believers
- *Interpretation:* The manna economy is God's designed economy: sufficiency for all, excess for none. Those who tried to hoard found it rotted; those who took only what they needed found it exactly right. Paul uses this as the theological basis for the New Testament principle of mutual economic care in the body of Christ — the manna principle is the foundation of Christian generosity.

### The Sabbath in Practice — Before Sinai

**Exodus 16:23-30** — The Sabbath practice established through the manna before the Law is given at Sinai

- **Genesis 2:2-3** — The Sabbath pattern established at creation — God rested on the seventh day and sanctified it
- **Exodus 20:8-11** — The fourth commandment: "Remember the sabbath day" — note "Remember," not "observe for the first time"; the manna Sabbath is the pre-Sinai practice being "remembered"
- **Hebrews 4:9-10** — "There remaineth therefore a rest (sabbatismos) to the people of God. For he that is entered into his rest, he also hath ceased from his own works, as God did from his"
- **Mark 2:27** — "The sabbath was made for man, and not man for the sabbath" — Jesus' principle; the Sabbath is a gift of rest, not a burden
- *Interpretation:* The Sabbath in Exodus 16 precedes the Law at Sinai — this proves it is not merely a Mosaic institution. It is grounded in creation (Genesis 2) and practiced before Sinai. The "Remember" of Exodus 20:8 refers back to this creation pattern. Hebrews 4 reveals that the ultimate Sabbath is entering into God's rest through faith in Christ — the weekly Sabbath was the type; the rest of faith is the antitype.

### "I Am the LORD Your God" — The Provision Proves the Identity

**Exodus 16:12** — "ye shall know that I am the LORD your God"

- **Ezekiel 37:13** — "Ye shall know that I am the LORD, when I have opened your graves" — the same pattern: God's provision/resurrection proves His identity
- **John 10:38** — Jesus: "believe the works: that ye may know, and believe, that the Father is in me, and I in him"
- *Interpretation:* Throughout Exodus, God's acts are accompanied by this declaration: "that ye shall know that I am the LORD your God." The manna is not merely food — it is a revelation of who God is. Every time He provides, He is teaching His identity. This is the pattern: God's miraculous provision is always revelatory — it teaches who He is, not just what He does.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### MANNA (Hebrew: man)
- **Exodus 16:15** — "What is it?" — man hu; the name is the question
- **Psalm 78:24** — "rained down manna upon them to eat, and had given them of the corn of heaven"
- **Psalm 78:25** — "Man did eat angels' food" — manna = the food of angels
- *Definition here:* A supernatural, daily provision from God; white, sweet, given freely, received by gathering; it sustained life in the wilderness and could not be stored beyond the daily need (except on the Sabbath); the type of Christ as the true Bread of life

### OMER (Hebrew: omer)
- **Exodus 16:16** — "Gather of it every man according to his eating, an omer for every man"
- An omer = approximately 2 liters / 2 quarts; one-tenth of an ephah (Exodus 16:36)
- The specific measure God prescribed — not more, not less; exactly sufficient
- *Definition here:* The exact daily measure of God's provision for each person; the omer principle = God gives exactly what each person needs, no more, no less

### PROVE (Hebrew: nasah)
- **Exodus 16:4** — "that I may prove them, whether they will walk in my law, or no"
- nasah = to test, to try, to put to the proof
- **Deuteronomy 8:2** — "to humble thee, and to prove thee, to know what was in thine heart"
- **James 1:3** — "the trying of your faith worketh patience"
- *Definition here:* Divine testing through provision to reveal what is actually in the heart; the manna's daily requirement (gather each day, trust for tomorrow) is designed to expose and develop faith

## THE ACTUAL VERSES — EXODUS 16 (KJV)

**1** And they took their journey from Elim, and all the congregation of the children of Israel came unto the wilderness of Sin, which is between Elim and Sinai, on the fifteenth day of the second month after their departing out of the land of Egypt.
**2** And the whole congregation of the children of Israel murmured against Moses and Aaron in the wilderness:
**3** And the children of Israel said unto them, Would to God we had died by the hand of the LORD in the land of Egypt, when we sat by the flesh pots, and when we did eat bread to the full; for ye have brought us forth into this wilderness, to kill this whole assembly with hunger.
**4** Then said the LORD unto Moses, Behold, I will rain bread from heaven for you; and the people shall go out and gather a certain rate every day, that I may prove them, whether they will walk in my law, or no.
**5** And it shall come to pass, that on the sixth day they shall prepare that which they bring in; and it shall be twice as much as they gather daily.
**6** And Moses and Aaron said unto all the children of Israel, At even, then ye shall know that the LORD hath brought you out from the land of Egypt:
**7** And in the morning, then ye shall see the glory of the LORD; for that he heareth your murmurings against the LORD: and what are we, that ye murmur against us?
**8** And Moses said, This shall be, when the LORD shall give you in the evening flesh to eat, and in the morning bread to the full; for that the LORD heareth your murmurings which ye murmur against him: and what are we? your murmurings are not against us, but against the LORD.
**9** And Moses spake unto Aaron, Say unto all the congregation of the children of Israel, Come near before the LORD: for he hath heard your murmurings.
**10** And it came to pass, as Aaron spake unto the whole congregation of the children of Israel, that they looked toward the wilderness, and, behold, the glory of the LORD appeared in the cloud.
**11** And the LORD spake unto Moses, saying,
**12** I have heard the murmurings of the children of Israel: speak unto them, saying, At even ye shall eat flesh, and in the morning ye shall be filled with bread; and ye shall know that I am the LORD your God.
**13** And it came to pass, that at even the quails came up, and covered the camp: and in the morning the dew lay round about the host.
**14** And when the dew that lay was gone up, behold, upon the face of the wilderness there lay a small round thing, as small as the hoar frost on the ground.
**15** And when the children of Israel saw it, they said one to another, It is manna: for they wist not what it was. And Moses said unto them, This is the bread which the LORD hath given you to eat.
**16** This is the thing which the LORD hath commanded, Gather of it every man according to his eating, an omer for every man, according to the number of your persons; take ye every man for them which are in his tents.
**17** And the children of Israel did so, and gathered, some more, some less.
**18** And when they did mete it with an omer, he that gathered much had nothing over, and he that gathered little had no lack; they gathered every man according to his eating.
**19** And Moses said, Let no man leave of it till the morning.
**20** Notwithstanding they hearkened not unto Moses; but some of them left of it until the morning, and it bred worms, and stank: and Moses was wroth with them.
**21** And they gathered it every morning, every man according to his eating: and when the sun waxed hot, it melted.
**22** And it came to pass, that on the sixth day they gathered twice as much bread, two omers for one man: and all the rulers of the congregation came and told Moses.
**23** And he said unto them, This is that which the LORD hath said, To morrow is the rest of the holy sabbath unto the LORD: bake that which ye will bake to day, and seethe that ye will seethe; and that which remaineth over lay up for you to be kept until the morning.
**24** And they laid it up till the morning, as Moses bade: and it did not stink, neither was there any worm therein.
**25** And Moses said, Eat that to day; for to day is a sabbath unto the LORD: to day ye shall not find it in the field.
**26** Six days ye shall gather it; but on the seventh day, which is the sabbath, in it there shall be none.
**27** And it came to pass, that there went out some of the people on the seventh day for to gather, and they found none.
**28** And the LORD said unto Moses, How long refuse ye to keep my commandments and my laws?
**29** See, for that the LORD hath given you the sabbath, therefore he giveth you on the sixth day the bread of two days; abide ye every man in his place, let no man go out of his place on the seventh day.
**30** So the people rested on the seventh day.
**31** And the house of Israel called the name thereof Manna: and it was like coriander seed, white; and the taste of it was like wafers made with honey.
**32** And Moses said, This is the thing which the LORD commandeth, Fill an omer of it to be kept for your generations; that they may see the bread wherewith I have fed you in the wilderness, when I brought you forth from the land of Egypt.
**33** And Moses said unto Aaron, Take a pot, and put an omer full of manna therein, and lay it up before the LORD, to be kept for your generations.
**34** As the LORD commanded Moses, so Aaron laid it up before the Testimony, to be kept.
**35** And the children of Israel did eat manna forty years, until they came to a land inhabited; they did eat manna, until they came unto the borders of the land of Canaan.
**36** Now an omer is the tenth part of an ephah.

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The Manna — Full Typological Chart

| Manna (Exodus 16) | Christ (John 6) |
|---|---|
| Came from heaven (v.4) | "I came down from heaven" (John 6:38) |
| Given freely to all (v.15) | Free gift of salvation (Romans 6:23) |
| Must be received daily | "Give us this day our daily bread" (Matthew 6:11) |
| Cannot be stored/hoarded (v.19-20) | Grace is not accumulated; it is received fresh |
| White — pure (v.31) | Without blemish (1 Peter 1:19) |
| Sweet as honey (v.31) | "How sweet are thy words unto my taste!" (Psalm 119:103) |
| Came with the dew | The Spirit falls with the provision |
| Hidden manna kept in ark (v.33-34) | "Hidden manna" for overcomers (Revelation 2:17) |
| Those who ate it died (John 6:49) | Whoever eats the true Bread lives forever (John 6:51) |
| Fed Israel 40 years | Christ sustains believers through the entire wilderness |

### The Lord's Prayer and the Manna

- **Matthew 6:11** — "Give us this day our daily bread" — the Lord's Prayer is built on the manna pattern; daily, not stored; ask for today's provision, not a lifetime supply
- The manna teaches: God's people live by daily dependence, not accumulated reserves. The prayer of "give us this day" reflects the Exodus 16 economy — trust for each day's provision, not anxiety about tomorrow.
