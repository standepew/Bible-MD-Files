## CHAPTER SUMMARY

The civil code concludes. Justice laws: no false witness; do not follow a crowd to do evil; do not pervert justice for the poor; restore your enemy's lost animal; take no bribe. Sabbath year: every seventh year the land rests and the poor eat from it. Sabbath day extended to all — including servants, strangers, and animals. Three annual feasts: Unleavened Bread, Harvest (Firstfruits), and Ingathering. Firstfruits brought to God's house. No seething a kid in its mother's milk. God sends an Angel before Israel whose name contains God's name — obey him; he will not pardon transgression; he will bring Israel to the prepared place. Covenant blessings for obedience: enemies turned back, sickness removed, no barrenness, full number of days. The conquest will be gradual — "by little and little" — lest the land become desolate. God will set Israel's borders from the Red Sea to the Euphrates. No covenant with Canaan or their gods — "it will surely be a snare unto thee."

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Thou Shalt Not Follow a Multitude to Do Evil" — The Crowd Is Not the Standard

**Exodus 23:2** — "Thou shalt not follow a multitude to do evil; neither shalt thou speak in a cause to decline after many to wrest judgment"

- **1 Kings 18:18-21** — Elijah alone against 850 false prophets; "How long halt ye between two opinions?" — the majority was wrong
- **Matthew 27:20-23** — The multitude persuaded to call for Barabbas and crucify Jesus — the multitude doing evil
- **Romans 12:2** — "be not conformed to this world" — the multitude-conformity principle
- **Acts 5:29** — "We ought to obey God rather than men" — Peter and John before the council
- *Interpretation:* The prohibition on following the multitude to do evil is one of Scripture's most counter-cultural commands. Truth is not determined by majority. Justice is not defined by consensus. The crowd crucified Jesus; the crowd demanded Barabbas; the crowd stoned Stephen. "Many" going in a direction is not evidence that the direction is right (Matthew 7:13-14 — the broad way that leadeth to destruction is the many). This principle underlies every prophetic stand in the Bible against prevailing culture.

### "Thou Shalt Not Take a Gift" — Bribery and Judicial Blindness

**Exodus 23:8** — "Thou shalt take no gift: for the gift blindeth the wise, and perverteth the words of the righteous"

- **Deuteronomy 16:19** — "Thou shalt not wrest judgment; thou shalt not respect persons, neither take a gift: for a gift doth blind the eyes of the wise, and pervert the words of the righteous"
- **1 Samuel 8:3** — Samuel's sons "turned aside after lucre, and took bribes, and perverted judgment" — the trigger for Israel's demand for a king
- **Proverbs 17:23** — "A wicked man taketh a gift out of the bosom to pervert the ways of judgment"
- **Isaiah 1:23** — "Thy princes are rebellious... every one loveth gifts, and followeth after rewards: they judge not the fatherless, neither doth the cause of the widow come unto them" — judicial bribery as the symptom of national apostasy
- **Micah 3:11** — "The heads thereof judge for reward, and the priests thereof teach for hire, and the prophets thereof divine for money" — all three offices corrupted by money
- *Interpretation:* Bribery does not merely produce unjust decisions — it "blindeth the wise." The person who takes a bribe loses the capacity to see clearly; the gift corrupts perception itself. This is the internal mechanism of corruption: the heart follows the money, and then the mind follows the heart. The only remedy is to never take the gift in the first place. This principle applies to judicial, prophetic, and priestly roles — Micah 3:11 shows all three offices can be corrupted by financial incentive.

### The Three Annual Feasts — The Prophetic Calendar of Redemption

**Exodus 23:14-16** — "Three times thou shalt keep a feast unto me in the year"
1. Feast of Unleavened Bread (Passover season — Abib)
2. Feast of Harvest / Firstfruits (Pentecost — 50 days after Passover)
3. Feast of Ingathering (Tabernacles — end of year)

- **Leviticus 23** — The full calendar of feasts: Passover, Unleavened Bread, Firstfruits, Pentecost, Trumpets, Atonement, Tabernacles
- **1 Corinthians 5:7** — "Christ our passover is sacrificed for us" — Passover fulfilled
- **Acts 2:1-4** — Pentecost: the Spirit poured out on the Feast of Harvest — Firstfruits fulfilled
- **Zechariah 14:16** — All nations keeping the Feast of Tabernacles in the Millennium — Ingathering yet to be fulfilled
- *Interpretation:* The three pilgrimage feasts are the prophetic calendar of redemption. All three are centered on harvest — spring (Passover/firstfruits of barley), early summer (Pentecost/wheat harvest), and autumn (Tabernacles/final harvest). The agricultural pattern is also the redemptive pattern: Christ's death and resurrection (spring/Passover), the outpouring of the Spirit and the church age (summer/Pentecost), and the final ingathering of all nations (autumn/Tabernacles). The first two feasts have been fulfilled historically; the third awaits fulfillment at the end of the age.

### "My Name Is in Him" — The Angel Before Israel

**Exodus 23:20-21** — "Behold, I send an Angel before thee, to keep thee in the way, and to bring thee into the place which I have prepared. Beware of him, and obey his voice, provoke him not; for he will not pardon your transgressions: for my name is in him"

- **Exodus 3:2** — The Angel of the LORD in the burning bush — identified with YHWH
- **Isaiah 63:9** — "The angel of his presence saved them" — the redemptive Angel identified with God's own presence
- **John 8:58** — "Before Abraham was, I am" — the pre-incarnate Christ's self-identification
- **John 1:14** — "The Word was made flesh, and dwelt (tabernacled) among us" — the Angel in whom God's name dwells becomes incarnate
- **Colossians 2:9** — "In him dwelleth all the fulness of the Godhead bodily" — in Christ, the fullness of the divine Name
- *Interpretation:* "My name is in him" is the most direct identification of the Angel as a divine Person, not merely a created messenger. A created angel carries God's authority as an ambassador; but this Angel carries God's name — God's essential identity is in Him. This is the pre-incarnate Christ: the Angel of the Presence who guided Israel through the wilderness, who appeared in the burning bush, who fought for Israel at the Red Sea, and who later became flesh and tabernacled among men (John 1:14). The continuity from Exodus 23 to John 1 is unbroken.

### "By Little and Little I Will Drive Them Out" — The Pace of God's Kingdom

**Exodus 23:29-30** — "I will not drive them out from before thee in one year; lest the land become desolate, and the beast of the field multiply against thee. By little and little I will drive them out from before thee, until thou be increased, and inherit the land"

- **Luke 13:18-21** — The kingdom of God is like a mustard seed that grows, or leaven that spreads through the whole lump — gradual, organic growth
- **Mark 4:28** — "the earth bringeth forth fruit of herself; first the blade, then the ear, after that the full corn in the ear"
- **Proverbs 13:11** — "Wealth gotten by vanity shall be diminished: but he that gathereth by labour shall increase"
- *Interpretation:* "By little and little" is God's stated strategy for the conquest of Canaan — and it is His pattern for the kingdom's advance in general. Instantaneous total conquest would leave the land desolate; gradual displacement allows the land to be maintained. The spiritual parallel: God does not transform a person or a community all at once. The progressive sanctification, the gradual displacement of sin, the slow growth of kingdom culture — these are features of divine wisdom, not failures of divine power. God is patient with the pace of His own work.

---

### "It Will Surely Be a Snare unto Thee" — The Danger of Partial Obedience

**Exodus 23:33** — "They shall not dwell in thy land, lest they make thee sin against me: for if thou serve their gods, it will surely be a snare unto thee"

- **Judges 2:3** — God repeats this exact warning: "their gods shall be a snare unto you" — after Israel failed to drive out the Canaanites
- **Judges 2:10-13** — The next generation "knew not the LORD... And they forsook the LORD, and served Baal and Ashtaroth" — the snare was sprung
- **1 Corinthians 15:33** — "Evil communications corrupt good manners" — the New Testament principle: what you tolerate influences you
- **2 Corinthians 6:14** — "Be ye not unequally yoked together with unbelievers" — the covenant separation principle
- *Interpretation:* The "snare" warning is one of the most repeatedly vindicated warnings in the Old Testament. Israel was told exactly what would happen if they made covenants with Canaan. They made the covenants. The snare was sprung — exactly as predicted. Judges is the historical fulfillment of Exodus 23:33. The principle: what you do not displace will eventually displace you. Partial obedience in the area of spiritual allegiance always leads to full corruption over time.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### SNARE (Hebrew: moqesh)
- **Exodus 23:33** — "it will surely be a snare (moqesh) unto thee" — moqesh = a bait/trap; the trigger of a fowler's trap that springs when stepped on
- **Psalm 91:3** — "He shall deliver thee from the snare (moqesh) of the fowler"
- *Definition here:* A hidden mechanism that catches the unwary; tolerated sin that appears harmless until it springs shut; the false gods of Canaan are not merely bad religion — they are a covenant trap that will capture and hold those who entertain them*

### FEAST OF INGATHERING (Hebrew: chag ha-asif)
- **Exodus 23:16** — "the feast of ingathering, which is in the end of the year" — the Feast of Tabernacles/Booths; asif = harvest, ingathering, collecting in
- The final harvest feast; the great celebration of all that has been brought in
- **Zechariah 14:16** — All nations will celebrate this feast in the Millennium
- *Definition here:* The eschatological feast — the final gathering in of all nations at the end of the age; the harvest of the whole earth*

## THE ACTUAL VERSES — EXODUS 23 (KJV)

**1** Thou shalt not raise a false report: put not thine hand with the wicked to be an unrighteous witness.
**2** Thou shalt not follow a multitude to do evil; neither shalt thou speak in a cause to decline after many to wrest judgment:
**3** Neither shalt thou countenance a poor man in his cause.
**4** If thou meet thine enemy's ox or his ass going astray, thou shalt surely bring it back to him again.
**5** If thou see the ass of him that hateth thee lying under his burden, and wouldest forbear to help him, thou shalt surely help with him.
**6** Thou shalt not wrest the judgment of thy poor in his cause.
**7** Keep thee far from a false matter; and the innocent and righteous slay thou not: for I will not justify the wicked.
**8** And thou shalt take no gift: for the gift blindeth the wise, and perverteth the words of the righteous.
**9** Also thou shalt not oppress a stranger: for ye know the heart of a stranger, seeing ye were strangers in the land of Egypt.
**10** And six years thou shalt sow thy land, and shalt gather in the fruits thereof:
**11** But the seventh year thou shalt let it rest and lie still; that the poor of thy people may eat: and what they leave the beasts of the field shall eat. In like manner thou shalt deal with thy vineyard, and with thy oliveyard.
**12** Six days thou shalt do thy work, and on the seventh day thou shalt rest: that thine ox and thine ass may rest, and the son of thy handmaid, and the stranger, may be refreshed.
**13** And in all things that I have said unto you be circumspect: and make no mention of the name of other gods, neither let it be heard out of thy mouth.
**14** Three times thou shalt keep a feast unto me in the year.
**15** Thou shalt keep the feast of unleavened bread: (thou shalt eat unleavened bread seven days, as I commanded thee, in the time appointed of the month Abib; for in it thou camest out from Egypt: and none shall appear before me empty:)
**16** And the feast of harvest, the firstfruits of thy labours, which thou hast sown in the field: and the feast of ingathering, which is in the end of the year, when thou hast gathered in thy labours out of the field.
**17** Three times in the year all thy males shall appear before the Lord GOD.
**18** Thou shalt not offer the blood of my sacrifice with leavened bread; neither shall the fat of my sacrifice remain until the morning.
**19** The first of the firstfruits of thy land thou shalt bring into the house of the LORD thy God. Thou shalt not seethe a kid in his mother's milk.
**20** Behold, I send an Angel before thee, to keep thee in the way, and to bring thee into the place which I have prepared.
**21** Beware of him, and obey his voice, provoke him not; for he will not pardon your transgressions: for my name is in him.
**22** But if thou shalt indeed obey his voice, and do all that I speak; then I will be an enemy unto thine enemies, and an adversary unto thine adversaries.
**23** For mine Angel shall go before thee, and bring thee in unto the Amorites, and the Hittites, and the Perizzites, and the Canaanites, the Hivites, and the Jebusites: and I will cut them off.
**24** Thou shalt not bow down to their gods, nor serve them, nor do after their works: but thou shalt utterly overthrow them, and quite break down their images.
**25** And ye shall serve the LORD your God, and he shall bless thy bread, and thy water; and I will take sickness away from the midst of thee.
**26** There shall nothing cast their young, nor be barren, in thy land: the number of thy days I will fulfil.
**27** I will send my fear before thee, and will destroy all the people to whom thou shalt come, and I will make all thine enemies turn their backs unto thee.
**28** And I will send hornets before thee, which shall drive out the Hivite, the Canaanite, and the Hittite, from before thee.
**29** I will not drive them out from before thee in one year; lest the land become desolate, and the beast of the field multiply against thee.
**30** By little and little I will drive them out from before thee, until thou be increased, and inherit the land.
**31** And I will set thy bounds from the Red sea even unto the sea of the Philistines, and from the desert unto the river: for I will deliver the inhabitants of the land into your hand; and thou shalt drive them out before thee.
**32** Thou shalt make no covenant with them, nor with their gods.
**33** They shall not dwell in thy land, lest they make thee sin against me: for if thou serve their gods, it will surely be a snare unto thee.

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The Three Feasts and Christ's First and Second Coming

| Feast | Hebrew Name | Season | Fulfillment |
|---|---|---|---|
| Unleavened Bread / Passover | Pesach / Chag HaMatzot | Spring | Christ's death and resurrection (1 Cor. 5:7) |
| Harvest / Firstfruits / Weeks | Shavuot / Pentecost | Early Summer | Outpouring of the Spirit — Acts 2:1-4 |
| Ingathering / Tabernacles | Sukkot | Autumn | Final ingathering of nations — Zechariah 14:16 |

The two spring feasts = Christ's first coming. The autumn feast = Christ's second coming. The summer feast = the entire church age between the two advents.
