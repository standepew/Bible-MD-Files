## CHAPTER SUMMARY

God gives Moses precise specifications for the Tabernacle structure. The innermost layer: ten curtains of fine twisted linen with blue, purple, and scarlet, embroidered with cherubim — coupled together with fifty gold clasps into one complete covering. Second layer: eleven curtains of goats' hair, coupled with fifty bronze clasps. Third and fourth layers: rams' skins dyed red and badgers' skins. The structural frame: boards of shittim wood, each 10 cubits by 1.5 cubits, standing upright in silver sockets (two sockets per board), held together by horizontal bars of gold-overlaid shittim wood — the middle bar running the entire length. The veil: blue, purple, and scarlet fine linen embroidered with cherubim, hung on four gold-overlaid pillars with gold hooks in silver sockets — dividing the Holy Place from the Most Holy. Behind the veil: the Ark and mercy seat. Before the veil: the table (north) and menorah (south). The entrance: a woven hanging on five gold-overlaid pillars with bronze sockets.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### The Veil — Dividing the Holy from the Most Holy

**Exodus 26:33** — "the vail shall divide unto you between the holy place and the most holy"

- **Leviticus 16:2** — "Speak unto Aaron thy brother, that he come not at all times into the holy place within the vail before the mercy seat, that he die not: for I will appear in the cloud upon the mercy seat" — only the high priest, once a year, on the Day of Atonement
- **Hebrews 9:7-8** — "But into the second went the high priest alone once every year, not without blood... The Holy Ghost this signifying, that the way into the holiest of all was not yet made manifest, while as the first tabernacle was yet standing"
- **Matthew 27:51** — "And, behold, the veil of the temple was rent in twain from the top to the bottom; and the earth did quake" — at Christ's death, the veil is torn from top to bottom — by God, not men
- **Hebrews 10:19-20** — "Having therefore, brethren, boldness to enter into the holiest by the blood of Jesus, By a new and living way, which he hath consecrated for us, through the veil, that is to say, his flesh"
- *Interpretation:* The veil is the theological center of the Tabernacle's structure. Everything about it says: "You cannot enter here." It was thick (Josephus says it took 300 priests to carry), beautiful (cherubim woven into it), and backed by the death penalty (Leviticus 16:2). It stood as the visible declaration that the way to God was not yet fully open. When Christ died, God tore it — from top to bottom, indicating the act was divine, not human. Hebrews 10:19-20 identifies the veil with Christ's flesh: access to the Father is now through Christ's broken body. The torn veil = the opened way.

---

### The Silver Sockets — The Foundation of Redemption

**Exodus 26:19** — "thou shalt make forty sockets of silver under the twenty boards; two sockets under one board"

- The entire Tabernacle frame stood on silver sockets
- **Exodus 30:11-16** — The silver came from the half-shekel "ransom money" of every numbered Israelite — the atonement census
- **1 Peter 1:18-19** — "ye were not redeemed with corruptible things, as silver and gold... But with the precious blood of Christ" — the silver of redemption is the type; Christ's blood is the antitype
- *Interpretation:* The Tabernacle stood on silver — and the silver was the ransom money paid for the souls of all Israel. Every board, every pillar rested on silver sockets that were the price of a soul. The foundation of God's dwelling place among His people was the ransom paid for those people. The Tabernacle quite literally stood on redemption money. This is the type: the New Covenant dwelling of God among men (the church, and ultimately the new Jerusalem) also stands on redemption — Christ's blood, not silver, is the foundation of access to God.

---

### The Four Layers of the Tabernacle Coverings — Types of Christ

| Layer | Material | Typological Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Innermost: ten curtains | Fine linen + blue/purple/scarlet + cherubim | The righteousness of Christ; His royal dignity; blood-red sacrifice |
| Second: eleven goat-hair curtains | Goats' hair | The sin offering — the goat is the animal of the sin offering (Leviticus 16) |
| Third: rams' skins dyed red | Rams' skins — red | The ram of consecration; the burnt offering; blood of dedication |
| Outer: badgers' skins | Rough, unattractive hide | Isaiah 53:2 — "no beauty that we should desire him" — the outer appearance of Christ to the world |

*The Tabernacle's outer appearance was rough and unimpressive (badgers' skins); its inner beauty was incomparable (gold and fine linen). So with Christ: "he hath no form nor comeliness" to the world (Isaiah 53:2), but to those who enter, He is the glory of God.*

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The Torn Veil — The Opened Way

- The veil separated the Holy Place (priests) from the Most Holy (high priest alone, once a year)
- **Matthew 27:51** — Torn top to bottom at Christ's death — God tore it from above
- **Hebrews 10:19-22** — "Boldness to enter the Holiest by the blood of Jesus... through the veil, that is to say, his flesh"
- The veil was Christ's flesh — the barrier between man and God; His broken body is the opened way. When His flesh was torn (the cross), the veil was torn. Access to God is now through the torn flesh of the Son.

## CHAPTER SUMMARY

The Altar of Burnt Offering: shittim wood overlaid with brass, five cubits square and three cubits high, a horn on each of its four corners, with a brass grate and brass implements, carried on staves. This is the first thing encountered when entering the Tabernacle courtyard — the place of sacrifice. The Court of the Tabernacle: 100 cubits long by 50 wide, enclosed with fine linen hangings on pillars with brass sockets and silver hooks. The gate on the east side: a 20-cubit hanging of blue, purple, and scarlet on four pillars. Everything visible from outside is linen and brass; everything inside is gold. Command for the perpetual lamp: pure beaten olive oil, kept burning from evening to morning by Aaron and his sons, as a statute forever before the LORD.

---

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### The Altar of Burnt Offering — The Cross of the Tabernacle

**Exodus 27:1-8** — The first object encountered entering the Tabernacle court

- The altar is the threshold of approach — you cannot enter God's presence without passing the place of sacrifice
- **Hebrews 13:10** — "We have an altar, whereof they have no right to eat which serve the tabernacle" — the New Testament altar is Christ's cross
- **John 14:6** — "I am the way, the truth, and the life: no man cometh unto the Father, but by me" — no approach to God except through sacrifice; no entry to the Father except through Christ
- **Romans 3:25** — "Whom God hath set forth to be a propitiation through faith in his blood"
- *Interpretation:* The layout of the Tabernacle is the gospel in geography: you enter the gate (there is only one gate — John 10:9), and the first thing you encounter is the altar of sacrifice. There is no path past the altar to God; the altar must be passed through. This is the irreducible truth of the gospel: no one comes to the Father except through the cross. The altar's position is not incidental — it is the theological claim of the entire system.

---

### The Horns of the Altar — The Place of Refuge

**Exodus 27:2** — "And thou shalt make the horns of it upon the four corners thereof: his horns shall be of the same"

- **Exodus 21:14** — A murderer is taken even from the altar's horns to die — the altar horn is a place of refuge for the innocent but not the guilty
- **1 Kings 1:50** — Adonijah "caught hold on the horns of the altar" when he feared Solomon
- **1 Kings 2:28** — Joab fled and "caught hold on the horns of the altar" — Solomon's judgment reached him there
- **Psalm 118:27** — "Bind the sacrifice with cords, even unto the horns of the altar"
- *Interpretation:* The horns of the altar were the four protruding points at each corner — the place where the blood of atonement was applied (Leviticus 4:7) and the place to which one who feared death fled for refuge. The four corners and four horns = God's protection extending in all four directions. The altar simultaneously executes judgment on the sacrifice and provides refuge for the person; Christ's cross simultaneously executes God's judgment on sin and provides refuge for the sinner.

---

### The Single Gate of the Court — "I Am the Door"

**Exodus 27:16** — "And for the gate of the court shall be an hanging of twenty cubits, of blue, and purple, and scarlet, and fine twined linen"

- One gate on the east side; no other entry into the court
- **John 10:9** — "I am the door: by me if any man enter in, he shall be saved, and shall go in and out, and find pasture"
- **John 14:6** — "No man cometh unto the Father, but by me"
- **Acts 4:12** — "Neither is there salvation in any other: for there is none other name under heaven given among men, whereby we must be saved"
- *Interpretation:* The Tabernacle court had one gate. Not multiple entrances for different traditions or approaches — one gate, facing east (toward the rising sun; the direction from which Israel camped in Numbers 3). The gate was 20 cubits wide — large enough for all to enter, but only one. This is the architectural statement of Acts 4:12: one way in. Jesus' identification as "the door" (John 10:9) is the New Testament interpretation of the single gate of the Tabernacle court.

---

### The Perpetual Lamp — Evening to Morning

**Exodus 27:20-21** — "pure oil olive beaten for the light, to cause the lamp to burn always... from evening to morning before the LORD"

- **Leviticus 24:2-4** — The lamp burns continually; Aaron tends it from evening to morning in the Holy Place
- **Psalm 119:105** — "Thy word is a lamp unto my feet, and a light unto my path" — the lamp as the word of God
- **Zechariah 4:1-6** — The golden lampstand with the oil from the olive trees: "Not by might, nor by power, but by my spirit" — the oil is the Holy Spirit
- **Revelation 4:5** — "And there were seven lamps of fire burning before the throne, which are the seven Spirits of God"
- *Interpretation:* The lamp burns from evening to morning — through the night, when darkness is complete, the light in the sanctuary never goes out. God's light does not fail in the darkness. The oil (the Holy Spirit) is the fuel; the priests maintain the supply; the light is perpetual. This is the promise of Christ's presence: "lo, I am with you alway, even unto the end of the world" (Matthew 28:20) — the perpetual lamp of His presence never goes out in the darkness of this age.

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The Tabernacle as the Gospel in Architecture

Moving from outside to inside traces the path of salvation:

1. **The single gate (east)** → John 10:9 — "I am the door"
2. **The Altar of Burnt Offering** → Romans 3:25 — propitiation through faith in His blood
3. **The Laver** (ch.30) → John 13:10 — "he that is washed needeth not save to wash his feet"
4. **The Holy Place: Table, Menorah, Altar of Incense** → Fellowship with God: the Word, the Spirit, prayer
5. **The Veil** → Matthew 27:51 — torn at the cross; access now open
6. **The Most Holy Place: Ark and Mercy Seat** → Hebrews 10:19 — boldness to enter the Holiest

Every piece of furniture, every spatial arrangement preaches the path to God through Christ.
