## CHAPTER SUMMARY

After the golden calf judgment, God tells Moses: go to the promised land — I'll send an angel before you — but I will not go in your midst, for you are a stiffnecked people and My presence among you would consume you. The people mourn and strip their ornaments. Moses pitches a tent outside the camp called the Tent of Meeting; whoever seeks the LORD goes out to it. The pillar of cloud descends and stands at its door; God speaks with Moses face to face, as a man speaks to his friend. Moses then makes one of the boldest requests in Scripture: "Shew me now thy way." God promises His presence. Moses escalates: "If thy presence go not with me, carry us not up hence." God grants it. Moses pushes further: "I beseech thee, shew me thy glory." God replies: "I will make all my goodness pass before thee... I will be gracious to whom I will be gracious, and will shew mercy on whom I will shew mercy. Thou canst not see my face: for there shall no man see me, and live." Moses is placed in the cleft of the rock, covered by God's hand while the glory passes — he will see God's back, not His face.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "I Will Not Go Up in the Midst of Thee" — The Terror of Holiness Among Sinners

**Exodus 33:3, 5** — "I will not go up in the midst of thee; for thou art a stiffnecked people: lest I consume thee in the way"

- **Isaiah 6:5** — Isaiah sees the LORD: "Woe is me! for I am undone; because I am a man of unclean lips... for mine eyes have seen the King, the LORD of hosts"
- **Luke 5:8** — Peter: "Depart from me; for I am a sinful man, O Lord" — the instinctive response to holiness
- **Hebrews 12:29** — "For our God is a consuming fire"
- *Interpretation:* God's withdrawal is not rejection — it is mercy. His holiness in direct contact with a sinful people would consume them. This is the same principle as "no man shall see me and live" (v.20) — not arbitrary restriction but the ontological reality that uncovered sin cannot survive in direct contact with absolute holiness. The entire sacrificial/priestly system exists because God wants to be near His people without consuming them. The problem of Exodus 33 is solved only by a perfect mediator and a sufficient atonement — which the Tabernacle types and Christ fulfills.

---

### "Face to Face, as a Man Speaketh Unto His Friend"

**Exodus 33:11** — "And the LORD spake unto Moses face to face, as a man speaketh unto his friend"

- **Numbers 12:6-8** — God distinguishes Moses from all other prophets: "With him will I speak mouth to mouth, even apparently, and not in dark speeches; and the similitude of the LORD shall he behold" — Moses' intimacy with God is unique
- **Deuteronomy 34:10** — "And there arose not a prophet since in Israel like unto Moses, whom the LORD knew face to face"
- **John 15:15** — Jesus to His disciples: "I have called you friends; for all things that I have heard of my Father I have made known unto you" — the New Covenant transforms the whole covenant community into "friends" in the way Moses was a friend
- **James 2:23** — Abraham was "called the Friend of God" — the same relational category
- *Interpretation:* Moses' face-to-face friendship with God is a unique privilege under the Old Covenant — the closest approach a human being made to God before the Incarnation. Jesus in John 15:15 deliberately elevates every disciple to this same relational status: "I call you friends." The New Covenant makes the Moses-level of friendship with God available to every believer through Christ the Mediator — what was Moses' exclusive privilege becomes the common inheritance of the redeemed.

---

### "If Thy Presence Go Not with Me, Carry Us Not Up Hence"

**Exodus 33:15** — "And he said unto him, If thy presence go not with me, carry us not up hence"

- Moses refuses the promised land without God's presence; a land without God is worse than the wilderness with God
- **Psalm 84:10** — "I had rather be a doorkeeper in the house of my God, than to dwell in the tents of wickedness"
- **Psalm 73:25** — "Whom have I in heaven but thee? and there is none upon earth that I desire beside thee"
- **Philippians 1:21** — "For to me to live is Christ, and to die is gain" — the New Covenant equivalent: Christ is the presence, not the geography
- *Interpretation:* Moses' refusal is one of the great declarations of covenant theology: God's presence is the point, not the land. The promised land without God is not the promise — it is just real estate. This principle governs the entire narrative of redemptive history: the goal is not a place but a Presence. The new Jerusalem is the goal not because it is a great city but because "the tabernacle of God is with men" (Revelation 21:3). Moses understood this before Israel did.

---

### "I Will Make All My Goodness Pass Before Thee" — The Self-Revelation of God

**Exodus 33:19** — "I will make all my goodness pass before thee, and I will proclaim the name of the LORD before thee; and will be gracious to whom I will be gracious, and will shew mercy on whom I will shew mercy"

- **Exodus 34:6-7** — The proclamation that follows: "The LORD, The LORD God, merciful and gracious, longsuffering, and abundant in goodness and truth, keeping mercy for thousands, forgiving iniquity and transgression and sin, and that will by no means clear the guilty"
- **Romans 9:15-16** — Paul quotes Exodus 33:19 directly: "For he saith to Moses, I will have mercy on whom I will have mercy, and I will have compassion on whom I will have compassion. So then it is not of him that willeth, nor of him that runneth, but of God that sheweth mercy" — the basis of Paul's doctrine of divine sovereignty in election
- *Interpretation:* God's response to Moses' request — "shew me thy glory" — is: "I will make all my goodness pass before thee." God's glory IS His goodness. The self-revelation of God is not a vision of power or majesty primarily — it is the proclamation of His character: mercy, grace, patience, truth. This is the deepest thing Moses was allowed to know about God. Romans 9:15 identifies this passage as the foundation of election theology: the distribution of mercy is God's sovereign prerogative, not earned by human will or effort.

---

### "The Cleft of the Rock" — Hidden in Christ

**Exodus 33:22** — "I will put thee in a clift of the rock, and will cover thee with my hand while I pass by"

- **1 Corinthians 10:4** — "that Rock was Christ" — the rock that follows Israel is identified with Christ; therefore the cleft in which Moses is hidden = hidden in Christ
- **Colossians 3:3** — "For ye are dead, and your life is hid with Christ in God"
- **Isaiah 32:2** — "A man shall be as an hiding place from the wind, and a covert from the tempest; as rivers of water in a dry place, as the shadow of a great rock in a weary land"
- **Psalm 32:7** — "Thou art my hiding place"
- *Interpretation:* Moses sheltered in the cleft of the rock while God's glory passed is the type of the believer hidden in Christ. No human being can survive direct unmediated exposure to God's glory — but in the rock's cleft, covered by God's own hand, Moses sees the afterglow and lives. The type is exact: we cannot stand before God in our own persons — but hidden in Christ (Colossians 3:3), we see God's goodness and live.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### FACE TO FACE (Hebrew: panim el panim)
- **Exodus 33:11** — panim = face/presence (same word); el = to/toward; the idiom for direct, unmediated encounter
- Paradox: v.11 says "face to face"; v.20 says "thou canst not see my face and live"
- *Resolution:* The "face to face" is the intimacy and directness of the communication — like a friend; the "cannot see my face" is the unveiled, full direct ontological exposure to divine glory. Moses communicated directly; he could not sustain uncovered, unveiled direct vision of God's essential being.

### GOODNESS (Hebrew: tov)
- **Exodus 33:19** — "I will make all my goodness (tov) pass before thee" — tov = good, pleasant, beautiful, kind
- God's glory, when asked to be revealed to Moses, is defined as His tov — His moral and relational character of goodness
- *Definition here:* God's glory is not primarily His power or His splendor but His moral character — the totality of His kindness, mercy, faithfulness, and truth expressed in covenant relationship*

## THE ACTUAL VERSES — EXODUS 33 (KJV)

**1** And the LORD said unto Moses, Depart, and go up hence, thou and the people which thou hast brought up out of the land of Egypt, unto the land which I sware unto Abraham, to Isaac, and to Jacob, saying, Unto thy seed will I give it:
**2** And I will send an angel before thee; and I will drive out the Canaanite, the Amorite, and the Hittite, and the Perizzite, the Hivite, and the Jebusite:
**3** Unto a land flowing with milk and honey: for I will not go up in the midst of thee; for thou art a stiffnecked people: lest I consume thee in the way.
**4** And when the people heard these evil tidings, they mourned: and no man did put on him his ornaments.
**5** For the LORD had said unto Moses, Say unto the children of Israel, Ye are a stiffnecked people: I will come up into the midst of thee in a moment, and consume thee: therefore now put off thy ornaments from thee, that I may know what to do unto thee.
**6** And the children of Israel stripped themselves of their ornaments by the mount Horeb.
**7** And Moses took the tabernacle, and pitched it without the camp, afar off from the camp, and called it the Tabernacle of the congregation. And it came to pass, that every one which sought the LORD went out unto the tabernacle of the congregation, which was without the camp.
**8** And it came to pass, when Moses went out unto the tabernacle, that all the people rose up, and stood every man at his tent door, and looked after Moses, until he was gone into the tabernacle.
**9** And it came to pass, as Moses entered into the tabernacle, the cloudy pillar descended, and stood at the door of the tabernacle, and the LORD talked with Moses.
**10** And all the people saw the cloudy pillar stand at the tabernacle door: and all the people rose up and worshipped, every man in his tent door.
**11** And the LORD spake unto Moses face to face, as a man speaketh unto his friend. And he turned again into the camp: but his servant Joshua, the son of Nun, a young man, departed not out of the tabernacle.
**12** And Moses said unto the LORD, See, thou sayest unto me, Bring up this people: and thou hast not let me know whom thou wilt send with me. Yet thou hast said, I know thee by name, and thou hast also found grace in my sight.
**13** Now therefore, I pray thee, if I have found grace in thy sight, shew me now thy way, that I may know thee, that I may find grace in thy sight: and consider that this nation is thy people.
**14** And he said, My presence shall go with thee, and I will give thee rest.
**15** And he said unto him, If thy presence go not with me, carry us not up hence.
**16** For wherein shall it be known here that I and thy people have found grace in thy sight? is it not in that thou goest with us? so shall we be separated, I and thy people, from all the people that are upon the face of the earth.
**17** And the LORD said unto Moses, I will do this thing also that thou hast spoken: for thou hast found grace in my sight, and I know thee by name.
**18** And he said, I beseech thee, shew me thy glory.
**19** And he said, I will make all my goodness pass before thee, and I will proclaim the name of the LORD before thee; and will be gracious to whom I will be gracious, and will shew mercy on whom I will shew mercy.
**20** And he said, Thou canst not see my face: for there shall no man see me, and live.
**21** And the LORD said, Behold, there is a place by me, and thou shalt stand upon a rock:
**22** And it shall come to pass, while my glory passeth by, that I will put thee in a clift of the rock, and will cover thee with my hand while I pass by:
**23** And I will take away mine hand, and thou shalt see my back parts: but my face shall not be seen.

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The Tent Outside the Camp — A Type of Christ "Outside the Camp"

- **Exodus 33:7** — Moses pitches the tent of meeting outside the camp after the golden calf; those who seek God must go out from the corrupt camp to find Him
- **Hebrews 13:12-13** — "Wherefore Jesus also, that he might sanctify the people with his own blood, suffered without the gate. Let us go forth therefore unto him without the camp, bearing his reproach"
- *Type:* When Israel's camp became defiled by idolatry, the meeting place with God moved outside. The type is Christ crucified outside Jerusalem's gate — the holy One bearing the reproach outside the established religious system. To find God, you go outside the camp of religious establishment, to the place of reproach, bearing His cross.
