## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Moses assembles the entire congregation. He opens with the Sabbath command — no work, no fire on the Sabbath, death for violation. Then he calls for the freewill offering: gold, silver, brass, fine cloth, skins, wood, oil, spices, onyx stones — "whosoever is of a willing heart, let him bring it." The people depart. Then comes the response: men and women, "every one whose heart stirred him up, and every one whom his spirit made willing," bring jewelry, cloth, skins, wood, oil, spice, stones. Wise-hearted women spin with their hands — blue, purple, scarlet, fine linen. Other women whose hearts stirred them in wisdom spun goats' hair. Rulers brought onyx stones, spices, oil. Moses then presents Bezaleel and Aholiab by name to the congregation — filled with the Spirit of God for wisdom, understanding, and knowledge in all manner of workmanship — and Aholiab is also equipped to teach.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Every One Whose Heart Stirred Him Up" — Willing-Heart Giving

**Exodus 35:21** — "every one whose heart stirred him up, and every one whom his spirit made willing"

- Hebrew: nasa libo = "lifted up his heart"; nadav rucho = "his spirit was generous/willing"
- **2 Corinthians 9:7** — "Every man according as he purposeth in his heart, so let him give; not grudgingly, or of necessity: for God loveth a cheerful giver"
- **1 Chronicles 29:5-9** — David's offering for the Temple: "Who then is willing to consecrate his service this day unto the LORD?... the people rejoiced, for that they offered willingly, because with perfect heart they offered willingly to the LORD"
- **Exodus 32:2-3** — Less than six weeks earlier, these same people stripped off their gold earrings for the golden calf; now their stirred hearts bring them to the Tabernacle project
- *Interpretation:* The Tabernacle is funded entirely by willing hearts — not taxation, not compulsion, not guilt. The Spirit stirs; the people respond. This is the contrast with Exodus 32: the same gold that built the calf (taken by Aaron under pressure) now builds the sanctuary (given freely by stirred hearts). Repentance produces generosity. 2 Corinthians 9:7 is the New Covenant principle of the same truth: the motivation of giving is the Spirit-moved heart, not external obligation.

---

### Women Spinning at Home — Every Contribution Matters

**Exodus 35:25-26** — "all the women that were wise hearted did spin with their hands... all the women whose heart stirred them up in wisdom spun goats' hair"

- The spinning women contributed the work of their hands in their households — not gold
- Without them, the goat-hair curtains (the second layer of the Tabernacle covering) could not be made
- **Luke 21:2-4** — The widow's two mites: "she of her penury hath cast in all the living that she had"
- **1 Corinthians 12:22** — "those members of the body, which seem to be more feeble, are necessary"
- *Interpretation:* The Tabernacle requires every gift: the spectacular (gold for the Ark) and the practical (spun goat hair for the outer covering). The women spinning in their tents contributed as essentially as the rulers who brought onyx stones. God's dwelling is built from all levels of consecrated effort. No contribution offered from a willing heart is too small.

---

### Aholiab as Teacher — The Gift of Impartation

**Exodus 35:34** — "And he hath put in his heart that he may teach, both he, and Aholiab"

- The ability to teach craft — to pass the Spirit's skill to others — is itself a gift of God placed in the heart
- **Ephesians 4:11-12** — "And he gave some, apostles; and some, prophets; and some, evangelists; and some, pastors and teachers; For the perfecting of the saints, for the work of the ministry"
- **2 Timothy 2:2** — "The same commit thou to faithful men, who shall be able to teach others also"
- *Interpretation:* Aholiab is not only a craftsman — he is a craftsman who can teach. One generation of Spirit-gifted builders must pass skills to the next. The Spirit gives not only the ability to do but the ability to impart. This is the New Covenant discipleship principle: gifts must be transmitted, not hoarded.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### WILLING HEART / STIRRED SPIRIT (Hebrew: nasa libo / nadav rucho)
- **Exodus 35:21** — "whose heart stirred him up" = nasa libo (his heart lifted him); "whose spirit made him willing" = nadav rucho (his spirit was generous)
- Nadav = voluntary, willing, generous; the root of the name Nadab (Aaron's son)
- *Definition here:* The internal motivation produced by the Spirit that moves a person to give freely and fully; the opposite of grudging or compelled giving; the standard of all New Covenant generosity

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### The Same Gold — From the Calf to the Sanctuary

- **Exodus 32:2-3** — Israel gave gold earrings to Aaron for the calf — taken under pressure, for idolatry
- **Exodus 35:22** — Israel gives gold earrings, bracelets, rings willingly — for the sanctuary
- *The same material. The same people. Forty days between Exodus 32 and Exodus 35. The difference is the heart. This is the pattern of true repentance and renewal: what was given to idols is reconsecrated for God. The gold doesn't change; the direction of the heart does.*
