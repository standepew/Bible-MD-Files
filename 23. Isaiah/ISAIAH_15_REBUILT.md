## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### JUDGMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine justice, righteous retribution"
- **Context:** God's judgment on Moab for their pride and rebellion
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's righteous judgment
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual judgment (Revelation 8:8)

### MOURNING
- **Meaning:** "Spiritual grief, repentant sorrow"
- **Context:** Moab's mourning in sackcloth and baldness
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's call to repentance
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual mourning (Matthew 11:21)

### DESTRUCTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine punishment, complete ruin"
- **Context:** Complete destruction of Moab's cities and resources
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's victory over rebellion
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual destruction (Joel 1:10)

### REMNANT
- **Meaning:** "Faithful few, preserved people"
- **Context:** Even the remnant of Moab faces judgment
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's preservation of the faithful
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual remnant (Romans 11:5)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 The burden of Moab. Because in the night Ar of Moab is laid waste, and brought to silence; because in the night Kir of Moab is laid waste, and brought to silence;  

2 He is gone up to Bajith, and to Dibon, the high places, to weep: Moab shall howl over Nebo, and over Medeba: on all their heads shall be baldness, and every beard cut off.  

3 In their streets they shall gird themselves with sackcloth: on the tops of their houses, and in their streets, every one shall howl, weeping abundantly.  

4 And Heshbon shall cry, and Elealeh: their voice shall be heard even unto Jahaz: therefore the armed soldiers of Moab shall cry out; his life shall be grievous unto him.  

5 My heart shall cry out for Moab; his fugitives shall flee unto Zoar, an heifer of three years old: for by the mounting up of Luhith with weeping shall they go it up; for in the way of Horonaim they shall raise up a cry of destruction.  

6 For the waters of Nimrim shall be desolate: for the hay is withered away, the grass faileth, there is no green thing.  

7 Therefore the abundance they have gotten, and that which they have laid up, shall they carry away to the brook of the willows.  

8 For the cry is gone round about the borders of Moab; the howling thereof unto Eglaim, and the howling thereof unto Beer-elim.  

9 For the waters of Dimon shall be full of blood: for I will bring more upon Dimon, lions upon him that escapeth of Moab, and upon the remnant of the land.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "The burden of Moab"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about economic hardship
**Biblical context:** This is about divine judgment - Burden Moab represents God's judgment, not just economic issues.

### "For the waters of Nimrim shall be desolate"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about drought
**Biblical context:** This is about spiritual desolation - Waters desolate represents spiritual emptiness, not just lack of water.
