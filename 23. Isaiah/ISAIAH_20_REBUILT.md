## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### SIGN
- **Meaning:** "Divine message, prophetic demonstration"
- **Context:** Isaiah's nakedness as sign of coming judgment
- **Significance:** Model of Christ as ultimate sign
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sign (Matthew 12:39)

### NAKEDNESS
- **Meaning:** "Spiritual vulnerability, divine exposure"
- **Context:** Physical nakedness representing spiritual judgment
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's spiritual exposure
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual nakedness (Revelation 3:17)

### CAPTIVITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine judgment, spiritual bondage"
- **Context:** Assyrian captivity of Egypt and Ethiopia
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's judgment on rebellion
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual captivity (Romans 7:23)

### EXPECTATION
- **Meaning:** "False hope, misplaced trust"
- **Context:** Egypt's failed expectation of deliverance
- **Significance:** Model of Christ as true hope
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual expectation (Hebrews 11:1)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 In the year that Tartan came unto Ashdod, (when Sargon the king of Assyria sent him,) and fought against Ashdod, and took it;  

2 At the same time spake the LORD by Isaiah the son of Amoz, saying, Go and loose the sackcloth from off thy loins, and put off thy shoe from thy foot. And he did so, walking naked and barefoot.  

3 And the LORD said, Like as my servant Isaiah hath walked naked and barefoot three years for a sign and wonder upon Egypt and upon Ethiopia;  

4 So shall the king of Assyria lead away the Egyptians prisoners, and the Ethiopians captives, young and old, naked and barefoot, even with their buttocks uncovered, to the shame of Egypt.  

5 And they shall be afraid and ashamed of Ethiopia their expectation, and of Egypt their glory.  

6 And the inhabitant of this isle shall say in that day, Behold, such is our expectation, whither we flee for help to be delivered from the king of Assyria: and how shall we escape?

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Like as my servant Isaiah hath walked naked and barefoot three years for a sign and wonder"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about personal hardship
**Biblical context:** This is about prophetic demonstration - Naked barefoot represents spiritual judgment, not just personal suffering.

### "And the inhabitant of this isle shall say in that day, Behold, such is our expectation"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about political alliances
**Biblical context:** This is about misplaced trust - Expectation represents false hope, not just diplomacy.
