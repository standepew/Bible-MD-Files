## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### COMING
- **Meaning:** "Christ's appearance, divine manifestation"
- **Context:** Prayer for God to rend heavens and come down in power
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's glorious return
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual coming (Mark 15:38)

### POWER
- **Meaning:** "Divine might, Christ's strength"
- **Context:** God's awesome power that makes mountains flow and nations tremble
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's sovereign power
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual power (Hebrews 12:29)

### HUMILITY
- **Meaning:** "Human sinfulness, Christ's grace"
- **Context:** Recognition of human uncleanness and need for divine mercy
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's humble service
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual humility (Romans 3:23)

### CREATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine workmanship, Christ's sovereignty"
- **Context:** God as potter and humans as clay in His hands
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's creative authority
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual creation (Romans 9:21)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 Oh that thou wouldest rend the heavens, that thou wouldest come down, that the mountains might flow down at thy presence,  

2 As when the melting fire burneth, the fire causeth the waters to boil, to make thy name known to thine adversaries, that the nations may tremble at thy presence!  

3 When thou didst terrible things which we looked not for, thou camest down, the mountains flowed down at thy presence.  

4 For since the beginning of the world men have not heard, nor perceived by the ear, neither hath the eye seen, O God, beside thee, what he hath prepared for him that waiteth for him.  

5 Thou meetest him that rejoiceth and worketh righteousness, those that remember thee in thy ways: behold, thou art wroth; for we have sinned: in those is continuance, and we shall be saved.  

6 But we are all as an unclean thing, and all our righteousnesses are as filthy rags; and we all do fade as a leaf; and our iniquities, like the wind, have taken us away.  

7 And there is none that calleth upon thy name, that stirreth up himself to take hold of thee: for thou hast hid thy face from us, and hast consumed us, because of our iniquities.  

8 But now, O LORD, thou art our father; we are the clay, and thou our potter; and we all are the work of thy hand.  

9 Be not wroth very sore, O LORD, neither remember iniquity for ever: behold, see, we beseech thee, we are all thy people.  

10 Thy holy cities are a wilderness, Zion is a wilderness, Jerusalem a desolation.  

11 Our holy and our beautiful house, where our fathers praised thee, is burned up with fire: and all our pleasant things are laid waste.  

12 Wilt thou refrain thyself for these things, O LORD? wilt thou hold thy peace, and afflict us very sore?  

13 Wilt thou refrain thyself for these things, O LORD? wilt thou hold thy peace, and afflict us very sore?  

14 Wilt thou refrain thyself for these things, O LORD? wilt thou hold thy peace, and afflict us very sore?  

15 Wilt thou refrain thyself for these things, O LORD? wilt thou hold thy peace, and afflict us very sore.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Oh that thou wouldest rend the heavens"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any tearing
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's glorious return - Rend heavens represents divine intervention, not just destruction.

### "We are the clay, and thou our potter"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any pottery
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's sovereign authority - Clay represents human submission, not just craft.
