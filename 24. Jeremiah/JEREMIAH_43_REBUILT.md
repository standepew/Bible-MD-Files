## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### DISOBEDIENCE
- **Meaning:** "Spiritual rebellion, Christ's rejection"
- **Context:** The people's disobedience to God's clear command to stay in Judah
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's rejection of those who disobey His word
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual disobedience (John 8:44)

### FOLLY
- **Meaning:** "Spiritual foolishness, Christ's error"
- **Context:** The folly of fleeing to Egypt for security instead of trusting God
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's judgment on human wisdom versus divine wisdom
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual folly (Acts 7:52)

### JUDGMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine punishment, Christ's wrath"
- **Context:** God's judgment through Nebuchadnezzar's conquest of Egypt
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's judgment on those who trust in false security
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual judgment (1 Samuel 15:23)

### PROPHECY
- **Meaning:** "Divine revelation, Christ's foretelling"
- **Context:** The symbolic prophecy of stones representing Babylon's throne in Egypt
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's prophetic revelation of future events
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual prophecy (Joshua 24:27)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 And it came to pass, that when Jeremiah had made an end of speaking unto all the people all the words of the LORD their God, for which the LORD their God had sent him to them, even all these words,  

2 Then spake Azariah the son of Hoshaiah, and Johanan the son of Kareah, and all the proud men, saying unto Jeremiah, Thou speakest falsely: the LORD our God hath not sent thee to say, Go not into Egypt to sojourn there:  

3 But Baruch the son of Neriah setteth thee on against us, for to deliver us into the hand of the Chaldeans, that they might put us to death, and carry us away captives into Babylon.  

4 So Johanan the son of Kareah, and all the captains of the forces, and all the people, obeyed not the voice of the LORD, to dwell in the land of Judah.  

5 But Johanan the son of Kareah, and all the captains of the forces, took all the remnant of Judah, that were returned from all nations, whither they had been driven, to dwell in the land of Judah;  

6 Even men, and women, and children, and the king's daughters, and every person that Nebuzar-adan the captain of the guard had left with Gedaliah the son of Ahikam the son of Shaphan, and Jeremiah the prophet, and Baruch the son of Neriah.  

7 So they came into the land of Egypt: for they obeyed not the voice of the LORD: thus came they even to Tahpanhes.  

8 Then came the word of the LORD unto Jeremiah in Tahpanhes, saying,  

9 Take great stones in thine hand, and hide them in the clay in the brickkiln, which is at the entry of Pharaoh's house in Tahpanhes, in the sight of the men of Judah;  

10 And say unto them, Thus saith the LORD of hosts, the God of Israel; Behold, I will send and take Nebuchadrezzar the king of Babylon, my servant, and will set his throne upon these stones that I have hid; and he shall spread his royal pavilion over them.  

11 And when he cometh, he shall smite the land of Egypt, and deliver such as are for death to death; and such as are for captivity to captivity; and such as are for the sword to the sword.  

12 And I will kindle a fire in the houses of the gods of Egypt; and he shall burn them, and carry them away captives: and he shall array himself with the land of Egypt, as a shepherd putteth on his garment; and he shall go forth from thence in peace.  

13 He shall break also the images of Beth-shemesh, that is in the land of Egypt; and the houses of the gods of the Egyptians shall he burn with fire.  

14 He shall break also the images of Beth-shemesh, that is in the land of Egypt; and the houses of the gods of the Egyptians shall he burn with fire.  

15 He shall break also the images of Beth-shemesh, that is in the land of Egypt; and the houses of the gods of the Egyptians shall he burn with fire.  

16 He shall break also the images of Beth-shemesh, that is in the land of Egypt; and the houses of the gods of the Egyptians shall he burn with fire.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Thou speakest falsely: the LORD our God hath not sent thee to say, Go not into Egypt to sojourn there"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any disagreement
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's rejection of truth - Falsely represents spiritual deception, not just honest mistake.

### "Take great stones in thine hand, and hide them in the clay in the brickkiln"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any construction
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's symbolic prophecy - Stones represent divine authority, not just building materials.
