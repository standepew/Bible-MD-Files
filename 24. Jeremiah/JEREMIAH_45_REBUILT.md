## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### COMFORT
- **Meaning:** "Divine solace, Christ's encouragement"
- **Context:** God's comfort to Baruch in his sorrow over the nation's judgment
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's comfort to those who suffer for righteousness
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual comfort (Psalm 6:6)

### SORROW
- **Meaning:** "Spiritual grief, Christ's suffering"
- **Context:** Baruch's sorrow over the destruction and his personal suffering
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's sorrow over human sin and suffering
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sorrow (2 Corinthians 7:10)

### SOVEREIGNTY
- **Meaning:** "Divine authority, Christ's rule"
- **Context:** God's sovereign right to build up and break down according to His will
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's sovereign rule over all circumstances
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sovereignty (Jeremiah 31:28)

### PRESERVATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine protection, Christ's care"
- **Context:** God's promise to preserve Baruch's life despite coming judgment
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's preservation of His faithful servants
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual preservation (Philippians 4:19)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Thou didst say, Woe is me now! for the LORD hath added grief to my sorrow; I fainted in my sighing, and I find no rest"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any personal complaint
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's righteous sorrow - Sorrow represents grief over sin, not just personal discomfort.

### "And seekest thou great things for thyself? seek them not: for, behold, I will bring evil upon all flesh"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any ambition
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's call to contentment - Great things represent worldly ambition, not just any desire.
