## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### JUDGMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine punishment, Christ's wrath"
- **Context:** God's judgment on the Philistines through the invading army from the north
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's judgment on those who oppose His people
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual judgment (Isaiah 8:7)

### DEFEAT
- **Meaning:** "Divine overthrow, Christ's victory"
- **Context:** The complete defeat of the Philistines and their cities
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's victory over all opposition
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual defeat (Revelation 12:15)

### OVERFLOW
- **Meaning:** "Divine overwhelming, Christ's power"
- **Context:** The overwhelming flood representing the invading army's unstoppable advance
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's overwhelming power against evil
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual overflow (Jeremiah 4:13)

### SWORD
- **Meaning:** "Divine weapon, Christ's word"
- **Context:** The sword of the LORD as the instrument of judgment that eventually rests
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's word as the instrument of divine justice
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sword (Hebrews 4:12)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Thus saith the LORD; Behold, waters rise up out of the north, and shall be an overflowing flood, and shall overflow the land"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any natural disaster
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's divine judgment - Waters represent invading forces, not just literal water.

### "O thou sword of the LORD, how long will it be ere thou be quiet? put up thyself into thy scabbard, rest, and be still"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any weapon
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's word of judgment - Sword represents divine justice, not just metal weapon.
