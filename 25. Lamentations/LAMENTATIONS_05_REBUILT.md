## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### REJECTION
- **Meaning:** "Spiritual abandonment, Christ's forsakenness"
- **Context:** Jerusalem's rejection and abandonment, representing Christ's experience on the cross
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's bearing of human rejection and abandonment
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual rejection (Psalm 25:11)

### HUMILIATION
- **Meaning:** "Spiritual shame, Christ's disgrace"
- **Context:** The complete humiliation of Jerusalem's people and loss of all dignity
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's humiliation and suffering for humanity
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual humiliation (Leviticus 25:23)

### FORGETFULNESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine silence, Christ's hiddenness"
- **Context:** The sense of God's forgetfulness and abandonment in the midst of suffering
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's experience of apparent divine abandonment
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual forgetfulness (Psalm 68:5)

### RESTORATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine renewal, Christ's salvation"
- **Context:** The prayer for restoration and renewal of God's favor
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's work of restoration and salvation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual restoration (James 1:27)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 Remember, O LORD, what is come upon us: consider, and behold our reproach.  

2 Our inheritance is turned to strangers, our houses to aliens.  

3 We are orphans and fatherless, our mothers are as widows.  

4 We have drunken our water for money; our wood is sold unto us.  

5 Our necks are under persecution: we labour, and have no rest.  

6 We have given the hand to the Egyptians, and to the Assyrians, to be satisfied with bread.  

7 Our fathers have sinned, and are not; and we have borne their iniquities.  

8 Servants have ruled over us: there is none that doth deliver us out of their hand.  

9 We gat our bread with the peril of our lives because of the sword of the wilderness.  

10 Our skin was black like an oven because of the terrible famine.  

11 They ravished the women in Zion, and the maids in the cities of Judah.  

12 Princes are hanged up by their hand: the faces of elders were not honoured.  

13 They took the young men to grind, and the children fell under the wood.  

14 The elders have ceased from the gate, the young men from their musick.  

15 The joy of our heart is ceased; our dance is turned into mourning.  

16 The crown is fallen from our head: woe unto us, that we have sinned!  

17 For this our heart is faint; for these things our eyes are dim.  

18 Because of the mountain of Zion, which is desolate, the foxes walk upon it.  

19 Thou, O LORD, remainest for ever; thy throne from generation to generation.  

20 Wherefore dost thou forget us for ever, and forsake us so long time?  

21 Turn thou us unto thee, O LORD, and we shall be turned; renew our days as of old.  

22 But thou hast utterly rejected us; thou art very wroth against us.  

23 But thou hast utterly rejected us; thou art very wroth against us.  

24 But thou hast utterly rejected us; thou art very wroth against us.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Our inheritance is turned to strangers, our houses to aliens"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any loss of property
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's rejection - Inheritance represents God's covenant promises, not just physical property.

### "The crown is fallen from our head: woe unto us, that we have sinned!"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any loss of status
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's humiliation - Crown represents divine authority and glory, not just human honor.
