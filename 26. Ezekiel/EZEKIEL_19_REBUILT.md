## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### LAMENTATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine sorrow, Christ's mourning"
- **Context:** The lamentation over the princes of Israel who failed as leaders
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's mourning over the failure of human leadership
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual lamentation (Lamentations 1:1)

### LION
- **Meaning:** "Royal power, Christ's majesty"
- **Context:** The lion imagery representing the royal house of Judah
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's royal majesty and power
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual lion (Genesis 49:9)

### VINE
- **Meaning:** "God's people, Christ's Church"
- **Context:** The vine representing Judah's former fruitfulness and current desolation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's Church and His people
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual vine (Psalm 80:8)

### JUDGMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine punishment, Christ's wrath"
- **Context:** The judgment that brought down the princes and the vine
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's judgment on unfaithful leadership
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual judgment (Jeremiah 4:7)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 Moreover take thou up a lamentation for the princes of Israel,  

2 And say, What is thy mother? A lioness: she lay down among lions, she nourished her whelps among young lions.  

3 And she brought up one of her whelps: it became a young lion, and it learned to catch the prey; it devoured men.  

4 The nations also heard of him; he was taken in their pit, and they brought him with chains unto the land of Egypt.  

5 Now when she saw that she had waited, and her hope was lost, then she took another of her whelps, and made him a young lion.  

6 And he went up and down among the lions, he became a young lion, and learned to catch the prey, and devoured men.  

7 And he knew their desolate palaces, and he laid waste their cities; and the land was desolate, and the fulness thereof, by the noise of his roaring.  

8 Then the nations set against him on every side from the provinces, and spread their net over him: he was taken in their pit.  

9 And they put him in ward in chains, and brought him to the king of Babylon: they brought him into holds, that his voice should no more be heard upon the mountains of Israel.  

10 Thy mother is like a vine in thy blood, planted by the waters: she was fruitful and full of branches by reason of many waters.  

11 And she had strong rods for the sceptres of them that bare rule, and her stature was exalted among the thick branches, and she appeared in her height with the multitude of her branches.  

12 But she was plucked up in fury, she was cast down to the ground, and the east wind dried up her fruit: her strong rods were broken and withered; the fire consumed them.  

13 And now she is planted in the wilderness, in a dry and thirsty ground.  

14 And fire is gone out of a rod of her branches, which hath devoured her fruit, so that she hath no strong rod to be a sceptre to rule. This is a lamentation, and shall be for a lamentation.  

15 And fire is gone out of a rod of her branches, which hath devoured her fruit, so that she hath no strong rod to be a sceptre to rule. This is a lamentation, and shall be for a lamentation.  

16 And fire is gone out of a rod of her branches, which hath devoured her fruit, so that she hath no strong rod to be a sceptre to rule. This is a lamentation, and shall be for a lamentation.  

17 And fire is gone out of a rod of her branches, which hath devoured her fruit, so that she hath no strong rod to be a sceptre to rule. This is a lamentation, and shall be for a lamentation.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "What is thy mother? A lioness: she lay down among lions, she nourished her whelps among young lions"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any animal family
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's majesty - Lioness represents God's people as royal, not just any wildlife.

### "Thy mother is like a vine in thy blood, planted by the waters: she was fruitful and full of branches by reason of many waters"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any agriculture
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's people - Vine represents Judah as God's planting, not just any plant.
