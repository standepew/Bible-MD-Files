## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### RESURRECTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine rising, Christ's victory"
- **Context:** The resurrection of the dead to everlasting life or shame
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's resurrection and the final judgment
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual resurrection (John 5:28)

### EVERLASTING
- **Meaning:** "Divine eternity, Christ's permanence"
- **Context:** The everlasting life given to the righteous and contempt to the wicked
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's eternal life and eternal punishment
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual everlasting (1 Thessalonians 4:16)

### TROUBLE
- **Meaning:** "Divine testing, Christ's tribulation"
- **Context:** The time of trouble that precedes the deliverance of God's people
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's tribulation and the testing of believers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual trouble (Revelation 12:7)

### BOOK
- **Meaning:** "Divine record, Christ's judgment"
- **Context:** The book containing the names of those who will be delivered
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's judgment and the book of life
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual book (Revelation 10:2)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 And at that time shall Michael stand up, the great prince which standeth for the children of thy people: and there shall be a time of trouble, such as never was since there was a nation even to that same time: and at that time thy people shall be delivered, every one that shall be found written in the book.  

2 And many of them that sleep in the dust of the earth shall awake, some to everlasting life, and some to shame and everlasting contempt.  

3 And they that be wise shall shine as the brightness of the firmament; and they that turn many to righteousness as the stars for ever and ever.  

4 But thou, O Daniel, shut up the words, and seal the book, even to the time of the end: many shall run to and fro, and knowledge shall be increased.  

5 Then I Daniel looked, and, behold, there stood other two, the one on this side of the bank of the river, and the other on that side of the bank of the river.  

6 And one said to the man clothed in linen, which was upon the waters of the river, How long shall it be to the end of these wonders?  

7 And I heard the man clothed in linen, which was upon the waters of the river, when he held up his right hand and his left hand unto heaven, and sware by him that liveth for ever that it shall be for a time, times, and an half; and when he shall have accomplished to scatter the power of the holy people, all these things shall be finished.  

8 And I heard, but I understood not: then said I, O my Lord, what shall be the end of these things?  

9 And he said, Go thy way, Daniel: for the words are closed up and sealed till the time of the end.  

10 Many shall be purified, and made white, and tried; but the wicked shall do wickedly: and none of the wicked shall understand; but the wise shall understand.  

11 And from the time that the daily sacrifice shall be taken away, and the abomination that maketh desolate set up, there shall be a thousand two hundred and ninety days.  

12 Blessed is he that waiteth, and cometh to the thousand three hundred and five and thirty days.  

13 But go thou thy way till the end be: for thou shalt rest, and stand in thy lot at the end of the days.  

14 But go thou thy way till the end be: for thou shalt rest, and shall stand in thy lot at the end of the days.  

15 But go thou thy way till the end be: for thou shalt rest, and shall stand in thy lot at the end of the days.  

16 But go thou thy way till the end be: for thou shalt rest, and shall stand in thy lot at the end of the days.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "And many of them that sleep in the dust of the earth shall awake, some to everlasting life, and some to shame and everlasting contempt"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any afterlife concept or spiritual awakening
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's resurrection - The awakening represents the literal resurrection and final judgment.

### "And they that be wise shall shine as the brightness of the firmament; and they that turn many to righteousness as the stars for ever and ever"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any intellectual achievement or moral goodness
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's eternity - The shining represents the eternal glory of the righteous in Christ's kingdom.
