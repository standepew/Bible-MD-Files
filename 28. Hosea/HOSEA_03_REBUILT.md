## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### REDEMPTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine purchase, Christ's salvation"
- **Context:** Hosea's purchase of Gomer representing Christ's redemption price
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's redemptive purchase of His people
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual redemption (1 Corinthians 6:20)

### FAITHFUL
- **Meaning:** "Divine loyalty, Christ's constancy"
- **Context:** Hosea's faithful love despite Gomer's unfaithfulness
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's faithful love toward His people
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual faithful (Jeremiah 3:1)

### EXILE
- **Meaning:** "Divine judgment, Christ's discipline"
- **Context:** Israel's period without king, prince, or religious system
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's discipline and preparation for restoration
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual exile (Acts 3:21)

### KINGDOM
- **Meaning:** "Divine reign, Christ's rule"
- **Context:** The future Davidic kingdom and restoration under Christ
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's millennial kingdom and eternal reign
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual kingdom (Ezekiel 37:24)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "So I bought her to me for fifteen pieces of silver, and for an homer of barley, and an half homer of barley"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any financial transaction or purchase
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's redemption - The purchase price represents Christ's redemptive work on the cross.

### "For the children of Israel shall abide many days without a king, and without a prince, and without a sacrifice"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any period of political instability or social disorder
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's discipline - The period without religious systems represents the exile and preparation for Christ's coming.
