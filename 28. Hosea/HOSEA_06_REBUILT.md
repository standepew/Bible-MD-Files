## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### HEALING
- **Meaning:** "Divine restoration, Christ's salvation"
- **Context:** God's healing and binding up of His people after tearing and smiting
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's healing and restoration through His suffering
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual healing (1 Peter 2:24)

### RESURRECTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine rising, Christ's victory"
- **Context:** The third day resurrection and raising up to live in God's sight
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's resurrection and victory over death
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual resurrection (1 Corinthians 15:4)

### MERCY
- **Meaning:** "Divine compassion, Christ's forgiveness"
- **Context:** God's desire for mercy over sacrifice and knowledge over burnt offerings
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's mercy and forgiveness over ritual observance
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual mercy (Matthew 9:13)

### COVENANT
- **Meaning:** "Divine agreement, Christ's relationship"
- **Context:** The covenant transgression and treacherous dealing against God
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's covenant and the importance of faithfulness
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual covenant (Hebrews 8:6)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 Come, and let us return unto the LORD: for he hath torn, and he will heal us; he hath smitten, and he will bind us up.  

2 After two days will he revive us: in the third day he will raise us up, and we shall live in his sight.  

3 Then shall we know, if we follow on to know the LORD: his going forth is prepared as the morning; and he shall come unto us as the rain, as the latter and former rain unto the earth.  

4 O Ephraim, what shall I do unto thee? O Judah, what shall I do unto thee? for your goodness is as a morning cloud, and as the early dew it goeth away.  

5 Therefore have I hewed them by the prophets; I have slain them by the words of my mouth: and thy judgments are as the light that goeth forth.  

6 For I desired mercy, and not sacrifice; and the knowledge of God more than burnt offerings.  

7 But they like men have transgressed the covenant: there have they dealt treacherously against me.  

8 Gilead is a city of them that work iniquity, and is polluted with blood.  

9 And as troops of robbers wait for a man, so the company of priests murder in the way by consent: for they commit lewdness.  

10 I have seen an horrible thing in the house of Israel: there is the whoredom of Ephraim, Israel is defiled.  

11 Also, O Judah, he hath set an harvest for thee, when I returned the captivity of my people.  

12 Also, O Judah, he hath set an harvest for thee, when I returned the captivity of my people.  

13 Also, O Judah, he hath set an harvest for thee, when I returned the captivity of my people.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Come, and let us return unto the LORD: for he hath torn, and he will heal us; he hath smitten, and he will bind us up"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any physical healing or recovery from illness
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's salvation - The tearing and smiting represent Christ's suffering, and healing represents salvation.

### "After two days will he revive us: in the third day he will raise us up, and we shall live in his sight"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any recovery or revival experience
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's resurrection - The third day clearly points to Christ's resurrection and our resurrection life.
