## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### CONTROVERSY
- **Meaning:** "Divine dispute, Christ's judgment"
- **Context:** God's controversy with Judah and Jacob for their ways
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's judgment against sin and call to repentance
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual controversy (Jeremiah 2:18)

### WRESTLING
- **Meaning:** "Divine struggle, Christ's victory"
- **Context:** Jacob's wrestling with God and prevailing through supplication
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's victory through prayer and persistence
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual wrestling (Genesis 32:25)

### PROPHET
- **Meaning:** "Divine messenger, Christ's witness"
- **Context:** The prophetic ministry that brought Israel out of Egypt and preserved them
- **Significance:** Model of Christ as the ultimate prophet and preserver
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual prophet (Acts 3:22)

### PRESERVATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine protection, Christ's care"
- **Context:** God's preservation of Israel through prophetic ministry
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's protection and care for His people
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual preservation (Acts 7:37)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 Ephraim feedeth on wind, and followeth after the east wind: he daily increaseth lies and desolation; and they do make a covenant with the Assyrians, and oil is carried into Egypt.  

2 The LORD hath also a controversy with Judah, and will punish Jacob according to his ways; according to his doings will he recompense him.  

3 He took his brother by the heel in the womb, and by his strength he had power with God:  

4 Yea, he had power over the angel, and prevailed: he wept, and made supplication unto him: he found him in Beth-el, and there he spake with us;  

5 Even the LORD God of hosts; the LORD is his memorial.  

6 Therefore turn thou to thy God: keep mercy and judgment, and wait on thy God continually.  

7 He is a merchant, the balances of deceit are in his hand: he loveth to oppress.  

8 And Ephraim said, Yet I am become rich, I have found me out substance: in all my labours they shall find none iniquity in me that were sin.  

9 And I that am the LORD thy God from the land of Egypt will yet make thee to dwell in tabernacles, as in the days of the solemn feast.  

10 I have also spoken by the prophets, and I have multiplied visions, and used similitudes, by the ministry of the prophets.  

11 Is there iniquity in Gilead? surely they are vanity: they sacrifice bullocks in Gilgal; yea, their altars are as heaps in the furrows of the fields.  

12 And Jacob fled into the country of Syria, and Israel served for a wife, and for a wife he kept sheep.  

13 And by a prophet the LORD brought Israel out of Egypt, and by a prophet was he preserved.  

14 Ephraim provoked him to anger most bitterly: therefore shall he leave his blood upon him, and his reproach shall his Lord return unto him.  

15 Ephraim provoked him to anger most bitterly: therefore shall he leave his blood upon him, and his reproach shall his Lord return unto him.

**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's victory - The wrestling represents spiritual struggle and victory through prayer.

**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any moral advice or ethical teaching
