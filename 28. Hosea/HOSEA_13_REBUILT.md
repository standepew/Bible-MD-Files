## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### SOVEREIGNTY
- **Meaning:** "Divine rule, Christ's lordship"
- **Context:** God's declaration as the only true God and Savior from Egypt
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's exclusive lordship and salvation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sovereignty (Isaiah 43:11)

### JUDGMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine justice, Christ's wrath"
- **Context:** God's judgment through transience and destruction like chaff and smoke
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's judgment against idolatry and pride
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual judgment (James 4:14)

### DEATH
- **Meaning:** "Divine victory, Christ's resurrection"
- **Context:** God's victory over death and the grave through ransom and redemption
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's victory over death through His sacrifice
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual death (1 Corinthians 15:54)

### DESTRUCTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine end, Christ's return"
- **Context:** The final destruction of Samaria and Israel due to rebellion
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's final judgment and return
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual destruction (2 Kings 17:6)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Yet I am the LORD thy God from the land of Egypt, and thou shalt know no god but me: for there is no saviour beside me"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any religious exclusivity or intolerance
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's lordship - The exclusive claim represents Christ's unique role as Savior and Lord.

### "I will ransom them from the power of the grave; I will redeem them from death: O death, I will be thy plagues; O grave, I will be thy destruction"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any escape from danger or threat
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's victory - The ransom and redemption represent Christ's victory over death and the grave.
