## CHAPTER SUMMARY

God addresses trespass against a neighbor: fraud with deposits, theft, extortion, finding a lost item and lying about it, false oaths — all require full restitution of the principal plus 20% on the day of bringing the trespass offering. Then the priestly laws: the burnt offering burns all night; in the morning the priest dresses in full linen, removes the ashes to a clean place outside the camp, then keeps the fire burning with fresh wood and the fat of the peace offerings. "The fire shall ever be burning upon the altar; it shall never go out" — stated three times. The grain offering: Aaron and his sons eat the remainder in the court without leaven. The high priest's daily grain offering: a tenth of an ephah, half morning half evening, wholly burned — the priest's own offering is never eaten by him. The sin offering: eaten in the holy place by the officiating priest; whatever touches its flesh becomes holy; blood sprinkled on a garment must be washed in the holy place; the earthen vessel it was cooked in must be broken; the bronze pot must be scoured. No sin offering whose blood was brought inside for reconciliation may be eaten — it is burned.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### Restitution Before the Altar — Horizontal Before Vertical

**Leviticus 6:4-5** — "he shall restore that which he took violently away... he shall even restore it in the principal, and shall add the fifth part more thereto... And he shall bring his trespass offering unto the LORD"

- Restitution to the wronged person must happen before the offering is presented
- **Matthew 5:23-24** — "Therefore if thou bring thy gift to the altar, and there rememberest that thy brother hath ought against thee; Leave there thy gift before the altar, and go thy way; first be reconciled to thy brother, and then come and offer thy gift" — Jesus cites the exact Levitical principle
- **Luke 19:8** — Zacchaeus: "if I have taken any thing from any man by false accusation, I restore him fourfold" — the trespass offering principle in action under the New Covenant
- *Interpretation:* Leviticus 6 establishes that God will not receive worship from a person who has wronged another and done nothing to address it. The trespass offering is invalid without prior restitution. Jesus in Matthew 5:23-24 does not innovate — He cites this principle as the governing standard. Vertical reconciliation with God requires prior horizontal restitution to the neighbor. This is not works-righteousness; it is the demonstration that repentance is genuine — proven by the willingness to restore what sin took.*

### The Perpetual Fire — "It Shall Never Go Out"

**Leviticus 6:12-13** — "And the fire upon the altar shall be burning in it; it shall not be put out: and the priest shall burn wood on it every morning, and lay the burnt offering in order upon it... The fire shall ever be burning upon the altar; it shall never go out"

- Stated three times: vv.9, 12, 13 — the threefold emphasis demands theological attention
- The fire was originally kindled by God Himself: **Leviticus 9:24** — "And there came a fire out from before the LORD, and consumed upon the altar the burnt offering and the fat: which when all the people saw, they shouted, and fell on their faces"
- **Numbers 3:4; Leviticus 10:1** — Nadab and Abihu brought "strange fire" (foreign fire, not from the altar) — they died; preserving the divine fire matters
- **Hebrews 7:25** — "he ever liveth to make intercession for them" — the New Covenant perpetual fire; Christ's intercession never ceases
- **Hebrews 13:8** — "Jesus Christ the same yesterday, and to day, and for ever"
- *Interpretation:* The fire that could never go out on the altar was God's own fire, maintained by priestly faithfulness. Every morning the priest added wood; every day the fire burned. The perpetual fire declares that the access to God through sacrifice is never interrupted — there is no day when the altar goes cold. Hebrews 7:25 is the New Covenant fulfillment: Christ lives forever to intercede, meaning the New Covenant altar fire never goes cold either. The intercession of the eternal High Priest is the perpetual fire of the New Covenant.*

### The High Priest's Own Daily Offering — Wholly Burned

**Leviticus 6:20-23** — "This is the offering of Aaron and of his sons, which they shall offer unto the LORD in the day when he is anointed; the tenth part of an ephah of fine flour for a meat offering perpetual, half of it in the morning, and half thereof at night... it shall be wholly burnt: it shall not be eaten. For every meat offering for the priest shall be wholly burnt"

- The priest who mediates for others must himself bring a daily offering
- His offering — unlike the people's — is wholly burned; no portion eaten by him
- **Hebrews 7:26-27** — "For such an high priest became us, who is holy, harmless, undefiled, separate from sinners... who needeth not daily, as those high priests, to offer up sacrifice, first for his own sins, and then for the people's: for this he did once, when he offered up himself"
- *Interpretation:* The Aaronic high priest needed his own daily offering because he was a sinner. The priest's grain offering, wholly burned (not eaten back by him), represents the full surrender of the mediator — he cannot retain what he offers as his own. Hebrews 7:27 identifies this: Jesus, unlike Aaron, had no sin of His own requiring daily sacrifice. His one offering was complete, unrepeatable, and wholly given — He did not "take back" what He offered. Christ's wholly-burned offering is His entire self, given once, fully consumed for us.*

### The Broken Earthen Vessel — What Absorbs Sin Cannot Be Reused

**Leviticus 6:28** — "But the earthen vessel wherein it is sodden shall be broken: and if it be sodden in a brasen pot, it shall be both scoured, and rinsed in water"

- The clay pot that cooked the sin offering absorbed the holiness/sin-bearing quality of the flesh and could not be returned to ordinary use — it must be broken
- The bronze pot can be thoroughly cleaned and reused; clay is porous and cannot be fully purged
- **2 Corinthians 4:7** — "But we have this treasure in earthen vessels, that the excellency of the power may be of God, and not of us" — believers as clay vessels carrying the holiness of Christ
- *Interpretation:* The clay vessel that held the sin offering flesh becomes so saturated with what it carried that it cannot serve dual purposes — ordinary and holy. It is broken, not defiled. The principle: what has been the vessel of atonement cannot be casually returned to common use. 2 Corinthians 4:7 uses the same image — the believer as an earthen vessel containing the treasure of the gospel — suggesting that the vessel that carries Christ cannot be simultaneously devoted to common purposes without breakage.*

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### PERPETUAL FIRE (Hebrew: esh tamid)
- **Leviticus 6:13** — esh = fire; tamid = continual, perpetual, regular, without interruption
- Same word as "tamid" = the daily/continual offering (Exodus 29:38-42); the altar fire = tamid; the offering = tamid; both are perpetual, uninterrupted
- The tamid offering's cessation is the defining act of the antichrist in Daniel 8:11; 9:27; 11:31; 12:11 — stopping what is perpetual
- *Definition here:* The unceasing fire of divine acceptance burning on the altar; the continuous availability of atonement; in Christ — the unceasing intercession of the eternal High Priest (Hebrews 7:25); the tamid whose cessation will signal the final desecration (Daniel 12:11)*

### MOST HOLY (Hebrew: qodesh qodashim)
- **Leviticus 6:17, 25, 29** — the sin offering, the grain offering, and the trespass offering are all designated "most holy" when eaten by priests
- Qodesh qodashim = holy of holies; the superlative degree of holiness; only the Most Holy Place and these offerings carry this designation
- *Definition here:* The highest category of holiness in the Levitical system; what is most holy cannot be treated as ordinary; contact with it either sanctifies or requires extraordinary handling; the offerings that atone for sin are the most holy things in the system — the irony that what touches sin is the holiest thing in Israel*

**1** And the LORD spake unto Moses, saying,

**21** In a pan it shall be made with oil; and when it is baken, thou shalt bring it in: and the baken pieces of the meat offering shalt thou offer for a sweet savour unto the LORD.
**22** And the priest of his sons that is anointed in his stead shall offer it: it is a statute for ever unto the LORD; it shall be wholly burnt.
**23** For every meat offering for the priest shall be wholly burnt: it shall not be eaten.
**24** And the LORD spake unto Moses, saying,
**25** Speak unto Aaron and to his sons, saying, This is the law of the sin offering: In the place where the burnt offering is killed shall the sin offering be killed before the LORD: it is most holy.
**26** The priest that offereth it for sin shall eat it: in the holy place shall it be eaten, in the court of the tabernacle of the congregation.
**27** Whatsoever shall touch the flesh thereof shall be holy: and when there is sprinkled of the blood thereof upon any garment, thou shalt wash that whereon it was sprinkled in the holy place.

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### Daniel's "Tamid" and the Perpetual Fire

**Leviticus 6:13** — "The fire shall ever be burning upon the altar; it shall never go out" — the tamid fire

**Daniel 8:11; 11:31; 12:11** — The "daily sacrifice" (tamid) is taken away by the antichrist as the defining act of the abomination of desolation

- The tamid fire of Leviticus 6 = the continual access to God through sacrifice
- The cessation of the tamid in Daniel = the interruption of that access; the abomination that replaces it
- **Matthew 24:15** — "When ye therefore shall see the abomination of desolation, spoken of by Daniel the prophet, stand in the holy place" — Jesus identifies Daniel's tamid-cessation as the eschatological sign
- *The perpetual fire of Leviticus 6 is not merely a procedural requirement — it is a prophetic marker. When the fire goes out — when the tamid ceases — the great tribulation has begun. The New Covenant answer: Hebrews 7:25 — "he ever liveth to make intercession." Christ is the tamid that cannot be extinguished.*

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### Matthew 5:23-24 — "Leave Your Gift Before the Altar" — Misread as General Advice About Relationships

**The misuse:** Jesus' instruction — "if thou bring thy gift to the altar, and there rememberest that thy brother hath ought against thee; Leave there thy gift before the altar, and go thy way; first be reconciled to thy brother, and then come and offer thy gift" — is often cited as general relational advice: resolve conflict before worshipping. True as far as it goes, but the weight of the command is missed.

**What the text is actually doing:** Jesus is citing Leviticus 6:1-7 precisely. The trespass offering for defrauding a neighbor was INVALID until restitution was made first. Jesus is not inventing a new principle — He is applying the Leviticus 6 law directly: the offering is refused until the wrong is righted. The altar is not a place to feel religious while harboring unresolved injustice against another person.

**The correction from within Scripture:**
- **Leviticus 6:4-5** — restitution of principal plus a fifth BEFORE the guilt offering — this is the law Jesus is citing
- **Amos 5:21-24** — "I hate, I despise your feast days... though ye offer me burnt offerings... Take thou away from me the noise of thy songs... But let judgment run down as waters, and righteousness as a mighty stream" — God rejects worship offered while injustice is maintained
- **Isaiah 1:13-17** — "Bring no more vain oblations... learn to do well; seek judgment, relieve the oppressed" — the same principle
- **1 John 4:20** — "for he that loveth not his brother whom he hath seen, how can he love God whom he hath not seen?" — the relational requirement is woven into the fabric of acceptable worship

---

### Leviticus 6:13 — "The Fire Shall Never Go Out" — Misapplied to Eternal Hellfire Arguments

**The misuse:** "The fire shall ever be burning upon the altar; it shall never go out" is occasionally cited in arguments about eternal conscious torment — as if perpetual fire here supports unending punitive fire.

**What the text actually says:** The fire of Leviticus 6:13 is the ALTAR FIRE — the fire of divine acceptance kept perpetually burning so that offerings can be received at any moment. It is the fire of God's ongoing presence and accessibility, not punitive fire.

**The correction from within Scripture:**
- **Leviticus 9:24** — the original fire came from before the LORD (divine origin); it is the fire of acceptance
- **Isaiah 33:14** — "who among us shall dwell with the devouring fire? who among us shall dwell with everlasting burnings?" — answered in vv.15-16: "He that walketh righteously..." — even the fire of God's holiness, framed as perpetual, is about who can dwell WITH God, not about punitive duration
- Every use of "eternal/everlasting fire" in Scripture requires contextual examination per the roadmap's instruction on *olam* (age-lasting, until completion, not necessarily endless)

## SYMBOLIC THREADS

### The Perpetual Fire — A Thread Through All of Scripture

**Leviticus 6:13** — "The fire shall ever be burning upon the altar; it shall never go out"

The thread of the never-extinguished fire:
- **Leviticus 9:24** — the fire's divine origin: it came from before the LORD
- **1 Kings 18:38** — Elijah on Carmel: the fire of the LORD falls and consumes — the same divine fire as a sign of acceptance
- **2 Chronicles 7:1** — fire from heaven consumes Solomon's burnt offering at the Temple dedication
- **Isaiah 31:9** — "the LORD, whose fire is in Zion, and his furnace in Jerusalem" — the fire of God's presence identified with His dwelling place
- **Daniel 7:9-10** — "his throne was like the fiery flame... a fiery stream issued and came forth from before him" — the heavenly altar fire behind the earthly one
- **Hebrews 7:25** — "he ever liveth to make intercession" — the New Covenant tamid; the perpetual fire of Christ's intercessory priesthood that cannot go out
- **Revelation 8:5** — "the angel took the censer, and filled it with fire of the altar" — the altar fire still present before the throne in the eschaton

**What the Bible establishes:** The perpetual altar fire is the emblem of perpetual access to God through sacrifice. It cannot go out because God's acceptance of the atoning work is permanent. The New Covenant counterpart is Christ's ever-living intercession (Hebrews 7:25) — the fire of His finished work burns perpetually before the Father on behalf of those He redeemed.

### The Linen Garments — Humility and Righteousness at the Altar

**Leviticus 6:10** — "And the priest shall put on his linen garment, and his linen breeches shall he put upon his flesh, and take up the ashes which the fire hath consumed"

The linen thread at the altar:
- **Leviticus 16:4** — Aaron wears linen (not the glorious vestments) on the Day of Atonement when he enters the Most Holy Place — his simplest garments for his most holy act
- **Ezekiel 9:2-3, 11** — the man clothed in linen among the executors of judgment — linen as the marker of the holy
- **Revelation 15:6** — "clothed in pure and white linen" — the angels of the final plagues wear linen
- **Revelation 19:8** — "the fine linen is the righteousness of saints" — linen = righteousness defined by Scripture
- **Revelation 19:14** — "clothed in fine linen, white and clean" — the armies of heaven

**What the Bible establishes (Revelation 1:20 principle — symbols defined by Scripture):** Linen = the righteousness that is clean, white, and unadorned. When the priest wore linen to clean the altar, he was doing the humblest, most physical work of the sanctuary in the garment of righteousness. The greatest act (the Day of Atonement) and the most menial act (removing ashes) both required linen. Righteousness is required for every level of service before God, not only the spectacular.
