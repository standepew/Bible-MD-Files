## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### JUDGMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine justice, Christ's wrath"
- **Context:** God's judgment against Israel for social injustice and breaking covenant
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's judgment against those who oppress the poor
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual judgment (Matthew 15:3)

### INJUSTICE
- **Meaning:** "Divine oppression, Christ's suffering"
- **Context:** Israel's social injustice in selling the righteous and poor for gain
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's suffering for the oppressed and His opposition to injustice
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual injustice (James 5:4)

### PRIVILEGE
- **Meaning:** "Divine blessing, Christ's grace"
- **Context:** Israel's privileged position that they abused through disobedience
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's grace and the responsibility that comes with privilege
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual privilege (John 7:19)

### NAZARITE
- **Meaning:** "Divine consecration, Christ's holiness"
- **Context:** The Nazarite vow of separation that Israel corrupted and violated
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's holiness and call to consecration
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual Nazarite (Numbers 6:2)

1 Thus saith the LORD; For three transgressions of Moab, and for four, I will not turn away the punishment thereof; because he burned the bones of the king of Edom into lime:  

2 But I will send a fire upon Moab, and it shall devour the palaces of Kerioth: and Moab shall die with tumult, with shouting, and with the sound of the trumpet:  

3 And I will cut off the judge from the midst thereof, and will slay all the princes thereof with him, saith the LORD.  

4 Thus saith the LORD; For three transgressions of Judah, and for four, I will not turn away the punishment thereof; because they have despised the law of the LORD, and have not kept his commandments, and their lies caused them to err, after the which their fathers have walked:  

5 But I will send a fire upon Judah, and it shall devour the palaces of Jerusalem.  

6 Thus saith the LORD; For three transgressions of Israel, and for four, I will not turn away the punishment thereof; because they sold the righteous for silver, and the poor for a pair of shoes;  

7 That pant after the dust of the earth on the head of the poor, and turn aside the way of the meek: and a man and his father will go in unto the same maid, to profane my holy name:  

8 And they lay themselves down upon clothes laid to pledge by every altar, and they drink the wine of the condemned in the house of their god.  

9 Yet destroyed I the Amorite before them, whose height was like the height of the cedars, and he was strong as the oaks; yet I destroyed his fruit from above, and his roots from beneath.  

10 Also I brought you up from the land of Egypt, and led you forty years through the wilderness, to possess the land of the Amorite.  

11 And I raised up of your sons for prophets, and of your young men for Nazarites. Is it not even thus, O ye children of Israel? saith the LORD.  

12 But ye gave the Nazarites wine to drink; and commanded the prophets, saying, Prophesy not.  

13 Behold, I am pressed under you, as a cart is pressed that is full of sheaves.  

14 Therefore the flight shall perish from the swift, and the strong shall not strengthen his force, neither shall the mighty deliver himself:  

15 Neither shall he stand that handleth the bow; and he that is swift of foot shall not deliver himself: neither shall he that rideth the horse deliver himself.  

16 And he that is courageous among the mighty shall flee away naked in that day, saith the LORD.  

17 And he that is courageous among the mighty shall flee away naked in that day, saith the LORD.  

18 And he that is courageous among the mighty shall flee away naked in that day, saith the LORD.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Thus saith the LORD; For three transgressions of Israel, and for four, I will not turn away the punishment thereof; because they sold the righteous for silver, and the poor for a pair of shoes"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any economic transaction or business dealing
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's justice - The selling represents social injustice and exploitation of the poor.

### "And I raised up of your sons for prophets, and of your young men for Nazarites. Is it not even thus, O ye children of Israel? saith the LORD"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any leadership development or religious training
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's holiness - The prophets and Nazarites represent God's call to holy living that Israel rejected.
