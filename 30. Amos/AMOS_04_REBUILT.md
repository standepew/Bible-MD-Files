## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### REPENTANCE
- **Meaning:** "Divine restoration, Christ's forgiveness"
- **Context:** God's repeated calls for Israel to return to Him despite their refusal
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's persistent call to repentance and restoration
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual repentance (Matthew 5:6)

### DISCIPLINE
- **Meaning:** "Divine correction, Christ's training"
- **Context:** God's progressive discipline through famine, drought, pestilence, and destruction
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's corrective discipline to bring repentance
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual discipline (Deuteronomy 28:48)

### CREATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine power, Christ's authority"
- **Context:** God's sovereign power over creation as reason to prepare to meet Him
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's authority as Creator and Lord
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual creation (Colossians 1:16)

### PREPARATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine readiness, Christ's return"
- **Context:** The call to prepare to meet God who controls all creation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's return and the need to be prepared
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual preparation (Job 38:4)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 Hear this word, ye kine of Bashan, that are in the mountain of Samaria, which oppress the poor, which crush the needy, which say to their masters, Bring, and let us drink.  

2 The Lord GOD hath sworn by his holiness, that, lo, the days shall come upon you, that he will take you away with hooks, and your posterity with fishhooks.  

3 And ye shall go out at the breaches, every cow at that which is before her; and ye shall cast them into the palace, saith the LORD.  

4 Come to Beth-el, and transgress; at Gilgal multiply transgression; and bring your sacrifices every morning, and your tithes after three years:  

5 And offer a sacrifice of thanksgiving with leaven, and proclaim and publish the free offerings: for this liketh you, O ye children of Israel, saith the Lord GOD.  

6 And I also have given you cleanness of teeth in all your cities, and want of bread in all your places: yet have ye not returned unto me, saith the LORD.  

7 And also I have withholden the rain from you, when there were yet three months to the harvest: and I caused it to rain upon one city, and caused it not to rain upon another city: one piece was rained upon, and the piece whereupon it rained not withered.  

8 So two or three cities wandered unto one city, to drink water; but they were not satisfied: yet have ye not returned unto me, saith the LORD.  

9 I have smitten you with blasting and mildew: when your gardens and your vineyards and your fig trees and your olive trees increased, the palmerworm devoured them: yet have ye not returned unto me, saith the LORD.  

10 I have sent among you the pestilence after the manner of Egypt: your young men have I slain with the sword, and have taken away your horses; and I have made the stink of your camps to come up unto your nostrils: yet have ye not returned unto me, saith the LORD.  

11 I have overthrown some of you, as God overthrew Sodom and Gomorrah, and ye were as a firebrand plucked out of the burning: yet have ye not returned unto me, saith the LORD.  

12 Therefore thus will I do unto thee, O Israel: and because I will do this unto thee, prepare to meet thy God, O Israel.  

13 For, lo, he that formeth the mountains, and createth the wind, and declareth unto man what is his thought, that maketh the morning darkness, and treadeth upon the high places of the earth, The LORD, The God of hosts, is his name.  

14 For, lo, he that formeth the mountains, and createth the wind, and declareth unto man what is his thought, that maketh the morning darkness, and treadeth upon the high places of the earth, The LORD, The God of hosts, is his name.  

15 For, lo, he that formeth the mountains, and createth the wind, and declareth unto man what is his thought, that maketh the morning darkness, and treadeth upon the high places of the earth, The LORD, The God of hosts, is his name.  

16 For, lo, he that formeth the mountains, and createth the wind, and declareth unto man what is his thought, that maketh the morning darkness, and treadeth upon the high places of the earth, The LORD, The God of hosts, is his name.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Hear this word, ye kine of Bashan, that are in the mountain of Samaria, which oppress the poor, which crush the needy"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any agricultural or livestock management
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's call to repentance - The kine represent the wealthy oppressors who crush the poor.

### "For, lo, he that formeth the mountains, and createth the wind, and declareth unto man what is his thought"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any natural phenomenon or weather pattern
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's authority - The creation represents God's sovereign power and reason to prepare to meet Him.
