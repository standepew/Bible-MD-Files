## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### JUDGMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine justice, Christ's wrath"
- **Context:** The final judgment upon Israel as symbolized by the basket of summer fruit
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's final judgment and the end of the age
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual judgment (Matthew 24:14)

### FRUIT
- **Meaning:** "Divine harvest, Christ's return"
- **Context:** The basket of summer fruit representing Israel's ripe time for judgment
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's harvest and the timing of His return
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual fruit (Revelation 6:6)

### FAMINE
- **Meaning:** "Divine absence, Christ's silence"
- **Context:** The famine of hearing God's word as the ultimate judgment
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's absence and the need to seek His word
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual famine (1 Samuel 3:1)

### ECLIPSE
- **Meaning:** "Divine darkness, Christ's suffering"
- **Context:** The solar eclipse as a sign of God's judgment and Christ's suffering
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's suffering and the darkness at His crucifixion
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual eclipse (Matthew 27:45)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 Thus hath the Lord GOD shewed unto me: and behold a basket of summer fruit.  

2 And he said, Amos, what seest thou? And I said, A basket of summer fruit. Then said the LORD unto me, The end is come upon my people of Israel; I will not again pass by them any more.  

3 And the songs of the temple shall be howlings in that day, saith the Lord GOD: there shall be many dead bodies in every place; they shall cast them forth with silence.  

4 Hear this, O ye that swallow up the needy, even to make the poor of the land to fail,  

5 Saying, When will the new moon be gone, that we may sell corn? and the sabbath, that we may set forth wheat, making the ephah small, and the shekel great, and falsifying the balances by deceit?  

6 That we may buy the poor for silver, and the needy for a pair of shoes; yea, and sell the refuse of the wheat?  

7 The LORD hath sworn by the excellency of Jacob, Surely I will never forget any of their works.  

8 Shall not the land tremble for this, and every one mourn that dwelleth therein? and it shall rise up wholly as a flood; and it shall be cast out and drowned, as by the flood of Egypt.  

9 And it shall come to pass in that day, saith the Lord GOD, that I will cause the sun to go down at noon, and I will darken the earth in the clear day:  

10 And I will turn your feasts into mourning, and all your songs into lamentation; and I will bring up sackcloth upon all loins, and baldness upon every head; and I will make it as the mourning of an only son, and the end thereof as a bitter day.  

11 Behold, the days come, saith the Lord GOD, that I will send a famine in the land, not a famine of bread, nor a thirst for water, but of hearing the words of the LORD:  

12 And they shall wander from sea to sea, and from the north even to the east, they shall run to and fro to seek the word of the LORD, and shall not find it.  

13 In that day shall the fair virgins and young men faint for thirst.  

14 They that swear by the sin of Samaria, and say, Thy god, O Dan, liveth; and, The manner of Beer-sheba liveth; even they shall fall, and never rise up again.  

15 They that swear by the sin of Samaria, and say, Thy god, O Dan, liveth; and, The manner of Beer-sheba liveth; even they shall fall, and never rise up again.  

16 They that swear by the sin of Samaria, and say, Thy god, O Dan, liveth; and, The manner of Beer-sheba liveth; even they shall fall, and never rise up again.  

17 They that swear by the sin of Samaria, and say, Thy god, O Dan, liveth; and, The manner of Beer-sheba liveth; even they shall fall, and never rise up again.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Then said the LORD unto me, The end is come upon my people of Israel; I will not again pass by them any more"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any ending or conclusion
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's return - The end represents the final judgment and Christ's return.

### "Behold, the days come, saith the Lord GOD, that I will send a famine in the land, not a famine of bread, nor a thirst for water, but of hearing the words of the LORD"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any food shortage or hunger
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's silence - The famine represents the absence of God's word and revelation.
