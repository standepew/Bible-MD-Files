## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### MISSION
- **Meaning:** "Divine calling, Christ's commission"
- **Context:** God's second call to Jonah to preach to Nineveh
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's persistent call to preach the gospel
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual mission (Mark 16:15)

### REPENTANCE
- **Meaning:** "Divine restoration, Christ's forgiveness"
- **Context:** Nineveh's repentance through fasting, sackcloth, and turning from evil
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's call to repentance and God's forgiveness
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual repentance (Acts 13:48)

### MERCY
- **Meaning:** "Divine compassion, Christ's grace"
- **Context:** God's mercy in relenting from judgment when Nineveh repented
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's mercy and grace toward the repentant
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual mercy (2 Peter 3:9)

### RELIEF
- **Meaning:** "Divine relief, Christ's deliverance"
- **Context:** God's relief from judgment through Nineveh's repentance
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's deliverance from judgment through repentance
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual relief (Romans 2:4)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 And the word of the LORD came unto Jonah the second time, saying,  

2 Arise, go unto Nineveh, that great city, and preach unto it the preaching that I bid thee.  

3 So Jonah arose, and went unto Nineveh, according to the word of the LORD. Now Nineveh was an exceeding great city of three days' journey.  

4 And Jonah began to enter into the city a day's journey, and he cried, and said, Yet forty days, and Nineveh shall be overthrown.  

5 So the people of Nineveh believed God, and proclaimed a fast, and put on sackcloth, from the greatest of them even to the least of them.  

6 For word came unto the king of Nineveh, and he arose from his throne, and laid his robe from him, and covered him with sackcloth, and sat in ashes.  

7 And he caused it to be proclaimed and published through Nineveh by the decree of the king and his nobles, saying, Let neither man nor beast, herd nor flock, taste any thing: let them not feed, nor drink water:  

8 But let man and beast be covered with sackcloth, and cry mightily unto God: yea, let them turn every one from his evil way, and from the violence that is in their hands.  

9 Who can tell if God will turn and repent, and turn away from his fierce anger, that we perish not?  

10 And God saw their works, that they turned from their evil way; and God repented of the evil, that he had said that he would do unto them; and he did it not.  

11 And God saw their works, that they turned from their evil way; and God repented of the evil, that he had said that he would do unto them; and he did it not.  

12 And God saw their works, that they turned from their evil way; and God repented of the evil, that he had said that he would do unto them; and he did it not.  

13 And God saw their works, that they turned from their evil way; and God repented of the evil, that he had said that he would do unto them; and he did it not.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "And the word of the LORD came unto Jonah the second time, saying, Arise, go unto Nineveh, that great city, and preach unto it the preaching that I bid thee"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any second chance or opportunity
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's persistent commission - The second call represents God's persistent call to preach the gospel and His mercy in giving second chances.

### "And God saw their works, that they turned from their evil way; and God repented of the evil, that he had said that he would do unto them; and he did it not"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any change of mind or decision
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's deliverance - The repenting represents God's mercy in relenting from judgment when people truly repent.
