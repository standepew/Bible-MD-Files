## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### COMPASSION
- **Meaning:** "Divine mercy, Christ's love"
- **Context:** God's compassion for Nineveh and His object lesson to Jonah about mercy
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's compassion for all people and His universal salvation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual compassion (Matthew 18:12)

### MERCY
- **Meaning:** "Divine grace, Christ's forgiveness"
- **Context:** God's mercy in sparing Nineveh despite their wickedness
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's mercy and grace toward the Gentiles
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual mercy (2 Peter 3:9)

### LESSON
- **Meaning:** "Divine teaching, Christ's instruction"
- **Context:** God's object lesson through the gourd, worm, and wind to teach Jonah
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's teaching through object lessons and parables
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual lesson (Matthew 6:28)

### SALVATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine deliverance, Christ's redemption"
- **Context:** God's universal salvation extending to Gentiles and even cattle
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's universal salvation and concern for all creation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual salvation (Philippians 1:23)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 But it displeased Jonah exceedingly, and he was very angry.  

2 And he prayed unto the LORD, and said, I pray thee, O LORD, was not this my saying, when I was yet in my country? Therefore I fled before unto Tarshish: for I knew that thou art a gracious God, and merciful, slow to anger, and of great kindness, and repentest thee of the evil.  

3 Therefore now, O LORD, take, I beseech thee, my life from me; for it is better for me to die than to live.  

4 Then said the LORD, Doest thou well to be angry?  

5 So Jonah went out of the city, and sat on the east side of the city, and there made him a booth, and sat under it in the shadow, till he might see what would become of the city.  

6 And the LORD God prepared a gourd, and made it to come up over Jonah, that it might be a shadow over his head, to deliver him from his grief. So Jonah was exceeding glad of the gourd.  

7 But God prepared a worm when the morning rose the next day, and it smote the gourd that it withered.  

8 And it came to pass, when the sun did arise, that God prepared a vehement east wind; and the sun beat upon the head of Jonah, that he fainted, and wished in himself to die, and said, It is better for me to die than to live.  

9 And God said to Jonah, Doest thou well to be angry for the gourd? And he said, I do well to be angry, even unto death.  

10 Then said the LORD, Thou hast had pity on the gourd, for the which thou hast not laboured, neither madest it grow; which came up in a night, and perished in a night:  

11 And should not I spare Nineveh, that great city, wherein are more than sixscore thousand persons that cannot discern between their right hand and their left hand; and also much cattle?  

12 And should not I spare Nineveh, that great city, wherein are more than sixscore thousand persons that cannot discern between their right hand and their left hand; and also much cattle?  

13 And should not I spare Nineveh, that great city, wherein are more than sixscore thousand persons that cannot discern between their right hand and their left hand; and also much cattle?  

14 And should not I spare Nineveh, that great city, wherein are more than sixscore thousand persons that cannot discern between their right hand and their left hand; and also much cattle?

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "But it displeased Jonah exceedingly, and he was very angry"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any anger or displeasure
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's love - The displeasure represents Jonah's struggle with God's mercy toward Gentiles and Christ's universal love.

### "And should not I spare Nineveh, that great city, wherein are more than sixscore thousand persons that cannot discern between their right hand and their left hand; and also much cattle?"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any city or population
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's universal salvation - The sparing represents God's mercy extending to all people, including those who cannot discern and even animals.
