## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### JUDGMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine justice, Christ's wrath"
- **Context:** God's coming judgment upon Samaria and Jerusalem for their sins
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's final judgment and His righteous wrath against sin
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual judgment (Matthew 24:27)

### WITNESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine testimony, Christ's truth"
- **Context:** God as witness against His people from His holy temple
- **Significance:** Model of Christ as the faithful witness and testimony of truth
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual witness (Hebrews 1:1)

### IDOLATRY
- **Meaning:** "Divine opposition, Christ's purity"
- **Context:** The destruction of graven images and idols as judgment for idolatry
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's opposition to idolatry and call to pure worship
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual idolatry (Acts 19:19)

### CAPTIVITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine discipline, Christ's correction"
- **Context:** The captivity of God's people as judgment for their sins
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's discipline and correction to bring repentance
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual captivity (Revelation 18:9)

8 Therefore I will wail and howl, I will go stripped and naked: I will make a wailing like the dragons, and mourning as the owls.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "For, behold, the LORD cometh forth out of his place, and will come down, and tread upon the high places of the earth"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any natural disaster or earthquake
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's return - The coming represents Christ's second coming and judgment upon the earth.

### "And all the graven images thereof shall be beaten to pieces, and all the hires thereof shall be burned with the fire"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any destruction of art or cultural objects
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's purity - The destruction represents Christ's judgment against idolatry and false worship.
