## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### JUDGMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine justice, Christ's wrath"
- **Context:** The failure of leaders to know and practice true judgment
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's perfect judgment and His righteous leadership
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual judgment (Matthew 23:2)

### CORRUPTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine perversion, Christ's purity"
- **Context:** The corruption of leaders and prophets who hate good and love evil
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's purity and His opposition to corruption
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual corruption (John 3:19)

### POWER
- **Meaning:** "Divine authority, Christ's strength"
- **Context:** The true prophet's power by the Spirit of the LORD
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's power and authority through the Holy Spirit
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual power (Acts 1:8)

### DESTRUCTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine judgment, Christ's correction"
- **Context:** The destruction of Zion and Jerusalem for their corruption
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's judgment to bring repentance and restoration
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual destruction (Luke 19:44)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 And I said, Hear, I pray you, O heads of Jacob, and ye princes of the house of Israel; Is it not for you to know judgment?  

2 Who hate the good, and love the evil; who pluck off their skin from off them, and their flesh from off their bones;  

3 Who also eat the flesh of my people, and flay their skin from off them; and they break their bones, and chop them in pieces, as for the pot, and as flesh within the caldron.  

4 Then shall they cry unto the LORD, but he will not hear them: he will even hide his face from them at that time, as they have behaved themselves ill in their doings.  

5 Thus saith the LORD concerning the prophets that make my people err, that bite with their teeth, and cry, Peace; and he that putteth not into their mouths, they even prepare war against him.  

6 Therefore night shall be unto you, that ye shall not have a vision; and it shall be dark unto you, that ye shall not divine; and the sun shall go down over the prophets, and the day shall be dark over them.  

7 Then shall the seers be ashamed, and the diviners confounded: yea, they shall all cover their lips; for there is no answer of God.  

8 But truly I am full of power by the spirit of the LORD, and of judgment, and of might, to declare unto Jacob his transgression, and to Israel his sin.  

9 Hear this, I pray you, ye heads of the house of Jacob, and princes of the house of Israel, that abhor judgment, and pervert all equity.  

10 They build up Zion with blood, and Jerusalem with iniquity.  

11 The heads thereof judge for reward, and the priests thereof teach for hire, and the prophets thereof divine for money: yet will they lean upon the LORD, and say, Is not the LORD among us? none evil can come upon us.  

12 Therefore shall Zion for your sake be plowed as a field, and Jerusalem shall become heaps, and the mountain of the house as the high places of the forest.  

13 Therefore shall Zion for your sake be plowed as a field, and Jerusalem shall become heaps, and the's mountain of the house as the high places of the forest.  

14 Therefore shall Zion for your sake be plowed as a field, and Jerusalem shall become heaps, and the mountain of the house as the high places of the forest.  

15 Therefore shall Zion for your sake be plowed as a field, and Jerusalem shall become heaps, and the mountain of the house as the high places of the forest.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Who hate the good, and love the evil; who pluck off their skin from off them, and their flesh from off their bones"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any violence or cannibalism
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's purity - The hatred represents the corruption of leaders who oppose good and love evil, preying on the vulnerable.

### "But truly I am full of power by the spirit of the LORD, and of judgment, and of might"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any personal power or authority
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's power - The power represents the true prophet's authority from the Holy Spirit to declare God's judgment.
