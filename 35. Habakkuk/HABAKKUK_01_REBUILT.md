## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### SUFFERING
- **Meaning:** "Divine trial, Christ's passion"
- **Context:** The prophet's cry over suffering and violence without divine intervention
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's suffering and His question of God's timing in justice
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual suffering (Revelation 6:10)

### JUSTICE
- **Meaning:** "Divine righteousness, Christ's judgment"
- **Context:** The apparent lack of justice as the wicked oppress the righteous
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's justice and His timing in divine judgment
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual justice (Luke 18:7)

### JUDGMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine wrath, Christ's correction"
- **Context:** God's raising of the Chaldeans as instruments of judgment
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's judgment and His use of nations for correction
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual judgment (Matthew 24:6)

### SOVEREIGNTY
- **Meaning:** "Divine rule, Christ's authority"
- **Context:** God's eternal nature and sovereign control over all events
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's sovereignty and His eternal authority over all
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sovereignty (Hebrews 13:8)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 The burden which Habakkuk the prophet did see.  

2 O LORD, how long shall I cry, and thou wilt not hear! even cry out unto thee of violence, and thou wilt not save!  

3 Why dost thou shew me iniquity, and cause me to behold grievance? for spoiling and violence are before me: and there are that raise up strife and contention.  

4 Therefore the law is slacked, and judgment doth never go forth: for the wicked doth compass about the righteous; therefore wrong judgment proceedeth.  

5 Behold ye among the heathen, and regard, and wonder marvellously: for I will work a work in your days, which ye will not believe, though it be told you.  

6 For, lo, I raise up the Chaldeans, that bitter and hasty nation, which shall march through the breadth of the land, to possess the dwellingplaces that are not theirs.  

7 They are terrible and dreadful: their judgment and their dignity shall proceed of themselves.  

8 Their horses also are swifter than the leopards, and are more fierce than the evening wolves: and their horsemen shall spread themselves, and their horsemen shall come from far; they shall fly as the eagle that hasteth to eat.  

9 They shall come all for violence: their faces shall sup up as the east wind, and they shall gather the captivity as the sand.  

10 And they shall scoff at the kings, and the princes shall be a scorn unto them: they shall deride every strong hold; for they shall heap dust, and take it.  

11 Then shall his mind change, and he shall pass over, and offend, imputing this his power unto his god.  

12 Art thou not from everlasting, O LORD my God, mine Holy One? we shall not die. Lord, thou hast ordained them for judgment; and, O mighty God, thou hast established them for correction.  

13 Thou art of purer eyes than to behold evil, and canst not look on iniquity: wherefore lookest thou upon them that deal treacherously, and holdest thy tongue when the wicked devoureth the man that is more righteous than he?  

14 And makest men as the fishes of the sea, as the creeping things, that have no ruler over them?  

15 They take up all of them with the angle, they catch them in their net, and gather them in their drag: therefore they rejoice and are glad.  

16 Therefore they sacrifice unto their net, and burn incense unto their drag; because by them their portion is fat, and their meat plenteous.  

17 Shall they therefore empty their net, and not spare continually to slay the nations?

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "O LORD, how long shall I cry, and thou wilt not hear! even cry out unto thee of violence, and thou wilt not save!"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any unanswered prayer or suffering
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's passion - The cry represents the suffering of the righteous and Christ's own question of God's timing in bringing justice.

### "The just shall live by his faith" (appears in chapter 2, but foreshadowed here)
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any general statement about faith
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's righteousness - The living represents Christ's call to faith in the face of suffering and injustice.
