## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### OBEDIENCE
- **Meaning:** "Divine submission, Christ's following"
- **Context:** The call to true obedience rather than empty rituals and self-serving fasting
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's call to genuine obedience and His rejection of hypocrisy
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual obedience (Matthew 6:16)

### JUSTICE
- **Meaning:** "Divine righteousness, Christ's fairness"
- **Context:** The command to execute true judgment and show mercy to all
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's justice and His call to social righteousness
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual justice (James 2:13)

### COMPASSION
- **Meaning:** "Divine mercy, Christ's love"
- **Context:** The command to show compassion to the vulnerable and oppressed
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's compassion and His love for the marginalized
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual compassion (James 1:27)

### REJECTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine judgment, Christ's discipline"
- **Context:** God's rejection of those who refuse to hear His word and obey His commands
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's rejection of hypocrisy and His discipline of the disobedient
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual rejection (Hebrews 3:15)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Execute true judgment, and shew mercy and compassions every man to his brother"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any general kindness or fairness
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's righteousness - The true judgment represents Christ's call to genuine righteousness and social justice, not just religious rituals.

### "But they refused to hearken, and pulled away the shoulder, and stopped their ears, that they should not hear"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any stubbornness or disobedience
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's rejection of hypocrisy - The refusal represents willful disobedience to God's word and Christ's judgment upon such hardness of heart.
