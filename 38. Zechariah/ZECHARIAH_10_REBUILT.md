## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### PROVISION
- **Meaning:** "Divine blessing, Christ's supply"
- **Context:** God's provision of rain and blessing upon the land and His people
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's provision and His blessing upon His church
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual provision (James 5:7)

### SHEPHERD
- **Meaning:** "Divine leadership, Christ's care"
- **Context:** The contrast between false shepherds and God's true shepherding of His flock
- **Significance:** Model of Christ as the Good Shepherd and His care for His people
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual shepherd (John 10:11)

### STRENGTH
- **Meaning:** "Divine power, Christ's enablement"
- **Context:** God's strengthening of His people and making them mighty in battle
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's strength and His enablement of His followers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual strength (Ephesians 6:10)

### RESTORATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine renewal, Christ's salvation"
- **Context:** The restoration of Israel and Judah and their return to their land
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's restoration and His salvation of His people
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual restoration (Romans 11:26)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 Ask ye of the LORD rain in the time of the latter rain; so the LORD shall make bright clouds, and give them showers of rain, to every one grass in the field.  

2 For the idols have spoken vanity, and the diviners have seen a lie, and have told false dreams; they comfort in vain: therefore they went their way as a flock, they were troubled, because there was no shepherd.  

3 Mine anger was kindled against the shepherds, and I punished the goats: for the LORD of hosts hath visited his flock the house of Judah, and hath made them as his goodly horse in the battle.  

4 Out of him came forth the corner, out of him the nail, out of him the battle bow, out of him every oppressor together.  

5 And they shall be as mighty men, which tread down their enemies in the mire of the streets in the battle: and they shall fight, because the LORD is with them, and the riders on horses shall be confounded.  

6 And I will strengthen the house of Judah, and I will save the house of Joseph, and I will bring them again to place them; for I have mercy upon them: and they shall be as though I had not cast them off: for I am the LORD their God, and will hear them.  

7 And they of Ephraim shall be like a mighty man, and their heart shall rejoice as through wine: yea, their children shall see it, and be glad; their heart shall rejoice in the LORD.  

8 I will hiss for them, and gather them; for I have redeemed them: and they shall increase as they have increased.  

9 And I will sow them among the people: and they shall remember me in far countries; and they shall live with their children, and turn again.  

10 And I will bring them again also out of the land of Egypt, and gather them out of Assyria; and I will bring them into the land of Gilead and Lebanon; and place shall not be found for them.  

11 And he shall pass through the sea with affliction, and shall smite the waves in the sea, and all the deeps of the river shall dry up: and the pride of Assyria shall be brought down, and the sceptre of Egypt shall depart away.  

12 And I will strengthen them in the LORD; and they shall walk up and down in his name, saith the LORD.  

13 And I will strengthen them in the LORD; and they shall walk up and down in his name, saith the LORD.  

14 And I will strengthen them in the LORD; and they shall walk up and down in his name, saith the LORD.  

15 And I will strengthen them in the LORD; and they shall walk up and down in his name, saith the LORD.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Ask ye of the LORD rain in the time of the latter rain; so the LORD shall make bright clouds, and give them showers of rain"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any weather or agricultural blessing
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's blessing - The rain represents God's spiritual blessing and Christ's provision for His people's spiritual needs.

### "For the idols have spoken vanity, and the diviners have seen a lie, and have told false dreams; they comfort in vain: therefore they went their way as a flock, they were troubled, because there was no shepherd"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any false religion or spiritual confusion
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's leadership - The lack of shepherd represents the need for Christ's true leadership and His care for His people.
