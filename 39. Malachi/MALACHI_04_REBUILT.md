## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### JUDGMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine wrath, Christ's return"
- **Context:** The final day of judgment that burns the wicked as stubble
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's final judgment and His righteous wrath
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual judgment (2 Peter 3:7)

### RIGHTEOUSNESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine holiness, Christ's salvation"
- **Context:** The Sun of righteousness arising with healing for those who fear God
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's righteousness and His healing salvation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual righteousness (John 8:12)

### RESTORATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine renewal, Christ's victory"
- **Context:** The restoration of the righteous and their victory over the wicked
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's restoration and His victory over evil
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual restoration (Romans 16:20)

### RECONCILIATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine unity, Christ's peace"
- **Context:** The reconciliation of hearts through Elijah's ministry
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's reconciliation and His peace between generations
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual reconciliation (Luke 1:17)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "But unto you that fear my name shall the Sun of righteousness arise with healing in his wings"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any sunrise or natural phenomenon
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's healing - The Sun of righteousness represents Christ as the light of salvation who brings healing to those who fear Him.

### "Behold, I will send you Elijah the prophet before the coming of the great and dreadful day of the LORD"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any prophet or messenger
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's forerunner - The Elijah represents John the Baptist preparing the way for Christ's second coming.
