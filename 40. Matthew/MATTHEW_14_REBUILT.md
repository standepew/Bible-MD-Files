## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### COMPASSION
- **Meaning:** "Divine mercy, Christ's care"
- **Context:** Christ's compassion for the multitudes and His healing of their sick
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's mercy and His care for suffering humanity
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual compassion (Mark 6:34)

### PROVISION
- **Meaning:** "Divine supply, Christ's abundance"
- **Context:** The miraculous feeding of the five thousand with five loaves and two fishes
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's abundance and His supernatural provision
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual provision (John 6:11)

### FAITH
- **Meaning:** "Divine trust, Christ's testing"
- **Context:** Peter's faith and doubt when walking on water toward Christ
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's testing and His response to genuine faith
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual faith (James 1:6)

### HEALING
- **Meaning:** "Divine restoration, Christ's power"
- **Context:** The healing of all who touched Christ's garment in Gennesaret
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's power and His restoration through faith
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual healing (Mark 6:56)

2 And said unto his servants, This is John the Baptist; he is risen from the dead; and therefore mighty works do shew forth themselves in him.

4 For John said unto him, It is not lawful for thee to have her.  

5 And when he would have put him to death, he feared the multitude, because they counted him as a prophet.

17 And they say unto him, We have here but five loaves, and two fishes.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "They need not depart; give ye them to eat"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any food or provision
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's provision - The feeding represents Christ's supernatural provision and His ability to meet human needs beyond natural resources.

### "And immediately Jesus stretched forth his hand, and caught him, and said unto him, O thou of little faith, wherefore didst thou doubt?"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any doubt or fear
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's faithfulness - The doubt represents human weakness and Christ's faithful response to those who falter in faith.
