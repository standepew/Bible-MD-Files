## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### IDENTITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine nature, Christ's person"
- **Context:** Peter's confession of Christ as the Son of the living God and Christ's divine identity
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's person and His divine nature as the Messiah
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual identity (John 6:69)

### CHURCH
- **Meaning:** "Divine assembly, Christ's body"
- **Context:** Christ's promise to build His church upon the rock of Peter's confession
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's body and His establishment of the universal church
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual church (Ephesians 2:20)

### AUTHORITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine power, Christ's rule"
- **Context:** The keys of the kingdom given to Peter and the authority to bind and loose
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's rule and His delegation of spiritual authority
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual authority (Isaiah 22:22)

### DISCIPLESHIP
- **Meaning:** "Divine following, Christ's call"
- **Context:** The call to deny self, take up the cross, and follow Christ at the cost of one's life
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's call and His requirement of total commitment
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual discipleship (Luke 9:23)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "And I say also unto thee, That thou art Peter, and upon this rock I will build my church; and the gates of hell shall not prevail against it"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any church organization or building
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's body - The rock represents Peter's confession of Christ as the Messiah, and the church represents Christ's universal body built on that foundation.

### "If any man will come after me, let him deny himself, and take up his cross, and follow me"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any hardship or sacrifice
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's following - The cross represents total commitment to Christ even unto death, and the following represents complete devotion to His will.
