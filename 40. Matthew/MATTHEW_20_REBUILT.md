## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### GENEROSITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine grace, Christ's mercy"
- **Context:** The householder's generosity in giving equal wages to all laborers regardless of work time
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's mercy and His unmerited grace toward all believers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual generosity (Romans 4:4)

### SUFFERING
- **Meaning:** "Divine passion, Christ's redemption"
- **Context:** Christ's prediction of His coming suffering, death, and resurrection in Jerusalem
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's redemption and His sacrificial mission for humanity
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual suffering (Mark 10:33)

### SERVICE
- **Meaning:** "Divine ministry, Christ's example"
- **Context:** The call to servant leadership rather than worldly authority and Christ's example
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's example and His call to humble service
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual service (Luke 22:26)

### HEALING
- **Meaning:** "Divine restoration, Christ's compassion"
- **Context:** The healing of the two blind men and Christ's compassion toward the suffering
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's compassion and His power to restore sight and faith
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual healing (Mark 10:52)

1 For the kingdom of heaven is like unto a man that is an householder, which went out early in the morning to hire labourers into his vineyard.  

2 And when he had agreed with the labourers for a penny a day, he sent them into his vineyard.  

3 And he went out about the third hour, and saw others standing idle in the marketplace,  

4 And said unto them; Go ye also into the vineyard, and whatsoever is right I will give you. And they went their way.  

5 Again he went out about the sixth and ninth hour, and did likewise.  

6 And about the eleventh hour he went out, and found others standing idle, and saith unto them, Why stand ye here all the day idle?

9 And when they came that were hired about the eleventh hour, they received every man a penny.  

10 But when the first came, they supposed that they should have received more; and they likewise received every man a penny.  

11 And when they had received it, they murmured against the goodman of the house,  

12 Saying, These last have wrought but one hour, and thou hast made them equal unto us, which have borne the burden and heat of the day.  

13 But he answered one of them, and said, Friend, I do thee no wrong: didst not thou agree with me for a penny?  

14 Take that thine is, and go thy way: I will give unto this last, even as unto thee.

23 And he saith unto them, Ye shall drink indeed of my cup, and be baptized with the baptism that I am baptized with: but to sit on my right hand, and on my left, is not mine to give, but it shall be given to them for whom it is prepared of my Father.

31 And the multitude rebuked them, because they should hold their peace: but they cried the more, saying, Have mercy on us, O Lord, thou Son of David.  

32 And Jesus stood still, and called them, and said, What will ye that I shall do unto you?  

33 They say unto him, Lord, that our eyes may be opened.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "So the last shall be first, and the first last: for many be called, but few chosen"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any reversal of order or selection
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's grace - The reversal represents God's sovereign grace that transcends human merit and timing.

### "Even as the Son of man came not to be ministered unto, but to minister, and to give his life a ransom for many"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any service or sacrifice
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's redemption - The ransom represents Christ's sacrificial death that pays the price for human sin.
