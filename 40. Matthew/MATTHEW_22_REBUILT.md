## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### INVITATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine calling, Christ's gospel"
- **Context:** The kingdom invitation to the wedding feast and the rejection by those invited
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's gospel and His invitation to salvation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual invitation (Revelation 19:7)

### WORTHINESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine qualification, Christ's righteousness"
- **Context:** The requirement of the wedding garment representing Christ's righteousness
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's righteousness and His requirement for salvation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual worthiness (Revelation 19:8)

### RESURRECTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine life, Christ's victory"
- **Context:** The Sadducees' question about resurrection and Christ's teaching on eternal life
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's victory and His power over death
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual resurrection (Mark 12:25)

### LOVE
- **Meaning:** "Divine devotion, Christ's example"
- **Context:** The great commandments to love God and neighbor as the foundation of all law
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's example and His summary of divine law
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual love (Deuteronomy 6:5)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "For many are called, but few are chosen"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any selection or election
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's salvation - The calling represents God's general invitation to salvation, while the choosing represents those who respond with genuine faith and righteousness.

### "Render therefore unto Caesar the things which are Caesar's; and unto God the things that are God's"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any political or civic duty
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's wisdom - The rendering represents proper distinction between earthly obligations and divine worship, showing Christ's authority over both realms.
