## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### HYPOCRISY
- **Meaning:** "Divine deception, Christ's exposure"
- **Context:** The religious leaders' external righteousness contrasted with internal corruption
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's exposure and His condemnation of false religion
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual hypocrisy (Luke 11:52)

### BURDEN
- **Meaning:** "Divine oppression, Christ's liberation"
- **Context:** The heavy religious requirements imposed by the Pharisees that they themselves don't keep
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's liberation and His freedom from legalistic oppression
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual burden (Acts 15:10)

### JUDGMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine condemnation, Christ's warning"
- **Context:** The eight woe pronouncements announcing judgment on religious hypocrisy
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's warning and His righteous judgment on false religion
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual judgment (Revelation 3:8)

### LAMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine sorrow, Christ's compassion"
- **Context:** Christ's sorrow over Jerusalem's rejection of the prophets and Himself
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's compassion and His grief over human rebellion
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual lament (Romans 9:2)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "For they bind heavy burdens and grievous to be borne, and lay them on men's shoulders; but they themselves will not move them with one of their fingers"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any difficult requirements or rules
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's liberation - The burdens represent religious legalism that Christ frees believers from through His grace.

### "And whosoever shall exalt himself shall be abased; and he that shall humble himself shall be exalted"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any humility or pride
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's example - The exaltation represents divine reversal where God honors the humble and resists the proud.
