## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### PROPHECY
- **Meaning:** "Divine revelation, Christ's foretelling"
- **Context:** Christ's detailed prophecy of end times and the destruction of Jerusalem
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's foretelling and His revelation of future events
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual prophecy (Mark 13:2)

### TRIBULATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine judgment, Christ's warning"
- **Context:** The great tribulation period and Christ's warning of unprecedented suffering
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's warning and His preparation of believers for judgment
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual tribulation (Daniel 12:1)

### COMING
- **Meaning:** "Divine return, Christ's second advent"
- **Context:** The second coming of Christ with power and glory to gather His elect
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's second advent and His final victory
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual coming (Revelation 1:7)

### READINESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine preparation, Christ's exhortation"
- **Context:** The call to be ready and watchful for Christ's unexpected return
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's exhortation and His requirement of spiritual vigilance
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual readiness (Luke 12:40)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "For then shall be great tribulation, such as was not since the beginning of the world to this time, no, nor ever shall be"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any suffering or difficulty
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's judgment - The tribulation represents unprecedented divine judgment upon the world for rejecting Christ.

### "But of that day and hour knoweth no man, no, not the angels of heaven, but my Father only"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any timing or prediction
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's sovereignty - The timing represents God's exclusive authority over end times and Christ's submission to the Father's will.
