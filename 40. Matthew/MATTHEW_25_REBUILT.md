## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### READINESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine preparation, Christ's vigilance"
- **Context:** The wise virgins who prepared with oil and the faithful servants who used their talents
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's vigilance and His requirement of spiritual preparation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual readiness (1 Thessalonians 5:6)

### STEWARDSHIP
- **Meaning:** "Divine responsibility, Christ's accountability"
- **Context:** The servants entrusted with talents and their accountability for their use
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's accountability and His reward for faithful service
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual stewardship (1 Corinthians 4:2)

### JUDGMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine justice, Christ's verdict"
- **Context:** The final judgment separating sheep from goats based on their treatment of Christ
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's verdict and His righteous judgment of humanity
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual judgment (Revelation 20:11)

### COMPASSION
- **Meaning:** "Divine mercy, Christ's empathy"
- **Context:** The identification of Christ with the needy and reward for compassionate service
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's empathy and His value of merciful service
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual compassion (Hebrews 13:3)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Watch therefore, for ye know neither the day nor the hour wherein the Son of man cometh"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any waiting or anticipation
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's vigilance - The watching represents spiritual readiness for Christ's return and the need for constant vigilance.

### "Well done, thou good and faithful servant: thou hast been faithful over a few things, I will make thee ruler over many things"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any good work or service
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's stewardship - The faithfulness represents proper use of God-given gifts and Christ's reward for faithful service.
