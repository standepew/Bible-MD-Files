## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### PASSION
- **Meaning:** "Divine suffering, Christ's sacrifice"
- **Context:** The beginning of Christ's suffering through betrayal, arrest, and trial
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's sacrifice and His voluntary suffering for humanity
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual passion (Mark 14:1)

### ANOINTING
- **Meaning:** "Divine preparation, Christ's burial"
- **Context:** The woman's anointing of Christ for His burial and its memorial significance
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's burial and His preparation for death
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual anointing (John 12:3)

### COVENANT
- **Meaning:** "Divine agreement, Christ's testament"
- **Context:** The institution of the Lord's Supper as the new covenant in Christ's blood
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's testament and His establishment of the new covenant
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual covenant (Luke 22:20)

### PRAYER
- **Meaning:** "Divine submission, Christ's obedience"
- **Context:** Christ's prayer of submission in Gethsemane and His obedience to the Father's will
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's obedience and His perfect submission to God's will
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual prayer (Luke 22:42)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "This is my body... this is my blood of the new testament"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any memorial or ritual
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's covenant - The body and blood represent Christ's sacrificial death and the establishment of the new covenant through His blood.

### "Watch and pray, that ye enter not into temptation: the spirit indeed is willing, but the flesh is weak"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any weakness or temptation
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's obedience - The spirit and flesh represent the conflict between divine will and human weakness, showing Christ's perfect obedience despite temptation.
