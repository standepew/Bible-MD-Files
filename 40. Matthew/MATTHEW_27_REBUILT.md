## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### CRUCIFIXION
- **Meaning:** "Divine suffering, Christ's atonement"
- **Context:** The physical crucifixion of Christ and His atoning death for humanity's sins
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's atonement and His sacrificial death as the Lamb of God
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual crucifixion (Mark 15:1)

### MOCKERY
- **Meaning:** "Divine humiliation, Christ's scorn"
- **Context:** The mocking of Christ as King with crown of thorns and purple robe
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's scorn and His endurance of human ridicule
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual mockery (Mark 15:17)

### DARKNESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine judgment, Christ's suffering"
- **Context:** The supernatural darkness during Christ's crucifixion representing God's judgment
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's suffering and His bearing of divine judgment
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual darkness (Mark 15:33)

### BURIAL
- **Meaning:** "Divine rest, Christ's preparation"
- **Context:** The burial of Christ in a new tomb and His preparation for resurrection
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's preparation and His fulfillment of burial prophecy
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual burial (Mark 15:46)

## PROPHETIC/TYPOLOGICAL CONNECTIONS

### Christ's Crucifixion as Type of Christ's Atonement
- **Physical crucifixion:** Pontius Pilate (Matthew 27:4)
* **Spiritual crucifixion:* Christ's atonement (Mark 15:1)
* **Christ's crucifixion:* Perfect atonement (Luke 23:1)
* **Eternal crucifixion:* Complete sacrifice (Revelation 22)

### Death on Cross as Type of Christ's Victory
- **Physical death:** Crown thorns (Matthew 27:31)
* **Spiritual death:* Christ's victory (Mark 15:17)
* **Christ's death:* Perfect victory (John 19:2)
* **Eternal death:* Complete triumph (Revelation 22)

### Burial in Tomb as Type of Christ's Preparation
- **Physical burial:** Joseph Arimathæa (Matthew 27:59)
