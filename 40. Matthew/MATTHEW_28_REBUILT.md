## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### RESURRECTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine victory, Christ's triumph"
- **Context:** The resurrection of Christ from the dead and His victory over death
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's triumph and His defeat of death through His power
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual resurrection (Mark 16:1)

### PROCLAMATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine announcement, Christ's message"
- **Context:** The angel's announcement of Christ's resurrection to the women
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's message and His proclamation of victory
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual proclamation (Luke 24:1)

### COMMISSION
- **Meaning:** "Divine mission, Christ's command"
- **Context:** The Great Commission to make disciples of all nations
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's command and His mission for the church
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual commission (Mark 16:15)

### AUTHORITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine sovereignty, Christ's rule"
- **Context:** Christ's declaration of all authority in heaven and earth
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's rule and His sovereign authority over all creation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual authority (Philippians 2:9)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "All power is given unto me in heaven and in earth"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any power or authority
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's sovereignty - The power represents Christ's complete authority as the risen Lord over all creation.

### "Go ye therefore, and teach all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Ghost"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any teaching or baptism
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's mission - The commission represents Christ's command to make disciples through the authority of the Triune God.
