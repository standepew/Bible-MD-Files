## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### FORGIVENESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine mercy, Christ's authority"
- **Context:** Christ's power to forgive sins demonstrated through healing the paralytic
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's authority and His divine power to forgive sin
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual forgiveness (Matthew 9:2)

### HEALING
- **Meaning:** "Divine restoration, Christ's compassion"
- **Context:** The physical healing of the paralytic as evidence of Christ's forgiveness power
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's compassion and His power to restore both body and soul
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual healing (Luke 5:20)

### CALLING
- **Meaning:** "Divine invitation, Christ's discipleship"
- **Context:** The calling of Levi (Matthew) from tax collecting to discipleship
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's invitation and His call to leave all for His service
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual calling (Matthew 9:9)

### AUTHORITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine rule, Christ's lordship"
- **Context:** Christ's authority over the Sabbath and His lordship as Son of man
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's lordship and His authority over religious traditions
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual authority (Matthew 12:8)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "I came not to call the righteous, but sinners to repentance"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any calling or invitation
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's mission - The calling represents Christ's purpose to seek and save the lost, not those who think they are righteous.

### "The sabbath was made for man, and not man for the sabbath"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any day of rest or religious practice
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's authority - The sabbath represents God's provision for human benefit, not a burden to be imposed on people.
