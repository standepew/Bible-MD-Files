## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### PARABLE
- **Meaning:** "Divine illustration, Christ's teaching"
- **Context:** Christ's use of parables to reveal kingdom mysteries to believers while concealing from unbelievers
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's teaching and His revelation of spiritual truth through earthly stories
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual parable (Matthew 13:3)

### KINGDOM
- **Meaning:** "Divine reign, Christ's rule"
- **Context:** The kingdom of God growing mysteriously like seed and becoming great like a mustard tree
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's rule and His establishment of God's reign through seemingly small beginnings
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual kingdom (Matthew 13:11)

### FAITH
- **Meaning:** "Divine trust, Christ's requirement"
- **Context:** The disciples' lack of faith during the storm contrasted with Christ's complete authority
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's requirement and His power over all creation through faith
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual faith (Matthew 8:26)

### MYSTERY
- **Meaning:** "Divine revelation, Christ's disclosure"
- **Context:** The mysteries of the kingdom given to disciples but hidden from outsiders through parables
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's disclosure and His selective revelation of spiritual truth
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual mystery (Luke 8:10)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Unto you it is given to know the mystery of the kingdom of God: but unto them that are without, all these things are done in parables"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any mystery or secret knowledge
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's revelation - The mysteries represent spiritual truth revealed to believers through Christ's teaching while concealed from unbelievers.

### "Peace, be still"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any peace or calm
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's authority - The peace represents Christ's complete authority over creation and His power to bring order out of chaos.
