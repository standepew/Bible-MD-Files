## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### COMPASSION
- **Meaning:** "Divine mercy, Christ's care"
- **Context:** Christ's compassion for the multitude who had been with Him three days without food
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's care and His concern for human physical needs
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual compassion (Matthew 15:32)

### SIGN
- **Meaning:** "Divine proof, Christ's authority"
- **Context:** The Pharisees' demand for a sign from heaven and Christ's refusal to give one
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's authority and His rejection of testing by unbelievers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sign (Matthew 16:1)

### HEALING
- **Meaning:** "Divine restoration, Christ's power"
- **Context:** The progressive healing of the blind man in two stages
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's power and His gradual restoration of spiritual sight
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual healing (John 9:6)

### CONFESSION
- **Meaning:** "Divine revelation, Christ's identity"
- **Context:** Peter's confession that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of the living God
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's identity and His revelation to believers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual confession (Matthew 16:16)

### DISCIPLESHIP
- **Meaning:** "Divine calling, Christ's requirement"
- **Context:** Christ's teaching on denying self, taking up the cross, and following Him
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's requirement and His call to radical commitment
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual discipleship (Luke 9:23)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "I have compassion on the multitude, because they have now been with me three days, and have nothing to eat"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any compassion or feeding
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's provision - The compassion represents Christ's concern for both spiritual and physical needs, demonstrating His complete care for His followers.

### "Get thee behind me, Satan: for thou savourest not the things that be of God, but the things that be of men"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any correction or rebuke
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's suffering - The rebuke represents Christ's rejection of human thinking that opposes God's redemptive plan.

The chapter points forward to Christ as the perfectly providing Savior whose provision is miraculous, whose authority is divine, whose restoration is progressive, and whose revelation is complete. The feeding four thousand points forward to Christ's perfect provision. The sign of heaven points forward to Christ's perfect authority. The chapter demonstrates that when feeding four thousand occurs, it brings Christ's provision, when sign of heaven is demanded, it brings Christ's authority, when healing of blind man happens, it brings Christ's restoration, and when Peter's confession is made, it brings Christ's revelation, showing how feeding four thousand and sign of heaven work together to accomplish His purposes of provision and authority.
