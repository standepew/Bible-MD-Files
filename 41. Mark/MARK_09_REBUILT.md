## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### TRANSFIGURATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine glory, Christ's revelation"
- **Context:** Christ's glorious transformation before three disciples, revealing His divine nature
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's revelation and His demonstration of divine glory
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual transfiguration (Matthew 17:1)

### DELIVERANCE
- **Meaning:** "Divine power, Christ's authority"
- **Context:** Christ's deliverance of the demon-possessed boy through His authority over evil spirits
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's authority and His power to overcome spiritual oppression
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual deliverance (Matthew 17:15)

### GREATNESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine service, Christ's humility"
- **Context:** Christ's teaching that true greatness comes through servanthood and receiving children
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's humility and His redefinition of greatness as service
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual greatness (Matthew 18:3)

### PURITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine holiness, Christ's warning"
- **Context:** Christ's warning against sin and the radical measures needed to maintain purity
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's warning and His call to radical holiness
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual purity (Matthew 5:29)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "This is my beloved Son: hear him"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any voice or command
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's authority - The voice represents God's divine testimony to Christ's identity and His command to listen to Him.

### "If thou canst believe, all things are possible to him that believeth"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any belief or possibility
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's power - The belief represents trust in Christ's power that makes all things possible according to God's will.
