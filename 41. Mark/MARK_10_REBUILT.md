## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### MARRIAGE
- **Meaning:** "Divine covenant, Christ's design"
- **Context:** Christ's teaching on marriage as a divine covenant that cannot be broken by human divorce
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's design and His establishment of marriage as a sacred union
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual marriage (Ephesians 5:31)

### CHILDREN
- **Meaning:** "Divine humility, Christ's welcome"
- **Context:** Christ's welcome of little children and His teaching that the kingdom belongs to such as them
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's welcome and His requirement of childlike faith
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual children (Matthew 19:14)

### RICHES
- **Meaning:** "Divine obstacle, Christ's warning"
- **Context:** Christ's warning that wealth can be a barrier to entering God's kingdom
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's warning and His call to prioritize eternal treasure over earthly wealth
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual riches (Matthew 19:24)

### SERVICE
- **Meaning:** "Divine calling, Christ's example"
- **Context:** Christ's teaching that true greatness comes through service, not position
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's example and His call to servant leadership
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual service (Matthew 20:26)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "What therefore God hath joined together, let not man put asunder"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any relationship or commitment
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's covenant - The joining represents God's divine establishment of marriage as a sacred covenant that humans cannot break.

### "It is easier for a camel to go through the eye of a needle, than for a rich man to enter into the kingdom of God"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any wealth or difficulty
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's warning - The camel represents the impossibility of salvation through human effort when wealth becomes an idol.
