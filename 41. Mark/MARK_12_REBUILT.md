## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### REJECTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine suffering, Christ's crucifixion"
- **Context:** The rejection and killing of the son in the parable representing Christ's rejection by Israel's leaders
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's crucifixion and His suffering at the hands of those who should have received Him
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual rejection (Matthew 21:33)

### AUTHORITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine rule, Christ's sovereignty"
- **Context:** Christ's teaching on proper authority relationships between civil government and divine authority
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's sovereignty and His proper ordering of earthly and heavenly allegiances
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual authority (Matthew 22:21)

### RESURRECTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine life, Christ's victory"
- **Context:** Christ's teaching on the resurrection refuting the Sadducees and affirming eternal life
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's victory and His power over death through resurrection
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual resurrection (Matthew 22:31)

### LOVE
- **Meaning:** "Divine devotion, Christ's command"
- **Context:** Christ's summary of the law as love for God and neighbor, the greatest commandments
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's command and His requirement for complete devotion to God and others
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual love (Deuteronomy 6:5)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Render to Cæsar the things that are Cæsar's, and to God the things that are God's"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any politics or government
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's authority - The rendering represents Christ's teaching on proper relationships between civil and divine authority.

### "The stone which the builders rejected is become the head of the corner"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any building or construction
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's rejection - The stone represents Christ who was rejected by Israel's leaders but became the foundation of God's redemptive plan.
