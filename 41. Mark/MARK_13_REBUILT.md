## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### TRIBULATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine judgment, Christ's warning"
- **Context:** The great tribulation preceding Christ's return, a time of unprecedented suffering
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's warning and His preparation of believers for end times
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual tribulation (Matthew 24:21)

### SECOND COMING
- **Meaning:** "Divine return, Christ's glory"
- **Context:** Christ's glorious return in the clouds with power and great glory
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's return and His ultimate victory over all opposition
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual return (Matthew 24:30)

### WATCHFULNESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine readiness, Christ's command"
- **Context:** Christ's command to watch and pray in anticipation of His return
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's command and His requirement for spiritual readiness
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual watchfulness (Matthew 24:42)

### FAITHFULNESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine endurance, Christ's reward"
- **Context:** The endurance of believers through tribulation and their final salvation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's reward and His blessing for faithful perseverance
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual faithfulness (Matthew 24:13)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Heaven and earth shall pass away: but my words shall not pass away"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any words or promises
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's authority - The words represent Christ's divine authority and the eternal certainty of His prophetic declarations.

### "Watch therefore: for ye know not what hour your Lord doth come"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any watching or waiting
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's return - The watch represents spiritual readiness and anticipation of Christ's glorious return.
