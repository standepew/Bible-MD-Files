## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### CRUCIFIXION
- **Meaning:** "Divine atonement, Christ's sacrifice"
- **Context:** Christ's crucifixion at Golgotha, the place of the skull, fulfilling prophecy
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's sacrifice and His atoning death for sinners
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual crucifixion (Matthew 27:32)

### MOCKERY
- **Meaning:** "Divine suffering, Christ's humiliation"
- **Context:** The mockery of Christ as King of the Jews with crown of thorns and purple robe
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's humiliation and His endurance of scorn for our salvation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual mockery (John 19:2)

### DARKNESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine judgment, Christ's suffering"
- **Context:** The supernatural darkness from the sixth to ninth hour during Christ's crucifixion
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's suffering and God's judgment at the crucifixion
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual darkness (Matthew 27:45)

### RESURRECTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine victory, Christ's triumph"
- **Context:** The centurion's confession that Christ is the Son of God at His death
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's triumph and His victory even in death
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual resurrection (Matthew 27:54)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Truly this man was the Son of God"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any good person or moral teacher
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's divinity - The centurion's confession represents recognition of Christ's true identity as the Son of God even in His death.

### "Eloi, Eloi, lama sabachthani"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any despair or suffering
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's suffering - The cry represents Christ's fulfillment of Psalm 22 and His experience of bearing the sins of the world.
