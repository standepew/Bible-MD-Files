## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### ANNUNCIATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine revelation, Christ's incarnation"
- **Context:** The angel Gabriel's announcement to Mary that she would conceive and bear the Son of God
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's incarnation and His divine conception through the Holy Spirit
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual annunciation (Matthew 1:20)

### PROPHECY
- **Meaning:** "Divine foretelling, Christ's fulfillment"
- **Context:** Zacharias' prophecy concerning Christ's salvation and John's preparatory ministry
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's fulfillment and His role as the promised Messiah
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual prophecy (Matthew 1:22)

### OBEDIENCE
- **Meaning:** "Divine submission, Christ's example"
- **Context:** Mary's willing submission to God's plan despite the personal consequences
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's example and His perfect obedience to the Father's will
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual obedience (John 1:14)

### SALVATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine deliverance, Christ's redemption"
- **Context:** God's salvation promised to Israel and fulfilled in Christ, the horn of salvation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's redemption and His deliverance from sin and death
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual salvation (John 3:16)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

2 Even as they delivered them unto us, which from the beginning were eyewitnesses, and ministers of the word;  

3 It seemed good to me also, having had perfect understanding of all things from the very first, to write unto thee in order, most excellent Theophilus,  

4 That thou mightest know the certainty of those things, wherein thou hast been instructed.  

5 There was in the days of Herod, the king of Judæa, a certain priest named Zacharias, of the course of Abia: and his wife was of the daughters of Aaron, and her name was Elisabeth.

**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any human possibility or achievement

**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's salvation - Mary's song represents recognition of God's mighty acts in bringing salvation through the promised Messiah.
