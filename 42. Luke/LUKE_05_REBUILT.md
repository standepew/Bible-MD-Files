## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### CALLING
- **Meaning:** "Divine invitation, Christ's discipleship"
- **Context:** Christ's calling of His first disciples from their fishing profession to become fishers of men
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's discipleship and His invitation to follow Him
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual calling (Matthew 4:19)

### HEALING
- **Meaning:** "Divine restoration, Christ's compassion"
- **Context:** Christ's healing of leprosy and paralysis, demonstrating His power over disease and sin
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's compassion and His authority to heal both body and soul
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual healing (Matthew 8:2)

### FORGIVENESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine mercy, Christ's authority"
- **Context:** Christ's forgiveness of sins, demonstrating His divine authority to forgive
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's mercy and His unique authority to forgive sins
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual forgiveness (Matthew 9:6)

### TRANSFORMATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine renewal, Christ's newness"
- **Context:** Christ's teaching about new wine and new garments, representing the new covenant
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's renewal and His establishment of the new covenant
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual transformation (2 Corinthians 5:17)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "I came not to call the righteous, but sinners to repentance"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any social outreach or ministry to the marginalized
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's mission - The sinners represent those who recognize their need for salvation, while the righteous represent those who think they don't need Christ.

### "No man putteth new wine into old bottles"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any change or innovation
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's new covenant - The new wine represents the gospel and the new bottles represent the new covenant community that can contain it.
