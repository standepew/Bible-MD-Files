## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### SABBATH
- **Meaning:** "Divine rest, Christ's authority"
- **Context:** Christ's teaching on His authority over the Sabbath and proper Sabbath observance
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's authority and His lordship over all creation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sabbath (Matthew 12:8)

### APOSTLESHIP
- **Meaning:** "Divine calling, Christ's commission"
- **Context:** Christ's choosing of twelve apostles for His ministry and mission
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's commission and His establishment of the church
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual apostleship (Mark 3:14)

### BEATITUDE
- **Meaning:** "Divine blessing, Christ's promise"
- **Context:** Christ's blessings for the poor, hungry, and persecuted, promising future reward
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's blessing and His promise of future glory
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual beatitude (Matthew 5:3)

### OBEDIENCE
- **Meaning:** "Divine submission, Christ's requirement"
- **Context:** Christ's teaching on obedience to His words as the foundation of true discipleship
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's requirement and His call for complete obedience
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual obedience (Matthew 7:21)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Judge not, and ye shall not be judged"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any judgment or discernment
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's teaching - The judgment represents hypocritical condemnation, not proper discernment or righteous judgment.

### "Can the blind lead the blind?"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any leadership or guidance
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's teaching - The blind leading represents spiritual blindness and the inability of those without true understanding to guide others.
