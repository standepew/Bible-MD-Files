## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### AUTHORITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine power, Christ's supremacy"
- **Context:** The centurion's recognition of Christ's authority to heal with a word
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's supremacy and His power over distance and disease
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual authority (Matthew 8:9)

### COMPASSION
- **Meaning:** "Divine mercy, Christ's tenderness"
- **Context:** Christ's compassion for the widow of Nain and His raising of her son
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's tenderness and His power over death
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual compassion (Matthew 9:36)

### FAITH
- **Meaning:** "Divine trust, Christ's requirement"
- **Context:** The centurion's great faith and the sinful woman's faith in Christ
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's requirement and His response to genuine faith
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual faith (Hebrews 11:1)

### FORGIVENESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine mercy, Christ's grace"
- **Context:** Christ's forgiveness of the sinful woman based on her love and faith
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's grace and His power to forgive sins
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual forgiveness (Matthew 9:2)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "I have not found so great faith, no, not in Israel"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any faith or belief system
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's authority - The great faith represents recognition of Christ's divine authority and power, not just any religious belief.

### "Her sins, which are many, are forgiven; for she loved much"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any forgiveness based on good works
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's grace - The forgiveness represents Christ's response to genuine love and faith, not earned by works but flowing from them.
