## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### AUTHORITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine power, Christ's commission"
- **Context:** Christ's giving of authority to the twelve disciples for ministry
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's commission and His delegation of authority to His followers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual authority (Matthew 10:1)

### PROVISION
- **Meaning:** "Divine supply, Christ's miracle"
- **Context:** The feeding of the five thousand demonstrating Christ's miraculous provision
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's miracle and His power to provide abundantly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual provision (John 6:1)

### GLORY
- **Meaning:** "Divine majesty, Christ's transfiguration"
- **Context:** Christ's transfiguration revealing His divine glory to Peter, James, and John
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's majesty and His revelation of divine nature
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual glory (Matthew 17:1)

### COMMITMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine dedication, Christ's call"
- **Context:** Christ's teaching on the cost of discipleship and total commitment
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's call and His requirement for complete dedication
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual commitment (Matthew 16:24)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "If any man will come after me, let him deny himself, and take up his cross daily, and follow me"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any self-improvement or personal sacrifice
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's discipleship - The cross represents total surrender to Christ's will and complete identification with His mission.

### "Let the dead bury their dead"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any neglect of family duties
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's priority - The dead represent those spiritually dead, and the call is to prioritize the kingdom of God over earthly obligations.
