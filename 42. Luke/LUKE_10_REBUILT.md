## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### MISSION
- **Meaning:** "Divine commission, Christ's sending"
- **Context:** Christ's sending of the seventy to preach and heal in various cities
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's commission and His expansion of the missionary enterprise
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual mission (Matthew 28:19)

### JUDGMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine justice, Christ's warning"
- **Context:** Christ's woes to unrepentant cities and warning of judgment
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's justice and His warning against spiritual hardness
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual judgment (Matthew 11:21)

### AUTHORITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine power, Christ's dominion"
- **Context:** Christ's authority over demons and His power given to the seventy
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's dominion and His delegation of spiritual authority
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual authority (Luke 10:19)

### COMPASSION
- **Meaning:** "Divine mercy, Christ's love"
- **Context:** The parable of the Good Samaritan demonstrating true compassion for those in need
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's love and His call for practical compassion

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "The harvest truly is great, but the labourers are few"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any work or labor shortage
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's mission - The harvest represents souls ready for salvation, and the labourers represent those called to bring in the harvest.

### "Go, and do thou likewise"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any good deeds or charity
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's compassion - The Samaritan represents true neighborly love that crosses cultural and religious boundaries.
