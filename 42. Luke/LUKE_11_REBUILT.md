## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### PRAYER
- **Meaning:** "Divine communion, Christ's teaching"
- **Context:** Christ's teaching on prayer through the Lord's Prayer and parable of persistent prayer
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's teaching and His pattern for effective prayer
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual prayer (Matthew 6:9)

### AUTHORITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine power, Christ's dominion"
- **Context:** Christ's authority over demons and His defense against accusations of using Beelzebub's power
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's dominion and His power over spiritual forces
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual authority (Matthew 12:28)

### JUDGMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine justice, Christ's warning"
- **Context:** Christ's warnings to the Pharisees and lawyers about their hypocrisy and coming judgment
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's justice and His warning against religious hypocrisy
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual judgment (Matthew 23:25)

### ILLUMINATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine light, Christ's revelation"
- **Context:** Christ's teaching on spiritual illumination through the light of the body and the eye
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's revelation and His call for spiritual enlightenment
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual illumination (Matthew 6:22)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Ask, and it shall be given you; seek, and ye shall find; knock, and it shall be opened unto you"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any request or desire fulfillment
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's promise - The asking, seeking, and knocking represent persistent prayer aligned with God's will, not selfish desires.

### "The light of the body is the eye: therefore when thine eye is single, thy whole body also is full of light"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any vision or perspective
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's teaching - The single eye represents spiritual focus and purity of heart, not physical eyesight.
