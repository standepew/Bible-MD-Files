### REPENTANCE
- **Meaning:** "Divine transformation, Christ's requirement"
- **Context:** Christ's teaching that all must repent or perish, using current events as warnings
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's requirement and His call for universal repentance
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual repentance (Acts 2:38)

- **Meaning:** "Divine productivity, Christ's expectation"
- **Context:** The parable of the fig tree showing God's expectation of spiritual fruit
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's expectation and His patience before judgment
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual fruitfulness (John 15:2)

- **Meaning:** "Divine restoration, Christ's compassion"
- **Context:** Christ's healing of the woman on the Sabbath, demonstrating His compassion over legalism
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's compassion and His authority over the Sabbath

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Except ye repent, ye shall all likewise perish"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any moral improvement or religious reform
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's warning - The repentance represents complete turning from sin, not just moral improvement, and the perishing represents eternal judgment.

### "Strive to enter in at the strait gate"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any effort or self-improvement
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's call - The striving represents earnest seeking of salvation through Christ, not human effort alone.
