## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### HUMILITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine meekness, Christ's teaching"
- **Context:** Christ's teaching on humility through the parable of taking the lowest place at a wedding feast
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's teaching and His call for genuine humility over self-exaltation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual humility (Philippians 2:8)

### GENEROSITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine giving, Christ's example"
- **Context:** Christ's teaching on generosity through inviting the poor, maimed, lame, and blind to feasts
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's example and His call for giving without expectation of return
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual generosity (2 Corinthians 9:7)

### COMMITMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine dedication, Christ's requirement"
- **Context:** Christ's teaching on the cost of discipleship through hating family and bearing one's cross
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's requirement and His call for total commitment to Him above all else
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual commitment (Matthew 16:24)

### PRESERVATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine preservation, Christ's purpose"
- **Context:** Christ's teaching on salt losing its savor and the importance of maintaining spiritual effectiveness
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's purpose and His call for believers to maintain their spiritual influence
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual preservation (Matthew 5:13)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "For whosoever exalteth himself shall be abased; and he that humbleth himself shall be exalted"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any social etiquette or modest behavior
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's teaching - The exaltation and humbling represent spiritual positions before God, not just social status.

### "If any man come to me, and hate not his father, and mother, and wife, and children, and brethren, and sisters, yea, and his own life also, he cannot be my disciple"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any family hatred or neglect
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's priority - The hate represents prioritizing Christ above all earthly relationships, not literal hatred.
