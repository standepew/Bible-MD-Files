## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### REPENTANCE
- **Meaning:** "Divine restoration, Christ's salvation"
- **Context:** The joy in heaven over one sinner who repents, showing God's delight in salvation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's salvation and God's gracious response to repentance
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual repentance (Acts 3:19)

### COMPASSION
- **Meaning:** "Divine mercy, Christ's forgiveness"
- **Context:** The father's compassion for the returning prodigal son, demonstrating God's mercy
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's forgiveness and God's unconditional love for repentant sinners
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual compassion (Luke 15:20)

### RESTORATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine renewal, Christ's redemption"
- **Context:** The prodigal son's restoration through the best robe, ring, and shoes
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's redemption and the complete restoration of believers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual restoration (Zechariah 3:4)

### JEALOUSY
- **Meaning:** "Divine warning, Christ's teaching"
- **Context:** The elder son's jealousy and anger at his brother's restoration
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's teaching and the danger of spiritual jealousy
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual jealousy (Matthew 20:12)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "For this my son was dead, and is alive again; he was lost, and is found"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any return or recovery
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's salvation - The death and life represent spiritual death and new life in Christ, not just physical circumstances.

### "It was meet that we should make merry, and be glad: for this thy brother was dead, and is alive again; and was lost, and is found"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any celebration or happiness
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's joy - The merriment represents heavenly joy over salvation, not just earthly celebration.
