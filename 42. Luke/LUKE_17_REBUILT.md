## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### FORGIVENESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine mercy, Christ's command"
- **Context:** Christ's teaching on forgiving a brother who repents, even seven times in a day
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's command and His call for unlimited forgiveness to the repentant
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual forgiveness (Colossians 3:13)

### FAITH
- **Meaning:** "Divine trust, Christ's power"
- **Context:** Christ's teaching that faith as small as a mustard seed can accomplish great things
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's power and His demonstration of faith's effectiveness
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual faith (Hebrews 11:1)

### GRATITUDE
- **Meaning:** "Divine thanksgiving, Christ's example"
- **Context:** Only one leper returned to give thanks, demonstrating true gratitude for God's mercy
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's example and His delight in grateful hearts
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual gratitude (1 Thessalonians 5:18)

### READINESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine preparedness, Christ's warning"
- **Context:** Christ's teaching on being ready for His return, using Lot's wife as a warning
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's warning and His call for spiritual readiness
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual readiness (Matthew 24:44)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "If ye had faith as a grain of mustard seed, ye might say unto this sycamine tree, Be thou plucked up by the root, and be thou planted in the sea"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any positive thinking or self-confidence
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's power - The faith represents trust in Christ's authority, not self-generated power to accomplish miracles.

### "The kingdom of God is within you"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any inner spirituality or self-realization
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's reign - The kingdom within represents Christ's rule in the hearts of believers, not human potential.
