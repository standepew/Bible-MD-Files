## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### PERSISTENCE
- **Meaning:** "Divine perseverance, Christ's teaching"
- **Context:** The persistent widow who continually comes to the unjust judge, teaching persistence in prayer
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's teaching and His call for persistent faith in prayer
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual persistence (Ephesians 6:18)

### HUMILITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine meekness, Christ's requirement"
- **Context:** The publican's humble prayer compared to the Pharisee's proud self-righteousness
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's requirement and His blessing on genuine humility
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual humility (James 4:6)

### CHILDLIKE FAITH
- **Meaning:** "Divine trust, Christ's example"
- **Context:** Christ's teaching that the kingdom belongs to those who receive it like little children
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's example and His call for childlike trust and dependence
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual childlike faith (Matthew 18:3)

### SACRIFICE
- **Meaning:** "Divine dedication, Christ's call"
- **Context:** The rich young ruler's unwillingness to sacrifice his riches for eternal treasure
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's call and His requirement for total sacrifice for the kingdom
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sacrifice (Romans 12:1)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "For it is easier for a camel to go through a needle's eye, than for a rich man to enter into the kingdom of God"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any difficulty or impossibility
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's teaching - The camel and needle represent the impossibility of salvation for those who trust in riches rather than God.

### "The things which are impossible with men are possible with God"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any human limitation or divine power
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's power - The impossibility refers specifically to salvation apart from God's grace, not general human limitations.
