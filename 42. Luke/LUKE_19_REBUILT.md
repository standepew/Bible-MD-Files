## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### TRANSFORMATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine change, Christ's salvation"
- **Context:** Zacchaeus's transformation from tax collector to generous giver after encountering Christ
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's salvation and His power to transform lives completely
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual transformation (2 Corinthians 5:17)

### STEWARDSHIP
- **Meaning:** "Divine responsibility, Christ's accountability"
- **Context:** The parable of the ten pounds showing faithful and unfaithful stewardship
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's accountability and His reward for faithful service
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual stewardship (1 Corinthians 4:2)

### WORSHIP
- **Meaning:** "Divine adoration, Christ's lordship"
- **Context:** The triumphal entry with crowds praising Christ as King
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's lordship and His rightful place as King
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual worship (Revelation 19:6)

### JUDGMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine justice, Christ's warning"
- **Context:** Christ's weeping over Jerusalem and warning of coming judgment
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's warning and His sorrow over unrepentant hearts
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual judgment (Matthew 23:37)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "For the Son of man is come to seek and to save that which was lost"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any lost items or missing persons
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's mission - The lost represents spiritually lost humanity, and the seeking represents Christ's redemptive purpose.

### "If these should hold their peace, the stones would immediately cry out"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any natural phenomena or creation's response
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's lordship - The stones represent creation's testimony to Christ's kingship when humans are silent.
