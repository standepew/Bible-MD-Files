## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### AUTHORITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine power, Christ's commission"
- **Context:** Christ's authority questioned by religious leaders, demonstrated through His wisdom
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's commission and His divine authority from God
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual authority (Matthew 21:23)

### REJECTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine scorn, Christ's suffering"
- **Context:** The rejection and killing of the beloved son in the parable, representing Christ
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's suffering and the world's rejection of God's messengers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual rejection (John 1:11)

### CORNERSTONE
- **Meaning:** "Divine foundation, Christ's supremacy"
- **Context:** Christ as the rejected stone who becomes the cornerstone of God's building
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's supremacy and His exaltation after rejection
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual cornerstone (Ephesians 2:20)

### RESURRECTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine victory, Christ's power"
- **Context:** Christ's teaching on resurrection, refuting the Sadducees' denial
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's power over death and the reality of eternal life
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual resurrection (1 Corinthians 15:52)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Render therefore unto Cæsar the things which be Cæsar's, and unto God the things which be God's"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any separation of church and state or political obligations
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's wisdom - The rendering represents proper distinction between earthly and spiritual obligations, not political separation.

### "For he is not a God of the dead, but of the living: for all live unto him"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any concept of life after death or spiritual existence
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's teaching - The living represents those who have eternal life through relationship with God, not mere biological existence.
