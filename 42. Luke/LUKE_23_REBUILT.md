## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### INNOCENCE
- **Meaning:** "Divine purity, Christ's righteousness"
- **Context:** Christ's innocence declared by Pilate, Herod, and the centurion
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's righteousness and His perfect sinlessness
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual innocence (2 Corinthians 5:21)

### FORGIVENESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine mercy, Christ's intercession"
- **Context:** Christ's prayer for forgiveness for His executioners
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's intercession and His boundless mercy
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual forgiveness (Acts 7:60)

### SALVATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine deliverance, Christ's promise"
- **Context:** Christ's promise of paradise to the repentant criminal
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's promise and His power to save
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual salvation (Ephesians 2:8)

### DEVOTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine dedication, Christ's following"
- **Context:** Joseph of Arimathæa's bold request for Christ's body
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's following and His call for courageous devotion
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual devotion (Romans 12:1)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Father, forgive them; for they know not what they do"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any forgiveness or excusing of wrongdoing
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's intercession - The forgiveness represents Christ's prayer for those executing Him, showing His mercy even in suffering.

### "Today shalt thou be with me in paradise"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any immediate entry into heaven or afterlife
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's promise - The paradise represents the blessed presence of Christ with the repentant sinner, showing His saving power.
