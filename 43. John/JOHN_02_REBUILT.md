## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### MIRACLE
- **Meaning:** "Divine power, Christ's authority"
- **Context:** Christ's first miracle of turning water into wine, demonstrating His divine power
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's authority and His power over creation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual miracle (2 Corinthians 5:17)

### TEMPLE
- **Meaning:** "Divine presence, Christ's body"
- **Context:** Christ's reference to His body as the temple and His cleansing of the physical temple
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's presence and His authority over true worship
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual temple (1 Corinthians 3:16)

### GLORY
- **Meaning:** "Divine manifestation, Christ's revelation"
- **Context:** Christ's glory manifested through miracles, revealing His divine nature
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's revelation and His visible demonstration of divinity
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual glory (2 Corinthians 3:18)

### RESURRECTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine victory, Christ's triumph"
- **Context:** Christ's prophecy of His resurrection in three days, pointing to His victory
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's triumph and His power over death
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual resurrection (Romans 6:4)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Destroy this temple, and in three days I will raise it up"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any rebuilding or restoration project
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's resurrection - The temple represents Christ's body, and the three days represent His death and resurrection.

### "Woman, what have I to do with thee? mine hour is not yet come"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any disrespect or disregard for family
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's divine timing - The hour represents God's perfect timing for Christ's redemptive work.
