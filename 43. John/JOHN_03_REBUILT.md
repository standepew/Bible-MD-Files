## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### NEW BIRTH
- **Meaning:** "Divine regeneration, Christ's salvation"
- **Context:** Being born again of water and Spirit to enter God's kingdom
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's salvation and the necessity of spiritual transformation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual regeneration (Titus 3:5)

### ETERNAL LIFE
- **Meaning:** "Divine immortality, Christ's gift"
- **Context:** Everlasting life through faith in Christ, God's love for the world
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's gift and His provision of eternal salvation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual immortality (1 John 5:11)

### BELIEF
- **Meaning:** "Divine faith, Christ's requirement"
- **Context:** Faith in Christ as the only way to avoid condemnation and gain eternal life
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's requirement and His exclusive path to salvation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual faith (Hebrews 11:1)

### WRATH
- **Meaning:** "Divine judgment, Christ's warning"
- **Context:** God's wrath abiding on those who reject Christ, the consequence of unbelief
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's warning and His revelation of divine justice
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual judgment (Romans 1:18)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "For God so loved the world, that he gave his only begotten Son"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any universal love or sentimental affection
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's sacrificial love - The love represents God's redemptive purpose in giving His Son for salvation.

### "Ye must be born again"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any moral improvement or religious experience
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's regeneration - The new birth represents the supernatural work of the Spirit in salvation.
