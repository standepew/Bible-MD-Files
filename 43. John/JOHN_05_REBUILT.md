## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### HEALING
- **Meaning:** "Divine restoration, Christ's power"
- **Context:** Christ's healing of the man at Bethesda, demonstrating His power over infirmity
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's power and His compassion for suffering humanity
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual healing (1 Peter 2:24)

### SABBATH
- **Meaning:** "Divine rest, Christ's authority"
- **Context:** Christ's authority over the Sabbath, showing His Lordship over religious tradition
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's authority and His supremacy over religious systems
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual rest (Hebrews 4:9)

### JUDGMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine justice, Christ's authority"
- **Context:** Christ's authority to judge, given by the Father, demonstrating His divine role
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's justice and His role as final judge
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual judgment (2 Corinthians 5:10)

### RESURRECTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine victory, Christ's power"
- **Context:** Christ's power to raise the dead and give eternal life, demonstrating His victory over death
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's victory and His power over mortality
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual resurrection (Romans 6:4)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "My Father worketh hitherto, and I work"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any work activity or labor
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's divine authority - The work represents Christ's equality with the Father in His divine activities and purposes.

### "Search the scriptures; for in them ye think ye have eternal life"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any intellectual study or academic pursuit
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's revelation in Scripture - The scriptures contain testimony about Christ that leads to eternal life.
