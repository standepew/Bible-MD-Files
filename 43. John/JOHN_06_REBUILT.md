## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### BREAD OF LIFE
- **Meaning:** "Divine sustenance, Christ's salvation"
- **Context:** Christ as the living bread that gives eternal life to those who believe
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's salvation and His provision of spiritual nourishment
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sustenance (1 Corinthians 10:3)

### ETERNAL LIFE
- **Meaning:** "Divine immortality, Christ's gift"
- **Context:** Everlasting life through faith in Christ, the ultimate gift from the Father
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's gift and His victory over death
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual immortality (1 John 5:11)

### FLESH AND BLOOD
- **Meaning:** "Divine sacrifice, Christ's atonement"
- **Context:** Christ's flesh and blood as the means of eternal life, representing His sacrifice
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's atonement and His provision of salvation through death
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual communion (1 Corinthians 11:24)

### SPIRITUAL DISCERNMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine understanding, Christ's revelation"
- **Context:** The need for spiritual discernment to understand Christ's teachings about Himself
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's revelation and His requirement for spiritual perception
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual wisdom (1 Corinthians 2:14)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "I am the bread of life: he that cometh to me shall never hunger; and he that believeth on me shall never thirst"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any physical nourishment or material satisfaction
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's spiritual provision - The bread represents Christ's complete salvation that satisfies eternally.

### "It is the spirit that quickeneth; the flesh profiteth nothing"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any anti-material or anti-physical philosophy
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's spiritual truth - The spirit represents the divine life that Christ gives, while flesh represents human limitation.
