## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### BLINDNESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine condition, Christ's healing"
- **Context:** Physical and spiritual blindness, demonstrating human need for Christ's healing
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's healing and His power over both physical and spiritual darkness
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual blindness (2 Corinthians 4:4)

### SIGHT
- **Meaning:** "Divine revelation, Christ's illumination"
- **Context:** Physical sight given to the blind man, representing spiritual illumination through Christ
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's revelation and His power to bring spiritual understanding
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sight (Ephesians 1:18)

### DISCIPLESHIP
- **Meaning:** "Divine following, Christ's calling"
- **Context:** The contrast between Christ's disciples and Moses' disciples, showing true allegiance
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's calling and His requirement for exclusive devotion
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual discipleship (Matthew 16:24)

### JUDGMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine justice, Christ's authority"
- **Context:** Christ's judgment that reveals true spiritual condition, exposing spiritual blindness
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's justice and His authority to discern spiritual reality
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual judgment (1 Corinthians 2:15)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "I must work the works of him that sent me, while it is day: the night cometh, when no man can work"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any work activity or human effort
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's divine mission - The works represent Christ's redemptive purpose and the limited time for His earthly ministry.

### "If ye were blind, ye should have no sin: but now ye say, We see; therefore your sin remaineth"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any ignorance or lack of knowledge
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's spiritual judgment - The blindness represents spiritual ignorance that would excuse sin, while claiming sight represents willful rejection.
