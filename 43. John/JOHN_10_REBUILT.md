## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### SHEPHERD
- **Meaning:** "Divine care, Christ's protection"
- **Context:** Christ as the good shepherd who lays down His life for the sheep
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's care and His sacrificial protection of His people
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual shepherd (1 Peter 2:25)

### DOOR
- **Meaning:** "Divine access, Christ's salvation"
- **Context:** Christ as the door through which sheep enter the fold and find salvation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's salvation and His exclusive role as mediator
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual access (John 14:6)

### ETERNAL LIFE
- **Meaning:** "Divine immortality, Christ's gift"
- **Context:** Eternal life given to Christ's sheep, with security in His hand
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's gift and His assurance of eternal security
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual immortality (1 John 5:11)

### UNITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine oneness, Christ's deity"
- **Context:** Christ's unity with the Father, demonstrating His divine nature
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's deity and His perfect relationship with the Father
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual unity (Philippians 2:6)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "I am the good shepherd: the good shepherd giveth his life for the sheep"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any leadership or pastoral care
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's sacrificial love - The shepherd represents Christ's complete self-giving for His people's salvation.

### "I and my Father are one"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any unity or agreement between people
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's divine nature - The oneness represents Christ's complete equality with the Father in deity.
