## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### DEATH
- **Meaning:** "Divine separation, Christ's victory"
- **Context:** Lazarus's death and Christ's power over death, demonstrating His authority
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's victory and His power over mortality
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual death (Romans 6:23)

### RESURRECTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine restoration, Christ's power"
- **Context:** Christ as the resurrection and life, demonstrating His power to give eternal life
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's power and His provision of eternal life
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual resurrection (1 Corinthians 15:52)

### GLORY
- **Meaning:** "Divine manifestation, Christ's revelation"
- **Context:** God's glory manifested through Lazarus's resurrection, revealing Christ's divine power
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's revelation and His demonstration of divine authority
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual glory (2 Corinthians 3:18)

### COUNCIL
- **Meaning:** "Divine opposition, Christ's rejection"
- **Context:** The council against Jesus, demonstrating human rejection of Christ's ministry
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's rejection and His facing of human opposition
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual opposition (Acts 5:36)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "I am the resurrection, and the life: he that believeth in me, though he were dead, yet shall live"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any physical resurrection or future hope
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's divine power - The resurrection represents Christ's complete authority over death and His provision of eternal life.

### "Jesus wept"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any human sorrow or grief
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's compassion - The tears represent Christ's perfect humanity and His compassion for human suffering.
