## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### VINE
- **Meaning:** "Divine source, Christ's provision"
- **Context:** Christ as the true vine that provides life and fruitfulness to believers
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's provision and His role as the source of spiritual life
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual source (Colossians 2:19)

### ABIDE
- **Meaning:** "Divine relationship, Christ's fellowship"
- **Context:** The believer's abiding in Christ, demonstrating intimate fellowship
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's fellowship and His requirement for spiritual relationship
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual fellowship (1 John 2:6)

### FRUIT
- **Meaning:** "Divine productivity, Christ's purpose"
- **Context:** Spiritual fruit produced through abiding in Christ, demonstrating spiritual effectiveness
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's purpose and His desire for spiritual productivity
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual fruit (Galatians 5:22)

### HATRED
- **Meaning:** "Divine rejection, Christ's suffering"
- **Context:** The world's hatred of believers, demonstrating rejection of Christ
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's suffering and His experience of worldly opposition
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual hatred (1 John 3:13)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "I am the true vine, and my Father is the husbandman"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any agricultural metaphor or natural growth
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's vital relationship - The vine represents Christ's essential role as the source of spiritual life.

### "Greater love hath no man than this, that a man lay down his life for his friends"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any human friendship or sentimental affection
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's sacrificial love - The laying down of life represents Christ's complete self-giving for His people.
