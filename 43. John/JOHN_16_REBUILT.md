## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### OFFENSE
- **Meaning:** "Divine persecution, Christ's warning"
- **Context:** The persecution believers will face for Christ's sake, including being put out of synagogues
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's warning and His preparation of believers for suffering
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual persecution (2 Timothy 3:12)

### COMFORTER
- **Meaning:** "Divine presence, Christ's provision"
- **Context:** The Holy Spirit as Christ's provision to guide believers into all truth
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's provision and His sending of the Spirit for guidance
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual guidance (Romans 8:14)

### SORROW
- **Meaning:** "Divine suffering, Christ's experience"
- **Context:** The sorrow of believers that will be turned to joy, like childbirth pain
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's suffering and His transformation of suffering into joy
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual suffering (2 Corinthians 4:17)

### VICTORY
- **Meaning:** "Divine triumph, Christ's conquest"
- **Context:** Christ's victory over the world, providing believers with confidence in tribulation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's triumph and His provision of spiritual victory
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual victory (1 John 5:4)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "In the world ye shall have tribulation: but be of good cheer; I have overcome the world"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any worldly difficulties or human struggles
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's spiritual victory - The overcoming represents Christ's complete conquest over the world system and its opposition.

### "A woman when she is in travail hath sorrow, because her hour is come: but as soon as she is delivered of the child, she remembereth no more the anguish, for joy that a man is born into the world"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any childbirth experience or natural pain
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's suffering and resurrection - The travail represents Christ's suffering that brings forth the joy of resurrection.
