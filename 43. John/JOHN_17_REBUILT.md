## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### GLORIFICATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine honor, Christ's exaltation"
- **Context:** Christ's prayer for glorification, demonstrating His divine honor and relationship with the Father
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's exaltation and His perfect relationship with the Father
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual glorification (Romans 8:30)

### ETERNAL LIFE
- **Meaning:** "Divine immortality, Christ's gift"
- **Context:** Eternal life as knowing the Father and Christ, demonstrating relational immortality
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's gift and His provision of eternal relationship
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual immortality (1 John 5:11)

### SANCTIFICATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine holiness, Christ's purification"
- **Context:** The sanctification of believers through truth, demonstrating spiritual purification
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's purification and His provision of spiritual holiness
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual holiness (2 Corinthians 7:1)

### UNITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine oneness, Christ's fellowship"
- **Context:** The unity of believers with Christ and the Father, demonstrating spiritual fellowship
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's fellowship and His desire for spiritual unity
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual unity (Ephesians 4:3)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "And this is life eternal, that they might know thee the only true God, and Jesus Christ, whom thou hast sent"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any intellectual knowledge or theological understanding
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's relational knowledge - The knowing represents intimate relationship with the Father and Christ.

### "Sanctify them through thy truth: thy word is truth"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any moral improvement or ethical behavior
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's spiritual purification - The sanctification represents spiritual holiness through the truth of God's word.
