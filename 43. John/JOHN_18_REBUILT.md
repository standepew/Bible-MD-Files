## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### BETRAYAL
- **Meaning:** "Divine rejection, Christ's suffering"
- **Context:** Judas's betrayal of Christ in the garden, demonstrating human rejection of Christ
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's suffering and His fulfillment of prophetic scripture
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual betrayal (2 Timothy 3:4)

### ARREST
- **Meaning:** "Divine submission, Christ's obedience"
- **Context:** Christ's voluntary arrest, demonstrating His submission to the Father's will
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's obedience and His acceptance of suffering
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual obedience (Philippians 2:8)

### DENIAL
- **Meaning:** "Divine testing, Christ's foreknowledge"
- **Context:** Peter's three denials, demonstrating human weakness and Christ's foreknowledge
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's foreknowledge and His understanding of human frailty
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual testing (1 Peter 5:8)

### TRUTH
- **Meaning:** "Divine reality, Christ's witness"
- **Context:** Christ's witness to truth before Pilate, demonstrating His divine mission
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's witness and His commitment to divine truth
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual truth (John 14:6)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "I am he"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any self-identification or personal declaration
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's divine identity - The "I am" represents Christ's divine nature and authority.

### "My kingdom is not of this world"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any political kingdom or earthly rule
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's spiritual kingdom - The kingdom represents Christ's divine authority that transcends earthly politics.
