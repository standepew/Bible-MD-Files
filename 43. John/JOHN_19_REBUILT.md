## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### SCOURGING
- **Meaning:** "Divine suffering, Christ's punishment"
- **Context:** Christ's scourging, demonstrating His suffering for humanity's sins
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's suffering and His bearing of divine punishment
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual suffering (1 Peter 2:24)

### CRUCIFIXION
- **Meaning:** "Divine sacrifice, Christ's atonement"
- **Context:** Christ's crucifixion, demonstrating His sacrificial death for humanity's redemption
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's sacrifice and His provision of eternal salvation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sacrifice (Hebrews 10:10)

### FINISHED
- **Meaning:** "Divine completion, Christ's victory"
- **Context:** Christ's declaration of completion, demonstrating His victory over sin and death
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's victory and His completion of redemptive work
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual completion (Revelation 21:6)

### BURIAL
- **Meaning:** "Divine honor, Christ's dignity"
- **Context:** Christ's honorable burial, demonstrating His dignity even in death
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's dignity and His fulfillment of prophetic scripture
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual burial (Romans 6:4)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "It is finished"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any completion or ending of a task
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's redemptive victory - The finished represents Christ's complete accomplishment of salvation work.

### "Behold thy son! Behold thy mother!"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any family relationship or care arrangement
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's provision - The care represents Christ's provision for His mother and His creation of a new spiritual family.
