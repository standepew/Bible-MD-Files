## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### RESURRECTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine victory, Christ's triumph"
- **Context:** Christ's resurrection from the dead, demonstrating His victory over death
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's triumph and His provision of eternal life
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual resurrection (1 Corinthians 15:52)

### APPEARANCE
- **Meaning:** "Divine presence, Christ's revelation"
- **Context:** Christ's post-resurrection appearances, demonstrating His continued presence
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's presence and His confirmation of resurrection
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual presence (Matthew 28:20)

### FAITH
- **Meaning:** "Divine trust, Christ's provision"
- **Context:** Faith in Christ's resurrection, demonstrating trust in His divine power
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's provision and His requirement for believing
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual faith (Hebrews 11:1)

### HOLY SPIRIT
- **Meaning:** "Divine presence, Christ's gift"
- **Context:** Christ's giving of the Holy Spirit, demonstrating His provision for believers
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's provision and His sending of the Spirit
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual presence (Acts 2:4)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Touch me not; for I am not yet ascended to my Father"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any physical contact or prohibition of touching
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's glorified state - The prohibition represents Christ's transition between earthly ministry and heavenly glorification.

### "Blessed are they that have not seen, and yet have believed"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any blind faith or unreasoned belief
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's commendation of faith - The blessedness represents Christ's approval of faith based on testimony rather than sight.
