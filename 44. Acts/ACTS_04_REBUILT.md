## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### PERSECUTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine testing, Christ's suffering"
- **Context:** The persecution of Peter and John, demonstrating opposition to Christ's work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's suffering and His call to endure persecution
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual persecution (2 Timothy 3:12)

### BOLDNESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine courage, Christ's empowerment"
- **Context:** The boldness of Peter and John, demonstrating Christ's empowerment for witness
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's empowerment and His provision of spiritual courage
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual boldness (Ephesians 6:19)

### UNITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine fellowship, Christ's body"
- **Context:** The unity of believers, demonstrating Christ's body on earth
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's body and His establishment of spiritual community
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual unity (Philippians 2:2)

### GENEROSITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine provision, Christ's abundance"
- **Context:** The generosity of believers, demonstrating Christ's provision through His people
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's provision and His call to share with others
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual generosity (2 Corinthians 9:7)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Neither is there salvation in any other: for there is none other name under heaven given among men, whereby we must be saved"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any religious exclusivity or intolerance
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's unique salvation - The name represents Christ's exclusive role as the only way of salvation.

### "Neither said any of them that ought of the things which he possessed was his own; but they had all things common"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any communism or forced redistribution of wealth
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's voluntary generosity - The commonality represents voluntary sharing out of love for Christ and fellow believers.
