## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### DECEPTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine judgment, Christ's holiness"
- **Context:** The deception of Ananias and Sapphira, demonstrating God's judgment on hypocrisy
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's holiness and His requirement for truth in the church
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual deception (2 Timothy 3:13)

### MIRACLES
- **Meaning:** "Divine power, Christ's authority"
- **Context:** The miracles of the apostles, demonstrating Christ's continued power through His servants
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's authority and His provision for healing and deliverance
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual miracles (1 Corinthians 12:10)

### PERSECUTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine testing, Christ's suffering"
- **Context:** The persecution of the apostles, demonstrating opposition to Christ's work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's suffering and His call to endure persecution
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual persecution (2 Timothy 3:12)

### WISDOM
- **Meaning:** "Divine counsel, Christ's guidance"
- **Context:** Gamaliel's wisdom, demonstrating Christ's provision for wise counsel
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's guidance and His use of human wisdom for divine purposes
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual wisdom (James 1:5)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Thou hast not lied unto men, but unto God"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any dishonesty or lying in general
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's holiness in the church - The lying represents deception against the Holy Spirit and the church's purity.

### "We ought to obey God rather than men"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any civil disobedience or rebellion against authority
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's supreme authority - The obedience represents Christ's authority over human authority when they conflict.
