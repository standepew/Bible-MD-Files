## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### CONVERSION
- **Meaning:** "Divine transformation, Christ's salvation"
- **Context:** Saul's conversion on the road to Damascus, demonstrating Christ's power to transform
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's salvation and His ability to change the hardest hearts
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual conversion (2 Corinthians 5:17)

### PERSECUTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine testing, Christ's suffering"
- **Context:** Saul's persecution of the church, demonstrating opposition to Christ's work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's suffering and His use of persecution for divine purposes
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual persecution (2 Timothy 3:12)

### HEALING
- **Meaning:** "Divine restoration, Christ's power"
- **Context:** Peter's healing of Æneas and Dorcas, demonstrating Christ's power to restore
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's power and His compassion for the suffering
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual healing (1 Peter 2:24)

### SERVICE
- **Meaning:** "Divine ministry, Christ's compassion"
- **Context:** Dorcas's service to the widows, demonstrating Christ's compassion through service
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's compassion and His call to serve others
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual service (Galatians 5:13)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "It is hard for thee to kick against the pricks"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any stubbornness or resistance in general
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's divine authority - The pricks represent God's divine discipline that cannot be resisted.

### "He is a chosen vessel unto me, to bear my name before the Gentiles, and kings, and the children of Israel"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any special calling or ministry
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's sovereign choice - The vessel represents Christ's divine selection and purpose for Saul's life.
