## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### DISCIPLESHIP
- **Meaning:** "Divine calling, Christ's training"
- **Context:** Timothy joining Paul, demonstrating Christ's calling and training of servants
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's calling and His preparation of workers for the harvest
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual discipleship (2 Timothy 2:15)

### GUIDANCE
- **Meaning:** "Divine direction, Christ's leading"
- **Context:** The Macedonian call, demonstrating Christ's guidance for ministry
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's leading and His direction for missionary work
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual guidance (John 16:13)

### SALVATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine deliverance, Christ's redemption"
- **Context:** Lydia's conversion and the jailer's salvation, demonstrating Christ's salvation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's redemption and His power to save all people
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual salvation (Ephesians 2:8)

### PERSECUTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine testing, Christ's suffering"
- **Context:** Paul and Silas in prison, demonstrating persecution for Christ's sake
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's suffering and His victory through persecution
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual persecution (2 Timothy 3:12)

* **Christ's prison:* Perfect suffering (Acts 5:19)
* **Eternal prison:* Complete testing (Revelation 22)

### "Believe on the Lord Jesus Christ, and thou shalt be saved"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any intellectual belief or mental assent
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's exclusive salvation - The belief represents complete trust in Christ's person and work for salvation.

### "Do thyself no harm: for we are all here"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any self-preservation or safety concern
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's protection and sovereignty - The harm represents the futility of opposing God's will and His protection of His servants.
