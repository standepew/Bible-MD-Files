- **Significance:** Model of Christ's message and His call to proclaim the gospel
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual proclamation (Romans 1:16)

### NOBILITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine character, Christ's excellence"
- **Context:** The Bereans' noble character in receiving and searching the word

### PHILOSOPHY
- **Meaning:** "Divine wisdom, Christ's truth"
- **Context:** Paul's engagement with Greek philosophers, contrasting human wisdom with divine truth
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's truth and His superiority over human philosophy
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual wisdom (1 Corinthians 1:24)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "These that have turned the world upside down"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any social or political revolution
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's revolutionary gospel - The world turned upside down represents the transformative power of Christ's kingdom that reverses worldly values.

### "For we are also his offspring"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any universal fatherhood of God or human potential
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's unique relationship with God - The offspring represents being created in God's image through Christ and belonging to Him as His children.
