- **Context:** Disciples receiving the Holy Spirit, demonstrating Christ's full salvation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's fullness and His complete provision for believers

- **Context:** Paul's miracles and casting out evil spirits, demonstrating Christ's authority
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's power and His authority over spiritual forces

- **Significance:** Model of Christ's renewal and His power to transform lives

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Jesus I know, and Paul I know; but who are ye?"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any personal recognition or fame
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's exclusive authority - The knowing represents recognition of Christ's true authority and Paul's genuine calling, contrasting with false attempts to use Christ's name.

### "Great is Diana of the Ephesians"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any cultural pride or religious devotion
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's supremacy over idols - The greatness represents the empty worship of false gods that Christ came to replace with true worship.
