## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### RESURRECTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine restoration, Christ's power"
- **Context:** Eutychus raised from the dead, demonstrating Christ's power over death
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's power and His authority to give life
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual resurrection (Romans 6:4)

### LEADERSHIP
- **Meaning:** "Divine stewardship, Christ's shepherding"
- **Context:** Paul's farewell to elders, demonstrating Christ's model of leadership
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's shepherding and His call to faithful leadership
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual leadership (1 Peter 5:2)

### SACRIFICE
- **Meaning:** "Divine offering, Christ's selflessness"
- **Context:** Paul's willingness to suffer for Christ, demonstrating Christ's sacrifice
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's selflessness and His call to sacrificial service
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sacrifice (Romans 12:1)

### GENEROSITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine giving, Christ's provision"
- **Context:** Paul's example of working to support others, demonstrating Christ's generosity
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's provision and His call to generous living
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual generosity (2 Corinthians 9:7)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "It is more blessed to give than to receive"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any charitable giving or social welfare
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's selfless service - The giving represents Christ's example of sacrificial service and the blessing that comes from following His example of selflessness.

### "I am pure from the blood of all men"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any personal innocence or moral perfection
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's faithfulness in ministry - The blood represents Paul's faithful proclamation of the gospel, making him innocent of the blood of those who reject Christ's message.
