## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### OBEDIENCE
- **Meaning:** "Divine submission, Christ's surrender"
- **Context:** Paul going to Jerusalem despite prophecy, demonstrating Christ's submission to God's will
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's surrender and His call to sacrificial obedience
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual obedience (Philippians 2:8)

### PROPHECY
- **Meaning:** "Divine revelation, Christ's foreknowledge"
- **Context:** Agabus prophesying Paul's arrest, demonstrating Christ's foreknowledge of suffering
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's foreknowledge and His preparation of His servants
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual prophecy (Revelation 19:10)

### PERSEVERANCE
- **Meaning:** "Divine endurance, Christ's steadfastness"
- **Context:** Paul's willingness to die for Christ, demonstrating Christ's steadfastness in suffering
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's steadfastness and His call to endure persecution
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual perseverance (Hebrews 12:1)

### TESTIMONY
- **Meaning:** "Divine witness, Christ's proclamation"
- **Context:** Paul speaking to the crowd, demonstrating Christ's witness before the world
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's witness and His call to testify boldly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual testimony (1 Peter 3:15)

2 And finding a ship sailing over unto Phenicia, we went aboard, and set forth.  

3 Now when we had discovered Cyprus, we left it on the left hand, and sailed into Syria, and landed at Tyre: for there the ship was to unlade her burden.

5 And when we had accomplished those days, we departed and went our way; and they all brought us on our way, with wives and children, till we were out of the city: and we kneeled down on the shore, and prayed.  

6 And when we had taken our leave one of another, we took ship; and they returned home again.  

7 And when we had finished our course from Tyre, we came to Ptolemais, and saluted the brethren, and abode with them one day.  

8 And the next day we that were of Paul's company departed, and came unto Cæsarea: and we entered into the house of Philip the evangelist, which was one of the seven; and abode with him.  

9 And the same man had four daughters, virgins, which did prophesy.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "I am ready not to be bound only, but also to die at Jerusalem for the name of the Lord Jesus"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any personal courage or martyrdom complex
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's sacrificial love - The readiness represents Christ's willingness to suffer and die for the salvation of others, not personal glory.

### "Art not thou that Egyptian, which before these days madest an uproar"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any mistaken identity or confusion about Paul
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's true identity - The Egyptian represents the world's failure to recognize Christ's true nature and mission.
