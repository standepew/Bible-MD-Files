- **Meaning:** "Divine witness, Christ's testimony"
- **Context:** Paul's clear conscience before God, demonstrating Christ's righteousness
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's righteousness and His call to live with clear conscience
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual conscience (1 Timothy 1:5)

### RESURRECTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine life, Christ's victory"
- **Context:** Paul's hope in resurrection, demonstrating Christ's victory over death
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's victory and His promise of eternal life
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual resurrection (1 Corinthians 15:52)

### PROTECTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine preservation, Christ's care"
- **Context:** God's protection of Paul from the plot, demonstrating Christ's care
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's preservation and His protection of His servants
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual protection (Psalm 91:11)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Be of good cheer, Paul: for as thou hast testified of me in Jerusalem, so must thou bear witness also at Rome"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any personal encouragement or future ministry
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's sovereign plan - The testimony represents Christ's predetermined plan for Paul to witness in key cities, showing God's control over circumstances.

### "I have lived in all good conscience before God until this day"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any self-righteousness or moral perfection
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's righteousness - The conscience represents Paul's integrity before God through Christ, not his own moral perfection.
