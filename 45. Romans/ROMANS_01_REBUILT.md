## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### GOSPEL
- **Meaning:** "Divine good news, Christ's salvation"
- **Context:** Paul's separation unto the gospel of God, demonstrating Christ's redemptive message
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's salvation and His power to save all who believe
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual gospel (1 Corinthians 15:1)

### RIGHTEOUSNESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine justification, Christ's righteousness"
- **Context:** God's righteousness revealed from faith to faith, demonstrating Christ's justifying work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's righteousness and His power to justify sinners
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual righteousness (2 Corinthians 5:21)

### FAITH
- **Meaning:** "Divine trust, Christ's gift"
- **Context:** The just shall live by faith, demonstrating Christ's requirement for salvation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's faith and His call to trust in His redemptive work
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual faith (Ephesians 2:8)

### WRATH
- **Meaning:** "Divine judgment, Christ's justice"
- **Context:** God's wrath against ungodliness and unrighteousness, demonstrating Christ's justice
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's justice and His judgment against sin
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual wrath (John 3:36)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "The just shall live by faith"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any religious faith or human belief system
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's righteousness - The faith represents trust in Christ's redemptive work, not human religious effort.

### "I am not ashamed of the gospel of Christ"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any confidence in religious beliefs or personal convictions
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's power - The gospel represents the divine power of God for salvation through Christ alone.
