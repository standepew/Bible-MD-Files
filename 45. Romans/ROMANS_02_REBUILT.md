## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### JUDGMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine justice, Christ's righteousness"
- **Context:** God's righteous judgment according to truth, demonstrating Christ's just evaluation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's justice and His righteous judgment of all people
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual judgment (2 Corinthians 5:10)

### REPENTANCE
- **Meaning:** "Divine turning, Christ's transformation"
- **Context:** God's goodness leading to repentance, demonstrating Christ's call to turn from sin
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's transformation and His call to spiritual renewal
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual repentance (2 Corinthians 7:10)

### CIRCUMCISION
- **Meaning:** "Divine covenant, Christ's righteousness"
- **Context:** True circumcision of the heart, demonstrating Christ's spiritual covenant
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's covenant and His spiritual transformation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual circumcision (Colossians 2:11)

### CONSCIENCE
- **Meaning:** "Divine witness, Christ's testimony"
- **Context:** The law written in hearts with conscience bearing witness, demonstrating Christ's internal testimony
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's internal witness and His work in the human heart
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual conscience (1 Timothy 1:5)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Judge not, that ye be not judged"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any tolerance of sin or moral relativism
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's call to righteous judgment - The judgment represents self-condemnation when judging others for the same sins.

### "For there is no respect of persons with God"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any human equality or social justice
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's impartial justice - The lack of respect of persons represents God's equal justice for all, regardless of status.
