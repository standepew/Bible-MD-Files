## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### ORACLES
- **Meaning:** "Divine revelation, Christ's word"
- **Context:** The oracles of God committed to the Jews, demonstrating Christ's revelation through His people
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's revelation and His communication of divine truth
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual oracles (Hebrews 5:12)

### RIGHTEOUSNESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine justification, Christ's righteousness"
- **Context:** The righteousness of God by faith in Jesus Christ, demonstrating Christ's justifying work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's righteousness and His power to justify sinners
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual righteousness (2 Corinthians 5:21)

### JUSTIFICATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine declaration, Christ's salvation"
- **Context:** Justification by faith without deeds of the law, demonstrating Christ's saving work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's salvation and His declaration of righteousness
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual justification (Galatians 2:16)

### PROPITIATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine sacrifice, Christ's atonement"
- **Context:** Christ as propitiation through faith in His blood, demonstrating Christ's atoning work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's atonement and His satisfaction of divine justice
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual propitiation (1 John 2:2)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "There is none righteous, no, not one"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any human moral failure or ethical shortcomings
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's universal need for salvation - The lack of righteousness represents the universal condition that requires Christ's redemptive work.

### "For all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any human imperfection or moral weakness
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's glory and our need for His righteousness - The coming short represents the gap between human sinfulness and Christ's perfect glory.
