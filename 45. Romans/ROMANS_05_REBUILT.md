## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### PEACE
- **Meaning:** "Divine reconciliation, Christ's restoration"
- **Context:** Peace with God through justification by faith, demonstrating Christ's reconciling work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's reconciliation and His restoration of our relationship with God
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual peace (Ephesians 2:14)

### HOPE
- **Meaning:** "Divine confidence, Christ's promise"
- **Context:** Hope in the glory of God that does not shame, demonstrating Christ's faithful promises
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's promise and His faithful fulfillment of our hope
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual hope (1 Peter 1:3)

### RECONCILIATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine restoration, Christ's atonement"
- **Context:** Reconciliation to God through Christ's death, demonstrating Christ's atoning work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's atonement and His restoration of our relationship with God
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual reconciliation (2 Corinthians 5:18)

### GRACE
- **Meaning:** "Divine favor, Christ's gift"
- **Context:** Grace that abounds where sin abounded, demonstrating Christ's abundant provision
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's grace and His abundant provision for our salvation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual grace (Ephesians 2:8)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "We glory in tribulations also"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any human suffering or earthly trials
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's redemptive purpose in suffering - The tribulation represents the process through which Christ produces spiritual maturity.

### "Where sin abounded, grace did much more abound"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any moral failure or human weakness
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's overwhelming grace - The grace represents Christ's redemptive power that far exceeds human sinfulness.
