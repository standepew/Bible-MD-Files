## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### BAPTISM
- **Meaning:** "Divine identification, Christ's death and resurrection"
- **Context:** Baptism into Christ's death and resurrection, demonstrating Christ's identification with believers
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's identification and His death and resurrection applied to believers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual baptism (Colossians 2:12)

### NEWNESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine transformation, Christ's resurrection life"
- **Context:** Newness of life through Christ's resurrection, demonstrating Christ's transformative power
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's transformation and His resurrection life in believers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual newness (2 Corinthians 5:17)

### FREEDOM
- **Meaning:** "Divine liberation, Christ's deliverance"
- **Context:** Freedom from sin through Christ's death, demonstrating Christ's liberating work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's liberation and His deliverance from sin's power
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual freedom (Galatians 5:1)

### SERVICE
- **Meaning:** "Divine dedication, Christ's lordship"
- **Context:** Service to righteousness instead of sin, demonstrating Christ's lordship over believers
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's lordship and His call to dedicated service
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual service (1 Peter 2:16)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Shall we continue in sin, that grace may abound?"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any license to sin or moral permissiveness
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's victory over sin - The question represents a misunderstanding of grace that leads to holy living, not sinful living.

### "The wages of sin is death; but the gift of God is eternal life"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any moral consequences or divine punishment
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's substitutionary death - The wages represent what Christ paid, and the gift represents what Christ provides.
