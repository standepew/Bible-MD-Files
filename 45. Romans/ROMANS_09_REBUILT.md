## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### ELECTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine choice, Christ's selection"
- **Context:** God's sovereign election according to His purpose, demonstrating Christ's divine selection
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's selection and His sovereign purpose in salvation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual election (Ephesians 1:4)

### MERCY
- **Meaning:** "Divine compassion, Christ's grace"
- **Context:** God's mercy on whom He will have mercy, demonstrating Christ's compassionate grace
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's compassion and His gracious mercy toward sinners
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual mercy (Ephesians 2:4)

### SOVEREIGNTY
- **Meaning:** "Divine authority, Christ's lordship"
- **Context:** God's sovereign power over creation and humanity, demonstrating Christ's absolute lordship
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's lordship and His absolute authority over all things
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sovereignty (Colossians 1:16)

### REMNANT
- **Meaning:** "Divine preservation, Christ's faithfulness"
- **Context:** The remnant of Israel saved by God's grace, demonstrating Christ's faithful preservation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's faithfulness and His preservation of His people
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual remnant (Romans 11:5)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Jacob have I loved, but Esau have I hated"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any human favoritism or arbitrary preference
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's sovereign choice in salvation - The love and hate represent God's sovereign election according to His redemptive purpose.

### "Not of works, but of him that calleth"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any passivity or lack of human responsibility
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's initiating grace - The calling represents God's sovereign initiative that precedes any human response.
