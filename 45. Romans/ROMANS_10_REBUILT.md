## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### RIGHTEOUSNESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine justification, Christ's righteousness"
- **Context:** God's righteousness versus human righteousness, demonstrating Christ's justifying work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's righteousness and His power to justify sinners
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual righteousness (2 Corinthians 5:21)

### FAITH
- **Meaning:** "Divine trust, Christ's gift"
- **Context:** Faith that comes by hearing the word of God, demonstrating Christ's requirement for salvation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's faith and His call to trust in His redemptive work
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual faith (Ephesians 2:8)

### SALVATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine deliverance, Christ's redemption"
- **Context:** Salvation through confession and belief in Christ, demonstrating Christ's saving work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's salvation and His deliverance from sin and death
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual salvation (Acts 16:31)

### GOSPEL
- **Meaning:** "Divine good news, Christ's message"
- **Context:** The gospel of peace preached by beautiful feet, demonstrating Christ's redemptive message
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's message and His call to proclaim the good news
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual gospel (Romans 1:16)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Christ is the end of the law"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any legal system or moral code being abolished
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's fulfillment of the law - The end represents the completion and fulfillment of the law's purpose in Christ.

### "Faith cometh by hearing, and hearing by the word of God"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any human learning or intellectual understanding
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's revelation - The hearing represents spiritual reception of Christ's redemptive message through His word.
