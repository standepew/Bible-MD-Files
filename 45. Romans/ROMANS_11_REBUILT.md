## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### REMNANT
- **Meaning:** "Divine preservation, Christ's faithfulness"
- **Context:** The remnant according to election of grace, demonstrating Christ's faithful preservation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's faithfulness and His preservation of His people
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual remnant (Romans 11:5)

### ELECTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine choice, Christ's selection"
- **Context:** Election of grace that obtained what Israel sought, demonstrating Christ's sovereign selection
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's selection and His sovereign purpose in salvation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual election (Ephesians 1:4)

### OLIVE TREE
- **Meaning:** "Divine covenant, Christ's people"
- **Context:** The olive tree representing God's people with natural and wild branches, demonstrating Christ's covenant people
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's people and His covenant relationship with them
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual olive tree (Jeremiah 11:16)

### MYSTERY
- **Meaning:** "Divine revelation, Christ's truth"
- **Context:** The mystery of Israel's partial blindness until Gentiles come in, demonstrating Christ's redemptive plan
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's truth and His revelation of divine purposes
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual mystery (Ephesians 3:4)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "God hath not cast away his people"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any human rejection or abandonment
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's faithfulness to Israel - The casting away represents God's temporary discipline, not permanent rejection, in His redemptive plan.

### "All Israel shall be saved"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any national salvation or political restoration
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's redemptive purpose - The salvation represents spiritual deliverance through Christ, not earthly restoration.
