## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### SACRIFICE
- **Meaning:** "Divine offering, Christ's atonement"
- **Context:** Living sacrifice holy and acceptable to God, demonstrating Christ's atoning work applied to believers
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's atonement and His call to holy living
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sacrifice (1 Peter 2:5)

### TRANSFORMATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine change, Christ's renewal"
- **Context:** Transformation by renewing of the mind, demonstrating Christ's renewing power
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's renewal and His transformative work in believers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual transformation (2 Corinthians 5:17)

### GIFTS
- **Meaning:** "Divine enablement, Christ's empowerment"
- **Context:** Spiritual gifts differing according to grace, demonstrating Christ's empowerment for service
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's empowerment and His equipping of believers for ministry
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual gifts (1 Corinthians 12:4)

### LOVE
- **Meaning:** "Divine affection, Christ's compassion"
- **Context:** Love without dissimulation and brotherly love, demonstrating Christ's compassionate character
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's compassion and His call to genuine love
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual love (1 John 4:8)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 I beseech you therefore, brethren, by the mercies of God, that ye present your bodies a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable unto God, which is your reasonable service.  

2 And be not conformed to this world: but be ye transformed by the renewing of your mind, that ye may prove what is that good, and acceptable, and perfect, will of God.  

3 For I say, through the grace given unto me, to every man that is among you, not to think of himself more highly than he ought to think; but to think soberly, according as God hath dealt to every man the measure of faith.  

4 For as we have many members in one body, and all members have not the same office:

8 Or he that exhorteth, on exhortation: he that giveth, let him do it with simplicity; he that ruleth, with diligence; he that sheweth mercy, with cheerfulness.  

9 Let love be without dissimulation. Abhor that which is evil; cleave to that which is good.  

10 Be kindly affectioned one to another with brotherly love; in honour preferring one another;  

11 Not slothful in business; fervent in spirit; serving the Lord;  

12 Rejoicing in hope; patient in tribulation; continuing instant in prayer;  

13 Distributing to the necessity of saints; given to hospitality.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Be not conformed to this world"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any cultural avoidance or social withdrawal
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's transformation - The conformity represents worldly patterns that Christ transforms through renewed minds.

### "Overcome evil with good"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any positive thinking or human effort
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's victory through love - The overcoming represents Christ's triumph that works through active good toward enemies.
