## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### AUTHORITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine order, Christ's lordship"
- **Context:** Submission to higher powers ordained by God, demonstrating Christ's divine order
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's lordship and His establishment of divine authority
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual authority (1 Peter 2:13)

### LOVE
- **Meaning:** "Divine affection, Christ's compassion"
- **Context:** Love that fulfills the law and works no ill to neighbor, demonstrating Christ's compassionate character
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's compassion and His fulfillment of the law through love
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual love (Galatians 5:14)

### LIGHT
- **Meaning:** "Divine truth, Christ's righteousness"
- **Context:** The armor of light and walking honestly in the day, demonstrating Christ's righteous character
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's righteousness and His call to holy living
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual light (1 Thessalonians 5:5)

### CHRIST
- **Meaning:** "Divine Savior, Christ's lordship"
- **Context:** Putting on the Lord Jesus Christ, demonstrating Christ's transformative presence
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's transformation and His indwelling presence in believers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual Christ (Galatians 3:27)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Let every soul be subject unto the higher powers"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any political allegiance or blind obedience
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's divine order - The submission represents recognition of God's sovereign establishment of authority for His purposes.

### "Love is the fulfilling of the law"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any human affection or emotional feeling
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's righteousness - The love represents Christ's character that perfectly fulfills God's moral law.
