## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### FAITH
- **Meaning:** "Divine trust, Christ's gift"
- **Context:** Weak and strong faith regarding liberty and conscience, demonstrating Christ's gift of trust
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's faith and His call to trust in His redemptive work
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual faith (Hebrews 11:6)

### CONSCIENCE
- **Meaning:** "Divine witness, Christ's guidance"
- **Context:** Conscience regarding what is clean and unclean, demonstrating Christ's internal guidance
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's guidance and His work in the human conscience
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual conscience (1 Timothy 1:5)

### LIBERTY
- **Meaning:** "Divine freedom, Christ's redemption"
- **Context:** Liberty in Christ that considers weaker brothers, demonstrating Christ's redemptive freedom
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's freedom and His call to loving consideration
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual liberty (Galatians 5:1)

### KINGDOM
- **Meaning:** "Divine reign, Christ's rule"
- **Context:** The kingdom of God characterized by righteousness, peace, and joy, demonstrating Christ's reign
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's reign and His kingdom values
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual kingdom (Matthew 6:33)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "The kingdom of God is not meat and drink"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any religious practices or external rituals
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's kingdom values - The kingdom represents spiritual realities of righteousness, peace, and joy in the Holy Spirit, not external observances.

### "Whatsoever is not of faith is sin"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any doubt or uncertainty
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's call to trust - The faith represents confident trust in Christ's redemptive work, not human certainty.
