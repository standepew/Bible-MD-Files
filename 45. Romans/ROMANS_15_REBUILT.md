## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### STRENGTH
- **Meaning:** "Divine enablement, Christ's power"
- **Context:** Strong believers bearing with the weak, demonstrating Christ's enabling power used for others
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's power and His call to use strength for service
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual strength (2 Corinthians 12:9)

### UNITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine oneness, Christ's fellowship"
- **Context:** Unity in worship with one mind and mouth, demonstrating Christ's unifying presence
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's fellowship and His power to unite believers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual unity (Ephesians 4:3)

### MINISTRY
- **Meaning:** "Divine service, Christ's mission"
- **Context:** Paul's ministry to the Gentiles confirming God's promises, demonstrating Christ's mission
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's mission and His continuation through servants
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual ministry (Ephesians 3:7)

### HOPE
- **Meaning:** "Divine confidence, Christ's promise"
- **Context:** Hope through patience and comfort of scriptures, demonstrating Christ's faithful promises
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's promise and His faithful fulfillment of hope
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual hope (Romans 15:13)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "We then that are strong ought to bear the infirmities of the weak"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any human weakness or social welfare
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's compassion - The bearing represents Christ's call to use spiritual strength to support weaker believers.

### "The God of hope fill you with all joy and peace"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any positive emotions or worldly happiness
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's spiritual blessings - The joy and peace represent spiritual fruit through the Holy Spirit's power.
