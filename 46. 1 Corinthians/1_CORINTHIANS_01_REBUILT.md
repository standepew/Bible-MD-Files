## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### SANCTIFICATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine holiness, Christ's righteousness"
- **Context:** Sanctified in Christ Jesus and made sanctification by God, demonstrating Christ's holy character
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's holiness and His power to make believers holy
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sanctification (2 Thessalonians 2:13)

### WISDOM
- **Meaning:** "Divine understanding, Christ's knowledge"
- **Context:** Christ as the wisdom of God made unto us, demonstrating Christ's divine understanding
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's knowledge and His power to give true wisdom
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual wisdom (Colossians 2:3)

### RIGHTEOUSNESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine justification, Christ's righteousness"
- **Context:** Christ made unto us righteousness from God, demonstrating Christ's justifying work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's righteousness and His power to justify sinners
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual righteousness (2 Corinthians 5:21)

### REDEMPTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine deliverance, Christ's salvation"
- **Context:** Christ made unto us redemption from God, demonstrating Christ's saving work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's salvation and His power to deliver from sin
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual redemption (Ephesians 1:7)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "I am of Paul; and I of Apollos; and I of Cephas; and I of Christ"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any church leadership or denominational loyalty
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's exclusive lordship - The divisions represent factionalism that denies Christ's unity as the only foundation.

### "God hath chosen the foolish things of the world"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any human foolishness or lack of education
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's counter-cultural wisdom - The foolishness represents God's wisdom that confounds human wisdom through the cross.
