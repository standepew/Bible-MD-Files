## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### WISDOM
- **Meaning:** "Divine understanding, Christ's knowledge"
- **Context:** The wisdom of God in a mystery, hidden from the world, demonstrating Christ's divine knowledge
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's knowledge and His power to reveal divine wisdom
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual wisdom (Colossians 2:3)

### SPIRIT
- **Meaning:** "Divine presence, Christ's indwelling"
- **Context:** The Spirit of God revealing deep things, demonstrating Christ's indwelling presence
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's presence and His power to reveal spiritual truth
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual Spirit (John 16:13)

### MYSTERY
- **Meaning:** "Divine revelation, Christ's truth"
- **Context:** The hidden wisdom of God in a mystery, demonstrating Christ's revealed truth
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's truth and His power to reveal divine purposes
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual mystery (Ephesians 3:4)

### DISCERNMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine perception, Christ's understanding"
- **Context:** Spiritual discernment of the things of God, demonstrating Christ's perceptive power
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's understanding and His power to give spiritual insight
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual discernment (Hebrews 5:14)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "I determined not to know any thing among you, save Jesus Christ, and him crucified"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any ignorance or lack of knowledge
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's exclusive centrality - The determination represents complete focus on Christ's crucifixion as the only foundation of knowledge.

### "The natural man receiveth not the things of the Spirit of God"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any human inability or lack of intelligence
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's spiritual revelation - The natural man represents human wisdom that cannot receive divine truth without the Spirit.
