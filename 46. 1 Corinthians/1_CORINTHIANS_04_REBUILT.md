## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### STEWARD
- **Meaning:** "Divine responsibility, Christ's service"
- **Context:** Stewards of the mysteries of God required to be faithful, demonstrating Christ's faithful service
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's service and His call to faithful responsibility
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual steward (Luke 12:42)

### JUDGMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine evaluation, Christ's assessment"
- **Context:** The Lord's judgment that brings hidden things to light, demonstrating Christ's righteous assessment
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's assessment and His power to evaluate all things
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual judgment (2 Corinthians 5:10)

### SPECTACLE
- **Meaning:** "Divine witness, Christ's testimony"
- **Context:** Apostles made a spectacle to the world and angels, demonstrating Christ's public testimony
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's testimony and His call to public witness
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual spectacle (Hebrews 12:1)

### KINGDOM
- **Meaning:** "Divine reign, Christ's rule"
- **Context:** The kingdom of God in power rather than word, demonstrating Christ's powerful reign
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's reign and His power to establish His kingdom
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual kingdom (Romans 14:17)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "I judge not mine own self"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any lack of self-examination or moral relativism
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's ultimate authority - The judgment represents deferring to Christ's final assessment rather than human self-justification.

### "We are fools for Christ's sake"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any foolishness or lack of intelligence
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's counter-cultural wisdom - The foolishness represents the world's perception of apostolic ministry that contradicts human wisdom.
