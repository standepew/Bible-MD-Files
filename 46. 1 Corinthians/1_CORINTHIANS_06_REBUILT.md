## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### JUDGMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine authority, Christ's rule"
- **Context:** Saints judging the world and angels, demonstrating Christ's authority given to believers
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's rule and His power to give believers authority
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual judgment (Matthew 19:28)

### UNRIGHTEOUS
- **Meaning:** "Divine corruption, Christ's righteousness"
- **Context:** The unrighteous who will not inherit God's kingdom, demonstrating Christ's righteous standard
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's righteousness and His power to transform sinners
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual unrighteousness (Galatians 5:19)

### SANCTIFICATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine holiness, Christ's purity"
- **Context:** Believers washed, sanctified, and justified in Christ, demonstrating Christ's purifying work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's purity and His power to make believers holy
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sanctification (Acts 22:16)

### TEMPLE
- **Meaning:** "Divine dwelling, Christ's presence"
- **Context:** The body as the temple of the Holy Ghost, demonstrating Christ's indwelling presence
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's presence and His indwelling in believers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual temple (1 Corinthians 3:16)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "All things are lawful unto me, but all things are not expedient"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any moral relativism or license to sin
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's freedom - The lawful represents freedom in Christ, while not expedient means not all things are beneficial or constructive for spiritual growth.

### "Flee fornication"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any sexual sin or immorality
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's call to purity - The fleeing represents decisive action against sexual immorality which particularly affects the body as Christ's temple.
