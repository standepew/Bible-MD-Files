## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### KNOWLEDGE
- **Meaning:** "Divine understanding, Christ's wisdom"
- **Context:** Knowledge that puffs up versus love that builds up, demonstrating Christ's true wisdom
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's wisdom and His power to give true understanding
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual knowledge (1 John 4:8)

### CHARITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine love, Christ's compassion"
- **Context:** Charity that edifies versus knowledge that puffs up, demonstrating Christ's building love
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's compassion and His power to build up believers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual charity (James 1:17)

### CONSCIENCE
- **Meaning:** "Divine witness, Christ's guidance"
- **Context:** Weak conscience defiled by eating idol meat, demonstrating Christ's internal guidance
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's guidance and His work in the human conscience
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual conscience (Romans 14:2)

### LIBERTY
- **Meaning:** "Divine freedom, Christ's redemption"
- **Context:** Liberty in Christ that must not become a stumbling block, demonstrating Christ's responsible freedom
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's freedom and His call to loving consideration
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual liberty (Colossians 2:16)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 Now as touching things offered unto idols, we know that we all have knowledge. Knowledge puffeth up, but charity edifieth.  

2 And if any man think that he knoweth any thing, he knoweth nothing yet as he ought to know.  

3 But if any man love God, the same is known of him.  

4 As concerning therefore the eating of those things that are offered in sacrifice unto idols, we know that an idol is nothing in the world, and that there is none other God but one.  

5 For though there be that are called gods, whether in heaven or in earth, (as there be gods many, and lords many,)  

6 But to us there is but one God, the Father, of whom are all things, and we in him; and one Lord Jesus Christ, by whom are all things, and we by him.  

7 Howbeit there is not in every man that knowledge: for some with conscience of the idol unto this hour eat it as a thing offered unto an idol; and their conscience being weak is defiled.  

8 But meat commendeth us not to God: for neither, if we eat, are we the better; neither, if we eat not, are we the worse.  

9 But take heed lest by any means this liberty of yours become a stumblingblock to them that are weak.  

10 For if any man see thee which hast knowledge sit at meat in the idol's temple, shall not the conscience of him which is weak be emboldened to eat those things which are offered to idols;  

11 And through thy knowledge shall the weak brother perish, for whom Christ died?  

12 But when ye sin so against the brethren, and wound their weak conscience, ye sin against Christ.  

13 Wherefore, if meat make my brother to offend, I will eat no flesh while the world standeth, lest I make my brother to offend.  

14 Wherefore, if meat make my brother to offend, I will eat no flesh while the world standeth, lest I make my brother to offend.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Knowledge puffeth up, but charity edifieth"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any intellectual learning or emotional feeling
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's wisdom - The knowledge represents human understanding without love, while charity represents Christ's building love that truly edifies.

### "An idol is nothing in the world"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any false gods or religious statues
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's supremacy - The idol represents false deities that have no real power compared to the one true God.
