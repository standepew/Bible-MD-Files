## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### APOSTLESHIP
- **Meaning:** "Divine commission, Christ's authority"
- **Context:** Paul's defense of his apostolic authority and rights, demonstrating Christ's commission
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's authority and His power to commission servants
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual apostleship (Acts 1:22)

### GOSPEL
- **Meaning:** "Divine message, Christ's proclamation"
- **Context:** The gospel as Paul's life message and ministry, demonstrating Christ's proclamation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's message and His power to transform through the gospel
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual gospel (Philippians 2:17)

### SERVICE
- **Meaning:** "Divine ministry, Christ's servanthood"
- **Context:** Service to all men to win them to Christ, demonstrating Christ's servanthood
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's servanthood and His call to serve others
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual service (Matthew 20:28)

### DISCIPLINE
- **Meaning:** "Divine self-control, Christ's mastery"
- **Context:** Self-discipline in running the race and fighting the fight, demonstrating Christ's mastery
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's mastery and His power to give self-control
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual discipline (Galatians 5:24)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "I have made myself servant unto all"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any servitude or people-pleasing
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's servanthood - The servanthood represents voluntary service to win people to Christ, not mere accommodation.

### "I keep under my body, and bring it into subjection"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any self-discipline or bodily control
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's mastery - The subjection represents spiritual mastery to avoid disqualification, not mere physical discipline.
