## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### EXAMPLE
- **Meaning:** "Divine pattern, Christ's warning"
- **Context:** Israel's experiences as examples for believers, demonstrating Christ's warning patterns
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's warning and His power to teach through history
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual example (James 1:13)

### TEMPTATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine testing, Christ's trial"
- **Context:** Common temptations with God's faithfulness and way of escape, demonstrating Christ's testing
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's trial and His power to provide deliverance
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual temptation (2 Peter 2:9)

### COMMUNION
- **Meaning:** "Divine fellowship, Christ's presence"
- **Context:** The Lord's table as communion with Christ's body and blood, demonstrating Christ's presence
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's presence and His power to unite believers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual communion (Matthew 26:26)

### GLORY
- **Meaning:** "Divine honor, Christ's magnification"
- **Context:** All things done to God's glory, demonstrating Christ's ultimate purpose
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's magnification and His call to honor God
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual glory (Colossians 3:17)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "All things are lawful for me, but all things are not expedient"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about any moral relativism or license to sin
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's wisdom - The lawful represents freedom in Christ, while not expedient means not all things are beneficial or constructive for spiritual growth.

### "Do all to the glory of God"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any religious activity or good works
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's ultimate purpose - The glory represents bringing honor to God in all actions, not mere religious performance.
