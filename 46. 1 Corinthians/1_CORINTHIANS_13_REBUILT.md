## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### CHARITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine love, Christ's compassion"
- **Context:** Charity as the supreme virtue that never fails, demonstrating Christ's perfect love
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's compassion and His power to love perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual charity (Matthew 7:22)

### FAITH
- **Meaning:** "Divine trust, Christ's confidence"
- **Context:** Faith as one of the three abiding virtues, demonstrating Christ's complete trust
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's confidence and His power to trust perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual faith (1 John 3:17)

### HOPE
- **Meaning:** "Divine expectation, Christ's anticipation"
- **Context:** Hope as one of the three abiding virtues, demonstrating Christ's joyful expectation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's anticipation and His power to hope perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual hope (Romans 12:10)

### PERFECT
- **Meaning:** "Divine completeness, Christ's fullness"
- **Context:** The perfect that is to come which makes the partial obsolete, demonstrating Christ's fullness
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's fullness and His power to bring complete revelation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual perfect (Philippians 2:3)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Charity suffereth long, and is kind"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any human patience or kindness
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's divine love - The suffering and kindness represent Christ's perfect love that endures all things and acts with perfect kindness.

### "We see through a glass, darkly"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any lack of understanding or confusion
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's revelation - The dark glass represents our current partial understanding of divine truth, which will be made complete when Christ returns.
