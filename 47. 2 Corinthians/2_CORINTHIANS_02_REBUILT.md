## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### HEAVINESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine sorrow, Christ's grief"
- **Context:** Paul's heaviness in dealing with the Corinthians, demonstrating Christ's pastoral grief
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's grief and His power to minister with compassion
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual heaviness (Philippians 2:2)

### FORGIVENESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine mercy, Christ's pardon"
- **Context:** Forgiveness toward the offender to prevent overwhelming sorrow, demonstrating Christ's redemptive mercy
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's pardon and His power to restore through forgiveness
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual forgiveness (Matthew 18:21)

### TRIUMPH
- **Meaning:** "Divine victory, Christ's conquest"
- **Context:** God's triumph in Christ that makes believers victorious, demonstrating Christ's complete conquest
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's conquest and His power to give victory
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual triumph (Romans 8:37)

### SAVOUR
- **Meaning:** "Divine fragrance, Christ's influence"
- **Context:** The sweet savour of Christ that affects both saved and perishing, demonstrating Christ's pervasive influence
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's influence and His power to affect all people
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual savour (Ephesians 5:2)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "I wrote unto you with many tears"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any emotional writing or sentimental expression
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's pastoral care - The tears represent Paul's Christ-like anguish and love for the Corinthians, not mere sentimentality.

### "We are unto God a sweet savour of Christ"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any pleasant aroma or good reputation
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's redemptive influence - The sweet savour represents the fragrance of Christ's sacrificial death that affects both those who accept and reject Him.
