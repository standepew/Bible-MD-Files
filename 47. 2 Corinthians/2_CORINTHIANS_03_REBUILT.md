## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### EPISTLE
- **Meaning:** "Divine message, Christ's testimony"
- **Context:** Believers as Christ's epistle written by the Spirit, demonstrating Christ's living testimony
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's testimony and His power to write His message on hearts
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual epistle (Jeremiah 31:33)

### SUFFICIENCY
- **Meaning:** "Divine adequacy, Christ's competence"
- **Context:** Sufficiency that comes from God for ministry, demonstrating Christ's complete adequacy
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's competence and His power to make ministers sufficient
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sufficiency (John 6:63)

### TESTAMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine covenant, Christ's new covenant"
- **Context:** New testament ministry of the Spirit versus letter, demonstrating Christ's covenant ministry
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's covenant and His power to establish a new covenant
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual testament (Romans 8:2)

### GLORY
- **Meaning:** "Divine radiance, Christ's presence"
- **Context:** The glory of the Lord that transforms believers from glory to glory, demonstrating Christ's radiant presence
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's presence and His power to transform with His glory
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual glory (Exodus 34:29)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "The letter killeth, but the spirit giveth life"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any literal interpretation versus spiritual understanding
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's new covenant - The letter represents the old covenant law that brings condemnation, while the spirit represents the new covenant that brings life through Christ.

### "We all, with open face beholding as in a glass the glory of the Lord"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any reflection or self-improvement
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's transformation - The beholding represents seeing Christ's glory which transforms believers into His image from one degree of glory to another.
