## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### TABERNACLE
- **Meaning:** "Divine dwelling, Christ's body"
- **Context:** Earthly tabernacle that will be dissolved for heavenly building, demonstrating Christ's incarnational dwelling
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's dwelling and His power to transform earthly into heavenly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual tabernacle (2 Peter 1:13)

### EARNEST
- **Meaning:** "Divine guarantee, Christ's deposit"
- **Context:** The earnest of the Spirit as guarantee of heavenly glory, demonstrating Christ's down payment
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's guarantee and His power to give assurance of eternity
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual earnest (Romans 8:23)

### JUDGMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine evaluation, Christ's assessment"
- **Context:** Judgment seat of Christ where all will be evaluated, demonstrating Christ's righteous assessment
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's assessment and His power to judge righteously
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual judgment (Philippians 1:23)

### RECONCILIATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine restoration, Christ's peace"
- **Context:** Reconciliation to God through Christ, demonstrating Christ's peace-making work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's peace and His power to restore relationship with God
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual reconciliation (Hebrews 10:31)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "We walk by faith, not by sight"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any blind faith or lack of evidence
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's spiritual perspective - The walking represents living by spiritual reality rather than physical appearance, demonstrating faith in unseen eternal truths.

### "If any man be in Christ, he is a new creature"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any moral improvement or self-help
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's regenerative work - The new creature represents complete transformation through union with Christ, not behavioral modification.
