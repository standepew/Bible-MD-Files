## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### WORKERS
- **Meaning:** "Divine laborers, Christ's servants"
- **Context:** Workers together with God in ministry, demonstrating Christ's cooperative service
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's servants and His power to work through believers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual workers (Isaiah 49:8)

### ARMOUR
- **Meaning:** "Divine protection, Christ's defense"
- **Context:** Armour of righteousness for ministry, demonstrating Christ's protective covering
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's defense and His power to protect in ministry
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual armour (Romans 12:12)

### YOKED
- **Meaning:** "Divine separation, Christ's holiness"
- **Context:** Unequal yoking with unbelievers forbidden, demonstrating Christ's call to separation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's holiness and His power to call believers to purity
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual yoked (Deuteronomy 22:10)

### TEMPLE
- **Meaning:** "Divine dwelling, Christ's presence"
- **Context:** Believers as temple of the living God, demonstrating Christ's indwelling presence
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's presence and His power to dwell in believers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual temple (Leviticus 26:12)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Be ye not unequally yoked together with unbelievers"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any business partnerships or social relationships
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's spiritual separation - The yoking represents spiritual union and fellowship that should be between believers, not merely external associations.

### "The temple of the living God"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any church building or physical structure
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's indwelling presence - The temple represents the believer's body as the dwelling place of the living God, not a physical building.
