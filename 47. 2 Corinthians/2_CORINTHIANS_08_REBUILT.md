## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### GRACE
- **Meaning:** "Divine enablement, Christ's generosity"
- **Context:** Grace of God that enables generous giving, demonstrating Christ's empowering presence
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's generosity and His power to enable sacrificial giving
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual grace (Acts 11:29)

### LIBERALITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine generosity, Christ's giving"
- **Context:** Liberality that abounds despite poverty, demonstrating Christ's generous spirit
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's giving and His power to give generously
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual liberality (Romans 15:26)

### POVERTY
- **Meaning:** "Divine humility, Christ's incarnation"
- **Context:** Christ's poverty that makes believers rich, demonstrating Christ's incarnational humility
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's humility and His power to enrich through sacrifice
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual poverty (Philippians 2:7)

### EQUALITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine balance, Christ's justice"
- **Context:** Equality in giving that supplies mutual needs, demonstrating Christ's just provision
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's justice and His power to provide balanced care
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual equality (Exodus 16:18)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Though he was rich, yet for your sakes he became poor"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any financial poverty or economic hardship
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's incarnation - The poverty represents Christ's voluntary humiliation in becoming human, not mere material lack.

### "God loveth a cheerful giver"
**Common misinterpretation:* This verse is not in 2 Corinthians 8, but commonly misattributed
**Biblical context:** This is actually from 2 Corinthians 9:7 - The cheerful giving represents willing, joyful generosity motivated by love for God.
