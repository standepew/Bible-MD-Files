## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### REVELATIONS
- **Meaning:** "Divine disclosure, Christ's manifestation"
- **Context:** Visions and revelations of being caught up to the third heaven, demonstrating Christ's heavenly revelation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's manifestation and His power to reveal heavenly realities
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual revelations (Revelation 4:1)

### THORN
- **Meaning:** "Divine testing, Christ's suffering"
- **Context:** Thorn in the flesh to prevent exaltation, demonstrating Christ's humbling suffering
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's suffering and His power to keep believers humble
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual thorn (Job 2:7)

### GRACE
- **Meaning:** "Divine enablement, Christ's sufficiency"
- **Context:** Grace sufficient for weakness, demonstrating Christ's empowering presence
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's sufficiency and His power to strengthen in weakness
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual grace (Hebrews 4:16)

### SIGNS
- **Meaning:** "Divine confirmation, Christ's authentication"
- **Context:** Signs of an apostle confirming ministry, demonstrating Christ's authenticating power
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's authentication and His power to confirm true ministry
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual signs (Acts 2:43)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "My grace is sufficient for thee: for my strength is made perfect in weakness"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any human weakness or inability
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's divine strength - The weakness represents human inability that becomes the perfect backdrop for Christ's divine strength and grace.

### "When I am weak, then am I strong"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any paradox or contradiction
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's power in weakness - The strength represents Christ's supernatural power that operates most perfectly through human weakness.
