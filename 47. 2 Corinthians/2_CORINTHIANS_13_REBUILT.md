## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### WITNESSES
- **Meaning:** "Divine testimony, Christ's confirmation"
- **Context:** Two or three witnesses establishing truth, demonstrating Christ's confirming testimony
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's confirmation and His power to establish truth
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual witnesses (Deuteronomy 19:15)

### CRUCIFIED
- **Meaning:** "Divine sacrifice, Christ's death"
- **Context:** Christ crucified through weakness yet living by God's power, demonstrating Christ's sacrificial death
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's death and His power to live through divine power
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual crucified (Philippians 2:8)

### EXAMINE
- **Meaning:** "Divine testing, Christ's evaluation"
- **Context:** Self-examination to be in the faith, demonstrating Christ's spiritual evaluation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's evaluation and His power to test spiritual condition
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual examine (1 Corinthians 11:28)

### PERFECTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine completeness, Christ's maturity"
- **Context:** Desire for believers' perfection, demonstrating Christ's complete maturity
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's maturity and His power to bring believers to completeness
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual perfection (John 8:32)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Examine yourselves, whether ye be in the faith"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any self-analysis or psychological evaluation
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's spiritual testing - The examination represents testing whether Christ truly dwells within, not mere self-assessment.

### "God of love and peace shall be with you"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any general well-wishing or good fortune
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's divine presence - The God of love and peace represents the triune God's presence with believers, not mere good wishes.
