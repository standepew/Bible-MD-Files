## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### APOSTLE
- **Meaning:** "Divine messenger, Christ's representative"
- **Context:** Paul's apostleship not from men but from Jesus Christ and God the Father, demonstrating Christ's divine commissioning
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's representative and His power to authorize messengers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual apostle (Acts 9:15)

### GOSPEL
- **Meaning:** "Divine message, Christ's good news"
- **Context:** The true gospel versus perverted gospels, demonstrating Christ's authentic message
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's message and His power to preserve truth
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual gospel (2 Corinthians 11:4)

### REVELATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine disclosure, Christ's manifestation"
- **Context:** Paul's gospel received by revelation of Jesus Christ, demonstrating Christ's self-revelation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's manifestation and His power to reveal truth
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual revelation (Ephesians 3:3)

### PERSECUTED
- **Meaning:** "Divine testing, Christ's suffering"
- **Context:** Paul's persecution of the church before conversion, demonstrating Christ's redemptive transformation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's suffering and His power to transform persecutors
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual persecuted (Acts 8:1)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "I marvel that ye are so soon removed from him that called you into the grace of Christ"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any change of mind or church switching
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's exclusive gospel - The removal represents abandoning Christ's grace for a perverted gospel, not mere preference change.

### "But when it pleased God, who separated me from my mother's womb"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any natural talent or calling
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's divine election - The separation represents God's sovereign calling from birth, not human merit.
