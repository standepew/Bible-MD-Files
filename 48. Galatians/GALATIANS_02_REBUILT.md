## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### REVELATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine guidance, Christ's direction"
- **Context:** Revelation directing Paul to Jerusalem, demonstrating Christ's divine guidance for ministry
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's direction and His power to guide ministry
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual revelation (Acts 15:2)

### LIBERTY
- **Meaning:** "Divine freedom, Christ's liberation"
- **Context:** Liberty in Christ that false brethren sought to destroy, demonstrating Christ's liberating power
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's liberation and His power to give spiritual freedom
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual liberty (Colossians 2:8)

### JUSTIFIED
- **Meaning:** "Divine righteousness, Christ's salvation"
- **Context:** Justification by faith in Christ versus works of the law, demonstrating Christ's saving righteousness
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's salvation and His power to justify by faith
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual justified (Romans 3:20)

### CRUCIFIED
- **Meaning:** "Divine identification, Christ's death"
- **Context:** Being crucified with Christ while living by faith, demonstrating Christ's death to self
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's death and His power to die to self and live for God
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual crucified (Romans 6:6)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "I am crucified with Christ: nevertheless I live; yet not I, but Christ liveth in me"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any self-improvement or personal development
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's death to self - The crucifixion represents dying to self and living by Christ's life through faith, not mere self-betterment.

### "A man is not justified by the works of the law, but by the faith of Jesus Christ"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any faith or belief system
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's exclusive salvation - The justification represents being made right with God through Christ's faith, not human effort or works.
