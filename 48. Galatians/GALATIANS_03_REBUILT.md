## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### FAITH
- **Meaning:** "Divine trust, Christ's belief"
- **Context:** Faith as the means of receiving the Spirit and justification, demonstrating Christ's trusting nature
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's trust and His power to justify by faith
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual faith (Acts 10:44)

### ABRAHAM
- **Meaning:** "Divine father, Christ's ancestor"
- **Context:** Abraham as father of all who believe, demonstrating Christ's covenantal lineage
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's lineage and His power to include all believers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual Abraham (Genesis 15:6)

### CURSE
- **Meaning:** "Divine judgment, Christ's condemnation"
- **Context:** Curse of the law that Christ redeemed us from, demonstrating Christ's redemptive work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's redemption and His power to remove the curse
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual curse (Deuteronomy 27:26)

### SCHOOLMASTER
- **Meaning:** "Divine tutor, Christ's instructor"
- **Context:** Law as schoolmaster to bring us to Christ, demonstrating Christ's instructional role
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's instruction and His power to teach through the law
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual schoolmaster (Romans 3:20)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "The just shall live by faith"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any moral living or ethical behavior
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's justification - The living represents being made right with God through faith in Christ, not moral effort.

### "There is neither Jew nor Greek, bond nor free, male nor female: for ye are all one in Christ Jesus"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any social equality or human rights
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's spiritual unity - The unity represents being united in Christ through faith, not social or political equality.
