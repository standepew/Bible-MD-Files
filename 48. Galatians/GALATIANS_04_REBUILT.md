## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### HEIR
- **Meaning:** "Divine inheritance, Christ's legacy"
- **Context:** Heir under tutors until appointed time, demonstrating Christ's future inheritance
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's legacy and His power to give inheritance
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual heir (Romans 8:15)

### ADOPTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine sonship, Christ's family"
- **Context:** Adoption as sons through Christ, demonstrating Christ's family relationship
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's family and His power to make believers His children
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual adoption (Ephesians 1:5)

### ALLEGORY
- **Meaning:** "Divine illustration, Christ's typology"
- **Context:** Allegory of two covenants through Hagar and Sarah, demonstrating Christ's typological teaching
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's typology and His power to illustrate truth through types
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual allegory (Exodus 19:2)

### JERUSALEM
- **Meaning:** "Divine city, Christ's church"
- **Context:** Jerusalem above as free mother of all believers, demonstrating Christ's heavenly church
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's church and His power to establish a heavenly city
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual Jerusalem (Isaiah 54:1)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "When the fulness of the time was come, God sent forth his Son"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any timing or historical event
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's incarnation - The fulness represents God's perfect timing for sending Christ to redeem those under the law.

### "We are not children of the bondwoman, but of the free"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any social or political freedom
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's spiritual freedom - The freedom represents being free from the law through Christ, not human liberation.
