## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### LIBERTY
- **Meaning:** "Divine freedom, Christ's liberation"
- **Context:** Liberty in Christ that frees from bondage, demonstrating Christ's liberating power
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's liberation and His power to give spiritual freedom
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual liberty (John 8:32)

### CIRCUMCISION
- **Meaning:** "Divine covenant, Christ's law"
- **Context:** Circumcision as bondage that makes Christ of no effect, demonstrating Christ's superiority over law
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's superiority and His power to fulfill and transcend the law
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual circumcision (Romans 7:4)

### LEAVEN
- **Meaning:** "Divine influence, Christ's teaching"
- **Context:** Leaven that leavens the whole lump, demonstrating Christ's pervasive influence
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's influence and His power to affect the whole community
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual leaven (1 Corinthians 5:6)

### FRUIT
- **Meaning:** "Divine character, Christ's nature"
- **Context:** Fruit of the Spirit versus works of the flesh, demonstrating Christ's transforming character
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's character and His power to produce spiritual fruit
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual fruit (Ephesians 5:9)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Stand fast therefore in the liberty wherewith Christ hath made us free"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any personal freedom or civil liberty
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's spiritual freedom - The liberty represents freedom from the law and sin through Christ, not human rights.

### "The fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, longsuffering, gentleness, goodness, faith, Meekness, temperance"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any moral character or personality traits
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's spiritual character - The fruit represents the character produced by the Holy Spirit, not natural human qualities.
