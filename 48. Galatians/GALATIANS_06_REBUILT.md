## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### RESTORE
- **Meaning:** "Divine healing, Christ's restoration"
- **Context:** Restoring those overtaken in fault with meekness, demonstrating Christ's restorative ministry
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's restoration and His power to heal the fallen
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual restore (James 5:19)

### BURDENS
- **Meaning:** "Divine care, Christ's support"
- **Context:** Bearing one another's burdens to fulfill Christ's law, demonstrating Christ's supportive love
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's support and His power to bear burdens
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual burdens (Romans 15:1)

### SOWETH
- **Meaning:** "Divine planting, Christ's investment"
- **Context:** Sowing to the Spirit reaping eternal life, demonstrating Christ's investment principle
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's investment and His power to produce eternal returns
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual soweth (1 Corinthians 9:11)

### CROSS
- **Meaning:** "Divine sacrifice, Christ's crucifixion"
- **Context:** Glory only in the cross of Christ, demonstrating Christ's crucified glory
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's crucifixion and His power to crucify the world
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual cross (1 Corinthians 1:18)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Bear ye one another's burdens, and so fulfil the law of Christ"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any social work or charitable activity
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's spiritual support - The burdens represent spiritual struggles that believers help each other bear, not material needs.

### "Whatsoever a man soweth, that shall he also reap"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any cause and effect or karma
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's spiritual harvest - The sowing represents spiritual investment with eternal consequences, not mere natural outcomes.
