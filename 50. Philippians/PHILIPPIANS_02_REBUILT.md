## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### LIKEMINDED
- **Meaning:** "Divine unity, Christ's oneness"
- **Context:** Being likeminded with Christ's mind, demonstrating Christ's unified thinking
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's unity and His power to create like-mindedness
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual likeminded (Romans 12:10)

### FORM
- **Meaning:** "Divine nature, Christ's essence"
- **Context:** Form of God and form of servant, demonstrating Christ's dual nature
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's nature and His power to take on different forms
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual form (John 1:14)

### EXALTED
- **Meaning:** "Divine elevation, Christ's glorification"
- **Context:** Highly exalted with name above every name, demonstrating Christ's supreme elevation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's elevation and His power to receive highest honor
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual exalted (Isaiah 45:23)

### WORK
- **Meaning:** "Divine salvation, Christ's transformation"
- **Context:** Working out salvation with fear and trembling, demonstrating Christ's saving work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's salvation and His power to transform believers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual work (Ephesians 2:10)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Let this mind be in you, which was also in Christ Jesus"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any positive thinking or attitude
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's humble mindset - The mind represents Christ's self-emptying humility, not mere positive thinking.

### "Work out your own salvation with fear and trembling"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any self-effort or works-based salvation
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's sanctifying work - The working represents God's power working in believers, not human effort.
