## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### SEEK
- **Meaning:** "Divine pursuit, Christ's desire"
- **Context:** Seeking things above where Christ sits, demonstrating Christ's heavenly pursuit
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's pursuit and His power to draw believers upward
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual seek (Matthew 6:19)

### MORTIFY
- **Meaning:** "Divine death, Christ's crucifixion"
- **Context:** Mortifying earthly members, demonstrating Christ's death to sin
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's mortification and His power to kill sin
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual mortify (Romans 8:13)

### NEW
- **Meaning:** "Divine transformation, Christ's creation"
- **Context:** Putting on the new man renewed in knowledge, demonstrating Christ's transforming power
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's transformation and His power to create anew
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual new (Ephesians 4:22)

### PEACE
- **Meaning:** "Divine tranquility, Christ's rest"
- **Context:** Peace of God ruling in hearts, demonstrating Christ's governing peace
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's peace and His power to rule hearts
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual peace (Romans 12:10)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Set your affection on things above, not on things on the earth"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any heavenly-mindedness or future focus
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's heavenly position - The affection represents being focused on Christ's heavenly reign, not mere optimism.

### "And whatsoever ye do, do it heartily, as to the Lord, and not unto men"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any work ethic or excellence
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's Lordship over all work - The heartily represents serving Christ as ultimate master, not human achievement.
