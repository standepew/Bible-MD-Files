## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### BOLD
- **Meaning:** "Divine courage, Christ's confidence"
- **Context:** Bold in God to speak the gospel despite suffering, demonstrating Christ's courageous witness
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's boldness and His power to speak truth despite opposition
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual bold (Acts 16:22)

### GENTLE
- **Meaning:** "Divine tenderness, Christ's care"
- **Context:** Gentle as a nurse cherishing children, demonstrating Christ's tender ministry
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's gentleness and His power to nurture believers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual gentle (2 Corinthians 2:17)

### WORTHY
- **Meaning:** "Divine qualification, Christ's fitness"
- **Context:** Walking worthy of God's kingdom and glory, demonstrating Christ's worthy conduct
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's worthiness and His power to enable righteous living
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual worthy (Ephesians 4:1)

### HINDERED
- **Meaning:** "Divine opposition, Christ's testing"
- **Context:** Satan hindering Paul's return to Thessalonica, demonstrating Christ's overcoming opposition
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's overcoming and His power to work through hindrances
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual hindered (Matthew 23:37)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "But we were gentle among you, even as a nurse cherisheth her children"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any gentleness or kindness
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's nurturing ministry - The gentleness represents Christ's tender care for His spiritual children, not mere niceness.

### "For what is our hope, or joy, or crown of rejoicing?"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any achievement or success
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's eternal reward - The crown represents believers as Christ's joy and reward at His coming, not earthly success.
