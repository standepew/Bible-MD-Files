## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### FORBEAR
- **Meaning:** "Divine patience, Christ's endurance"
- **Context:** No longer able to forbear concern for Thessalonians, demonstrating Christ's patient love
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's forbearance and His power to endure concern
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual forbear (Acts 17:16)

### ESTABLISH
- **Meaning:** "Divine foundation, Christ's stability"
- **Context:** Timothy sent to establish and comfort their faith, demonstrating Christ's establishing ministry
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's establishment and His power to create stability
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual establish (1 Corinthians 3:6)

### COMFORT
- **Meaning:** "Divine consolation, Christ's encouragement"
- **Context:** Comforted by Thessalonians' faith in affliction, demonstrating Christ's comforting presence
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's comfort and His power to console suffering
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual comfort (John 16:33)

### STABLISH
- **Meaning:** "Divine security, Christ's preservation"
- **Context:** Hearts established unblameable in holiness, demonstrating Christ's securing work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's establishment and His power to preserve in holiness
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual stablish (Ephesians 3:17)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "For now we live, if ye stand fast in the Lord"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any survival or existence
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's life-giving presence - The living represents spiritual vitality through Christ's steadfastness, not mere existence.

### "Night and day praying exceedingly"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any intense prayer or concern
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's continual intercession - The praying represents Christ's unceasing prayer for believers' perfection, not human anxiety.
