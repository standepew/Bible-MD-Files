## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### SANCTIFICATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine holiness, Christ's purity"
- **Context:** God's will is sanctification, abstaining from fornication, demonstrating Christ's holy calling
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's sanctification and His power to make believers holy
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sanctification (1 Corinthians 6:18)

### HOLINESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine purity, Christ's righteousness"
- **Context:** Called to holiness not uncleanness, demonstrating Christ's pure standard
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's holiness and His power to set apart believers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual holiness (Hebrews 13:4)

### TAUGHT
- **Meaning:** "Divine instruction, Christ's teaching"
- **Context:** Taught of God to love one another, demonstrating Christ's divine teaching
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's teaching and His power to instill love
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual taught (John 13:34)

### DESCEND
- **Meaning:** "Divine coming, Christ's return"
- **Context:** Lord descending from heaven with shout, demonstrating Christ's glorious return
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's coming and His power to gather His people
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual descend (Matthew 24:31)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "For this is the will of God, even your sanctification"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any moral improvement or self-discipline
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's holy calling - The sanctification represents being set apart by Christ's Spirit, not human effort.

### "Then we which are alive and remain shall be caught up together with them in the clouds"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any ascension or spiritual experience
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific rapture - The caught up represents being gathered to Christ at His return, not general spiritual elevation.
