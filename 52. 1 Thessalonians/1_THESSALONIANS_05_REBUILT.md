## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### THIEF
- **Meaning:** "Divine surprise, Christ's unexpected coming"
- **Context:** Day of Lord coming as thief in night, demonstrating Christ's sudden return
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's coming and His power to surprise unexpectedly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual thief (Matthew 24:43)

### LIGHT
- **Meaning:** "Divine illumination, Christ's presence"
- **Context:** Children of light and day, not darkness, demonstrating Christ's revealing presence
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's light and His power to illuminate believers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual light (Ephesians 5:8)

### SOBER
- **Meaning:** "Divine alertness, Christ's watchfulness"
- **Context:** Being sober and watchful as children of day, demonstrating Christ's vigilant readiness
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's sobriety and His power to keep believers alert
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sober (Ephesians 6:14)

### SANCTIFY
- **Meaning:** "Divine holiness, Christ's complete purification"
- **Context:** God sanctifying wholly spirit, soul, and body, demonstrating Christ's complete sanctification
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's sanctification and His power to make wholly holy
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sanctify (Romans 5:9)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Pray without ceasing"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any constant prayer activity
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's continual intercession - The praying represents constant communion with Christ, not mere verbal activity.

### "In every thing give thanks: for this is the will of God in Christ Jesus concerning you"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any gratitude or positive attitude
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's thankful will - The thanks represents Christ's gratitude working through believers in all circumstances, not mere optimism.
