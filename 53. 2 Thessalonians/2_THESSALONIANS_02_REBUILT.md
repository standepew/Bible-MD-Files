## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### DECEIVE
- **Meaning:** "Divine falsehood, Christ's opposition"
- **Context:** Deception about the day of Christ being at hand, demonstrating Christ's warning against false teaching
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's truth and His power to expose deception
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual deceive (Matthew 24:24)

### PERDITION
- **Meaning:** "Divine destruction, Christ's judgment"
- **Context:** Son of perdition revealed in man of sin, demonstrating Christ's ultimate judgment
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's judgment and His power to condemn evil
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual perdition (1 Timothy 4:1)

### MYSTERY
- **Meaning:** "Divine revelation, Christ's truth"
- **Context:** Mystery of iniquity already working, demonstrating Christ's revealing of hidden evil
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's revelation and His power to expose mystery
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual mystery (Daniel 11:36)

### DELUSION
- **Meaning:** "Divine judgment, Christ's deception"
- **Context:** Strong delusion sent by God to those who reject truth, demonstrating Christ's judicial hardening
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's judgment and His power to judicially deceive
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual delusion (Romans 1:25)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Let no man deceive you by any means: for that day shall not come, except there come a falling away first"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any apostasy or falling away from faith
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific end-times sequence - The falling away represents the great apostasy preceding Christ's return, not general backsliding.

### "The mystery of iniquity doth already work"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any evil or corruption in the world
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's revealing of the antichrist spirit - The mystery represents the hidden working of the man of sin system, not general wickedness.
