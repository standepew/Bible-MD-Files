## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### PRAY
- **Meaning:** "Divine communication, Christ's intercession"
- **Context:** Praying for the word of the Lord to have free course, demonstrating Christ's intercessory prayer
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's prayer and His power to advance the gospel
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual pray (1 Thessalonians 5:25)

### FAITHFUL
- **Meaning:** "Divine reliability, Christ's trustworthiness"
- **Context:** Lord faithful to establish and keep from evil, demonstrating Christ's faithful protection
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's faithfulness and His power to keep believers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual faithful (1 Corinthians 16:9)

### WORK
- **Meaning:** "Divine labor, Christ's service"
- **Context:** Working with labor and travail as example, demonstrating Christ's working ministry
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's work and His power to provide through labor
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual work (Philippians 1:6)

### ADMONISH
- **Meaning:** "Divine correction, Christ's discipline"
- **Context:** Admonishing disobedient as brothers, not enemies, demonstrating Christ's corrective love
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's admonition and His power to restore through correction
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual admonish (Ephesians 3:17)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "If any would not work, neither should he eat"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any work ethic or employment
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's principle of provision - The work represents Christ's provision through diligent labor, not mere employment.

### "Be not weary in well doing"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any persistence or endurance
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's spiritual perseverance - The well doing represents Christ's righteous work, not general good deeds.
