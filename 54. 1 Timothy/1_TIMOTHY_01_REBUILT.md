## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### COMMANDMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine authority, Christ's instruction"
- **Context:** Paul's apostleship by Christ's commandment, demonstrating Christ's authoritative calling
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's authority and His power to commission ministers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual commandment (2 Timothy 1:13)

### CHARITY
- **Meaning:** "Divine love, Christ's affection"
- **Context:** Charity from pure heart and good conscience, demonstrating Christ's loving motivation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's charity and His power to create pure love
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual charity (Titus 3:9)

### LAW
- **Meaning:** "Divine standard, Christ's righteousness"
- **Context:** Law good when used lawfully for lawless, demonstrating Christ's righteous standard
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's law and His power to reveal sin
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual law (Romans 7:12)

### WARFARE
- **Meaning:** "Divine battle, Christ's conflict"
- **Context:** Good warfare with faith and good conscience, demonstrating Christ's spiritual battle
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's warfare and His power to fight spiritually
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual warfare (Ephesians 6:12)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "The end of the commandment is charity out of a pure heart, and of a good conscience, and of faith unfeigned"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any moral improvement or good intentions
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's holy motivation - The charity represents Christ's love working through pure hearts, not human goodness.

### "Christ Jesus came into the world to save sinners; of whom I am chief"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any humility or self-deprecation
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's saving purpose - The chief represents the magnitude of Christ's mercy to the worst of sinners, not false modesty.
