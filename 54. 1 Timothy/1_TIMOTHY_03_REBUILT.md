## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### BISHOP
- **Meaning:** "Divine overseer, Christ's shepherd"
- **Context:** Bishop must be blameless ruling house and church, demonstrating Christ's shepherding leadership
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's oversight and His power to lead His church
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual bishop (Titus 1:7)

### DEACON
- **Meaning:** "Divine servant, Christ's minister"
- **Context:** Deacons holding mystery of faith in pure conscience, demonstrating Christ's serving ministry
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's service and His power to minister faithfully
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual deacon (Acts 6:3)

### MYSTERY
- **Meaning:** "Divine revelation, Christ's truth"
- **Context:** Mystery of faith and godliness revealed, demonstrating Christ's revealing truth
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's mystery and His power to reveal hidden truth
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual mystery (Colossians 1:26)

### CHURCH
- **Meaning:** "Divine body, Christ's assembly"
- **Context:** Church as pillar and ground of truth, demonstrating Christ's foundational body
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's church and His power to establish truth
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual church (Ephesians 2:20)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "If a man desire the office of a bishop, he desireth a good work"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any leadership position or ambition
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's shepherding calling - The desire represents Christ's calling to oversee His flock, not human ambition.

### "God was manifest in the flesh"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any divine manifestation or spiritual presence
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific incarnation - The manifest represents Christ becoming human in the flesh, not general divine appearance.
