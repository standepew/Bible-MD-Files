## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### APOSTASY
- **Meaning:** "Divine falling away, Christ's rejection"
- **Context:** Departing from faith in latter times, demonstrating Christ's warning against rejection
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's warning and His power to expose apostasy
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual apostasy (2 Thessalonians 2:3)

### CONSCIENCE
- **Meaning:** "Divine awareness, Christ's moral compass"
- **Context:** Conscience seared with hot iron through hypocrisy, demonstrating Christ's corrupted conscience
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's conscience and His power to convict sin
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual conscience (Matthew 24:24)

### GODLINESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine holiness, Christ's sanctification"
- **Context:** Godliness profitable for all things with life promise, demonstrating Christ's profitable holiness
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's godliness and His power to sanctify completely
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual godliness (2 Timothy 2:15)

### GIFT
- **Meaning:** "Divine endowment, Christ's spiritual ability"
- **Context:** Gift given by prophecy with laying on of hands, demonstrating Christ's spiritual empowerment
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's gift and His power to equip ministry
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual gift (Hebrews 13:15)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Bodily exercise profiteth little: but godliness is profitable unto all things"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any physical fitness vs. spiritual discipline
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's eternal priorities - The godliness represents Christ's spiritual discipline with eternal rewards, not mere exercise comparison.

### "Neglect not the gift that is in thee, which was given thee by prophecy, with the laying on of the hands of the presbytery"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any natural talent or ability
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's spiritual gifts - The gift represents Christ's spiritual endowment through church recognition, not natural abilities.
