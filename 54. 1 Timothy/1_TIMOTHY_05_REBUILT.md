## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### ELDER
- **Meaning:** "Divine leader, Christ's overseer"
- **Context:** Elders to be treated as fathers with double honour for ruling well, demonstrating Christ's honoring leadership
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's eldership and His power to lead with honor
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual elder (Leviticus 19:32)

### WIDOW
- **Meaning:** "Divine dependent, Christ's cared"
- **Context:** True widows trusting God with prayers, false widows living in pleasure, demonstrating Christ's care for the dependent
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's widowhood and His power to provide for the needy
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual widow (Luke 2:37)

### PROVIDE
- **Meaning:** "Divine responsibility, Christ's care"
- **Context:** Providing for own household as faith requirement, demonstrating Christ's family responsibility
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's provision and His power to care for families
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual provide (James 1:27)

### DISCIPLINE
- **Meaning:** "Divine correction, Christ's order"
- **Context:** Church discipline with witnesses and impartiality, demonstrating Christ's corrective order
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's discipline and His power to maintain order
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual discipline (Deuteronomy 25:4)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "If any provide not for his own, and specially for those of his own house, he hath denied the faith, and is worse than an infidel"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any financial responsibility or family duty
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's family provision - The providing represents Christ's care for family as faith evidence, not mere financial support.

### "The labourer is worthy of his reward"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any work compensation or fair wages
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's ministry support - The labourer represents Christ's ministers worthy of church support, not general employment.
