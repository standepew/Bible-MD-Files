## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### SERVANTS
- **Meaning:** "Divine workers, Christ's laborers"
- **Context:** Servants honoring masters to glorify God, demonstrating Christ's service ethic
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's servanthood and His power to work honorably
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual servants (Ephesians 6:5)

### GAIN
- **Meaning:** "Divine profit, Christ's benefit"
- **Context:** False teachers supposing gain is godliness, demonstrating Christ's true gain
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's gain and His power to provide true profit
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual gain (2 Peter 2:3)

### CONTENTMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine satisfaction, Christ's sufficiency"
- **Context:** Godliness with contentment as great gain, demonstrating Christ's sufficient provision
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's contentment and His power to satisfy completely
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual contentment (Hebrews 13:5)

### FIGHT
- **Meaning:** "Divine battle, Christ's warfare"
- **Context:** Fighting the good fight of faith for eternal life, demonstrating Christ's spiritual warfare
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's fight and His power to overcome spiritually
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual fight (2 Timothy 2:22)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "The love of money is the root of all evil"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any financial wealth or material possessions
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's spiritual priority - The love represents covetousness that replaces God, not money itself.

### "Fight the good fight of faith"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any struggle or effort
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's spiritual warfare - The fight represents Christ's battle for faith, not human striving.
