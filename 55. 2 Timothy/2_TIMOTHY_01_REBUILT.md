## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### PROMISE
- **Meaning:** "Divine guarantee, Christ's assurance"
- **Context:** Promise of life in Christ Jesus, demonstrating Christ's life-giving promise
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's promise and His power to give eternal life
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual promise (1 Timothy 1:2)

### FAITH
- **Meaning:** "Divine trust, Christ's belief"
- **Context:** Unfeigned faith dwelling in grandmother, mother, and Timothy, demonstrating Christ's generational faith
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's faith and His power to create genuine trust
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual faith (Acts 16:1)

### SPIRIT
- **Meaning:** "Divine presence, Christ's empowerment"
- **Context:** Spirit of power, love, and sound mind, not fear, demonstrating Christ's empowering Spirit
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's Spirit and His power to give boldness
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual spirit (Romans 8:15)

### CALLING
- **Meaning:** "Divine summons, Christ's invitation"
- **Context:** Holy calling according to God's purpose and grace, demonstrating Christ's gracious calling
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's calling and His power to invite to salvation
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual calling (Ephesians 2:8)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "For God hath not given us the spirit of fear; but of power, and of love, and of a sound mind"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any confidence or mental health
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's spiritual empowerment - The spirit represents Christ's divine presence giving boldness, not natural courage.

### "I know whom I have believed, and am persuaded that he is able to keep that which I have committed unto him against that day"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any trust or confidence in people
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific keeping power - The keeping represents Christ's ability to preserve faith until His return, not general reliability.
