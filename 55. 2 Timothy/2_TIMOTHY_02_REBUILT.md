## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### STRONG
- **Meaning:** "Divine power, Christ's strength"
- **Context:** Strong in grace of Christ Jesus, demonstrating Christ's empowering grace
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's strength and His power to make believers strong
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual strong (Ephesians 6:10)

### SOLDIER
- **Meaning:** "Divine warrior, Christ's fighter"
- **Context:** Good soldier of Jesus Christ not entangled in earthly affairs, demonstrating Christ's spiritual warfare
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's soldiery and His power to fight spiritually
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual soldier (1 Corinthians 9:7)

### STRIVE
- **Meaning:** "Divine competition, Christ's contest"
- **Context:** Striving lawfully for masteries and crown, demonstrating Christ's proper striving
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's striving and His power to win according to rules
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual strive (1 Corinthians 9:24)

### VESSEL
- **Meaning:** "Divine container, Christ's instrument"
- **Context:** Vessel unto honor sanctified for master's use, demonstrating Christ's honorable purpose
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's vessel and His power to sanctify for His use
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual vessel (Romans 9:21)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Study to shew thyself approved unto God, a workman that needeth not to be ashamed, rightly dividing the word of truth"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any academic study or intellectual pursuit
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's biblical interpretation - The study represents Christ's diligent handling of Scripture, not general education.

### "If a man therefore purge himself from these, he shall be a vessel unto honour, sanctified, and meet for the master's use"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any self-improvement or moral effort
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's sanctifying work - The purging represents Christ's cleansing for honorable service, not human effort.
