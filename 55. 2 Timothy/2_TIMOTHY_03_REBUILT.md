## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### PERILOUS
- **Meaning:** "Divine danger, Christ's warning"
- **Context:** Perilous times in last days with evil men, demonstrating Christ's warning of end times
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's warning and His power to expose danger
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual perilous (Matthew 24:12)

### GODLINESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine holiness, Christ's righteousness"
- **Context:** Form of godliness without power, demonstrating Christ's true versus false godliness
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's godliness and His power to distinguish true from false
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual godliness (2 Peter 3:3)

### SCRIPTURES
- **Meaning:** "Divine word, Christ's revelation"
- **Context:** Holy scriptures making wise unto salvation, demonstrating Christ's saving word
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's scriptures and His power to save through wisdom
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual scriptures (Exodus 7:11)

### INSPIRATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine breath, Christ's authorship"
- **Context:** Scripture given by inspiration of God for doctrine and righteousness, demonstrating Christ's divine authorship
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's inspiration and His power to author Scripture
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual inspiration (2 Peter 1:21)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "All that will live godly in Christ Jesus shall suffer persecution"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any suffering or hardship
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific persecution - The persecution represents opposition for following Christ, not general suffering.

### "All scripture is given by inspiration of God"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any religious writing or inspiration
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific inspiration - The scripture represents Christ's divine authorship of the Bible, not general inspiration.
