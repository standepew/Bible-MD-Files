## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### CHARGE
- **Meaning:** "Divine command, Christ's commission"
- **Context:** Final charge before God and Christ to preach the word, demonstrating Christ's authoritative commission
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's charge and His power to command ministry
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual charge (Acts 20:27)

### PREACH
- **Meaning:** "Divine proclamation, Christ's heralding"
- **Context:** Preach the word in season and out of season, demonstrating Christ's urgent proclamation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's preaching and His power to proclaim truth
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual preach (1 Peter 4:11)

### FIGHT
- **Meaning:** "Divine battle, Christ's warfare"
- **Context:** Fought good fight and finished course, demonstrating Christ's victorious warfare
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's fight and His power to win spiritually
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual fight (2 Timothy 2:15)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "I have fought a good fight, I have finished my course, I have kept the faith"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any personal achievement or accomplishment
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's spiritual victory - The fight represents Christ's spiritual warfare, course represents Christ's ministry, and faith represents Christ's trust, not human success.

### "The Lord stood with me, and strengthened me"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any divine help or support
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific presence - The stood represents Christ's faithful presence in persecution, not general assistance.
