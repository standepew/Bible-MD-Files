## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### DOCTRINE
- **Meaning:** "Divine teaching, Christ's instruction"
- **Context:** Sound doctrine for all groups in the church, demonstrating Christ's proper teaching
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's doctrine and His power to teach soundly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual doctrine (1 Timothy 3:2)

### PATTERN
- **Meaning:** "Divine example, Christ's model"
- **Context:** Pattern of good works with uncorrupt doctrine, demonstrating Christ's exemplary life
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's pattern and His power to exemplify righteousness
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual pattern (1 Peter 5:3)

### GRACE
- **Meaning:** "Divine favor, Christ's unmerited blessing"
- **Context:** Grace of God bringing salvation to all men, demonstrating Christ's saving grace
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's grace and His power to save completely
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual grace (John 1:14)

### REDEEM
- **Meaning:** "Divine purchase, Christ's salvation"
- **Context:** Christ gave Himself to redeem from iniquity and purify peculiar people, demonstrating Christ's redemptive work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's redemption and His power to purchase and purify
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual redeem (1 Peter 1:18)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Looking for that blessed hope, and the glorious appearing of the great God and our Saviour Jesus Christ"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any hope or optimistic expectation
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific return - The hope represents Christ's blessed hope of His glorious appearing, not general optimism.

### "Who gave himself for us, that he might redeem us from all iniquity, and purify unto himself a peculiar people"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any moral improvement or self-purification
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific redemption - The redeem represents Christ's purchase from iniquity, not human effort.
