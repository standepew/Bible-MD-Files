## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### SUBJECT
- **Meaning:** "Divine submission, Christ's obedience"
- **Context:** Subject to principalities and powers with good works, demonstrating Christ's obedient submission
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's submission and His power to submit to authority
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual subject (Romans 13:1)

### KINDNESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine goodness, Christ's benevolence"
- **Context:** Kindness and love of God Savior appearing to man, demonstrating Christ's loving kindness
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's kindness and His power to show benevolence
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual kindness (Ephesians 2:3)

### REGENERATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine rebirth, Christ's new birth"
- **Context:** Washing of regeneration and renewing of Holy Ghost, demonstrating Christ's regenerating work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's regeneration and His power to rebirth spiritually
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual regeneration (John 3:5)

### MAINTAIN
- **Meaning:** "Divine perseverance, Christ's continuation"
- **Context:** Learn to maintain good works for necessary uses, demonstrating Christ's persevering work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's maintenance and His power to continue good works
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual maintain (1 Timothy 1:15)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Not by works of righteousness which we have done, but according to his mercy he saved us"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any good works or moral effort
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific salvation - The works represent human righteousness that cannot save, not general good deeds.

### "Let ours also learn to maintain good works for necessary uses, that they be not unfruitful"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any productivity or usefulness
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific service - The works represent Christ's good works for necessary uses, not general productivity.
