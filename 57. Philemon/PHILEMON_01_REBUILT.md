## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### PRISONER
- **Meaning:** "Divine captive, Christ's bound servant"
- **Context:** Paul as prisoner of Jesus Christ, demonstrating Christ's binding service
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's imprisonment and His power to bind in service
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual prisoner (Colossians 4:9)

### LOVE
- **Meaning:** "Divine affection, Christ's compassion"
- **Context:** Philemon's love and faith toward Lord Jesus and saints, demonstrating Christ's loving fellowship
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's love and His power to create affectionate believers
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual love (Romans 1:8)

### BEGOTTEN
- **Meaning:** "Divine birth, Christ's spiritual fatherhood"
- **Context:** Paul begotten Onesimus in bonds as spiritual son, demonstrating Christ's spiritual generation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's begetting and His power to give spiritual birth
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual begotten (2 Corinthians 5:20)

### BROTHER
- **Meaning:** "Divine family, Christ's fellowship"
- **Context:** Onesimus now as brother beloved, not servant, demonstrating Christ's transforming fellowship
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's brotherhood and His power to create family relationships
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual brother (Galatians 3:28)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Not now as a servant, but above a servant, a brother beloved"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any social equality or human relationships
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's spiritual transformation - The brother represents Christ's transforming fellowship, not social reform.

### "If he hath wronged thee, or oweth thee ought, put that on mine account"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any debt forgiveness or financial matters
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's substitutionary payment - The account represents Christ's taking on our debts, not general forgiveness.
