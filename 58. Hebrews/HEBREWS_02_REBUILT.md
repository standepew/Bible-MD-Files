## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "For it became him, for whom are all things, and by whom are all things, in bringing many sons unto glory"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any leadership or bringing people to success
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific redemptive work - The bringing represents Christ's purposeful work of bringing sons to glory, not general leadership.

### "He also himself likewise took part of the same; that through death he might destroy him that had the power of death"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any death or destruction
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific sacrificial death - The death represents Christ's substitutionary death to destroy the devil's power, not general mortality.
