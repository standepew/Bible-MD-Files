## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### PERFECTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine completeness, Christ's maturity"
- **Context:** Going on to perfection leaving elementary principles, demonstrating Christ's spiritual maturity
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's perfection and His power to bring to completeness
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual perfection (Acts 2:38)

### APOSTASY
- **Meaning:** "Divine falling away, Christ's warning"
- **Context:** Falling away after enlightenment crucifying Christ afresh, demonstrating Christ's warning against rejection
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's apostasy and His power to warn against falling
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual apostasy (1 Corinthians 15:52)

### PROMISE
- **Meaning:** "Divine guarantee, Christ's covenant"
- **Context:** God's promise to Abraham confirmed by oath, demonstrating Christ's covenantal faithfulness
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's promise and His power to guarantee eternally
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual promise (Hebrews 10:26)

### ANCHOR
- **Meaning:** "Divine security, Christ's hope"
- **Context:** Hope as anchor of the soul sure and steadfast, demonstrating Christ's securing hope
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's anchor and His power to secure eternally
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual anchor (Genesis 22:16)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Therefore leaving the principles of the doctrine of Christ, let us go on unto perfection"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any self-improvement or personal development
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific spiritual maturity - The perfection represents Christ's spiritual completeness, not human achievement.

### "Which hope we have as an anchor of the soul, both sure and stedfast"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any hope or optimism
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific securing hope - The anchor represents Christ's unshakeable hope that secures the soul, not general optimism.
