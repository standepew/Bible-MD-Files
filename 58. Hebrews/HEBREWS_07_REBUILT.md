## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### MELCHISEDEC
- **Meaning:** "Divine priest-king, Christ's priesthood"
- **Context:** Priest-king without genealogy who blessed Abraham, demonstrating Christ's eternal priesthood
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's Melchizedek and His power to priest eternally
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual Melchisedec (Genesis 14:18)

### PRIESTHOOD
- **Meaning:** "Divine mediation, Christ's intercession"
- **Context:** Levitical priesthood changed for Christ's superior priesthood, demonstrating Christ's perfect mediation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's priesthood and His power to mediate perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual priesthood (Psalm 110:4)

### LAW
- **Meaning:** "Divine command, Christ's covenant"
- **Context:** Law changed because of Christ's superior priesthood, demonstrating Christ's new covenant
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's law and His power to establish new covenant
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual law (Numbers 18:21)

### INTERCESSION
- **Meaning:** "Divine mediation, Christ's prayer"
- **Context:** Christ's unchangeable priesthood enabling eternal intercession, demonstrating Christ's continual mediation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's intercession and His power to mediate eternally
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual intercession (Genesis 14:20)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Without father, without mother, without descent, having neither beginning of days, nor end of life; but made like unto the Son of God"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any mysterious or unknown origins
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific divine nature - The without represents Christ's eternal nature as Son of God, not human mystery.

### "But this man, because he continueth ever, hath an unchangeable priesthood"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any stability or permanence
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific eternal priesthood - The unchangeable represents Christ's permanent priestly ministry, not human consistency.
