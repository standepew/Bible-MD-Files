## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### TABERNACLE
- **Meaning:** "Divine dwelling, Christ's sanctuary"
- **Context:** True tabernacle pitched by Lord not man, demonstrating Christ's heavenly sanctuary
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's tabernacle and His power to dwell perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual tabernacle (Hebrews 9:24)

### COVENANT
- **Meaning:** "Divine agreement, Christ's testament"
- **Context:** Better covenant established on better promises, demonstrating Christ's superior agreement
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's covenant and His power to guarantee eternally
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual covenant (Exodus 25:8)

### MIND
- **Meaning:** "Divine understanding, Christ's knowledge"
- **Context:** Laws put in mind and written on hearts, demonstrating Christ's internal transformation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's mind and His power to transform internally
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual mind (Colossians 2:17)

### MERCIFUL
- **Meaning:** "Divine compassion, Christ's forgiveness"
- **Context:** Merciful to unrighteousness, remembering sins no more, demonstrating Christ's forgiving mercy
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's mercy and His power to forgive completely
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual merciful (Exodus 25:40)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "I will put my laws into their mind, and write them in their hearts"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any moral education or character development
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific internal transformation - The writing represents Christ's supernatural work of internalizing His law, not human effort.

### "For I will be merciful to their unrighteousness, and their sins and their iniquities will I remember no more"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any forgiveness or forgetting
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific divine forgiveness - The remembering represents Christ's complete forgiveness through His sacrifice, not human forgetfulness.
