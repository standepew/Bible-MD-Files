## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### SANCTUARY
- **Meaning:** "Divine dwelling, Christ's holy place"
- **Context:** Earthly sanctuary with candlestick and table as type of heavenly sanctuary, demonstrating Christ's holy dwelling
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's sanctuary and His power to dwell perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sanctuary (Exodus 25:23)

### HOLIEST
- **Meaning:** "Divine presence, Christ's throne room"
- **Context:** Holiest of all with ark and mercyseat entered only by high priest, demonstrating Christ's presence access
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's holiest and His power to grant access
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual holiest (Exodus 25:31)

### REDEMPTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine deliverance, Christ's salvation"
- **Context:** Eternal redemption obtained by Christ's blood entering holy place, demonstrating Christ's complete salvation
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's redemption and His power to deliver eternally
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual redemption (Leviticus 16:2)

### TESTATOR
- **Meaning:** "Divine covenant-maker, Christ's sacrifice"
- **Context:** Death of testator necessary for testament to be in force, demonstrating Christ's sacrificial death
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's testator and His power to establish covenant
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual testator (Leviticus 16:34)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "But Christ being come an high priest of good things to come, by a greater and more perfect tabernacle, not made with hands"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any spiritual building or heavenly place
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific heavenly sanctuary - The tabernacle represents Christ's perfect heavenly sanctuary, not human construction.

### "And without shedding of blood is no remission"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any bloodshed or violence
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific sacrificial blood - The shedding represents Christ's substitutionary blood sacrifice, not general violence.
