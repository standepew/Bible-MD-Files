## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### FAITH
- **Meaning:** "Divine trust, Christ's confidence"
- **Context:** Substance of things hoped for and evidence of things not seen, demonstrating Christ's saving faith
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's faith and His power to trust perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual faith (Romans 8:24)

### SUBSTANCE
- **Meaning:** "Divine reality, Christ's assurance"
- **Context:** Faith as substance of things hoped for, demonstrating Christ's assuring work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's substance and His power to assure completely
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual substance (John 1:3)

### TRANSLATED
- **Meaning:** "Divine transformation, Christ's glorification"
- **Context:** Enoch translated that he should not see death, demonstrating Christ's transforming work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's translation and His power to transform eternally
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual translated (Genesis 4:4)

### RESURRECTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine rising, Christ's victory"
- **Context:** Better resurrection through faith and suffering, demonstrating Christ's victorious work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's resurrection and His power to raise completely
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual resurrection (Genesis 5:24)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Now faith is the substance of things hoped for, the evidence of things not seen"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any belief or optimism
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific saving faith - The substance represents Christ's assuring faith that gives reality to hoped-for things, not general belief.

### "Without faith it is impossible to please him: for he that cometh to God must believe that he is, and that he is a rewarder of them that diligently seek him"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any religious effort or seeking
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific approach to God - The seeking represents Christ's faithful approach to God through faith, not human effort.
