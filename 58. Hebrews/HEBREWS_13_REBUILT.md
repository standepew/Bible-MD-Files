## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### STRANGERS
- **Meaning:** "Divine visitors, Christ's messengers"
- **Context:** Entertaining strangers who may be angels unawares, demonstrating Christ's visiting work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's strangers and His power to visit unexpectedly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual strangers (Romans 12:13)

### CONTENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine satisfaction, Christ's sufficiency"
- **Context:** Content with what we have because God will never leave us, demonstrating Christ's satisfying work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's content and His power to satisfy completely
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual content (Genesis 18:3)

### SACRIFICE
- **Meaning:** "Divine offering, Christ's worship"
- **Context:** Sacrifice of praise and good works pleasing to God, demonstrating Christ's worshipful work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's sacrifice and His power to worship perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sacrifice (1 Corinthians 7:2)

### SHEPHERD

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Jesus Christ the same yesterday, and to day, and for ever"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any consistency or stability
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific unchangeable nature - The sameness represents Christ's eternal nature and faithfulness, not human consistency.

### "Let brotherly love continue"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any friendship or social kindness
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific divine love - The brotherly love represents Christ's spiritual family love, not natural affection.
