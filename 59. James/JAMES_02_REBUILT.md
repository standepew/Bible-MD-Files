## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### RESPECT
- **Meaning:** "Divine impartiality, Christ's equality"
- **Context:** No respect of persons in faith, showing Christ's impartial treatment, demonstrating Christ's equal work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's respect and His power to treat equally
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual respect (Leviticus 19:15)

### LAW
- **Meaning:** "Divine command, Christ's standard"
- **Context:** Royal law of love fulfilled through works, demonstrating Christ's commanding work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's law and His power to command perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual law (Deuteronomy 10:19)

### FAITH
- **Meaning:** "Divine trust, Christ's belief"
- **Context:** Faith without works is dead, demonstrating Christ's living faith, demonstrating Christ's trusting work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's faith and His power to trust actively
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual faith (Luke 6:20)

### WORKS
- **Meaning:** "Divine action, Christ's service"
- **Context:** Works justify faith as seen in Abraham and Rahab, demonstrating Christ's serving work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's works and His power to serve effectively
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual works (Proverbs 22:2)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Faith without works is dead"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any good works or moral actions
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific living faith - The works represent Christ's active faith that produces obedience, not human merit.

### "Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any human love or kindness
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific divine love - The love represents Christ's royal law of sacrificial love, not natural affection.
