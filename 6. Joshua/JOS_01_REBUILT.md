## CHAPTER SUMMARY

After Moses' death, God commissions Joshua as leader, commanding him to cross the Jordan and possess the promised land. God promises that wherever Joshua's feet tread will be given to Israel, from Lebanon to the Euphrates, and that no one will stand against him. God assures Joshua of His constant presence, just as He was with Moses, and commands him to be strong and courageous to divide the land as inheritance. Joshua must meditate on God's law day and night to obey it completely, ensuring prosperity and success. God repeats the command to be strong and courageous, promising His presence wherever Joshua goes. Joshua commands the people to prepare provisions for crossing Jordan in three days. To the eastern tribes (Reuben, Gad, half-Manasseh), Joshua reminds them of Moses' command to help their brethren possess the land while their families remain east of Jordan. The tribes pledge complete obedience to Joshua, promising to follow him as they followed Moses, with the condition that God is with him. They declare that anyone who rebels against Joshua's commands will be put to death, and charge Joshua to be strong and courageous.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Arise, go over this Jordan" — Leadership Commission

**Joshua 1:2-3** — "Now after the death of Moses the servant of the LORD it came to pass, that the LORD spake unto Joshua the son of Nun, Moses' minister, saying, Moses my servant is dead; now therefore arise, go over this Jordan, thou, and all this people, unto the land which I do give to them"

- **Deuteronomy 31:23** — "And he gave Joshua the son of Nun a charge, and said, Be strong and of a good courage: for thou shalt bring the children of Israel into the land which I sware unto them: and I will be with thee."
  - *Interpretation:* Moses' earlier commissioning of Joshua is now fulfilled by God directly, showing the continuity of divine leadership.

- **Acts 7:45** — "Which also our fathers that came after brought in with Jesus into the possession of the Gentiles, whom God drave out before the face of our fathers, unto the days of David"
  - *Interpretation:* Stephen's sermon references Joshua bringing Israel into the land, showing this was a pivotal moment in Israel's history.

- **Hebrews 4:8** — "For if Jesus had given them rest, then would he not afterward have spoken of another day."
  - *Interpretation:* Hebrews uses Joshua as a type of Christ, showing that Joshua's bringing rest was a shadow of the greater rest Christ provides.

### "Every place that the sole of your foot shall tread upon" — Land Promised

**Joshua 1:3** — "Every place that the sole of your foot shall tread upon, that have I given unto you, as I said unto Moses"

- **Deuteronomy 11:24** — "Every place whereon the soles of your feet shall tread shall be yours: from the wilderness and Lebanon, from the river, the river Euphrates, even unto the uttermost sea shall your coast be."
  - *Interpretation:* God repeats to Joshua the same promise He gave Moses, showing the continuity of the land promise.

- **Genesis 13:17** — "Arise, walk through the land in the length of it and in the breadth of it; for I will give it unto thee."
  - *Interpretation:* God's original promise to Abraham about walking the land is now being fulfilled through Joshua.

- **Psalm 37:31** — "The law of his God is in his heart; none of his steps shall slide."
  - *Interpretation:* The psalmist connects walking securely with God's law in heart, which Joshua is commanded to meditate on.

**Joshua 1:5** — "There shall not any man be able to stand before thee all the days of thy life: as I was with Moses, so I will be with thee: I will not fail thee, nor forsake thee"

  - *Interpretation:* Jesus gives the Great Commission with the same promise of continual presence, showing this is the foundation for Christian mission.

- **Romans 8:31** — "What shall we then say to these things? If God be for us, who can be against us?"
  - *Interpretation:* Paul applies this principle of divine presence and support to believers' spiritual battles.

**Joshua 1:8** — "This book of the law shall not depart out of thy mouth; but thou shalt meditate therein day and night, that thou mayest observe to do according to all that is written therein: for then thou shalt make thy way prosperous, and then thou shalt have good success"

  - *Interpretation:* The psalmist describes the same principle of law meditation leading to prosperity, showing this was the path to success for all godly leaders.

- **Colossians 3:16** — "Let the word of Christ dwell in you richly in all wisdom; teaching and admonishing one another in psalms and hymns and spiritual songs, singing with grace in your hearts to the Lord."
  - *Interpretation:* Paul applies this principle to the New Testament, showing that Christ's word should dwell richly in believers as God's word did in Joshua.

- **James 1:25** — "But whoso looketh into the perfect law of liberty, and continueth therein, he being not a forgetful hearer, but a doer of the work, this man shall be blessed in his deed."
  - *Interpretation:* James emphasizes both looking into the law and continuing in it, showing that blessing comes from both knowledge and obedience.

### "Be strong and of a good courage" — Courage Command

**Joshua 1:6, 7, 9, 18** — "Be strong and of a good courage... Only be thou strong and very courageous... Have not I commanded thee? Be strong and of a good courage; be not afraid, neither be thou dismayed... only be strong and of a good courage"

- **Deuteronomy 31:6** — "Be strong and of a good courage, fear not, nor be afraid of them: for the LORD thy God, he it is that doth go with thee; he will not fail thee, nor forsake thee."
  - *Interpretation:* Moses first gave this charge to Joshua, which God now repeats, showing the importance of this command for leadership.

- **1 Chronicles 28:20** — "And David said to Solomon his son, Be strong and of good courage, and do it: fear not, neither be thou dismayed: for the LORD God, even my God, will be with thee; he will not fail thee, nor forsake thee, until thou hast finished all the work for the service of the house of the LORD."
  - *Interpretation:* David gives Solomon the same charge, showing this became the standard formula for leadership transition in Israel.

- **2 Timothy 2:1** — "Thou therefore, my son, be strong in the grace that is in Christ Jesus."
  - *Interpretation:* Paul adapts this principle for spiritual leadership, showing that strength comes from God's grace rather than human courage.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Be strong and of a good courage"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about human self-confidence or positive thinking
**Biblical context:** This command is always accompanied by the promise "for the LORD thy God, he it is that doth go with thee; he will not fail thee, nor forsake thee." The courage comes from God's presence, not human strength. It's divine enablement, not natural bravery.

### "This book of the law shall not depart out of thy mouth"
**Common misinterpretation:** This means literal memorization without understanding
**Biblical context:** This includes both speaking and meditating "day and night" for the purpose of "observing to do according to all that is written." It's about internalizing and applying God's word, not just reciting it.

**Chapter 1 establishes the foundation for Joshua's leadership:** God directly commissions Joshua after Moses' death, promising His presence and the land. The repeated command to be strong and courageous emphasizes that leadership requires divine enablement. The requirement to meditate on God's law day and night shows that success comes from obedience to divine revelation. Joshua immediately organizes the people for conquest and secures the eastern tribes' cooperation, demonstrating practical leadership. The people's response shows their recognition of Joshua's divine appointment and their commitment to follow him as they followed Moses. This chapter sets the pattern for successful spiritual leadership: divine commission, God's presence, meditation on Scripture, and practical preparation for God's mission.
