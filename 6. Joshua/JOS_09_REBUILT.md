## CHAPTER SUMMARY

When Canaanite kings hear of Israel's victories, they unite to fight. Gibeon's inhabitants, hearing of Jericho and Ai's destruction, work craftily. They disguise themselves as ambassadors from a far country with old sacks, wine bottles, shoes, and garments, and dry, moldy bread. They go to Gilgal and ask Israel to make a league, claiming to come from afar because of God's fame. Israel questions if they dwell nearby, but the Gibeonites claim to be servants from a distant country who heard of God's works in Egypt and against Sihon and Og. Their elders sent them with provisions for the long journey. Israel takes their provisions but doesn't consult God. Joshua makes peace and a league to let them live, with the princes swearing by God. Three days later, Israel discovers the Gibeonites are nearby neighbors. Israel doesn't attack because the princes swore by God, though the congregation murmurs. The princes insist they cannot break their oath without bringing wrath, so they let the Gibeonites live as wood hewers and water drawers. Joshua confronts the Gibeonites about their deception, declaring them cursed to be bondservants forever. The Gibeonites explain they feared for their lives knowing God commanded Israel to destroy all inhabitants. They submit to Joshua's judgment. Joshua makes them wood hewers and water drawers for the congregation and altar service.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "They did work wilily, and went and made as if they had been ambassadors" — Deceptive Strategy

**Joshua 9:4-6** — "They did work wilily, and went and made as if they had been ambassadors, and took old sacks upon their asses, and wine bottles, old, and rent, and bound up... And they went to Joshua unto the camp at Gilgal, and said unto him, and to the men of Israel, We be come from a far country: now therefore make ye a league with us"

- **Genesis 27:19-24** — "And Jacob said unto his father, I am Esau thy firstborn... And Jacob said unto his father, I am thy son: thy firstborn, Esau. And he said, Bring it near to me, and I will eat of my son's venison, that my soul may bless thee. And he brought it near to him, and he did eat: and he brought him wine, and he drank."
  - *Interpretation:* Jacob's deception of Isaac to receive blessing parallels the Gibeonites' deception to preserve their lives, showing that deception for self-preservation is a recurring biblical pattern.

- **2 Corinthians 11:13-15** — "For such are false apostles, deceitful workers, transforming themselves into the apostles of Christ. And no marvel; for Satan himself is transformed into an angel of light. Therefore it is no great thing if his ministers also be transformed as the ministers of righteousness"
  - *Interpretation:* Paul warns that false workers transform themselves to appear legitimate, exactly what the Gibeonites did by appearing as distant ambassadors.

- **Revelation 12:9** — "And the great dragon was cast out, that old serpent, called the Devil, and Satan, which deceiveth the whole world: he was cast out into the earth, and his angels were cast out with him."
  *Interpretation:* Revelation shows that deception is Satan's primary tactic, which the Gibeonites employed for survival rather than evil purposes.

- **Purpose:** Self-preservation through misrepresentation

- **Meaning:** Covenant, treaty, binding agreement

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 And it came to pass, when all the kings which were on this side Jordan, in the hills, and in the valleys, and in all the coasts of the great sea over against Lebanon, the Hittite, and the Amorite, the Canaanite, the Perizzite, the Hivite, and the Jebusite, heard thereof; 

2 That they gathered themselves together, to fight with Joshua and with Israel, with one accord. 

3 And when the inhabitants of Gibeon heard what Joshua had done unto Jericho and to Ai, 

4 They did work wilily, and went and made as if they had been ambassadors, and took old sacks upon their asses, and wine bottles, old, and rent, and bound up; 

5 And old shoes and clouted upon their feet, and old garments upon them; and all the bread of their provision was dry and mouldy. 

6 And they went to Joshua unto the camp at Gilgal, and said unto him, and to the men of Israel, We be come from a far country: now therefore make ye a league with us. 

7 And the men of Israel said unto the Hivites, Peradventure ye dwell among us; and how shall we make a league with you? 

8 And they said unto Joshua, We are thy servants. And Joshua said unto them, Who are ye? and from whence come ye? 

9 And they said unto him, From a very far country thy servants are come because of the name of the LORD thy God: for we have heard the fame of him, and all that he did in Egypt, 

10 And all that he did to the two kings of the Amorites, that were beyond Jordan, to Sihon king of Heshbon, and to Og king of Bashan, which was at Ashtaroth. 

11 Wherefore our elders and all the inhabitants of our country spake to us, saying, Take victuals with you for the journey, and go to meet them, and say unto them, We are your servants: therefore now make ye a league with us. 

12 This our bread we took hot for our provision out of our houses on the day we came forth to go unto you; but now, behold, it is dry, and it is mouldy: 

13 And these bottles of wine, which we filled, were new; and, behold, they be rent: and these our garments and our shoes are become old by reason of the very long journey. 

14 And the men took of their victuals, and asked not counsel at the mouth of the LORD. 

15 And Joshua made peace with them, and made a league with them, to let them live: and the princes of the congregation sware unto them. 

16 And it came to pass at the end of three days after they had made a league with them, that they heard that they were their neighbours, and that they dwelt among them. 

17 And the children of Israel journeyed, and came unto their cities on the third day. Now their cities were Gibeon, and Chephirah, and Beeroth, and Kirjath-jearim. 

18 And the children of Israel smote them not, because the princes of the congregation had sworn unto them by the LORD God of Israel. And all the congregation murmured against the princes. 

19 But all the princes said unto all the congregation, We have sworn unto them by the LORD God of Israel: now therefore we may not touch them. 

20 This we will do to them; we will even let them live, lest wrath be upon us, because of the oath which we sware unto them. 

21 And the princes said unto them, Let them live; but let them be hewers of wood and drawers of water unto all the congregation; as the princes had promised them. 

22 And Joshua called for them, and he spake unto them, saying, Wherefore have ye beguiled us, saying, We are very far from you; when ye dwell among us? 

23 Now therefore ye are cursed, and there shall none of you be freed from being bondmen, and hewers of wood and drawers of water for the house of my God. 

24 And they answered Joshua, and said, Because it was certainly told thy servants, how that the LORD thy God commanded his servant Moses to give you all the land, and to destroy all the inhabitants of the land from before you, therefore we were sore afraid of their lives because of you, and have done this thing. 

25 And now, behold, we are in thine hand: as it seemeth good and right unto thee to do unto us, do. 

26 And so did he unto them, and delivered them out of the hand of the children of Israel, that they slew them not. 

27 And Joshua made them that day hewers of wood and drawers of water for the congregation, and for the altar of the LORD, even unto this day, in the place which he should choose.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "And asked not counsel at the mouth of the LORD"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is just about making bad decisions
**Biblical context:** This is a serious failure to seek divine guidance in a covenant matter. The result was binding Israel to a deceptive treaty. This shows that decisions affecting God's covenant community require divine counsel.

### "Now therefore ye are cursed, and there shall none of you be freed from being bondmen"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about harsh punishment for deception
**Biblical context:* This is not a curse of destruction but of perpetual servitude. The Gibeonites wanted to live, and they receive life but in service. Their deception results in the very thing they feared—loss of freedom—but preserves their lives.

**Chapter 9 demonstrates the consequences of decisions without divine counsel:** The Gibeonites' fear of Israel's God leads them to crafty deception, which succeeds because Israel fails to seek God's guidance. The old provisions and worn appearance create a convincing false narrative of distant origin. Israel's acceptance of the league without divine counsel creates a binding oath that must be honored even when discovered as deception. The princes' integrity in keeping their oath despite the congregation's murmuring shows the importance of oath-keeping. The Gibeonites' explanation reveals that their deception came from fear of God's command to destroy Canaan's inhabitants. Joshua's judgment preserves their lives but consigns them to perpetual servitude in God's service. This chapter shows that fear of God can lead to both deception and submission, and that human wisdom without divine guidance leads to binding mistakes that must be honored. The Gibeonites become permanent servants in Israel's worship, showing that even deception can be redeemed for divine service.
