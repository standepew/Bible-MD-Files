## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Joshua, now old and stricken in years, is told by God that much land remains to be possessed. God describes the remaining land: Philistine borders from Sihor (before Egypt) to Ekron, including five Philistine lords (Gazathites, Ashdothites, Eshkalonites, Gittites, Ekronites, and Avites). From south to Canaanite land beside Sidonians to Aphek at Amorite borders, including Giblites and all Lebanon from Baal-gad under Mount Hermon to Hamath entrance. God promises to drive out hill country inhabitants from Lebanon to Misrephoth-maim and all Sidonians, commanding Joshua to divide the land by lot to nine tribes and half-Manasseh, while Reuben, Gad, and half-Manasseh already received inheritance east of Jordan from Moses. Moses gave Reuben inheritance from Aroer on Arnon to the city in the river's midst, including Medeba plain, Heshbon and its cities, Dibon, Bamoth-baal, Beth-baal-meon, Jahazah, Kedemoth, Mephaath, Kirjathaim, Sibmah, Zareth-shahar, Beth-peor, Ashdoth-pisgah, Beth-jeshimoth, and all Sihon's kingdom cities, including the Midianite princes slain with Sihon, and Balaam the soothsayer. Reuben's border was Jordan. Moses gave Gad inheritance from Jazer and all Gilead cities to half Ammon's land to Aroer before Rabbah, from Heshbon to Ramath-mizpeh and Betonim, from Mahanaim to Debir border, including valley cities Beth-aram, Beth-nimrah, Succoth, Zaphon, and the rest of Sihon's kingdom to Chinnereth sea. Moses gave half-Manasseh inheritance from Mahanaim, all Bashan, Og's entire kingdom, and Jair's sixty cities in Bashan, plus half Gilead, Ashtaroth, and Edrei to Machir's family. These were the countries Moses distributed in Moab plains east of Jordan by Jericho. Levi received no inheritance; the LORD God of Israel was their inheritance.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Thou art old and stricken in years" — Leadership Transition

**Joshua 13:1** — "Now Joshua was old and stricken in years; and the LORD said unto him, Thou art old and stricken in years, and there remaineth yet very much land to be possessed"

- **Deuteronomy 31:23** — "And he gave Joshua the son of Nun a charge, and said, Be strong and of a good courage: for thou shalt bring the children of Israel into the land which I sware unto them: and I will be with thee."
  - *Interpretation:* Moses earlier commissioned Joshua, who now in old age has largely fulfilled that commission but has unfinished work remaining.

- **Deuteronomy 34:7** — "And Moses was an hundred and twenty years old when he died: his eye was not dim, nor his natural force abated."
  - *Interpretation:* Moses also served until old age, showing that God's servants often continue in leadership until death, though physical strength may decline.

- **Luke 2:25-26** — "And, behold, there was a man in Jerusalem, whose name was Simeon; and the same man was just and devout, waiting for the consolation of Israel: and the Holy Ghost was upon him. And it was revealed unto him by the Holy Ghost, that he should not see death, before he had seen the Lord's Christ."
  *Interpretation:* Simeon also served until old age, showing that elderly servants can still accomplish divine purposes.

### "Only unto the tribe of Levi he gave none inheritance" — Levitical Portion

**Joshua 13:14, 33-35** — "Only unto the tribe of Levi he gave none inheritance; the sacrifices of the LORD God of Israel made by fire are their inheritance... But unto the tribe of Levi Moses gave not any inheritance: the LORD God of Israel was their inheritance, as he said unto them"

- **Numbers 18:20** — "And the LORD spake unto Aaron, Thou shalt have no inheritance in their land, neither shalt thou have any part among them: I am thy part and thine inheritance among the children of Israel."
  - *Interpretation:* God established that the Levites' inheritance would be Himself, not territorial land, showing spiritual inheritance supersedes physical inheritance.

- **Deuteronomy 18:1-2** — "The priests the Levites, and all the tribe of Levi, shall have no part nor inheritance with Israel: they shall eat the offerings of the LORD made by fire, and his inheritance. Therefore shall they have no inheritance among their brethren: the LORD is their inheritance, as he said unto them."
  - *Interpretation:* Deuteronomy repeats this principle, showing that serving God directly provides better inheritance than land.

- **1 Peter 2:5** — "Ye also, as lively stones, are built up a spiritual house, an holy priesthood, to offer up spiritual sacrifices, acceptable to God by Jesus Christ."
  *Interpretation:* Peter shows that believers become a spiritual priesthood with God as their inheritance, building on the Levitical principle.

---

### "Balaam also the son of Beor, the soothsayer, did the children of Israel slay" — Prophet's Death

**Joshua 13:22** — "Balaam also the son of Beor, the soothsayer, did the children of Israel slay with the sword among them that were slain by them"

- **Numbers 31:8** — "And they slew the kings of Midian, beside the rest of them that were slain; namely, Evi, and Rekem, and Zur, and Hur, and Reba, five kings of Midian: Balaam also the son of Beor they slew with the sword."
  - *Interpretation:* Numbers provides the account of Balaam's death during Midian's defeat, showing that false prophets face divine judgment.

- **Numbers 22:18** — "And he answered and said unto Balak, Lo, here I am: have I now any power at all to say any thing? the word that God putteth in my mouth, that shall I speak."
  - *Interpretation:* Balaam claimed to speak only God's words, yet his actions showed he sought personal gain, leading to his death.

- **2 Peter 2:15** — "Which have forsaken the right way, and are gone astray, following the way of Balaam the son of Bosor, who loved the wages of unrighteousness"
  *Interpretation:* Peter uses Balaam as an example of false teachers who love money more than truth, showing his continued negative example.

---

### "The LORD God of Israel was their inheritance" — Spiritual Inheritance

**Joshua 13:33** — "But unto the tribe of Levi Moses gave not any inheritance: the LORD God of Israel was their inheritance, as he said unto them"

- **Psalm 16:5** — "The LORD is the portion of mine inheritance and of my cup: thou maintainest my lot."
  - *Interpretation:* The psalmist uses the same language of inheritance to describe having God as his portion, showing this was a recognized spiritual principle.

- **Psalm 73:26** — "My flesh and my heart faileth: but God is the strength of my heart, and my portion for ever."
  - *Interpretation:* The psalmist contrasts physical weakness with God as his eternal portion, building on the Levitical principle.

- **Jeremiah 10:16** — "The portion of Jacob is not like them: for he is the former of all things; and Israel is the rod of his inheritance: The LORD of hosts is his name."
  *Interpretation:* Jeremiah shows that God's people have God Himself as their inheritance, continuing this spiritual principle.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### STRICKEN IN YEARS
- **Meaning:** Advanced in age, physically declining
- **Context:** Joshua's age limits his active leadership
- **Significance:** Transition period needed
* **Spiritual application:** Wisdom of elderly (Job 12:12)

### INHERITANCE
- **Physical meaning:** Land possession
- **Spiritual meaning:** God's provision and blessing
- **Levitical meaning:** God Himself as inheritance
* **Eternal application:** Eternal inheritance (1 Peter 1:4)

### SOOTHSAYER
- **Meaning:** False prophet, diviner
- **Context:** Balaam who sought personal gain
- **Significance:** Divine judgment on false prophecy
* **New Testament parallel:** False teachers (2 Peter 2:15)

### PORTION
- **Meaning:** Share, allotment, inheritance
- **Context:** God as Levites' portion
- **Spiritual meaning:** God as our satisfaction
* **New Testament application:** Christ as our portion (Colossians 1:27)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Thou art old and stricken in years"
**Common misinterpretation:** This means Joshua is no longer useful
**Biblical context:** This is a divine acknowledgment of Joshua's age but also a commission to continue the work of land distribution. Age brings wisdom and experience, even if physical strength declines.

### "The LORD God of Israel was their inheritance"
**Common misinterpretation:** This means the Levites got nothing
**Biblical context:* This is the highest possible inheritance—God Himself. The Levites received something better than land: direct access to God through sacrificial service, representing the principle that spiritual inheritance surpasses material inheritance.

**Chapter 13 transitions from conquest to distribution:** Joshua's old age signals the need for systematic land distribution rather than continued military conquest. God acknowledges that much land remains unpossessed, particularly Philistine and northern territories. The detailed description of remaining land shows that conquest was not yet complete. The command to divide land by lot to nine tribes and half-Manasseh shows the orderly transition from military to civil administration. The repetition of Levi's unique inheritance—God Himself—demonstrates the principle that spiritual service brings greater reward than material possession. The detailed boundaries for Reuben, Gad, and half-Manasseh show the fulfillment of Moses' earlier promises to these tribes. The mention of Balaam's death serves as a reminder that false prophecy brings divine judgment. The note that some peoples (Geshurites, Maachathites) were not expelled shows that complete conquest would require future effort. This chapter establishes the principle that while military conquest may pause, the work of possessing and distributing the land continues through systematic organization and obedience to divine commands.
