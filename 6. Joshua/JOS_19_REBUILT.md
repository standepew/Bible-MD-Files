## CHAPTER SUMMARY

The second lot goes to Simeon within Judah's inheritance because Judah's portion was too large. Simeon receives Beer-sheba, Moladah, Hazar-shual, Balah, Azem, Eltolad, Bethul, Hormah, Ziklag, Beth-marcaboth, Hazar-susah, Beth-lebaoth, Sharuhen (13 cities); Ain, Remmon, Ether, Ashan (4 cities); and surrounding villages to Baalath-beer and Ramath of the south. The third lot goes to Zebulun, whose border goes to Sarid, then to the sea, Maralah, Dabbasheth, and the river before Jokneam. From Sarid eastward to Chisloth-tabor, Daberath, Japhia, then east to Gittah-hepher, Ittah-kazin, Remmon-methoar to Neah. The border goes north to Hannathon, ending in Jiphthah-el valley. Zebulun receives Kattath, Nahallal, Shimron, Idalah, Beth-lehem (12 cities). The fourth lot goes to Issachar, whose border goes to Jezreel, Chesulloth, Shunem, Hapharaim, Shion, Anaharath, Rabbith, Kishion, Abez, Remeth, En-gannim, En-haddah, Beth-pazzez, reaching to Tabor, Shahazimah, Beth-shemesh, ending at Jordan (16 cities). The fifth lot goes to Asher, whose border goes from Helkath, Hali, Beten, Achshaph, Alammelech, Amad, Misheal, reaching to Carmel westward and Shihor-libnath, then eastward to Beth-dagon, Zebulun, Jiphthah-el valley north of Beth-emek, Neiel, Cabul, Hebron, Rehob, Hammon, Kanah to great Zidon, then to Ramah, strong city Tyre, Hosah, ending at the sea from coast to Achzib, including Ummah, Aphek, Rehob (22 cities). The sixth lot goes to Naphtali, whose coast goes from Heleph, Allon to Zaanannim, Adami, Nekeb, Jabneel, Lakum, ending at Jordan, then westward to Aznoth-tabor, Hukkok, reaching Zebulun south, Asher west, Judah at Jordan eastward. Naphtali receives fenced cities: Ziddim, Zer, Hammath, Rakkath, Chinnereth, Adamah, Ramah, Hazor, Kedesh, Edrei, En-hazor, Iron, Migdal-el, Horem, Beth-anath, Beth-shemesh (19 cities). The seventh lot goes to Dan, whose coast includes Zorah, Eshtaol, Ir-shemesh, Shaalabbin, Ajalon, Jethlah, Elon, Thimnathah, Ekron, Eltekeh, Gibbethon, Baalath, Jehud, Bene-berak, Gath-rimmon, Me-jarkon, Rakkon with border before Japho. Dan's inheritance was too small, so they fought Leshem, took it with the sword, possessed it, dwelt there, and called it Dan after their father. After dividing the land, Israel gave Joshua inheritance: Timnath-serah in mount Ephraim as requested. He built and dwelt there. Eleazar, Joshua, and tribal heads divided inheritances by lot in Shiloh before the LORD at the tabernacle door, completing the country's division.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "The inheritance of the children of Simeon was within the inheritance of the children of Judah" — Shared Territory

**Joshua 19:1, 9** — "And the second lot came forth to Simeon... And their inheritance was within the inheritance of the children of Judah... Out of the portion of the children of Judah was the inheritance of the children of Simeon: for the part of the children of Judah was too much for them"

- **Genesis 49:5-7** — "Simeon and Levi are brethren; instruments of cruelty are in their habitations... I will divide them in Jacob, and scatter them in Israel."
  - *Interpretation:* Jacob's prophecy that Simeon would be scattered is fulfilled by Simeon receiving inheritance within Judah's territory rather than a separate portion.

- **1 Chronicles 4:24-25** — "The sons of Simeon were, Nemuel, and Jamin, Jarib, Zerah, and Shaul: Shallum his son, Mibsam his son, Mishma his son. And the sons of Mishma; Hamuel his son, Zacchur his son, Shimei his son."
  *Interpretation:* Chronicles shows that Simeon's descendants remained, but their scattered inheritance limited their tribal prominence.

---

### "The children of Dan went up to fight against Leshem, and took it" — Territorial Expansion

**Joshua 19:47** — "And the coast of the children of Dan went out too little for them: therefore the children of Dan went up to fight against Leshem, and took it, and smote it with the edge of the sword, and possessed it, and dwelt therein, and called Leshem, Dan, after the name of Dan their father"

- **Judges 18:27-29** — "And they took the things which Micah had made, and the priest which he had, and came unto Laish, unto a people that were at quiet and secure: and they smote them with the edge of the sword, and burnt the city with fire... And they called the name of the city Dan, after the name of Dan their father, which was born unto Israel: howbeit the name of the city was Laish at the first."
  - *Interpretation:* Judges provides more detail, showing that Dan conquered Laish (Leshem) later, establishing a new northern territory and developing idolatrous worship.

- **Judges 1:34** - "And the Amorites forced the children of Dan into the mountain: for they would not suffer them to come down to the valley"
  - *Interpretation:* Judges shows that Dan initially struggled with the Amorites, explaining why they needed to expand northward to secure territory.

---

### "They gave an inheritance to Joshua the son of Nun among them" — Leader's Reward

**Joshua 19:49-51** — "When they had made an end of dividing the land for inheritance by their coasts, the children of Israel gave an inheritance to Joshua the son of Nun among them: According to the word of the LORD they gave him the city which he asked, even Timnath-serah in mount Ephraim"

- **Numbers 14:24** — "But my servant Caleb, because he had another spirit with him, and hath followed me fully, him will I bring into the land whereinto he went; and his seed shall possess it."
  - *Interpretation:* Like Caleb, Joshua receives a special inheritance because of his faithfulness, demonstrating that God rewards those who wholly follow Him.

- **Joshua 24:30** - "And they buried him in the border of his inheritance in Timnath-serah, which is in mount Ephraim, on the north side of the hill of Gaash."
  - *Interpretation:* Joshua's burial place confirms his inheritance location, showing that he remained among his people in death as in life.

---

### "So they made an end of dividing the country" — Completion of Conquest

**Joshua 19:51** — "These are the inheritances, which Eleazar the priest, and Joshua the son of Nun, and the heads of the fathers of the tribes of the children of Israel, divided for an inheritance by lot in Shiloh before the LORD, at the door of the tabernacle of the congregation. So they made an end of dividing the country"

- **Deuteronomy 1:38** - "But ye shall pass over Jordan, and possess this good land; which the LORD God of your fathers giveth you"
  - *Interpretation:* Moses' command to possess the land is now fulfilled through the systematic division of inheritances.

- **Joshua 11:23** - "So Joshua took the whole land, according to all that the LORD said unto Moses; and Joshua gave it for an inheritance unto Israel according to their divisions by their tribes. And the land rested from war"
  - *Interpretation:* Joshua 11 records the completion of conquest, while chapter 19 records the completion of settlement, showing the two phases of possessing the land.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### WITHIN THE INHERITANCE
- **Meaning:** Shared territory within another tribe's land
- **Context:** Simeon within Judah's portion
- **Significance:** Judah's portion was too large
* **Spiritual application:** Shared blessings in Christ (Ephesians 4:4)

### TOO LITTLE
- **Meaning:** Insufficient territory for tribe's needs
- **Context:** Dan's inheritance was too small
- **Resolution:** Military expansion to secure more land
* **Spiritual application:** Seeking God's provision (Matthew 6:33)

### INHERITANCE AMONG THEM
- **Meaning:** Leader receiving portion among people
- **Context:** Joshua receiving Timnath-serah
- **Significance:** Recognition of faithful leadership
* **Christological parallel:** Christ's inheritance among people (John 17:10)

### MADE AN END
- **Meaning:** Completion of task
- **Context:** Finished dividing the country
- **Significance:** Settlement phase completed
* **Spiritual application:** Completion of God's work (Philippians 1:6)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "The inheritance of the children of Simeon was within the inheritance of the children of Judah"
**Common misinterpretation:** This shows Simeon's weakness
**Biblical context:** This shows Judah's generosity and the practical solution to Judah having too much land. It also fulfills Jacob's prophecy about Simeon being scattered (Genesis 49:7).

### "The children of Dan went up to fight against Leshem, and took it"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about aggressive conquest
**Biblical context:* This is about taking initiative when God's provision seems insufficient. Dan actively secured the territory they needed, showing that God's people sometimes must take action to claim their full inheritance.
