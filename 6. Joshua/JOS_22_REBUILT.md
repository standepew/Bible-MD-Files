## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Joshua calls Reubenites, Gadites, and half-Manasseh, commending their obedience to Moses' commands and their faithful service during the conquest. Since God has given rest to their brethren, Joshua dismisses them to return to their possessions east of Jordan, charging them to diligently obey God's law, love God, walk in His ways, keep His commandments, cleave to Him, and serve Him with all heart and soul. Joshua blesses them and sends them away with much riches and spoil to divide with their brethren. The eastern tribes return from Shiloh to Gilead, but at Jordan's border in Canaan, they build a great altar. Israel hears of this and gathers at Shiloh to go to war against them, fearing rebellion. Israel sends Phinehas (Eleazar's son) and ten princes to confront the eastern tribes in Gilead. They accuse them of trespassing against God, rebelling like at Peor, and warn that Achan's sin brought wrath on all Israel. The eastern tribes respond affirming their loyalty to God, explaining they built the altar as a witness for future generations because Jordan separates them from Israel's tabernacle. They fear future generations might say they have no part in the LORD. The altar is not for sacrifices but a witness patterned after the altar of the LORD. Phinehas and the princes are pleased, perceiving that God is among them and that they delivered Israel from God's wrath. They return to Israel with the report, pleasing Israel and preventing war. The eastern tribes call the altar Ed, meaning "Witness," affirming the LORD is God.

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "Ye have kept all that Moses the servant of the LORD commanded you" — Faithful Service

**Joshua 22:2-3** — "And said unto them, Ye have kept all that Moses the servant of the LORD commanded you, and have obeyed my voice in all that I commanded you: Ye have not left your brethren these many days unto this day, but have kept the charge of the commandment of the LORD your God"

- **Numbers 32:1-5** — "Now the children of Reuben and the children of Gad had a very great multitude of cattle: and when they saw the land of Jazer, and the land of Gilead, that, behold, the place was a place for cattle... Wherefore, said they, if we have found grace in thy sight, let this land be given unto thy servants for a possession, and bring us not over Jordan."
  - *Interpretation:* Numbers records the original agreement where these tribes requested land east of Jordan, promising to help their brethren conquer Canaan, which Joshua now commends them for fulfilling.

- **Deuteronomy 3:18-20** - "And I commanded you at that time, saying, The LORD your God hath given you this land to possess it: ye shall pass over armed, before your brethren the children of Israel, all that are meet for the war. But your wives, and your little ones, and your cattle, (for I know that ye have much cattle,) shall abide in the cities which I have given you"
  -Interpretation:* Moses reminded them of their commitment to help their brethren, which Joshua now affirms they fulfilled completely.

### "Is the iniquity of Peor too little for us" — Warning Against Idolatry

**Joshua 22:17-18** — "Is the iniquity of Peor too little for us, from which we are not cleansed until this day, although there was a plague in the congregation of the LORD... That ye must turn away this day from following the LORD? and it will be, seeing ye rebel to day against the LORD, that to morrow he will be wroth with the whole congregation of Israel"

- **Numbers 25:1-9** — "And Israel abode in Shittim, and the people began to commit whoredom with the daughters of Moab... And the LORD said unto Moses, Take all the heads of the people, and hang them up before the LORD against the sun, that the fierce anger of the LORD may be turned away from Israel... And those that died in the plague were twenty and four thousand."
  - *Interpretation:* Numbers records the Peor incident where Israel's idolatry brought plague, showing why Israel was so sensitive to any appearance of rebellion or idolatry.

- **Revelation 2:14** - "But I have a few things against thee, because thou hast there them that hold the doctrine of Balaam, who taught Balac to cast a stumblingblock before the children of Israel, to eat things sacrificed unto idols, and to commit fornication"
  -Interpretation:* Revelation references Balaam's role at Peor, showing this incident remained a warning against idolatry throughout biblical history.

### "Did not Achan the son of Zerah commit a trespass" — Warning Against Individual Sin

**Joshua 22:20** — "Did not Achan the son of Zerah commit a trespass in the accursed thing, and wrath fell on all the congregation of Israel? and that man perished not alone in his iniquity"

- **Joshua 7:1, 20-26** — "But the children of Israel committed a trespass in the accursed thing: for Achan... took of the accursed thing; and the anger of the LORD was kindled against the children of Israel... And Joshua said, Why hast thou troubled us? the LORD shall trouble thee this day. And all Israel stoned him with stones, and burned them with fire"
  - *Interpretation:* Joshua 7 records how Achan's individual sin brought defeat and judgment on all Israel, showing why the congregation was concerned about any trespass.

- **1 Corinthians 5:6** - "Your glorying is not good. Know ye not that a little leaven leaveneth the whole lump?"
  -Interpretation:* Paul uses this principle that individual sin affects the whole community, which Israel understood from Achan's example.

### "That it may be a witness between us, and you, and our generations after us" — Memorial Purpose

**Joshua 22:27-28** — "For the LORD hath made Jordan a border between us and you... Therefore we said, Let us now prepare to build us an altar, not for burnt offering, nor for sacrifice: But that it may be a witness between us, and you, and our generations after us"

- **Genesis 31:44-52** — "Now therefore come thou, let us make a covenant, I and thou; and let it be for a witness between me and thee... And Jacob said to his brethren, Gather stones; and they took stones, and made an heap: and they did eat there upon the heap. And Laban called it Jegar-sahadutha: but Jacob called it Galeed. And Laban said, This heap be a witness, and this pillar be a witness, that I will not pass over this heap to thee, and that thou shalt not pass over this heap or this pillar unto me, for harm."
  - *Interpretation:* Jacob and Laban also built a memorial heap as a witness between them, showing this was a recognized method of establishing binding agreements.

- **Joshua 4:7** - "Then ye shall answer them, That the waters of Jordan were cut off before the ark of the covenant of the LORD; when it passed over Jordan, the waters of Jordan were cut off: and these stones shall be for a memorial unto the children of Israel for ever"
  -Interpretation:* Joshua also used memorial stones to preserve testimony for future generations, showing this was a consistent pattern in his leadership.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Is the iniquity of Peor too little for us"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about holding grudges
**Biblical context:** This is about remembering past consequences to prevent future sin. Israel remembers how idolatry at Peor brought plague, showing they understood the seriousness of any appearance of rebellion.

### "That it may be a witness between us, and you, and our generations after us"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about division
**Biblical context:* This is about preventing division through a shared memorial. The altar was meant to maintain unity between east and west Jordan tribes by reminding future generations they all worship the same God.
