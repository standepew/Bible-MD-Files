## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### HOPE
- **Meaning:** "Divine expectation, Christ's anticipation"
- **Context:** Lively hope through resurrection of Jesus Christ, demonstrating Christ's expecting work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's hope and His power to anticipate perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual hope (Romans 8:29)

### FAITH
- **Meaning:** "Divine trust, Christ's belief"
- **Context:** Faith more precious than gold tried by fire, demonstrating Christ's trusting work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's faith and His power to trust perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual faith (Exodus 24:8)

### HOLY
- **Meaning:** "Divine purity, Christ's righteousness"
- **Context:** Called to be holy as God is holy, demonstrating Christ's purifying work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's holy and His power to purify completely
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual holy (John 3:3)

### BLOOD
- **Meaning:** "Divine sacrifice, Christ's redemption"
- **Context:** Precious blood of Christ as lamb without blemish, demonstrating Christ's redeeming work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's blood and His power to redeem eternally
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual blood (Matthew 6:20)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Being born again, not of corruptible seed, but of incorruptible, by the word of God"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any spiritual rebirth or religious experience
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific new birth - The rebirth represents Christ's regeneration through His eternal word, not human effort.

### "Be ye holy; for I am holy"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any moral perfection or religious piety
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific divine holiness - The holiness represents Christ's perfect righteousness that believers are called to emulate, not human morality.
