## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### STONE
- **Meaning:** "Divine foundation, Christ's building"
- **Context:** Living stones built into spiritual house with Christ as chief cornerstone, demonstrating Christ's foundational work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's stone and His power to build eternally
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual stone (1 Corinthians 3:2)

### PRIESTHOOD
- **Meaning:** "Divine service, Christ's ministry"
- **Context:** Royal priesthood offering spiritual sacrifices, demonstrating Christ's ministering work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's priesthood and His power to serve perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual priesthood (Psalm 34:8)

### LIBERTY
- **Meaning:** "Divine freedom, Christ's deliverance"
- **Context:** Christian liberty not used for maliciousness but as servants of God, demonstrating Christ's freeing work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's liberty and His power to deliver completely
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual liberty (Ephesians 2:20)

### SHEPHERD
- **Meaning:** "Divine caretaker, Christ's leadership"
- **Context:** Christ as Shepherd and Bishop who returns wandering sheep, demonstrating Christ's caring work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's shepherd and His power to care eternally
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual shepherd (Psalm 118:22)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Ye are a chosen generation, a royal priesthood, an holy nation, a peculiar people"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any special status or religious privilege
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific calling of believers - The priesthood represents Christ's royal service through His people, not human status.

### "Submit yourselves to every ordinance of man for the Lord's sake"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any government authority or civil obedience
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific submission to authority - The submission represents Christ's orderly submission for God's purposes, not blind obedience.
