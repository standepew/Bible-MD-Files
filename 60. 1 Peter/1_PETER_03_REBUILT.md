## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### SUBJECTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine submission, Christ's obedience"
- **Context:** Wife's subjection to husband that can win unbelieving husbands, demonstrating Christ's submitting work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's subjection and His power to submit perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual subjection (Ephesians 5:22)

### ADORNING
- **Meaning:** "Divine beauty, Christ's character"
- **Context:** Hidden man of heart as meek and quiet spirit, demonstrating Christ's beautifying work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's adorning and His power to beautify internally
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual adorning (1 Timothy 2:9)

### CONSCIENCE
- **Meaning:** "Divine awareness, Christ's moral compass"
- **Context:** Good conscience that answers for hope, demonstrating Christ's moral work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's conscience and His power to guide morally
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual conscience (Genesis 18:12)

### BAPTISM
- **Meaning:** "Divine cleansing, Christ's purification"
- **Context:** Baptism saves through resurrection as answer of good conscience, demonstrating Christ's cleansing work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's baptism and His power to purify completely
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual baptism (Ephesians 5:25)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Be ye all of one mind, having compassion one of another, love as brethren, be pitiful, be courteous"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any social harmony or kindness
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific spiritual unity - The unity represents Christ's oneness of mind and purpose among believers, not human agreement.

### "Sanctify the Lord God in your hearts: and be ready always to give an answer to every man that asketh you a reason of the hope that is in you"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any apologetics or religious debate
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific spiritual defense - The answer represents Christ's reasoned hope through sanctified hearts, not intellectual arguments.
