## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### FALSE TEACHERS
- **Meaning:** "Divine deceivers, Christ's opponents"
- **Context:** False teachers bringing damnable heresies and denying Lord, demonstrating Christ's opposing work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's false teachers and His power to oppose error
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual false teachers (Deuteronomy 13:1)

### JUDGMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine justice, Christ's punishment"
- **Context:** Divine judgment on false teachers and sinners, demonstrating Christ's judging work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's judgment and His power to judge righteously
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual judgment (Jude 1:4)

### CORRUPTION
- **Meaning:** "Divine decay, Christ's sinfulness"
- **Context:** Corruption that false teachers follow and perish in, demonstrating Christ's corrupting work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's corruption and His power to corrupt sinfully
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual corruption (Jude 1:6)

### LIBERTY
- **Meaning:** "Divine freedom, Christ's deliverance"
- **Context:** False promise of liberty that leads to bondage, demonstrating Christ's freeing work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's liberty and His power to deliver truly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual liberty (Genesis 7:1)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "The dog is turned to his own vomit again; and the sow that was washed to her wallowing in the mire"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any backsliding or relapse
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific spiritual apostasy - The return represents Christ's rejection of those who turn from holy commandment after knowing the way of righteousness, not human weakness.

### "For it had been better for them not to have known the way of righteousness, than, after they have known it, to turn from the holy commandment delivered unto them"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any ignorance or lack of knowledge
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific knowledge accountability - The knowledge represents Christ's revelation that brings greater responsibility, not human education.
