## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### SCOFFERS
- **Meaning:** "Divine mockers, Christ's opponents"
- **Context:** Scoffers in last days denying Christ's coming, demonstrating Christ's opposing work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's scoffers and His power to oppose mockery
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual scoffers (Jude 1:17)

### CREATION
- **Meaning:** "Divine making, Christ's foundation"
- **Context:** Creation by God's word with heavens and earth, demonstrating Christ's creating work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's creation and His power to make perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual creation (1 Timothy 4:1)

### JUDGMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine justice, Christ's punishment"
- **Context:** Day of judgment with fire destroying ungodly, demonstrating Christ's judging work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's judgment and His power to judge righteously
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual judgment (Genesis 1:6)

### GRACE
- **Meaning:** "Divine favor, Christ's blessing"
- **Context:** Growing in grace and knowledge of Christ, demonstrating Christ's favoring work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's grace and His power to favor eternally
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual grace (Genesis 7:23)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "One day is with the Lord as a thousand years, and a thousand years as one day"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any time perspective or patience
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific eternal perspective - The time represents Christ's eternal nature that transcends human time, not human patience.

### "The day of the Lord will come as a thief in the night"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any surprise or unexpected event
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific sudden return - The thief represents Christ's unexpected coming that requires constant readiness, not human surprise.
