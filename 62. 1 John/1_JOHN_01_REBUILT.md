## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### WORD
- **Meaning:** "Divine revelation, Christ's communication"
- **Context:** Word of life manifested and handled by apostles, demonstrating Christ's revealing work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's word and His power to communicate perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual word (John 1:1)

### LIGHT
- **Meaning:** "Divine illumination, Christ's righteousness"
- **Context:** God is light with no darkness, demonstrating Christ's illuminating work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's light and His power to illuminate completely
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual light (John 8:12)

### FELLOWSHIP
- **Meaning:** "Divine communion, Christ's relationship"
- **Context:** Fellowship with Father and Son through walking in light, demonstrating Christ's relating work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's fellowship and His power to relate intimately
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual fellowship (Ephesians 5:8)

### SIN
- **Meaning:** "Divine corruption, Christ's atonement"
- **Context:** Sin confessed and forgiven through Christ's blood, demonstrating Christ's atoning work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's sin and His power to atone completely
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sin (Psalm 32:5)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "If we say that we have no sin, we deceive ourselves, and the truth is not in us"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any self-deception or denial
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific spiritual reality - The deception represents Christ's truth about human sinfulness, not human psychology.

### "God is light, and in him is no darkness at all"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any goodness or purity
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific divine nature - The light represents Christ's perfect righteousness that excludes all darkness, not human morality.
