## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### SPIRITS
- **Meaning:** "Divine beings, Christ's messengers"
- **Context:** Testing spirits to distinguish truth from error, demonstrating Christ's discerning work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's spirits and His power to discern perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual spirits (1 John 5:1)

### LOVE
- **Meaning:** "Divine affection, Christ's compassion"
- **Context:** God's love manifested through sending Son, demonstrating Christ's loving work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's love and His power to love completely
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual love (John 1:14)

### FLESH
- **Meaning:** "Divine incarnation, Christ's humanity"
- **Context:** Christ coming in flesh as test of true spirits, demonstrating Christ's incarnating work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's flesh and His power to become human perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual flesh (Romans 8:31)

### PERFECT
- **Meaning:** "Divine completion, Christ's maturity"
- **Context:** Perfect love that casts out fear, demonstrating Christ's completing work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's perfect and His power to complete fully
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual perfect (John 10:27)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "God is love"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any universal love or human affection
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific divine nature - The love represents Christ's sacrificial nature that sent His Son as propitiation, not human emotion.

### "Perfect love casteth out fear"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any confidence or courage
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific spiritual boldness - The perfect love represents Christ's boldness in judgment that comes from dwelling in God, not human bravery.
