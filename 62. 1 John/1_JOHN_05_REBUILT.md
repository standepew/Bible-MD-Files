## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### FAITH
- **Meaning:** "Divine trust, Christ's belief"
- **Context:** Faith as victory that overcomes the world, demonstrating Christ's trusting work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's faith and His power to trust perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual faith (John 1:12)

### VICTORY
- **Meaning:** "Divine triumph, Christ's conquest"
- **Context:** Victory over world through faith in Christ, demonstrating Christ's conquering work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's victory and His power to conquer completely
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual victory (1 Corinthians 15:57)

### WITNESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine testimony, Christ's evidence"
- **Context:** Threefold witness of Father, Word, Holy Ghost, demonstrating Christ's testifying work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's witness and His power to testify perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual witness (John 19:34)

### IDOLS
- **Meaning:** "Divine exclusivity, Christ's uniqueness"
- **Context:** Final warning to keep from idols, demonstrating Christ's exclusive work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's idols and His power to be unique completely
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual idols (Matthew 3:16)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "For there are three that bear record in heaven, the Father, the Word, and the Holy Ghost: and these three are one"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any unity or cooperation
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific divine testimony - The three represent Christ's complete witness of Father, Son, and Holy Ghost as one divine testimony, not human agreement.

### "All unrighteousness is sin: and there is a sin not unto death"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any sin classification or judgment
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific spiritual discernment - The sin represents Christ's distinction between sins that lead to death vs those that don't, not human moral categories.
