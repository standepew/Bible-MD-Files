## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### TRUTH
- **Meaning:** "Divine reality, Christ's revelation"
- **Context:** Truth that dwells in believers and brings love, demonstrating Christ's revealing work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's truth and His power to reveal perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual truth (John 17:17)

### COMMANDMENT
- **Meaning:** "Divine instruction, Christ's teaching"
- **Context:** Commandment to love one another as evidence of walking in truth, demonstrating Christ's instructing work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's commandment and His power to instruct perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual commandment (Ephesians 4:15)

### DECEIVERS
- **Meaning:** "Divine opponents, Christ's enemies"
- **Context:** Deceivers denying Christ's incarnation, demonstrating Christ's opposing work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's deceivers and His power to oppose error
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual deceivers (1 John 2:7)

### DOCTRINE
- **Meaning:** "Divine teaching, Christ's instruction"
- **Context:** Doctrine of Christ as test of having Father and Son, demonstrating Christ's teaching work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's doctrine and His power to teach perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual doctrine (John 13:34)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "If there come any unto you, and bring not this doctrine, receive him not into your house, neither bid him God speed"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any hospitality or social exclusion
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific spiritual separation - The house represents Christ's spiritual household that must be protected from false doctrine, not human hospitality.

### "For he that biddeth him God speed is partaker of his evil deeds"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any association or endorsement
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific spiritual complicity - The participation represents Christ's warning against supporting false teachers, not human social interaction.
