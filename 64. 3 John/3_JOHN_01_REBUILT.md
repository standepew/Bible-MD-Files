## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### TRUTH
- **Meaning:** "Divine reality, Christ's revelation"
- **Context:** Truth that Gaius walks in and brings joy to John, demonstrating Christ's revealing work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's truth and His power to reveal perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual truth (3 John 1:4)

### FAITHFULLY
- **Meaning:** "Divine service, Christ's ministry"
- **Context:** Faithful service to brethren and strangers, demonstrating Christ's serving work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's faithfully and His power to serve completely
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual faithfully (2 John 1:4)

### PREEMINENCE
- **Meaning:** "Divine pride, Christ's opposition"
- **Context:** Diotrephes loving preeminence over others, demonstrating Christ's opposing work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's preeminence and His power to oppose pride
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual preeminence (Romans 12:13)

### GOOD
- **Meaning:** "Divine righteousness, Christ's character"
- **Context:** Good works as evidence of knowing God, demonstrating Christ's righteous work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's good and His power to be righteous perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual good (1 Corinthians 9:14)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "I have no greater joy than to hear that my children walk in truth"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any parental pride or happiness
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific spiritual joy - The joy represents Christ's delight in believers walking in truth, not human emotional satisfaction.

### "Beloved, follow not that which is evil, but that which is good. He that doeth good is of God: but he that doeth evil hath not seen God"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any moral advice or ethical teaching
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific spiritual reality - The good represents Christ's righteousness that comes from knowing God, not human moral achievement.
