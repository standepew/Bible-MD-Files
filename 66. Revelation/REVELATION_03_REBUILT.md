## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "Behold, I stand at the door, and knock: if any man hear my voice, and open the door, I will come in to him, and will sup with him, and he with me"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any invitation or fellowship
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific spiritual relationship - The door represents Christ's invitation to intimate fellowship with Him, not human hospitality.

### "I know thy works, that thou art neither cold nor hot: I would thou wert cold or hot. So then because thou art lukewarm, and neither cold nor hot, I will spue thee out of my mouth"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any temperature or enthusiasm
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific spiritual condition - The lukewarm represents Christ's rejection of complacent half-hearted commitment, not human temperature.
