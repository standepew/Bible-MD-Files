## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### SEVEN SEALS
- **Meaning:** "Divine revelation, Christ's disclosure"
- **Context:** Book sealed with seven seals that only Christ can open, demonstrating Christ's disclosing work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's seven seals and His power to disclose perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual seven seals (Daniel 12:4)

### LION OF JUDAH
- **Meaning:** "Divine royalty, Christ's kingship"
- **Context:** Lion of tribe of Judah who prevailed to open the book, demonstrating Christ's kingly work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's lion of Judah and His power to reign perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual lion of Judah (Genesis 49:9)

### LAMB
- **Meaning:** "Divine sacrifice, Christ's atonement"
- **Context:** Lamb as it had been slain with seven horns and seven eyes, demonstrating Christ's atoning work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's lamb and His power to atone perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual lamb (Isaiah 11:1)

### NEW SONG
- **Meaning:** "Divine praise, Christ's redemption"
- **Context:** New song sung by redeemed ones about Christ's worthiness, demonstrating Christ's redeeming work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's new song and His power to redeem perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual new song (Psalm 33:3)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "And no man in heaven, nor in earth, neither under the earth, was able to open the book, neither to look thereon"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any inability or unworthiness
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific unique worthiness - The inability represents Christ's complete uniqueness as the only one worthy to open God's revelation, not human limitation.

### "Worthy is the Lamb that was slain to receive power, and riches, and wisdom, and strength, and honour, and glory, and blessing"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any worthiness or praise
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific sacrificial worthiness - The worthiness represents Christ's complete worthiness through His sacrificial death, not human achievement.
