## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### WHITE HORSE
- **Meaning:** "Divine conquest, Christ's victory"
- **Context:** White horse with bow and crown going forth to conquer, demonstrating Christ's conquering work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's white horse and His power to conquer perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual white horse (Zechariah 1:8)

### RED HORSE
- **Meaning:** "Divine warfare, Christ's judgment"
- **Context:** Red horse taking peace from earth with great sword, demonstrating Christ's judging work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's red horse and His power to judge righteously
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual red horse (Matthew 24:7)

### BLACK HORSE
- **Meaning:** "Divine scarcity, Christ's provision"
- **Context:** Black horse with balances representing famine, demonstrating Christ's providing work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's black horse and His power to provide perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual black horse (Ezekiel 4:16)

### PALE HORSE
- **Meaning:** "Divine death, Christ's power"
- **Context:** Pale horse named Death with Hell following, demonstrating Christ's death-dealing work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's pale horse and His power to deal death completely
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual pale horse (Jeremiah 14:12)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "And I saw, and behold a white horse: and he that sat on him had a bow; and a crown was given unto him: and he went forth conquering, and to conquer"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any conquest or victory
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific divine conquest - The white horse represents Christ's complete conquest through His divine authority, not human military victory.

### "And the kings of the earth, and the great men, and the rich men, and the chief captains, and the mighty men, and every bondman, and every free man, hid themselves in the dens and in the rocks of the mountains"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any fear or hiding
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific final judgment - The hiding represents all humanity's inability to stand before Christ's wrath, not human fear.
