## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### FOUR WINDS
- **Meaning:** "Divine judgment, Christ's power"
- **Context:** Four winds held by angels until servants are sealed, demonstrating Christ's controlling work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's four winds and His power to control perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual four winds (Jeremiah 49:36)

### SEAL
- **Meaning:** "Divine protection, Christ's preservation"
- **Context:** Seal of living God on servants' foreheads, demonstrating Christ's preserving work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's seal and His power to preserve perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual seal (Ezekiel 9:4)

- **Meaning:** "Divine purity, Christ's righteousness"
- **Context:** White robes washed in blood of Lamb, demonstrating Christ's purifying work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's white robes and His power to purify perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual white robes (Ezekiel 48:31)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "And there were sealed an hundred and forty and four thousand of all the tribes of the children of Israel"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any literal number or group
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific complete protection - The 144,000 represents Christ's complete protection of His people through divine sealing, not human counting.

### "These are they which came out of great tribulation, and have washed their robes, and made them white in the blood of the Lamb"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any suffering or cleansing
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific tribulation victory - The robes washed white represent Christ's complete cleansing through His sacrificial blood, not human purification.
