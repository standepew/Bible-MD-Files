## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### BOTTOMLESS PIT
- **Meaning:** "Divine imprisonment, Christ's confinement"
- **Context:** Bottomless pit opened releasing locusts, demonstrating Christ's confining work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's bottomless pit and His power to confine perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual bottomless pit (Isaiah 14:15)

### LOCUSTS
- **Meaning:** "Divine torment, Christ's judgment"
- **Context:** Locusts from pit tormenting men without seal, demonstrating Christ's judging work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's locusts and His power to judge righteously
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual locusts (Exodus 10:12)

### APOLLYON
- **Meaning:** "Divine destruction, Christ's power"
- **Context:** Angel of bottomless pit named Apollyon (Destroyer), demonstrating Christ's destroying work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's Apollyon and His power to destroy completely
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual Apollyon (Joel 2:25)

### HORSEMEN
- **Meaning:** "Divine warriors, Christ's army"
- **Context:** Two hundred million horsemen killing third part of men, demonstrating Christ's warring work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's horsemen and His power to wage war perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual horsemen (Joel 2:4)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "And it was commanded them that they should not hurt the grass of the earth, neither any green thing, neither any tree; but only those men which have not the seal of God in their foreheads"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any protection or exemption
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific divine protection - The seal represents Christ's complete protection of His people during judgment, not human safety.

### "And the rest of the men which were not killed by these plagues yet repented not of the works of their hands, that they should not worship devils, and idols of gold, and silver, and brass, and stone, and of wood"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any stubbornness or idolatry
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific human rebellion - The impenitence represents humanity's complete refusal to repent even amid judgment, not human weakness.
