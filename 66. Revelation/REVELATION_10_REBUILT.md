## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### MIGHTY ANGEL
- **Meaning:** "Divine messenger, Christ's representative"
- **Context:** Mighty angel with little book standing on sea and earth, demonstrating Christ's representing work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's mighty angel and His power to represent perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual mighty angel (Exodus 34:29)

### SEVEN THUNDERS
- **Meaning:** "Divine mystery, Christ's revelation"
- **Context:** Seven thunders whose voices were sealed up, demonstrating Christ's revealing work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's seven thunders and His power to reveal perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual seven thunders (Daniel 12:7)

### LITTLE BOOK
- **Meaning:** "Divine message, Christ's word"
- **Context:** Little book that was sweet in mouth but bitter in belly, demonstrating Christ's word work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's little book and His power to communicate perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual little book (John 12:28)

### PROPHESY
- **Meaning:** "Divine proclamation, Christ's testimony"
- **Context:** Command to prophesy again before many peoples, demonstrating Christ's proclaiming work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's prophecy and His power to proclaim perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual prophecy (Hebrews 6:13)

1 And I saw another mighty angel come down from heaven, clothed with a cloud: and a rainbow was upon his head, and his face was as it were the sun, and his feet as pillars of fire:

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "And when the seven thunders had uttered their voices, I was about to write: and I heard a voice from heaven saying unto me, Seal up those things which the seven thunders uttered, and write them not"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any secrecy or hidden knowledge
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific divine timing - The sealing represents Christ's complete control over when revelations are to be disclosed, not human secrecy.

### "Take it, and eat it up; and it shall make thy belly bitter, but it shall be in my mouth sweet as honey"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any experience or message
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific prophetic calling - The sweet and bitter represents Christ's dual nature of prophetic truth that is sweet to receive but bitter to digest, not human experience.
