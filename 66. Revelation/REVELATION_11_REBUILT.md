## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### TEMPLE
- **Meaning:** "Divine dwelling, Christ's presence"
- **Context:** Temple of God measured for worship, demonstrating Christ's dwelling work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's temple and His power to dwell perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual temple (Ezekiel 40:3)

### WITNESSES
- **Meaning:** "Divine testimony, Christ's proclamation"
- **Context:** Two witnesses prophesying with miraculous power, demonstrating Christ's testifying work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's witnesses and His power to testify perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual witnesses (Zechariah 4:3)

### BEAST
- **Meaning:** "Divine opposition, Christ's enemy"
- **Context:** Beast from bottomless pit killing witnesses, demonstrating Christ's opposing work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's beast and His power to oppose completely
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual beast (Luke 21:24)

### KINGDOMS
- **Meaning:** "Divine rule, Christ's sovereignty"
- **Context:** Kingdoms of world become Christ's kingdoms, demonstrating Christ's ruling work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's kingdoms and His power to reign perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual kingdoms (Daniel 2:44)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "And I will give power unto my two witnesses, and they shall prophesy a thousand two hundred and threescore days, clothed in sackcloth"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any prophecy or testimony
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific prophetic ministry - The witnesses represent Christ's complete prophetic power through miraculous signs, not human preaching.

### "The kingdoms of this world are become the kingdoms of our Lord, and of his Christ; and he shall reign for ever and ever"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any political or earthly victory
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific divine sovereignty - The kingdoms represent Christ's complete takeover of all earthly authority, not human government.
