- **Meaning:** "Divine judgment, Christ's wrath"

- **Significance:** Model of Christ's seven last plagues and His power to judge completely

- **Meaning:** "Divine purity, Christ's holiness"
- **Context:** Sea of glass where victorious saints stand, demonstrating Christ's purifying work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's sea of glass and His power to purify perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sea of glass (Revelation 4:6)

### SONG OF MOSES
- **Meaning:** "Divine praise, Christ's redemption"
- **Context:** Song of Moses and Lamb sung by victorious saints, demonstrating Christ's redeeming work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's song of Moses and His power to redeem perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual song of Moses (Deuteronomy 32:3)

### GOLDEN VIALS
- **Meaning:** "Divine wrath, Christ's judgment"

- **Significance:** Model of Christ's golden vials and His power to execute wrath perfectly

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 And I saw another sign in heaven, great and marvellous, seven angels having the seven last plagues; for in them is filled up the wrath of God.  

2 And I saw as it were a sea of glass mingled with fire: and them that had gotten the victory over the beast, and over his image, and over his mark, and over the number of his name, stand on the sea of glass, having the harps of God.  

3 And they sing the song of Moses the servant of God, and the song of the Lamb, saying, Great and marvellous are thy works, Lord God Almighty; just and true are thy ways, thou King of saints.  

4 Who shall not fear thee, O Lord, and glorify thy name? for thou only art holy: for all nations shall come and worship before thee; for thy judgments are made manifest.  

5 And after that I looked, and, behold, the temple of the tabernacle of the testimony in heaven was opened:  

6 And the seven angels came out of the temple, having the seven plagues, clothed in pure and white linen, and having their breasts girded with golden girdles.  

7 And one of the four beasts gave unto the seven angels seven golden vials full of the wrath of God, who liveth for ever and ever.  

8 And the temple was filled with smoke from the glory of God, and from his power; and no man was able to enter into the temple, till the seven plagues of the seven angels were fulfilled.  

9 And the temple was filled with smoke from the glory of God, and from his power; and no man was able to enter into the temple, till the seven plagues of the seven angels were fulfilled.  

10 And the temple was filled with smoke from the glory of God, and from his power; and no man was able to enter into the temple, till the seven plagues of the seven angels were fulfilled.  

11 And the temple was filled with smoke from the glory of God, and from his power; and no man was able to enter into the temple, till the seven plagues of the seven angels were fulfilled.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "And them that had gotten the victory over the beast, and over his image, and over his mark, and over the number of his name, stand on the sea of glass, having the harps of God"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any victory or achievement
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific spiritual victory - The victory represents Christ's complete victory over the beast system through faith, not human accomplishment.

### "And the temple was filled with smoke from the glory of God, and from his power; and no man was able to enter into the temple, till the seven plagues of the seven angels were fulfilled"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any smoke or obstruction
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific divine presence - The smoke represents Christ's complete divine glory preventing access until judgment is complete, not human limitation.
