## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### VIALS
- **Meaning:** "Divine wrath, Christ's judgment"
- **Context:** Seven vials poured out bringing plagues, demonstrating Christ's judging work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's vials and His power to judge completely
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual vials (Exodus 9:9)

### SORES
- **Meaning:** "Divine affliction, Christ's punishment"
- **Context:** Grievous sores on beast worshippers, demonstrating Christ's punishing work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's sores and His power to punish righteously
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual sores (Deuteronomy 28:35)

### DARKNESS
- **Meaning:** "Divine judgment, Christ's wrath"
- **Context:** Darkness upon beast's kingdom, demonstrating Christ's wrathful work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's darkness and His power to judge completely
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual darkness (Exodus 7:17)

### ARMAGEDDON
- **Meaning:** "Divine battle, Christ's warfare"
- **Context:** Gathering place for final battle, demonstrating Christ's warring work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's Armageddon and His power to wage war perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual Armageddon (Numbers 21:6)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 And I heard a great voice out of the temple saying to the seven angels, Go your ways, and pour out the vials of the wrath of God upon the earth.  

2 And the first went, and poured out his vial upon the earth; and there fell a noisome and grievous sore upon the men which had the mark of the beast, and upon them which worshipped his image.  

3 And the second angel poured out his vial upon the sea; and it became as the blood of a dead man: and every living soul died in the sea.  

4 And the third angel poured out his vial upon the rivers and fountains of waters; and they became blood.  

5 And I heard the angel of the waters say, Thou art righteous, O Lord, which art, and wast, and shalt be, because thou hast judged thus.  

6 For they have shed the blood of saints and prophets, and thou hast given them blood to drink; for they are worthy.  

7 And I heard another out of the altar say, Even so, Lord God Almighty, true and righteous are thy judgments.  

8 And the fourth angel poured out his vial upon the sun; and power was given unto him to scorch men with fire.  

9 And men were scorched with great heat, and blasphemed the name of God, which hath power over these plagues: and they repented not to give him glory.  

10 And the fifth angel poured out his vial upon the seat of the beast; and his kingdom was full of darkness; and they gnawed their tongues for pain,

14 For they are the spirits of devils, working miracles, which go forth unto the kings of the earth and of the whole world, to gather them to the battle of that great day of God Almighty.

19 And the great city was divided into three parts, and the cities of the nations fell: and great Babylon came in remembrance before God, to give unto her the cup of the wine of the fierceness of his wrath.  

20 And every island fled away, and the mountains were not found.  

21 And there fell upon men a great hail out of heaven, every stone about the weight of a talent: and men blasphemed God because of the plague of the hail; for the plague thereof was exceeding great.  

22 And there fell upon men a great hail out of heaven, every stone about the weight of a talent: and men blasphemed God because of the plague of the hail; for the plague thereof was exceeding great.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "And men were scorched with great heat, and blasphemed the name of God, which hath power over these plagues: and they repented not to give him glory"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any suffering or stubbornness
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific human rebellion - The blasphemy represents humanity's complete refusal to repent even amid severe judgment, not human weakness.

### "Behold, I come as a thief. Blessed is he that watcheth, and keepeth his garments, lest he walk naked, and they see his shame"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any watchfulness or preparation
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific sudden return - The thief represents Christ's complete unexpectedness in His return, not human burglary.
