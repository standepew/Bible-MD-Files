## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### NEW HEAVEN
- **Meaning:** "Divine creation, Christ's renewal"
- **Context:** New heaven and new earth replacing old creation, demonstrating Christ's renewing work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's new heaven and His power to renew perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual new heaven (Isaiah 65:17)

### NEW JERUSALEM
- **Meaning:** "Divine city, Christ's dwelling"
- **Context:** Holy city descending from heaven as bride, demonstrating Christ's dwelling work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's new Jerusalem and His power to dwell perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual new Jerusalem (2 Peter 3:13)

### WATER OF LIFE
- **Meaning:** "Divine refreshment, Christ's salvation"
- **Context:** Fountain of water of life given freely to thirsty, demonstrating Christ's saving work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's water of life and His power to save perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual water of life (John 4:14)

### PURE GOLD
- **Meaning:** "Divine purity, Christ's holiness"
- **Context:** City streets of pure gold like clear glass, demonstrating Christ's purifying work
- **Significance:** Model of Christ's pure gold and His power to purify perfectly
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual pure gold (1 John 5:4)

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "And God shall wipe away all tears from their eyes; and there shall be no more death, neither sorrow, nor crying, neither shall there be any more pain: for the former things are passed away"
**Common mispretation:** This is about any comfort or healing
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific eternal comfort - The wiping of tears represents Christ's complete removal of all suffering in eternity, not human consolation.

### "He that overcometh shall inherit all things; and I will be his God, and he shall be my son"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about any achievement or inheritance
**Biblical context:** This is about Christ's specific spiritual victory - The overcoming represents Christ's complete victory through faith that results in divine inheritance, not human accomplishment.
