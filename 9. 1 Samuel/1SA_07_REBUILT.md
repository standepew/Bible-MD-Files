## CHAPTER SUMMARY

Kirjath-jearim men fetched the ark, bringing it to Abinadab's house on the hill, sanctifying Eleazar his son to keep it. The ark abode in Kirjath-jearim twenty years while Israel lamented after the LORD. Samuel spoke to Israel, saying if they returned to the LORD with all hearts, they should put away strange gods and Ashtaroth, prepare hearts to the LORD, serve Him only, and He would deliver them from Philistines. Israel put away Baalim and Ashtaroth, serving the LORD only. Samuel told Israel to gather at Mizpeh where he would pray for them. They gathered at Mizpeh, drew water, poured it before the LORD, fasted that day, and confessed sin. Samuel judged Israel at Mizpeh. Philistines heard Israel gathered at Mizpeh and went up against them. Israel was afraid of Philistines and told Samuel to cease not crying to the LORD for deliverance. Samuel took a sucking lamb for burnt offering wholly to the LORD and cried for Israel; the LORD heard him. As Samuel offered the burnt offering, Philistines drew near for battle, but the LORD thundered with great thunder upon Philistines, discomfiting them; they were smitten before Israel. Israel went from Mizpeh, pursued Philistines, smiting them until Beth-car. Samuel set a stone between Mizpeh and Shen, naming it Ebenezer, saying "Hitherto hath the LORD helped us." Philistines were subdued, coming no more to Israel's coast; the LORD's hand was against Philistines all Samuel's days. Cities Philistines took from Israel were restored from Ekron to Gath; Israel delivered coasts from Philistines. Peace existed between Israel and Amorites. Samuel judged Israel all his life, traveling yearly in circuit to Beth-el, Gilgal, and Mizpeh, judging Israel there. His return was to Ramah where his house was; he judged Israel and built an altar to the LORD.

**1 Samuel 7:3** — "And Samuel spake unto all the house of Israel, saying, If ye do return unto the LORD with all your hearts, then put away the strange gods and Ashtaroth from among you, and prepare your hearts unto the LORD, and serve him only: and he will deliver you out of the hand of the Philistines"

  -Interpretation:* John commands believers to keep from idols, showing this principle continues in New Testament.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### KIRJATH-JEARIM
- **Meaning:** "City of forests"
- **Context:** Ark's resting place for 20 years
- **Significance:** Temporary home for God's presence
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual growth (Psalm 1:3)

### ASHTAROTH
- **Meaning:** Canaanite fertility goddess
- **Context:** Idol Israel must put away
- **Significance:** False worship opposed to true worship
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual adultery (James 4:4)

### MIZPEH
- **Meaning:** "Watchtower"
- **Context:** Place of Israel's repentance and victory
- **Significance:** Place of divine intervention
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual watchfulness (Mark 13:33)

### EBEN-EZER
- **Meaning:** "Stone of help"
- **Context:** Memorial of God's help
- **Significance:** Reminder of divine assistance
* **New Testament parallel:** Christ as helper (Hebrews 13:6)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 And the men of Kirjath-jearim came, and fetched up the ark of the LORD, and brought it into the house of Abinadab in the hill, and sanctified Eleazar his son to keep the ark of the LORD. 

2 And it came to pass, while the ark abode in Kirjath-jearim, that the time was long; for it was twenty years: and all the house of Israel lamented after the LORD. 

3 And Samuel spake unto all the house of Israel, saying, If ye do return unto the LORD with all your hearts, then put away the strange gods and Ashtaroth from among you, and prepare your hearts unto the LORD, and serve him only: and he will deliver you out of the hand of the Philistines. 

4 Then the children of Israel did put away Baalim and Ashtaroth, and served the LORD only. 

5 And Samuel said, Gather all Israel to Mizpeh, and I will pray for you unto the LORD. 

6 And they gathered together to Mizpeh, and drew water, and poured it out before the LORD, and on that day, and said there, We have sinned against the LORD. And Samuel judged the children of Israel in Mizpeh. 

7 And while the Philistines heard that the children of Israel were were gathered together to Mizpeh, the lords of the Philistines went up against Israel. And when the children of Israel heard it, they were afraid of the Philistines. 

8 And the children of Israel said to Samuel, Cease not to cry unto the LORD our God for us, that he will save us out of the hand of the Philistines. 

9 And Samuel took a sucking lamb, and offered it for a burnt offering wholly unto the LORD: and Samuel cried unto the LORD for Israel; and the LORD heard him. 

10 And as Samuel was offering up the burnt offering, the natural Philistines drew near to battle against Israel: but the LORD thundered with a great thunder on that day upon the Philistines, and discomfited them; and they were smitten before Israel. 

11 And the men of Israel went out of Mizpeh, and pursued the Philistines, and they commanded them, until they came under Beth-car. 

12 Then Samuel took a stone, and set it between Mizpeh and Shen, and called the name of it Eben-ezer, saying, Hitherto hath the LORD helped us.  

13 So the Philistines were subdued, and they came no more into the coast of Israel: and the hand of the LORD was against the Philistines all the days of Samuel. 

14 And the cities which the Philistines had taken from Israel were restored to Israel, from Ekron even unto Gath; and the coasts thereof did Israel deliver out of the hands of the Philistines. And there was peace between Israel and the Amorites. 

15 And Samuel judged Israel all the days of his life.  

16 And he went from year to year in circuit to Beth-el, and Gilgal, and Mizpeh, and judged Israel in all those places.  

17 And his return was to Ramah; for there was his house; and there he judged Israel; and there he built an altar unto the LORD.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "If ye do return unto the LORD with all your hearts"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about emotional commitment
**Biblical context:** This requires putting away idols and serving God only - wholehearted commitment demonstrated by action, not just emotion.

### "The LORD thundered with a great thunder"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is just weather
**Biblical context:** This is divine intervention - God using His power to defeat Israel's enemies while Samuel offers sacrifice.

**Chapter 7 demonstrates the power of true repentance and God's faithfulness to those who return to Him:** The twenty years at Kirjath-jearim show the consequences of spiritual neglect - God's presence remains but Israel doesn't seek Him. Israel's lamentation shows their recognition of spiritual need after prolonged absence of divine blessing. Samuel's call to return with all hearts shows that true repentance requires wholehearted commitment, not partial devotion. Putting away Baalim and Ashtaroth shows that repentance requires removing false worship completely. Serving the LORD only shows exclusive devotion to God. Gathering at Mizpeh shows corporate repentance and unity in seeking God. Drawing water and pouring it out shows symbolic emptying of self before God. Fasting shows humility and seriousness in seeking God. Confessing sin shows acknowledgment of wrongdoing. Samuel's judging shows leadership in spiritual renewal. The Philistine attack shows that spiritual renewal often faces opposition. Israel's fear shows human weakness despite repentance. Their request for Samuel's continued prayer shows recognition of the need for intercession. The sucking lamb as offering shows the principle of giving the best to God. Samuel's crying shows earnest intercession. God's thunder shows His direct intervention in response to prayer and sacrifice. The discomfiting of Philistines shows God's power working supernaturally. Israel's pursuit shows confidence gained through divine victory. The Ebenezer stone shows the importance of remembering God's help. The subduing of Philistines shows complete victory through divine power. The restoration of cities shows reversal of previous losses. Peace with Amorites shows broader blessing from obedience. Samuel's lifelong judgeship shows the stability that comes from godly leadership. The circuit ministry shows systematic care for all Israel. The altar at Ramah shows personal devotion alongside public ministry. This chapter establishes crucial themes: true repentance brings divine deliverance, wholehearted devotion removes false worship, intercessory prayer brings divine intervention, and God's help should be remembered. It demonstrates that God responds to genuine repentance with supernatural power, that spiritual leadership requires both public and private ministry, and that divine victory brings lasting peace. The chapter shows Samuel as a model spiritual leader - calling for repentance, interceding for the people, providing consistent leadership, and maintaining personal devotion. It illustrates the principle that God's presence brings blessing when properly honored, that repentance requires both removing evil and embracing good, and that divine intervention often comes at the moment of human weakness and dependence. The chapter points forward to Christ as the perfect intercessor who brings true deliverance from spiritual enemies, and to the ultimate victory over all powers through divine intervention. The Ebenezer stone points forward to Christ as the ultimate "stone of help" who provides eternal assistance. The chapter demonstrates that when God's people return to Him wholeheartedly, He responds with supernatural power that brings complete victory and lasting peace.
