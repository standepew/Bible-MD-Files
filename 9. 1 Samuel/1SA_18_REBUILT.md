## CHAPTER SUMMARY

When he finished speaking to Saul, Jonathan's soul was knit with David's soul, and Jonathan loved him as his own soul. Saul took him that day and wouldn't let him go home to father's house. Jonathan and David made covenant because he loved him as his own soul. Jonathan stripped himself of robe on him, gave it to David, his garments, even to his sword, bow, and girdle. David went wherever Saul sent him, behaved himself wisely. Saul set him over men of war, and he was accepted in sight of all people and also in Saul's servants' sight. When David returned from Philistine slaughter, women came from all cities singing and dancing to meet king Saul with tabrets, joy, and musick instruments. Women answered one another as they played, saying Saul had slain his thousands, David his ten thousands. Saul was very wroth, displeased by this, saying they ascribed to David ten thousands, to him thousands but what could he have more but kingdom? Saul eyed David from that day forward. Next morning evil spirit from God came upon Saul, he prophesied in house midst, David played with his hand as at other times, javelin in Saul's hand. Saul cast javelin, thinking to smite David to wall with it. David avoided his presence twice. Saul was afraid of David because LORD was with him and was departed from Saul. Therefore Saul removed him from him, made him captain over thousand, and he went out before people. David behaved himself wisely in all his ways; LORD was with him. When Saul saw he behaved himself very wisely, he was afraid of him. But all Israel and Judah loved David because he went out and came in before them. Saul said to David, behold my elder daughter Merab, her I'll give to wife; only be valiant for me and fight LORD's battles. For Saul said, let not my hand be upon him, but let Philistines' hand be upon him. David said to Saul, who am I, what's my life or my father's family in Israel, that I should be son-in-law to king? But it came to pass at time when Merab Saul's daughter should be given to David, that she was given to Adriel the Mehohathite to wife. Michal Saul's daughter loved David; they told Saul, and thing pleased him. Saul said, I'll give him her, that she may be snare to him, and Philistines' hand may be against him. Therefore Saul commanded his servants, commune with David secretly, say behold king delights in you, all his servants love you, now therefore be king's son-in-law. Saul's servants spoke those words in David's ears. David said, seems it a light thing to you to be king's son-in-law, seeing I'm a poor man, lightly esteemed? Saul's servants told him, on this manner spoke David. Saul said, thus shall you say to David, king desires not any dowry, but a hundred foreskins of Philistines to be avenged of king's enemies. But Saul thought to make David fall by Philistines' hand. When his servants told David these words, it pleased David well to be king's son-in-law. And days weren't expired. David arose and went, his men, and slew of Philistines two hundred men. David brought their foreskins, gave them in full tale to king, that he might be king's son-in-law. Saul gave him Michal his daughter to wife. Saul saw and knew LORD was with David, and Michal Saul's daughter loved David. Saul was yet more afraid of David, and became David's enemy continually. Princes of Philistines went forth; it came to pass after they went forth, David behaved himself more wisely than all Saul's servants, so his name was much set by. Saul saw and knew LORD was with David, and Michal Saul's daughter loved David.

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 And it came to pass, when he had made an end of speaking unto Saul, that the soul of Jonathan was knit with the soul of David, and Jonathan loved him as his own soul.  

2 And Saul took him that day, and would let him go no more home to his father's house.  

3 Then Jonathan and David made a covenant, because he loved him as his own soul.  

4 And Jonathan stripped himself of the robe that was upon him, and gave it to David, and his garments, even to his sword, and to his bow, and to his girdle.  

5 And David went out whithersoever Saul sent him, and behaved himself wisely: and Saul set him over the men of war, and he was accepted in the sight of all the people, and also in the sight of Saul's servants.  

6 And it came to pass as they came, when David was returned from the slaughter of the Philistine, that the women came out of all cities of Israel, singing and dancing, to meet king Saul, with tabrets, with joy, and with instruments of musick.  

7 And the women answered one another as they played, and said, Saul hath slain his thousands, and David his ten thousands.  

8 And Saul was very wroth, and displeased him; and he said, They have ascribed unto David ten thousands, and to me they have ascribed but thousands: and what can he have more but the kingdom?  

9 And Saul eyed David from that day and forward.  

10 And it came to pass on the morrow, that an evil spirit from God came upon Saul, and he prophesied in the midst of the house: and David played with his hand, as at other times: and there was a javelin in Saul's hand.  

11 And Saul cast the javelin; for he said, I will smite David even to the wall with it. And David avoided out of his presence twice.  

12 And Saul was afraid of David, because that the LORD was with him, and was departed from Saul.  

13 Therefore Saul removed him from him, and made him his captain over a thousand; and he went out and came in before the people.  

14 And David behaved himself wisely in all his ways; and the LORD was with him.  

15 Wherefore when Saul saw that he behaved himself very wisely, he was afraid of him.  

16 But all Israel and Judah loved David, because he went out and came in before them.  

17 And Saul said unto David, Behold my elder daughter Merab, her will I give thee to wife: only be thou valiant for me, and fight the LORD's battles. For Saul said, Let not mine hand be upon him, but let the hand of the Philistines be upon him.  

18 And David said unto Saul, Who am I? and what is my life, or my father's family in Israel, that I should be son in law to the king?  

19 But it came to pass at the time when Merab Saul's daughter should have been given to David, that she was given unto Adriel the Mehohathite to wife.  

20 And Michal Saul's daughter loved David: and they told Saul, and the thing pleased him.  

21 And Saul said, I will give him her, that she may be a snare to him, and that the hand of the Philistines may be against him.  

22 Therefore Saul commanded his servants, saying, Commune with David secretly, and say, Behold, the king delighteth in thee, and all his servants love thee: now therefore be the king's son in law.  

23 And Saul's servants spake those words in the ears of David. And David said, Seemeth it to you a light thing to be the king's son in law, seeing that I am a poor man, and lightly esteemed?  

24 And the servants of Saul told him, saying, On this manner spake David.  

25 And Saul said, Thus shall ye say to David, The king desireth not any dowry, but an hundred foreskins of the Philistines, to be avenged of the king's enemies. But Saul thought to make David fall by the hand of the Philistines.  

26 And when his servants told David these words, it pleased David well to be the king's son in law. And the days were not expired.  

27 And David arose, and went, he and his men, and slew of the Philistines two hundred men: and David brought their foreskins, and gave them in full tale to the king, that he might be the king's son in law.  

28 And Saul gave him Michal his daughter to wife.  

29 And Saul saw and knew that the LORD was with David, and that Michal Saul's daughter loved David.  

30 And Saul was yet more afraid of David, and became David's enemy continually.  

31 Then the princes of the Philistines went forth; and it came to pass, after they went forth, that David behaved himself more wisely than all the servants of Saul: so that his name was much set by.  

32 And Saul saw and knew that the LORD was with David, and that Michal Saul's daughter loved David.

## COMMONLY MISQUOTED PASSAGES

### "The soul of Jonathan was knit with the soul of David"
**Common misinterpretation:** This is about emotional friendship
**Biblical context:** This is about divine spiritual unity - their souls were knit together by God, showing true friendship as spiritual bond.

### "Saul eyed David from that day and forward"
**Common misinterpretation:* This is about watching someone
**Biblical context:** This is about jealous hostility - Saul's watchful eye represents growing hatred and desire to harm.

**Chapter 18 demonstrates the power of true friendship and wisdom in the face of jealousy and opposition:** Jonathan's soul being knit with David's shows divine friendship that transcends circumstances. Jonathan stripping his robe and giving it to David shows transferring honor and position to God's chosen. Their covenant shows faithful friendship based on spiritual recognition rather than external circumstances. David's wisdom in all his ways shows that God's blessing follows wise conduct. The women's song about David's ten thousands versus Saul's thousands shows public recognition of divine favor. Saul's jealousy shows how human insecurity responds to divine blessing on others. The evil spirit troubling Saul shows continued spiritual decline. Saul's attempt to kill David with javelin shows human violence against God's chosen. David's avoidance shows wisdom and divine protection. Saul's fear shows recognition of God's presence with David. David's continued success shows God's blessing on the faithful. The marriage plot shows human attempts to destroy through manipulation. David's acceptance of marriage terms shows wisdom in difficult circumstances. His military success shows God's continued blessing despite human opposition. Michal's love for David shows God's protection through relationships. Saul's continued fear shows his spiritual blindness. The Philistine princes' recognition of David's wisdom shows that even enemies acknowledge God's blessing. This chapter establishes crucial themes: true friendship transcends circumstances, wisdom brings divine blessing, jealousy destroys relationships, and God protects His chosen. It demonstrates that human opposition cannot thwart God's purposes, that wisdom in conduct brings success, and that true friendship recognizes divine calling. The chapter shows Jonathan as model friend - selfless, supportive, and spiritually discerning. It illustrates the principle that God's blessing on the faithful creates jealousy in the insecure, that wisdom protects against human schemes, and that divine purposes prevail despite opposition. The chapter points forward to Christ as the perfect friend who lays down His life for His friends, whose wisdom brings perfect salvation, and whose friendship transcends all earthly bonds. The covenant between Jonathan and David points forward to Christ's perfect covenant with His people. The chapter demonstrates that true friendship recognizes and supports God's chosen, that wisdom protects against human jealousy, and that God's blessing continues despite opposition.
