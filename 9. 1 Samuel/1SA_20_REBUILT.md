## CHAPTER SUMMARY

David fled from Naioth in Ramah, came to Jonathan asking what he had done, his iniquity, his sin before his father that he sought his life. Jonathan said God forbid, David wouldn't die; his father would do nothing great or small without showing it him, why would father hide this? David swore saying father certainly knew he found grace in Jonathan's eyes, father said "Let not Jonathan know this, lest he be grieved" but as LORD lived and Jonathan's soul lived, there was but step between him and death. Jonathan said whatever David's soul desired, he would do. David said tomorrow was new moon, he should sit with king at meat, but let him go hide in field until third day at even. If father missed him, say David earnestly asked leave to run to Beth-lehem his city for yearly sacrifice for all family. If father said "It is well," servant would have peace; if very wroth, evil was determined. Deal kindly with servant since brought into covenant of LORD; if iniquity, slay him himself, why bring to father? Jonathan said far be it; if he knew evil determined by father to come upon David, he wouldn't tell him? David asked who would tell him, what if father answered roughly. Jonathan told David to go into field; they went both into field. Jonathan said LORD God of Israel, when he sounded father tomorrow or third day, if good toward David and he didn't send and show, LORD do so and more to Jonathan; but if father pleased to do evil to David, he would show and send away that David might go in peace, LORD be with him as with father. David should show kindness of LORD while Jonathan lived that he die not; also not cut kindness from Jonathan's house forever, not when LORD cut off David's enemies from earth. Jonathan made covenant with David's house, saying LORD require it at David's enemies' hands. Jonathan caused David to swear again because he loved him as his own soul. Jonathan said tomorrow new moon, David would be missed because seat empty. When David stayed three days, go down quickly to place where he hid when business was in hand, remain by stone Ezel. He would shoot three arrows on side as though at mark. Send lad saying "Go, find arrows." If he expressly said "The arrows are on this side of thee, take them," David should come for peace and no hurt as LORD lived. If he said "The arrows are beyond thee, go thy way," LORD sent him away. As to matter they spoke of, LORD be between them forever. David hid in field; when new moon came, king sat to eat meat. King sat on seat as at other times, seat by wall; Jonathan arose, Abner sat by Saul's side, David's place empty. Saul said nothing that day, thinking something befell David, he wasn't clean. Next day, second day of month, David's place empty. Saul asked Jonathan why Jesse's son didn't come to meat yesterday or today. Jonathan answered David earnestly asked leave to go to Beth-lehem, family had sacrifice in city, brother commanded him to be there, if found favor in eyes, get away to see brethren, so didn't come to king's table. Saul's anger kindled against Jonathan, saying "You son of perverse rebellious woman, don't I know you chose Jesse's son to your confusion and your mother's nakedness? As long as Jesse's son lives on ground, you won't be established nor your kingdom. Send and fetch him, he shall surely die." Jonathan answered Saul his father, why should he be slain, what had he done? Saul cast javelin at him to smite him; Jonathan knew father determined to slay David. Jonathan arose from table in fierce anger, ate no meat second day of month, grieved for David because father did him shame. Morning, Jonathan went to field at time appointed with David, lad with him. He told lad to run find arrows he shot. As lad ran, he shot arrow beyond him. When lad came to place of arrow Jonathan shot, Jonathan cried after lad asking if arrow beyond him. Jonathan cried after lad to make speed, haste, stay not. Jonathan's lad gathered arrows, came to master. Lad knew nothing; only Jonathan and David knew matter. Jonathan gave artillery to lad, told him to carry to city. As soon as lad gone, David arose from place toward south, fell on face to ground, bowed three times; they kissed one another, wept together until David exceeded. Jonathan told David to go in peace, since they swore in LORD's name, LORD be between them and between their seed forever. He arose and departed; Jonathan went to city.

## KEY TERMS WITH CONTEXTUAL DEFINITIONS

### NEW MOON
- **Meaning:** Beginning of Hebrew month
- **Context:** Time of royal feast and religious observance
- **Significance:** Sacred time for gathering and worship
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual renewal (2 Corinthians 5:17)

### COVENANT
- **Meaning:** Binding agreement before God
- **Context:** Jonathan and David's lifelong commitment
- **Significance:** Faithful friendship based on divine witness
* **Spiritual application:** New covenant (Matthew 26:28)

### STONE EZEL
- **Meaning:** "Departure stone"
- **Context:** Meeting place for signal
- **Significance:** Location of divine guidance
* **Spiritual application:** Spiritual landmarks (1 Corinthians 10:4)

### ARTILLERY
- **Meaning:** Weapons, military equipment
- **Context:** Jonathan's weapons given to servant
- **Significance:** Transfer of responsibility
* **New Testament parallel:** Spiritual weapons (Ephesians 6:11)

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 And David fled from Naioth in Ramah, and came and said before Jonathan, What have I done? what is mine iniquity? and what is my sin before thy father, that he seeketh my life?  

2 And he said unto him, God forbid; thou shalt not die: behold, my father will do nothing either great or small, but that he will shew it me: and why should my father hide this thing from me? it is not so.  

3 And David sware moreover, and said, Thy father certainly knoweth that I have found grace in thine eyes; and he saith, Let not Jonathan know this, lest he be grieved: but truly as the LORD liveth, and as thy soul liveth, there is but a step between me and death.  

4 Then said Jonathan unto David, Whatsoever thy soul desireth, I will even do it for thee.  

5 And David said unto Jonathan, Behold, to morrow is the new moon, and I should not fail to sit with the king at meat: but let me go, that I may hide myself in the field unto the third day at even.  

6 If thy father at all miss me, then say, David earnestly asked leave of me that he might run to Beth-lehem his city: for there is a yearly sacrifice there for all the fierce.  

7 If he say thus, It is well; thy servant shall have peace: but if he be very wroth, then be sure that evil is determined by him.  

8 Therefore thou shalt deal kindly with thy servant; for thou hast brought thy servant into a covenant of the LORD with thee: notwithstanding, if there be in me iniquity, slay me thyself; for why shouldest thou bring me to thy father?  

9 And Jonathan said, Far be it from thee: for if I knew certainly that evil were determined by my division to come upon thee, then would not I tell it thee?  

10 Then said David to Jonathan, Who shall tell me? or what if thy father answer thee roughly?  

11 And Jonathan said unto David, Come, and let us go out into the field. And they went out both of them into the field.  

12 And Jonathan said unto David, O LORD God of Israel, when I chose to sound my father about to morrow any time, or the third day, and, behold, if there be good toward David, and I then send not unto thee, and shew it thee;  

13 The LORD do so and much more to Jonathan: but if it please my father to do thee evil, then I will shew it thee, and send thee away, that thou mayest go in peace: and the LORD be with thee, as he hath been with my father.  

14 And thou shalt not only while yet I live shew me the kindness of the LORD, that I die not:  

15 But also thou shalt not cut off thy kindness from my house for ever: no, not when the LORD hath cut off the enemies of David every one from the face of the earth.  

16 So Jonathan made a covenant with the house of David, saying, Let the LORD even require it at the hand of David's enemies.  

17 And Jonathan caused David to swear again, because he loved him: for he loved him as he loved his own soul.  

18 Then Jonathan said to table, To morrow is the new moon: and thou shalt be missed, because thy seat will be empty.  

19 And when thou hast stayed three days, then thou shalt go down quickly, and come to the place where thou didst hide thyself when the business was in hand, and shalt remain by the stone Ezel.  

20 And I will shoot three arrows on the side thereof, as though I shot at a mark.  

21 And, behold, I will send a lad, saying, Go, find out the arrows. If I expressly say unto the lad, Behold, the arrows are on this side of thee, take them; then come thou: for there is peace to thee, and no hurt; as the LORD liveth.  

22 But if I say thus unto the the young man, Behold, the arrows are beyond thee; go thy way: beyond for the LORD hath sent thee away.  

23 And as touching the matter which thou and I have spoken of, behold, the LORD be between thee and me for ever.  

24 So David hid himself in the field: and when the new moon was come, the king sat him down to eat meat.  

25 And the king sat upon his seat, as at other times, even upon a seat by the wall: and Jonathan arose, and Abner sat by Saul's side, and David's place was empty.  

26 Nevertheless Saul spake not any thing that day: for he thought, Something hath befallen him, he is not clean; surely he is not clean.  

27 And it came to pass on the morrow, which was the second day of the month, that David's place was empty: and Saul asked Jonathan his son, Wherefore cometh not the son of Jesse to meat, neither yesterday, nor to day?  

28 And Jonathan answered Saul, David earnestly asked leave of me to go to Beth-lehem:  

29 And he said, Let me go, I pray thee; for our family hath a sacrifice in the city; and my mother, he hath commanded me to be there: and now, if I have found favour in thine eyes, let me get away, I pray thee, and see my brethren. Therefore he cometh not unto the king's table.  

30 Then Saul's anger was kindled against Jonathan, and he said unto him, Thou son of the perverse rebellious woman, do not I know that thou hast chosen the son of Jesse to thine own confusion, and unto the confusion of thy mother's nakedness?  

31 For as long as the son of Jesse liveth upon the ground, thou shalt not be established, nor thy kingdom. Wherefore now send and send and fetch him unto me, for he shall surely die.  

32 And Jonathan answered Saul his father, and said unto him, Wherefore shall he be slain? what hath he done?  

33 And Saul cast a javelin at him to smite him: whereby Jonathan knew that it was determined of his father to slay David.  

34 So Jonathan arose from the table in fierce anger, and did eat no meat the second day of the month: for he was grieved for David, because his father had done him shame.  

35 And it came to pass in the morning, that Jonathan went out into the field at the time appointed with David, and a little lad with him.  

36 And he said unto his lad, Run, find out now the arrows which I shoot. And as the lad ran, they shot an arrow beyond him.  

37 And when the lad was come to the place of the arrow which Jonathan had shot, Jonathan cried after the lad, and said, Is not the arrow beyond thee?  

38 And Jonathan cried after the lad, Make speed, haste, stay not. And Jonathan's lad gathered up the arrows, and came to his master.  

39 But the lad knew not any thing: only Jonathan and David knew the matter.  

40 And Jonathan gave his artillery unto his lad, and said unto him, Go, carry them to the city.  

41 And as soon as the lad was gone, David arose out of a place toward the south, and fell on his face to the ground, and bowed himself three times: and they kissed one another, and wept one with another, until David exceeded.  

42 And Jonathan said to David, Go in peace, forasmuch as we have sworn both of us in the name of the LORD, saying, The LORD be between me and thee, and between my seed and thy seed for ever. And he arose and departed: and Jonathan went into the city.

**Chapter 20 demonstrates the depth of true friendship and the painful cost of loyalty in the face of family conflict:** David's plea to Jonathan shows his desperation and innocence. Jonathan's immediate defense shows true friendship that stands against family pressure. The covenant renewal shows their commitment before God, not just human sentiment. The elaborate plan to test Saul shows wisdom in dangerous circumstances. The new moon feast shows religious observance becoming setting for family conflict. David's absence shows the cost of divine calling - losing normal fellowship. Saul's initial silence shows his calculating nature - waiting to reveal his hatred. Jonathan's defense of David shows courage to speak truth to power. Saul's explosive anger shows the depth of his jealousy and insecurity. His insult to Jonathan shows how hatred corrupts family relationships. His threat to Jonathan shows how opposition to God's chosen affects families. The javelin thrown at Jonathan shows violence against family loyalty. Jonathan's fierce anger shows righteous response to injustice. His fasting shows grief over family betrayal. The signal arrows show careful planning and divine guidance. The coded communication shows wisdom in dangerous times. The lad's ignorance shows how God's purposes remain hidden from others. The final farewell shows the pain of separation for righteousness. Their weeping together shows genuine love and shared sorrow. David's exceeding in weeping shows his deep gratitude and grief. The final covenant blessing shows their eternal commitment before God. This chapter establishes crucial themes: true friendship requires sacrifice, family loyalty conflicts with divine calling, wisdom protects the innocent, and covenant faithfulness transcends circumstances. It demonstrates that God's chosen often face opposition from unexpected sources, that true friendship involves risk, and that divine purposes require painful separations. The chapter shows Jonathan as model of faithful friend - courageous, wise, self-sacrificing, and spiritually discerning. It illustrates the principle that true friendship stands against family pressure for righteousness, that divine calling costs relationships, and that covenant faithfulness endures through separation. The chapter points forward to Christ as the perfect friend who lays down His life for His friends, whose covenant brings eternal salvation, and whose friendship transcends all earthly bonds. The covenant between Jonathan and David points forward to Christ's perfect covenant with His people. The chapter demonstrates that when divine calling creates family conflict, true friendship provides protection, wisdom, and spiritual support, even at great personal cost.
