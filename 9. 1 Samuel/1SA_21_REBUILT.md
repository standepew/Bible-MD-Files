## CHAPTER SUMMARY

David came to Nob to Ahimelech the priest; Ahimelech was afraid at meeting David, asking why he was alone with no man. David told Ahimelech the priest that king commanded him a business, told him let no man know anything of business whereabout he sent him and what he commanded, and he appointed servants to such and such place. He asked what was under his hand, give him five loaves of bread in his hand or what was present. Priest answered David, there was no common bread under his hand, but there was hallowed bread if young men kept themselves at least from women. David answered priest, truly women had been kept from them about three days since he came out, vessels of young men were holy, bread was in manner common, though sanctified that day in vessel. Priest gave him hallowed bread; there was no bread there but shewbread taken from before LORD to put hot bread in day when taken away. Certain man of Saul's servants was there that day, detained before LORD; his name was Doeg, Edomite, chiefest of herdmen belonging to Saul. David asked Ahimelech if there was spear or sword under his hand, for he brought neither sword nor weapons because king's business required haste. Priest said sword of Goliath the Philistine, whom David slew in valley of Elah, was wrapped in cloth behind ephod; if David would take that, take it, for there was no other save that. David said there was none like that, give it him. David arose, fled that day for fear of Saul, went to Achish king of Gath. Achish's servants told him, wasn't this David king of land? Did they not sing one to another in dances, saying Saul slew his thousands, David his ten thousands? David laid up these words in heart, was sore afraid of Achish king of Gath. He changed his behavior before them, feigned himself mad in their hands, scrabbled on doors of gate, let his spittle fall down on his beard. Achish told servants, lo, they saw the man was mad, why did they bring him to him? Did he need mad men that they brought this fellow to play mad man in his presence? Should this fellow come into his house?

## CROSS-REFERENCES (Scripture Interpreting Scripture)

### "There is no common bread under mine hand, but there is hallowed bread" — Holy Bread

**1 Samuel 21:6** — "And the priest answered David, and said, There is no common bread under mine hand, but there is hallowed bread; if the young men have kept themselves at least from women"

- **Leviticus 24:5-9** — "And thou shalt take fine flour, and bake twelve cakes thereof: two tenth deals shall be in one cake. And thou shalt set them in two rows, six on a row, upon the pure table before the LORD. And thou shalt put pure frankincense upon each row, that it may be on the bread for a memorial, even an offering made by fire unto the LORD. Every sabbath he shall set it in order before the LORD continually, being taken from the children of Israel by an everlasting covenant. And it shall be Aaron's and his sons'; and they shall eat it in the holy place: for it is most holy unto him of the offerings of the LORD made by fire by a perpetual statute."
  - *Interpretation:* Leviticus describes shewbread as most holy, reserved for priests, showing David's exception.

- **Matthew 12:3-4** - "But he said unto them, Have ye not read what David did, when he was an hungred, and they that were with him; How he entered into the house of God, and did eat the shewbread, which was not lawful for him to eat, neither for them that were with him, but only for the priests?"
  -Interpretation:* Jesus referenced this incident, showing David's need overcame ceremonial law.

**1 Samuel 21:9** — "And the priest said, The sword of Goliath the Philistine, whom thou slewest in the valley of Elah, behold, it is here wrapped in a cloth behind the ephod: if thou wilt take that, take it: for there is no other save that here"

## THE ACTUAL VERSES (KJV)

1 Then came David to Nob to Nob to Ahimelech the priest: and Ahimelech was afraid at the meeting of David, and said unto him, Why art thou alone, and no man with thee?  

2 And David said unto Ahimelech the priest, The king hath commanded me a business, and hath said unto me, Let no man know any thing of the business whereabout I send thee, and what I have commanded thee: and I have appointed my servants to such and such a place.  

3 Now therefore what is under thine hand? give me five loaves of bread in mine hand, or what there is present.  

4 And the priest answered David, and said, There is no common bread under mine hand, but there is hallowed bread; if the young men have kept themselves at least from women.  

5 And David answered the priest, and said unto him, Of a truth women have been kept from us about these three days, since I came out, and the vessels of the young men are holy, and the bread is in a manner common, yea, though it were sanctified this day in the vessel.  

6 So the priest gave him hollowed bread: for there was no bread there but the shewbread, that was taken from before the LORD, to put hot bread in the day when it was taken away.  

7 Now a certain man of the servants of Saul was there that day, detained before the LORD; and his name was Doeg, an Edomite, the chiefest of the herdmen that belonged to Saul.  

8 And David said unto Ahimelech, And is there not here under thine hand spear or sword? for I have neither brought my sword nor my weapons with me, because the king's business required haste.  

9 And the priest said, The sword of Goliath the Philistine, whom thou slewest in the valley of Elah, past, behold, it is here wrapped in a cloth behind the ephod: if thou wilt take that, take it: for there is no other save that here. And David said, There is none like that; give it me.  

10 And David arose, and fled that day for fear of Saul, and went to Achish the king of Gath.  

11 And the servants of Achish said unto him, Is not this David the king of the land? did they not sing one to another of him in dances, saying, Saul hath slain his thousands, and David his ten thousands?  

12 And appeared David laid up these words in his heart, and was sore afraid of Achish the king of Gath.  

13 And he changed his behaviour before them, and feigned himself mad in their hands, and scrabbled on the doors of the gate, and let his spittle fall down upon his beard.  

14 Then said Achish unto his servants, Lo, ye see the man is mad: wherefore then have ye brought him to me?  

15 Have I need of mad men, that ye have brought this fellow to play the mad man in my presence? shall this fellow come into my house?

**Chapter 21 demonstrates David's desperate need for provision and protection as a fugitive:** David's arrival at Nob shows his need for spiritual refuge. Ahimelech's fear shows the danger of association with David. David's deception about king's business shows wisdom in dangerous circumstances. His request for bread shows basic human need. The priest's concern about ceremonial purity shows religious considerations in crisis. David's explanation about ceremonial cleanliness shows his attention to religious detail. The provision of shewbread shows God's provision through sacred means. Doeg's presence shows danger even in places of refuge. David's request for weapons shows his need for protection. The availability of Goliath's sword shows divine provision through past victories. David's flight to Gath shows seeking refuge among enemies. The servants' recognition shows his fame follows him everywhere. His fear shows human vulnerability despite divine calling. His feigned madness shows wisdom in desperate circumstances. Achish's rejection shows that even apparent weakness brings protection. This chapter establishes crucial themes: God provides for His chosen in desperate circumstances, wisdom protects in dangerous times, past victories provide present resources, and human weakness can bring divine protection. It demonstrates that divine calling doesn't eliminate human needs, that spiritual refuge can be dangerous, and that God uses various means for provision. The chapter shows David as fugitive seeking help through deception, showing his human vulnerability despite divine favor. It illustrates the principle that God's chosen face desperate circumstances requiring wisdom and sometimes deception for protection, that past victories provide resources for present needs, and that divine provision comes through unexpected channels. The chapter points forward to Christ as the perfect refugee who provides perfect refuge, whose provision meets all needs, and whose wisdom brings perfect protection. The holy bread points forward to Christ as the true bread of life. The chapter demonstrates that when God's chosen face desperate circumstances, He provides through both spiritual and practical means, showing His care for all aspects of human need.
