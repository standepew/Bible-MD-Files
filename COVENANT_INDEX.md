## C1 — The Adamic Covenant of Works (Pre-Fall)
**Source:** [[GEN_01_REBUILT|Genesis 1]] · [[GEN_02_REBUILT|Genesis 2]]
**Type:** Conditional — obedience required for continued life and blessing

### Parties
- God (initiator) ↔ Adam (federal head of humanity)

### Terms
- **Dominion mandate:** Fill the earth, subdue it, rule over creatures (Gen 1:28)
- **Garden mandate:** Tend and keep the garden (Gen 2:15)
- **Prohibition:** Do not eat of the Tree of the Knowledge of Good and Evil (Gen 2:17)
- **Sanction:** *"In the day you eat of it you shall surely die"*

### Theological Significance
Adam served as the **federal representative** of all humanity. His obedience would have secured life for all; his disobedience plunged all into death (Rom 5:12). This is the covenant that Christ as the Last Adam came to fulfill — providing through his perfect obedience what Adam failed to provide (Rom 5:19; 1 Cor 15:45–49).

### Status: BROKEN
Adam ate. The covenant of works was broken, triggering the curse (Gen 3:17–19). It was not annulled — its demands were carried to completion by Christ (Gal 4:4–5; Rom 10:4).

## C2 — The Covenant of Grace (Protoevangelium)
**Source:** [[GEN_03_REBUILT|Genesis 3:15]]
**Type:** Unconditional — God's sovereign promise, not contingent on human performance

### The First Messianic Promise
> *"And I will put enmity between you and the woman, and between your seed and her Seed; He shall bruise your head, and you shall bruise His heel."* — Genesis 3:15

### Parties
- God (initiator) ↔ All humanity through the line of the woman

### Terms
- A *Seed* of the woman will come who will deal a fatal blow to the serpent
- The serpent will wound the Seed (the cross — a heel-bruise)
- The Seed will destroy the serpent (the resurrection and final judgment — a head-crush)

### Theological Significance
This is the **first prophecy in Scripture** and the seed of all subsequent covenants. Every covenant that follows is an **unfolding of this one promise**. The entire Bible is the story of how God keeps this word. The Seed is ultimately Christ (Gal 3:16).

### Progressive Revelation of the Seed
Gen 3:15 → The Seed is *human* → Gen 12 → The Seed is *Abrahamic* → 2 Sam 7 → The Seed is *Davidic* → Gal 3:16 → The Seed is *Christ*

## C3 — The Noahic Covenant
**Source:** [[GEN_09_REBUILT|Genesis 9:8–17]]
**Type:** Unconditional — made with all living creatures, not just Israel

### Parties
- God ↔ Noah, his descendants, and every living creature

### Terms
- God will *never again* destroy all life with a flood
- The structure of creation (seasons, day/night, sowing/harvesting) will be preserved until God's purposes are complete (Gen 8:22)
- Human government instituted: capital punishment for murder (Gen 9:6) — the basis of civil law

### Sign
🌈 **The Rainbow** — appears in the clouds as a reminder of the covenant

### Theological Significance
The Noahic covenant preserves the **stage** on which redemption history plays out. Without it, judgment could terminate history before the Seed arrives. It guarantees that creation will endure until the New Creation (Rev 21–22).

### Status: ACTIVE — perpetual until the new heavens and earth

### Key Chapters

## C4 — The Abrahamic Covenant
**Source:** [[GEN_12_REBUILT|Genesis 12:1–3]] · [[GEN_15_REBUILT|Genesis 15]] · [[GEN_17_REBUILT|Genesis 17]]
**Type:** Unconditional — ratified by God alone while Abraham slept (Gen 15)

### Parties
- God ↔ Abraham and his descendants (physical and spiritual — Gal 3:29)

### The Three-Strand Promise (Gen 12:1–3)
1. **A great nation** — Israel as a people and nation
2. **A great land** — the land of Canaan (and ultimately the whole earth — Rom 4:13)
3. **A great blessing** — *"in you all the families of the earth shall be blessed"*

### The Ratification (Genesis 15 — The Smoking Furnace)
God alone passed between the covenant pieces — making the covenant **entirely unconditional**. Even Abraham's faith was a gift; the covenant did not rest on Abraham's performance. This is the covenant Paul unpacks in Romans 4 and Galatians 3.

### Sign
✂️ **Circumcision** (Gen 17) — seal of the righteousness Abraham had *by faith* before circumcision (Rom 4:11). Fulfilled in the *circumcision of the heart* (Deut 30:6; Col 2:11).

### The Seed Clarification (Galatians 3:16)
> *"Now to Abraham and his Seed were the promises made. He does not say, 'And to seeds,' as of many, but as of one, 'And to your Seed,' who is Christ."*

### Status: ACTIVE — Every believer is Abraham's seed (Gal 3:29)

## C5 — The Mosaic (Sinaitic) Covenant
**Source:** [[EXO_19_REBUILT|Exodus 19–24]] · [[EXO_20_REBUILT|Exodus 20]]
**Type:** Conditional — national covenant with Israel, not a salvation covenant

### Parties
- God ↔ The nation of Israel specifically (not the church, not the nations)

### Terms
- **Israel's obligation:** Keep the Law (613 commandments: moral, civil, ceremonial)
- **Blessing for obedience:** Rain, fruitful land, safety, national flourishing (Deut 28:1–14)
- **Curse for disobedience:** Drought, disease, exile, destruction (Deut 28:15–68)

### The Mosaic Covenant and Salvation
The Mosaic covenant **did not save**. Salvation was always by faith (Gen 15:6; Hab 2:4; Heb 11). The Law's purpose:
1. Reveal the holiness of God
2. Define sin (Rom 3:20; 7:7)
3. Drive Israel to the need for a Mediator
4. Be a **tutor/guardian** pointing to Christ (Gal 3:24)
5. Provide the type-structure (sacrifices, priesthood, temple) that Christ would fulfill

### Sign
🕎 **The Sabbath** — a sign between God and Israel specifically (Exo 31:13, 17)

### Status: FULFILLED — Christ is the end of the Law for righteousness to everyone who believes (Rom 10:4). The ceremonial and civil law are fulfilled; the moral law is written on hearts under the New Covenant (Heb 8:10).

## C6 — The Davidic Covenant
**Source:** [[2SA_07_REBUILT|2 Samuel 7:8–16]]
**Type:** Unconditional — God's sovereign promise, not contingent on David's successors

### The Core Promise (2 Samuel 7:12–16)
> *"I will raise up your descendant after you...I will establish the throne of his kingdom forever...I will be his Father, and he shall be My son...My lovingkindness shall not depart from him...your house and your kingdom shall endure before Me forever; your throne shall be established forever."*

### Parties
- God ↔ David and his dynastic line

### The Four Guarantees
1. **A son** — Solomon first, ultimately Christ (Luke 1:32–33)
2. **A house** — a permanent dynasty
3. **A kingdom** — a realm to rule
4. **A throne** — forever, not temporarily

### The Dual Fulfillment
- **Immediate:** Solomon's reign (1 Kings 1–11) — partial, flawed, conditional
- **Ultimate:** Christ's eternal reign (Rev 19:16; 22:16) — complete, perfect, unconditional

### The Critical Link
The Davidic covenant explains *why* the Messiah must be born in Bethlehem, of the tribe of Judah, in the line of David. Matthew 1 and Luke 3 both carefully trace this genealogy precisely because the Davidic covenant demands it.

### Status: BEING FULFILLED — Christ reigns now at God's right hand (Acts 2:30–36); his kingdom will be consummated at his return

## C7 — The New Covenant
**Source:** [[JEREMIAH_31_REBUILT|Jeremiah 31:31–34]] · [[LUKE_22_REBUILT|Luke 22:20]] · [[HEBREWS_08_REBUILT|Hebrews 8–10]]
**Type:** Unconditional — secured entirely by Christ's blood

### The Prophetic Declaration (Jeremiah 31:31–34)
> *"Behold, the days are coming, says the LORD, when I will make a new covenant with the house of Israel and with the house of Judah — not according to the covenant that I made with their fathers...I will put My law in their minds, and write it on their hearts; and I will be their God, and they shall be My people...I will forgive their iniquity, and their sin I will remember no more."*

### Parties
- God ↔ All who believe (Jew and Gentile — Eph 2:11–16; Gal 3:28–29)

### The Five Provisions (Jer 31:31–34)
1. **Internalized law** — written on the heart, not stone (Heb 8:10)
2. **Direct relationship** — *"I will be their God"* (2 Cor 6:16)
3. **Universal knowledge of God** — *"all shall know Me"* (1 John 2:27)
4. **Complete forgiveness** — *"I will remember their sin no more"* (Heb 10:17)
5. **Permanent Spirit indwelling** — sealed for the day of redemption (Eph 1:13–14; 4:30)

### The Ratification: The Lord's Supper
> *"This cup is the new covenant in My blood, which is shed for you."* — Luke 22:20

The New Covenant was ratified at Calvary. It is better than the Mosaic in every dimension (Heb 7:22; 8:6): better **mediator** (Christ vs. Moses), better **sacrifice** (once-for-all vs. yearly), better **tabernacle** (heavenly vs. earthly), better **promises** (certain vs. conditional).

### Sign
🍞🍷 **The Lord's Supper** (Luke 22:19–20) and the **Indwelling Spirit** (Eph 1:13–14)

### Status: ACTIVE — inaugurated at the cross, to be consummated at Christ's return
