# Biblical People Index
*Major Figures, Their Typological Role, and Their Arc Across the Canon*

---

> **Purpose**
> Tracks every major biblical figure with: all chapters where they appear, their typological significance, their prophetic role, their character arc, and how they connect to Christ.
>
> **Note on "Composite Types":** Many OT figures each capture a *different facet* of Christ — Moses as Deliverer/Lawgiver, David as King/Shepherd, Solomon as Peaceful King/Temple Builder, Joshua as the One Who Leads into Rest. None is the complete picture; Christ fulfills all simultaneously.

### Adam
*"...Adam, who is a type of Him who was to come."* — Romans 5:14

**Covenant:** C1 (Adamic Works) → C2 (Adamic Grace)
**Key Chapters:** [[GEN_01_REBUILT|Gen 1]] · [[GEN_02_REBUILT|Gen 2]] · [[GEN_03_REBUILT|Gen 3]] · [[ROMANS_05_REBUILT|Rom 5]] · [[1_CORINTHIANS_15_REBUILT|1 Cor 15]]

**Character Arc:**
Created in the image of God with dominion over all creation. Placed in the garden to tend and keep it. Given the one prohibition. Tempted by the serpent (through Eve). Chose self-will over God-will. The result: sin, shame, hiding, blame-shifting, death, and curse. Expelled from the garden.

**Typological Function:**
- Federal head of the old humanity → Christ is federal head of the new humanity
- Failed under trial in a garden → Christ succeeded under trial in a garden (Gethsemane)
- His sin brought death to all → Christ's obedience brings life to all who are in him
- Made from dust → returned to dust | Christ taken from dust (incarnation) → returned to glory
- Wore fig leaves (self-righteousness) → God clothed him in animal skins (blood atonement)

**Prophetic Significance:**
The pattern of Adam's fall and God's redemptive response in Gen 3 is the *template* for all of redemption history. The rest of Scripture is the answer to Genesis 3.

### Enoch
*"Enoch walked with God; and he was not, for God took him."* — Genesis 5:24

**Key Chapters:** [[GEN_05_REBUILT|Gen 5]] · [[HEBREWS_11_REBUILT|Heb 11:5]] · [[JUDE_01_REBUILT|Jude 14–15]]

**Character Arc:**
Seventh from Adam. At age 65, after the birth of Methuselah, *he began to walk with God*. He walked with God for 300 years. At age 365, "he was not, for God took him." Never died. One of only two people in Scripture taken to heaven without dying (the other: Elijah).

**Typological Function:**
Enoch is the most direct OT type of the catching away (harpazo) — a man taken before divine judgment (Methuselah means "his death shall bring" — the flood came the year Methuselah died). Enoch was removed *before the judgment*; Noah was preserved *through the judgment*. These two patterns establish two distinct modes of God's preservation of his people — both recorded in Scripture without theological-system labels.

**What Enoch Prophesied:**
Jude 14–15 records Enoch's prophecy about the Second Coming: *"Behold, the Lord comes with ten thousands of His saints, to execute judgment on all."* The seventh from Adam spoke of what will happen at the end of history.

### Noah
*"Noah found grace in the eyes of the LORD."* — Genesis 6:8

**Covenant:** C3 (Noahic)
**Key Chapters:** [[GEN_06_REBUILT|Gen 6]] · [[GEN_07_REBUILT|Gen 7]] · [[GEN_08_REBUILT|Gen 8]] · [[GEN_09_REBUILT|Gen 9]] · [[HEBREWS_11_REBUILT|Heb 11:7]] · [[1_PETER_03_REBUILT|1 Pet 3:20–21]] · [[2_PETER_02_REBUILT|2 Pet 2:5]]

**Character Arc:**
In a generation of universal corruption and violence, Noah was righteous and blameless. God chose him to preserve life through the global judgment. Built the ark over approximately 120 years — a preacher of righteousness (2 Pet 2:5) to a generation that did not listen. Entered the ark. Survived the flood. Made a covenant with God. Later failed through drunkenness — showing that even the faithful need more than a new world; they need a new heart.

**Typological Function:**
- The ark = Christ (the only place of safety from judgment)
- The flood = baptism (1 Pet 3:20–21) — passing through the water of judgment
- Noah's family in the ark = those "in Christ" — the judgment falls on the ark (Christ) rather than on those inside
- The dove sent out = the Holy Spirit hovering over the waters (Gen 1:2; Matt 3:16)
- The rainbow = the New Covenant promise (not to destroy again)

**Jesus' Use of Noah:**
Jesus explicitly used Noah's generation as a type of the days before his return: *"As it was in the days of Noah, so it will be also in the days of the Son of Man"* (Luke 17:26; Matt 24:37–39). The pattern: people eating, drinking, marrying — normal life oblivious to coming judgment — then sudden destruction.

### Moses
*"And there has not arisen in Israel a prophet like Moses, whom the LORD knew face to face."* — Deuteronomy 34:10
*"For Moses wrote of Me."* — John 5:46

**Covenant:** C5 (Mosaic mediator)
**Key Chapters:** [[EXO_02_REBUILT|Exo 2]] · [[EXO_03_REBUILT|Exo 3]] · [[EXO_14_REBUILT|Exo 14]] · [[EXO_20_REBUILT|Exo 20]] · [[DEU_18_REBUILT|Deu 18:15]] · [[DEU_34_REBUILT|Deu 34]] · [[HEBREWS_03_REBUILT|Heb 3]] · [[ACTS_03_REBUILT|Acts 3:22]]

**Character Arc:**
Born into slavery under a death decree (pharaoh ordered all male Hebrew infants killed). Hidden by his mother, placed in a basket, found by Pharaoh's daughter — raised in the palace. Killed an Egyptian oppressor; fled to Midian. Called at the burning bush. Returned to Egypt with signs. Led the Exodus through ten plagues and the parting of the Red Sea. Received the Law at Sinai. Served as mediator between God and Israel for 40 years. Saw the Promised Land but never entered (due to striking the rock twice).

**Typological Function — Christ Parallels:**
| Moses | Christ |
|-------|--------|
| Birth under a death decree | Birth under Herod's death decree |
| Hidden in Egypt | Hidden in Egypt (Matt 2:14–15) |
| Rejects palace luxury (Heb 11:24–26) | Empties himself of divine prerogatives (Phil 2:7) |
| Mediator of the Old Covenant | Mediator of the New Covenant (Heb 9:15) |
| Gives the Law | Fulfills the Law (Matt 5:17) |
| 40 days on the mountain fasting | 40 days in the wilderness fasting |
| Face shone with reflected glory | Transfiguration — his own glory shone through (Matt 17) |
| Interceded for sinful Israel | *"Father, forgive them"* (Luke 23:34) |
| Could not enter the land | Christ leads into the true rest (Heb 4:8–9) |

**The Prophecy Moses Made:**
> *"The LORD your God will raise up for you a Prophet like me from your midst, from your brethren. Him you shall hear."* — Deuteronomy 18:15

Peter quotes this in Acts 3:22, applying it to Christ. It is the clearest OT self-referential messianic prophecy — Moses knew he was a type.

### David
*"I have found David the son of Jesse, a man after My own heart, who will do all My will."* — Acts 13:22

**Covenant:** C6 (Davidic)
**Key Chapters:** [[1SA_16_REBUILT|1 Sam 16]] · [[1SA_17_REBUILT|1 Sam 17]] · [[2SA_07_REBUILT|2 Sam 7]] · [[2SA_11_REBUILT|2 Sam 11]] · [[PSALM_022_REBUILT|Psa 22]] · [[PSALM_023_REBUILT|Psa 23]] · [[PSALM_110_REBUILT|Psa 110]] · [[ACTS_02_REBUILT|Acts 2:30–36]]

**Character Arc:**
Youngest son, overlooked by his family, tending sheep — called by Samuel while his brothers were presented. Anointed king but served Saul for years before ascending. Killed Goliath as a teenager. Became a mighty warrior. Waited 13+ years between anointing and coronation. Brought the Ark to Jerusalem. Desired to build the Temple — God said no; his son would. Committed adultery with Bathsheba; arranged Uriah's murder. Confronted by Nathan; repented deeply (Psalm 51). Faced Absalom's rebellion. Died having provided for the Temple his son would build.

**Typological Function:**
- Shepherd who becomes king → Christ the Good Shepherd who is also King of Kings
- Slays the giant that terrifies Israel → Christ destroys the Enemy that holds humanity in terror
- Anointed but long-suffering before coronation → Christ anointed at baptism, reigning now but full kingdom at his return
- Writes Psalms 22 and 69 — detailed prophecies of the crucifixion 1,000 years before it happened
- His "greater son" (Luke 20:41–44) is also his Lord (Psalm 110:1)

**David's Psalms as Prophecy:**
- **Psalm 22** — The Crucifixion: *"My God, my God, why have You forsaken Me?"* → quoted from the cross
- **Psalm 69** — The Passion: gall and vinegar (v.21), zeal for God's house (v.9) → both fulfilled
- **Psalm 110** — The Ascension and Priesthood: *"Sit at My right hand"* → most-quoted OT verse in NT

### Elijah
*"Behold, I will send you Elijah the prophet before the coming of the great and dreadful day of the LORD."* — Malachi 4:5

**Key Chapters:** [[1KI_17_REBUILT|1 Kgs 17]] · [[1KI_18_REBUILT|1 Kgs 18]] · [[1KI_19_REBUILT|1 Kgs 19]] · [[2KI_02_REBUILT|2 Kgs 2]] · [[MALACHI_04_REBUILT|Mal 4:5–6]] · [[MATTHEW_11_REBUILT|Matt 11:14]] · [[MATTHEW_17_REBUILT|Matt 17:1–13]] · [[LUKE_01_REBUILT|Luke 1:17]] · [[REVELATION_11_REBUILT|Rev 11:6]]

**Character Arc:**
Appears suddenly with no recorded genealogy (typologically significant: like Melchizedek and the eternal Christ). Pronounced judgment on Ahab's kingdom. Fed by ravens at Cherith; then by a widow at Zarephath. Called fire from heaven to consume the sacrifice on Carmel. Killed 450 prophets of Baal. Fled from Jezebel in depression to the wilderness. Renewed at Horeb. Ascended to heaven in a chariot of fire — never died.

**The Three Elijahs:**
1. **Elijah himself** — the literal historical prophet
2. **John the Baptist** — came *"in the spirit and power of Elijah"* (Luke 1:17; Matt 11:14; Matt 17:12–13). Jesus confirmed this identification explicitly.
3. **One of the Two Witnesses** — Rev 11:6 says they have power to *"shut heaven, so that no rain falls"* — the exact sign of Elijah (1 Kgs 17:1). Most scholars identify one witness as Elijah (literally returning, consistent with Mal 4:5 not yet fully fulfilled).

**Typological Function:**
- Confrontation of false religion at the national level → anticipates Christ's cleansing of the Temple and ultimate overthrow of the Antichrist's religion
- Translation without death → type of the catching away (harpazo — see T22 in [[TYPOLOGY_REGISTER]])
- Calling fire from heaven → the Two Witnesses in Rev 11:5 do the same

### Daniel
*"O Daniel, you are greatly beloved."* — Daniel 10:11

**Key Chapters:** [[DANIEL_01_REBUILT|Dan 1]] · [[DANIEL_02_REBUILT|Dan 2]] · [[DANIEL_07_REBUILT|Dan 7]] · [[DANIEL_08_REBUILT|Dan 8]] · [[DANIEL_09_REBUILT|Dan 9]] · [[DANIEL_10_REBUILT|Dan 10]] · [[DANIEL_11_REBUILT|Dan 11]] · [[DANIEL_12_REBUILT|Dan 12]]

**Character Arc:**
Taken captive to Babylon as a teenager. Resolved not to defile himself. Given wisdom and understanding beyond all others. Interpreted Nebuchadnezzar's dreams (chs. 2 and 4). Survived the fiery furnace (his friends). Survived the lions' den. Received the most comprehensive series of end-times visions in the OT: the four world empires (ch. 2, 7), the 70 Weeks (ch. 9), the kings of the north and south (ch. 11), the Time of the End and resurrection (ch. 12 — "a time of trouble such as never was").

**Daniel's Master Prophetic Calendar (Daniel 9:24–27):**
The 70 Weeks prophecy:
- 70 × 7 = 490 years decreed on Israel
- 69 weeks (483 years) from the decree to restore Jerusalem → to the Messiah being cut off (fulfilled precisely to Passion Week)
- The 70th week is separated — the final 7-year unit of Daniel's 490 years: a "Time of Jacob's Trouble" (Jer 30:7), the "1,260 Days" and "42 Months" (Rev 11–13), the "Time of the End" (Dan 12:9)
- The gap between the 69th and 70th week = an interval unmarked by Daniel's text, during which Gentiles are included (a mystery hidden in OT)

This is called "the backbone of biblical prophecy" — every NT prophecy about the end times hangs on it. See [[PROPHECY_FULFILLMENT_INDEX]] P043–P052.

### John the Baptist
*"Elijah has already come, and they did not know him."* — Matthew 17:12

**Key Chapters:** [[MALACHI_03_REBUILT|Mal 3:1]] · [[MALACHI_04_REBUILT|Mal 4:5]] · [[ISAIAH_40_REBUILT|Isa 40:3]] · [[LUKE_01_REBUILT|Luke 1]] · [[MATTHEW_03_REBUILT|Matt 3]] · [[MATTHEW_11_REBUILT|Matt 11]] · [[JOHN_01_REBUILT|John 1:19–37]] · [[JOHN_03_REBUILT|John 3:22–36]]

**Character Arc:**
Born 6 months before Jesus to elderly parents (Elizabeth and Zechariah) — a miraculous birth announcing the arrival of the miraculous. Named *John* (meaning: "The LORD is gracious") at the insistence of his mother, despite no family precedent. Filled with the Holy Spirit from the womb. Lived as a Nazirite in the wilderness. Appeared at the Jordan preaching repentance and baptizing. Baptized Jesus — the one moment in history when the Trinity was simultaneously manifest (Father spoke, Son baptized, Spirit descended). Said *"He must increase, I must decrease."* Imprisoned by Herod Antipas for calling out his unlawful marriage. Beheaded. Jesus said there was no greater man born of woman — yet the least in the kingdom of heaven is greater (indicating the qualitative difference the Spirit's indwelling makes).

**Typological/Prophetic Significance:**
- Fulfills Mal 3:1 (the messenger who prepares the way) and Isa 40:3 (the voice in the wilderness)
- Fulfills Mal 4:5 partially (comes in the spirit of Elijah — Luke 1:17; Matt 17:12)
- His ministry pattern = the two-witness pattern of Rev 11 (calling to repentance, calling out apostasy, martyrdom)
- His decrease-increase principle = the whole theology of sanctification

### Paul (Saul of Tarsus)
*"I have been crucified with Christ; it is no longer I who live, but Christ lives in me."* — Galatians 2:20

**Key Chapters:** [[ACTS_07_REBUILT|Acts 7–8]] · [[ACTS_09_REBUILT|Acts 9]] · [[ACTS_13_REBUILT|Acts 13–28]] · [[ROMANS_01_REBUILT|Romans (all)]] · [[GALATIANS_01_REBUILT|Galatians]] · [[EPHESIANS_01_REBUILT|Ephesians]] · [[PHILIPPIANS_01_REBUILT|Philippians]] · [[2_TIMOTHY_04_REBUILT|2 Tim 4]]

**Character Arc:**
Pharisee of Pharisees, zealous for the Law, student of Gamaliel. Consented to and oversaw Stephen's stoning. Ravaged the church. On the road to Damascus, encountered the risen Christ in blinding light — *"Saul, Saul, why are you persecuting Me?"* Three days blind, fasting, praying. Filled with the Holy Spirit through Ananias. Immediately preached Christ in the synagogues. Spent years in Arabia and Tarsus before active ministry. Three missionary journeys covering the Mediterranean world. Wrote 13 (or 14) NT letters — constituting approximately 1/3 of the NT. Imprisoned in Rome. Executed by Nero, approximately 67 AD.

**Theological Contribution:**
Paul's letters contain the deepest systematic unpacking of the gospel in Scripture:
- **Justification by faith alone** — Romans 3–5; Galatians 2–3
- **Union with Christ** — Romans 6–8; Ephesians 1–3
- **The mystery of the Church** — Ephesians 2–3; Colossians 1–2
- **The resurrection** — 1 Corinthians 15
- **Eschatology** — 1–2 Thessalonians; 2 Timothy 3–4

**Irony in Paul's Story:**
Paul the persecutor became Paul the persecuted. The man who sent Christians to prison was imprisoned for Christ. The man who approved Stephen's martyrdom was himself martyred. His transformation is itself a proof of the resurrection — he had every human motivation to *not* believe.
