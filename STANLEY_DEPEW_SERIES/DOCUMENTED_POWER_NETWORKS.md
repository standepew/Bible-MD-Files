## I. THE LIFELOG / FACEBOOK CONNECTION — February 4, 2004

### The Date Alignment: Verified

On **February 4, 2004**, DARPA (Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency) officially **cancelled** its LifeLog program.

On the **same date — February 4, 2004** — Mark Zuckerberg launched **Facebook** from his Harvard dorm room.

These two events share an identical date. This is not disputed.

### What Was LifeLog?

LifeLog was a DARPA program whose stated objective was to build a comprehensive, searchable database of an individual's entire life: every communication, relationship, activity, purchase, movement, and behavior. The program aimed to create a permanent, total record of human activity that could be mined for pattern analysis and behavioral prediction.

**LifeLog's stated capabilities:**
- Comprehensive tracking of all communications (email, phone, in-person)
- Mapping of all personal relationships and social networks
- Recording of all purchases and financial transactions
- Logging of physical location and movement patterns
- Creating behavioral profiles enabling prediction of future actions

### What Does Facebook Actually Do?

Facebook, as it has evolved from 2004 to the present, performs every function LifeLog outlined:

- Tracks all communications through Messenger, comments, posts
- Maps all personal relationships through the Friends system and social graph
- Records purchases and financial behavior through Marketplace and ad tracking
- Logs physical location through check-ins, photo metadata, and device GPS
- Builds behavioral profiles sold to advertisers and available to intelligence agencies

The structural function of Facebook is identical to the stated objective of LifeLog. The difference is that Facebook achieved **voluntary participation at a global scale** — billions of people providing their own data willingly — which LifeLog as a government program could never have accomplished without legal and constitutional obstacles.

**Sources:**
- Wired (2004): *"A Memory Machine for Everything"* — LifeLog program description
