> **How Biblical Symbolism Works**
> Scripture uses a consistent symbolic vocabulary across all 66 books, established by the principle of **first mention** (the first occurrence of a symbol defines its meaning for all later occurrences). Because the same Spirit inspired all Scripture, symbols are not arbitrary — they form a coherent language that, once learned, accelerates understanding across the entire Bible.
>
> *"Now all these things happened to them as examples, and they were written for our admonition."* — 1 Corinthians 10:11

### Blood
*"For the life of the flesh is in the blood, and I have given it to you upon the altar to make atonement for your souls."* — Leviticus 17:11

**First Mention:** Genesis 4:10 — Abel's blood crying from the ground
**Consistent Meaning:** Blood = life. Shed blood = death and atonement. Applied blood = protection and covenant.

| Occurrence | Text | Meaning |
|-----------|------|---------|
| Gen 4:10 | Abel's blood cries to God | Innocent blood demands justice |
| Gen 9:5–6 | Blood of murder avenged | The sacredness of human life (image of God) |
| Exo 12 | Blood on doorposts | Applied blood = protection from judgment |
| Lev 17:11 | Life is in the blood | Why blood atonement is necessary |
| Isa 53:5 | By his stripes we are healed | The mechanism of atoning suffering |
| Matt 26:28 | *"My blood of the new covenant"* | Covenant ratified by blood |
| John 6:53–56 | Drink his blood | Appropriation of his life by faith |
| Heb 9:22 | *"Without shedding of blood there is no remission"* | The irreducible necessity of blood atonement |
| Heb 12:24 | Christ's blood speaks better than Abel's | Justice → Mercy; accusation → forgiveness |
| Rev 7:14 | *"Washed their robes...made them white in the blood of the Lamb"* | Paradox: blood that cleanses |

*"For our God is a consuming fire."* — Hebrews 12:29

**Consistent Meaning:** Fire = the presence of God's holiness. For the righteous: warmth, guidance, purification. For the rebellious: consuming judgment.

### Lamb
*"Behold! The Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world!"* — John 1:29

**First Mention:** Genesis 4:2 — Abel's firstling lambs offered to God
**Consistent Meaning:** The sacrificial animal pointing forward to Christ, the ultimate and final Lamb.

**The Progressive Revelation of the Lamb:**
- **Gen 4:2** — Abel's firstling: the first recorded sacrifice, accepted by God
- **Gen 22:7–8** — Isaac asks *"where is the lamb?"* → Abraham: *"God will provide for Himself the lamb"* → a ram is provided
- **Exo 12** — The Passover lamb: specific requirements (unblemished, male, kept 4 days, bones not broken) — all fulfilled by Christ
- **Isa 53:7** — *"He was led as a lamb to the slaughter"* — the Servant is the Lamb
- **John 1:29** — John the Baptist identifies Jesus: *"Behold, the Lamb of God"*
- **Acts 8:32** — Philip explains the Suffering Servant to the Ethiopian eunuch
- **1 Pet 1:19** — *"The precious blood of Christ, as of a lamb without blemish and without spot"*
- **Rev 5** — The Lamb standing as if slain, at the center of the throne, worthy to open the scroll
- **Rev 13:8** — *"The Lamb slain from the foundation of the world"* — the sacrifice was planned before creation
- **Rev 21–22** — The Lamb is the temple, the light, and the husband of the New Jerusalem

The Lamb symbol resolves the tension of the entire OT sacrificial system: **all those lambs were pointing to one Lamb**. Rev 5 is the climax of this thread — the Lamb who was slain is now the Lamb who reigns.

**Key Chapters:** [[GEN_22_REBUILT|Gen 22]] · [[EXO_12_REBUILT|Exo 12]] · [[ISAIAH_53_REBUILT|Isa 53]] · [[JOHN_01_REBUILT|John 1]] · [[REVELATION_05_REBUILT|Rev 5]] · [[REVELATION_21_REBUILT|Rev 21]]

### Serpent / Dragon
*"The great dragon was cast out, that serpent of old, called the Devil and Satan."* — Revelation 12:9

**First Mention:** Genesis 3:1 — the serpent in the garden
**Consistent Meaning:** Satan; deception; the anti-God power operating through human systems; the cosmic enemy.

**The Serpent's Career in Scripture:**
1. **Gen 3** — Eden: the serpent deceives Eve; introduces the lie *"Has God said?"*
2. **Gen 3:15** — The protoevangelium: enmity declared; ultimate defeat prophesied
3. **Num 21** — Bronze serpent on a pole: the serpent's form becomes the instrument of healing (Christ made sin)
4. **Isa 27:1** — *"Leviathan...that twisted serpent"* → God's final destruction of Satan
5. **Ezek 28:11–19** — The King of Tyre as a figure for Satan's original glory and fall
6. **Dan 7** — The Beast rising from the sea = the dragon's earthly manifestation
7. **Matt 4** — Satan tempts Christ in the wilderness — heel-bruise; Christ overcomes
8. **John 8:44** — *"He was a murderer from the beginning...the father of lies"*
9. **Rev 12** — The red dragon: 7 heads, 10 horns; war in heaven; cast down; pursues the woman
10. **Rev 20:2** — The dragon is bound for 1,000 years
11. **Rev 20:10** — *"Cast into the lake of fire"* — the head is finally crushed (Gen 3:15 fulfilled)

**Key Chapters:** [[GEN_03_REBUILT|Gen 3]] · [[NUM_21_REBUILT|Num 21]] · [[ISAIAH_27_REBUILT|Isa 27]] · [[REVELATION_12_REBUILT|Rev 12]] · [[REVELATION_20_REBUILT|Rev 20]]

### Number 7
*"By the seventh day God had finished the work he had been doing; so on the seventh day he rested from all his work."* — Genesis 2:2

**First Mention:** Genesis 2:2–3 — the seventh day of creation; sanctified and blessed
**Consistent Meaning:** Completion; perfection; divine fullness; God's rest.

**Occurrences of Significance:**
- **7 days of creation** → the divine template for completeness
- **7th day Sabbath** → rest as the goal; the eschatological rest in Christ (Heb 4)
- **7 pairs of clean animals** on the ark (Gen 7:2)
- **7 days** of Passover celebration (Exo 12:15)
- **7 feasts** of Israel (Lev 23) → 7 stages of redemption history
- **7 churches** of Revelation → the complete church
- **7 seals, 7 trumpets, 7 bowls** → complete and total judgment
- **7 Spirits of God** (Rev 1:4; 4:5) → the fullness of the Holy Spirit (Isa 11:2)
- **"Worthy is the Lamb"** — praised with 7 qualities (Rev 5:12)
- **777** implied as the number of divine perfection (contra 666 — man's number)

### Sea / Waters (Nations)
*"Then I stood on the sand of the sea. And I saw a beast rising up out of the sea."* — Revelation 13:1
*"The waters which you saw...are peoples, multitudes, nations, and tongues."* — Revelation 17:15

**First Mention:** Genesis 1:2 — the deep, formless waters before creation
**Consistent Meaning:** The nations in their chaotic, God-rejecting state; the realm of death and the abyss; what is NOT God's order.

The sea in Rev 21:1 (*"there was no more sea"*) is therefore not primarily about geography — it means **there will be no more chaos, no more rebellious nations, no more abyss**. The new creation is one of total order, peace, and God's presence — the primordial chaos is finally gone forever.

**Key Chapters:** [[GEN_01_REBUILT|Gen 1]] · [[DANIEL_07_REBUILT|Dan 7]] · [[REVELATION_13_REBUILT|Rev 13]] · [[REVELATION_17_REBUILT|Rev 17]] · [[REVELATION_21_REBUILT|Rev 21]]

### Woman
*"A great sign appeared in heaven: a woman clothed with the sun, with the moon under her feet, and on her head a crown of twelve stars."* — Revelation 12:1

**First Mention:** Genesis 2:23 — the woman given to the man; complementary design
**Consistent Meaning:** *Positive* — God's covenant people (Israel in OT, the church in NT), the faithful community, the bride of Christ. *Negative* — the apostate religious system, Babylon, the harlot (religious corruption and idolatry).

| Reference | Woman = | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| Gen 2:23 | Eve | The complementary partner; also a type of the church |
| Rev 12:1 | Israel | Clothed with Joseph's dream (sun, moon, 12 stars = Gen 37:9) — the nation that gave birth to the Messiah |
| Eph 5:25–32 | The church | Christ's bride; the analogy of marriage applied to Christ and the church |
| Rev 17 | Babylon | The harlot — apostate, world-compromised religious system riding the Beast |
| Rev 19:7–8 | The bride | The church in final purity — *"His wife has made herself ready"* |
| Rev 21:2 | New Jerusalem | The consummated bride, descended from heaven |

The contrast between the two women in Revelation — the bride (ch. 19, 21) and the harlot (ch. 17) — is the eschatological climax of the symbol: ultimate fidelity vs. ultimate corruption; the true church vs. false religion; Christ's bride vs. Babylon's consort.

**Key Chapters:** [[GEN_02_REBUILT|Gen 2]] · [[REVELATION_12_REBUILT|Rev 12]] · [[REVELATION_17_REBUILT|Rev 17]] · [[REVELATION_19_REBUILT|Rev 19]] · [[REVELATION_21_REBUILT|Rev 21]]
