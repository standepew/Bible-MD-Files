# Typology Register
*The Complete Type → Antitype Database for the Bible Vault*

---

> **What is Biblical Typology?**
> A *type* is a divinely ordained, historical person, event, or institution in the OT that prefigures a greater, corresponding reality (*antitype*) in the NT. Types are not allegory — they are real history that God intentionally structured as prophetic shadows. The antitype is always greater than the type.
>
> *"These are a shadow of the things to come, but the substance belongs to Christ."* — Colossians 2:17
> *"These things happened to them as examples, and they were written for our instruction."* — 1 Corinthians 10:11

### T01 — Adam → Christ (Last Adam)

> *"Death reigned from Adam to Moses...Nevertheless, death reigned...even over those who had not sinned according to the likeness of the transgression of Adam, who is a type of Him who was to come."* — Romans 5:14

**Type:** Adam — the first man, federal head of humanity, failed in the garden
**Antitype:** Christ — the Last Adam, federal head of the redeemed, obedient in the garden

| Dimension | Adam (Type) | Christ (Antitype) |
|-----------|-------------|------------------|
| Origin | Created from dust | Pre-existent God incarnate |
| Garden test | Gethsemane precursor — yielded to temptation | Gethsemane — *"not my will, but yours"* |
| First command | *"Do not eat"* | *"Eat this bread"* (Lord's Supper) |
| Federal headship | Plunged all into death | Raises all his people to life |
| Nature imparted | Sinful nature passed to all (Rom 5:12) | Righteous nature given to all who believe (2 Cor 5:21) |
| Outcome | Curse enters creation | Curse removed at the cross |

### T02 — Abel → Christ (the Innocent Victim)

> *"And to Jesus the mediator of the new covenant, and to the blood of sprinkling that speaks better things than that of Abel."* — Hebrews 12:24

**Type:** Abel — the righteous man whose blood cried out after unjust murder
**Antitype:** Christ — the righteous man whose blood *speaks* forgiveness, not accusation

| Dimension | Abel (Type) | Christ (Antitype) |
|-----------|-------------|------------------|
| Righteousness | Righteous (1 John 3:12; Heb 11:4) | Perfectly righteous (2 Cor 5:21) |
| Sacrifice | Brought the firstborn of flock — accepted | Himself the Lamb — fully accepted |
| Rejection | Killed by his brother Cain | Killed by his own people (John 1:11) |
| Blood | Cried from the ground for justice | Speaks *mercy* (Heb 12:24) |

**Key Chapters:** [[GEN_04_REBUILT|Gen 4]] · [[HEBREWS_11_REBUILT|Heb 11]] · [[HEBREWS_12_REBUILT|Heb 12]] · [[1_JOHN_03_REBUILT|1 John 3]]

### T03 — Melchizedek → Christ (Eternal Priest-King)

> *"You are a priest forever according to the order of Melchizedek."* — Psalm 110:4; Hebrews 5:6

**Type:** Melchizedek — priest-king of Salem, no recorded genealogy, receives tithe from Abraham
**Antitype:** Christ — priest-king forever, his priesthood transcends the Levitical order

| Dimension | Melchizedek (Type) | Christ (Antitype) |
|-----------|--------------------|------------------|
| Title | King of Salem (peace) | King of Kings, Prince of Peace |
| Priesthood | No beginning or end recorded | Eternal (Heb 7:24) |
| Offering | Bread and wine | His body and blood (Lord's Supper) |
| Greater than | Greater than Levi (Abraham paid tithe) | Greater than all earthly priests |
| Appointed | Sudden appearance, no succession | Appointed by oath of God (Psa 110:4) |

**Key Chapters:** [[GEN_14_REBUILT|Gen 14]] · [[PSALM_110_REBUILT|Psa 110]] · [[HEBREWS_05_REBUILT|Heb 5]] · [[HEBREWS_06_REBUILT|Heb 6]] · [[HEBREWS_07_REBUILT|Heb 7]]

### T04 — Isaac → Christ (The Son Who Was Offered)

> *"By faith Abraham, when he was tested, offered up Isaac...He considered that God was able to raise him even from the dead, from which he also received him back as a type."* — Hebrews 11:17–19

**Type:** Isaac — beloved son of promise, offered on Mt. Moriah (Jerusalem), received back as if from the dead
**Antitype:** Christ — the beloved Son of the Father, offered on the same hill, actually raised from the dead

| Dimension | Isaac (Type) | Christ (Antitype) |
|-----------|--------------|------------------|
| Identity | Only son of promise | Only-begotten Son of God (John 3:16) |
| Father's heart | Abraham willingly offered Isaac | God *"did not spare His own Son"* (Rom 8:32) |
| Location | Mt. Moriah | Calvary (same mountain complex) |
| Wood | Isaac carried the wood for his own sacrifice | Christ carried his own cross (John 19:17) |
| Substitution | Ram provided at the last moment | No substitute — Christ died fully |
| Outcome | Received back from near-death | Actually raised bodily (Heb 11:19) |

**Key Chapters:** [[GEN_22_REBUILT|Gen 22]] · [[ROMANS_08_REBUILT|Rom 8:32]] · [[HEBREWS_11_REBUILT|Heb 11]] · [[JOHN_03_REBUILT|John 3:16]]

### T05 — Joseph → Christ (Rejected, Sold, Exalted, Savior)

> *"God sent me before you to preserve life."* — Genesis 45:5
> *"He raised up David as their king...of this man's seed God has brought to Israel a Savior, Jesus."* — Acts 13:22–23

Joseph is the most comprehensive OT type of Christ, with over 25 parallel points:

| Dimension | Joseph (Type) | Christ (Antitype) |
|-----------|---------------|------------------|
| Father's love | Beloved of his father Jacob | Beloved Son of the Father (Matt 3:17) |
| Sent on mission | Sent to find his brothers | *"God so loved the world that He sent..."* (John 3:16) |
| Rejected | His brothers hated him without cause | *"He came to His own, and His own did not receive Him"* (John 1:11) |
| Betrayal price | Sold for 20 pieces of silver | Betrayed for 30 pieces of silver |
| Falsely accused | Potiphar's wife accused falsely | Accused by false witnesses at trial |
| Imprisoned with two | Between two prisoners (one saved, one perished) | Crucified between two thieves (one saved) |
| Exaltation | Elevated to right hand of Pharaoh | Seated at right hand of God (Heb 1:3) |
| Name changed | Given Egyptian name, Gentile bride | Takes the name above all names; Gentile bride (the church) |
| Savior of the world | Saved Egypt and surrounding nations from famine | Saves all who come to him from spiritual death |
| Reveals himself | Made himself known to brothers weeping | Israel will look on him whom they pierced (Zech 12:10) |
| Forgiveness | Forgave his brothers fully | Full forgiveness available to those who believe |

**Key Chapters:** [[GEN_37_REBUILT|Gen 37]] · [[GEN_39_REBUILT|Gen 39]] · [[GEN_40_REBUILT|Gen 40]] · [[GEN_41_REBUILT|Gen 41]] · [[GEN_45_REBUILT|Gen 45]] · [[GEN_50_REBUILT|Gen 50]] · [[ACTS_07_REBUILT|Acts 7]] · [[REVELATION_05_REBUILT|Rev 5]]

### T11 — Jonah → Christ (3 Days in the Deep, Raised)

> *"For as Jonah was three days and three nights in the belly of the great fish, so will the Son of Man be three days and three nights in the heart of the earth."* — Matthew 12:40

**The Sign of Jonah** is the only sign Jesus explicitly gave to validate his messianic claim. The parallel is tight:

| Dimension | Jonah (Type) | Christ (Antitype) |
|-----------|--------------|------------------|
| Sleep in the storm | Slept in the boat during the storm | Slept in the boat during the storm (Mark 4:38) |
| Thrown into the sea | Cast into death's domain | Descended into the grave |
| Three days below | In the whale 3 days / 3 nights | In the heart of the earth 3 days |
| Prayer from depths | *"Out of the belly of Sheol I cried"* (Jon 2:2) | *"My God, my God, why have you forsaken me"* (Ps 22:1) |
| Resurrection | Vomited out alive | Raised bodily on the third day |
| Message to Gentiles | Preached to Nineveh — they repented | Gospel to all nations |

**Key Chapters:** [[JONAH_01_REBUILT|Jonah 1]] · [[JONAH_02_REBUILT|Jonah 2]] · [[MATTHEW_12_REBUILT|Matt 12]] · [[MATTHEW_16_REBUILT|Matt 16]] · [[LUKE_11_REBUILT|Luke 11]]

### T15 — The Passover → The Crucifixion

> *"For indeed Christ, our Passover, was sacrificed for us."* — 1 Corinthians 5:7
> *"Behold! The Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world."* — John 1:29

The most explicit type-antitype in all of Scripture, confirmed by the NT directly:

| Dimension | Passover Lamb (Type) | Christ (Antitype) |
|-----------|---------------------|------------------|
| Selection | Chosen 4 days before Nisan 14 (Exo 12:3) | Triumphal entry 4 days before crucifixion |
| Examination | Inspected for blemishes | Examined by Pilate, Herod, priests — all found no fault |
| Death time | Killed *between the evenings* (3 PM) | *"At the ninth hour"* = 3 PM (Mark 15:34) |
| Blood applied | On doorposts — judgment *passed over* | His blood applied by faith — God's wrath passes over |
| Bones unbroken | *"You shall not break a bone"* (Exo 12:46) | *"Not one of his bones shall be broken"* (John 19:33–36) |
| Eaten fully | Consumed entirely that night | Christ fully appropriated by faith |
| Bitter herbs | With bitterness of slavery | Remembering the bitterness of sin |

**Key Chapters:** [[EXO_12_REBUILT|Exo 12]] · [[JOHN_01_REBUILT|John 1]] · [[JOHN_19_REBUILT|John 19]] · [[1_CORINTHIANS_05_REBUILT|1 Cor 5]] · [[HEBREWS_09_REBUILT|Heb 9]] · [[REVELATION_05_REBUILT|Rev 5]]

### T19 — The Brazen Serpent → Christ Lifted Up

> *"And as Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, even so must the Son of Man be lifted up, that whoever believes in Him should not perish but have eternal life."* — John 3:14–15

| Dimension | Brazen Serpent (Type) | Christ (Antitype) |
|-----------|-----------------------|------------------|
| The problem | Serpents biting = sin and death | Sin = spiritual death |
| The solution | A serpent *form* on a pole | Christ *made sin* (2 Cor 5:21) on the cross |
| How it works | Look in faith and be healed | Believe in faith and be saved |
| The material | Bronze = judgment (no mercy in bronze) | Christ bore full judgment |
| Access | Available to all who looked | Available to *"whoever believes"* (John 3:16) |

**Key Chapters:** [[NUM_21_REBUILT|Num 21]] · [[JOHN_03_REBUILT|John 3]] · [[2_CORINTHIANS_05_REBUILT|2 Cor 5]] · [[GALATIANS_03_REBUILT|Gal 3:13]]

### T20 — Day of Atonement → Christ's Final Sacrifice

> *"But into the second part the high priest went alone once a year, not without blood...The Holy Spirit indicating this, that the way into the Holiest of All was not yet made manifest while the first tabernacle was still standing."* — Hebrews 9:7–8

The most theologically significant OT ceremony, unpacked in Hebrews 9–10:

| Dimension | Yom Kippur (Type) | Christ (Antitype) |
|-----------|-------------------|------------------|
| Who | High Priest alone | Christ alone |
| Frequency | Once per year | Once for all (Heb 9:12; 10:10) |
| Access | Only through the veil | Veil torn — all may enter (Heb 10:19–20) |
| Blood | Animal blood | Christ's own blood (Heb 9:12) |
| Scapegoat | Sins symbolically transferred to goat | Sins actually transferred to Christ (Isa 53:6) |
| Effect | Ceremonial cleansing | *"Perfected forever"* those who are sanctified (Heb 10:14) |
| Garments | High Priest wore white linen | Christ's righteousness clothes us |

**Key Chapters:** [[LEV_16_REBUILT|Lev 16]] · [[HEBREWS_09_REBUILT|Heb 9]] · [[HEBREWS_10_REBUILT|Heb 10]] · [[ROMANS_03_REBUILT|Rom 3:25]] · [[ISAIAH_53_REBUILT|Isa 53]]

### T26 — The Tabernacle → Christ and His Body

> *"Jesus answered and said to them, 'Destroy this temple, and in three days I will raise it up'...But He was speaking of the temple of His body."* — John 2:19–21

The Tabernacle is the single richest typological structure in the OT — every element is a shadow of Christ:

| Element | Location | What It Types |
|---------|----------|---------------|
| Outer gate | East, only entrance | Christ: *"I am the door"* (John 10:9) |
| Bronze Altar | Outer court | The cross — judgment for sin |
| Bronze Laver | Between altar and tent | Baptism; washing of regeneration |
| Lampstand | Holy Place, south | Christ: *"I am the light of the world"* |
| Showbread (12 loaves) | Holy Place, north | Christ: *"I am the bread of life"* |
| Altar of Incense | Holy Place, before the veil | Christ's intercession (Heb 7:25) |
| The Veil | Entrance to Most Holy | Christ's flesh (Heb 10:20) — torn at his death |
| Ark of the Covenant | Most Holy Place | Christ — the presence, the law, the mercy |
| Mercy Seat | Ark's lid | Christ as propitiation (Rom 3:25) |
| Cherubim on mercy seat | Above Ark | Angels at the resurrection tomb (John 20:12) |
| The cloud of glory | Above the Tent | God the Holy Spirit indwelling the church |

**Key Chapters:** [[EXO_25_REBUILT|Exo 25]] · [[EXO_26_REBUILT|Exo 26]] · [[EXO_40_REBUILT|Exo 40]] · [[HEBREWS_08_REBUILT|Heb 8]] · [[HEBREWS_09_REBUILT|Heb 9]] · [[JOHN_01_REBUILT|John 1:14 — "tabernacled among us"]]

### T22 — Elijah's Translation → The Rapture

> *"Elijah went up by a whirlwind into heaven."* — 2 Kings 2:11
> *"Then we who are alive and remain shall be caught up together with them in the clouds to meet the Lord in the air."* — 1 Thessalonians 4:17

| Dimension | Elijah's Translation (Type) | The Rapture (Antitype) |
|-----------|-----------------------------|----------------------|
| Change | Taken without dying | *"We shall not all sleep, but we shall all be changed"* (1 Cor 15:51) |
| Upward | Taken *up* in a whirlwind | *"Caught up...in the clouds"* (1 Thess 4:17) |
| Witness left behind | Elisha witnesses and cries out | Those left behind see the event |
| Elisha receives double portion | Elisha filled with double the Spirit | Church indwelt by Holy Spirit after resurrection |
| Horses and chariot of fire | Angelic escort | *"With the voice of the archangel and the trump of God"* |

**Key Chapters:** [[2KI_02_REBUILT|2 Kgs 2]] · [[MALACHI_04_REBUILT|Mal 4]] · [[1_THESSALONIANS_04_REBUILT|1 Thess 4]] · [[1_CORINTHIANS_15_REBUILT|1 Cor 15]] · [[REVELATION_04_REBUILT|Rev 4:1 — "Come up here"]]
